Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Shaykh Sabah al-Ahmad al-Jabir al-Sabah, Kuwait's deputy premier and foreign minister, and Ahmad Bin-Jasim Al Thani, Qatar's foreign minister, arrived in Damascus today. They were received at Damascus International Airport by Foreign Minister Faruq al-Shar', a number of top Foreign Ministry officials, the Kuwaiti ambassador in Damascus, and the Qatari charge d'affaires in Damascus. Shaykh Sabah made a statement to a SANA correspondent. He said: We are here to attend the meeting of the eight countries that signed the Damascus Declaration and believe in the declaration's principles. These principles have three focal points: cooperation in the political, economic, and military fields. He added: We hope that cooperation among us in the political field will increase. As for economic cooperation, we will continue discussing the agreements reached in the recent meeting held by the finance ministers. Concerning military cooperation, it is carried out on a bilateral level among the eight countries. He said: We are happy to meet in Damascus in order to renew the principles of the Damascus Declaration that were announced in Damascus. In a statement to a SANA correspondent, Shaykh Hamad Bin-Jasim Al Thani said: We, in Qatar, are concerned to have the Damascus Declaration as a nucleus for understanding and a new Arab order. During this meeting, we will hold discussions with the rest of the ministers on certain developments in the Arab arena as well as other developments to serve the Damascus Declaration countries and the Arab nation. He hope that the summit between President Hafiz al-Asad and U.S. President Clinton will result in progress on the Syrian and Lebanese tracks.
