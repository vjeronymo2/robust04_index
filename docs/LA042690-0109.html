April 26, 1990, Thursday, Home Edition

WESTSIDE DIGEST: CULVER CITY;

NINE NEW RECYCLING SITES

Newspaper, cans and bottles can be dropped off at nine new recycling sites in Culver City.

The sites, set up by the city, will take glass bottles and jars, aluminum and tin cans, plastic bottles, and newspapers.

The materials do not have to be separated. The drop-off spots are part of a recycling plan that the city is developing in an effort to reduce by 25% its waste going to landfills.

Recycling locations are: City Hall, Duquesne Avenue and Culver Boulevard; National Guard Armory, 10808 Culver Blvd.; Tellefson Park, Washington Place and Tilden Avenue; Culver West Park, 4162 Wade Ave.; Culver-Palms Methodist Church, 4464 Sepulveda Blvd.; Lindberg Park, 11020 Ocean Drive; Sunkist Park, Port Road and Dawes Avenue; Culver Park, Duquesne Avenue and Jefferson Boulevard, and McManus Park, 3459 McManus Ave.
