BFN [Text] Baghdad, 29 May (INA) -- Masatu Okita, the higher adviser of the Social Democratic Party of Japan [name and title as received], and the accompanying delegation left Baghdad today at the end of a visit to the country lasting several days at the invitation of the Iraqi National Assembly. In a statement to INA, Masatu Okita voiced his happiness over visiting Iraq and described it as constructive and fruitful. He said that the visit would promote friendly relations and cooperation in parliamentary and party areas in the two friendly countries. The Japanese delegation held talks with National Assembly Speaker Sa'di Mahdi Salih and several state and party officials. They also visited health centers in the country and became acquainted with the acute shortage of food and medicine as a result of the unjust economic blockade.
