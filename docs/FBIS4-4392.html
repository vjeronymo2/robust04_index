BFN [Text] Tokyo, March 31 KYODO -- Prime Ministers Morihiro Hosokawa of Japan and Begum Khaleda Zia of Bangladesh issued a joint press statement Thursday [31 March] saying Zia's official visit to Japan has served to strengthen bilateral relations. The statement said that in summit talks on Tuesday, Hosokawa hailed Zia's efforts for democratization and economic development. Zia outlined her government's achievements in consolidating democracy, implementing economic reforms and pursuing social and economic development, it said. She explained her policies aimed at alleviating poverty, creating rural employment and improving primary education and basic health care. Zia expressed gratitude for Japan's economic assistance to date and for its new offer to cofinance a major bridge spanning the Jamuna River, which should serve to promote economic development in the northern areas of Bangladesh. The statement said Hosokawa urged Bangladesh to take care to ensure that Japan's official development assistance (ODA) is implemented promptly and smoothly. It said the two countries agreed to start negotiations on concluding a bilateral agreement concerning the encouragement and protection of investment. Zia, who arrived in Japan on Monday for a five-day official visit, reconfirmed Bangladesh's support for the permanent membership of Japan on the UN Security Council, the statement said.
