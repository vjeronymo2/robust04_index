BFN [Text] THE Army again warned Cambodian government troops fighting the Khmer Rouge near the border yesterday that Thailand will respond "in a tough manner" if stray artillery shells continue to land on Thai soil. Army spokesman Phalangkun Klahan said the shells could have "intentionally'' been aimed against Thai territory. "The artillery shells which have fallen on our soil lately are becoming increasingly suspect," the spokesman said. " We are analyzing possibilities to determine whether they (Cambodian troops) had some motive and intended to fire directly into Thailand. "We are preparing to respond in a tough manner" to ensure Thailand's sovereignty, said Phalangkun. The warning came amidst heightened tension between Thailand and Cambodia over Phnom Penh's allegation that the Thai military is still supporting the outlawed Khmer Rouge. Cambodian leaders have blamed Thailand for the Khmer Rouge's success in retaking the strategic and gem-rich western town of Pailin, an accusation Bangkok described as a sore loser's attempt to find a scapegoat. Phnom Penh accused some units of the Thai military of having business interests with the Khmer Rouge in Pailin, which were seized by the Cambodian army a month before the Khmer Rouge recaptured it about a week ago. Supreme Command spokesman Anuson Kritsanaserani last week complained about artillery shells fired by Cambodian troops landing in Thailand. The military has also pointed out that while Phnom Penh has been "smearing" Thailand's image, Thais living along the border have had to bear the brunt of the military spillover from the Cambodian civil war as well as the burden of never-ending refugee problems. "Thai soldiers are taking care of Khmer refugees at the border. This shows we are not siding with anybody," Phalangkun said. Army Commander-in-Chief Wimon Wongwanit has been at the forefront of the diplomatic row between-Thailand and Cambodia. He last week threatened to cut humanitarian assistance to the war-torn country if it continued its verbal attacks on the government here. Wimon yesterday said he wanted to see peace in Cambodia as soon as possible and an end to the refugee problem.
