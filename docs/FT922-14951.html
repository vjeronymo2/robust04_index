920403 FT 03 APR 92 / World News in Brief: Ship's master faulted in Titanic sinking The captain of a ship which was near the sinking Titanic in 1912 could have done nothing to save the 1,490 people who died, an official UK inquiry concluded. But it criticised Captain Stanley Lord (left) for not responding to the luxury liner's distress signals. Capt Lord, censured by a 1912 inquiry, died in 1962 but supporters have fought to clear his name.
