January 6, 1989, Friday, Valley Edition

BERNARDI FEELING 'JUST FINE' A DAY AFTER FALL IN HOME

Los Angeles City Councilman Ernani Bernardi said Thursday that he feels "just fine" after being hospitalized for a head injury from a fall at his Van Nuys home.

Asked if he still plans to run for reelection in April, the northeast San Fernando Valley councilman said in a telephone interview from his room at Kaiser Permanente Medical Center in Panorama City, "as of now, certainly."

The 77-year-old Bernardi, suffering from a flu-like virus, received five stitches to the back of his head Wednesday after collapsing and hitting his head on a kitchen cabinet.

He said he expects to stay at the hospital for a few days for tests.

'Better to Stay'

"I think it's better to stay in here a couple of days to be sure that there are no complications, to be sure I am in good shape," said Bernardi, who has gotten up and walked around his hospital room. "I hope to be out in a few days."

"There are no indications of major problems," added hospital spokeswoman Karen Large.

"I fell and apparently hit my head against the sharp edge of a kitchen cabinet," he said. "It knocked me out, besides putting a gash in my head."

Bernardi said he is in the hospital's coronary care unit for privacy.

Bernardi is the council dean with 27 years of service.
