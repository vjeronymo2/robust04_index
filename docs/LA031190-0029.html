March 11, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

BOOING BOO-YAA

Concerning "Rap's Got a Brand New Bag," March 4:

So, the Boo-Yaa T.R.I.B.E. brothers are trying to get it across to other Pacific Islanders that they are going legit, making money off rap music. ("We want other Samoan kids to wake up and say, 'Hey man . . . if these big crazy-looking dudes can stand up on stage and get away from gangs and drugs, so can I.' ")

Interesting! I guess that's why they were photographed wearing gang rags, with one member pointing what appears to be a weapon toward the camera lens.

And the word Boo-Yaa represents the sound of a sawed-off shotgun?

Shame, shame, (executive) Kim Buie and Island Records. While Operation Safe Streets Deputies work hard to de-glamorize gangs, their progress gets erased by people like you. Anything for money!

MICHAEL D. MAXWELL

and HELEN MAXWELL

Long Beach

Michael D. Maxwell, a former sheriff's deputy, is deputy director, Department of Public Safety, Naval Station, Long Beach, and Helen Maxwell is West Coast correspondent for the National Assn. of Chiefs of Police. (The rap group member in the photograph actually is pointing a walking stick that he uses because of an injury.)
