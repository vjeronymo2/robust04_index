BFN [Text] Tehran, May 12, IRNA -- The visiting deputy prime minister of Belarus, Mikolay M. Kostikaw, in a meeting with Iranian Minister of Industries Mohammad Reza Ne'matzadeh here Wednesday voiced his country's readiness for industrial and medical cooperation with Iran. He referred to industrial achievements in his country especially in such fields as textiles, consumer goods and machine manufacturing and called for Tehran-Minsk cooperation in the said areas. In reply Ne'matzadeh said Iran is interested in bilateral cooperation with Belarus in [such] areas as joint venture, transfer of technology, chemicals, electronics and medicine. Kostikaw, heading a high-ranking economic and political delegation, arrived here Monday at the invitation of Commerce Minister Yahya ol-Eshaq.
