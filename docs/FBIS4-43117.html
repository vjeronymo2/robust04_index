BFN [Text] Bonn (DDP/ADN) -- The federal returning officer announced the preliminary final results of the European elections in Bonn this morning. The CDU [Christian Democratic Union] and CSU [Christian Social Union] are the strongest forces in Germany, with 38.8 percent between them. The SPD [Social Democratic Party] suffered losses and reached only 32.2 percent. At 4.1 percent, the FDP [Free Democratic Party] failed to overcome the 5-percent hurdle. On the other hand, Alliance 90/Greens improved their vote to 10.1 percent. The PDS [Party of Democratic Socialism], the successor party to the SED, received 4.7 percent and the extreme right-wing Republicans got 3.9 percent. The turnout was 60.1 percent.
