January 11, 1990, Thursday, Home Edition

MORNING REPORT: STAGE

'Miss Saigon' Seeks Broadway Home: The London musical "Miss Saigon" is expected to be the next big blockbuster on Broadway, but it may not get there until the spring of 1991 or later. The show, a modern "Madama Butterfly" that tells the story of the last days of American involvement in the Vietnam War, was supposed to get to New York this fall.

Following the smash success of his "Phantom of the Opera," producer Cameron Mackintosh is having trouble finding a theater big enough to house his next grand production, which includes a 40-member cast and a dramatic helicopter landing scene. Mackintosh refuses to take any shortcuts. "I'm not going to put 'Miss Saigon' on in New York in any less of a way than it is appearing in London," he said. The show is estimated to cost $9 million, which would make it the most expensive in Broadway history.
