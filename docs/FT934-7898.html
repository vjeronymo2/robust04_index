931118 FT 18 NOV 93 / Eggar backs market forces A STRONG restatement of the government's determination to allow market forces to rule in the energy market was given yesterday by Mr Tim Eggar, the energy minister. He said the government wanted to ensure that people and businesses got the energy they wanted at competitive prices. 'This is achieved most efficiently and effectively through competitive markets, which expose real costs and lead to keenest pricing.' He speech comes when the government is addressing issues such as the monopoly in the gas market and the future of nuclear power. The only hint Mr Eggar gave of these issues was when he referred to the Monopolies and Mergers Commission's recent recommendation to break up British Gas. He made no reference to the state-owned nuclear power industry whose future will soon be decided in a government review. Mr Eggar's speech coincided with a plea from Mr James Hann, the chairman of Scottish Nuclear, that the government cast the review in wide terms. 'As far as we can judge the key question, if not the only question in ministers' minds for the forthcoming nuclear review, is whether and on what basis our industry might be privatisable,' he told a Financial Times conference on world electricity in London yesterday. He added: 'I regard this attitude with concern.'
