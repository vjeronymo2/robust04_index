April 6, 1990, Friday, Orange County Edition

PREP ROUNDUP;

MISSION VIEJO BOYS WIN IN SWIMMING

Mission Viejo High School avoided losing two consecutive South Coast League boys' dual swimming meets for the first time in Coach Mike Pelton's 16-year career with an 81-75 victory over El Toro on Thursday.

Mission Viejo clinched the victory by winning the final event, the 400-yard freestyle relay, in 3 minutes 19.74 seconds, nearly 11 seconds in front of El Toro.

"It's a relief to get these two weeks over with," said Pelton, whose team lost to Capistrano Valley for the second consecutive year last Thursday. "I'm real pleased with the way the kids handled it."

The Diablos were again led by junior Eric Diehl and senior Tsukasa Kawabuchi. Diehl won the 200 individual medley in 1:57.68 and 100 backstroke in 53.13. Kawabuchi doubled in the 100 freestyle (47.25) and 200 freestyle (1:44.33) for the second week in a row.

The El Toro 200-yard medley relay team posted one of Orange County's top times this season by winning in 1:39.98. Mission Viejo is 4-1, 2-1. El Toro is 7-1, 2-1.

El Toro won the girls' portion of the meet, 84-72. The Chargers won seven of the 10 events to pull away from previously undefeated Mission Viejo.

El Toro (3-0, 9-0) had two double winners in Cindy Focht and Michelle Perry. Focht won the 200 freestyle (1:55.14) and 500 freestyle (5:04.17). Perry won the 200 IM (2:10.82) and 100 freestyle (53.46).
