BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on Honduran economic developments monitored through 7 June. Finance Minister Juan Ferrera has announced that to fulfill the international financial organizations' requirement to reduce the fiscal deficit from 11 percent to 5 percent, the government still needs to make an adjustment of some 1.5 billion lempiras. Meanwhile, Minister Ferrera explained that the Honduran Government will not eliminate subsidies on transportation or on the price of kerosene. (Tegucigalpa EL PERIODICO DE HONDURAS in Spanish 31 May 94 p 11) The government collected 1 billion lempiras in taxes in the first five months of the year, according to Deputy Finance Minister Ramiro Lozano Landa. He added that tax revenues were 300 million lempiras higher than in the same period in 1993. Nevertheless, Lozano Landa pointed out that the increased revenues still need to be accompanied by a 10 percent reduction in government spending, as recently noted by Hugo Neo Pino, president of the Central Bank of Honduras (BCH). (San Pedro Sula TIEMPO in Spanish 1 Jun 94 p 6) According to a report issued by the Economic Studies Department of the BCH, inflation reached 12.7 percent in the first five months of the year. The BCH report added that, if the growth rate continues at its current pace, the government will not be able to meet its goal of an 18-percent rate of inflation by the end of 1994. (Tegucigalpa LA TRIBUNA in Spanish 3 Jun 94 p 13)
