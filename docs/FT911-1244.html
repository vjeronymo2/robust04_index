910507 FT 07 MAY 91 / International Company News: Cross Border M&A Deals Activity in the Asia-Pacific region was a feature of last week's international mergers and acquisitions, writes Brian Bollen. The sale of Australian merchant bank Dominguez Barry Samuel Montagu continues Midland Bank's strategy of selling non-core assets outside Europe and enables purchaser Swiss Bank Corp to expand its international investment banking business. Morgan Grenfell furthered its ambition to establish a strong independent presence in the south east Asian securities market, taking a 30 per cent stake in Malaysian stockbroker K&N Kenanga. Laporte of the UK is expanding its business in printed wiring board chemicals into a fast growing market for electronics, through the acquisition of Taipei-based Taiwan Spechem. Elsewhere, the tender offer by South African-controlled Oceana for a stake in UK fashion retailer Etam confirms the suspicions of some market players that the UK retailing sector has bottomed out. While Oceana said it is considering an offer for the whole company and is using the tender to gauge shareholder response, Etam attacked it as an attempt to gain back door control at an unacceptably low price. In the ongoing restructuring of the global car industry, Japan's car assembly presence will increase when Mitsubishi Motors completes its purchase of one third of Volvo Cars, joining the Dutch government and Volvo as an equal partner.
