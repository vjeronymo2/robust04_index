Language: German Article Type:BFN [Text] Kronberg -- Russian Economics Minister Aleksandr Shokhin has expressed confidence concerning the upcoming talks with the finance ministers and heads of central banks of the seven major industrialized nations. After arriving in Kronberg this afternoon for the G-7 meeting, Shokhin stated: "We want to take this opportunity to improve our relations with the international financial organizations." His delegation will first brief the G-7 representatives on the economic and political changes in Russia. Russia's goal is closer cooperation with the G-7 and the IMF. With its Bosnia initiative, Russia showed a few days ago that it continues to be a major power, Shokin pointed out. Russia must improve its cooperation with the West, both in political and in economic areas. Thus, the economics minister considers that expanding the G-7 -- which now consists of the United States, Japan, Great Britain, France, Italy, Germany, and Canada -- to include Russia is desirable for political and economic reasons.
