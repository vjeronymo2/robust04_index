BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities in Chile monitored through 16 June. The Italian multinational company Parmalat S.P.A. bought 51 percent of the shares of the Chilean company Soprocar Inc., which makes and sells Copalca dairy products. On 25 May, Parmalat President Calisto Tanzi and Soprocar President Hernan Bozzo signed the agreement through which the Italian company officially entered the Chilean dairy products market under the name of Parmalat Chile. Soprocar general manager Gonzalo Martino reported that the initial investment totals $20 million. (Santiago LA TERCERA DE LA HORA in Spanish 26 May 94 Economic Section p 4) The National Statistics Institute, INE, reported that the unemployment rate was 5.3 percent in the February-April period, an increase of 0.1 percentage point compared with the previous quarter, and 0.9 percentage point compared with the same period last year. This is the highest rate since September 1992. The number of employed people was 4,962,230, an increase of 2.6 percent compared with 1993. This means that 126,490 jobs were created. The INE also reported that the unemployment rate dropped in five of the 13 regions during this period. (Santiago LA TERCERA DE LA HORA in Spanish 2 Jun 94 p 3) Mexican and Chilean aeronautics authorities signed an agreement through which Chilean airlines will transport passengers from Mexico to San Jose de Costa Rica and Guatemala on their routes from Mexico to Chile. Air traffic between Chile and Mexico has increased considerably in the past few months. A total of 11,714 passengers and 551 metric tons of cargo were transported in the first four months of the year, an increase of 5 percent and 65 percent, respectively, compared with the same period last year. (Madrid EFE in Spanish 1618 GMT 10 Jun 94) The Chilean companies Copec, Sipetrol, and Enap, which made up the Latin American Oil Company, Clapsa, and the U.S. company Oryx Energy won the bid to prospect for oil in Ecuador for four years. They signed a risk contract in which they will invest $45 million. The Chilean companies share the contract with 32.5 percent and the U.S. company with 50 percent. The area to be prospected totals 2,000 square kilometers. (Madrid EFE in Spanish 1608 GMT 10 Jun 94)
