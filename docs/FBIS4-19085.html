BFN [Announcer-read report over video] [Text] The official talks between Turkey and Croatia were held in Ankara today. An air transportation agreement was signed between the two countries. Mate Granic, Croatian deputy prime minister and foreign minister who arrived in Ankara this morning, called on Foreign Minister Hikmet Cetin and conferred with him for some time. Later, talks were held between delegations headed by the foreign ministers of the two countries. In a statement before the meeting, Cetin noted that there are historical ties between Turkey and Croatia and expressed the belief that economic and political relations will develop even further. Cetin remarked that the Serbs should also take part in the Washington agreement signed between Bosnians and Croatians. The Washington agreement is important in creating a multinational and multicultural Bosnia-Herzegovina, Cetin stressed. Expressing gratitude for Croatia's assistance to Turkish troop participation in the peace force in Bosnia-Herzegovina, Cetin declared that Turkish soldiers will go to Bosnia-Herzegovina in order to contribute to peace. In turn, Granic thanked Turkey for its contribution to the peace process. Explaining that the Washington agreement is important because it grants equal rights to Muslims and Croatians, Granic said: It is our aim to get the Serbs to also take part in this agreement. Later, Turkey and Croatia signed an air transportation agreement. The agreement was signed by Ambassador Tugay Ulucevik, deputy under secretary of the Foreign Ministry, and Croatian Ambassador to Ankara (Hidayet Vichdivic). The agreement envisages bilateral cooperation in the field of civil aviation.
