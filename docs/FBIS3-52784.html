Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] People's Assembly Speaker 'Abd-al-Qadir Qaddurah received Tahir al-Masri, speaker of the Jordanian House of Representatives, at noon today. Shakir Is'id, head of the Arab and Foreign Affairs Committee, attended the meeting. Discussions dealt with bilateral parliamentary relations and the exchange of visits and delegations between the two assemblies in the two fraternal countries. Discussions also dealt with issues pertaining to the Arab Parliamentary Union [APU], its activities, ways of activating its role, and the next APU session. They also discussed the agenda of the next Interparliamentary Union's conference and coordination at this conference among Arab parliaments. Foreign Minister Faruq al-Shar' received Tahir al-Masri this morning. During the meeting, discussions dealt with developments in the peace process, Arab coordination, and the importance of the Geneva summit between Presidents Hafiz al-Asad and Bill Clinton in achieving a just, comprehensive peace in the region. The speaker of the Jordanian House of Representatives arrived in Damascus via the Dar'a border post this morning. He was welcomed at the Dar'a border post by Dar'a Governor Dr. Walid 'Uthman; Shakir Is'id, head of the Arab and Foreign Affairs Committee at the People's Assembly; and the Jordanian ambassador in Damascus. Tahir al-Masri made a statement to a SANA correspondent in Dar'a in which he said that he is happy to visit sisterly Syria and meet with Syrian People's Assembly Speaker 'Abd al-Qadir Qaddurah and the assembly members. He added that this visit opens the way for the exchange of visits between the two assemblies in the two sisterly countries. He said that discussions during the visit will deal with some bilateral issues pertaining to the work of the two assemblies, general issues pertaining to the APU, and bilateral issues. Al- Masri said the visit is particularly important because it emphasizes interaction and communication between Jordan and Syria and between the two parliaments in the two sisterly countries. He also noted that he is looking forward to fruitful and useful meetings in Syria.
