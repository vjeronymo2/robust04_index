Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [From the "National Newscast"] [Text] In an inflexible attitude and rejecting talks to study their demands which have been proposed by the government, a small group of former Government Ministry soldiers have carried out coercive pressure at the UN headquarters. The government has decided to discontinue discussing their demands until they stop the pressure. The government will enforce the state of law and guarantee the country's institutionalization. The Government Ministry was scheduled to meet with this group, and Minister Alfredo Mendieta, who has been sick for the past three days, authorized a ministry delegation, including deputy ministers, to meet with the group. At first, however, the protesters stubbornly refused to hold the meeting, demanding Minister Mendieta attend the meeting. This same group has occupied the Managua Cathedral twice before. They also tried to ruin the awards ceremony when Salvadoran President Alfredo Cristiani visited our country recently. The government delegation talked to representatives of the Nicaraguan Human Rights Center to persuade it to convince the protesters to change their minds regarding their demands that the minister be present at the meeting. The protesters agreed but, at the same time, a small group of protesters began exerting pressure at the UN headquarters. The government decided not to continue talking to them until they stop exerting pressure at the UN headquarters. The Government Ministry reiterates its willingness to advance the state of law and guarantee the democratic development of the country's institutionalization. [Panama City ACAN in Spanish at 0029 GMT on 10 Mar 94 cites Managua radio stations as reporting that "at least 50 former Sandinist soldiers occupied today, Wednesday, the building where the UN headquarters is located in Nicaragua to pressure Violeta Chamorro's government to compensate them for retiring to civilian life." ACAN adds that according to media sources, the former soldiers "are holding Francesco Vincenti, UN representative in Nicaragua, at the UN installation," and they want him to mediate to get the Nicaraguan Government to accept their demands. The report adds: "A riot police contingent has cordoned off the UN building, located near Plaza Espana, in the southwestern part of Managua."]
