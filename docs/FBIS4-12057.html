BFN [Announcer-read report followed by 'Imanu'el Rosen video report] [Excerpt] The representatives of the Foreign and Defense ministries of the three countries which are contributing personnel to the international force in Hebron will arrive in Israel tomorrow. Foreign Minister Shim'on Peres today promised the Norwegian, Danish, and Italian ambassadors in Israel that the IDF [Israel Defense Forces] will guarantee the safety of the international force. Our political correspondent 'Imanu'el Rosen reports: [Begin recording] [Rosen] Foreign Minister Shim'on Peres today sought to assuage the concern of the ambassadors of the three countries which agreed to send observers to Hebron. For this reason he also summoned Major General Dani Yatom, the officer who will be in charge of the safety of the 160 international observers as of next week. The complicated problems of coordination will be tackled, among other ways, by special uniforms and easily identified vehicles. Peres believes that the headache is not too bad: [Peres] In English we refer to the temporary international presence in Hebron as TIPH, Temporary International Presence in Hebron [preceding five words in English]. In Hebrew we can refer to it as tipa [meaning one drop in Hebrew]. [passage omitted] [end recording]
