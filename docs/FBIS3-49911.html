Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Prague, Jan 12 (CTK) -- Slovakia will use the U.S. Partnership for Peace project to seek full membership of NATO, Slovak President Michal Kovac said in a meeting today with Czech President Vaclav Havel. Czech Presidential office head Lubos Dobrovsky told journalists that Kovac described the partnership initiative as a "great opportunity for central European countries' integration into Euro-American civilization and the sphere of European values, which are protected and guaranteed by NATO as a militarily-formed security structure." Havel's talks with Kovac were one of a series of talks with heads of state of other Visegrad Group countries (Poland and Hungary) held at Prague castle today. After the talks with Havel, Kovac left for talks with U.S. President Bill Clinton. After their negotiations there will be a working dinner given by Clinton for his Visegrad partners at the U.S. Ambassador's residence.
