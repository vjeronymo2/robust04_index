Language: English Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Sanaa, Feb 21 (AFP) -- Rival Yemeni troops clashed Monday leaving four dead and 20 wounded, a Defence Ministry source said. The fighting broke out a day after country's leaders signed a deal patching up their differences. A tank was also destroyed when brigades from the north and south fought for an hour in the Abyan region, 100 kilometres (60 miles) east Aden, a source close to Defense Minister Haytham Qasim Tahir said. Each side accused the other of sparking the clash after moving troops toward the old border between the former North Yemen and poorer South Yemen, which merged in 1990. On Sunday, Yemeni President 'Ali 'Abdallah Salih and his southern rival Vice-President 'Ali Salim al-Bid met in the Jordanian capital Amman to sign a deal calling for major economic, political and military reforms. The accord, aimed at ending a dispute which threatened to split the country, called for withdrawing troops facing each other along the old border and banned troop movements in cities and on main roads. Monday's fighting ended after the intervention of a military commission that had been set up to implement withdrawals, officials said. Salim Salih Muhammad, a top YSP [Yemeni Socialist Party] official and member of the Presidential Council, also told AFP that Salih, al-Bid and Jordan's King Husayn had contacted officials in Abyan to restore calm. King Husayn said Sunday that the accord was "a positive start for Arabs to settle their own differences." Muhammad accused northern troops of having deployed further south, closer to Aden. But a northern official who asked not to be named alleged that southern troops had moved forward. [passage omitted] Al-Bid underlined his commitment to the accord despite "reservations and wounds." He said he could visualize the faces of relatives killed in political violence, but "Yemen is dearer to us. "We are going to turn over a new leaf...and we will start applying this document," Al-Bid added. Salih thanked the United States and European Union for helping in the reconciliation.
