BFN [Text] Tirana, June 6 (ATA) -- In yesterday's sitting of the trial of 10 former senior officials of the Albanian communist system, five witnesses, former employees of the Interior Branch of Sarande, gave testimony on the practices used in guarding the sea border, the way of realising the imitation and procedures carried out to transport drowned corpses who had attempted to cross the border. Mithat Bala, Luan Zani, Reiz Maksuti, (non-commissioned officers), confessed before the jury that the order for the transportation of corpses on board of the motorboat and their demonstration was given by the former chairman of the district's interior branch, Defendant Veiz Haderi, to show in this way "the vigilant guarding of the border." Witness Maqo Como, political prisoner, and former deputy and minister, bore testimony of the history of his family in prisons and internment, a history which bears the seal of the Expulsion-Internment Commission. Zvetllana Marku, the daughter of General Gjin Marku executed by the regime of dictator Hoxha, testified to the injustices and intolerance of this commission.
