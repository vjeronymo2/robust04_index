BFN [Report by political correspondent Shim'on Schiffer] [Excerpt] For the first time since the beginning of the Israeli-Syrian negotiations, high-ranking political sources last night voiced "cautious optimism" concerning the contacts with Damascus. U.S. Secretary of State Warren Christopher, who arrived in Israel yesterday afternoon after a series of talks with President al-Asad and Foreign Minister Faruq al-Shar' in Damascus, met with Prime Minister Yitzhaq Rabin at his Jerusalem residence late last night. The meeting, which was attended by Dennis Ross and Professor Itamar Rabinovich, lasted about an hour and a half. After the meeting, Itamar Rabinovich, Israel's ambassador to the United States, said: "The negotiations held in Damascus should be viewed as a few more small steps at the start of a long trek. Further declarations were made and answers given in the course of Christopher's talks. The talks should be viewed in terms of protracted negotiations. Not every declaration or answer immediately creates a new reality." Nevertheless, last night both American and Israeli sources were under the impression that practical Israeli-Syrian negotiations have been launched and that while the gaps between the parties' stands are very profound, Christopher sensed that the Syrian stand has shifted. Last night, Christopher presented Rabin with detailed Syrian answers to the clarifications Israel had requested concerning the arrangement proposed by Syria.
