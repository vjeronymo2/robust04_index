930414 FT 14 APR 93 / Arts: Today's Television There will, I imagine, be cheering as today's Dispatches goes out (9.00 C4). It reports on small businessmen who live in fear of the Inland Revenue and recounts stories of intimidation by tax inspectors. One former tax man asserts 'Most inspectors believe that all small traders are fiddling'. There are horrific tales of a couple who had to pay their life savings to the Revenue, and of a publican who died after being pursued by inspectors over profits he could never have made. Tomorrow's World screens a report from Paris featuring rental schemes for electric cars, a system for superimposing different adverts on billboards, and computerisation of the organ in Notre Dame (7.30 BBC1). QED seeks to show what will be the effects of the next Tokyo earthquake; the last was 70 years ago and the next is now due. This time, owing to Tokyo's key position in the world economy, the effects may be international (9.35 BBC1). BBC2 begins a six-part series which has been causing difficulties before even reaching the screen: Living Islam. It attempts to demystify the religion (8.10).
