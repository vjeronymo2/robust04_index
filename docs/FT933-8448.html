930816 FT 16 AUG 93 / Private plans for child offenders THE GOVERNMENT is to press ahead with plans to establish private-sector homes for dangerous children, shrugging off Labour comparisons with the troubled privatisation of the prison escort service. Mrs Virginia Bottomley, health secretary, will publish a consultation paper today recommending a change in the law to allow private and voluntary organisations to offer accommodation. Officials said the proposals followed a rise in the cost of local authority provision of secure accommodation, which was reserved for children convicted of serious crimes who were too young to be sent to prison. The average cost of a local authority place is Pounds 1,680 a week. Mr David Blunkett, shadow health secretary, said the proposals fell into the same bracket as the 'farcical' transfer of the prison escort service to Group 4, which has suffered several embarrassing escapes by prisoners. 'A privatised service can only be profitable if it relies on larger units that often become colleges of crime,' he said. Ms Liz Lynne, Liberal Democrat community care spokeswoman, said: 'This is just another crazy scheme that could end up in catastrophe.'
