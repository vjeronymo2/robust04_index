BFN [Text] The National Council on Belize [Consejo Nacional de Belize], CNB, was formally constituted yesterday after President Ramiro de Leon Carpio signed a decree creating this consultative and advisory organization under the Foreign Ministry. Foreign Minister Maritza Ruiz de Vielman will chair the CNB, and its members are: Fernando Andrade Diaz-Duran, Roberto Bermejo Gonzalez, Eduardo Castillo Carriola, Edwin Fernando Guzman Ovalle, Alberto Herrarte Gonzalez, Roberto Herrera Ibarguen, Carlos Larios Ochaita, Jorge Lujan Munoz, Oscar Clemente Marroquin Godoy, Gabriel Orellana Rojas, Jorge Skinner Klee, and Francisco Villaran Kramer. These 12 individuals will be sworn in shortly. The CNB was created by government Decree 259-94 that asserts that the territorial difference with Belize and the search for a solution that conforms to the Constitution's precepts is a matter of national interest. The purpose is to modernize and reorganize the CNB as a study, planning, consultative, and advisory office of the Foreign Ministry for everything related to the territorial disagreement with Belize. According to the decree, the CNB will be authorized to appoint permanent or temporary commissions or sub-commissions as well as take the necessary measures to regulate their work. The decree will take effect when it is published in the official daily.
