BFN [Text] Belgrade, May 11 (TANJUG) -- Many casualties have been reported in Wednesday's heavy clashes in western Bosnia-Herzegovina between Moslem units loyal to the Moslem leadership in Sarajevo and that of the Autonomous Province of Western Bosnia (APZB), Moslem-controlled Sarajevo radio reported. The radio, controlled by the authorities loyal to Moslem leader Alija Izetbegovic, said that the APZB forces continued for a third day in a row their "savage attacks on the Cazin border regions, Todorovo and the area of Buzim." The radio reported major damage to civilian facilities. The APZB was proclaimed in a referendum of the Moslem population in the region in September 1993 and local businessman and former Bosnian presidency member Fikret Abdic became its president. Abdic, a political opponent to Izetbegovic, has in the meantime concluded peace agreements with the Serb Republic, the state of the Bosnian Serbs.
