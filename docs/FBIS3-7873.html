Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Bucharest ROMPRES, 28/2/1993 -- Malta's President Censu Tabone ended his visit in Romania on Monday [28 February] noon with an official ceremony to his departure. After a last round of talks, attended also by the members of the delegations, the two presidents made short statements to the press. Romanian President Ion Iliescu underlined the political significance of the visit -- the first at that level since the December revolution in Romania and Mr. Censu Tabone's first visit in Romania -- and the favourable political climate of the relations between Romania and Malta. "President Censu Tabone's presence in Romania represents an expression of the political willingness to consolidate the bilateral relations and the collaboration within international bodies," said the Romanian head of state. Both parties have agreed to intensify the cooperation in the fields of shipment, telecommunications, services, tourism, science and culture. The Romanian Foreign Ministry will draw up a draft treaty of friendship between the two countries to be signed, if possible, within the current year, President Iliescu said. In his turn, the Maltese head of state said that during the three days of his visit in Romania he learned of Romania as of a country experiencing a development process, a people who regained the democratic beliefs and who affirmed an European vocation, who is boldly integrating within the European structures. President Tabone expressed hope that the preparation of the friendship treaty would not last too long as its finalisation within the current year would enable President Iliescu to make a visit in the Republic of Malta. President Tabone said he would ask the Maltese Parliament and Government to invite the Romanian president for a visit in Malta. The Maltese president's visit in Romania included interviews with the speaker of the Chamber of Deputies, Adrian Nastase, and with the Senate's vice-president Ioan Doru Taracila (the Senate president was making a visit in Japan at the time). An invitation was made that a Romanian parliamentary delegation make a visit in the Republic of Malta. During his stay, President Tabone visited also Sinaia mountain resort and the Brasov County, central Romania.
