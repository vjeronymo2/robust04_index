BFN [Text] Kiev, 22 Jun -- The Iranian Foreign Minister Dr. 'Ali Akbar Velayati has sent a letter to Ukraine's Foreign Minister Anatoliy Zlenko concerning the developments in Crimea following a decision by the autonomy's Supreme Council to restore the Crimean Republic's Constitution of 1992. "The Islamic Republic of Iran is convinced," the letter stresses, "that this problem must be solved on the basis of full adherence to the principles of international law: Respect of sovereignty, territorial integrity and inviolability of borders, nonviolent conflict resolution, and noninterference in the states' internal affairs." The author of the letter noted that the "Crimean issue" was Ukraine's internal problem and could be resolved peacefully. Dr. Velayati stressed that Iran strived to achieve the above goal by means of mutual trust and peaceful coexistence.
