920122 FT 22 JAN 92 / UK News (Employment): Strike action planned for Ford New Holland SHOP STEWARDS at Ford New Holland, the tractor maker owned by Fiat and Ford, decided yesterday to organise selective strike action in an attempt to gain improvements in a three-year pay offer providing 4 per cent in the first year, Michael Smith writes. Mr Dave Vos, convener, would not say what form the action would take or when it would happen, but on Monday he mentioned the possibility of one-day and two-day strikes. The 1,600 manual workers who have been made the offer oppose the duration of the deal and the company's proposal to introduce temporary workers. Skilled workers want financial recognition for new responsibilities they say they have taken on. Ford New Holland has refused to change its offer.
