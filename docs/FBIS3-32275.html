Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on Panamanian economic developments monitored through 2 February. Planning Minister Delia de Cardenas reported today the Panamanian Government had taken the first step to restructure its foreign debt with private banks by issuing bonds for more than $450 million in European Currency Units, Yens, and Eurobonds. (Panama City ACAN in Spanish 0202 GMT 1 Feb 94) An Economic Commission for Latin America (ECLA) report has stated that Panama attained its economic recovery goal for 1992 after the crisis of 1988 to 1989 by registering an 8-percent growth. According to the latest statistics available at the Comptroller General's Office, the Panamanian economy registered a 5.9-percent growth in its gross domestic product, and unemployment decreased from 14.2 percent to 12.5 percent in 1992. (Panama City EL PANAMA AMERICA in Spanish 31 Jan 94 p 17a) According to a document issued by the National Baking Commission, the international banking center that operates in Panama increased its assets by 11 percent in the last quarter of 1993 as compared to the same period in 1992. (Panama City LA PRENSA in Spanish 26 Jan 94 p 11a) Panama's fuel debt with Mexico and Venezuela is more than $250 million, while interests accumulate at a rate of $800,000 per month, Roberto Alfaro, commerce and trade minister, reported yesterday. Panama has presented two proposals to negotiate the fuel debt and is waiting for the response from both governments. (Panama City ACAN in Spanish 0001 GMT 26 Jan 94) Eduardo Stein, president of the Foundation for the Economic and Social Development of Central America, has told members of the Panamanian Association of Business Executives regarding NAFTA that because 70 percent of Panama's gross domestic product is represented by the service sector, it, therefore, has a significant negotiating advantage over the rest of the Central American nations. (Panama City EL PANAMA AMERICA in Spanish 29 Jan 94 p 11a)
