Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] The Serbian assembly has been holding a consultative session for three days and yet the chairman of the assembly has not been elected, not even in the morning part of the session. The assembly is currently in recess until 1600 [1500 GMT] so that the heads of the deputy caucuses can reach an agreement. Srdjan Savic reports: [Savic] At the beginning of the session, chairman Vojislav Dimitrijevic told deputies that he had received a new nomination only from the Socialist Party of Serbia for the post of the chairman of the assembly. The party's new candidate is Dragan Tomic, head of the party's deputy caucus. Vuk Draskovic, president of the Serbian Renewal Movement, explained why DEPOS [Democratic Movement of Serbia] had not proposed its new candidate today. He said that the parliament's left and right parties' attempts to outvote one another were in vain, and that it was necessary to reach a political agreement on the division of responsibility for the legislative, executive, and judiciary authorities. The Socialists bear the greatest responsible for all compromises and agreements, because they are the first party on the political ladder, Draskovic said. [passage omitted]
