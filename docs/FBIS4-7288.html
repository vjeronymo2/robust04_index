BFN [Text] Ulaanbaatar, April 28 (OANA-MONTSAME) -- The newspaper "ARDYN ERH" informs that the head of the Central Intelligence Board (CIB) of Mongolia has been charged by the prosecutor general of Mongolia to strengthen the actions of the board for ensuring security. In an official paper addressing himself it was said that on the pages of newspapers came to be published some copies of the secret documents pertaining to the classified or even top secret information. Thus there has been created unfavourable conditions in reference to the state security. The document stresses the significance of strengthening the efficient arrangement of the work of the CIB for investigating such actions bearing a criminal nature as the divulgence of the state top secrets through the mass media and the losing of secret papers as well.
