940318 FT 18 MAR 94 / International Company News: GE Capital Dollars 2.2bn bid for Kemper rejected The prospect of the first big hostile bid for a US fund management-based company moved a step closer yesterday as the board of Kemper voted unanimously to reject a Dollars 2.2bn bid from GE Capital. Directors of the Chicago-based fund management, insurance and broking company were meeting three days after GE Capital, the financial services arm of General Electric, first publicised its interest in an acquisition. Mr David Mathis, Kemper chairman and chief executive, accused GE of trying to 'steal Kemper with a 'low ball' proposal'. Shareholders would benefit more from 'providing current management with the opportunity to realise Kemper's potential,' he added. The comments, contained in a letter to Mr Jack Welch, chairman of GE, which was released after the meeting, echo Kemper's earlier rejection of the uninvited advances. GE replied: 'We will have a definitive response and will make it at the time of our choosing.'
