Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Malaysia's foreign minister said he would like to see more contacts with Singapore at a younger level. Mr. Abdullah Ahmad Badawi, who is in Singapore on a two-day visit, said bilateral relations are good and there is very [as heard] contact at the ministers' level. But he said there is a need to replace the old schoolboy network forged through the Raffles College link. Mr. Abdullah was speaking in an informal dialogue session with young PAP [People's Action Party] members. At a news conference this afternoon, Mr. Abdullah said that the ASEAN regional forum on security and the need to ensure its success was a topic discussed by Singapore leaders. On the growth triangle, the foreign minister said the discussions touched on the need to have an easy flow of labor among the three countries in the group. Mr. Abdullah said the three should maximize their advantages in labor resources on a project basis.
