March 29, 1990, Thursday, South Bay Edition

TYRONE PAUL, LARRY WILLIAMS WIN PLAYER OF THE YEAR HONORS

Senior forwards Tyrone Paul of Morningside High School and Larry Williams of Chadwick have been named CIF-Southern Section basketball Players of the Year in their respective divisions.

Paul, who led Morningside to the Ocean League title and into the Southern Section 3-AA playoffs as the top seed, was chosen Division III Co-Player of the Year with center Chris Gaines of 3-A champion Dos Pueblos High of Goleta after a tie vote by members of the First Interstate Bank-Amateur Athletic Foundation board of sportswriters.

The 6-foot-6 Paul averaged 20.5 points a game for Morningside, which was ranked No. 1 in the state among Division III teams before it was stunned by Lompoc, 83-80, in the quarterfinals of the 3-AA playoffs.

Paul is only the third Morningside boy to be named Southern Section Player of the Year in basketball and the first since 1974, when Michael Santos was chosen 3-A MVP.

Paul heads five South Bay players selected to the All-Division III team.

Also named to the first team were guards Martell Bland of Morningside and Steve Clover of Rolling Hills. Second-team honors went to junior forwards Roger Hendrix of Rolling Hills and Wyking Jones of St. Bernard.

Chadwick's Williams was selected Division V Player of the Year. The 6-5, 205-pounder led the Dolphins to the semifinals of the Southern Section 1-A playoffs by averaging a team-leading 19.5 points and 12.5 rebounds a game. Williams was also Prep League Most Valuable Player.

Four area players were named All-CIF in Division IV, which includes the Southern Section 2-AA and 2-A divisions.

Guards Kenny Davis of Serra and Scott Panfil of El Segundo were named to the first team, while El Segundo center Kenny Talanoa and Miraleste forward David Terrell were second-team picks.

Forward Shon Tarver of state champion Santa Clara of Oxnard was a repeat choice as Division VI Player of the Year. ROB FERNAS
