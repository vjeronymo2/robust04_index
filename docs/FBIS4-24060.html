BFN [Excerpts] Brazzaville, 22 June (PANA) -- The Congolese foreign affairs minister, Benjamin Bounkoulou, has reiterated his country's support to the creation of the United Nations Assistance Mission in Rwanda (UNAMIR II) to save lives and put an end to the wanton killings. In a press statement released Tuesday [21 June] in Brazzaville, Bounkoulou recalled that Congo was not only involved in the OAU military observer mission, but also supports the further expansion of the United Nations [words indistinct] in that war torn country. Congo has accepted to contribute 300 troops to Rwanda within the framework of the proposed UNAMIR. One hundred troops already volunteered to go to Rwanda while 200 others will be chosen by the government. Already, 25 Congolese troops were [words indistinct] the UNAMIR. [passage omitted] Congo is among the 16 African countries asked by the United Nations to send 5,500 troops to Rwanda to carry out humanitarian assignments and peace-keeping work.
