BFN [Text] Croatian Foreign Minister Mate Granic this evening received special UN envoy Charles Redman. Following the talks Charles Redman stated that the United States has very close and good cooperation with the Croatian Government: [Begin Redman recording in English with superimposed Serbo-Croatian translation] On this occasion I am staying in Croatia just very briefly, on my way from Sarajevo to Washington. Some very important decisions ought to be reached as regards what we can do in order to strengthen the UN role in Bosnia. At the same time, nobody will forget that we ought to work on some very important issues here in Croatia. We have made significant progress, but we still need to do more, and I hope that my meeting and talks with Minister Granic will strengthen this process and achieve progress in the right direction. We are both pleased with the progress that has been achieved in the past months. On behalf of the United States, I would like to say that it is our intention to stay in close touch with the Croatian Government so that this process should proceed further. [end recording] Croatian Foreign Minister Mate Granic said that one of the main issues discussed in talks with the special U.S. envoy was the current situation in Bosnia-Herzegovina: [Begin Granic recording] We discussed the situation in Bosnia-Herzegovina, in particular [word indistinct] Gorazde, progress reached in talks between the Croats and the Bosniaks regarding setting up the federation of Bosnia-Herzegovina, and the situation in UN protected areas, the latest behaviur displayed by the Serbs in the UN protected areas and in Bosnia-Herzegovina, and the possible U.S. and UN moves. [end recording]
