Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Seoul, Jan. 21 (YONHAP) -- South Korea is prepared for the possibility that North Korea will improve its relations not only with South Korea but also with Western nations when its nuclear issue is solved. If the nuclear problem is settled, inter-Korean relations will rapidly improve and at the same time North Korea's relations with Western nations will make considerable improvement, unification minister Yi Yong-tok told the Advisory Council on Democratic and Peaceful Unification on Friday. The government has a set of measures to prepare for such a possibility, he said. "North Korea will guardedly seek to open its doors on a limited scale so that the open-door policy doesn't stand in the way of maintaining its political system," he said. "And I think the North Korean nuclear issue will be solved in a manner desirable to us, though the timing is a question." He stressed the need for a positive posture toward North Korea. "We must now slough off the negative and passive view of North Korea we held in the past and take a positive posture toward desirable change in North Korea." He warned against inter-Korean dialogue for dialogue's sake or for show. "We must now strive to hold a dialogue that will be of substantial help to solving the problem."
