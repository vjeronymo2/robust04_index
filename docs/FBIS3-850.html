Language: English Article Type:BFN [From the "African News" program] [Text] Relief organizations in Angola have withdrawn from the southeastern city of Menongue after heavy shelling around the airport. The organizations, which include Care International and Medecins Sans Frontieres [Doctors Without Borders], have evacuated 14 of their workers. Menongue, which is government-controlled, has been under siege by the UNITA [National Union for the Total Independence of Angola] rebels for more than one year. They control most of the surrounding countryside. The attacks were largely ineffective, but intensified at the end of last week.
