Language: French Article Type:BFN ["PARIS MATCH"-B.V.A. Opinion Poll: "Balladur Down Two Points: End of His Period of Grace, for 58 Percent of the French, He Lulls the People. Juppe Enjoys Highest Confidence Rating: Up Nine Points"] [Text] The latest PARIS MATCH-B.V.A. opinion poll discloses that the period of grace which the prime minister was enjoying, is over. Edouard Balladur's popularity dropped by 2 percent, although he is still supported by 53 percent of the people. Our survey reveals that 58 percent of the French think that he has a tendency to lull the French to sleep; 51 percent think he does not speak the truth. An absolute majority of those interviewed think that the prime minister has the style of a head of state, but there is also a negative side to this: 51 percent state that he thinks too much about the presidential election. Francois Mitterrand sees his popularity ratings rise by 3 percent, tilting the balance between positive and negative opinions into the positive region again. The question referring to legislative election voting intentions was asked to 2,948 persons between 15 and 18 February and between 1 and 4 March 1994; the other questions were out to 973 persons between 1 and 4 March. Voting intentions are calculated on the basis of the number of persons registered for the elections. Sampling was done according to the quota method: sex; age; occupation of family head; and region and area (city, rural...). Results should be interpreted taking into account the usual error margins related to any poll and the size of the sample. There error margins are: approximately one to two points for voting intentions; approximately two to three points for the overall results; approximately four to five points for the results referring to the large political families.
