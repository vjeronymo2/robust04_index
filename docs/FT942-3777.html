940613 FT 13 JUN 94 / European Elections: Tension to the end for headmaster The swing to Labour in London North West, a seat the party should have won easily, was less than 3 per cent. The party's candidate, Mr Robert Evans, a local headmaster, was tense right up to the count. He paced up and down the vast gym at the Harrow leisure centre where the count for the was being held, and said: 'I am cautiously optimistic.' It was only towards midnight, when the boxes piled up on the right of the table clearly showed that Mr Evans had made it, that he began to relax. Lord Bethell, the longest-serving English MEP to stand in the election, blamed mid-term blues for the loss of the seat. 'There was a feeling amongst the people that they wanted to punish the government, give it a kick, and I'm one of the victims.'
