930923 FT 23 SEP 93 / Arts: Today's Television A new series of Critical Eye on Channel 4 begins by asking whether the Polish people are better off living under a market economy rather than the command economy of the communist era. Made by the leftwing Tattooist group, the programme looks at Brazil - a country with a capitalist economy which has failed to reach the standard of living achieved by Poland in its 40 years of socialism (9.00). It seems surprising that television makes so few programmes about dogs, given the widespread enthusiasm for them. There is, of course, 'One Man And His Dog', and there was once upon a time Barbara Woodhouse. So the start of The Dog's Tale on BBC2 is something of an occasion. The seven-part series roams the world to tell the story of man's best friend ..and slave, and sometimes dinner (9.30). Small Objects Of Desire, is a series in which the subject matter is at times obscured by the programme-making techniques. Today's object, the sandwich, must have been with us since the invention of the loaf, even if the Earl of Sandwich did do his famous three-layer trick in 1762 (10.10 BBC2).
