BFN [Text] Shenzhen, May 28 (XINHUA) -- Shenzhen Foreign Exchange Trading Center started business today. The center, in south China's Guangdong Province, is the first of its kind. It is authorized to buy and sell foreign exchange, to conduct transactions in bills of exchange, borrowing and lending of foreign currencies, services connected with investment in foreign exchange and consultancy on development trends of foreign exchange. Enterprises and individuals on the Chinese mainland will be able to maintain the value of their foreign exchange and prevent any risks in foreign exchange through operations at the center, XINHUA was told by officials of the center. The officials said the center has got financial legal status with a membership system, and it has a registered capital of 100 million yuan. At present, it has 14 banks and financial organizations as its member share-holders.
