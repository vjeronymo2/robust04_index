BFN [Text] A call for an immediate cessation of hostilities and for preservation of the national unity of Yemen are among the most significant issues contained in the draft resolution of the 11th Ministerial Summit of the Nonaligned Movement. On 31 May, experts began to work on the resolution, which will be presented for final approval on 3 June. Meanwhile, the heads of the delegations are meeting behind closed doors in what the organizers are calling a brainstorm, which will last into the night. In the morning, Robaina is expected to give an address on the democratization of international relations. Since the beginning of the summit, numerous delegations have defended the need for radical transformation of the UN Security Council and increased openness in its proceedings+. In an address before the plenum on 31 May, Robaina issued a call to thwart efforts to turn the United Nations into an intervention tool against underdeveloped nations. Robaina has also conducted an intensive exchange of opinions with foreign ministers from diverse countries. Robaina will meet today with representatives from Kenya, Belize, South Africa, Palestine, Algeria, and Qatar.
