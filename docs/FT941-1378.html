940325 FT 25 MAR 94 / Arts: Today's Television BBC1 is claiming that Tomorrow's World will undertake 'the biggest experiment in the world' (7.30). As part of British Science Week viewers will be asked to participate via telephone in deciding whether people tell lies more convincingly on camera than on radio or in print. Is there any conceivable value in this beyond a ratings-winning gimmick? The programme makers seem chiefly preoccupied with providing sufficient telephone lines. Perhaps it was just that Friday night feeling, but last week's opening episode in ITV's new comedy, Time After Time, had me rolling around the old green sofa in mirth. There is nothing 'alternative' or even very original here. Car thief Kenny has come out of jail determined to go straight, but his criminal family and friends either refuse to believe him or, like his mother, feel deeply ashamed at this rejection of ancient family traditions (8.30). Geldof Goes Goondiwindi is a documentary record of the final concert played by Bob Geldof on his Australian tour last summer (11.05 C4).
