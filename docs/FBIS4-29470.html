BFN [Text] Speaking at a lecture in Tokyo on the afternoon of 16 June, Social Democratic Party of Japan [SDPJ] General Secretary Wataru Kubo touched on a coalition blueprint the SDPJ plans to submit as early as next week. He said: "Even if we place our policies highly, this does not always meet the people's expectations. Our policies should be broad ones that can win the support of the various coalition parties." In this way, he indicated his view that in hammering out the party's alliance proposal, the party will give priority to basic agreements reached with the ruling parties.
