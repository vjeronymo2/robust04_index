March 4, 1989, Saturday, Home Edition

CAT AND THE AYATOLLAH

Khomeini does not represent me as a Muslim; he is the leader of the Shia minority, often self-righteous and misguided in their interpretation of Islamic law.

His threat against Rushdie is wrong, and, I think, actually illegal, Islamically speaking.

But Rushdie knew exactly what he was doing in savagely attacking truths all Muslims hold dear. My objection to Charles Champlin's article ("The Rushdie Affair -- a View From the Sidelines," Feb. 23) is this: Muslims have rules of civilized conduct, in which blasphemy is punishable; to defend the West as having defined the rules of civilized conduct for the entire world is hypocritical if one means to say that the West is superior in such matters.

ABD AL-HAYY MOORE

Santa Barbara
