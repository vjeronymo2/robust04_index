920715 FT 15 JUL 92 / Parliament and Politics: Call for ban on tobacco ads LABOUR yesterday renewed its calls for the government to ban tobacco advertising in fierce Commons exchanges. Mr Robin Cook, shadow health secretary, questioned whether the government could be expected to 'take on the tobacco barons' when it was benefiting from their money in the form of corporate contributions to party coffers. Mr Brian Mawhinney, health minister, retorted by saying that tobacco consumption had fallen in recent years. Measured against this decline, tobacco companies had not achieved their aims, he said.
