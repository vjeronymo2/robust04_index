BFN [Text] Mr. Haydar Abu-Bakr al-'Attas, the prime minister, arrived in Abu-Dhabi today from Cairo after a two-day visit to Egypt. Mr. al-'Attas had met in Cairo with the Egyptian president, Muhammad Husni Mubarak, and held talks with the Egyptian officials on the current situation in Yemen. In a press conference, the prime minister said that while democratic Yemen announced its preparedness to receive the fact-finding commission and to cooperate with it and facilitate its mission in order to achieve peace in Yemen and resort to dialogue, the Sanaa regime refused to comply with the Security Council [resolution] and the advice of the Arab leaders, among them President Muhammad Husni Mubarak of Egypt, Shaykh Zayid Bin Sultan Al Nuhayyan of the United Arab Emirates, King Fahd Bin-'Abd-al-'Aziz, king of Saudi Arabia, and the Syrian president, Hafiz al-Asad. He added that the Sanaa regime had (?accepted) conditional agreement on cease-fire and then broke it up on weak excuses. This proves that there is open contradiction in the decision making circle there. He affirmed that the situation in the last 48 hours had witnessed a dangerous escalation. The Sanaa regime used in its attacks all destructive weapons, including rockets and long-range heavy artillery. He indicated that the residential quarters and utilities had been the targets of the rockets and warplane missiles. Al-'Attas described the shelling of Aden refinery as a sign of the times. He said that this operation had shocked the Arab and world public opinion.
