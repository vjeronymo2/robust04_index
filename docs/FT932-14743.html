930416 FT 16 APR 93 / Armenians escape blame in Caucasus A UN report on the conflict in the Caucasus falls short of alleging Armenian government involvement in the latest fighting in Azerbaijan. Western diplomats say it is unlikely to lead to a binding Security Council resolution imposing sanctions on Armenia, reports John Murray Brown in Ankara The UN findings will come as a disappointment to Turkey, which has lobbied for UN action to stop the fighting as Azerbaijan claimed Armenian forces had seized 10 per cent of its territory. The Armenian government has denied that its forces are involved.
