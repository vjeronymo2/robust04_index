920612 FT 12 JUN 92 / International Company News: Central Capital bidders to meet regulator terms THE THREE companies bidding for Central Guaranty Trust, the core company in the troubled Central Capital financial services group have now agreed to to buy all of it, writes Robert Gibbens. National Bank of Canada and Canada Trustco, a subsidiary of Imasco, the finance conglomerate, had proposed buying two-thirds of Guaranty's business, leaving the remaining one-third unsold. These two companies, to satisfy federal regulators, now say they will be more flexible on the Guaranty assets they seek. Together with Montreal Trust, a subsidiary of BCE, they will make a new offer.
