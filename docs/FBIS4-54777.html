BFN [Text] Budapest, 4 May (MTI) -- According to the experience of the Hungarian Socialist Party (MSZP), most Hungarians believe that the current government proved its incompetence over the past four years, and rewarded loyalty rather than expertise and skill, MSZP Chairman Gyula Horn told a rally in Tatabanya, western Hungary, today. Furthermore, due to their decreasing living standards, Hungarians would like May to be a month of change, Horn said. Referring to recent harsh attacks on Horn, a journalist asked the MSZP Chairman whether he was considering giving up his political career. Horn replied: "The support and encouragement I receive everywhere in the country lends me strength. The idea of giving up my political career because of a series of slanders has never occurred to me at all. All the more because I know that this is what they would like to achieve through these provocations. I do not cling on to any office, but over the past four years my primary goal was to bring the new, democratic, European left-wing to victory in Hungary," Horn said.
