BFN [Excerpt] Brasilia, 5 May (EFE) -- A Brazilian Foreign Ministry spokesperson told EFE today that Uruguayan Foreign Minister Sergio Abreu today guaranteed his Brazilian colleague, Celso Amorim, that Uruguay will not withdraw from the negotiations to establish the Common Market of the South (Mercosur) import tariffs. Abreu got ahold of Amorim in Paris, where he is on an official visit, and during a telephone conversation denied a report published in Montevideo claiming that Uruguay had decided to withdraw from the negotiations due to disagreements over the Brazilian initiative to create a free trade zone in South America. The Uruguayan delegation today continued its participation in Mercosur's Common Market Group, the main technical level for the integration mechanism, which is meeting this week in the Argentine capital.
