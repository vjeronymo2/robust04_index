Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Seoul, March 7 (YONHAP) -- The government has appointed Air Force Gen. Kim Hong-nae, director of the Defense Intelligence Agency, as Air Force chief of Staff to succeed Cho Kun-hae, who died last Saturday in a helicopter crash. President Kim Yong-sam presented Air Force chief of Staff credentials to Kim Hong-nae, whose appointment was approved at a morning cabinet session. Kim, 55, was born in Koje, Southern Kyongsang Province, also President Kim's hometown. He graduated with the 10th class of the Air Force Academy and built up experience and knowledge as a member of jet fighter squads. He has served as vice commander of the Combat Air Command, deputy chief of Staff for personnel, deputy chief of Staff for intelligence and vice chief of Staff. Kim has a son and a daughter with his 49-year-old wife, Kim Kyu- yong.
