BFN [Excerpt] On 6 April, the Dominican newspaper EL DIARIO denounced the smuggling of oil products between the Dominican Republic and Haiti, which is being done extensively, according to the newspaper. A reporter of the daily confirmed that transactions between Haitians and Dominicans are made daily in homes, located not far from the border between the two countries, where the products are stored. The trafficking of fuel and other products affected by the international embargo is done for the profit of the Haitian military regime, according to the Dominican daily. The article points out that in Dajabon, a region located 350 km northeast of Santo Domingo, a gallon of gasoline is sold for 55 Dominican pesos, that is, $5; whereas the regular price is 20 pesos, or $1.50.
