Language: English Article Type:BFN [From the "Diplomatic Panorama" feature by diplomatic correspondents Dmitriy Voskoboynikov, Igor Porshnev, Vitaliy Trubetskoy and others] [Text] In the next few days Russia's Foreign Ministry will hold consultations with a delegation of NATO experts concerning procedural issues connected with the "Partnership for Peace" [PFP] the foreign ministry's spokesman, Mikhail Demurin, said at Tuesday's [8 February] briefing in Moscow. In answer to a question from Interfax's correspondent he emphasized that Russia's attitude to the "Partnership for Peace" idea is positive. As for the organizational side of this program, the NATO Council has approved a document on procedures connected with the "Partnership for Peace" program, and forwarded it to the embassies of countries involved in the North-Atlantic Council for Cooperation (NACC), and to Europe's neutral states, the diplomat said. According to Demurin, those who drafted the document took into account the wishes of Russia, which called for developing partnership on the basis of the NACC, and for enabling neutral countries to join it as equitable members.
