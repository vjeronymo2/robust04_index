Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Sofia, February 21 (BTA) -- A delegation of the Russian Parliament, led by Ivan Rybkin, chairman of the State Duma, met with Bulgarian National Assembly Chairman Aleksandur Yordanov this morning. The delegation arrived here on Saturday [19 February] on the first visit of Russian Duma members abroad. Mr. Yordanov and Mr. Rybkin discussed the development of relations between Bulgaria and Russia and their economic crisis. "We discussed the crisis in Bosnia and agreed that it can only be settled by peaceful means," Mr. Rybkin told reporters after the meeting. "All sanctions against former Yugoslavia which hinder our two countries' economies should be lifted," he said. "I familiarized Mr. Rybkin with Bulgaria's principled and invariable position of neutrality in the current complicated situation in ex- Yugoslavia. I also told him that for two years now Bulgaria has been calling on all countries in the Balkans to refrain from involvement in the Yugoconflict," the head of the Bulgarian Parliament said. "We assessed NATO's ultimatum as a means for faster settlement of the conflict, and the differences in our stands are minor," Mr. Yordanov added. Later in the day, the Russian MP's discussed bilateral relations and the Yugocrisis at successive meetings with M.P's of the Bulgarian Socialist Party (BSP), the Union of Democratic Forces (SDS), the ethnic Turks' Movement for Rights and Freedoms (DPS) and the New Union for Democracy (formed by expellees or breakways from the parliamentary SDS). BSP floor leader Nora Ananieva described the meeting as a beginning of contacts between the two parliaments in the light of a general tendency towards a left and left-of-centre orientation of East European countries, the Parliamentary Press Centre announced after the meeting. According to BSP leader Zhan Videnov, the Russian delegation's visit is part of the active policy which Russia has been pursuing in the Balkans. BSP believes Russia's decisive interference to prevent further escalation of the conflict was not accidental and could be viewed as a serious success, crowning the several months' efforts of Russian foreign policy, Mr. Videnov told reporters after the meeting. "The Yugoembargo should not stand in the way of the peace process in former Yugoslavia. If the peace process gets an impetus, in the next few days we will have to reconsider the positive and possibly negative effects of the embargo. This does not concern the arms embargo which by all means should be kept against all warring parties," Mr. Videnov said, commenting on Mr. Rybkin's opinion on lifting the embargo against former Yugoslavia. Mr. Videnov announced that a delegation of the Bulgarian Socialist Party will visit Moscow in a week's time to meet Russian left-wing parties. The parliamentary SDS set forth its position in favour of Bulgaria's independent foreign policy based on two priorities: Integration into the European structures and policy in the Balkans, stressing that the time of Balkan axes and blocs has gone, said Asparukh Panov MP of the SDS, chief Bulgarian delegate to the Council of Europe Parliamentary Assembly. "We expressed our satisfaction with the demilitarization of Sarajevo and congratulated our guests on Russia's active diplomatic interference in the last few days, which contributed to the success of NATO's ultimatum," Mr. Panov added. Asked if Bulgarian MP's sitting in the CE [Council of Europe] Parliamentary Assembly would support Russia's application for membership in the CE, Mr. Panov said that Russia is a major factor in the European processes but should meet some requirements to qualify for full membership in the CE, mainly concerning human and minority rights. Conferring with members of the Parliamentary DPS, Mr. Rybkin stressed that the members of the State Duma support President Yeltsin in his efforts towards peaceful settlement of the conflict in Bosnia, DPS spokesman Yunal Lyutfi said after the meeting. DPS MP's have been formally invited by Mr. Rybkin to visit the Russian Duma.
