Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tehran, March, 8, IRNA -- The Foreign Ministry Tuesday strongly denied a claim by Egyptian President Husni Mubarak that Iran was involved in an attempt on the life of Egyptian Premier, 'Atif Sidqi. Foreign Ministry Spokesman Morteza Sarmadi told IRNA that such baseless and false allegations indicated not only the "Cairo regime's helplessness and confusion but also the critical situation in that country." Sarmadi expressed "deep regret" that a person at the head of state level raised "such false claims based on fabrications" against the Islamic Republic of Iran. In an interview published in the latest issue of Kuwaiti daily, AL-QABAS, Mubarak accused Iran of paying US$13,000 to an Egyptian terrorist to assassinate the Egyptain prime minister.
