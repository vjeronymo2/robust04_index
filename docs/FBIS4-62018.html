BFN [Text] Bonn May 11 TASS -- German chancellor Helmut Kohl said Germany and the rest of the West are interested in a democratic, politically stable and economically strong Russia. "We, Germans, and, I think, the majority of people in Europe and the West are interested in a democratic, politically stable and economically strong Russia," he said. Speaking at a joint news conference after his talks with visiting President Boris Yeltsin on Wednesday, Kohl pledged to support by words and action Russian partners. He confirmed that Germany pins hopes on Yeltsin's economic reform and fully supports his policy. He stressed the need to let Russia in in international organisations, including the General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade. Kohl stressed that Germany will soon become president of the European Union [EU] and will call for the soonest possible signing of a cooperation accord between Russia and the EU. One of the key topics discussed at the talks which will continue on Thursday was European security, the German chancellor continued. "I explained to President Yeltsin the meaning and goals of the decision adopted at the NATO summit meeting in Brussels in January 1994. We are interested in Russia's participation in the Partnership for Peace," he said. Speaking about the Bosnian conflict, Kohl noted that Germany and Russia are working in the contact group with representatives of the United Nations, the EU, the United States, France and Britain in order to reach as soon as possible a comprehensive cease-fire in Bosnia and involve the warring sides in cooperation to establish a durable peace. Kohl and Yeltsin also discussed bilateral relations, including the withdrawal of Russian troops from Germany.
