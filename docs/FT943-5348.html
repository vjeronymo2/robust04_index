940905 FT 05 SEP 94 / Call for action on NHS dentistry The government must act now to prevent National Health Service dentistry disappearing completely, Mr David Blunkett, shadow health secretary, said yesterday. 'In many parts of the country new patients find it impossible to get an NHS dentist, and it will be a lot harder to find one in the future,' he said. His comments followed a survey that found three out of four dentists were prepared to leave the NHS. The survey - produced jointly by Denplan, which runs a private dental scheme, and the Observer newspaper - says 85 per cent of dentists were unlikely to take on any new adult NHS patients this year. Mr Tom Sackville, junior health minister, said the government's figures showed a growth in the number of registered dental patients.
