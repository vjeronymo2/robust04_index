920120 FT 20 JAN 92 / Construction Contracts: Pounds 40m workload for Willmott Dixon WILLMOTT DIXON companies have won contracts worth in excess of Pounds 40m. Willmott Dixon Symes has been awarded contracts valued at Pounds 11.7m. They are: a Pounds 2.4m contract at Hammersmith Hospital for a PACS x-ray unit; a Pounds 5.6m extension and refurbishment to 105 Piccadilly, London W1, a Grade II listed building, for Haslemere Estates; and a Pounds 2.7m library extension at the Institute of Education in Woburn Square, London WC1. Nine contracts have been awarded to Willmott Dixon Housing, including a Pounds 9m residential development for Victoria Park Housing Association at Parmiter Street, London E2. Other new contracts being carried out from the company's Bedfordshire headquarters are: a Pounds 2.2m contract for flats and houses for Notting Hill Housing Trust at Uxbridge Road, Southall and a Pounds 1.6m scheme for Network Housing Association in Willesden. Willmott Dixon Housing's workload includes a Pounds 2m contract to design and build houses and flats for Samuel Lewis Housing Trust in New Romney, Kent; a Pounds 732,000 block of flats for Threshold Housing Association at Shipka Road, Balham; and a Pounds 676,000 contract for Chichester Dicoesan Housing Association at Hove. Willmott Dixon Design & Build is currently working on three contracts valued at more than Pounds 4m. They include a distribution depot, valued at Pounds 1.8m, for Franthom Property Limberty at Rochester and a Pounds 1.5m swimming pool and sports complex at the Hurlingham Club in London.
