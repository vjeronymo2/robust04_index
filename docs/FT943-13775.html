940718 FT 18 JUL 94 / UK Company News: Difficulties of buy-outs at recs Regional electricity companies would make suitable candidates for leveraged buy-outs, though there would be political and regulatory risks, according to analysts at Hoare Govett, writes David Lascelles. In a report on the prospects for LBOs at the privatised electricity companies, the analysts say they enjoy monopoly profits on their distribution businesses and strong cash flow, which would support buy-outs. The expiry of the government's golden shares next March will also aid increased corporate activity by the recs. But they also question whether investment banks would be willing to arrange an LBO for a regulated utility which was heavily dependent upon regulatory decisions, and might become prone to political decisions as the next election approaches. In addition, the recs' shareholding in the National Grid Company would have to be unscrambled before any LBOs could take place. The analysts conclude: 'Despite their strong financial attractions, we question whether recs will become the subject of LBOs.'
