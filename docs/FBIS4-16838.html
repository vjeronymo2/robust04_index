BFN [By ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondent Vladimir Solntsev] [Text] Moscow, 19 Apr -- Japan positively views the recent Moscow initiative on convening an international conference on the questions of security in the Korean peninsula and its nonnuclear status, according to the formula "six plus two" and thinks it reflects Russia's serious interests in solving the North Korean nuclear problem, stated Japanese Ambassador Koji Watanabe in his exclusive interview with the ITAR-TASS correspondent. Having pointed out the similarity of positions taken by Tokyo and Moscow, the diplomat stressed Japan's intention to continue to exchange views on this problems in close contact with Moscow. The ambassador said that questions of easing the situation in the Korean Peninsula, including the problem of Korean unification, are constantly on the agenda in the Japanese-Russian political dialogue. Watanabe expressed the view that the most important thing at present will be to make sure that the DPRK allows the IAEA [International Atomic Energy Agency] inspectors to examine its most suspect nuclear installations in accordance with the recent appeal made by the UN Security Council chairman. The diplomat also pointed at Japan's intention to "resolutely demand" that North Korea continues to remain within the framework of the nuclear Nonproliferation Treaty and adhere to the North-South joint declaration on the nonnuclear status of the Korean peninsula.
