Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Colonel Ibrahim Muhanna, commander of the Palestinian police force in the Gaza strip, has denied the affiliations of applicants to the force make a difference in their chances of appointment. He told the BBC that candidates for appointment to the police force are selected in accordance with Palestinian law and draft regulations. He indicated that out of approximately 11,000 candidates who have applied to join the Palestinian police force, 3,000 have so far passed the medical test. I have no right to ask any applicant about his affiliation, he stressed. Muhanna indicated that the spread of arms carries grave risks for the Palestinian people. When Palestinian police are deployed in the occupied territories, they will operate under a Palestinian penal code, and law breakers will be prosecuted. Muhanna noted that, in certain respects, that there is cooperation between Palestinian police and Israeli authorities.
