Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Riga, Mar 17, BNS -- The British Government issued a press statement Wednesday [16 March] in which it speaks highly of the Latvian-Russian interstate agreements, which were initialed March 14 in Moscow. Under the agreements, Russia makes a commitment to get its remaining 12,000 troops out of Latvia by Aug. 31 while Latvia agrees that Russia operates the former Soviet military radar at Skrunda for four years and have 18 months to dismantle it. The British Government hopes that similar agreements will soon be concluded between Estonia and Russia, according to the statement.
