920918 FT 18 SEP 92 / Ukraine struggles to reform Ukraine's ex-communist government is clinging to the inefficient system of central planning but Russia is unwilling to play by the old rules. The cost is hyper-inflation, writes Chrystia Freeland in Kiev. Ukrainian industrial bosses are churning out unwanted goods and buying raw materials, in particular Russian oil, they cannot afford. With inflation running at close to 30 per cent a month and the Russian central bank charging 80 per cent interest annually, the Ukrainian national bank would now like to raise its interest rates to at least 50 per cent.
