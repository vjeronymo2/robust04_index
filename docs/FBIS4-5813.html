BFN [Text] Tokyo, April 14 KYODO -- Japan's crude steel production fell below 100 million tons in fiscal 1993, ended March 31, for the second year in a row, the Japan Iron and Steel Federation said Thursday [14 April]. Japanese steelmakers turned out a total of 97.11 million tons of crude steel, a 1.9 percent fall from the previous year and the smallest volume since fiscal 1986, it said in a report reflecting the weak national economy. In March alone, crude steel production fell for the sixth straight month of year-to-year declines, sliding 9.8 percent from a year earlier to 7.75 million tons, it said. The March production volume represents an increase of 7.1 percent from the month before, although March was the fifth consecutive month in which crude steel output fell below 8 million tons. Crude steel production is viewed as a key economic indicator, as steel products are used for a variety of industrial goods ranging from autos to machinery.
