BFN [Text] Medellin has suffered several bomb attacks in the past few hours. According to authorities, the bombs were of medium power. Eleven 7-kg bombs were detonated against banking and financial institutions, while two others were defused by the police. The attacks started at 2300 local and the explosions occurred simultaneously in central Medellin and the northeastern, northwestern, and southern areas of the city. [Begin recording] [General Luis Ernesto Gilibert, head of the Metropolitan Police] Since last night, we have been preparing for new terrorist attacks. [Reporter] Who are the perpetrators of these attacks? [Gen Gilibert] Subversives, who attempt to hamper the electoral process. [end recording] The explosions, which caused damage amounting to more than 100 million pesos, injured a young girl. Police have posted a reward of 10 million pesos for information on the identities of those responsible for the terrorist actions. The police believe members of an ELN [National Liberation Army] city cell are responsible for these incidents. The ELN is also blamed for an attack on a police patrol on Tuesday. Personnel of security agencies are on a first- level alert to guarantee public order in the Antioquia Department capital city. [Paris AFP at 1447 GMT on 17 June transmits a 229-word report stating that 17 bombs were set off Thursday night in Medellin, each with an estimated 5-kg weight]
