931229 FT 29 DEC 93 / International Company News: Olympic Air to seek Dr17bn loan from state Greece's state-owned Olympic Airways is to ask for a Dr17bn (Dollars 69m) loan from state commercial banks in the first three months of 1994, Mr Theodoros Tsakiridis, the company's managing director told reporters, Reuter reports from Athens. He said the loan would cover cash needs and other current operational expenses and be guaranteed by the government. Olympic Airways had losses of Dr22bn in 1993 and cumulative losses of about Dr400bn over the last five years. Meanwhile, the country's new socialist government is to re-apply to the European Union to write off Dollars 1bn of state-owned Olympic Airways debt, says transport minister, Mr Ioannis Charalambous. Informal discussions had begun already. Deutsche Bundespost Telekom is to acquire a stake in Societe Europeenne des Satellites (SES), a Luxembourg company which operates the Astra satellite television system, Reuter reports from Bonn. SES said the German state telecommunications monopoly would acquire a 'representative stake' as part of a planned SES capital increase, but gave no details. SES and Telekom will also form a joint entreprise to develop digital transmission technology for satellite and cable services in television and radio. Rothmans International said it would redefine its pan-Asian merger plans if a major shareholder of its Malaysian unit persists with objections to the original proposal, AP-DJ reports from Hong Kong. Shareholders in the unit, Rothmans of Pall Mall (Malaysia), will vote Friday on the plan, which has won unanimous backing from the other publicly-traded unit in the deal, Rothmans Industries of Singapore.
