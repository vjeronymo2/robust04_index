BFN [Text] Sofia, June 14 (BTA) -- Prime Minister Lyuben Berov is likely to nominate a replacement this week for Deputy Prime Minister and Trade Minister Valentin Karabashev, who has resigned, Socialist leader Zhan Videnov told a regular briefing today. It became clear that with this absolutely necessary change the cabinet will work until the elections. According to Videnov, the personnel and structural changes demanded from the prime minister by the Socialists in return for support for the cabinet would have made sense only if they had rallied the parliamentary majority to support the cabinet. Since this did not materialize and Bulgaria is heading for elections in the autumn, structural and personnel changes became rather pointless, Videnov said. Prime Minister Lyuben Berov yesterday submitted a proposal to parliament to relieve Karabashev of his post and appoint Kiril Tsochev in his place, it was announced later on. The Bulgarian Socialist Party (BSP) will not demand changes other than those already proposed, Videnov said after a meeting of the BSP parliamentary group this afternoon. According to him, the Socialists will not object to Tsochev's appointment as deputy prime minister as well. The BSP may back a motion by the Fatherland Party of Labour, its coalition partner, that parliament give up its summer recess in view of the possibility of elections in the autumn. According to Videnov, it is worth taking a shorter holiday so as to pass on second reading the Army and Armed Forces Bill or elections-related legislation. Videnov said the Army is in dire straits and it is unlikely that 2,000 million leva needed in the second half of the year will be allocated. Videnov has proposed to President Zhelev to convene the National Security Consultative Council for a discussion of this issue. The mayoral election in Veliko Turnovo (central Bulgaria) on Sunday [12 June] gives us grounds to believe that the trend to support the Union of Democratic Forces has begun to give way, Doncho Konakchiev, member of the BSP supreme leadership, said when asked to comment on the election results. The election was declared invalid because of a low turn-out. According to Konakchiev, the results show that a change in the reform model is needed. According to Videnov, the BSP reached 65 per cent of its electoral potential in Veliko Turnovo and its supporters were obviously the more active. Clearly, people are not interested in local elections yet and parliamentary elections are all that matters for the time being, Videnov said.
