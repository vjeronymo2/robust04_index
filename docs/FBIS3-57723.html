Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Moscow January 28 TASS -- The automatically controlled cargo spacecraft Progress M-21 was launched today at 05.12, Moscow time. The purpose of its launching is to deliver various cargoes to the Mir orbital complex. The Progress M-21 was put into orbit with the following parametres: -- maximum distance from the earth's surface -- 236 km, -- minimum distance from the earth's surface -- 194 km, -- period of revolution -- 88.5 minutes, -- inclination -- 51.6 degrees. According to the telemetric information, the on-board systems of the cargo spacecraft are functioning normally. The total weight of the cargo, together with fuel, is about two tonnes. Viktor Afanasiev, Yuriy Usachev and Valeriy Polyakov, who are working at the Mir orbital station, will receive the equipment they need, fuel, materials for scientific research, food, as well as parcels from their families. Aside from it, manual TV-controlled regime equipment will be tested at the Progress M-21. The crew of the 15th main expedition will receive a total of three cargo spacecraft.
