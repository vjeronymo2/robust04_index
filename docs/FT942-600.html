940628 FT 28 JUN 94 / World Commodities Prices: Tea The Tea Broker's Association reports, Strong and general demand. Bright East Africans and good mediums gained 3 to 8 pence while plainer mediums were fully firm. Central African teas proved a strong feature and often gained 5 to 10 pence. Ceylons attracted limited support with prices irregular and generally easier. Africans met good demand at firm to dearer rates. Ceylons came to a selective enquiry, mostly at lower levels where sold. Quotations best available 280p/kg nom, good 175p/kg, good medium 155p/kg, medium 125p/kg, low medium 92p/kg. The highest price realised this week was 335p for a Rwanda pd.
