BFN [Text] Tokyo, May 20 KYODO -- Japan's industrial production in March increased 4.6 percent from the preceding month, the Ministry of International Trade and Industry (MITI) said in a revised report Friday [20 May]. The seasonally adjusted production index for the mining and manufacturing sectors came to 92.7 against a base of 100 for 1990. The growth compares with a 3.3 percent rise in a preliminary report released in late February, MITI said. The shipment index, given as 95.3, increased 4.8 percent from February, revised upward from a 3.9 percent rise in the preliminary report. The inventory index, at 105.4, fell 1.1 percent against a preliminary 0.8 percent drop. The index of the inventory-to-sales ratio, at 112.7, declined 5.3 percent compared with a 4.5 percent fall in the earlier report. With the latest statistics, Japan's industrial output has scored ups and downs alternately in the past eight months. Before adjustments, March industrial production decreased a revised 2.1 percent from a year earlier, extending a record string of year-to-year declines to 30 months. Unadjusted shipments fell a revised 2.6 percent from a year before and unadjusted inventories a revised 3.4 percent.
