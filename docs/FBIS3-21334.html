Language: Persian Article Type:CSO [Text] The value of nonoil exports passing abroad through Yazd Customs reached $903,000, an increase of $42,000 over the same period last year. Mohammad Sadeq-Falah, chief of Yazd Customs, said on Monday: During this period 835 tons of goods were exported to England, Sweden, Germany, Russia, South Korea, Pakistan, Turkey and the nations of the Persian Gulf. He added: Exports from this customs port decreased 32 percent by weight compared to the same period last year. He said the reason for the decline in weight of the export goods is the export of 2,000 tons of decorative stone last year, and added: This is due to the increase in weight of the export goods in the year 1371 [21 March 1992-20 March 1993].
