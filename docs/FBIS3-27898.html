Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Seoul, Feb. 1 (YONHAP) -- The South Korean Ambassador to China said on Tuesday that relations between North Korea and China, which had deteriorated shortly after the establishment of diplomatic ties between South Korea and China, seem to have recovered. Hwang Pyong-tae, here to attend the annual conference of Korean ambassadors and envoys, said at a bull session with journalists that the recent visit to Beijing by Hwang Chang-yop, secretary of the North Korean Workers' Party, had helped to improve the two nations' relations. North Korea had criticized China for being "revisionist" and even "a traitor" since Seoul formed diplomatic ties with Beijing in August 1992. "North Korea recently sent many people to the Chinese cities of Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou and Shenzhen to make them learn directly the Chinese way of reform and opening. North Korea seems to be changing now," he said. Meanwhile, China's position on the North Korean nuclear issue is that if South Korea and the United States keep talking with North Korea, the problem will be solved, he said. "Especially, China thinks that North Korea will push ahead with policies of reform and opening once its nuclear problem is solved." Acknowledging the seriousness of the nuclear problem, Beijing is trying to persuade Pyongyang to settle the matter before Feb. 21, when the Board of Governors' meeting of the International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA) is scheduled, he added. On reports of a possible visit by Kim Il-song to Beijing, he said, "North Korean President Kim Il-song, who had visited China as many as 39 times, has not visited Beijing since 1992. I think it's about time for him to visit there, as relations between Pyongyang and Beijing have recovered." Meanwhile, he expected that before President Kim Yong-sam's visit to China in late March, Seoul and Beijing will be able to conclude aviation, cultural cooperation, and double taxation avoidance agreements.
