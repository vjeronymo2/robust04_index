Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of international reaction to the 23 March assassination in Tijuana, Baja California, of Luis Donaldo Colosio Murrieta, presidential candidate for the Mexican Institutional Revolutionary Party, PRI. Mexico City Radio ACIR Network in Spanish at 1600 GMT on 24 March broadcasted a one-minute report on a fax received from Nobel Prize winner Rigoberta Menchu expressing her consternation on and rejection of the attack on Colosio. Mexico City Radio ACIR Network in Spanish at 1600 on 24 March also broadcasted a one-minute report on U.N. Secretary General Butrus Butrus-Ghali repudiating the attack on Colosio. Butrus-Ghali adds that he deplores any form of violence. Panama City ACAN at 1904 GMT on 24 March carried a 150-word, Panama City-datelined report on the Christian Democratic Organization of America's, ODCA, rejecting Colosio's murder. The ODCA, which is holding a meeting in Panama, expressed in a communique their hope that "this regrettable incident will not adversely affect the electoral process and the modernization of the Mexican political system." Managua Radio Nicaragua in Spanish at 1200 GMT on 24 March broadcasted a two-minute report on a press communique issued by Violeta Chamorro's government rejecting the "brutal" murder of Luis Donaldo Colosio. The communique goes on to rule out any violent method that brings mourning to the Mexican people and to the whole world. Madrid EFE in Spanish at 1016 GMT on 24 March carried a 200-word, Havana-datelined item reporting Cuban President Fidel Castro's "consternation, pain, and repudiation" upon learning the death of PRI candidate Colosio. The item reports that Castro sent a message of condolence to Mexican President Carlos Salinas de Gortari. Hamburg DPA at 2121 GMT on 24 March carried a 650-word, Buenos Aires/Mexico City-datelined roundup of unanimous regional consternation over Colosio's murder. According the DPA roundup, Brazilian President Itamar Franco rejected the "absurd and shocking act of violence" that led to Colosio's death. Argentine President Carlos Menem said he "was profoundly moved by the tragic death" of the PRI candidate, and he expressed hope that "acts of vandalism and terrorism will soon be overcome in our community of nations." Venezuelan President Rafael Caldera sent a message to his Mexican counterpart expressing his "total shock" upon learning of Colosio's murder, noting that Mexico is going through "sad and difficult times." The DPA roundup noted Chilean President Eduardo Frei's "condemnation and rejection" of the murder. Frei sent his expressions of "regret and solidarity" to his Mexican counterpart, Salinas de Gortari. Costa Rican President Rafael Caldera's warned that this murder is an "act against democracy" in Mexico and in Latin America. The Honduran Government issued a communique rejecting violence as a method to solving political problems. Panamanian President Guillermo Endara was "pained" to learn of Colosio's death. At 2155 GMT, Panama City ACAN carried a 150-word, San Jose-datelined report that Oscar Arias, Nobel Peace Prize and former Panamanian president, said today that Colosio's death "is a cause of concern for the Americas. It is important now that calm, level-headedness, and national unity prevail, that the important Mexico that we know should not throw itself into despair and disorder," Arias added. Mexico City Radio ACIR Network in Spanish at 2135 GMT on 24 March carried a two-minute report noting that Brian Mulroney, former Canadian prime minister, labeled the incident in Baja California as "a cowardly political assassination against Mexico's democracy, perpetrated for reasons that are truly trite and irrational." Madrid EFE in Spanish at 2124 GMT on 24 March carried a 1,200-word, Madrid-datelined roundup of reaction to PRI candidate Colosio's murder. Guatemalan President Ramiro de Leon Carpio said that with his death, Colosio's "political message takes on a greater meaning and makes it more legitimate and pure as an example for all democratic sectors." Dominican Republican President Joaquin Balaguer expressed in a letter to President Salinas his "solidarity and sincere expressions of regret and sorrow." EFE notes that an Uruguay Foreign Ministry communique said Colosio's murder is "one more expression of political intolerance and barbarity that adversely affects not only the right to life but also the basic principles on which democracy supports its existence and stability." The Bolivian president sent a message that, according to Foreign Minister Antonio Aranibar, "expresses his sorrow to the Colosio family, the Mexican Government and people." Guatemalan national Alfredo Skinner-Klee, acting president of the Central American Parliament, said: "These acts of violence only contribute to the deterioration of the democratic process and the opening of new wounds of resentment." Enrique Iglesias, Inter-American Development Bank president, commented that Colosio's murder "hurts the Mexican nation and the democratic consciousness of all Latin America." Manuel Fraga, chief of the Galicia Regional Government in Spain, expressed his "profound condolence for and firm rejection" of such a "vile assassination" in a telegram to Mexican President Salinas. Mexico City NOTIMEX in Spanish at 2153 GMT on 24 March carried a 500-word, Paris-datelined item which reports on a message sent by Philippe Seguin, acting president of the 91st Parliamentary Conference being held in Paris. Seguin said: "In my name and that of my colleagues we express our sympathy for the Mexican delegation and once again we underline our absolute rejection of electoral violence." The NOTIMEX item also noted that in Madrid, Spanish Foreign Minister Javier Solana labeled Colosio's murder as "terrible." At a news conference, Felipe Gonzalez, president of the Spanish Government, expressed his pain for the crime and reiterated his solidarity with the Mexican Government. The ruling Spanish Labor Party, in a communique labeled Colosio's murder as "a criminal act, the sole goal of which is to absurdly hamper the Mexican electoral process." Santa Fe de Bogota Inravision Television Cadena 1 in Spanish at 0000 GMT on 25 March carried a 1-minute report which noted that Colombian presidential candidate Ernesto Samper sent a letter to Mexican President Salinas de Gortari recalling his meeting with Colosio four months ago. The same report noted reaction by Andres Pastrana, presidential candidate for the Colombian New Democratic Force, and by presidential hopeful Miguel Maza Marquez. Maza is quoted as saying: "Yesterday it was the incident in Chiapas, now this assassination; the result of intolerance." Pastrana is quoted as saying: "It is very regrettable to see how our democracy is being murdered, that is, Latin American democracy." Panama City Circuito RPC Television in Spanish at 2300 GMT on 24 March carried a one-minute report on Ernesto Perez Balladares, presidential candidate for the Panamanian Democratic Revolutionary Party, who told RPC that he hopes that incidents of this nature will not be repeated any other place. The same report adds that Arturo Vallarino, president of the Panamanian Legislative Assembly, repudiated Colosio's murder noting: "A shot has been fired not only at a candidate but at democracy."
