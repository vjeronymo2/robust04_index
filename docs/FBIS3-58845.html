Language: German Article Type:BFN [Text] Berlin (DDP/ADN) -- According to information from the BERLINER MORGENPOST, the units of the Western Group of Russian Armed Forces still stationed in Germany are missing over 80,000 tonnes of ammunition. Major General Hartmut Foertsch, the federal government's authorized representative for the withdrawal of the Western Group, told the paper (Sunday [23 January] edition) that on 1 January 1991, a total 677,000 tonnes of ammunition had been stored in Germany. Of this amount, 580,390 tonnes had been dispatched to Russia between 1991 and 1993. The Western Group had reported stocks of only 15,560 tonnes of ammunition on 1 January 1994. There was uncertainty about the whereabouts of the missing 81,050 tonnes. The general criticized the fact that the Russian side is "at present refusing to enter into discussion" with the German liaison command he heads.
