930831 FT 31 AUG 93 / Montedison shareholders approve suit against former executives SHAREHOLDERS in Montedison, the industrial subsidiary of Italy's troubled Ferruzzi group, yesterday gave its new management clearance to press for damages from five former executives and the heirs of Mr Raul Gardini, who killed himself last month, writes Haig Simonian. The new management, which was imposed by the group's bank creditors in June, has already won approval by a court to freeze assets of up to L500bn (Dollars 313m) each belonging to the five former managers and to Mr Gardini's heirs.
