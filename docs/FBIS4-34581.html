BFN [Text] Buenos Aires, 19 Jun (NA) -- Peruvian citizen Jorge Diaz Reategui, who was detained a week ago in the city of Tucuman on charges of being a member of the Shining Path [SL] guerrilla group, was released after it was confirmed that he is no longer a SL member. Diaz Reategui was released on 17 June after an Interpol report confirmed that Diaz Reategui is a SL deserter who received special OAS political asylum. The Peruvian police have confirmed that no arrest warrant has been issued on Diaz Reategui. It was also confirmed that his Argentine residence permit is authentic. Diaz Reategui was detained at a hotel in the poor district of "El Bajo," near the bus terminal of the city of San Miguel de Tucuman. At the time of Diaz Reategui's detention, it was reported that he was participating in a meeting with other alleged guerrillas who escaped. Interior Minister Carlos Ruckauf accused Diaz Reategui of participating in protests in some northern provinces. Ruckauf noted that those who were meeting with Diaz Reategui "had been spotted in other parts of our country, participating in public disorders."
