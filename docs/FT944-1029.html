941222 FT 22 DEC 94 / UK Company News: Tate & Lyle plans Staley expansion Tate & Lyle, the sugars and sweeteners group, yesterday announced a Dollars 78m (Pounds 50m) expansion project at AE Staley, its maize wet milling subsidiary in the US. As part of the project, due to be completed by June next year, Staley's Lafayette South plant will be expanded at a cost of Dollars 45m (Pounds 28.9m) to produce additional high fructose corn syrup. This is the first phase of an expansion which will eventually more than double capacity at the plant. Meanwhile, the Lafayette North (Sagamore) plant will be expanded at a cost of Dollars 33m (Pounds 21.1m), doubling the modified starch capacity. At the same time Staley will complete the Dollars 20m (Pounds 12.8m) modernisation of its starch processing facility at Decatur, Illinois. The expenditure forms part of Tate's planned Pounds 175m capital investment programme for the year to September 1995.
