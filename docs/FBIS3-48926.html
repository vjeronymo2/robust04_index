Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Zagreb, Dec 31 (AFP) -- A convoy carrying about 750 Bosnian Moslems and Croats civilians out of Sarajevo arrived late Thursday [30 December] at the Croatian port of Split, the official Croatian agency HINA said. The refugees, mainly women, children and elderly, left Sarajevo on Tuesday evening on board 16 buses. They will be housed temporarily in a sports centre in Split, before being transferred to refugee camps or leaving for new lives in another country. Many of the refugees had no documents with them, which created long hold-ups at the Croatian-Bosnian border, the Croat refugee office said quoted by HINA. Bad roads and a spate of technical hitches also delayed the convoy's progress. UNHCR spokesman Ray Wilkinson said earlier Thursday in Sarajevo: "It was not anticipated that it would take so long." Wilkinson said the UN High Commissioner for Refugees (UNHCR) was going to feed the evacuees before the last leg of the journey Thursday from Metkovic on the Croatian border to Split, 120 kilometres (70 miles) to the north. "These people are probably quite hungry, since they had only a few food supplies with them when they left Sarajevo on Tuesday," he said. A group of 150 Bosnian Serbs evacuated the same day, heading for Belgrade, arrived in Serbia on Wednesday.
