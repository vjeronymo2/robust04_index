BFN [Text] Vientiane, April 18 (KPL) -- Malaysian Prime Minister Datuk Sri Dr. Mahathir bin Mohamed on April 16 arrived here on a three-day official visit to the Lao PDR [Lao People's Democratic Republic] at the invitation of Lao Prime Minister Khamtai Siphandon. The visit was in response to the December 1992 visit of the Lao prime minister to Malaysia, where three agreements were signed between the two governments concerning investment promotion and protection, economic, scientific and technical cooperation and air transportation. During the visit, the Malaysian prime minister called on Lao President Nouhak Phoumsavan and met with Prime Minister Khamtai Siphandon. Talking with the Malaysian prime minister, President Nouhak Phoumsavan said this visit was very significant in the history of the Lao-Malaysian relations. He expressed hope that the bilateral relations would further develop fruitfully in many areas. In response, the Malaysian prime minister expressed pleasure over a warm hospitality the Lao Government and people gave to him and his party. He voiced support to the open-door policy of the Lao Government on economic relations and cooperation with foreign countries, and confirmed that he would do all he could to develop the friendly relations and cooperation between the two countries. Lao Prime Minister Khamtai Siphandon in his discussion with his Malaysian counterpart also said that the visit was historic. He considered it as implementing the three agreements signed between the two governments in 1992. On the same day afternoon, talks were held between the government delegations of the two countries headed by their respective prime ministers. At the talks, both sides discussed a wide range of issues concerning their bilateral cooperation, both public and private, in various fields. The two prime ministers then witnessed the signing of an agreement on banking settlement between the governors of the central banks of the two countries. In the evening, Prime Minister Khamtai Siphandon hosted a banquet in honour of his Malaysian counterpart. The Malaysian prime minister was officially welcomed at the airport by Lao Prime Minister Khamtai Siphandon together with Deputy Prime Minister Khamphoui Keoboualapha, Foreign Minister Somsavat Lengsavat and other members of the Lao Government officials. This three-day visit was the first ever made to the country by the Malaysian prime minister following the Lao prime minister's visit to Malaysia in December 1992. The Malaysian delegation was accompanied by several important figures in the business circle of Malaysia.
