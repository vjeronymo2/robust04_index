941215 FT 15 DEC 94 / Insurers want cap on motor liability Pressure is growing within the insurance industry for an end to unlimited liability motor policies, according to a leading Lloyd's of London motor underwriter. Mr Peter Routledge, chairman of a working party on public relations set up by the Lloyd's Motor Underwriters' Association, said unlimited liability was increasingly seen as nonsensical because no insurance or reinsurance company has unlimited assets. Any move towards capping exposure from motor liabilities would require a change in UK road traffic acts.
