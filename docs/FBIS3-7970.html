Language: Macedonian Article Type:BFN [Text] The Ministry of Urban Planning, Civil Engineering, Transport, and Ecology today received a letter from the relevant bodies of the Federal Republic of Yugoslavia [FRY] saying that, as of 2 March, airplanes belonging to the majority of Macedonian airlines will be banned from overflying Yugoslav territory. This refers to Palair-Macedonia [Macedonian flag-carrier], Vardar-Air, Macedonia-As, Avio-Impex, Meta, and Falcon. The reason for the ban, as the letter says, is the huge outstanding debt for the servicing of these airlines' overflights. We have learned from our ministry that contact with Yugoslav bodies was established; they were called on to postpone the implementation of the decision by at least one month in order to enable the companies to repay their debts. The letter also says that the same measure was applied to airlines from Bulgaria and Turkey that have incurred arrears in payments.
