May 27, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

'GURU' EDUCATION

I read with interest "The Gurus" by Russ Wiles (May 6) concerning the role consultants play in assisting residential developers in their most critical decisions on where, when and what to build.

Wiles correctly noted "there are no qualifying exams or required educational prerequisites to enter the consulting field."

This is true, but your readers might like to know that the American Society of Real Estate Counselors, founded 37 years ago and a professional affiliate of the National Assn. of Realtors, is dedicated to enhancing the quality of advice available to the public on property matters.

Membership in the society and the CRE (Counselor of Real Estate) designation is awarded only after an extensive evaluation of a real estate counselor by his peers. It recognizes than an individual has demonstrated judgment, integrity and experience in real estate counseling matters and client service. Obtaining the services of a CRE assures the user of securing unbiased, non-contingent-based advice.

Your readers may contact the society at 430 N. Michigan Ave., Chicago, Ill. 60611, or calling (312) 329-8427.

EUGENE P. CARVER

Los Angeles
