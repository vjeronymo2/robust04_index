941210 FT 10 DEC 94 / UK Company News: King & Shaxson A first time contribution of Pounds 877,0000 from Greig Middleton, the stockbroking offshoot acquired earlier this year, helped King & Shaxson Holdings to lift pre-tax profits by 64 per cent from Pounds 762,000 to Pounds 1.25m for the six months to October 31. Revenue jumped from Pounds 2.46m to Pounds 12.5m. Moneybroking turned in an increased contribution of Pounds 568,000 (Pounds 308,000), but discount house profits fell from Pounds 743,000 to Pounds 425,000 and futures management incurred a slightly higher loss of Pounds 92,000 (Pounds 89,000). Earnings per share rose to 4.1p (2.7p) or to 3.3p fully diluted and an unchanged interim dividend of 4p has been declared. Smith St Aubyn (Holdings), a wholy-owned subsidiary of King & Shaxson, made a lower interim pre-tax profit of Pounds 378,000 (Pounds 654,000).
