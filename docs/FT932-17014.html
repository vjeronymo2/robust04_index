930402 FT 02 APR 93 / UK Company News: UK problems hit African Lakes African Lakes, the mining, agriculture and motor trading group, said that substantial losses in the UK offset another 'excellent' year in Malawi despite a further devaluation. As a result overall pre-tax profits for the 12 months to September 30 fell from Pounds 1.55m to Pounds 736,000. Losses per share were 1.6p (earnings 6.48p), after minorities of Pounds 552,000 (Pounds 632,000). The dividend, however, is maintained at 2p. Directors said there were substantial losses in the UK motor operations. Another significant deficit was incurred by Wave Computer Supplies principally because of rationalisation, costs of setting up a new Irish subsidiary and certain other items relating to the pre-acquisition period. Sale of surplus properties in Malawi brought in an extraordinary Pounds 369,000 (Pounds 188,000). Turnover rose to Pounds 50.5m (Pounds 45.2m).
