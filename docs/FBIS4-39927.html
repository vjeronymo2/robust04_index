BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Nikolay Kondratenko] [Text] St. Petersburg June 10 TASS -- Finnish Prime Minister Esko Aho arrived in St. Petersburg on Friday to attend the ceremony of the opening of the third plenary session of the Commission for International Assistance to St. Petersburg and the Leningrad region to attract investments and develop business. Among members of the commission headed by Doctor Henry Kissinger and St. Petersburg Mayor Anatoliy Sobchak are representatives of state, governmental, business and acamedic circles of Great Britain, Russia, the United States, France, Finland and Sweden. Participants in the plenary session will discuss new projects aimed at developing banks, agrarian business and the reconstruction of the St. Petersburg maritime port. At the ceremony at the hotel "Astoria" the Finnish prime minister will address participants in the session and hold talks with chairman of the St. Petersburg government, Mayor Anatoliy Sobchak. Late in the evening he will leave for Finland.
