BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Galina Gridneva] [Text] Dushanbe June 24 TASS -- The Friday attack on a bus with children of Russian military serving in Tajikistan was "a planned terrorist act" and a "continuation of the well-planned and controlled terror against Russian troops deployed in Tajikistan," according to Vladimir Novikov, deputy commander of the Russian frontier troops in the Central Asian republic. Several Russian military have fallen victim to the terrorist campaign only in the first four days of June. The latest attack on the bus with some 30 kids and their parents did not harm any of the passengers, however a passer-by was killed and several others wounded. Unknown gunmen fired a grenade on the bus early on Friday. Novikov told reporters that Russian troops will mount protection of their personnel by seting up additional guarding posts and patrols in places where Russian servicemen live. He added that armoured vehicles will be used for protection. Novikov stressed that the leadership of the Russian frontier troops in Tajikistan is preparing a statement in connection with the attack on the bus. Russian border guards defend the Tajik-Afghan border to cut short the infiltration of armed Tajik opposition militants from Afghanistan.
