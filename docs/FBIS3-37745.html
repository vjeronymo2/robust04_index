Language: Azeri Article Type:BFN [Text] Azerbaijani Prime Minister Suret Guseynov received today the Georgian Prime Minister Otar Patsatsia and his deputy Zorab Kervalishvili. [Begin Sadiq Guzelov recording] Matters of mutual interest were discussed at the meeting. Otar Patsatsia requested a supply of fuel to Georgia. He also asked for support in organizing the visit of Georgian and Azerbaijani experts to Iran and the talks there in connection with the supply of the Iranian gas to Georgia. An agreement was reached on this issue. The problem of the diversion of a part of the fuel purchased from Azerbaijan to Armenia via Georgia was also seriously discussed. The Georgian prime minister said that various commercial organizations are behind this diversion. Suret Guseynov's proposal for Azerbaijani representatives to oversee this matter from inside Georgia was accepted. In briefing the guests about the grave situation existing in the republic as a result of the six-year-old aggression by Armenia, Azerbaijani Prime Minister Suret Guseynov said that despite all the difficulties Azerbaijan will not withhold its assistance to Georgia, with whom it has lived in friendship for centuries. Suret Guseynov also touched upon the problems of our more than half a million compatriots living in Georgia, and hoped that the Georgian Government will do everything to solve their problems. Deputy Prime Minister Abbas Abbasov and Economy Minister Samed Sadykhov also took part in the meeting.
