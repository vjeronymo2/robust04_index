Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, January 8 (XINHUA) -- Japanese Foreign Minister Tsutomu Hata left for Beijing today for a two-day visit to China for talks with Chinese leaders. Hata, who is also deputy prime minister, is the first minister in the cabinet of Prime Minister Morihiro Hosokawa to visit China. It will be the third meeting between Hata and his Chinese counterpart Qian Qichen. During his talks with Qian and Premier Li Peng later in the day, Hata will express Japan's continued support for China's ongoing economic reform, Japanese Foreign Ministry officials said. He will also meet with Chinese President Jiang Zemin on Sunday before returning to Tokyo later the same day. The Japanese foreign minister will reaffirm Tokyo's intention to help China recover membership in the General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade, the officials said.
