Language: Japanese Article Type:BFN [Text] At a news conference following a cabinet meeting on 1 March, International Trade and Industry Minister Hiroshi Kumagai commented on the state of the economy, saying: "There are signs that the economy has been improving since the beginning of the year." In this way, he indicated that the economy had gone through the worst period. In January, industrial production and shipping indexes increased and an inventory-shipment ratio declined. Referring to this, Kumagai noted that there were signs of recovery in consumer spending and other areas. He said: "Although the sales of high-priced products are still sluggish, they are gradually picking up." Concerning the imports of U.S. autos and auto parts discussed in the Japanese-U.S. framework trade talks, Kumagai said: "It is necessary for the Japanese industry to have a correct understanding of what the United States is thinking."
