Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, March 2 KYODO -- A jump in U.S. economic growth in the last three months of 1993 was mainly due to a steady recovery in U.S. competitiveness in the world market, a Bank of Japan [BOJ] source said late Tuesday [1 March]. The source made the remarks shortly after the U.S. Commerce Department announced that gross domestic product (GDP) grew 7.5 percent in annualized terms in the October-December quarter. "Expansion in exports contributed chiefly to the improvements," the source said. Touching on the yen-dollar rate, the BOJ source said, "Forex players will now shift their attention to economic conditions of both countries -- away from Japan's domestic political uncertainty -- with mounting expectations that a gap in their economic fundamentals will not shrink further for a while."
