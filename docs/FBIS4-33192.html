BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on developments related to the elections scheduled for 21 August. While visiting two towns in Chiapas State, Rafael Aguilar Talamantes, presidential candidate for the Cardenist Front for National Reconstruction Party, cited his party's support for farm occupations and takeovers. He said "we must discuss each case and hold talks to correct any abuses, even those committed on our side." (Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 0430 GMT 27 May 94) During his second day in Chiapas State, candidate Aguilar Talamantes told the residents of Guitiapan the "method of struggle Subcommander Marcos and the Zapatists use cannot work or effect good results; we know that." (Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 0430 GMT 28 May 94) PRD candidate Cardenas has visited Mesa de Alcoyotic, Mexico State, and commented on the announcement the PRD (Party of the Democratic Revolution) and PAN (National Action Party) had established a pact of nonaggression: "We do not sign pacts. We will behave respectably and adhere to PRD principles and respond how we must." He also expressed support for the idea of more debates with the other presidential candidates. (Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 0430 GMT 27 May 94) Commenting on an invitation from PRI (Institutional Revolutionary Party) candidate Zedillo to the other presidential candidates to hold a debate, PRD candidate Cardenas has asked the other candidates to agree on "this next debate, as well as on the others we may hold up to 21 August." He also proposed holding debates between two candidates. Cardenas has also said the news media, Televisa, Television Azteca, and Multivision in particular, should give all the candidates the opportunity to comment on the debates after they are over. (Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 0430 GMT 28 May 94) In Guerrero State, PAN candidate Fernandez has said he disagreed with Zedillo's proposal to hold a debate with all the presidential candidates. He did not rule out the idea, however, saying he supported "finding mechanisms and reasonable formulas to continue the debates." The debates, he said, should not be an "ambush to stop PAN's struggle in winning the Presidency." The PAN candidate also endorsed a final revision of the electoral lists with the participation of all the political parties. (Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 0430 GMT 27 May 94) At the conclusion of a meeting with businessmen from the southern area of the Federal District, candidate Fernandez announced his party and the PRD have established a "pact of nonaggression" in order to end mutual accusations. During his meeting with the businessmen, he pledged to amend the Federal Labor Law, without "hurting the gains of the workers," and to implement a "social market economy based on freedom and private initiative." (Mexico City EL FINANCIERO in Spanish 26 May 94 p12) Talking to tourism students at the San Angel Higher Studies Center, Mexican Green Ecologist Party presidential candidate Jorge Gonzalez described as "totally undemocratic" candidate Fernandez' rejection of a debate with all the presidential candidates. (Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 0430 GMT 28 May 94) At the Isthmus of Tehuantepec, Marcela Lombardo, presidential candidate of the Popular Socialist Party, has told reporters she accepts Zedillo's invitation to participate in a debate. (Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 0430 GMT 28 May 94) Pablo Emilio Madero, presidential candidate of the National Opposition Union (UNO), began a visit to Guanajuato State that will take him to 14 municipalities. He met with his supporters at Dolores Hidalgo Square where he said "peace and tranquility should not be the lack of armed violence only; there should be peace and tranquility because the votes are respected." (Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 0430 GMT 27 May 94) If elected, "I will make the tax burden more equitable," candidate Madero told his supporters at Juventino Rosas during his second day of campaigning in Guanajuato. (Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 0430 GMT 28 May 94) Speaking to party supporters, PRI national leader Ignacio Pichardo Pagaza has said the PRI trusts it will win in at least 40 districts of Mexico City. He said the Mexican revolution will be maintained until the turn of the century with "the majority vote of the Mexican people." He spoke after swearing in Fernando Lerdo de Tejada and Juan Carlos Sanchez Magallan as PRI president and secretary general, respectively, of the Federal District. (Mexico City UNOMASUNO in Spanish 1 Jun 94 p 9) During her first day of campaigning in Chiapas State, Labor Party (PT) presidential candidate Cecilia Soto Gonzalez agreed to hold a debate with the other eight presidential candidates and proposed it be held on 1 June. (Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 0430 GMT 27 May 94) While in San Cristobal de las Casas, Chiapas State, Soto said the peace process is not advancing, because "the state and federal governments lack a sufficient commitment to resolve the problem of landownership and land reform." (Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 0430 GMT 28 May 94) In Mexico State, Jorge Gonzalez Torres, Mexican Green Ecologist Party presidential candidate, has said Article 27 of the Constitution had to be amended back to the way it was originally drafted in order for the 21 August elections to be credible and honest. (Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 0430 GMT 27 May 94) Today, Alvaro Perez Trevino, presidential candidate of the Authentic Party of the Mexican Revolution, said Ernesto Zedillo is the country's best option. He reiterated what he said a month ago in the studios of Radio Acir. Trevino said if he does not win the elections, he will support Zedillo. He has said his party is not a parastate party or a satellite of the PRI. (Mexico City Radio Acir Network in Spanish 0000 GMT 1 Jun 94) During a ceremony at PRI headquarters, candidate Zedillo spoke in favor of maintaining a "healthy distance" between the government and PRI. Zedillo has said: "We want a nation of welfare, opportunities, and a firm hope," saying poverty in Mexico does not admit "indifference or evasion." Zedillo has said his efforts will be directed at increasing the people's security and property, eliminating impunity, and fighting narcotrafficking. Zedillo has recognized the merits of President Carlos, saying he has left the PRI government a legacy of patriotism and undaunted determination. (Mexico City EL FINANCIERO in Spanish 30 May 94 p 6) As instructed by Zedillo, the PRI has sent representatives to Japan, the United States, South America, Spain, and Portugal to brief the country representatives on the electoral process that will take place in Mexico in August, Jose Angel Gurria has announced. They will strive to guarantee the elections in Mexico will be clean so that foreign investors will not lose confidence in Mexico. (Monterrey EL NORTE (Foreign Edition) in Spanish 30 May 94 p 4A) During a meeting with reporters in Saltillo, Cahuila State, Zedillo has said that so far none of the presidential candidates has formally replied to his proposal to hold a debate. He said he hoped all the candidates would agree to participate. He described the disappointment among PRI members regarding the selection of candidates as normal, adding the PRI "has again shown its strength, unity, and will continue to strive to win democratically." (Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 0430 GMT 27 May 94) Zedillo has flown to Ciudad Juarez, Chihuahua State, and visited an assembly plant. He has promised to sustain sources of employment but, rather, "under decent conditions" for workers. During a meeting with local businessmen, he heard of their views on the bad economic situation, the crime wave, and drug trafficking. "We are going to reform our country's apparatus of justice," he said. Zedillo also met privately with PAN Governor Francisco Barrio. If elected, the PRI candidate affirmed, he will maintain close cooperation with local governments ruled by opposition parties. (Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 0430 GMT 27 May 94) Zedillo has expressed reservations about signing several points of the Agreement for Democracy. He asked for a four-week period to present suggestions to the commission sponsoring the agreement. Zedillo has said the idea behind the agreement is good but that the agenda must be broader on judicial matters and on the division of power. So far, only PAN candidate Diego Fernandez, PRD candidate Cuauhtemoc Cardenas, and PT candidate Cecilia Soto have signed the agreement. (Monterrey EL NORTE (Foreign Edition) in Spanish 31 May 94 p 1A)
