BFN [By Lilian Wu] [Text] Taipei, May 12 (CNA) -- Taiwan is seeking to boost trade with Southeast Asian nations by signing double taxation avoidance agreements with countries in the region, the Board of Foreign Trade (BOFT) said Thursday [12 May]. BOFT officials said the board has held consultations on how to avoid double taxation with Malaysia, Vietnam, Indonesia and Thailand. Although differences remain, the officials expressed hope that agreement could be signed soon. Meanwhile, Taiwan is also expected to ask the nations to sign more far-reaching taxation agreements in the future. The Finance Ministry has already evaluated the impact on Taiwan's tax revenue and trade relations if the expanded agreements are signed. The ministry has also mapped out a taxation agreement draft to help speed up consultations on the issue.
