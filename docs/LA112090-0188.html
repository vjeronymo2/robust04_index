November 20, 1990, Tuesday, P.M. Final

THE SIDELINES;

CADETS MISSED THE BOAT ON GOAT

Army cadets failed to get their goat from the Naval Academy.

Early Sunday morning, cadets from the U.S. Military Academy captured a goat they thought was Bill XXVI, the mascot of the U.S. Naval Academy.

But midshipmen can be great kidders, especially when it comes to anticipating a prank by rival Army cadets. The real Bill had been hidden by midshipmen just in case of a prank.

The Naval Academy said the kidnaped goat was one of several that had auditioned to succeed Bill XXV, who was retired in 1988.
