940321 FT 21 MAR 94 / Arts: Today's Television You cannot escape themes these days. This is British Science Week and Radio 3 is having a 1930s season. One programme manages to fit under both headings: Yesterday's Tomorrows looks back to a time when, it is claimed, 'there was no science fiction but there was scientific romance' from HG Wells to Aldous Huxley (9.30 R3). Before that Radio 3 offers an opportunity to hear Let 'Em Eat Cake, a musical by George and Ira Gershwin, first performed in 1933. This production stars Denis Quilley, Kim Criswell and Joss Ackland (7.30). There is more evidence of British Sience Week on BBC2, which is mounting four discussions on successive nights at Bristol's science museum, The Exploratory which is also the title of the series. Today's subject is why some people win Nobel Prizes while others are ostracised (11.15). Earlier Horizon looks back to the extraordinary hullabaloo which followed the claim five years ago that fusion had been achieved in water by Fleischmann and Pons in a test tube in an ordinary lab (8.00 BBC2).
