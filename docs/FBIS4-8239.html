BFN [Text] Tirana, April 9 (ATA) -- "The Albanian Army and people have a special respect for NATO as an alliance of peace and security," Lieutenant-General Ilia Vasho, chief of the Army General Staff, said in the meeting with NATO supreme commander and the commander-in-chief of the U.S. Forces in Europe, George Joulwan. Mr. Vasho described for his honoured guest the reforms carried out in the Albanian Army, focusing on the main tasks of the army to create new visions in training, improvement of living conditions for troops and officers, preparation of a new military doctrine, and upgrading the qualifications of the officers in contemporary military science. General Joulwan said he was impressed by the great progress that had been made in restructuring and modernizing the army. He extolled the character and high qualities of the Albanian fighting man which, General Joulwan said, were evident both in his meetings with the staff at the military bases and in his talks with Albanian officers. General Joulwan hailed the role of the alliance in promoting friendship among peoples. "The peoples appreciate freedom, friendship and sovereignty, these are the values that unite mankind and which our alliance manifests," General Joulwan said. During its stay in Tirana, the NATO delegation attended the inauguration of the Academy for Non-commissioned Officers.
