941119 FT 19 NOV 94 / How To Spend It: Trooping the colour the glamour-puss is back. Here comes the femme fatale, a dead ringer for Gianni Versace's Cyber Princess, teetering on her vertiginous heels, all dressed up in a languid, bias-cut slip of a dress and an elegant string of pearls. It is a look that only truly comes into its own at night, and it needs the make-up to match. A full, red mouth is the hallmark of this winter's face. lf you buy nothing else, buy a red lipstick. Reds, as every woman knows, come in many shades, and the rule of thumb is to take time and trouble to find the one that suits your mood: rich, vibrant and glossy for when you're feeling vampish; the barest of lip stains for when you want to show some restraint; or a deep vermilion for when you want to throw caution to the wind. For the faint-hearted, deep reds can be diluted with lip balm. For the bold and confident, a lip liner should be used first - to create a perfect lip shape and then the chosen red should be applied with a lip brush. There are shades of red for every skin tone. Go for a dramatic look in the evening. Draw a smoky line inside and outside the edge of the eye. For the really daring, coloured eyeshadow - even bright China blue, until recently a definite no-no - looks new and fresh. Every cosmetic house worth its lip gloss has come up with the foundations, the blushers and the colours that this winter's face requires - at a price. You could spend as little as pounds 29.70 at the least expensive house or as much as pounds 219 on the basic essentials for the new haute chic look.
