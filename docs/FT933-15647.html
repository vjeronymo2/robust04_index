930708 FT 08 JUL 93 / Indian banker fired over scandal India sacked the executive director of Syndicate Bank, one of the country's largest state-owned banks, yesterday for his role in a Dollars 1.28bn (Pounds 85m) securities scandal, the finance ministry announced, Reuter reports from New Delhi. Mr R S Pai, who was asked to go on leave last year pending investigations, has been sacked with immediate effect, a ministry statement said. Ministry officials later said an inquiry by the Reserve Bank of India, which submitted its findings in May, found that Mr Pai had caused a loss of Rs2bn (Dollars 64.5m) in portfolio investments.
