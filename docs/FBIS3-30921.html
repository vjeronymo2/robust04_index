Language: Polish Article Type:BFN [Text] A demonstration by Solidarity took place in Warsaw today, in protest against price increases, falling workers' incomes, and the increase of the number of unemployed. Changes in the budget for this year were demanded. In a speech made before the office of the Council of Ministers, Union Chairman Marian Krzaklewski demanded the adoption by 15 March this year of the laws comprising the pact on the state enterprise. He announced that there will be further demonstrations if this is not done. Over and above that, he said that Solidarity is preparing its own draft constitution, one that is representativeof the entire independence-oriented and Solidarity sector. The participants in the demonstration delivered petitions into the hands of Deputy Sejm Speaker Jozef Zych and Council of Ministers Office Head Michal Strak. In the petitions the trade unionists are demanding changes in the budget for this year that would allow for a decrease in the growing negative economic and social phenomena. The participants in the demonstration included Jan Olszewski, Jan Parys, Adam Glapinski, and also groups from the Movement for the Republic and the Third Republic Movement. According to the police controlling traffic, around 30,000 people took part in the demonstration, and according to Marian Kraklewski around 100,000.
