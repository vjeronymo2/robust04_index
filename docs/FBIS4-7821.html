BFN [Text] [Announcer] Top officials of the governments of the Croat republic of Herceg-Bosna led by Prime Minister Jadranko Prlic and of Bosnia-Herzegovina led by Deputy Prime Minister Edib Bukvic discussed the implementation of the Washington agreement and documents regarding the setting up of the federation of Bosnia-Herzegovina in Mostar. Zdenko Vranjes reporting from Mostar: [Vranjes] How to coordinate regulations of the government of Bosnia-Herzegovina and the government of the Croat republic of Herceg-Bosna which are still in force in the interim period until a final solution on the future of Bosnia-Herzegovina is reached and all federal bodies are established and regulations adopted. This and other tasks stemming from the documents on the setting up of the federation of Bosnia-Herzegovina were discussed by representatives of the two governments, or two administrations as they are defined in the constitution of the federation. A concrete result of their talks is the setting up of joint expert teams to deal with several aspects of the reconstruction project. A team of experts who are to coordinate financial and customs systems which are in force on the territories of the two governments has also been set up. They also spoke about the reconstruction of roads and railways, the Sarajevo-Ploce railway line in particular, telecommunications, and the communal infrastructure on the territory of Mostar. Representatives of the Bosnia-Herzegovina government and the government of the Croat republic of Herzeg-Bosna have also agreed joint tasks in the resolution of the problem of refugees and displaced persons as regards their return, in which, as they concluded, it will be necessary to obtain wider support from international organizations. They also spoke about media, particularly about the responsibility of the media on both sides for the strengthening of peace and the restoration of trust between the Croat and Bosniak nations. They have also agreed meetings between ministers of the two governments which are to take place soon.
