BFN [Text] Tianjin, June 21 (XINHUA) -- More and more passengers are flying between north China's port city of Tianjin and Seoul, capital of the Republic of Korea (ROK), as economic cooperation prospers. Last week, one more flight, a Boeing 737, was added to the present seven flights per week between the two cities. Sources at Tianjin airport said that the seat occupancy rate reached 80 percent. Tickets to Seoul have been booked to July. The air route is jointly flown by Air China and an air company of the ROK. More than 300 Sino-ROK joint ventures have been established in Tianjin.
