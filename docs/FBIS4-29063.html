BFN [Text] Tokyo, June 14 KYODO -- Prime Minister Tsutomu Hata told Japanese business leaders Tuesday [14 Jun] his government is willing to allow self-service gas stations in Japan on certain conditions, as part of deregulation, government officials said. Hata was responding to a proposal by Isao Nakauchi, vice chairman of the Federation of Economic Organizations (Keidanren), that self-service gas stations be introduced in Japan. During the meeting at Hata's official residence, Nakauchi also asked Hata for his leadership in implementing deregulation. Keidanren executives handed a petition to Hata, asking him to cut the number of economic regulations in half over the next five years, and map out a plan for scrapping and privatizing government-backed corporations as well as consolidating government departments.
