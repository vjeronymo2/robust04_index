Language: Hebrew Article Type:BFN [Report by Yisra'ela Shaqed] [Text] Some 169 new AIDS carriers were uncovered among 115,000 people tested in 1993, compared with 201 carriers uncovered among 80,000 people tested in 1992. This was announced by Dr. Tzvi Ben- Yishay, chairman of the Health Ministry's supreme committee on AIDS prevention. The findings point to a 15 percent drop in the number of new carriers in 1993 and to a rise in the number of female carriers, who now constitute 36 percent of the carriers, compared to 30 percent in 1992. To date, 278 Israelis have contracted the disease. Of these, 198 died and 15 left the country. The number of known carriers is 1,102, but the Health Ministry estimates their number at 2,500.
