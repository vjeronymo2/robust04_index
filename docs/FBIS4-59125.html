BFN [Text] Palestinian negotiator Jamil al-Tarifi says the Palestine National Authority has signed new temporary contracts with the Israeli water, electricity, and communications companies to continue their services in Gaza and Jericho until the PLO takes over. He says he expects the takeover to take place on 17 May. According to al-Tarifi, the Palestinian authority has already signed contracts with large foreign companies to supply the self-rule areas with the services they need. A Canadian company will supply Gaza with 100 megawatts of electricity from a ship off the Gaza coast, and after two months, the company will double its supply. Jericho will continue to receive electricity from Israel until an alternative becomes available. The Meqorot water company has also promised to continue supplying water until an alternative is found. [Jerusalem Qol Yisra'el in Hebrew at 0500 GMT on 7 May cites al-Tarifi as announcing that "a French company by the name of (Marbec) will supply the Strip with fuel and build a small oil refinery."]
