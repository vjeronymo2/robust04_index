940526 FT 26 MAY 94 / MP apologises over bill A chastened Lady Olga Maitland, Tory MP for Sutton and Cheam, yesterday apologised to the Commons for her role in blocking the backbench bill which sought to provide new rights for the disabled. Miss Betty Boothroyd, the speaker, said remarks in the Commons earlier this month by Lady Olga about the origin of amendments which she moved 'fell below the standards the House is entitled to expect from its members'. In a rare public admonition, Miss Boothroyd said: 'I strongly rebuke her for her conduct on that occasion.' When the civil rights disablement bill was blocked, Lady Olga had claimed amendments in her name were 'mine and mine only'. This was later contradicted by Mr Nicholas Scott, the minister responsible for the disabled. In apologising for misleading the House, Mr Scott admitted that the amendments had been prepared by parliamentary counsel in co-operation with officials of the Department of Social Security and made available to Conservative backbenchers who supported the government in opposing the bill. After the rebuke, Lady Olga told the speaker: 'In the light of your statement may I give an unreserved apology to you and this House.'
