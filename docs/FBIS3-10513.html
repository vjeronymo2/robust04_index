Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Cuba was elected a vice president to the preparatory meeting of Latin America and the Caribbean for the Ninth UN Congress on Crime Prevention and the Treatment of Criminals, which is being held in San Jose, Costa Rica. The proposal was made by the Bolivian delegation and was unanimously backed by the meeting's attending delegations. Argentina and Bolivia were elected as the other vice presidents of the meeting, and the host country was elected president. During the meeting's sessions, the delegations will analyze various topics in an effort to define the position that Latin America will take at the UN congress. Included among the topics are: international cooperation and technical assistance to strengthen application of the law in the prevention of crime, penal justice, prevention of delinquency, the crime commission [comision de delitos] in urban zones, and measures against economic crime. The Cuban delegation is composed of (Araceli Careaga), who is heading the delegation; (Eliseo Zamora), an official of the Foreign Ministry; and (Renin Quiros), a specialist in the Ministry of Justice.
