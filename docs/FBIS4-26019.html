BFN [Text] Pyongyang, June 16 (XINHUA) -- The President of the Democratic People's Republic of Korea (DPRK) Kim Il-song met here today with former United States President Jimmy Carter and his wife. Local press reported that they held talks in a cordial atmosphere, but did not disclose details. Carter's visit to the DPRK is aimed at facilitating exchanges of views on the nuclear issue which is currently taxing the United States, South Korea and the DPRK. Carter, who is on a private tour on the Korean peninsula, met with South Korean President Kim Yong-sam before arriving in the DPRK. He is scheduled to end his visit to the DPRK on Saturday [18 June] and travel to South Korea by way of the truce village of Panmunjon before returning to the U.S.
