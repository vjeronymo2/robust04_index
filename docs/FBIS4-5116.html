BFN [Text] The government has decided to form a Unification and Security Policy Coordination Council that will be in charge of summing up and adjusting policies on national unification and security to prevent confusion in policies on the North Korean nuclear issue. The Unification and Security Policy Coordination Council was organized in a meeting on 7 April of security-related ministers presided over by President Kim Yong-sam. The council will hold meetings once or more a week with the vice unification minister as the chairman and the foreign and defense ministers, and the senior presidential aide on foreign affairs and security will analyze and examine the prevailing security situation and coordinate various offices' opinion. Anyone of the council members can immediately call a meeting of the council. Proposing the organization of the Unification and Security Policy Coordination Council, President Kim Yong-sam asked security-related ministers to consult with one another more efficiently and to cope with situations promptly. Regarding the rumor on North Korea's possession of nuclear weapons that was recently reported by some media, today's meeting came to the conclusion that there is no clear evidence to prove that North Korea possesses nuclear weapons at this point of time. In addition, Defense Minister Yi Pyong-tae reported that there has been no particular moves by the military around the truce line or in other areas in North Korea.
