931207 FT 07 DEC 93 / Arts: Today's Television To the generation of journalists now in middle age, Robert Fisk is what James Cameron was to the previous generation: a man who seems to have been in every major war zone for a couple of decades, yet who writes about war without any of the gung-ho relish common among war reporters. How he has survived so long on so many fronts is a mystery, though last time we met he gave a hint. You could always tell the new boys when you came under heavy attack, he said, they dived for cover the moment they heard the explosions, but the old hands had gone down a split second sooner, as they felt the reverberation through the air. Today he begins a three part series on Islamic fundamentalism, From Beirut To Bosnia (9.00 C4). There seem to be lots of serious programmes tonight. Assignment (7.35 BBC2) reports on China's 'economic miracle' but that is followed by Human Rights, Human Wrongs which includes an assertion from Helen Suzman that torture is now endemic in China (8.20 BBC2).
