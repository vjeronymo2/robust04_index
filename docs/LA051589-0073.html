May 15, 1989, Monday, Home Edition

MORNING REPORT: MOVIES

Greer Garson, who was known in the 1940s as "The First Lady of the Screen," has made a $3 million gift to open a communication arts center at the College of Santa Fe, expected to be utilized by year's end. The donations include two professional sound stages for motion pictures and television productions and video recording studios for academic use. "The gift is especially gratifying to me because it will bring together two dominant interests in my life -- the academic study of the moving image and the professional management of the art," Garson said in a statement. The college president, James Fries, expects the Garson Communication Center to serve as headquarters for film production in New Mexico. CLAUDIA PUIG and ALEENE MacMINN
