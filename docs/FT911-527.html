910511 FT 11 MAY 91 / World Stock Markets (Asia Pacific): Arbitrage selling hits Nikkei in thin trading ARBITRAGE-related selling depressed the Nikkei average, which finished lower yesterday in spite of the overnight jump on Wall Street and falling bond yields, writes Emiko Terazono in Tokyo. The index closed down 163.21 at 26,274.29 - a 0.8 per cent fall on the week -after rising 128.71 on Thursday. It hit a day's high of 26,449.06 in the morning, but drifted down to a low of 26,212.64 on light index selling by investment trusts. Volume remained low at 340m shares, up from 330m. Advances marginally outpaced declines by 474 to 468, with 179 issues unchanged. The Topix index of all first-section issues rose 1.30 to 1,991.15 and, in London trading, the ISE/Nikkei 50 index declined by 2.01 to 1,497.32. Investors continued to trade in small and medium-capital stocks. The small-capital stock index on the first section rose 0.53 per cent while the second section index gained 0.45 per cent on strong volume of 25m shares. The over-the-counter index fell 54.55 to 3,227.98 on profit-taking. Heavy electrical issues finished higher as interest continued following the news of the strong April US semiconductor book-to-bill ratio. Hitachi rose Y20 to Y1,210 and Mitsubishi Electric climbed Y15 to Y780. Securities houses were sold on afternoon reports that Standard & Poor's, the US rating agency, was considering a downgrading of bonds issued by the big four brokerages. Daiwa Securities fell Y30 to Y1,370 and Nomura lost Y20 to Y2,200. Nihon Nohyaku, which fell on Thursday after an announcement about stricter margin requirements, rose Y50 to Y2,470 on renewed speculative interest. Speculators also sought Kawai Musical, which gained Y62 to Y932, and Fukusuke, up Y200 at Y4,490. Taiyo Sanso, an industrial gas manufacturer, fell Y10 to Y1,130, after hitting a year's high in the morning. The stock has been popular recently after strong sales of its semiconductor cleaning equipment. Bandai, the toymaker, rose Y140 to Y7,600 on an upward revision of its consolidated earnings for the previous year. Active trading of the company's warrant bonds overnight on the London market also encouraged investors. In Osaka, the OSE average rose 103.49 to 29,230.41 on volume of 34m shares. Toyo Tec, a security services operator, fell Y100 to Y4,900 on profit-taking. The issue had risen recently on reports of increased orders from banks. Roundup MOST PACIFIC Rim markets made gains on the week. MANILA saw Philippine Long Distance Telephone (PLDT) rise for a second day, closing up 12.50 pesos at 530, on news that its 18bn peso expansion programme had been approved. PLDT plans to install 296,400 new telephone lines in Manila and 147,900 lines in the provinces between 1992 and 1996. Otherwise trading was dull; the composite index shed 1.06 to 1,117.44, up 5.5 per cent on the week. NEW ZEALAND closed firm but off its earlier highs, as late profit-taking hit dual-listed issues. The Barclays index ended 5.37 higher at 1,573.86, up 3 per cent on the week but off its intraday peak of 1,586.20. Turnover slipped to NZDollars 19m from NZDollars 38m. Fletcher Challenge fell 1 cent to NZDollars 4.14. After the close it announced a NZDollars 195m international share placement. AUSTRALIA was also clipped by late profit-taking. The All Ordinaries index rose 2.0 to 1,548.3, its fifth consecutive rise and up 2 per cent on the week. Turnover fell to ADollars 231m from ADollars 307m. TAIWAN was mixed in nervous trading. The weighted index edged down 3.33 to 6,301.89, but was 7.6 per cent higher on the week. Turnover rose to TDollars 82.5bn from TDollars 76.9bn. HONG KONG lost most of its early Wall Street-inspired gains on profit-taking. The Hang Seng index closed at 3,750.20, up 5.74 on the day but little changed on the week. Turnover grew to HKDollars 1.3bn from HKDollars 948m. SEOUL resumed its downwards course, the composite index falling 4.89 to 629.83, a decline on the week of 3 per cent. Turnover shrank to Won62bn from Won81.5bn. BANGKOK saw heavy selling of four issues which made their debut yesterday. The SET index fell 8.11 to 845.88, down 2.6 per cent on the week. BOMBAY rose, as carry-forward charges for business done in the two weeks to Thursday were fixed at a lower-than-expected level. The BSE index rose 28.06 to 1,288.35.
