BFN [Text] Four container loads of chemicals being shipped from India to an Egyptian port have been detained in the Colombo harbor. According to reliable sources the containers each weighing 18 tonnes and carrying phosphorous penta-sulphide, an active ingredient in the manufacture of nerve gas outlawed worldwide, were on trans-shipments via the Colombo port when detained pending investigation. According to a senior port official, the shipment was stopped and the containers placed under security following instructions by both the Ministries of Defense and Foreign Affairs. Three of the containers had arrived in Colombo on board a Panamanian vessel (Everbridge) on April the 10th, while one had arrived earlier on board the ship (Zim Barcelona). The senior defense officials said that the containers were detained following information that they contained cargo being shipped in violation of international agreements. Assistant secretary of defense, Mr. (Mahinder Bandasena), said that the ministry has arranged for the cargo to be examined, but he declined to divulge who would conduct the examination.
