Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Hanoi VNA Jan. 22. -- Delegates at the mid-term conference of the Communist Party of Vietnam (CPV) on Jan. 20 and 21 discussed the political report delivered by Party General Secretary Do Muoi. The delegates focused on analyzing the all-sided achievements recorded over the past two years and a half and the most important, urgent issues in order to implement the resolutions of the seventh party congress. They also analyzed opportunities and challenges, advantages and disadvantages at home and in international relations the party and the entire people are facing, thus defining premises for gradually introducing the country into a new stage of development, that of industrialization and modernization. The delegates paid great attention to the goals and concrete steps to be taken, especially measures and capacity to mobilize resources for the construction of socio-economic infrastructure to meet both long-oerm and short-term demands. Many delegates showed their attention to the role of state management in the market economy, and methods for managing state-run businesses to ensure their leading position in key branches of the national economy. Social and cultural issues were enthusiastically discussed. The anti-corruption and smuggling work was also analyzed and criticized seriously. Debates also concentrated on how to train purify and develop the contingent of the party members to meet the demand of the party leadership in the current process of innovation. Today, the delegates will continue their discussion of the political report.
