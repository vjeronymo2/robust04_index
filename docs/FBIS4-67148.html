BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of fileworthy reports from 6-11 May on counternarcotics operations in countries monitored by FBIS London Bureau. Sources are given in parentheses at the end of each report.

Italy
An antidrug operation which began two days ago ended on 11 May with the arrest of 13 people. Police from the Rome III group seized 6.5 kgs of cocaine valued at 4 billion lire. Among those arrested are high-profile criminal figures from Rome, Palermo Catania, and Calabria in southern Italy. The gang had its operational bases in the towns of Anzio and Ciampino, on the outskirts of Rome. (Rome RAI Televideo Teletext in Italian 0727 GMT 11 May 94)
Poland
Former Costa Rican Ambassador to Poland Carlos Vargas Solis was charged by the prosecutor's office with smuggling 12.2 kg of heroin into Poland in April 1993. An indictment has been sent to the Provincial Court in Warsaw. On 20 April 1993 the Border Guard and customs officers at Warsaw's Okecie airport found 12.2 kg of heroin in Solis' luggage. The luggage arrived from Delhi via Moscow. Solis arrived from Delhi on an earlier plane. (Warsaw PAP in Polish 1126 GMT 5 May 94)
Russia
Sources in law enforcement bodies said a Moscow resident and an Afghan citizen were detained trying to sell 370 grams of heroin with a street value 172 million rubles. Investigations are being conducted. (Moscow INTERFAX in English 1610 GMT 6 May 94)
Spain
Police have arrested 32 people under Operation Pelican since it started on the morning of 10 May. The hashish ring under investigation is alleged to have brought 150 tons of hashish from Morocco to Spain over the last few years. The operation is being conducted in 14 provinces as well as abroad. (Madrid RNE-1 Radio Network in Spanish 1600 GMT 10 May 94)
