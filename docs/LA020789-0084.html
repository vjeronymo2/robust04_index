February 7, 1989, Tuesday, Home Edition

DGA NOMINATES 'THIRTYSOMETHING' CREATORS

Writing and producing partners Marshall Herskovitz and Edward Zwick, who created "thirtysomething" and serve as its executive producers, found themselves competitors Monday as each was nominated by the Directors Guild of America for outstanding TV directing in 1988.

Both nominations came in the dramatic series category -- Herskovitz's for an episode of their ABC series titled "Therapy," Zwick's for an episode called "Accounts Receivable." Also nominated in that category was Mark C. Tinker for the final episode of "St. Elsewhere" on NBC.

Nominees also were announced in four other TV categories. Winners will be named at the guild's 41st annual awards dinner March 11.

The three nominees in the dramatic special category were all cited for their work on historical programs. Dan Curtis was nominated for the World War II miniseries "War and Remembrance" on ABC; Rod Holcomb for the Vietnam War-era "China Beach" pilot on ABC, and Lamont Johnson for the two-part biography "Lincoln" on NBC.

The other nominations:

Comedy Series: Ellen Falcon, "Roseanne" pilot, ABC; Barnet Kellman, "Murphy Brown" pilot, CBS; Steve Miner, "The Wonder Years" pilot, ABC.

Musical/Variety: David Grossman, "The Smothers Brothers 20th Year Reunion," CBS; Hal Gurnee, "Late Night 6th Anniversary Special," NBC; Walter C. Miller, "100th Birthday Celebration, Irving Berlin," CBS.

Daytime Dramatic Special: Kevin Hooks, "CBS Schoolbreak Special: Home Sweet Homeless," CBS; Gilbert Moses, "CBS Schoolbreak Special: Daddy Can't Read," CBS; Jesus Salvador Trevino, "CBS Schoolbreak Special: Gangs," CBS.
