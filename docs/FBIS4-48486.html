BFN [Text] The Anambra State police command has discovered an illegal arms factory at Ntede in Oyi local government area of the state. Correspondent Eddy Okafor reports that the proprietor of the factory was arrested following information given by two armed robbery suspects: [Begin Okafor recording] Arraigning the illegal arms manufacturer in Government House, Oka, the state police commissioner, Alhaji Mamman Ankah, said two shotguns, one locally made pistol, and 12 live cartridges were recovered from the factory. Speaking while inspecting the arms and ammunition, the state administrator, Kuman Nakata, said he is not opposed to possession of arms for security purposes, provided such possession is within the provisions of the law. He directed the police to carry out thorough investigations with a view to detecting similar factories elsewhere. Earlier, the arrested arms manufacturer said he got his license to manufacture arms from colonial masters in 1952, adding that most of the guns recovered belong to vigilante groups from neighboring towns. Meanwhile, the two teenage robbery suspects who gave the tipoff after the arrest [words indistinct] confessed robbing a man of the sum of 1,500 naira. The state commissioner of police, Alhaji Mamman, told NTA News of the determination of his command to rid the state of criminals. He said the suspects would soon appear in court. [end recording]
