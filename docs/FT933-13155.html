930721 FT 21 JUL 93 / Russia hints at attack across Afghan border A TOP AIDE to Russia's security minister said yesterday that Russian forces in Tajikistan reserved the right to launch attacks across the Afghan border and that 'we have the moral right to invade their territory'. General Vladimir Bondarenko, the security minister's chief of staff, echoed the tough line taken last week by the military establishment after 25 Russian border guards were killed in a raid launched by Tajik opposition fighters who had taken refuge in Afghanistan after losing the struggle for control of their republic. Tajik and Russian officials have accused the government in Kabul of masterminding the attack, a charge the Afghan leadership has denied. Russian legislators, back from a trip to the embattled border region, admitted yesterday that Russian forces were behind a retaliatory attack into Afghan territory last week in which 380 people were killed or wounded and 6,000 villagers forced to flee their homes. Gen Bondarenko said yesterday that the security ministry had authorised its troops to fire into Afghanistan.
