Language: Ukrainian Article Type:BFN [Text] Today, Yuriy Serhyeyev, head of the [Foreign Ministry] Information Directorate, reported at a Defense Ministry briefing that the Foreign Affairs Ministry is once again asserting that Ukraine does not need the mediation of a CIS representative to represent its interests at the United Nations. Ukraine's positions, which Volodymyr Handohiy [Ukraine's acting permanent representative at the United Nations] related at a plenary session of the UN General Assembly -- that a CIS representative can only represent the interests of those states that have signed the CIS Charter -- remain unchanged. As Ukraine has not done this, and also does not consider the CIS to be a subject of international law, but only a regional organization, Ukrainian diplomacy is in favor of direct and not mediated relations with the United Nations.
