941014 FT 14 OCT 94 / Observer: Blair blooms Observer's request for Tony Blair jokes did not inspire a bumper crop. Stanley Alderson proposed that Blair will feature at Labour's Christmas party dressed as Sanity Claus, with Arthur Scargill rivalling as Santa Clause Four. Rod Currie offered an anagram of Tony Blair MP: I'm Tory Plan B. Robert Winter considered how Blair might fare in a driving test - he kept to the middle of the road and failed to notice the right turn. However, the best of the bunch involved Blair's colleagues suggesting their leader should crown his success by walking across the river Thames outside Parliament. He succeeds in doing so, watched closely by Ken Livingstone and Dennis Skinner. 'Just as I thought,' says Livingstone, 'he can't even swim.' Skinner: 'Aye, and the Thames would never 'ave supported 'im if it 'ad still been in public ownership.'
