Language: Persian Article Type:CSO [Text] Tehran, 25 Azar [16 Dec] -- The number of students at the expert guidance and doctoral levels has been increased for higher education in the Second Five-Year Plan. Dr. Hashemi-Golpayegani, minister of culture and higher education, announced the above at the 14th conference of educational deputies from universities and institutions of higher education. He discussed the shortage of professors and programs at this ministry. He said: This ministry must create an adequate incentive to attract Iranian specialists outside the country. Likewise, special facilities and resources must be created for teachers and instructors working at the universities to continue their studies to higher levels and programs. The minister of culture and higher education said: The Ministry of Culture and Higher Education has given the ministries and government organizations the prerogatives to enable them to meet the needs of the ministries and government organizations. Dr. Salehi, deputy minister of culture and higher education also announced during a speech: Based on the provisions that have been made, by the end of the Second Five-Year Plan, we will have about 46,000 expert guidance students and 5,000 doctoral students. He added: There are now about 16,000 students studying in expert guidance and doctoral programs.
