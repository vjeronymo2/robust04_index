930501 FT 01 MAY 93 / International Company News: UK, US gases groups take stakes in Polish producers BOC, the UK industrial gases group, and Liquid Carbonic, of the US, yesterday agreed to pay more than Dollars 40m between them for 70 per cent stakes in five Polish producers of industrial gases. According to Poland's Ministry of Industry, the companies have also committed 'up to Dollars 145m' over the next five years to modernise production and distribution. BOC has acquired three companies in Poznan, Siewierz and Wroclaw, while Liquid Carbonic, a subsidiary of Illinois-based CBI Industries, has bought plants in Warsaw and Gliwice. Liquid Carbonic will pay Dollars 15.2m to the state treasury and invest Dollars 5.2m in the two plants it has bought. BOC said it was committing Dollars 30m to the plants over five years. The five Polish enterprises reported revenues worth Dollars 42.6m in 1991. Poland's industry ministry was advised in the deal by Midland Montagu and Bain and Co.
