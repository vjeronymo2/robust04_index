Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Aleksey Kuznetsov] [Text] Delhi, 16 Feb -- Admiral Feliks Gromov, commander in chief of the Russian Navy, currently on a visit to India, has assured official Delhi that supplies will be resumed soon of spare parts for Russian armaments in the Indian Navy. In a conversation with his Indian colleague V.S. Shekhawat, he said the reason for his confidence was an outlined stabilization of the political situation in Russia. All obligations made by Moscow to arm the republic's armed forces, and among other things the navy, will be fulfilled. Gromov was also received by the Minister of State for Defense, M. Mallikarjum. He had meetings with top officials in the defense department and commanders of branches of the army. Matters of mutual interest were discussed. On Tuesday evening, the commander in chief of the Russian Navy went to Vishakhapatnam where he will visit a naval base and the headquarters of the eastern command of the republic's navy.
