Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, March 29 KYODO -- The strong yen has been reflected in prices of imported goods, the Finance Ministry and the Economic Planning Agency (EPA) reported Tuesday [29 March]. The report, based on a survey of price movements in March, said import prices at customs clearance declined in 24 of 30 items surveyed, while retail prices dropped in 15 of 17 items. Retail prices of imported beef dropped 14 percent, compared with February, it said. EPA officials said the survey showed that the decline of import prices declared at customs clearance was reflected in retail prices. The officials said prices of wine imported from France in February were down 37.8 percent compared with a year earlier. Prices of frozen octopus and Italian-made dress shirts have declined around 30 percent from the year before, they said.
