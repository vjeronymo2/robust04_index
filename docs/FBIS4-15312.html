BFN [Text] Tokyo 7 April, TASS-KAZTAG -- Japan and Kazakhstan are ready to pool efforts to implement Alma-Ata's initiative and convene a conference on interaction and confidence-building in Asia similar to the Conference on Security and Cooperation in Europe, Kazakhstan's President Nursultan Nazarbayev and Japan's Prime Minister Morihiro Hosokawa said during their talks in Tokyo on Thursday. Their meeting was held in the official residence of the head of the Japanese Government. It covered a wide array of political and economic items. As concerns the situation in Central Asia, Kazakhstan's president noted that Kazakhstan, Uzbekistan and Turkmenistan have to pursue a tough domestic policy in order to avert the spread of conflicts similar to that in Tajikistan throughout the region. As regards the domestic situation in Kazakhstan, Nazarbayev described it as politically stable. Nazarbayev called for expanding the scale of economic integration in Asia as a whole and asked Tokyo to come forth with an initiative for the organisation of a common continental market. He stressed his country's readiness to serve as a base for Tokyo's penetration to Central Asian markets. The president said that he is ready to give Japan preference in realizing major economic projects on the territory of his republic. For this purpose he suggested that Morihiro Hosokawa and himself set up a joint commission to prepare this important move. The Japanese prime minister assured Alma-Ata of his invariable support for the transformations underway in Kazakhstan and said the projects named by Kazakhstan in the field of economic cooperation require a detailed study by Japan's leadership.
