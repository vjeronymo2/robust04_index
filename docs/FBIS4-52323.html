BFN [Text] Statistics show that Japan remains Thailand's biggest trading partner with bilateral trade volume of 513,000 million baht in 1993, an increase of 15 percent. The Department of Business Economics says that in 1993, Thai exports to Japan accounted for 160,000 million baht, an increase of 10 percent. Thailand imported 535,000 [figure as heard] million baht from Japan. This represented an increase of 17 percent, widening Thailand's trade deficit with Japan to 193,000 million baht. The United States came second in trade relations with bilateral trade volume of 338,000 million baht, an increase of 10 percent over the figure of 1993. The European Union and ASEAN came next. Thailand suffered a trade deficit of 17,000 million baht with the European Union, while it registered a surplus of 56,000 million baht and six million baht over the United States and ASEAN respectively.
