May 19, 1990, Saturday, Ventura County Edition

GROUP GETS GO-AHEAD FOR ELY RECALL PETITION;

COLLEGE DISTRICT: THE 12 SIMI AND MOORPARK RESIDENTS HAVE 160 DAYS TO GATHER 9,900 SIGNATURES.

A citizens group that has demanded the resignation of Tom Ely, a trustee of the Ventura County Community College District, was given the green light Friday to start collecting signatures for a petition to recall him, county officials said.

The group's petition, along with Ely's response, was approved and certified by the Elections Division of the county clerk's office Friday afternoon, said Jenny Harrison, elections services coordinator for the county.

The citizens group last week demanded that Ely resign immediately or face the possibility of being recalled. The group cited "fiscal irresponsibility and unprofessional conduct" in regard to his "excessive travel expenses" as the main reason for the demand. Faculty leaders at each of Ventura County's three colleges have also called for Ely's resignation.

Ely, who could not be reached for comment Friday, has said he will not resign.

The group, made up of 12 Simi Valley and Moorpark residents, has 160 days to gather 15% of the signatures of the 66,000 registered voters in the 4th District, according to election officials. If the signatures are collected in time, the college Board of Trustees would be required to call a special election.

Caroline Paras, an organizer of the group, said members planned to start collecting signatures this morning.

For the recall to be on the November ballot, 9,900 signatures would have to be collected by June 15, said Ruth Schepler, director of the Elections Division. Otherwise, the board would not be able to call a special election until next year.

Ely filed a one-page response to the recall notice with the Elections Division on Thursday, Schepler said.

"You've elected me three times. I've always been prudent with your money," Ely wrote in his response. "For 11 years, I've been the only board member to donate time unselfishly, to represent you in Sacramento, Washington, D.C., or wherever necessary.

"My efforts have benefited my constituents. Naturally, my expenses have been higher than other board members," he said.

Ely has been the subject of controversy since it was disclosed in March that he had run up large gambling debts and had used his district telephone to call casinos in Nevada.

A district audit of his financial records has led to a criminal investigation by the district attorney's office involving the possible misuse of several thousand dollars in district funds.

Ely has said that the audit is flawed and that he has not violated the district's travel policy.
