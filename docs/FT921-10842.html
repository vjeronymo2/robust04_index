920204 FT 04 FEB 92 / VSEL to shed 600 jobs at shipyard SIX HUNDRED jobs are to be shed by the VSEL shipyard at Barrow-in-Furness, Cumbria, it was announced last night. The yard builds Trident nuclear submarines and the cuts will bring the number employed to below 10,000. The losses had been expected under the company's strategy to deal with the government's defence cuts. VSEL expects the number of employees will fall to between 7,000 and 9,000 by 1995, compared with about 13,000 two years ago. The company said the announcement did not take into account the effects of its failure last week to secure a Pounds 400m order for three Type 23 Navy frigates, which went to the Yarrow yards on the Clyde. Mr Alex Ferry, general secretary of the Confederation of Shipbuilding and Engineering Unions, said: 'We naturally welcome the peace dividend but the government has no programme to try to counter the worst effects arising from it.'
