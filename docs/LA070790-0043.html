July 7, 1990, Saturday, Valley Edition

OFFICIALS TAKE RACEWAY WIN FROM BRADER

It was easy come, easy go for Lynn Brader at Ventura Raceway on Friday night.

Brader, of Somis, squeezed between a pair of cars on the front straightaway of the final lap of the Street Stock 15-lap oval main event for an apparent victory. But the aggressive move was apparently too aggressive for the officials, who penalized Brader for rough driving and dropped him to fourth place, giving Brett Miller of Oxnard his first main-event win of the season.

Don Keeter of Ventura was awarded second with third going to Bill Famularo of Simi Valley.

Things were not quite so wild in the Mini Stock 20-lap oval main event as Ventura's Marty Desbrow drove to his second consecutive victory, boosting his season points leadership over Chris Raschke of Ventura, who finished third.

Paul Moore of Oxnard finished second.

Jim Firsich of Port Hueneme padded his figure-eight season points lead with an easy victory in the six-lap main event. Scott Olsen of Ventura was second and Miller third.

Joe Silva of Port Hueneme went wire-to-wire to win the Mini Stock eight-lap figure-eight main event. The victory moved Silva within 100 points of season points leader Richard Webster of Camarillo, who finished fourth.

Terry Swauger of Ojai and John Csorba of Oxnard were second and third, respectively.

Eight-lap Mini Stock heat-race winners were Mike Spencer of Oxnard, Kevin Parker of Ventura and Raschke. Street Stock heat winners were Don Famularo of Simi Valley and Bill Pate of Oxnard.

Gary Swenson of Ventura outclassed the field to win the Mini Stock 10-lap semi-main event to head-up a four-car contingent qualifying for the main event. Also moving up were Ken Hewitt of Moorpark, Spencer, and Brandon King of Oxnard.

Six-lap Trophy Dash winners were Keeter in the Street Stocks and Webster in the Mini Stocks.

Fast one-lap qualifying times were set by Jim Powell Jr. of Cypress in the Street Stocks, at 16.865 seconds, and Desbrow in the Mini Stocks at 17.767.

The Winged Outlaw Mini Sprints from the San Joaquin Valley area will make their only appearance of the season at the Raceway tonight at 7.

The Mini Stocks will also be running with a double-points, double-purse program.
