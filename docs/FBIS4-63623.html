BFN [Text] Russian President Boris Yeltsin has told the government to introduce in parliament by September 15 draft laws to lower the number of taxes, decrease the level of profit tax and VAT by 10 to 20 percent and also the level of taxes related to the rate of pay and production. At the same time, his decree of May 23 raises the level of tax for individuals as well as for the property of individuals and corporate entities. The decree, which aims to limit inflationary processes, reduces the number of goods eligible for a special 13 percent VAT rate (against the usual 23 percent). From June 1 the rate of payment to the Social Security fund will be lowered from 5.4 to 3.4 percent of the sum of charged wages. The government is also to adopt a resolution within the next two months concerning the possibilities of accelerated amortization of capital funds in highly technical branches of industry. The decree lays down that production enterprises, the foreign share of whose capital is no less than 30 percent or 10 million dollars and which were registered after January 1, 1994, will not have to pay profit tax for the next two years. Profit tax in Russia is up to 38 percent.
