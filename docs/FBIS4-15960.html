BFN [Text] Today, the command of the Northwestern Group of Russian Forces submitted to the Latvian bureau for monitoring the Russian troop withdrawal data on the number of discharged Russian military personnel living in Latvia. According to this information, 21,976 discharged Russian military personnel live in Latvia at the moment. The majority are over 60. More than 3,000 discharged Russian military personnel living in Latvia are Latvian citizens. Since 28 January 1992, when Russia officially took over the former USSR armed forces, almost 2,000 military personnel have been discharged from service in Latvia and have not left for Russia. The report explains that these people are waiting for apartments in Russia and will leave Latvia after they receive them. Ilgonis Upmalis, head of the Latvian bureau for monitoring the withdrawal of the Russian army, will also send this information to State President Guntis Ulmanis.
