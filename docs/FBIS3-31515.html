Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Bratislava, Feb 17 (CTK) -- The last MiG-29 fighter of the six with which Russia is partially paying off the debt of the former Soviet Union to Slovakia landed in Slovakia last night, a spokeswoman for the Slovak Defense Ministry told CTK. On November 30, 1993, Slovakia and Russia signed an agreement to partially decreasing the debts of the former Soviet Union and the Russian Federation to the Slovak Republic through supplies of special equipment. Under the agreement, Slovakia bought in December weapons and military equipment worth $180 million, including six MiG-29 fighters and spare parts for various types of weapons. During his January visit to Moscow, Slovak Defense Ministry State Secretary Igor Urban and his Russian partners reached an agreement that Slovakia will purchase other military equipment, mainly spare parts, worth $80 million.
