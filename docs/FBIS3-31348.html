Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] Belgrade, 16 Feb (TANJUG) -- Russian Deputy Foreign Minister Vitaliy Churkin, who arrived today on a suprise visit to Belgrade where he met Serbian President Slobodan Milosevic, will extend his stay in the Yugoslav capital by another day, TANJUG has learned from the office of the Russian NOVOSTI news agency in Belgrade. The same source disclosed that Churkin's latest visit to Belgrade is directly linked to the situation in Sarajevo -- the NATO ultimatum -- and threats that Serb positions around that city will be attacked if the artillery is not withdrawn to a distance of 20 km from Sarajevo.
