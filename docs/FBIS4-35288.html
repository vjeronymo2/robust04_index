BFN [Excerpts] The House of Representatives this morning started the sessions of the first period of the first round of its second annual term under brother Shaykh 'Abdallah Bin-Husayn al-Ahmar, the House speaker. On the occasion of the House starting its second annual term, the brother House of Representatives speaker delivered a speech in which he congratulated the House of Representatives members and the Yemeni people on the blessed 'Id al-Adha and the fourth unity anniversary, which will remain a shining point in the history of our people and a major turning point in their blessed march. The House of Representatives speaker noted that the parliamentary elections of 27 April [1993] provided clear evidence of our people's awareness and sound instincts. These elections, he said, produced a new political reality in the Yemeni arena. The House of Representatives speaker noted that the secessionist group in the Socialist Party leadership, which led the country to war and infighting, had also directed its poisonous arrows against the House of Representatives by taking advantage of its positions in the state's executive agencies, despite the fact that the House had to put up with a great deal of arrogance from some of these leaders out of concern for the march of goodness and prosperity, and out of concern for national unity. After that, the House reviewed and approved the agenda of the current parliamentary term. The House also listened to and approved the minutes of the last session of the previous parliamentary term. The House then listened to a letter addressed to it by Justice Minister 'Abdallah Ahmad Ghanim on the public actions committed by Ahmad 'Ubayd Bin-Daghr, Anis Hasan Yahya, Haydar Abu-Bakr al-'Attas, Salim Muhammad Jubran, Qasim 'Abd-al-Rabb, and Muhammad 'Ali al- Qayrahi, including the crimes of violating the Constitution, attacking citizens and private and public property, issuing orders in contravention of the law, and organizing rebellion against the public order despite being members of the House. He said confirmed information on these acts were received from the Defense Ministry, the Interior Ministry, and other parties. The justice minister's letter to the House states: We appreciate your positive response to allow us to take the legal measures against them in accordance with your prerogatives, as stipulated in the Constitution, and in accordance with Article 64-F. [passage omitted] In accordance with the aforementioned articles and following your permission, the Public Prosecutor issued an arrest warrant against these persons, as well as others. We hope you will take measures to inform the House of these details in accordance with the aforesaid articles. We will keep your esteemed House informed of all developments. The House then discussed the Public Prosecutor's report which was referred to it by the justice minister. Following a lengthy discussion of this subject, the matter was referred to the Constitutional and Legal Affairs Committee to study it in an urgent manner and to present a ruling to the House on the request to deprive the members mentioned in the justice minister's memorandum on their immunity, in accordance with the Constitutional texts and the House of Representatives bylaws issued by Law No. 5 of 1990. The people's deputies then discussed al-Bid's declaration of secession. The House members, including members of the Yemeni Socialist Party bloc, stressed that the declaration of secession by al-Bid and his gang is a stab in the heart of every Yemeni. Members stressed that the war is between unity and the enemies of unity and that everyone supports unity, and that the people will sacrifice everything dear to protect it. The House also stressed the need to preserve democracy and Constitutional legitimacy and its absolute rejection of any foreign intervention in the Republic of Yemen's internal affairs, because any intervention or recognition of the secessionist gang only expresses a flagrant animosity against the entire Yemeni people and a hostility against their Constitutional and democratic institutions. The House set up a committee comprising House Members Yahya 'Ali (al-Ra'i), 'Abdallah Sharaf (al-Humaydi), Yahya Mansur Abu-Usba', Muhammad 'Abdallah (al-Kifli), Mansur Ahmad Sayf, Muhammad Naji ('Allaw), 'Abdu Muhammad al-Zanadi, Muhammad Yahya Hammud (al- Sharafi) to draft a statement refuting the declaration of secession, stressing the need to protect unity through all means, and urging all states, particularly Arab states, to support unity and [words indistinct] overcome the sedition that was fabricated by some elements that have turned against the Constitution and legitimacy and against the will of the people as expressed by the elections of 27 April 1993. The House also set up a committee including members 'Abdallah 'Ali (Sa'tar), Muhammad 'Ali Ba Musallam, Sultan Sa'id (al- Barakani), 'Abdallah Mahdi 'Abdu, 'Abd-al-Wahhab al-Rawhani, and Sultan (Sayyan al-'Adwani) to draft letters to the Arab League, the UN secretary general, and to the parliaments of the fraternal and friendly states. The House also asked the government to send the brother foreign minister to attend the House's session tomorrow, Sunday [29 May], to explain the political situation concerning the developments in our country so that the parliament can take the necessary steps. Today's session was attended by brother Dr. 'Abd-Salam Khalid Salman, minister of legal affairs and minister of the House of Representatives affairs. The House will resume its sessions tomorrow, God willing.
