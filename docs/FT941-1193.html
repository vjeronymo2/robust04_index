940326 FT 26 MAR 94 / HK banks raise rates Hong Kong yesterday succumbed to the recent rises in US interest rates and announced a 0.25 percentage point increase in bank base rates to 6.75 per cent, writes Simon Holberton in Hong Kong. The decision by Hongkong Bank and Standard Chartered Bank to raise rates, effective Monday, ends the decline in borrowing costs which began in May 1991, when rates were cut to 9.5 per cent from 10.5 per cent. Hong Kong's currency is fixed to the US dollar at a rate of HKDollars 7.8 to the US unit; to maintain the link the colony needed to raise rates.
