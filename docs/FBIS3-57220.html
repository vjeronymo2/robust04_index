Language: Ukrainian Article Type:BFN [Text] Kiev -- Volodymyr Pryadko, chairman of the board of the State Property Fund of Ukraine, reported at the plenary session of the Supreme Council of Ukraine on 21 January that in accordance with the draft state privatization program, there are plans to privatize 20,000 facilities of unfinished construction and 8,000 medium and large enterprises in 1994. It is envisioned that as a result of implementing the proposed program, the share of the non-state sector in the economy will come to 28 percent and that the state will receive over 21 trillion karbovantsi and approximately $230 million. Delivering a report, Volodymyr Pylypchuk, chairman of the Supreme Council commission for issues of economic reform and management of the national economy, mentioned 11 observations regarding the draft of the said program, and stressed the necessity of applying administrative penalties to officials who disregard the privatization process. After Supreme Council commissions have elaborated the draft, taking into account proposals expressed by people's deputies during the discussion, it will be resubmitted for hearings during the next plenary cycle of sessions. A law "On the procedure for leaving and entering Ukraine for citizens of Ukraine" was adopted. It envisions simplification of the procedure for formalizing exit documents. Speaker Valentyn Nedryhaylo, first deputy minister of internal affairs of Ukraine, replied to questions on the investigation in the affair of the disappearance of Mykhaylo Boychyshyn, head of the People's Movement of Ukraine [Rukh] secretariat. Four main versions of the disappearance are being worked on, additional ones have appeared, and Interpol and investigative bodies from CIS countries have joined the investigation, but so far, according to Valentyn Nedryhaylo, "new signals have not been received." A law "On state secrets" was adopted without fundamental changes; the law "On data protection in automated systems" was approved in its first reading, and the Forestry Code of Ukraine, which was adopted, will come into force from the day of publication. The next plenary session of the Supreme Council will take place on 25 January.
