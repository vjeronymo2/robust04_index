BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Anatoliy Yurkin] [Text] Moscow May 10 TASS -- The preliminary results of the first stage of the poaching-control operation which ended on Tuesday, points to a sharp drop in fish poaching and theft of other marine wealth in Russian Far Eastern waters. Nikolay Kudinov, commander of naval forces of Russian Border Troops, told ITAR-TASS today that Far Eastern sea borders are securely protected. "The great power will not put up with violations of international norms and rules any more," he stressed. In the admiral's opinion, the operation will be also successful on the subsequent stages as well. "It is too early to sum up the final results, but one thing is clear: The operation will help protect Russia's national interests," Kudinov underlined. The admiral expressed opinion that part of funds earned from anti-poaching activities should be channelled for the needs of units participating in the operation.
