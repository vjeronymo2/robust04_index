BFN [Text] The Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev has declared that the protocol to the Russia-NATO program due to be approved in Brussels is not going to be signed. Answering a question from Interfax before his departure for Brussels on Tuesday he said the protocol does not require signing. "It registers the understanding that Russia and NATO are going to cooperate on key elements of the program the Russian Defence Minister Pavel Grachev submitted to the NATO headquarters about a month ago", Kozyrev said. According to him, Grachev described the military aspects of cooperation. "Now we are going to add a political element and officially proclaim the cooperation of Russia and NATO," Kozyrev said adding that he would sign the Partnership for Peace program in Brussels. "We welcome this program too", he said. The minister stressed that the protocol and program supplement, not contradict each other. According to Kozyrev, the protocol which is now getting its final touch reflects the special relationship of Russia and NATO and their potentials. "The interaction and mutually- beneficial cooperation of Russia and NATO are possible and necessary", the minister reiterated.
