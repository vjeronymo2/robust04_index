Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [From "The News" newscast] [Text] Gustavo Tarre Briceno, chairman of the Congressional Finance Commission, announced that the draft of the emergency law that will apply to the Banco Latino case will be ready today. Meanwhile, Gustavo Roosen, president of the bank's intervention board, said that the emergency law establishes financial aid for Banco Latino, something that will allow the implementation this week of strategies for the bank's rehabilitation. Roosen explained that the banking law that has been in effect since the beginning of this year envisages a rehabilitation phase as long as the rules set by the Bank Superintendency and the Deposit Guarantee Fund during the intervention phase are followed. Roosen said the bank has lost 150 billion bolivares, an amount that can increase or diminish depending on the work carried out by the current intervention board.
