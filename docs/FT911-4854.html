910417 FT 17 APR 91 / Parliament and Politics: Health reforms THE government's health service reforms, intended to produce an internal market in which the money follows the patient, are producing hardly any patient choice, according to a survey conducted by Ms Harriet Harman, an opposition health spokeswoman. The replies from just over 70 district health authorities showed that most of health authority budgets were spent in block contracts between purchasing health authorities - which do not have direct dealings with patients - and hospitals providing the services, leaving very little scope for referring patients for treatment elsewhere.
