BFN [Report by Ciro Ortega from the "RPC Television News" newscast] [Text] Harmodio Arias, deputy commerce and industries minister, has referred to the banana situation, saying that economic interests and not Latin American unity seem to prevail on this issue and that Costa Rica had not reciprocated Panama's actions in the banana business. Colombia's and Costa Rica's decision to negotiate, on their own, a banana agreement with the European Union negatively affects Latin American unity and directly harms Panama, Harmodio Arias has said. [Begin Arias recording] I believe this shows there is no Latin American unity. With Costa Rica, we have respected our promise to vote for Costa Rican Foreign Minister Bernd Niehaus [at the OAS elections], but they have broken our unity on the banana issue. [end recording] Arias said Colombia and Costa Rica want to get the best part of the banana business with Europe without caring about the rest of the region, and this cannot be. [Begin recording] [Arias] We cannot accept a quota for each country; this goes against everything that deals with free trade. It is not possible for several European gentlemen to be the ones to decide Panama only has the right to export 19 percent of bananas consumed by the European Economic Community. [Unidentified reporter] What does this 19 percent represent regarding our interests, farms, and workers? How much will this hurt us? [Arias] Crumbs. We are left with crumbs. Panama exports 70 percent of its banana production to Europe; thus, we have been left with only crumbs. [end recording] Arias was emphatic when he said the other banana-producing countries will not allow unilateral agreements with the European Union and will negotiate as a block to obtain added economic advantages.
