BFN [Text] San Jose, 13 Jun (ACAN-EFE) -- The Central American presidents today demanded a solution to the Cuban situation within the framework of the inter-American system, but ruled out taking this issue before the fourth Ibero-American summit, which will be held in Colombia tomorrow and Wednesday, or that it will be the subject of an agreement. At the conclusion of a two-day meeting in Costa Rica, Honduran President Carlos Roberto Reina said that this is a regional stand, although there may be details that they might not agree on. Reina said that Central America would like to see a "democratic transition" in Cuba, but attributed the responsibility of seeking a solution to the OAS. "It is a problem of the (inter-American) system, and we must find a solution to this special case," Reina pointed out when consulted on this issue, which was not included in the final document of the Central American meeting. The Honduran president and Costa Rican President Jose Maria Figueres agreed, however, that the solution of the Cuban problem "must come from within the Cuban family." Figueres said that now that the bipolar world is a thing of the past and the Cold War is over "we have an obligation to assess" our foreign relations, among others, with Cuba, a country with which Costa Rica broke relations in the 1960's, as did most of the Latin American countries. "In these new times we want to be sure of what we are doing," the chief of state said. He added that all efforts must be made to ensure that Cuba will be able to move forward "in a peaceful manner." The chiefs of state, however, refused to say whether they want to reestablish relations with Cuba, request an end to the economic blockade imposed by the United States for more than 30 years, or call for Cuba's reinstatement into the OAS, from which it was expelled in the 1960's. A diplomatic source that requested anonymity, however, said that the Cuban case could bring a surprise at the Ibero-American summit, which will take place tomorrow and Wednesday in Cartagena de Indias (Colombia), but refused to elaborate.
