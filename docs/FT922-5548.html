920530 FT 30 MAY 92 / Bulgarian N-safety plan BULGARIA has received international industry backing for plans to improve safety at its troubled Kozloduy nuclear power plant, officials said yesterda, Reuter reports from Sofia. The Balkan state hopes to restart two units currently out of action by the end of 1992. Kozloduy has six reactors, but the two oldest Soviet-built 440-megawatt units have been shut down since last September for repairs after international criticism over safety standards. The European Community has given Bulgaria Ecu11.5m (Pounds 8m) for the upgrading programme at Kozloduy, which provides 40 per cent of the country's power.
