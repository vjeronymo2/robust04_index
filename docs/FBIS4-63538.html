BFN [Text] The Lithuanian and Belarusian defense ministers hint that they expect their dialogue to be held on a regular basis from now on. A delegation from the Belarusian Defense Ministry, headed by Defense Minister Pavel Kazlowski, arrived in Vilnius today to meet with Lithuanian Defense Minister Linas Linkevicius. The sides agreed that the 720 km border between Lithuania and Belarus is a solid enough reason for bilateral consultations. Lithuania links its security with integration into NATO. Meanwhile, Belarus is an integral part of the CIS collective security system. In spite of the fact that the two countries belong to different security structures their defense ministers do not regard it as an obstacle on the road to mutual cooperation. Speaking at today's news conference, Lithuania's defense minister, Linas Linkevicius, said the two countries will establish closer contacts if Belarus joins NATO's Partnership for Peace program. In respone to this the Belarusian defense minister, Pavel Kazlowski, said the NATO program is [words indistinct] Belarus. The Belarusian authorities are currently discussing the possible participation of their country in the Partnership for Peace plan. Mr. Kazlowski also expressed interest in Lithuania's experience in this field.
