December 7, 1989, Thursday, Home Edition

DEATH AS A GOOD CAREER MOVE

Devine's logic doesn't make sense. Maybe talking to the leaves in Maine has something to do with it; a liberal dose of California smog just might clear his thinking.

He says Judas Iscariot made a "good career move" when he hanged himself with a halter from the limb of a tree. We were taught that Judas was a perfect example of a coward. Perhaps if he (Judas) had admitted his error and tried to make amends he would not have gone down in history as someone to be despised.

As for the cruel description of Bette Davis "dressed like an aging whore" on the David Letterman show, Devine states, "Her face was a monument to booze, disease and cigarettes." If he had done his homework, Devine would know she had overcome a stroke and bout with breast cancer. I think she showed exceptional courage during her long ordeal. If she chose to dress up in a red dress with glass beads, then I say go for it, Bette. I'm sure when Ms. Davis stood outside the "great fake mother-of-pearl gate," she didn't have time to say, "What a dump," because I bet she heard "Well done, my good and faithful servant."

DOROTHY CROWLEY-CAVECCHE

Palos Verdes Estates
