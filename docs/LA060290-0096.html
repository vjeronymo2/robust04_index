June 2, 1990, Saturday, Ventura County Edition

VENTURA COUNTY NEWS ROUNDUP: COUNTYWIDE;

10% INCREASE SOUGHT FOR POLLUTION FEES

The Ventura County Air Pollution Control District has proposed to hike the cost of permits by 10% to businesses that add pollutants to the air, county officials said.

The increase, subject to approval by the County Board of Supervisors, will increase fees for oil companies, power companies, gas stations and other businesses emitting pollutants and is expected to raise $119,000 annually, said Mike Villegas, a district spokesman.

"We're increasing fees and we're changing how we weight some of the pollutants," Villegas said.

Fees are being restructured to penalize companies more severely for emitting certain pollutants, such as those known to harm the ozone layer, and less for others.

Villegas said fees are charged on the basis of the number of pounds per hour of pollutants emitted, with different fees being assessed for different pollutants. He said most pollutants are assessed at the rate of $43 per pound per hour.

The increased revenues will be used to pay the salaries of seven new staff members and to finance new programs mandated by the California Clean Air Act of 1988.

There are now nearly 1,000 businesses with permits. Villegas said he expects that number to increase as more businesses move into the area and as county pollution standards become stricter.
