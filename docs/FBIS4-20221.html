BFN [Text] Frankfurt/Main (DPA) -- The M3 money supply rose by 4.8 percent in March compared with the fourth quarter of 1993. This is a projected annual rate of growth of money supply of 15.2 percent, the German Bundesbank announced on Tuesday [26 April]. The M3 money supply includes cash in circulation as well as sight deposits, time deposits with a duration of less than four years, as well as savings deposits. The Bundesbank is aiming for a money supply growth between 4 and 6 percent for 1994.
