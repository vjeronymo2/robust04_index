920513 FT 13 MAY 92 / Parliament and Politics: Archbishop warns on scriptures MINISTERS should not quote selectively from Christian principles in support of government policies if they wanted to have moral authority, Dr George Carey, the archbishop of Canterbury, said yesterday. In a rare speech in the House of Lords, Dr Carey added that it was against Christian teaching to emphasise individual rights without referring also to the individual's duty to society, and said that public morality could not be privatised. His speech comes soon after he aroused controversy with a sermon in which he criticised businessmen who awarded themselves big pay rises during the recession. 'All of us are commanded to love our neighbour and to subordinate ourselves to the service of God and of other people,' he said yesterday. 'To emphasise individual rights without acknowledging individual and social responsibilities to others is contrary to Christian teaching.'
