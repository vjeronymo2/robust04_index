BFN [Text] Riga, 22 April (LETA) -- The focal point of the meeting of the Latvian Government's head, Valdis Birkavs, with the delegation of the European Union [EU] was what issues Latvia wishes to discuss at the conference in Paris scheduled for late May and dedicated to the European stability pact. The governmental information agency reports that Valdis Birkavs confirmed his willingness to take an active part in the European stability pact. Speaking on the issues Latvia would like to consider during the coming conference in Paris, the prime minister said Latvia is interested to involve into the discussion with other countries of the issues of national identity preserving, national minorities' integration into a society and teaching a state language. Latvia would wish to view the possibility of launching the ombudsman institution. The issue of the repatriation of Russian military retirees is of the same importance and needs consideration in the context of the stability pact. All these matters Latvia would like to discuss at the multilateral talks. Mentioning the issue of the borders with Russia, Birkavs expressed doubt whether it is comprehensive to discuss the issue at the coming conference and stressed the fact that Latvia is forming its stand basing upon the peace pact of 1920 between the two states.
