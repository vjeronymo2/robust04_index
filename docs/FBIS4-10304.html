BFN [Text] Tegucigalpa, 10 Mar [date as received] (ACAN-EFE) -- Honduran Army Chief General Luis Alonso Discua has ordered the reinforcement of the nation's border with Nicaragua and ordered troops to answer "with force" any armed groups of Nicaraguan recontras. A clash with these groups left three people dead on 8 April. A Honduran corporal and two Nicaraguan guerrillas died in the clash between a recontra group and the 9th Infantry Battalion headquartered in Ojo de Agua in northeastern Honduras. Colonel Hernan Seaman, 9th Infantry Battalion commander, told ACAN-EFE: "At dawn, the border had an increased military presence because of Gen. Discua's orders. He ordered us to use force and to repel the recontras to guarantee the safety of people residing along the border." The officer said the armed Nicaraguans who entered the country "are criminal contra fighters who hid their weapons following their 1990 demobilization." He added: "They are currently attacking small businessmen, whom they targeted when the contras were in Honduras, and are murdering peasants, raping women, and raiding border towns." According to a Honduran military report, some 12-15 men brandishing "AK-47's, FAL rifles, and RPG-7 grenade launchers" opened fire on an Army patrol on 8 April in the area of Chilamates along the Nicaraguan border. Seaman pointed out that Cpl. Santos Armando Dominguez, 21, died during the clash, as well as two "recontras"; other attackers were wounded and returned to their country. Cpl. Rodriguez was buried today in the eastern city of Danli with honors corresponding to a "hero who died while carrying out his duties." He was posthumously promoted to sergeant. Seaman also said that two weeks ago, two recontras attacked a Buenos Aires woman, whom they murdered by shooting her in the mouth after stealing her television and radio. They later fought with one of the victim's sons, who wounded one of the recontras with a machete. The other recontra then shot the son. Col. Seaman added that Nicaraguan authorities were notified and that the two recontras were apprehended at a Nicaraguan border town. He said that thanks to the Nicaraguan authorities' cooperation, relatives of the slain woman will now be able to take the alleged perpetrators to court. The Nicaraguan Sandinist People's Army (EPS) has already been notified of the 8 April incident between Honduran military personnel and an armed group that, according to Seaman, could be linked to the recontras' 3-80 Northern Front led by Jose Angel Talavera, a.k.a. "El Chacal." The armies of the two Central American countries are carrying out joint exercises along their common border to counter movements by armed groups in the area.
