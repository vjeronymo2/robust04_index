BFN [Text] In an interview with INTERFAX Friday [13 May], Ukrainian Defense Minister Vitaliy Radetskyy rejected, in no uncertain terms, information on Ukrainian troop buildup on the Crimean Peninsula. Earlier, the Chairman of the Russian State Duma's Commission on Commonwealth Affairs Konstantin Zatulin said that Kiev sent mostly residents of its western provinces to serve in Crimea and that it had increased to 50,000 the strength of its Crimean contingent. Radetskyy did not know where Zatulin found the figure and said residents of all provinces were sent to serve in Crimea. According to Radetskyy, the Black Sea Fleet, police force, elite police units and paramilitary guards taken together would number 50,000.
