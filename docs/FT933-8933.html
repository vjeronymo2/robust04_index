930812 FT 12 AUG 93 / World Stock Markets (America): Dow sets new record as bondyields tumble Wall Street ANOTHER record-setting day saw US share prices soar yesterday afternoon on the back of tumbling bond yields, with strong gains in semiconductor stocks offsetting some losses in healthcare issues and mining stocks, writes Karen Zagor in New York. The Dow Jones Industrial Average added 10.62 to set a new record of 3,583.35. The more broadly based Standard & Poor's 500 was 1 higher at 450.46, while the Amex composite eased 0.32 at 438.87, and the Nasdaq composite firmed 1.69 at 718.77. Trading volume on the NYSE was more than 266m shares, and rises outnumbered declines by 1,057 to 856. In the absence of any significant economic reports yesterday, market movement was dominated by sector news. The semiconductor sector was bolstered by Tuesday's report of unexpectedly strong orders for chips. On the big board, shares in Motorola firmed Dollars 1 3/8 to Dollars 96 1/4 , Texas Instruments gained Dollars 1 to Dollars 77 1/2 after hitting a 52-week high earlier in the day, and Advanced Micro Devices rose Dollars 1 1/4 to Dollars 29 5/8 . Intel added Dollars 1 7/8 to Dollars 58 3/4 in Nasdaq trading. Shares in mining companies moved lower in tandem with falling gold prices. In Comex trading, the December gold price was quoted Dollars 5.70 an ounce lower at Dollars 378.70. American Barrick lost Dollars 3/8 to Dollars 25 1/8 , Newmont Mining fell Dollars 1 1/2 to Dollars 50 3/8 and Amax eased Dollars 1/8 to Dollars 24 5/8 . Among other featured issues, Eaton Corp added Dollars 3/4 to Dollars 45 3/8 , partly recouping its Tuesday losses of Dollars 1 1/4 following news that the company has entered a definitive agreement to buy Westinghouse Electric's distribution and control business for Dollars 1.1bn, plus the assumption of liabilities. Shares in Westinghouse slid Dollars 1/8 to Dollars 15 3/4 . In the tobacco sector, RJR Nabisco Holdings held steady in spite of reports in the Wall Street Journal that the FTC plan to recommend a ban of the company's popular Joe Camel advertising campaign. Shares in health maintenance companies tumbled after Donaldson, Lufkin & Jenrette cut its ratings on the group. Oxford Health Plans plummeted Dollars 8 1/4 to Dollars 62 3/4 , Ramsay-HMO dropped Dollars 2 1/8 to Dollars 28, Healthsource fell Dollars 4 3/4 at Dollars 37 1/8 and United Healthcare tumbled Dollars 6 3/4 to Dollars 52 3/4 . Chemical Waste Management lost Dollars 3/8 to Dollars 9 3/8 after the company said it was suspending its quarterly dividend payments because of uncertain market conditions. Equitable Companies climbed Dollars 4 to a 52-week high of Dollars 25 1/4 after the company turned in second quarter earnings of Dollars 47.5m against an operating loss of Dollars 18.8m a year earlier. Salomon Brothers lifted its rating on the stock to 'buy' from 'hold', reflecting the improved performance and predictions of improved sales momentum in the second half. Canada TORONTO stock prices closed mixed in busy trading, as the market eased after three days of solid gains. According to preliminary figures, the TSE-300 index fell 12.55 points, or 0.31 per cent, to 4,015.89, but advancing issues outnumbered declines 372 to 340. Volume was 66.431m shares, up from 63.038m shares on Tuesday, and trading value rose to CDollars 810.1m.
