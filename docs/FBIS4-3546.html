BFN [Text] Ashkhabad, April 22 (XINHUA) -- The delegation of Chinese entrepreneurs traveling with Premier Li Peng has signed with the government of Turkmenistan two agreements and four letters of intent here in the past two days. A three-party group of experts would be set up to conduct feasibility study on a pipeline project concerning China, Japan and Turkmenistan, which is considered to be rich in gas reserves, according to a letter of intent signed between the China national petroleum corporation and Turkmenistan's Ministry of Petroleum. China intends to participate in bidding for the contract of laying pipelines in Turkmenistan, the letter of intent says. According to an agreement signed between the China National Textiles Import and Export Corporation and Turkmenistan's Ministry of Agriculture, China will buy cotton totaling around 20,000 tons from Turkmenistan. Another agreement was signed between the China Council for the Promotion of International Trade and Turkmenistan's Chamber of Industry and Commerce on establishing cooperative ties. The other letters of intent cover construction, building materials, export of household electric appliances and setting up of iron and steel and aluminum plants.
