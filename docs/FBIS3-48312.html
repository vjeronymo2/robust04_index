Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] India and Japan have agreed to work together to bring about a nondiscriminatory Nuclear Nonproliferation Treaty [NPT]. The present treaty expires in 1995. The matter came up for discussion at the second round of bilateral talks on nuclear and disarmament issues between the two countries in Tokyo yesterday. The Indian delegation was led by the joint secretary in the Ministry of External Affairs, Mr. Dilip Lahiri, and the Japanese side by his counterpart, Mr. (Yukio Takiuchi).
