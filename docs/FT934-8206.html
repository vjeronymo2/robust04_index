931117 FT 17 NOV 93 / Papua New Guinea exchange Papua New Guinea could get its own stock exchange by the second half of 1994, the government announced yesterday, Nikki Tait writes from Sydney. The exchange would be based on Australian Stock Exchange rules and its formation would be subject to a minimum level of interest from brokers and dealers. However, 'Once a minimum of five members have agreed to participate the exchange will open for business,' Sir Julius Chan, PNG's finance and planning minister, said yesterday. The proposal emerged in the course the finance minister's budget speech, and Sir Julius added that taxation reforms were being introduced which would facilitate transactions on a PNG stock exchange. Reforms to the companies act and to foreign exchange regulations would be finalised by the next session of parliament.
