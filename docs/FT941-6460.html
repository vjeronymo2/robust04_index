940302 FT 02 MAR 94 / Brussels action over Macedonia The European Commission is today set to begin proceedings against Greece, which holds the presidency of the European Union, for closing off its border to trade with the former Yugoslav republic of Macedonia, David Gardner writes from Brussels. But officials in Brussels said there was division between commissioners pushing for immediate referral to the European Court of Justice - the option Sir Leon Brittan, trade commissioner, is understood to be pushing with support from Mr Jacques Delors, Commission president - and a slower track of reprimands, leading to longer-term court action.
