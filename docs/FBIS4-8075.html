BFN [Text] President of the Bosnia-Herzegovina Presidency Alija Izetbegovic today received U.S. President Clinton's special envoy Charles Redman regarding the most dramatic situation in Gorazde. The talks were also attended by Bosnia-Herzegovina Premier Haris Silajdzic and U.S. Ambassador to Bosnia-Herzegovina Victor Jakovich. The situation in Gorazde is critical indeed. We are examining the problem very seriously, and I hope the necessary steps will be taken. This is all I can tell you, Redman said following the meeting. Asked by reporters whether the ferocity of the fighting has subsided following the threat of air strikes unless an end is put to the offensive, Redman says: This was in the morning [as heard]. Since noon the situation has changed and become very serious due to Serb advances. Turkish President Demirel talked over the phone with Izetbegovic late this afternoon. President Demirel called President Izetbegovic to tell him that Turkey would do its best to initiate actions that would provide protection for the citizens of Gorazde.
