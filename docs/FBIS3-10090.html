Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [From the "National Newscast"] [Text] President Cesar Gaviria initiated an official visit to Japan today. Early this morning he met with leading financial officials to attract Far East investors to our country. President Gaviria's agenda for the first day of his official visit to Japan was tight and very productive. During a meeting with the Japanese financial sector, the Colombian president and the members of his cabinet emphasized economic and security advances achieved by the country in past years. They tried to convince Japanese investors of the benefits offered by Colombia in the business sector. On another topic, Gaviria asked Japan to reduce its tariff barriers and to reconsider its restrictions to the import of Colombian flowers. Japanese Government officials and media have viewed the Colombian president's visit favorably and expect it will yield positive results for both countries.
