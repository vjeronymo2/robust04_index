BFN [Text] In accordance with the decree signed by Georgian leader Eduard Shevardnadze on Friday, a national bureau to fight drug addiction and drug business has been created in Georgia. The bureau has been created on the basis of the department in charge for fighting drug addiction within Georgia's Interior Ministry in order to strengthen the fight against drug addiction and the drug business. A section in charge for supervision over the cases on illegal circulation of narcotics and psychotropic substances is being created in the republic's prosecutor's office "to observe legislation with respect to fighting drugs." The republic's customs department has been commissioned to create services to prevent transit of drugs through the border. The decree stresses that the difficult social-economic situation in Georgia, aggravation of the criminal situation, weakening of border control have facilitated the spread of drug addiction in Georgia. Spreading of drug addiction among young people arouses special concern. In addition, the drug mafia is showing an interest in Georgia as a country which is convenient for drug transit. Vice Premier Irakly Menagarishvili, chairman of the governmental commission on drug fighting declared at a conference on Friday in Tbilisi that there are about 100,000 drug addicts in Georgia now, more than 200,000 have died as a result of their addiction. He said that these are predominantly young people aged 16-20.
