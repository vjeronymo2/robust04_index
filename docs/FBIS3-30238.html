Language: Polish Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Warsaw, 31 Jan--The chairman of the Council of Ministers [Waldemar Pawlak] has made changes in top local state administration posts, the Government Press Office has reported. The prime minister has recalled the following provincial governors as of 31 January: Koszalin: Stanislaw Marian Socha Zamosc: Marcin Zamoyski The prime minister accepted the resignation of Miroslaw Styczen and recalled him from the post of Bielsko-Biala provincial governor as of 1 February. The chairman of the Council of Ministers thanked the former provincial governors for their work in the state administration. After consulting the local self-government assembly [Sejmik], the prime minister appointed Zygmunt Wielogorski provincial governor of Siedlce as of 1 February. Wielogorski is 45 and a law graduate of the Marie Curie-Sklodowska University in Lublin. [passage omitted] Since April 1992, he has been chairman of the provincial board of the Polish Peasant Party [PSL] in Siedlce and a member of the PSL Supreme Council.
