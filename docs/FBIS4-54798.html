BFN [Text] Bratislava, May 4 (CTK) -- The broad cooperation of the right-wing and center parties, as suggested by the Christian Democrats, could lead to "revival of the creatures from Jurassic Park" which do not belong to the Slovak politics any more, Deputy Chairman of the NDS-NA Anton Hrnko told journalists today. The NDS-NA (National Democratic Party-New Alternative), led by Ludovit Cernak, splintered from the Slovak National Party (SNS) in February. The NDS-NA has 5 deputies in the 150-member Slovak Parliament. "I am speaking about people who are not able to put up with the current political situation and who still long for the old federation (former Czechoslovakia)," Hrnko said. This could eventually damage the Slovak right, he added. On Saturday [30 April] the Christian Democratic Movement (KDH) put in an offer to its potential partners regarding "the open list of candidates up from a certain number." In addition to the NDS-NA, the offer relates to the Democratic Party and the Standing Conference of the Civic Institute. A coalition after the election (scheduled for September 30-October 1) with the left-leaning parties could be considered only "on the principles of national interest," Hrnko said, adding that his party was ruling out cooperation with the "authoritarian" parties, based on "principle of the personality cult," i.e. with Meciar's Movement for a Democratic Slovakia (HZDS) and Jan Slota's SNS.
