BFN [By Lo Kuang-jen and Danielle Yang] [Text] Miami, May 30 (CNA) -- Premier Lien Chan, leading a 21-member entourage, arrived in Miami Monday [30 May] for one-night stopover enroute to El Salvador. Lien was met at the Miami Airport by Huang Hsu-fu, director of the Coordination Council for North American Affairs' Office in Miami, and Economic Affairs Minister P. K. Chiang, who was earlier in Boston for a symposium. Lien will leave Miami for El Salvador Tuesday where he will attend the June 1 inauguration of newly elected President Armando Calderon Sol before flying to Guatemala June 2. On Tuesday afternoon, Lien will meet with Nicaraguan President Violeta Chamorro, who is in El Salvador for the inauguration. Later, he will attend a dinner party hosted by outgoing President Alfredo Cristiani. After the inauguration Wednesday, Lien will attend a luncheon hosted by President Calderon to welcome foreign dignitaries to his country. Lien is expected to meet with several presidents and premiers from Central American countries in the afternoon. His 21-member entourage includes Vice Foreign Minister Fang Chin-yen, Chairman of the Council of Agriculture Sun Ming-hsien, Government Information Office Director-General Jason Hu, and Deputy Governor of the Central Bank of China Yu Chen. Lien and his party are scheduled to return to Taipei June 7.
