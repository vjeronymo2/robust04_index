931130 FT 30 NOV 93 / Crown argued for multiple charges in Levitt case The Crown had wanted Mr Roger Levitt tried on a 'number of charges', Sir Derek Spencer, solicitor-general, said in the Commons yesterday. 'The charge upon which he was tried was a single charge,' Sir Derek said. He added: 'The Crown wanted him tried on a number of charges, and they argued strongly for the inclusion in the indictment of a number of charges. 'After the indictment had been reduced to a single charge, they argued strongly for the inclusion in the trial of a wide range of evidence. The judge ruled against them.'
