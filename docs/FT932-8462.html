930519 FT 19 MAY 93 / Parliament and Politics: PM rejects call for murder probe THE PRIME minister yesterday rejected repeated demands by Mr John Smith, the Labour leader, for a public inquiry into the Beverley Allitt child murder case. Mr Smith demanded: 'In view of the appalling negligence that has obviously occurred why is the government refusing to have a full public inquiry with the powers to require the evidence of witnesses on oath and compel the disclosure of all documents?'. But Mr Major stressed that Sir Cecil Clothier, a former health service commissioner who is to conduct an independent inquiry, believed people would be more willing to speak freely if the proceedings were not conducted in public.
