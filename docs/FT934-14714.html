931015 FT 15 OCT 93 / Policing insider dealing A CENTRAL enforcement agency should be created to police insider dealing, a seminar of stockbrokers and company finance directors was told yesterday, Tim Burt writes. The agency would also clarify guidelines on price sensitive information, said Mr Michael Ashe, a barrister and expert on insider dealing. It would combine the work of the Crown Prosecution Service, and the Securities and Investment Board and other self-regulating organisations. Mr Ashe's call was backed by Mr Richard Lines, investigations manager at the Stock Exchange's surveillance department, who agreed there was a need for such an agency. Speaking at a seminar in Birmingham, Mr Ashe said: 'There has been a problem with enforcement in the securities industry. It's time to have a single agency.'
