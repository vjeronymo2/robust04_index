940311 FT 11 MAR 94 / UK Company News: Lack of exceptionals helps Relyon Lower losses on discontinued activities and a lack of exceptional costs helped Relyon Group report 1993 profits of Pounds 3.15m pre-tax, against losses of Pounds 1.04m. The company makes beds, polyurethane foam and surveillance equipment. Turnover improved to Pounds 41.8m (Pounds 41.3m) including Pounds 459,000 (Pounds 1.51m) from discontinued activities. Operating profits on continuing activities fell to Pounds 3.65m (Pounds 4.03m). However, discontinued operations showed lower losses of Pounds 1.15m (Pounds 2.6m) and there was an exceptional credit of Pounds 750,000, against a charge of Pounds 2.61m. Earnings per share came out at 7.85p (losses 3.49p). A final dividend of 3.3p (3.15p) is recommended, for a total of 5.2p (4.9p).
