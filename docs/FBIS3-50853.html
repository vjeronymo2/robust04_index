Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [From "Caracol's 0600 to 0900" newscast] [Text] Government explanations notwithstanding, Colombians feel the presence of U.S. soldiers in Colombia is not good. This was seen in answers to Caracol's "Topic of the Day" question: 33.8 percent of those surveyed said it violates national sovereignty; 23.4 percent said Colombia has people to build a school or a health center, which are the projects the soldiers will work on; 18 percent felt the U.S. military presence is an indication that these soldiers might invade the country; 14.3 percent felt the goal is to install radar stations; and 10.4 percent stated the government must fully explain the U.S. military presence in the country.
