921119 FT 19 NOV 92 / Isles of Scilly tops GCSE table THE Isles of Scilly, the second-smallest education authority in England, topped the league tables although it only had 28 pupils taking GCSE exams this year, Bethan Hutton writes. Mr Philip Hygate, chief executive of Isles of Scilly council, said: 'It is not an aberration. It demonstrates the importance that education has on the Isles of Scilly. 'It is a historical and cultural issue.' The islands have five schools, only one of which caters for GCSE-level pupils.
