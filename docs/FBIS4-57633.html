BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities in Brazil monitored through 19 May. Average fuel prices were increased 18.11 percent on 14 May. After conversion of fuel prices into real value units, prices will be adjusted on a weekly basis until the circulation of the new currency on 1 July. (Sao Paulo O ESTADO DE SAO PAULO in Portuguese 12 May 94 Economy and Business Section p B1) Although the level of Brazilian exports went down during the first three months of the year, it recovered in April and totalled $3.6 billion. Compared to the same month last year, this is a 20.8 percent increase. (Sao Paulo FOLHA DE SAO PAULO in Portuguese 12 May 94 Section 2 p 3) Brazilian exports to Argentina from January to March totalled $891.4 million, which represents 70.5 percent of the Brazilian exports to Common Market of the South countries. Brazilian exports to Paraguay decreased from 20.3 percent in 1993 to 17.2 percent this year and totalled $217.9 million. Similarly, exports to Uruguay decreased from 15 percent to 12.3 percent during the same period and totalled $154.8 million. (Sao Paulo FOLHA DE SAO PAULO in Portuguese 12 May 94 Section 2 p 2) Federal tax revenue in Sao Paulo in April totalled $2.32 billion. It was 32 percent higher than the amount collected during the same month last year, and 11 percent higher than March's $2.09 billion. (Sao Paulo GAZETA MERCANTIL in Portuguese 12 May 94 p 5) The Brazilian stock market lost nearly $2.3 billion in March and April as a result of the political and economic instability in the country, as well as the increase in U.S. interest rates. Stock market expert Eduardo Moraes said the losses also can be attributed to the withdrawal of foreign investors from the stock market because they were responsible for about 30 percent of the financial operations in the field. Moraes added that stock market investors were frustrated by the end of the constitutional reform, the apparent failure of former Finance Minister Fernando Henrique Cardoso's presidential campaign, and the privatization program's slow implementation. (Madrid EFE in Spanish 1805 GMT 13 May 94) Treasury Secretary Murilo Portugal reported that as a result of the government having to pay two salaries to public employees in the same month, the budget had a 1.6 trillion real cruzeiros deficit in April. The overall Treasury revenue in April totalled 5.058 trillion real cruzeiros and expenditures totalled 6.692 trillion. (Sao Paulo O ESTADO DE SAO PAULO in Portuguese 14 May 94 Business and Economy Section p B6) According to Petrobras, Brazilian Petroleum Corporation, national oil and liquid gas production in April totalled an average 697,237 barrels per day, i.e., 17,604 more barrels per day than in March. Petrobras said natural gas production totalled 20.1 million cubic meters. (Sao Paulo FOLHA DE SAO PAULO in Portuguese 17 May 94 Section 2 p 2) The federal government granted 100 to 325 percent salary bonuses to Planalto Palace military employees. Civilian household chief Henrique Hargreaves said the increase seeks to level the bonuses paid to the military with those for the other public employees. Claudio Santana, director of the Federal Public Employees Union-Federal District, believes it is one more discriminatory act by the government. (Rio de Janeiro O GLOBO in Portuguese 18 May 94 p 9)
