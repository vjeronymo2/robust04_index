930517 FT 17 MAY 93 / Scottish bank's 'rollercoaster' warning on UK economy ECONOMISTS at Royal Bank of Scotland claim that lack of an economic policy framework has put the UK economy on a 'dangerous rollercoaster of boom and bust' which could damage long-term growth, Emma Tucker writes. Ms Marian Bell and former Treasury special adviser Mr Warwick Lightfoot say: 'With no proper framework of monetary control in place, and no appetite to tackle public expenditure and government borrowing, policy will eventually become unsustainable, leading to accelerating prices, and . . . could provoke a sterling crisis and a gilt strike.'
