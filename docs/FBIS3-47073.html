Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Kathmandu, Jan. 8 KYODO -- Japan announced a grant of 100,000 dollars here Saturday [8 January] to support the activities of the International Center for Integrated Mountain Development. The grant will be utilized for management and control of landslide hazards, the Japanese Embassy said in a press release. Earlier, Japan sent three experts to the center headquartered in Kathmandu. Set up in 1983 through the joint efforts of Germany, Switzerland, Nepal and the United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization (UNESCO), the center deals with issues of economic development and conservation of the environment in the Hindu-Kush and Himalayan region. The region covers Afghanistan, Bangladesh, Bhutan, China, India, Myanmar [Burma], Nepal and Pakistan.
