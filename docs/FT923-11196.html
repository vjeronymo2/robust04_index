920725 FT 25 JUL 92 / Trade talks plea at Madrid summit Leaders from Spain, Portugal and Latin America closed the working sessions of their summit in Madrid yesterday, expressing concern that failure of the Uruguay Round of trade negotiations would jeopardise economic reforms of recent years, writes Stephen Fidler. They also expressed a preoccupation with protectionist trends. Many countries in Latin America have unilaterally lowered trade barriers, but meet frustration in selling products to industrialised countries, including the EC.
