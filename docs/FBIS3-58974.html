Language: Turkish Article Type:BFN [Turhan Ipek video report] [Excerpt] [passage omitted] Israeli President Ever Weizman received Prime Minister Tansu Ciller at his hotel and conferred with her for some time. During the meeting, which lasted approximately 45 minutes, Weizman extended to Ciller a letter from Israeli Prime Minister Rabin inviting the Turkish prime minister to visit Israel. Foreign Minister Hikmet Cetin also attended the meeting. In a statement to TRT in connection with the meeting, Ambassador Volkan Vural, chief adviser at the Prime Minister's Office, said that Ciller informed the visiting president that Turkey supports the Middle East peace process and wishes to contribute to it. Vural noted that within this framework, Ciller touched upon the development of regional economic cooperation and the introduction of new institutions in the region. Vural added: [Begin recording] The prime minister underlined that new institutions are needed in the Middle East. She suggested that these institutions could encompass the development of economic cooperation among regional countries, the creation of joint projects, and the introduction of new structures that would meet the regional security needs of all the countries in this area. [end recording] According to Vural, Israel's water demands from Turkey were also discussed during the meeting. Agreement was also reached to expand cooperation in the struggle against terrorism. Weizman then received Motherland Party [ANAP] leader Mesut Yilmaz. In a statement after the meeting, Yilmaz reported that he told Weizman that, as the main opposition party, ANAP will support future moves to develop relations with Israel. Pointing out that there is still a significant distance to be covered in bilateral relations, Yilmaz expressed the hope that there will be an acceleration in the development of relations in light of the new political developments. President Suleyman Demirel will host a dinner in Weizman's honor at the Cankaya Mansion this evening.
