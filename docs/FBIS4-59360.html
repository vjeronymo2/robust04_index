BFN [Text] Cairo, 11 May (MENA)--Yemeni Foreign Minister Muhammad Salim Basindwah left Cairo this evening for Amman after a two-day visit to Cairo during which he met President Husni Mubarak. In a predeparture statement he said he conveyed to President Mubarak a verbal message from Yemeni President 'Ali 'Abdallah Salih in which he explained the ongoing events in Yemen. He said that what is happening in Yemen is a confrontation of the remnants of renegades and secessionists, a confrontation with the participation of all the people, led by their armed forces, which are loyal to constitutional legitimacy. He noted that the new Yemeni defense minister who took the constitutional oath yesterday hails from the southern governorates as does the new naval forces commander. Basindwah said he believed that the situation in Yemen has been decided and that what is going on will last no more than a day or two at most. He noted that during his meeting with President Mubarak he sensed considerable understanding, noting that the Yemeni people appreciate President Mubarak's great concern for Yemen's security and safety. He noted that he is going to Amman to convey a message to King Husayn of Jordan. His Jordan trip is part of a tour of Arab countries to convey verbal messages from President Salih to Arab leaders.
