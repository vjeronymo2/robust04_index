Language: Lithuanian Article Type:BFN [Text] Lithuanian Foreign Affairs Minister Povilas Gylys sent a letter to U.S. Secretary of State Warren Christopher. In his letter, the minister mentioned, once again, the meeting of the foreign ministers of the three Baltic states with the U.S. secretary of state in Riga and expressed his gratitude for the constant cooperation with Lithuania and other Baltic states by the United States. Lithuania, the letter reads, is especially interested in seeking the assistance and close cooperation with the United States in the areas of national security and defense and in reinforcing the economic reforms and democratic institutions. Although the last Russian soldier has left Lithuania, the letter goes on, we still remain in a security vacuum zone. The presence of foreign troops in Latvia and Estonia (?is a destabilizing factor in this region). The great concentration of Russian troops in the Kaliningrad region causes especially great concern. Therefore, Lithuania would like to draw the attention of international [passage indistinct]. Following the elections to the Russian parliament, Russian foreign policy may become more nationalistic and more aggressive toward the Baltic states. The minister stressed that Lithuania, while aspiring to integrate into the Western political, economic, and security systems, regards NATO as her future guarantee. Lithuania, free of foreign troops and national minority problems, is seeking to strengthen cooperation with NATO. We welcome the initiative of Partnership for Peace, hoping that it will stimulate cooperation from partnership to membership. Foreign investment is an important factor in the development of the economy of our country. In this context, investment by the United States is very significant to Lithuania.
