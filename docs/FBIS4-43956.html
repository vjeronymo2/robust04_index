BFN [Text] Ankara (AA) -- Foreign Ministry Under Secretary Ozdem Sanberk left Ankara today for Baghdad to conduct contacts on the Karkuk-Yumurtalik oil pipeline. Tomorrow, Ambassador Sanberk will proceed by land from Amman to Baghdad, where he will meet with Iraqi Foreign Ministry Under Secretary al-Qaysi. [name as received] In his news conference today, Foreign Ministry Spokesman Ferhat Ataman announced that Sanberk is also expected to meet with Iraqi Deputy Prime Minister Tariq 'Aziz and other government officials. Recalling that Sanberk previously held contacts on the subject in Paris, Washington, and New York, Ataman remarked that these meetings focused on resolving the issue at the UN Security Council with a technical resolution. Ataman noted that Sanberk will brief and exchange views with the Iraqis on the elements of such a resolution. Pointing out that Turkey has not received any official information with regard to Iraq's stand on the matter, Ataman said: "We hope that the oil pipeline, which constitutes a valuable and important investment, will be salvaged, and a humanitarian contribution will consequently be made to the Iraqi people within the framework of the United Nations."
