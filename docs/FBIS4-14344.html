BFN [Text] Tehran, April 26, IRNA -- The leader's representative for Afghan affairs, Hojjat ol-Eslam Ebrahimi, here Tuesday called on Afghan ulema to strive for ending the civil war in their country. Ebrahimi, who made the plea in a meeting with head of coordination council for northern Afghanistan and General Abdul-Rashid Dostam's special envoy Sirat Taleqani, said Afghan ulema can play an important role in improving current situation in Afghanistan and bringing unity to the feuding Afghan groups. "If they (Afghan ulema) fulfil their mission, clashes will end," reiterated the Iranian official. He said the clashes in Afghanistan have no result except the massacre of civilians. Unity and cohesion among all groups to elect just one leader or leadership council can put an end to the ongoing animosities, he added. Ebrahimi voiced the Iranian nation and leadership's sympathy with the situation of world Muslim, especially the Afghan people. Taleqani, who carried Dostam's written message to leader of the Islamic revolution, Ayatollah Seyyed 'Ali Khamene'i, thanked Iran for its humanitarian relief supplies to the Afghan people. The message expressed gratitude for Ayatollah Khamene'i's sympathies for the Afghan people, and called for continuation of Iran's assistance to the Afghan Muslims.
