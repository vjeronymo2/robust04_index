BFN [From the "24 Hours" newscast] [Text] Today, President Carlos Salinas de Gortari announced the creation of a commission of citizens, including some who are critical of or opposed to the government, to help investigate the murder of Luis Donaldo Colosio, presidential candidate of the Institutional Revolutionary Party [PRI]. In a brief statement made at the Vicente Guerrero Hall of the Los Pinos residence, the president noted that Mexico wants to know the truth. [Begin Salinas recording] At the same time, it is necessary to avoid a climate of mutual suspicion. Any inquiry is difficult; it takes time. We cannot, however, accept the fostering of a climate of doubt. We are determined to open the investigation so that citizens of the highest professional quality can participate. [end recording] The commission will comprise the following people: Carlos Payan, newsman and LA JORNADA daily editor; (Fernando Gomez Montt), National Action Party federal deputy and chairman of the Chamber of Deputies Justice Committee; Leonel Godoy, former Michoacan State attorney general, former deputy of the Party of the Democratic Revolution, and special prosecutor in the Obando and Hill case; lawyer (Agustin Santamarina); and Agustin Basabe, PRI deputy for Nuevo Leon State and former Colosio adviser. The president thanked these citizens for their willingness to participate in the task, which he described as delicate. He added that the Colosio Riojas family welcomes their participation.
