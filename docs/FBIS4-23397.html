BFN [Report by correspondent Alves Antonio in Lusaka] [Text] Some reputable sources contacted by Angola Radio Nacional here in Lusaka, who asked to remain anonymous, feel that the latest offer made by the government, which has already been presented to the National Union for the Total Independence of Angola [UNITA], reflects -- as has always been expressed by Angolan authorities -- the personal commitment of engineer Jose Eduardo dos Santos, president of the Republic, to reconciliation and national unity. These sources also say that the government offer should not in any way be viewed as a weakness. On the contrary, they add, it should be viewed by UNITA and its leader Jonas Savimbi, who have claimed to be champions of peace and democracy, as a realistic and beneficial stand for the nation. The figures contacted by Angola Radio Nacional have called on the international community to pressure UNITA to end the bloody war that will have unforeseen consequences for the country. Meanwhile, it is already known that, within the framework of the national sensitization campaign and pacification of hearts, the Voice of Resistance of the Black Cockerel will continue to broadcast in short wave, as an exception, for about 260 days once a peace agreement is reached in Lusaka. After that date and in accordance with the legislation in force, the UNITA radio will become a depoliticized radio station, broadcasting in adequate frequencies to be accorded to it.
