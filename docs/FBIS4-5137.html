BFN [Text] Langkawi, Mon -- The navies and marine police units of Malaysia and Thailand today began a five-day joint training exercise with the main objective of ironing out communication difficulties. The manoeuvres, the 21st in the Seaex Thamal series, will take place off Langkawi in Malaysia and Satun and Phuket Island in Thailand. About 300 personnel and eight vessels -- four from each country -- are taking part in the exercise launched by Malaysian Internal Security and Public Order director Ghazali Yaacob at the Bukit Malut Marine police base. The joint forces will also test their co-operation in patrolling formation, enforcement and search-and-rescue operations, as well as technical matters.
