April 28, 1990, Saturday, Home Edition

SEARCH FOR EXTRATERRESTRIALS

I must take issue with Konner's specious reasoning. He comes to the conclusion that "the likely result of contact is the most grotesque disaster that has ever befallen our species," the disaster being something on the order of what we humans have perpetrated upon animals within the melee of evolution.

Konner assumes, therefore, that if we were to send messages to the stars, the likely consequence would be that the listeners would dispatch ships to come to Earth and enslave us or kill us all.

His conclusion is derived from the assumptions that: Aliens have evolved exactly as we have; we represent a threat to their evolutional integrity, and they will conclude that the logical response is to come trillions of miles across space to deal with this threat. I hope people see this commentary for what it is -- misleading and ludicrous.

JEFF SWANSON

North Hollywood
