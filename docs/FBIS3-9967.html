Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Report by Alexandra Ortiz for the "TV Today" newscast] [Text] Starting today and continuing until 13 March, members of the police force, the Administrative Department of Security, and the Judicial Police and Investigations Directorate will conduct joint operations in Santa Fe de Bogota to counter guerrilla activities. Nearly 1,000 men from the state security organizations have been deployed to four areas in Santa Fe de Bogota. Elite units will remain posted at the entrances to the capital to prevent guerrillas from reaching downtown Santa Fe de Bogota. Raids will be conducted on the city's outskirts, where intelligence officials report the presence of subversives. There will be air and sea surveillance during demonstrations and political gatherings, and uniformed personnel will be in the 67 campaign centers 24 hours a day. [Begin Police Commander Enrique Montenegro recording] If we are able to accomplish this, we can guarantee relative security during the election campaign. [end recording] Public order forces will be deployed and security measures will be adopted in all cities to counter the guerrilla actions.
