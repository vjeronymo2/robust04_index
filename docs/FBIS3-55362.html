Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Riga, Jan 6 -- On January 5, the Latvian Ministry of Foreign Affairs released a statement saying that the leaders of the Baltic States and governments have repeatedly reiterated their common wish to strengthen their ties with NATO. The Foreign Ministry of the Republic of Latvia welcomes the initiative of the Republic of Lithuania to join the North Atlantic Treaty Organization (NATO). A positive solution of the question will be a serious step in strengthening the European and local security. The Ministry of Foreign Affairs of the Republic of Latvia appeals to all states of NATO to grant real guarantees of stability and security which can be offered by the North Atlantic Treaty Organization to the countries displaying their wish to integrate in NATO. The Ministry of Foreign Affairs of the Republic of Latvia approves the programme of NATO's active cooperation with the Baltic states. According to the Foreign Ministry, cooperation with the North Atlantic Treaty Organization and its separate members in political questions and the questions of security is an essential foreign priority of the Republic of Latvia in a gradual integration in political and military structures of NATO. The Latvian Foreign Ministry appeals to all states of NATO to promote the formation of necessary preconditions for the possibly quickest joining the North Atlantic Treaty Organization by Latvia.
