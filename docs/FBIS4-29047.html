BFN [Text] Tokyo, June 15 KYODO -- Japan and the United States will hold working group meetings on bilateral trade "Framework" talks in Tokyo later this week and next week to discuss government procurement areas, the Foreign Ministry announced Wednesday [15 June]. The working group meeting on telecommunications equipment will be held Thursday and Friday, and the meeting on medical equipment will be held June 22 and 23, both in Tokyo, the ministry said. Ministry sources have said vice ministerial-level meetings on the Framework talks between Deputy U.S. Trade Representative Charlene Barshefsky and Deputy Foreign Minister Sadayuki Hayashi will be held June 22 and 23 in Tokyo. Barshefsky is expected to arrive in Japan on June 21, the sources said. Japan and the U.S. agreed in May to restart the Framework talks after reaching common ground on the definition of objective criteria that it would be both quantitative and qualitative yardsticks instead of numerical targets for each of the negotiation sectors. Government procurement of medical and telecommunications equipment is one of the three priority areas along with automobile and auto parts, and insurance business.
