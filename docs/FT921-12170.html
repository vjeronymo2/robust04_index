920128 FT 28 JAN 92 / UK News (Employment): TUC will review Welsh economy THE Wales Trades Union Congress is to produce a review of the crucial issues facing the Welsh economy. It has commissioned academics from the University of Wales to produce reports on subjects such as low pay, training, unemployment and relations with Europe. The first report, on low pay, will be published early next month. Mr Kevin Crowley, Wales TUC chairman, said in Cardiff yesterday: 'Each of the publications will set out clearly the waste of talent and resources that has occurred over the last 13 years in Wales. This is the most comprehensive review of the Welsh economy we have undertaken and it will point out the shortfalls in government policy.' Mr David Jenkins, Wales TUC general secretary, said the WTUC had 'a duty to bring before the people of Wales a detached and thoughtful examination of the record of the present government. There are areas of policy where the government's record has been less than satisfactory. Our intention is to produce background information so that the people can decide for themselves whether they want change.'
