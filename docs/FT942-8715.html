940519 FT 19 MAY 94 / People: Top Management Programme The government's Top Management Programme, which brings together high fliers from the public and private sector, has a new programme director in Hugh Taylor, a Grade 3 at the Cabinet Office. Taylor, 44, is currently serving as head of the Cabinet Office Management Development Group. Previously he was at the Home Office, with responsibility for personnel management in the Prison Service. Taylor replaces Marianne Neville-Rolfe who has become director of the government's new north-west regional office. Neville-Rolfe's other job as head of the Civil Service College has also been filled recently by Stephen Hickey, formerly head of a planning and finance branch in the Department of Social Security. Hickey, 44, joined the DSS after leaving Oxford and has worked in the department ever since, apart from a secondment to Rank Xerox (UK). In 1988, he headed the team that recommended the creation of agencies to take over most of the department's executive functions.
