BFN [Excerpts] A session of talks was held at al-Mushrif Palace this morning between His Highness Shaykh Zayid Bin-Sultan Al Nuhayyan, President of the United Arab Emirates, and his brother His Excellency President Muhmmad Husni Mubarak of sisterly Egypt. [passage omitted] After the expanded meeting, a meeting confined to the two leaders was held. Egyptian Foreign Minister 'Amr Musa has stated that today's main session of talks between the two leaders dealt with the developments in the Gulf region and Arab developments in general, the Middle East peace process, the results of the two leaders' mediation to preserve Yemeni unity, the situation in Bosnia-Herzegovina, and bilateral ties in the economic and technical fields. In a statement to the EMIRATES NEWS AGENCY, Musa said that the two leaders noted their eagerness and efforts to preserve Yemeni Unity and implement the Yemeni Pledge and Accord Document. He noted that Badr Hammam, the assistant Egyptian foreign minister, is now in Sanaa to pursue contacts between Yemeni leaders in Sanaa and Aden. Answering a question on whether the talks dealt with arrangements to hold a quadrilateral Arab summit in Cairo to deal with the Yemeni situation, Musa said that preparations have not been made to hold this summit but the idea is still there. This summit would require good planning to ensure its success. Musa said that the two leaders expressed great interest in developments in Bosnia-Hrezegovina and stressed the need for the international community to end the Serb attack against the people of Gorazde. Musa praised the NATO ultimatum to attack Serb forces unless they withdrew from Gorazde and said that he considered this a good move in the right direction if it realizes the aim of stopping Serb attacks and establishing peace in Bosnia-Herzegovina.
