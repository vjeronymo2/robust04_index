BFN [Text] The Dutch Defense Minister Relus ter Beek has said he wants the UN Security Council decision to send a 5,500 strong force of African troops to Rwanda to be given preference over the French plan to send its own force to the African country. Mr. ter Beek's statement follows talks between him and the UN Secretary General Dr. Butrus Butrus Ghali in New York. Mr. ter Beek said he was not opposed to the French plan, but he preferred the option of a UN force because of the credibility the UN has. The UN Security Council has just been meeting to discuss the French plan to send a military force to Rwanda, but no decision is expected until later today.
