941012 FT 12 OCT 94 / International Company News: Saudi Hollandi Bank profits down 25% Saudi Hollandi Bank, which is 40 per cent owned by ABN Amro Bank of the Netherlands, reported net profit for the first nine months of 1994 of SR101.6m (Dollars 27m), down 25 per cent from the SR135.6m recorded in the same period last year, AP-DJ reports from Manama. The bank said there was a loss in investments of SR16.5m during the 1994 period, compared with a gain of SR60.4m last year. Operating revenues were up 30 per cent at SR740.4m, while operating expenses were 27 per cent higher at SR622.3m. The Saudi American Bank, which is 30 per cent owned by Citibank of the US, posted a 14 per cent increase in net income in the first three quarters of 1994, the bank said in a statement yesterday. Net income rose to SR784.26m during the nine months from SR690.57m a year-earlier.
