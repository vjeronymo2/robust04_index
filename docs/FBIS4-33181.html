BFN [Report by correspondent Froilan Arencibia from Cairo, Egypt] [Excepts] Participants in the 11th Ministerial Summit of the Nonaligned Movement [NAM] have enthusiastically welcomed the Latin American group's decision to nominate Colombia as host country for the upcoming heads of state summit. The nomination will be made official tomorrow during the closing session of the summit. It is expected to be easily approved. The news was also enthusiastically received by the members of the Cuban delegation who volunteered to cooperate with Colombia. In this regard, Foreign Minister Roberto Robaina affirmed that he trusts Colombia will contribute to the unity of the NAM and to revitalizing its role in the international arena. [passage omitted] Robaina continued his program of interviews, which today included the representative from Guyana and Egyptian Foreign Minister 'Amr Mahmud Musa. Robaina and Musa discussed ways to find common ground in the economic sphere and conducted an exchange of opinions on the NAM summit. Musa invited Robaina to conduct an official visit to Egypt and Robaina likewise invited Musa to visit Cuba. Later in the evening, Robaina met with businessmen, briefing them on the characteristics of Cuban economic policies.
