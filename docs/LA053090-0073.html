May 30, 1990, Wednesday, Home Edition

MORNING REPORT: PEOPLE WATCH

Henson Film Applauded: The late Muppet creator Jim Henson's movie "The Witches," about a dastardly scheme to turn children into mice, won him posthumous plaudits Thursday from London critics.

The movie stars Anjelica Huston as the head witch. "It leaves us a testament to the glory that was Jim Henson," Nigel Andrews wrote in the Financial Times. Annabel Llywelyn-Cowper, who described herself as high priestess of two English covens, wrote in the Independent that the film's effects were admirable but that it perpetuated the "tired old theme (of) the witch as loathsome creature hellbent on destroying young lives." It opens in the United States in the fall. GREG BRAXTON
