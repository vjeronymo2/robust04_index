BFN [Text] More than 3,000 people took part in the May-Day demonstration in Nalchik on Sunday. The demonstrators marched along the city's central street from the square commemorating the 400th anniversary of Kabarda's joining Russia over to Lenin Square. Among the demonstrators were Prime Minister of Kabardino-Balkaria Georgiy Cherkesov, Vice President Gennadiy Gubin and other leaders of the republic. The meeting in front of the government building was addressed by President of Kabardino-Balkaria Valeriy Kokov, who said that ethnic peace and agreement had been preserved and that May-Day celebrations were marked by optimism and confidence in the future. He also said that the embezzlement of national property had been prevented, the labor potential saved and social support provided for pensioners, disabled people and low-incomes groups. Speaking about the situation in the Russian Federation, Kokov said that the adoption of the new constitution last December had marked the beginning of a new process of sociopolitical stabilization. "The bitter fate of the Soviet Union, whose break up filled the hearts of million of people with pain, will not befell on the Russian Federation," he said. He also welcomed the signing of the Civic Accord agreement as a stabilizing factor which meets the interests of the people, who are tired of political confrontation. In conclusion, Kokov congratulated Christians on Easter and expressed hope that religious confessions will help strengthen the unity of Kabardino-Balkaria and preserve ethnic peace. After the meeting festivities were held involving professional and amateur performers.
