BFN [Excerpt] Madrid, April 14 (AFP) -- UN Secretary General Butrus Butrus-Ghali said Thursday [14 April] he would "not hesitate" to ask NATO forces to carry out new air strikes if UN peacekeepers were endangered in ex-Yugoslavia. The UN chief told a news conference: "We have the responsibility to protect the security of our United Nations troops in former Yugoslavia, and we will not hesitate to ask for an air support in case that the security of the United Nations forces, among which (are) Spanish troops, is in danger." He spoke alongside Spanish Foreign Minister Javier Solana after arriving here late Wednesday from Germany for a three-day official visit. Butrus-Ghali recalled that the UN Security Council had unanimously approved Resolution 836, which constitutes the legal basis for North Atlantic Treaty Organization air strikes. [passage omitted]
