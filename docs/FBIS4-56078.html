BFN [Report by Enes Musovic from Gorazde] [Excerpt] The long-awaited visit by General Michael Rose, commander of the UN forces in Bosnia-Herzegovina, to Gorazde yesterday lasted only 20 minutes. During that extremely short time General Rose spoke with (Ferid Buljubasic), commander of the Eastern Bosnia Operative Group of the Bosnia-Herzegovina Army in Gorazde. The issues touched upon in this blitz conversation were the cooperation of the military authorities in Gorazde with the units of the UN Protection Forces [UNPROFOR], and the problems connected with the implementation of the UN Security Council Resolution 913. The general returned to Sarajevo with his associates, while the people of Gorazde remain in the same situation as prior to his visit. The aggressor's soldiers, dressed in uniforms of so-called Serbian police, yesterday afternoon inflicted severe wounds on a 10-year-old boy in the city district of Bare with a M-84 machine gun.
