BFN [Report by ITAR-TASS correspondent Sergey Ostanin] [Text] Moscow 10 June TASS -- As a result of round-the-clock joint patrolling of streets in Dushanbe, the capital of Tajikistan, by the Republic's security, the Interior and Defence Ministry, as well as deployed there Russian military contingent units, over 30 firearms were confisticated, military sources in Dushanbe told ITAR-TASS today. As reported earlier, with the aim of stabilizing the situation in the Tajik capital, a decision was adopted at a session of the Coordination Council of the republic's law enforcement bodies on 6 June to take additional measures for fighting against crimes. The Russian 201 Army Division has set up posts, which are being jointly manned with police bodies. A mobile radio station is broadcasting the Russian division command's appeal to residents of the city to voluntarily surrender illegal arms in their possession. Pamphlets carrying text of the appeal are being distributed in the capital. The patrols have sweeping powers, including carrying out search of all types of vehicles, checking documents in possession of the citizens and confiscating illegally kept arms. Areas, where Russian servicemen and members of their families live, are being kept under special guard. It became known today that an attempt was made to attack the car of the 201st Divison commander during the evening checking of the posts on 7 June. The attacker was disarmed as a result of daring decisive action taken by driver Private Salavat Galimov. The would-be assassin, identified as Nazar Khoitov, born in 1967, was handed over to police.
