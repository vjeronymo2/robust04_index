April 4, 1990, Wednesday, P.M. Final

NEW YORK OVERNIGHT;

EX-GRIDDER MCCONKEY ANNOUNCES HOUSE RACE

EX-GIANT McCONKEY WILL RUN FOR CONGRESS: Just as we told you he would, former Giants football player Phil McConkey announced Tuesday that he's running for Congress as a Republican in New Jersey. He will seek the House seat vacated by Rep. Jim Courter, who ran unsuccessfully for governor.

McConkey, who is currently with the San Diego Chargers, told a gathering at the Somerville (N.J.) Inn that he will retire from football if elected.

"Some people have criticized me for running for Congress because I wasn't born in New Jersey," said the Vietnam veteran. "Well, I bet my grandmother in Italy and my grandfather in Ireland never dreamed I might someday be in Congress."

His opponent for the GOP nomination, Rodney P. Frelinghuysen III, traces his family to the Mayflower.

Phil, now living in Flemington, N.J., made the announcement with his father, Ed, and his girlfriend, model Diane Arabia, at his side.

HUD Secretary Jack Kemp, the ex-Buffalo Bills quarterback, urged him to run. "I told him that seats in Congress don't open up that often," Kemp said.

RON'S LETTER MAY FETCH SMALL FORTUNE: Back in '62, the note was just a friendly missive from a movie star to a frustrated playwright. But a lot has happened to Ronnie Reagan since he starred on TV's "G.E. Theater." And now, the Herman Darvick autograph people figure the note may fetch $1,800 at Thursday night's auction in the Sheraton City Squire Inn.

The letter was written in April, 1962, to a "Miss White," who'd sent him a play. "I must apologize for not getting back to you sooner about your play which you kindly forwarded," wrote Reagan. "Frankly, I was motivated by selfishness. Since Jan., we've been hanging motionless waiting to see if there would be a G.E. Theater next year. Now the ax has fallen. . . . I hope you won't mind but now that I know we aren't in the market, I'm taking your play to another producer whose show is going & who might be interested."

LITTLE THINGS MEAN A LOT: Hey, Broadway. Think small. At least musically, says Martin Charnin, because most of the big houses are occupied with hits. That's why Charnin, Jimmy Webb and Peter Stone are writing a "small piece for 10 to 12 actors" called "Love Me, Love My Dog." Marty will direct, Peter will write the book and lyrics, and Jimmy will do the score.

"Peter found an old play by Bill Manhoff, who wrote 'The Owl and the Pussycat,' " Charnin said.

"It's a very strange, exotic and bizarre little adventure. It'll be done sometime in our immediate lifetimes but all things being equal, that'll be late next season."
