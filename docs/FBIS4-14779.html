BFN [Text] The position of Armenia on a settlement in Nagorno Karabakh remains the same. Armenia stands for stable guaranteed armistice, lifting of the blockade of communications and only after this negotiations on the political status of Nagorno Karabakh, the Armenian Supreme Soviet's Chairman, Babken Ararktsyan, stated after the meeting between Armenian President Levon Ter-Petrosyan and the CIS Inter-Parliamentary Assembly's mission. The CIS mission is led by Kyrgyz Parliamentary Speaker, Meditkan Sherimkulov. Ararktsyan was satisfied with the negotiations with the CIS mission held by the Armenian president and the Armenian Supreme Soviet. Ararktsyan stressed that it was to give any evaluations to the negotiations first before the working groups finished discussion on the issue. Ararktsyan suggested that the mission's activities in Nagorno Karabakh would be of great importance. The mission will leave for Nagorno Karabakh on April 2. The final stage of the mission's activities provides for trilateral negotiations between Armenian, Azerbaijani and Nagorno Karabakh representatives. Ararktsyan did not rule out that the issue might also be discussed at the CIS summit in Moscow planned for April 15. Sherimkulov told journalists on his way to Yerevan that the meeting with Azerbaijani President Geydar Aliyev in Baku on Friday morning had justified his expectations. "I hoped for a frank conversation and we had it," Sherimkulov emphasized. Commenting on the changes introduce to the schedule (the mission had intended to leave Baku for Stepanakert), Sherimkulov announced that the Karabakh party had failed to guarantee security for the mission when crossing the line of Armenian-Azerbaijani confrontation. Sherimkulov said the mission would try to go to Karabakh from Armenia.
