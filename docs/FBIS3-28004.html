Language: Korean Article Type:BFN [AFP/YONHAP from Vienna] [Text] The International Atomic Energy Agency [IAEA] disclosed on 1 February that the possibility of completing inspections of North Korea's nuclear facilities before the IAEA Board of Governors meeting slated for 21 February are becoming thinner and thinner. IAEA officials pointed out that even if an inspection team leaves for Pyongyang at the end of this week, there is not enough time for even submitting a preliminary report to the IAEA Board of Governors meeting. The IAEA officials said that North Korea has assumed a firm stance on a third round of IAEA-North Korea negotiation for nuclear inspections. They stressed, however, that the IAEA has not yet given up all hope and that the door is still open for North Korea.
