Language: English Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Brussels, Jan 10 (AFP) -- NATO must expand gradually into East Europe with attention to the "special" importance of Russia and without undermining democratic reform there, British Prime Minister John Major said here Monday [10 January]. [passage omitted] French President Francois Mitterrand also cited the need to avoid "countering" the development of democracy in Russia. He described the plan for gradual expansion endorsed by NATO leaders, known as Partnership for Peace, as "reasonable" and said it was "a form of real guarantee" for the security needs of the new democracies in East Europe. A spokesman for Mitterrand said former Soviet states now worried about the potential of renewed Russian expansionism should interpret the partnership as a promise that "now war could threaten their territorial integrity and sovereignty."
