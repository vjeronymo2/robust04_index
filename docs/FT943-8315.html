940818 FT 18 AUG 94 / Taiwan officials jailed A Taiwan court yesterday handed down prison sentences to six officials from the Directorate General of Telecommunications and acquitted eight others in a highly publicised bidding scandal involving Dollars 220m (Pounds 142m) in cellular telephone contracts, Laura Tyson reports from Taipei. Mr Meng Chih-chung, former director of the DGT's Long-Distance Telecommunications Administration, was sentenced to seven years in jail for his role in the affair. He was convicted of forging documents and granting illegal favours to Ericsson of Sweden. Five other directorate officials received sentences of five to six years on similar charges. Ericsson said yesterday it had always rejected the bribery allegations.
