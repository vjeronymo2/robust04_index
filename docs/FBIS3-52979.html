Language: English Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Asyut, Egypt, Jan 10 (AFP)--Suspected Islamic militants shot dead a police officer and his bodyguard in Asyut in southern Egypt on Monday [10 January], police said. [passage omitted] Lieutenant Colonel Mustafa Khalil had just left home when he was gunned down by several suspected militants, who then fled the scene, police said. His bodyguard, Ahmad Mahmud, died of bullet wounds in hospital an hour later. Security forces arrested 14 suspected Muslim fundamentalists in southern Egypt on Sunday charged with involvement in recent attacks on policemen in Asyut, 380 kilometres (236 miles) south of Cairo, the AL-AHRAM daily said in its Monday edition. The detainees included Hamadah Nassar, who heads the outlawed al-Jama'ah al-Islamiyah in Abnub in Asyut province, as well as a key member of the group's military wing, 'Adil 'Abd-al-Ghani 'Abd-al-Rahim, the official paper said, citing police. Meanwhile in the Mediterranean port city of Alexandria security forces arrested an Islamic militant as he was preparing to set fire to a cinema, police said. Mu'tamid Mahrus was arrested on Sunday after an employee of the Laila cinema saw him placing a bag under his seat, which was found to contain three bottles filled with inflammable liquid. Mahrus, a grocer and known Islamic militant, later admitted planning to set fire to the cinema during a performance, police said. [passage omitted]
