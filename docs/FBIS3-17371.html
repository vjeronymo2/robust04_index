Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Riga, Mar 24, BNS -- Russian army troops stationed in Latvia owe Latvia 9 million 865 thousand lats (nearly 17 million U.S. dollars) for electricity, different taxes and services, announced the Latvian Controle Office for Russian army withdrawal. The debt for electricity equals 436,556 lats (nearly 763,000 U.S. dollars). The office says there are nearly 7,000 Russian militaries in Latvia at present, and 190 army units are stationed in 242 military objects. As many as 36 of these objects are Russian ships and other floating objects, and the number of army cars and trucks equals 827. Besides, there are 360 wagons with munition in Cekule and Garkalne ammunition depots. By observing all security measures, it is possible to withdraw from Latvia approximately 60 wagons a month. The office head, Ilgonis Upmalis, said Wednesday at a press conference that the Russian army leadership has disbanded 400 army units in Latvia without informing the Latvian authorities if the disbanded units' officers and their families left Latvia or remained here. The same can be said about the Russian Northwest Army Unit. Upmalis claimed he had information that the Russian army leadership has ordered to disband all army units still stationed in the territory of Latvia. Upmalis noted that the Russian Northwest Army Unit refused to make known the Russian army withdrawal schedule, and, when signing agreement on army withdrawal, the schedule would be unilateral and the interests of Latvia would be disregarded. The office also announced that Russia officially passed over to Latvia several military objects: Alksnis and Birjuzov Military school in Riga, Daugavpils Military school, and Jekabpils and Lociki airports. As many as 40 Latvian commercial structures have bought the former Russian army military objects.
