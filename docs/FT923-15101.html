920703 FT 03 JUL 92 / Japan urges US to keep forces in Pacific THE US must maintain armed forces in the Pacific as a 'stabilising factor' for the peace and prosperity of the region, Japanese prime minister Kiichi Miyazawa said on his first state visit to Washington. In a speech yesterday, Mr Miyazawa sought to shift the spotlight from contentious trade issues to US-Japanese economic and political partnership in the region. He said the most pressing regional concern was a reduction of tension on the Korean peninsula, where a North-South dialogue for reconciliation must be supported by China and Russia as well. Mr Miyazawa hoped to settle his differences with Russia over Japan's former Northern islands with Russian President Boris Yeltsin. 'I can easily imagine how the Russian people, who have long been taught that these islands are Soviet territory, may face a strong psychological block in coming to terms with the return of these islands to Japan,' he said. 'However, for our two countries to act as if the problem did not exist would only complicate the matter further.'
