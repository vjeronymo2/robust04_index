Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Fuzhou, February 9 (XINHUA) -- Local party committees and governments should pay more attention to the work in rural areas and take good care of farmers' welfare, said Wen Jiabao, alternate member of the Political Bureau of the Central Committee of the Chinese Communist Party (CPC). In a recent tour of the eastern part of Fujian Province, Wen, also a member of the Secretariat of the CPC Central Committee, said that stable economic and social development in rural areas is crucial to deepening the reforms and achieving a continuous, rapid and healthy development of the national economy. From February 5-9, Wen Jiabao visited farmers in poverty-stricken mountain areas in Fujian Province. He held discussions with them about their living conditions and production. He stressed the need to keep consistent the party's basic policy and deepen the reforms in rural areas so as to bring farmers' initiative into full play, promote the development of rural economy and increase farmers' incomes. He said that China is a large agricultural country and the rural population accounts for 80 percent of the total. The leaders at various levels in rural areas should have farmers firmly in mind and they should take care of their living conditions and rely on them, he said. At present, efforts should be made to increase farmers' incomes and continue to reduce their burdens, he said. He also talked about specific measures for supplying farmers with necessary production materials and for achieving a bumper harvest this year.
