Language: German Article Type:BFN [Report signed "ker/hh": "Roman Herzog Accepts Candidacy"] [Excerpt] Karlsruhe/Munich -- The president of the Federal Constitutional Court, Roman Herzog, officially declared his willingness to run for the office of federal president. According to the court, he sent identical letters to Christian Democratic Union [CDU] Chairman Helmut Kohl and Christian Social Union [CSU] Chairman Theo Waigel, informing them that he wants to run for president. Herzog had said for weeks he would not make his final decision until the CDU/CSU leadership had nominated him officially. On Monday evening [24 January], the CDU and CSU leaderships agreed to ask the 59-year-old Bavarian to run for president, to be elected by the Federal Assembly in Berlin on 23 May. Until then, Herzog wants to remain a constitutional judge and president of the court. [passage omitted].
