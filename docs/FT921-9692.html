920211 FT 11 FEB 92 / Arts: Today's Television In the third of its 'Fin de Siecle' conversations, Without Walls asks whether the cold war has given way to a new conflict between east and west in which Japan will move to the top of the heap and use the USA as its bread basket and Europe as its boutique. Some people, such as William Rees-Mogg, have moved beyond that scenario to worry about the one after, when the economic capital of the world will be not Tokyo but 'Hong Kong or some other Chinese city' (9.00 C4). ITV's documentary series Murder Squad (9.00) is concerned with an explosion at a hairdresser in London's New Cross. That is followed by the first episode in a new batch of That's Love (9.30) a rather superior comedy starring Jimmy Mulville and Diana Hardcastle as a middle class couple dealing with the vicissitudes of marriage in the 90s. Today Tony Slattery appears for the first time, playing a marriage guidance counsellor. Viewpoint 92 tells the story of journalist Geoffrey Lean who went into hospital for a minor operation last year and woke three weeks later paralysed and unable to speak. He says 'I believe that if my case had been decided on a cost-benefit basis I wouldn't be here to tell the tale' (10.40 ITV).
