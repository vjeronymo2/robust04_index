Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Report by Alvaro Ayala] [Text] During a ceremony in which Police Director General Octavio Vargas Silva submitted the final report on the modernization of his organization, President Cesar Gaviria took the opportunity to say he never intended to offend the State Council. He then said he considers the debate generated by the presence of U.S. troops in Juanchaco Ladrilleros and Bahia Malaga to be over and done with. [Begin Gaviria recording] Yes, undoubtedly, in the wake of the State Council's statement yesterday, what could be called a controversy has now ended. I did not intend to offend the State Council, and I do not believe I did offend the council. What I did was defend a legal position -- an interpretation of the Constitution -- and to respect Colombia's international treaties and their development, which I consider essential. I consider this essential not for the president or his position, but for the professionalization of the Armed Forces and for international cooperation in efforts to counter drug trafficking. [end recording] During the same ceremony, former Police Director General Miguel Antonio Gomez Padilla for the first time appeared in public wearing civilian clothes.
