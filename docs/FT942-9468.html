940516 FT 16 MAY 94 / Holiday companies escape check-ups A shortage of resources is hampering investigation of holiday companies accused of providing travellers with inadequate financial protection, says the body representing trading standards officers. Mr Alan Street, chief executive of the Institute of Trading Standards Administration, said in a letter to the Association of Independent Tour Operators (Aito) last month that the pressure of other work meant officers had to devote less time to holiday companies. Under a European Union directive introduced last year, trading standards officers are responsible for checking that holiday companies have made arrangements to ensure money can be returned to customers in the case of corporate collapse. Mr Street wrote: 'In some areas of the country, the enforcement of the Package Holiday Directive is not seen as the most pressing of demands and resources are committed to other matters such as sales of unclassified videos, unsafe food and consumer products, and attention to the statutory demands required by more than 50 acts of parliament, together with the associated 900-plus sets of regulations.' Aito had written to Mr Street in July last year complaining about 75 tour operators allegedly operating illegally by not providing proper financial protection. Earlier this month, Mr Street again wrote to Aito saying it appeared there were few operators which did not have adequate financial protection and that others had stopped selling package holidays. But he added: 'The greatest difficulty is establishing, without personal visits, which in themselves are time-consuming and may provoke some irritation on the part of the recipient operator, what arrangements are in being.'
