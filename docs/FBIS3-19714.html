Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] In answer to the UN's request for more troops on humanitarian missions in Bosnia, a total of 145 Spanish blue helmets are traveling to the area between Monday [21 March] and Tuesday to reinforce the 1,200 members of the Madrid Group. In April the Canaries Group will replace the Madrid one, which has been stationed in Bosnia since autumn. A radar detector unit left Madrid on Monday. It comprises 25 men, 15 vehicles, and an antimortar radar. Its mission is to monitor the cease-fire in the city of Mostar. On Tuesday 120 men will leave Seville. Once deployed in the conflict area, they will reinforce the monitoring operations and will be involved in the rebuilding of Mostar.
