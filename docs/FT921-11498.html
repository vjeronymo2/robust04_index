920131 FT 31 JAN 92 / International Company News: Net income flat at top insurer MARSH & MCLENNAN, the world's biggest insurance broker, yesterday turned in stagnant fourth-quarter and full-year earnings, writes Karen Zagor. For the three months to December 31, the company had net income of Dollars 54.2m, compared with Dollars 54.5m a year earlier. However, fewer outstanding shares helped earnings per share rise to 75 cents in the 1991 quarter from 74 cents the previous year. The 1991 figures include extraordinary pre-tax gains of Dollars 10m. Revenues were only slightly lower, at Dollars 665.9m against Dollars 668.5m in the 1990 quarter. For the whole of 1991, net earnings were Dollars 305.5m, or Dollars 4.18 a share, on revenues of Dollars 2.78bn, compared with earnings of Dollars 304.1m, or Dollars 4.15, on revenues of Dollars 2.72bn.
