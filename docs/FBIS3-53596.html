Language: English Article Type:BFN [From the "News at Ten" program] [Text] His Royal Highness Crown Prince Hasan received a U.S. economic delegation led by Commerce Secretary Ronald Brown. Mr. Brown conveyed to the regent a message from President Bill Clinton addressed to his majesty the king. The U.S. secretary met other officials for economic talks and the negative economic effects of the blockade on the port of al-'Aqabah. He said that the removal of trade barriers and the lifting of the Arab boycott of Israel would help speed the economic growth in the region. At the Royal Court meeting His Royal Highness Crown Prince Hasan told the American Secretary of Commerce that international trade should not be limited to the sole purpose of trade but also take into consideration human needs. The regent said that a humanitarian criteria in trade should be introduced to guarantee the rights of states and the individual, particularly with regard to the Middle East. His royal highness said a lot has to be done towards regional economic cooperation. [passage omitted] Commenting on calls for lifting the Arab boycott of Israel, the regent said: [Begin Hasan recording] This is a question that has to be discussed within the context of peace making clearly. But, I was very happy during the MEED [Middle East Economic Digest] conference in London last week to emphasize the importance of the Middle East as free trade area. This is a goal to which we all have to work with (?conscience) and a great deal of understanding. And I think that this understanding is developing as a result of these personal contacts. [end recording] Prime Minister 'Abd-al-Salam al-Majali also discussed with the American official economic ties and the inspection of shipping into the port of al-'Aqabah and the need to lift the blockade. [Begin Brown recording] I now am increasingly sensitive about the impact that it's having on the Jordanian economy and I intend to pursue those discussions when I get back to Washington. There certainly ought to be ways so as to be in compliance with United Nations resolutions but in ways that do not debilitate the economy of Jordan. [end recording]
