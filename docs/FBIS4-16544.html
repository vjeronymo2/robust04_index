BFN [By unidentified ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondent] [Text] Moscow April 17 TASS -- Talks between U.N. special envoy Yasushi Akashi and Bosnian Serb leader Radovan Karadzic, which got under way in the Sarajevo suburb of Pale with the participation of Russian mediator Vitaliy Churkin, are to result in pullback of troops from around Gorazde, consolidation of the U.N. designated "safe area" around the town and putting the areas under U.N. control, Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev told reporters upon returning from Belgrade. Earlier in the day, Kozyrev met with the Serb leadership to set priorities in the proposed settlement in Bosnia-Herzegovina. Serb President Slobodan Milosevic assured the Russian diplomat that Serbs were ready to enter talks to find a peaceful solution to the crisis around Gorazde.
