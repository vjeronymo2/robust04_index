Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [From "the Voz Andes Newscast"] [Text] The Ecuadoran Foreign Ministry will take whatever diplomatic measures are warranted with regard to U.S. Ambassador Peter Romero's remarks on the Peruvian-Ecuadoran territorial dispute. The Foreign Ministry made this statement after Foreign Minister Diego Paredes met with the U.S. diplomat, who was summoned to explain the remarks he made in an interview published by the Guayaquil press. The diplomat said that the Ecuadoran people are not prepared to recover their territory by the use of arms. The official Ecuadoran statement after the meeting limited itself to reporting that the ambassador had been called for consultations. Paredes indicated that his office will adopt whatever diplomatic measures are warranted and will issue a communique in this regard. The Ecuadoran foreign minister underscored that Ecuador is very respectful of the internal situations of other countries and expects the same treatment.
