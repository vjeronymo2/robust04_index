BFN [Text] Tashkent, 4 June (YONHAP) -- South Korea and Uzbekistan will promote partnership and cooperation by combining Korea's development experience and industrial technology and Uzbekistan's rich natural resources and industrious labor. This was agreed on in a summit between Presidents Kim Yong-sam and Islam Karimov in the state guest house Saturday afternoon, President Kim's spokesman Chu Ton-sik said. President Karimov, welcoming South Korean corporate investment in his country, agreed to provide as much legal and administrative support as possible to Korean corporations investing in Uzbekistan. Kim said South Korea would support the Central Asian country's economic development program, while pursuing a policy to promote economic cooperation between the two countries, according to the spokesman. On Sunday, Kim is to visit a farm run by an ethnic Korean near Samarkand, the old capital of Uzbekistan, and attend a reception for ethnic Koreans hosted by the Tashkent province governor.
