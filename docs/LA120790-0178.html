December 7, 1990, Friday, P.M. Final

LIZ SMITH: CBS' HEWITT TELLS WHY HE WALKED OUT ON PRIVATE PARTY

COMES DON HEWITT to give me his feelings on the recent teapot tempest in which he departed from a private party after being lectured by fellow guests on his and "60 Minutes" lack of "objectivity" in the airings of material on the Temple Mount killings in Jerusalem.

"I am not thin-skinned," the veteran CBS producer says. "Any viewer can rake me over the coals and tell me anything. But why should I stand around at a party and let myself be lectured by my competitors -- Barbara Walters and Mort Zuckerman? How objective are they? Zuckerman was named last Monday in an ad in the New York Times as one of the '100 Defenders of Jerusalem.' How objective does that make him as the publisher of U.S. News & World Report? Barbara Walters thinks it is OK to zero in on me in public and tell me what is proper in news coverage. Has she ever taken her own objections to the head of ABC, Roone Arledge, or addressed them to her own network's bureau chief in Tel Aviv?"

Why, I asked Hewitt, didn't you simply tell these people to blow it out their ears? "It was a nice private party. I didn't want to make a scene with them and I didn't want to stay there and be lectured. These competitors of CBS are hardly paragons of objectivity themselves."

THE DIVINE actress Margot Kidder has called me from Vancouver to say that the story in the New York Post had the basics about her accident down right but that "I am NOT paralyzed. This kind of thing is frightening enough without any wild excess."

Margot was injured in a car accident while making a TV film for Nelvana Co. The movie was to appear on the USA Network. Margot says she tried to go on working but finally collapsed and temporarily lost the use of her legs. She is in therapy every day, using flotation tanks and acupuncture.

Margot says she didn't exactly get a fair shake from Nelvana. "I never heard a single encouraging or nice or concerned word from either Nelvana or the USA Network. They had the nerve to say I was drunk and on drugs, then that I was faking my illness. I wasn't using anything, and it would be impossible to fake my CAT scans and spinal taps. So there is a large lawsuit in the offing."

We wish Margot all the best and a speedy continuing recovery.
