BFN [Text] Tokyo, April 25 KYODO -- Japan's Toshiba Corp. and the U.S.-owned National Semiconductor Japan Ltd. and Nippon Motorola Ltd. announced Monday they will start sample shipments in late may of a family of low-voltage semiconductor they have jointly developed. The companies said the pure three-volt CMOS (complementary metal oxide semiconductor) logic family with five-volt tolerant inputs and outputs is the first of its kind in the world. The devices operate at pure three volts or at both three and five volts in the same design without sacrificing performance, the companies said. They said the devices are designed for use in notebook personal computers, portable personal information tools and other portable devices.
