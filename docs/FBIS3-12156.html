Language: Hebrew Article Type:BFN [Text] Two Israelis were wounded by IDF [Israel Defense Forces] gunfire a short while ago near the village of 'Anabta in the Nabulus area. Our correspondent Moti Amir reports that initial reports indicate that IDF soldiers shot at an Israeli truck which drove through the roadblock they were manning. The truck's two passengers were wounded. One of the passengers was seriously wounded while the second man sustained light to moderate injuries. After being treated at the scene by IDF medical teams they were rushed to the hospital. The riots in the Bedouin town of Rahat in southern Israel are growing worse. A short while ago, the rioters hurled firebombs at policemen and at the Center for Bedouin Heritage. Our correspondent Nisim Qeynan reports that the police commissioner is currently meeting with local notables in an attempt to defuse tensions but apparently no agreement has been reached on a reduction of police forces and the evacuation of the police station. The roads leading to the eastern entrances to Beersheba are still closed because of rioting and stone throwing incidents. Following consultations with the police commissioner, Roni Milo, the mayor of Tel Aviv and Yafo, this evening announced that all the women and the youths arrested in the course of the past two days of rioting in Yafo would be released this evening. The mayor promised that if things remained quiet, the rest of the detainees would be released. Our correspondent Dani Gur-Arye reports that the mayor was speaking at a mourning ceremony at the Muslim Club in Yafo. At the ceremony, many people complained that the police had employed excessive violence. Councilman Khalid Habub asserted that the Arab residents of Yafo would accede to the mayor's request to show restraint if all the detainees were released. A firebomb was hurled tonight on Me'ir Naqar Street, in Jerusalem's Armon Hanetziv neighborhood. No one was hurt and no damage was caused. A child was lightly injured by glass shards on Hizma junction, near Pisgat Ze'ev on the outskirts of Jerusalem, when youths hurled rocks at a No. 25 bus. In al-Tira, the police arrested eight youths this evening. They are suspected of throwing stones at police patrol cars and setting tires on fire. Our correspondent Yafa Glick reports that a total of 38 youths from al-Tayyibah, Kafr Qasim, and al-Tirah were arrested this evening by the Sharon District police.
