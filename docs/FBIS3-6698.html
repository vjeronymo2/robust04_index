Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] New York, March 16 KYODO -- Japanese Ambassador to the United Nations Yoshio Hatano stressed anew Wednesday [16 March] that Japan is prepared to become a permanent member of the United Nations Security Council (UNSC). During a closed door session of the UN working group on the reform of the Security Council, Hatano said he is taking "this opportunity to emphasize once again Japan's firm determination in this regard." Japanese diplomatic sources said Japan expects to have veto power as a permanent security council member, although Hatano did not elaborate on the veto issue during the meeting. Hatano also proposed an increase in the number of nonpermanent members of the Security Council from the current 10. The five permanent members are Britain, China, France, Russia and the United States. Britain has joined the United States in expressing support for permanent UN Security Council seats for Japan and Germany.
