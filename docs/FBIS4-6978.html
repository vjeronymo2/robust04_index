BFN [Text] Tokyo, April 26 KYODO -- Newly elected Prime Minister Tsutomu Hata early Tuesday regretted the decision by the Social Democratic Party (SDP) to leave the governing coalition and appealed to the party to give up the decision. However, Hata defended the formation of the 130-legislator parliamentary group which excludes the SDP, the largest party in the coalition, drawing the latter's ire, saying, "The formation does not constitute any betrayal" of the socialists. In a press interview at the prime minister's official residence, Hata said such a parliamentary convergence is necessary to enable Japan to deal with the difficult times ahead, explaining, "unless we have such a group, coalition parties will not be able to cope with the difficult problems and act in solidarity."
