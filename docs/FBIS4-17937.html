BFN [From 29 April PREVALEHT, p.3; "News Bulletin" No. 82/94] [Text] The peacekeeping company currently being formed in Estonia will be ready to participate in the international peacekeeping forces joint exercise in the Netherlands this autumn, General Aleksander Einseln told the NACC [North Atlantic Cooperation Council] session yesterday. General Einseln has prepared a draft resolution on the forming of an independent peacekeeping infantry company and submitted it to the government. The Defence Ministry will have to prepare the budget and the listing of personnel for the company. The Foreign Ministry announced that the definite plan for Estonia's participation in the "Partnership for Peace" NATO cooperation programme will be completed by mid-May. The military resources to be turned over under NATO control need to be elaborated still. The government will have to approve the budget of the programme after which the plan will be submitted to NATO HQ.
