940330 FT 30 MAR 94 / International Company News: New World Development ahead 37% New World Development, the Hong Kong property, hotels and infrastructure group, yesterday reported a 37 per cent rise in after-tax earnings to HKDollars 1.9bn (USDollars 245m) from HKDollars 1.39bn in the six months to the end of December. Profits were struck on a 41 per cent growth in turnover to HKDollars 8.37bn from HKDollars 5.94bn, with most of the earnings growth coming from a strong performance by the hotels division. Earnings from rents and property sales were flat, while those for construction and piling were slightly down on the same period a year ago. Earnings per share advanced 34 per cent to HKDollars 1.21. Directors declared an interim dividend of 28 cents, 12 per cent up on a year ago. Directors sought to underline the importance of its activities in China. They said the company would maintain between 20 and 25 per cent of its net assets in mainland investments. Pre-tax profits from hotel interests rose 51 per cent to HKDollars 401.2m. The company said it had cut its stake in Asia Television from 47.5 to 27.5 per cent.
