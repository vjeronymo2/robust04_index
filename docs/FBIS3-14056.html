Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] PLO sources announced today that some progress was made during the talks between PLO officials and an Israeli delegation in Tunis in the past two days. The discussions revolved around ways of providing international protection for the Palestinians in the occupied territories in line with the relevant UN Security Council resolution. The sources added that the meetings between the two sides will be resumed in Cairo tomorrow. They pointed out that some ideas are being discussed that are likely to result in considerable progress. The Israeli delegation members returned to Israel from Tunis today to consult with their government. Meanwhile, it has been announced in Oslo that representatives of the donor countries that will extend aid for Palestinian autonomy in Gaza and Jericho will hold a one-day meeting in Cairo on Thursday [24 March]. They will discuss ways of establishing a Palestinian police force in the occupied territories. The Norwegian foreign minister said this step is likely to put the Middle East negotiations back on track.
