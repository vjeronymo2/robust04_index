BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on Colombian economic developments monitored through 10 May: Officials from the Tax and Customs Directorate will have to answer for the loss of 60,000 exportation forms, which has partially paralyzed export transactions. Customs authorities believe the forms were stolen with the intention of selling them on the black market for 20 million pesos each. (Santa Fe de Bogota Inravision Television Cadena 1 in Spanish 1730 GMT 4 May 94) Inflation so far this year is running at 12 percent. Some 2.8 million Colombians are unemployed, compared with the unemployment figure of 2 million recorded in 1990. Meanwhile, exports are down from 1991's high of $7.2 billion. Today, annual imports amount to about $9 billion, compared to $4 billion in 1991. (Santa Fe de Bogota Inravision Television Canal A Network in Spanish 0130 GMT 8 May 94)
