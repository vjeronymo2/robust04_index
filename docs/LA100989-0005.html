October 9, 1989, Monday, Home Edition

PLUTONIUM THREAT OF GALILEO PROBE

Planetary exploration at any risk?

NASA's scheduled shuttle launch on Oct. 12 will bring with it 48 pounds of plutonium aboard Project Galileo. As the Challenger disaster tragically illustrated in 1986, NASA's space program is not infallible. Furthermore, it is believed by some scientists that the plutonium-fueled generator casings are not capable of withstanding an accident like Challenger. An even distribution of this plutonium is unlikely, but exposure to intense radioactivity now threatens many Florida residents if an accident were to occur.

Therefore I ask, should life on Earth be jeopardized by this approach to the exploration of Jupiter's atmosphere? Let us insist on the implementation of technologies that protect (not threaten) the health of our own planet.

NANCY OWENS-RENNER

San Diego
