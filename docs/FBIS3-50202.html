Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Prague, Jan 18 (CTK) -- If parliamentary elections were held today, the Civic Democratic Party (ODS) would win with 32 percent of the vote, according to an opinion poll carried out among 1,561 respondents over age 18 by the Center for Empirical Research (STEM) at the beginning of January. In second place, according to the research, would be the Civic Democratic Alliance (ODA) with 14 percent of the vote. The Czech Social Democratic Party (CSSD) would gain 13 percent, the Communist Party of Bohemia and Moravia (KSCM) 9 percent and the Christian Democratic Union-Czechoslovak People's Party (KDU-CSL) 6 percent. Those parties winning less than 5 percent of the vote -- the cutoff for parliamentary representation -- would be the radical right-wing Assembly for the Republic-Czechoslovak Republican Party (SPR-RSC) with 4 percent, the Free Democrats (SD -- formerly the Civic Movement) with 2.5 percent, the Moravian-Silesian Centrist Party (MSS -- formerly the Movement for Self-Governing Democracy for Moravia and Silesia) with 2 percent, and the Greens and the Christian Democratic Party (KDS) with 1.5 percent each. The four parties making up the current governing coalition are the ODS, the ODA, the KDU-CSL, and the KDS. The research indicated that 63 percent of eligible voters would take part in the elections, while 14 percent would definitely not vote and 23 percent are still undecided. The ODS, the KDU-CSL, and the KSCM show the greatest stability of voter preference. The CSSD has experienced a drop in popularity, while the ODA has gained favor among voters. Supporters of the ODS are the most strongly convinced of their preference, and 83 percent would vote in elections.
