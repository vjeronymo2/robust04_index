Language: English Article Type:BFN [Unattributed article: "China Asks For Cut in Trade Restrictions"] [Text] Beijing, 23 Feb (ANSA) -- Amidst hopes for continued trade expansion between European Union [EU] countries and China, fears have been expressed about possible effects on China's exports from restrictions imposed by The Twelve on Chinese products. These fears were mentioned today by Zang Chunzheng, from the Chinese Foreign Trade Ministry, with reference to the thirteenth meeting between China and the European Union to begin Monday [28 February] with the participation of Commissioner Leon Brittan. Zang Chunzheng said he hoped the meeting would remove or weaken "the quotas and other limitations introduced by the Twelve". He described them as "discriminatory measures adopted to protect the European market against China". China's re-admission into GATT, the international commerce agreement, will be one of the issues examined during Brittan's visit.
