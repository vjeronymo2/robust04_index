BFN [Excerpts] Belgrade, 7 May (TANJUG) -- Representatives of the Serbian National Renewal [SNO] announced at a news conference today that the relevant authorities have banned the party's convention scheduled for Victory Day on Monday at the Republic Square in Belgrade. The SNO said that Vladimir Zhirinovskiy, chairman of the Liberal-Democratic Party of Russia, was to have addressed the convention. A decree banning the convention, signed by Milos Vukobrat, head of the interior affairs department of Belgrade's Stari Grad municipality, stresses that the decision was passed because "if the meeting goes ahead and there is a breach of public order, the safety of people and property will be threatened, and public transport will be interrupted." [passage omitted] [Zoran] Atanaskovic [chairman of the SNO executive committee] said: "We believe that this is a political ban, and the SNO's final stance is that the convention will go ahead as planned, regardless of whether our appeal to overturn the ban is accepted or not." According to him, there have never been any incidents at SNO meetings. He added that the party is ready to guarantee safety to all participants in the meeting, and that 200 bouncers will be provided. Jela Jovanovic, chairman of the SNO's political council, confirmed that Zhirinovskiy and his closest aides will arrive tomorrow afternoon and spend three days in Belgrade. According to her, immediately upon his arrival, Zhirinovskiy will have a meeting with republic of Srpska president Radovan Karadzic and then hold a news conference in the International Press Center.
