BFN [By UKRINFORM correspondent Mikhail Stetsyuk for TASS] [Text] Sevastopol June 29 TASS -- The results of elections of the city mayor and deputies to the Sevastopol City Council were discussed at a session of the Electoral Commission on Tuesday. The mandates won by 50 deputies and chairman of the executive committee Viktor Semenov were recognised valid. Viktor Semenov, in his first address as the city mayor, said that the new power would be tough and its goal would be to improve the life of city residents. Semenov pledged to suspend the activities of the Ukrainian president's mission in Sevastopol on the very first day when the newly elected city council comes into office, saying that organs of state administration would begin to operate in accordance with a regime of the executive committee of the city council of people's deputies. Semenov intends to put problems, pertaining to the possibility of Sevastopol's integration into the Crimean common economic space, on the agenda of the first session of the city council. In this connection, the city council shall appeal to the president and the parliament of the autonomous republic in order to apply the banking, currency and customs policy pursued by the Crimean authorities, on the city's territory. The new mayor intends to endorse the city's emblem and its anthem at the first session of the city council.
