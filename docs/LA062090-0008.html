June 20, 1990, Wednesday, Home Edition

POINDEXTER SENTENCED TO PRISON FOR ROLE IN IRAN-CONTRA SCANDAL

Thank you for your short but succinct editorial on the sentencing of John Poindexter ("Admiral Was Far Off Course," June 12).

There was hope that this trial would raise the still unanswered questions of the Iran-Contra scandal and publicize how our institutions failed to get to the bottom of it.

In the preceding trials of Oliver North, Richard Secord, Albert Hakim and Joseph Fernandez, claims of national security have been used to cover up criminal activity. Those who still consider these men as heroes seem to have forgotten that, as your editorial points out, the very foundation of American constitutionalism is shaken when the checks and balances are ignored. George McGovern has called Iran-Contra, "The greatest constitutional crisis since the Civil War."

Now a little justice has been meted out but it leaves relatively untouched and unpunished the high-ranking government officials who have put themselves above the law by hiding from prosecution behind the cloak of national security.

We can only hope that the non-government case still to be tried, the Christic Institute civil suit, will go forward. Its goal is a worthy one: to insure that the abuses committed in the Iran-Contra affair will be prevented in the future and that national security will once again become security for the nation rather than security from prosecution for those who commit crimes in the name of patriotism.

PATRICIA S. CARMEL

Rancho Santa Fe
