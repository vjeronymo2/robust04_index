Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, March 11 KYODO -- Japan on Friday [11 March] appointed veteran diplomat Sadayuki Hayashi as the new deputy foreign minister in charge of economic affairs, the Foreign Ministry said. Hayashi, 56, will replace Koichiro Matsuura. Matsuura will be assigned to the foreign minister's secretariat to wait for an ambasadorial post, the ministry said. Hayashi has been the director of the secretariat since July 1992 after serving as director general of the ministry's Economic Affairs Bureau and as minister at the Japanese delegation in Geneva.
