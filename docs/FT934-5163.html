931201 FT 01 DEC 93 / Observer: Shot in the toe Britain's education secretary John Patten obviously could not contain his excitement at securing a 6.8 per cent increase in funding from the Treasury. Why else should his department have released its budget news to the press more than five hours before the chancellor was due to stand up? However, it also meant that journalists had even more time to reflect on the full ramifications of a near halving in the government's contribution towards university students' tuition fees. Go to the back of the class, John.
