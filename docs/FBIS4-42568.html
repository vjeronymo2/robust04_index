BFN [Text] Upon his 1430 return at Ellinikon Airport today, Foreign Minister Karolos Papoulias expressed his satisfaction with his visit to Moscow. The foreign minister talked about full exchange of views with the Russian side on the Yugoslav issue, and pointed out that the two countries continue to support free navigation in the Canakkale Straits, as provided by the Montreux Treaty. Responding to a question on the Skopje [The Former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia--FYROM] issue, Mr. Papoulias said he does not expect much from the visit by President Clinton's special envoy Matthew Nimetz, and made the assessment that Mr. Nimetz will not bring anything new to the current phase.
