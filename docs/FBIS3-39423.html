Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Ankara, Feb 15 (A.A) -- Prime Minister Tansu Ciller on Tuesday [15 February] said that the decision by NATO was the most realistic and serious among those taken until now. Addressing the DYP [True Path Party] party meeting, she said that during her visit to Bosnia on Feb 2 with Pakistan's Premier Benazir Bhutto, she has seen the naked thruth of the situation. Ciller said it is essential that the people living there should be protected and the massacre ended, either by lifting the embargo or by air operation. "Turkey is ready to do every thing to help implement NATO decision and to end this targedy which is taking place right in the center of Europe," she added. NATO agreed on Feb 9 to order air strikes against Bosnian Serb forces besieging Sarajevo unless they withdraw artillery and other weapons or put them under UN control within 10 days. The 16 NATO member anbassadors agreed at a meeting in Brussels that there would be no extension of the ultimatum and no further warnings. Serb heavy weapons would have to be withdrawn at least 20 km (12 miles) from the centre of the besieged city, where 68 helpless civilians were massacred and 200 wounded on Saturday when Serb mortar turned a market place in central Sarajevo into a pool of blood.
