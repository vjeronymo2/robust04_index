Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Hanoi VNA March 15 -- the State Planning Committee has introduced orientation for building a key development zone in the north at a conference held here recently and chaired by Vice Premier Phan Van Khai. The northern key development zone expected to cover Hanoi, Haiphong and Hon Gai (to be renamed Ha Long soon), will play an important part in the implementation of the strategy for socio-economic development of the north and the whole country as well. The zone concentrates major industrial, activities and communication and transport junctures of northern Vietnam. It has a great economic potential in the way of natural resources, workforce, and tourisms. It has 300-km coastal line with deep gulfs favourable for sea port construction. In addition, the zone's hydro-electricity potential accounts for 60 of the country's reserves. It has 175,000 ha of forests, 318,000 ha of farm land, and 226,000 ha of bare hills. About 90 of its population are literate [sentence as received]. By the end of last year some 162 foreign investment projects had been licensed in this zone with a total investment capital of USD 1.63 billion: Hanoi has 120 projects with USD 1.04 billion, and Haiphong 24 projects with USD 523.5 million. The development orientation of all branches of activities in the zone from now to the year 2000 and 2010 is to change the economic structure in conformity with the market economy to increase the proportions of industrial production and services, and to encourage the development of non-governmental economic sectors and foreign investment into this zone.
