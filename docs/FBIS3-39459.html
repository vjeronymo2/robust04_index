Language: German Article Type:BFN [Text] Hamburg (DPA) -- Chancellor Helmut Kohl, Christian Democratic Union [CDU], and the CDU/Christian Social Union [CSU] have slightly caught up in popularity with the Social Democratic Party of Germany [SPD] and Rudolf Scharping, its candidate for the chancellor's office. This is shown by the RTL "Chancellor's Barometer," which was presented on Thursday [17 February]. According to this poll, Kohl's popularity rose by 1 percent to 26 percent compared with the previous week. The head of government, however, is still 9 percent behind Scharping (35 percent), who lost 2 percent. According to the poll, 31 percent of the voters would vote for the CDU/CSU, which is 1 percent more than last week. The SPD, on the other hand, would lose 2 percent, but would still have the support of 44 percent of the voters. With 6 percent of the votes, the Free Democratic Party of Germany would exceed 5 percent for the first time in weeks. The figures of Alliance 90/Greens (10 percent), as well as the Party of Democratic Socialism and the Republikaner (3 percent each), have remained unchanged. For this poll, the Forsa Institute questioned 2,514 German citizens on behalf of the RTL morning program "Good Morning, Germany."
