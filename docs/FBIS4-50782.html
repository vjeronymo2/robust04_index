BFN [Text] Singapore, May 22 (XINHUA) -- Chinese Vice Premier Li Lanqing left here today for home after concluding a four-day visit to this island country. He was here to attend the international conference "Waves of the Future -- ASEAN, Vietnam, China: Opportunities for World Business", which was organized by the Asia Society, Dow Jones and Company, Inc. and Institute of Policy Studies of Singapore. He also co-chaired with Singapore Deputy Prime Minister Lee Hsien Loong the inaugural meeting of the joint steering council of the Suzhou Industrial Park project, a major cooperation undertaking between the governments of the two countries. Li met with Prime Minister Goh Chok Tong, Senior Minister Lee Kuan Yew, and President Ong Teng Cheong, and other Singapore leaders during his stay here.
