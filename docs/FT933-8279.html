930817 FT 17 AUG 93 / Average consumer credit 'falls by 3%' CONSUMERS CONTINUED to pay off their debts in the second quarter of this year, with the number of people slightly in arrears on credit repayments falling compared with a year ago. Infolink, the credit information group, reported that the value of an average outstanding consumer-credit commitment was 3 per cent lower in the second quarter than in the same period a year earlier. The latest survey of lenders' instalment credit agreements - mainly loans on credit cards and hire purchase agreements - found that 90.5 per cent of instalment credit accounts were up to date. This compares with 90.2 per cent in the corresponding period a year ago. The average value of credit agreements in the survey was Pounds 3,500. While the percentage of accounts in arrears for up to three months was down on a year ago, those for more than three months rose slightly. Mr Brian Bailey, chairman of Infolink, said: 'While consumers are more financially stable than they were a year ago, it would appear that the economic climate has not improved sufficiently to alleviate all consumers from the burden of debt.'
