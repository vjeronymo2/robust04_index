Language: Turkish Article Type:BFN [Announcer-read report over video] [Text] Foreign Minister Hikmet Cetin hopes that NATO will decide in favor of air raids against the Serbian emplacements in Bosnia-Herzegovina, adding: Any other decision would only grant the Serbians more time to plan new attacks. In a statement prior to his meeting in Ankara with (Enest Drakovic), Bosnian minister of education, science, and culture, Cetin said that representations have been made with NATO and the United Nations following the recent Sarajevo attack in a bid to put an end to this barbarism as soon as possible. Cetin said the following: [Begin Cetin recording] Our ambassador has been instructed to apply to NATO, and the application has been made. Turkey was the first to apply to NATO to call the NATO Council to a meeting on the issue. Instructions were also given to apply to the UN Security Council on the issue of lifting the arms embargo. [end recording] In reply to a question, Cetin said that he does not believe that UN Secretary General Butrus-Ghali's approval is necessary concerning any decision NATO might make regarding intervention. (Drakovic) said that during his contacts he observed that Western countries do not want to exert efforts to end this massacre. Pointing out that Turkey's continued support strengthens the Bosnians, (Drakovic) added that grief caused by Saturday's [5 February] attack can be seen on the faces of the Turkish people.
