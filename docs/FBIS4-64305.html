BFN [Text] Ankara, April 30 (AA) -- Foreign Minister Hikmet Cetin on Saturday [30 April] said that in frame of previous agreements, Turkey will join in oil exploration projects in Turkmenistan and Uzbekistan. He told reporters upon his arrival at Ankara's Esenboga Airport following a visit to these countries that the Turkish Petroleum Company (TPAO) will be participating in the project, adding Turkmenistan is at the moment producing 5.5 million tonnes of oil yearly. Answering reporters' questions, Cetin expressed belief in the advantage on the continuation of talks process for finding a solution to the Cyprus problem. On the embargo imposed to Iraq, he said that Iraq is complying by the embargo rules, adding the United Nations Security Council also is expecting this country to take positive steps on the issue of Kuwait's independence and territorial borders. On Bosnia-Herzegovina, he underlined the importance of bringing to a stop Serbian offensive, stressing that this would not necessarily mean that the conflict has been solved. "What matters is the independence and sovereignty of Bosnia-Herzegovina. If a real cease-fire is reached, such a process can start." He said. Cetin also expressed belief that a continuation of UN-NATO cooperation is vital to at least set up safe areas where necessary.
