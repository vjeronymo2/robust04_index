BFN [Excerpt] Prime Minister Ms. Benazir Bhutto has said that a strong and stable economic development, self-reliance in energy, and population control are the three characteristics of the present government's economic policy for national development. Addressing a Karachi Stock Exchange award ceremony for top companies last night, Ms. Benazir Bhutto said that on these three pillars depend the future progress and prosperity of Pakistan, and her government is moving to achieve these objectives. We have already announced an energy development program, which is expected to attract 700 billion rupees as investment over the next five years. Work has started on the Ghazi-Barotha project and difficulties facing the Hab power project have been removed. Power production in Pakistan will increase by 40 percent as a result of the two projects. The government has devised a program for setting up a 300-mw nuclear power plant with Chinese assistance and efforts are underway to remove the difficulties in the way of acquiring a 900-mw nuclear power plant from France. The prime minister said the government has taken several steps to stabilize the country's economic infrastructure. Financial discipline has been introduced. Taxes on agriculture have been levied and bank borrowings, which stood at 62 billion rupees last year, have been brought down to 10 billion rupees. The State Bank has been made autonomous to free it from political pressures. Foreign exchange reserves have gone up to $1.8 billion. Referring to the policy of privatization, she said the government would like to encourage it, but ensuring that public units are sold to the right persons at the right price. Ms. Benazir Bhutto said that her government is not afraid of taking unpopular decisions which are in the interests of the nation and long-term development. She said that she believed a long-term solution of our problems would ensure the prosperity of the nation. [passage omitted]
