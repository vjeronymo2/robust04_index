BFN [Text] Today, Foreign Minister Kanat Saudabayev received U.S. Senators Arlen Specter and Robert Bennett who are currently on a visit to Almaty. They exchanged opinions on Kazakh-U.S. political, trade, and economic relations. The U.S. side, in particular, expressed interest in progress of privatization in the republic and its peculiarities as well as problems related to it. Kanat Saudabayev told the guests about the denationalization of state industrial enterprises which is in progress. He said that the biggest U.S. investments are being made in the Almaty tobacco factory and the Shymkent confectionery factory. The minister stressed that this testifies to the fact that the republic has serious intentions for the market economy. They also touched upon the multifaceted relations with Kazakhstan's closest neighbor, Russia, and Kazakhstan's cooperation with it. The senators handed Kanat Saudabayev a personal letter from U.S. Secretary of State Warren Christopher congratulating Saudabayev on his appointment as foreign minister.
