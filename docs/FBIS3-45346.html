Language: English Article Type:BFN [By Sofia Wu] [Text] Taipei, Jan. 15 (CNA) -- The Republic of China [ROC] will lend an additional US$10 million to Vietnam to help the Indochinese country build an industrial park in Hanoi to house Taiwan-invested factories, Vice Economic Affairs Minister Sheu Ke-sheng said Saturday [15 January]. "The move proves that our government attaches great importance to strengthening trade and economic cooperation [words indistinct] Vietnam," said Sheu, who returned to Taipei Thursday from a visit to Hanoi. The five-year loan, with an interest rate of 7 percent per annum, will be administered by the Vietnamese central bank to help Taiwan land developers build a 200-hectare industrial zone in Vietnam's capital city, Sheu noted. The money will come from the International Economic Cooperation and Development Fund established by the ROC Government in 1989 to offer financial aid to needy developing countries. Taiwan has already lent Vietnam US$30 million to expand a highway and another US$15 million to fund the development of small- and medium-sized Vietnamese companies. The planned Hanoi industrial park will be the second such zone Taiwan has helped build in Vietnam. A similar industrial park has been set up in Ho Chi Minh City to house Taiwan-invested firms. There are some 700 Taiwan-invested companies in Ho Chi Minh City and more than 40 local manufacturers have expressed intention to set up production lines in Hanoi, Sheu reported. Taiwan businessmen have poured more than US$1.2 billion for various investment projects in Vietnam since the late 1980s, making Taiwan one of Vietnam's major foreign capital source. Sheu said the government will send investment officials to Vietnam in the near future to help that country simplify its investment procedures.
