BFN [Text] Sofia, April 11 (BTA) -- The Bulgarian Aerospace Agency and the Russian Space Agency (RSA) signed a treaty on cooperation in the sphere of astronautics, RSA Deputy Director General Boris Ostroumov told a news conference today. Mr. Ostroumov is here as head of a Russian delegation visiting this country for the 15th anniversary of the first Russian-Bulgarian joint space flight. The treaty will be effective for 5 years and envisages the launching of a joint programme for space research including the development of spacecraft equipment, technologies and food. "It is a good day for me: the 15th anniversary of my flight with (Bulgarian) Georgi Ivanov," said Russian Nikolay Rukavishnikov, commander of the Soyuz-33 spacecraft in which the first Bulgarian astronaut Georgi Ivanov flew. An exhibition on Bulgarian-Russian cooperation in astronautics today opened at the Russian Cultural Centre here.
