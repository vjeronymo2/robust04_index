Language: Hebrew Article Type:BFN [Text] Two Arabs were killed in the territories today in clashes with IDF [Israel Defense Forces] soldiers, one in Nabulus and another in Hebron. Another 17 Arabs were wounded: three were seriously wounded in Nabulus, one was moderately injured in the village of Tuqu', and 13 were wounded in the Gaza Strip. Our correspondent Moti Amir reports that a riot occurred near the Cave of the Patriarchs, and an IDF soldier was lightly wounded by a stone. Disturbances continue in East Jerusalem, particularly in Shu'fat and in Al-Tur. Our police affairs correspondent Aviv Bushinsky reports that the rioters set tires on fire and threw stones at Border Police units. In East Jerusalem's central bus station, a burning object was thrown at an empty bus and the driver's seat was lightly damaged. Some 1,500 people marched in Haifa for the second day in a mourning procession over the massacre in Hebron. They marched from Wadi Nisnas to the municipality building. One of the speakers was Haifa Mayor 'Amram Mitzna'. Our correspondent Nati Zilberman reports that a stone was thrown at a police patrol car during the procession, but nobody was hurt. A 14-year-old boy who threw the stone was detained for questioning.
