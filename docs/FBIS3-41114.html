Language: English Article Type:BFN [By Tyappa Namutewa] [Text] The Drug Enforcement Bureau of the Namibian Police [Nampol] last year confiscated a total of 3,262kg of Cannabis valued at N$[Namibian dollars]10,426,362 and arrested 366 people in connection with drug smuggling. The police revealed this on Friday following a media enquiry. Apart from the massive Cannabis haul, 75,439 Mandrax tablets valued at N$2,263,170 were seized. Nampol said most of the drugs had been confiscated through the "excellent assistance of the public," while others had been seized in joint operations with neighbouring countries. In 1993 Cocaine valued at more than N$1.3 million had also been confiscated. Overall there had been 242 cases involving drugs during 1993 and 366 people had been arrested of whom 37 had been foreigners. The police again warned the public about the danger of drugs and urged people to report any cases of drug smuggling to the nearest police station.
