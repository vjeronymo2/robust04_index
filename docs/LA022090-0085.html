February 20, 1990, Tuesday, Southland Edition

NATION IN BRIEF;

PENNSYLVANIA;

LUNG DEVICE FAILS TO KEEP WOMAN ALIVE

A woman died 18 hours after doctors at the Milton S. Hershey Medical Center in Hershey, Pa., tried to save her life by using an experimental, temporary lung-assist device.

The woman, identified only as a 34-year-old Philadelphian, was the second recipient of the Intravascular Oxygenator, known as IVOX. A Texas girl, 16, received the first IVOX at a Salt Lake City hospital on Feb. 2. She also died.
