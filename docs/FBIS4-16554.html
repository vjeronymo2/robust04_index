BFN [Text] The UN Security Council and NATO will make decisions on further actions in Bosnia on the basis of the results of talks with the Serbian side, including today's meeting in Pale between UN Envoy Yasushi Akashi, Russian diplomat Vitaliy Churkin, and Bosnian Serb Leader Radovan Karadzic. ITAR-TASS has learned this from a spokesman for the NATO headquarters in Brussels. In his words, if UN observers ask for military support before any new political decisions are made, then NATO aircraft will launch a necessary air strike without delays. NATO will do this in compliance with last year's Security Council resolution, for which Russia voted too, the NATO spokesman stressed.
