Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing February 28 (XINHUA) -- China completed 1,182.9 billion yuan (203.9 billion U.S. dollars then) of investment in fixed assets last year, 50.6 percent up over 1992, or a real growth of 22 percent after inflation. This was revealed by Zhang Sai, head of the State Statistics Bureau (SSB), at a press conference today. He said that state-owned enterprises invested 464.7 billion yuan in capital construction last year, representing a 54.2 percent increase over 1992; investment in real estate for sale totalled 113.8 billion yuan, registering a 124.9 percent increase over the year before, and in other projects, 34.4 billion yuan, up 17 percent. The service industry had the most conspicuous increase in investment -- to 317.5 billion yuan -- by state-owned enterprises, said the official, noting its share surged from 38.3 percent to 44.3 percent. Investment in transport, postal and telecommunications facilities shared 20 percent of the total last year, up from 14.7 percent in 1992, he said. Zhang told the press that the newly increased production capacity in 1993 through capital construction projects included: eight million tons of coal mining, 14.38 million kilowatts of power generation, 15.43 million tons of oil extraction and 1 billion cubic meters of natural gas. Some 274 kilometers of railway tracks and 3,556 kilometers of highways were built, 37.52 million tons of cargo-handling capacity of coastal harbors were finished, he added. In addition, other production and transportation capacities of iron, timber, cement were remarkably enhanced last year through the increased investment in fixed assets, the official said.
