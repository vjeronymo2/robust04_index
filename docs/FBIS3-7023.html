Language: Burmese Article Type:BFN [Text] Members of the armed organizations in the jungle have been laying mines on railway lines, roads, and sidewalks with the intent to destroy them and to kill and maim the people who want to live peacefully. They are also hindering and disturbing smooth and secure transportation as well as local tranquility. At about 1600 on 2 March, Maung Tar Htay, the 19-year-old son of U Aye Myint Lwin of the village of Kyaukpya, Kyaikto Township, stepped on an antipersonnel mine laid by members of the Karen armed organization in the jungle while he was returning to Kyaukpya from the fields. He lost his toes in the accident. The responsible personnel took him to Kyaikto Hospital for treatment.
