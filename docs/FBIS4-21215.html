BFN [Text] The farmers in the northern Upper Huallaga (San Martin Department) who left that region after the brutal attacks by the Shining Path and the MRTA [Tupac Amaru Revolutionary Movement] are gradually but with determination returning to their land. The people living in that region agree that what is taking place now in that part of the country is the best result of the pacification policy. When subversion entered the Upper Huallaga region a few years ago--about the mid-1980's--mainly in some provinces of San Martin Department, thousands of farmers and peasants had to leave their farms or land to settle in the nearby cities or, in the worst cases, to settle in the capital. Subversion now has been practically eliminated and the people have returned, not with the intention of continuing to grow coca, which is the raw material for the sinister narco-terrorist alliance, but to grow other types of products that are more profitable and can be exported. For example, in 1992 rice growing in Tarapoto reached 12,000 hectares and so far this year the cultivation of this product has already reached 30,000 hectares. It is expected that 50,000 hectares of rice will be grown by the end of this year. The situation of the corn crop is also the same. Although the quality of corn is not the best, efforts are being made to improve it to the exportable type required by the important and demanding world markets. But despite this limitation, Tarapoto's corn is wanted by countries like Germany, which wants to buy 80,000 metric tons even though this year's production is only expected to reach 20,000 metric tons. This is why agricultural land is being prepared in order to increase corn production considerably next year. General Alfredo Rodriguez Riveros, general commander of the Huallaga Front, said: ``The farmers welcome the intentions of foreign importers.'' Regarding fruits, grape production is giving good results--it is being used for making wine and marmalade and sales are good. A Peruvian farmer said: ``In Cumbaza we have rich land with good production. Although the grape harvest is only once a year, we have two harvests here.'' Gen. Rodriguez said: ``We are gradually making progress in replacing coca, and the price of coca has dropped considerably in all of San Martin Department.'' As an effective complement to this situation in the northern Upper Huallaga, antidrug operations are eradicating cocaine base although not definitively. The work by the Air Police Directorate providing air support to National Antidrug Division (Dinandro) patrols, and sometimes to the Army, is ``suffocating'' the drug traffickers still operating in the region. An Air Police Directorate officer said: ``Ninety percent of the antidrug operations are carried out through the use of helicopters, and our unit is the only that can carry out this type of operation.'' Although the people who live in the areas where drug traffickers operate protected by the wild jungle of San Martin reject the police action because the people in some way also live off this activity, the ``blows'' have been hard and more drug traffickers had to leave for other regions, where a struggle also is being carried out against them. An Air Police pilot said: ``We carried out several interdiction operations in the last few weeks; an area is hit after confidential information is received. We seized precursors and drugs, and many people involved in this activity were arrested.'' The struggle against drug trafficking probably will take some time but much less than the struggle against terrorism. But the police operating in the area need more sophisticated equipment to face this social scourge in a more effective manner.
