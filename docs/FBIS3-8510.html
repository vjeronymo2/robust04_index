Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Text] The Press Center of the Bosnia-Herzegovina Army reports that Army General Rasim Delic and Brigadier-General Jovan Divjak held talks with General John Galvin, special military envoy of U.S. President Clinton, in Sarajevo today. The discussion concerned further steps in the implementation of the agreement between the Bosnia-Herzegovina Army and the Croatian Defense Council [HVO], which will be taken in the coming days. During the talks, General Delic informed General Galvin of the possible principles of a military solution in the future Bosnia-Herzegovina federation, which separately outlines possible solutions for the transitional period. At the end of today's talks General Galvin expressed optimism about the future course of the talks, absolutely acknowledging all the difficulties that are possible in the course of the realization of the agreement, especially in the transitional period. The talks of the representatives of the Bosnia-Herzegovina Army and the HVO about a military solution in the future Bosnia-Herzegovina federation are scheduled to commence tomorrow.
