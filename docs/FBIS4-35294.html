BFN ["Exclusive report" by Fathi al-Qatta'] [Text] AL-DUSTUR has contacted Dr. 'Abd-al-Karim al-Iryani in Sanaa. Al-Iryani is the minister of development and planning and a former foreign minister. He is also close to Yemeni President 'Ali 'Abdallah Salih and is sometimes called the engineer of Yemeni policy. During our telephone conversation, al-Iryani said that a decision to enter the city of Aden by force, following the two-week siege, would be a political and not a military question. There are 400,000 citizens in Aden and the leadership does not want to risk their lives. Asked about the failure of the Democratic Republic of Yemen, proclaimed on 21 May, to secure recognition, even from countries that do not have any political weight -- which could have been a victory for it that would open the door to further recognition -- al-Iryani said there is no alternative to reversing the secession. He said he expects the secessionists to go. On the current moves, namely, the request made by Egypt, the United Arab Emirates, Saudi Arabia, Oman, Kuwait, and Bahrain, to hold an urgent session of the Security Council to discuss the situation in Yemen, al-Iryani said: The Security Council member states have asserted to our mission in New York that they adhere to Yemen's unity and democracy and respect its sovereignty and independence. Any resolution adopted by the Council that contradicts this reality will be rejected. On the meeting scheduled to be held by the foreign ministers of the Gulf Cooperation Council in Saudi Arabia during the coming hours, al-Iryani said he had no comment, however, if this meeting aims at interfering in Yemen's domestic affairs, then it will be a serious precedent. Regarding a statement issued by the Political Bureau of the Socialist Party, carried by Aden radio yesterday, denying al-Iryani's statements to the media about a secret dialogue going on between those al-Iryani described as the unionists within the Socialist Party and the leadership in Sanaa, al-Iryani said: They are free to deny or confirm whatever they want. The important thing is that we have received a signal from a third party -- a senior Yemeni figure whom he declined to name -- according to which the brothers in Aden say verbatim: Let us first agree to a cease-fire and then everything is negotiable. This came in reply to previous messages. Regarding accusations propagated by some Arab media in their comments on Yemen that the General People's Congress [GPC] and President 'Ali 'Abdallah Salih himself, who is the GPC's secretary general, have sought to divide the ranks of the Socialist Party -- by trying to win over some of its influential leaders, such as Salim Salih Muhammad, the assistant secretary general of the Socialist Party and dismissed Presidential Council member who was appointed with the separatist state; and Haytham Qasim Tahir, member of the Political Bureau and the dismissed defense minister -- Dr. al-Iryani said he can prove that 'Ali Salim al-Bid is the one who proposed the question of merger. Al-Bid cannot deny this. Al-Iryani added that the separatists cannot talk about the accord document when they talk about secession. Had the document been implemented, it would have enhanced unity because it was signed by all the political forces in Yemen. The first to spurn this document was the Socialist Party. Al-Iryani denied that Haytham Qasim Tahir, the former defense minister, was killed recently. Dr. al-Iryani said all Yemenis, young and old, value Jordan's true fraternal position in support of the Yemeni people.
