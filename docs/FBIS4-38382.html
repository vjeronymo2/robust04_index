BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Aleksandr Valiyev] [Text] Pyongyang, 30 May (ITAR-TASS) -- A military delegation from Vietnam paid an unannounced visit to the DPRK this month. The talks with the North Korean side covered a number of issues, primarily the coming visit of the Vietnamese defense minister to Pyongyang on 16 June, informed sources told ITAR-TASS. The sources said the visit should have taken place three days ago but was postponed for undisclosed reasons. The republic's mass media said nothing about the Vietnamese military delegation's visit. Local observers believe that the sides are also making arrangements for the sale of rifles and other arms manufactured in the north of the Korean peninsula. Representatives of the Vietnamese Embassy in North Korea denied that a Vietnamese military delegation visited North Korea. According to them, the Vietnamese defense minister will visit Pyongyang this year as planned earlier, but they declined to say what is in the plan.
