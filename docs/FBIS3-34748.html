Language: Arabic Article Type:CSO [Report by Shafiq al-Asadi] [Text] Abu Dhabi--Sources in Abu Dhabi yesterday stated that Palestinian President Yasir 'Arafat will soon visit the UAE [United Arab Emirates] capital on his first official visit since August 1990. 'Arafat is expected to meet with Shaykh Zayid Bin-Sultan Al Nuhayyan, president of the UAE, and a number of senior officials to inform them of the new developments on the Middle East peace process and the Palestinian-Israeli negotiations to conclude an autonomy agreement for Gaza and Jericho. The sources did not mention the date of 'Arafat's arrival in Abu Dhabi, but they expect it to be after the end of his talks in Cairo with Israeli Foreign Minister Shim'on Peres. They asserted that a high-level UAE official had informed a delegation of the sons of the Palestinian colony in Abu Dhabi that the UAE had agreed to 'Arafat's visit. The Palestinian president stopped at Abu Dhabi International Airport in October on the way from Indonesia to Turkey. He met in the airport with Shaykh Sultan Bin-Zayid Al Nuhayyan, the deputy prime minister, and a number of senior UAE officials. Mahmud 'Abbas (Abu Mazin), a member of the PLO executive committee, is the highest Palestinian personality who has visited the UAE officially since the Gulf war. The sources state that 'Arafat will discuss with Shaykh Zayid the achievement of Arab interests and the PLO's relations with the Gulf Cooperation Council [GCC] states. It is expected that he will discuss the PLO's financial situation and aid from the Gulf states for Palestinian autonomy with the UAE officials. Shaykh Zayid has asserted more than once the UAE's continuing support for the Palestinian people and its intifadah in the occupied territories. The UAE has advocated offering financial aid of some $50 million within the framework of the aid, which will be offered to the autonomous areas in the occupied territories. The UAE and the other member states in the GCC have supported the Palestinian-Israeli agreement that was signed on 13 September in Washington, considering it a first steop toward the return of the Palestinian people's rights and the establishment of their independent state.
