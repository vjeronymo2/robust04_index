Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] A national referendum is under way in Turkmenistan to reveal the public view on the parliament's recent decision to keep the incumbent president Saparmurad Niyazov for the second five-year term without elections. As the Vice-Chairman of the Central Electoral Commission in Turkmenistan Alexander Dodonov has told Interfax, the referendum can be regarded as valid since by 10:00 local time more than 76% of the electorate cast their ballots. By preliminary returns from the commission, of the 1.95 Mn registered constituents, 1.93 Mn (98.9%) voted by noon local time. Although the final returns won't be known until Sunday's morning, even today it's clear that President Niyazov will stay in office for the next five years.
