August 6, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

LAWYERS AND TRUSTS

Attorney Bruss unfortunately was misleading in one important respect.

In the article, the reader was advised to "hold title in joint tenancy with right of survivorship." While this may be preferable in most cases to holding title to a home or other real estate as separate property in the name of the husband or wife alone, it is usually recommended that a husband and wife take title to property as community property, rather than as joint tenants.

The article also failed to mention one of the biggest advantages of a living trust, the ability to reduce or eliminate estate taxes.

STEPHEN C. CLARK

San Diego

Clark is an attorney.

Robert J. Bruss replies: Attorney Stephen Clark is probably unaware of IRS Revenue Ruling 87-98, which states that even if the title is held in joint tenancy, if the joint tenants acknowledge in writing that the property is community property, then the IRS will give it a new basis stepped up to market value on the date of the first spouse's death. Contrary to popular belief, the revenue ruling says that in community property states, it is no longer neccessary to hold title as community property, if the joint tenants have acknowledged in writing that their realty is community property.
