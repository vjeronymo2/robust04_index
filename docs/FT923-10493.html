920730 FT 30 JUL 92 / Commodities and Agriculture: Malaysian palm oil sales PAKISTAN AND Algeria are to buy Dollars 150m worth of Malaysian palm oil under the Malaysian government's recently inaugurated long term export credit financing programme. Mr Lim Keng Yaik, the Malaysian Primary Industries Minister, said contracts giving Pakistan a Dollars 100m credit facility and Algeria one of Dollars 50m would be signed early next month. Malaysia's export credit financing programme for palm oil extends a total of Dollars 500m to six countries - China, India, Egypt, the Commonwealth of Independent States, Pakistan and Algeria. Mr Lim said credits would be granted for up to two years as part of the government's strategy to secure big, long term palm oil contracts. 'The contracts will ensure a ready market for the commodity in the years to come,' he added.
