Language: English Article Type:BFN [Unattributed article: "Sextant (Thomson CSF) To Fit Electronics to MiG"; as released by Brussels Euroscope EIS Database] [Text] Sextant Avionique, a subsidiary of Thomson CSF, the French state-owned electronics and defence procurement group, has been awarded a contract by Mikoyan, the manufacturer of the MiG fighter aircraft. The contract covers the supply of onboard controls for the MiG AT trainer aircraft. The engines for the MiG AT are supplied by SNECMA, the French aircraft engine supplier. Sextant is supplying its Topflight electronic system which will be fitted to the aircraft with the assistance of the Russian group, Gosnias. The first prototype of the MiG AT is expected to take to the air in 1995. The potential market for such a trainer aircraft is estimated at between 600 and 1,200, of which 500 are for Russia alone.
