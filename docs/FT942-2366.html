940620 FT 20 JUN 94 / Business Travel: Free ride In a tale of government regulation turned cozy, US federal inspectors placed on cruise ships to detect illegal immigrants have been taking family and friends along for free or reduced fares, reports the Associated Press. Immigration and Naturalization Service agents allowed the cruise lines to pay the inspectors' travel expenses without documentation. The INS is required to check cruise ships for aliens as they reach US ports. But some inspectors took round trips that left them with idle time aboard the recreational vessels or at foreign ports, then filed for overtime pay.
