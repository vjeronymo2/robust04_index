BFN [Text] Baghdad, 25 Apr (INA) -- Official talks between Iraq and the UN Security Council's Special Commission were resumed at the Foreign Ministry this evening. The Iraqi side to the talks was headed by Deputy Prime Minister Tariq 'Aziz, while UN Special Commission Chairman Rolf Ekeus led the Commission's side. The Iraqi side comprised Foreign Minister Muhammad Sa'id al-Sahhaf; Lieutenant General Engineer 'Amir Muhammad Rashid, director of the Military Industrialization Organization; Dr. Riyad al-Qaysi, under secretary of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs; and several other officials. The Special Commission's side comprised Zifferero, deputy director of the International Atomic Energy Agency; Tim Terevan, official spokesman for the UN Special Commission; and several specialists. The IRAQI NEWS AGENCY has learned that a joint statement on the talks will be issued tomorrow. In reply to a question posed by a correspondent of the IRAQI NEWS AGENCY, Rolf Ekeus said that he will submit a report to the UN Security Council on the progress and outcome of the talks as soon as he returns to New York.
