BFN [Text] On 24 June, the Federation of Economic Organizations of Japan [Keidanren] (chairman Shoichiro Toyoda) decided to urge the government to include the complete abolition of 70 economic regulations, which was suggested in an interim report worked out by the Economic Reform Study Council (the Hiraiwa Council), in the five-year program for promoting deregulation (action program) to be formulated by the end of this fiscal year. The 70 regulations are: 19 regulations on participation in business operations, such as liquor production and sales; nine regulations on facilities requirements, including the Large Retail Store Law; 23 trade regulations, such as the state trade items; and 19 price regulations, such as government purchase prices for rice and wheat. Keidanren also demands the five-year plan include a schedule for reducing regulations by half in five years, at the longest, and completely abolishing them after 10 years.
