BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities monitored through 21 April. The Sao Paulo industrial sector dismissed 1,481 workers during the first week of April. Unemployment in greater Sao Paulo rose from 14.1 percent in February to 14.9 percent. (Sao Paulo GAZETA MERCANTIL in Portuguese 19 Apr 94 p 1) The National Bank for Economic and Social Development, BNDES, will offer for sale preferential shares of the Minas Gerais Iron and Steel Company, Usiminas, worth $400 million. The shares account for 17 percent of the enterprise's total capital. They were unsold at the privatization auction held in November 1991. BNDES expects to sell 80 percent of the shares abroad. (Sao Paulo GAZETA MERCANTIL in Portuguese 19 Apr 94 p 1) Ailton Fernandes, the acting industry and commerce minister, said on 15 April that during the first two months of 1994 the importation of machines and industrial equipment increased by 29.7 percent to $3.818 billion compared to the same period in 1993. (Madrid EFE in Spanish 2156 GMT 15 Apr 94) Jose Ferreira Lopes, Brazilian ambassador to the Arab Emirates, on 15 April reported that the balance of trade between Brazil and the Arab Emirates, which in 1989 registered a deficit of $120,000 for Brazil, in 1993 registered a surplus of $33 million. (Sao Paulo GAZETA MERCANTIL in Portuguese 16 Apr 94 p 5) Canada has doubled its coal sales to Brazil so far in 1994. Canadian coal sales to Brazil in 1993 totaled 900,000 tons. So far this year, contracts for the supply of 1.8 million tons of Canadian coal have already been signed. The reason for the increase is a discount of $3.85 per ton, which surpassed the $0.60 discount offered by Australian suppliers. (Sao Paulo GAZETA MERCANTIL in Portuguese 16 Apr 94 p 1) The Swift Armour meat packing plant, which is owned by the Bordon family, yesterday went bankrupt because of a debt of $143.5 million, which is almost twice the value of its net assets. The crisis in the meat packing plant sector has also affected Sadia, another big enterprise that last year began to reduce its activities. The Anglo meat packing plant was sold last year, and Floresta Industria de Alimentos discontinued its activities last week. (Sao Paulo GAZETA MERCANTIL in Portuguese 16 Apr 94 p 1) According to broker W.I. Carr of the Indosuez Banque group, the electric power sector will be the main beneficiary of a phase of the Brady Plan that Brazil arranged last week. The Light and Eletrobras -- Brazilian Electric Power Company, Inc. -- companies will receive the main benefits from the plan. Carr estimates the principal of the sector's debt was reduced by 13 percent as a result of the agreement reached with creditor banks. (Sao Paulo GAZETA MERCANTIL in Portuguese 19 Apr 94 p 1) Official sources on 15 April reported that between May and August 1994, the Brazilian Government will sell 2.4 billion bags of its coffee stockpile, seeking to reduce domestic market prices which currently stand at $0.05 above international market prices. The government may auction some 600,000 bags per month. (Madrid EFE in Spanish 2112 GMT 15 Apr 94) During an auction held on 15 April, 484,039 Arafertil Inc. shares were sold at the unit price of 24.53 cruzeiros for a total of 11,873,476 cruzeiros. The price of the shares was 0.012 percent higher than the minimum price, which had been set at 24.52713 cruzeiros. (Rio de Janeiro O GLOBO in Portuguese 16 Apr 94 p 27) National Treasury Secretary Murilo Portugal Filho has reported that in the first quarter of 1994 the Treasury registered a cash deficit of 186 billion cruzeiros. This virtually balances the public accounts. During this period, the Treasury income stood at 10,420 trillion cruzeiros while expenses stood at 10,607 trillion cruzeiros. The deficit registered in February was compensated by the surplus in January and March. (Sao Paulo O ESTADO DE SAO PAULO in Portuguese 14 Apr 94 p 1) According to the Brazilian Institute of Geography and Statistics on 18 April, Brazilian industrial production dropped by 2.8 percent in February after registering an accumulated growth rate of 14.2 percent between September 1993 and January 1994. Of the 17 areas checked in January and February, only the capital goods sector registered positive performance; it grew by 0.8 percent. The mechanical sector dropped by 8.2 percent, the electrical material sector by 7.2 percent, the chemical sector by 1.5 percent, the metallurgical sector by 1.7 percent, and the transport material sector by 2.6 percent. These industries account for more than 70 percent of the activities in the sector. (Sao Paulo O ESTADO DE SAO PAULO in Portuguese 19 Apr 94 p B6)
