Language: Chinese Article Type:BFN [Text] A Chinese military delegation led by General Liu Huaqing, vice chairman of the Chinese Central Military Commission, arrived in Bangkok yesterday for a six-day goodwill visit to Thailand at the invitation of the Thai Supreme Command. At 1647 yesterday, his majesty the king granted an audience to Gen. Liu Huaqing, who was accompanied by Supreme Commander Air Chief Marshal Woranat Aphichari, at the grand place. At 1700, Prime Minister Chuan Likphai received Gen. Liu Huaqing and his entourage at Government House. During the talks, Gen. Liu Huaqing thanked the prime minister and the Supreme Command for the warm reception accorded to his delegation. He felt greatly honored by an audience with his majesty the king. Gen. Liu Huaqing said that his delegation's visit to Thailand is aimed at promoting friendly relations between the Thai and Chinese peoples. He stressed that the friendship between the Thai and Chinese peoples is fraternal. Gen. Liu Huaqing also praised Thailand's rapid economic development. China is devoting itself to construction in all fields and is giving special attention to economic reform. Gen. Liu Huaqing stressed that China's military buildup is aimed at self-defense. China will never seek hegemony. Like Thailand, China hopes to see peace and stability in the Asia-Pacific region. On the occasion, he also conveyed Chinese Prime Minister Li Peng's best regards to Prime Minister Chuan. Prime Minister Chuan welcomed the visit of Gen. Liu Huaqing and his delegation to Thailand. He said that the Chinese military leader's visit to Thailand will further promote relations between Thailand and China, especially closer military contacts. Prime Minister Chuan praised the achievements in China's construction. He said that China has experience and capability in producing weapons. Thailand still needs to buy weapons from China. He said that he had visited the Shanghai Zhonghua Shipyard to observe the construction of Thai ships during his recent visit to China. Chuan praised China for playing a vital role in maintaining peace and stability in the Asia-Pacific region, especially its contribution to the restoration of peace in Cambodia. It was commendable that China also achieved the fastest economic growth rate in the world last year. After the talks, Prime Minister Chuan conferred the Order of the Knight, First Class, on Gen. Liu Huaqing for his important contributions to the friendly relations between the armed forces and peoples of the two countries.
