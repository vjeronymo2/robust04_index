BFN [Text] Bonn (DPA) -- German exports to the Arab states increased 2 percent to 15.8 billion German marks [DM] in 1993. The main importer of German products was Saudi Arabia, which bought goods worth DM3.6 billion (1992 -- DM4.2 billion) from Germany, the Federal Economics Ministry stated in Bonn on 19 May. The United Arab Emirates replaced Egypt as second most important buyer, purchasing goods worth DM2.2 billion (up 26.6 percent). Similarly high growth rates were reached in German exports to Syria and Lebanon. Exports to Egypt increased 11.8 percent to DM2.1 billion. German imports from the Arab states only reached the value of DM11.2 billion (down 7.3 percent). A good two-thirds of the imports was crude oil. Even though quantitatively, 1.2 percent more crude oil was imported, the oil bill decreased by 5.6 percent to DM7.5 billion because of lower crude oil prices.
