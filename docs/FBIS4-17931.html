BFN [Text] Today in Jurmala, groups of experts of the Latvian- Russian interstate talks completed the coordination of an additional protocol to the agreement on social guarantees for the discharged Russian military that was submitted by Latvia. With this, there are no obstacles now to the delegations signing the package of documents on the withdrawal of the Russian Army, Sergey Zotov, head of the Russian delegation, believes. Aivars Vovers, deputy head of the Latvian delegation, also admitted that there are no obstacles now for a meeting between the Latvian and Russian presidents, from the point of view of the readiness of the agreements. The protocol provides for several ways to tackle the issues related to emigration to Russia or other states by the Russian officers discharged in Latvia, if they wish to do so. It is envisaged to tackle the issue of the amount of payment for rent of the Skrunda radar station at the presidents' meeting.
