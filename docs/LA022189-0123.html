February 21, 1989, Tuesday, P.M. Final

P.M. BRIEFING;

EPA WANTS TO FINE 109 REPAIR SHOPS

The Environmental Protection Agency proposed fines of more than $1 million against 109 muffler and car repair shops today for installing improper emission-control devices on automobiles.

The EPA said the shops installed cheaper, less effective "two-way" catalytic converters on automobiles rather than the "three-way" converters required under federal laws to reduce air pollution.

The three-way converters control nitrogen oxide emissions in addition to hydrocarbons and carbon monoxide in car exhaust. The two-way converters do not adequately control nitrogen oxides, the EPA said.
