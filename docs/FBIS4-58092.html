BFN [Text] Druze leaders on the Golan Heights and in the Galilee have urged the Israeli Government, if its transfers Druze villages to Syria as part of a settlement, not to differentiate between the villages. Our correspondent Hayim Hecht notes that on the map prepared by the IDF [Israel Defense Forces] within the framework of the plan called Majdal Shams First, three Druze villages -- Majdal Shams, Mas'adah, and Buq'ata -- will be transferred to the Syrians, while 'Ayn Qunyah, which is situated on lower terrain, will remain with Israel. The Druze are demanding that the villages not be divided up and that all the lands that belong to them be transferred to Syria. Several Druze families on the Golan Heights have bought land in various locations in the Galilee in order to move there if the Golan is returned to Syria. Other Druze families are looking into that possibility and also the prospect of buying land on the Karmel. Our correspondent notes that Druze leaders are helping the Golan Druze.
