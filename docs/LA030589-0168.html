March 5, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

CHAIN SAW USE REQUIRES WATCH FOR INTERNAL STRESS

If you cut wood with a chain saw, be sure to position yourself so as to prevent the cut log section from rolling. Solo Inc. advises holding the saw on the right side of your body, outside the line of sight across the chain. If the log shows signs of internal stress -- a bowed appearance -- the operator should not cut on the stressed side, since this could lead to pinching and result in a kickback of the saw.
