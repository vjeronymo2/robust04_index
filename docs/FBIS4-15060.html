BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Aleksandr Zhudro] [Text] Tel Aviv April 5 TASS -- Israel acknowledges Russia's role as a super power on the international scene and in the Middle East with which our country is linked with considerable economic, political, spiritual and historical interests, Russian president's special envoy on Middle East settlement Viktor Posuvalyuk said in an interview with ITAR-TASS today. Sharing his impressions of his recent meetings with Israeli Prime Minister Yitzhak Rabin and Foreign Minister Shim'on Peres, Posuvalyuk noted that the Israeli leadership has made up its mind on positive interaction with Russia as a co-sponsor of the peace process in the region. According to the special Russian presidential envoy, during his stay in Israel detailed consultations were held on a wide range of international problems, Russian-Israeli relations, and also on preparations for the forthcoming visit to Moscow by the head of the Isreali Government. After completing his Middle East tour, during which he visited Syria, Jordan and Israel, Viktor Posuvalyuk flew home today.
