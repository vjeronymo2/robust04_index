BFN [Text] Qingdao, May 17 (XINHUA) -- Construction of an Asian industrial park, a Sino-Singapore joint venture, is well under way in Qingdao City, Shandong Province, a favorite spot for Singapore investors. Located in the Qingdao Economic and Technological Development Zone, the park covers an area of five square kilometers. Plans are to develop one square kilometer in the first year and the rest in three years. Upon completion, the park will mainly house high-tech industrial projects, a local official said. As of May 14, Qingdao and Singapore had set up 83 joint ventures and cooperative projects with a total investment of 330 million U.S. dollars. Qingdao is one of China's 14 open coastal cities as well as a popular tourist attraction.
