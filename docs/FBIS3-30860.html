Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Budapest, 8 February (MTI) -- Hungarian Prime Minister Peter Boross discussed East-Central European security and early settlement of the South Slav crisis with Ukrainian Defence Minister Army General Vitaliy Radetskyy in the Budapest parliament on Tuesday [8 February]. Spokesmen said that Boross had outlined the serious trade losses caused to Hungary from UN sanctions on rump Yugoslavia, and voiced concern over the fate of ethnic Hungarians in Vojvodina. Hungary hopes for help from Ukraine in peacefully settling the south Slav conflict, the prime minister added. Radetskyy noted Ukraine would increase its peace-keeping force in Bosnia under a parliamentary decision. Boross said why Hungary wants closer links with NATO, and stressed the importance of the peace-partnership plan. Both Boross and Radetskyy hoped for greater business links based on mutual advantages.
