Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] India has expressed willingness to cooperate with Poland in a manner beneficial to both the countries. This was stated by the president, Dr. Shankar Dayal Sharma, when the visiting president of Poland, Mr. Lech Walesa, called on him at Rashtrapati Bhavan today. During their 45-minute meeting, Mr. Walesa sought India's participation in the changeover to market economy in his country. Mr. Walesa said this can be done through cooperation in the industrial and technological spheres. Besides bilateral matters, the two leaders also exchanged views on various regional and international issues, including recent developments pertaining to security in Europe. Earlier, the president of Poland, Mr. Lech Walesa, was accorded a warm ceremonial reception on his state visit to India. The president, Dr. Shankar Dayal Sharma, the prime minister, Mr. P.V. Narasimha Rao, and his cabinet colleagues were present on the occasion at the Rashtrapati Bhavan this morning. Mr. Walesa is accompanied by his wife, Mrs. (Danuta), and a high-level delegation, including the foreign minister and minister for industry and trade. Talking to newsmen, Mr. Lech Walesa said that after the collapse of the Soviet Union, the world is looking for new solutions. Mr. Walesa said that his country has a lot to learn from the great traditions of India. He said there are a number of areas in which bilateral cooperation can be enhanced.
