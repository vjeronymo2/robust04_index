BFN [Excerpts] President Muhammad Husni Mubarak today received the UN population award at a special ceremony held at the UN Headquarters in Geneva. The award is in recognition of Mubarak's pioneering role in resolving the population problem since he took office. UN Secretary General Dr. Butrus Butrus-Ghali handed Mubarak the award. President Mubarak acknowledged it with a speech stating the need to resolve the problem of overpopulation in developing countries. [passage omitted] President Mubarak met Otto Stich, president of the Swiss Confederation, at the offices of the Swiss delegation to the United Nation at noon today. According to Information Minister Safwat al- Sharif, the two leaders exchanged views on a number of international and regional issues, particularly the Middle East. President Mubarak and Stich discussed recent progress in the peace process and the steps taken toward the implementation of the Palestinian-Israeli Agreement, according to Minister al-Sharif.
