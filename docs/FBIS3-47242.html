Language: Korean Article Type:BFN [Article by reporter An Hui-chang] [Text] North Korea is calling for reunification through the confederal system and is urging our youth and students to carry out "the reunification struggle" from the beginning of the new year. In his New Year address, Kim Il-song emphasized that "the only method for reunification" is through the confederal system. Also, from 1-7 January North Korea concentratedly discussed the reunification issue on 24 occasions through press statements by its high-ranking authorities and special articles. Kim Pyong-sik, DPRK vice president and chairman of the Korean Social Democratic Party, emphasized on 4 January: "All fellow countrymen in the North, South, and overseas must transcend differences in ideology, ideas, political views, and religion under the banner of the 10-point program of great national unity to firmly unite as one so that they can all vigorously launch into the work to found a pannational reunified state through the confederal system method." In the 3 January special article aired by Pyongyang radio it emphasized: "The South Korean youth and students, who are enthusiastically patriotic and who have a strong sense of justness, must firmly unite with the people from all walks of life in the new year to vigorously carry out the struggle to achieve reunification through the confederal system method." In particular, North Korea is instigating our youth and students by saying that "there is nothing to expect from the current South Korean Government." North Korea is denouncing our government by saying that there is no progress in the reunification issue because of the South side's dependency on outside forces and the promotion of its confrontational policy. Such moves by North Korea is related to the fact that North Korea is working to make 1995 "the first year of reunification." This implies various significance. It is not known exactly when but there are rumors by the North Korean people that "1995 will be the year of reunification." This was also shown when the North Korean people unhesitatingly told the members of our delegation of such moves who went to Pyongyang from 1990-1992 to participate in the South-North high-ranking talks. However, it is not clear whether North Korea officially set such a date for reunification. At the 7 November 1988 combined meeting of party organs, Foreign Minister Kim Yong-nam had emphasized: "We must work to make the year 1995, the 50th anniversary of national division and 50th anniversary of the fatherland's liberation, as a historical year in completing the sacred cause of reunification without fail." Through propaganda media toward South Korea such as the South Korea National Democratic Front, North Korea frequently said "1995 is the first year of reunification." The government views that such moves by North Korea are to unconsciously make the "South and North Korean people" realize that "reunification will be achieved in 1995." Thus, this is viewed as a strategy to make a more favorable condition to form a reunification front. In particular, from last year North Korea has been promoting on a full scale the preparation work to strengthen the reunification front. Such work is "the 10-point program of great national unity" announced by Kim Il-song, and the appointment Kim Pyong-sik and Yu Mi-yong (wife of Choe Tok-sin) who are from the South and overseas fellow countrymen, and Kang Chu-il who was in charge of operations of the pro-Pyongyang Federation of Korean Residents in Japan, as directors of the party reunification front. We can view that there are some strategic intentions based on advance work for North Korea to strengthen propaganda on the reunification issue from the beginning of the new year. In this connection an official of the National Unification Board said: "When considering the fact that next year will be the first year of reunification is widely spreading among the people, it seems that the priority goal of North Korea is to seek unity at home and abroad by turning the interest of the people to the reunification issue." He added: "Another intention of North Korea is to lure the antigovernment struggle through the reunification issue. Therefore, it seems that this year North Korea will work to actively improve its relations with the United States, while carrying out a multifaceted offensive against the South based on the reunification front strategy."
