930414 FT 14 APR 93 / EBRD defends spending on HQ THE European Bank for Reconstruction and Development yesterday defended the spending on its offices. It said it had obtained 'value for money', Robert Peston writes. It was responding to a report in the Financial Times that it had spent Pounds 55.5m on fitting out its new office and Pounds 18m on its previous office, of which Pounds 40m had been provided by the British government. The EBRD said: 'The bank needed a headquarters building that was suitable to its needs as an international public institution with 23 resident directorships representing 56 members.' From April 1991 until the end of last year, the EBRD disbursed Pounds 101m in loans compared with Pounds 201.5m spent on furnishing and equipping its offices, paying staff, travel and administrative overheads. The bank said that by the end of March disbursements had increased by Pounds 56m. It had also 'committed' more than Ecu2bn (Pounds 1.25bn) to projects. It added: 'The bank is proud of its record in central and eastern Europe and the former Soviet Union which it has achieved in difficult circumstances.' The Treasury yesterday said that the control of expenditure at the EBRD was 'a matter for the bank itself and its board of directors'. Mr Gordon Brown, the shadow chancellor, is pressing Mr Norman Lamont, the chancellor, for an explanation of why so much was spent on the new offices. But the Treasury said: 'The control of expenditure by the EBRD is not a matter for the UK government.'
