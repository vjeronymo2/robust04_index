Language: Urdu Article Type:BFN [Text] Pakistan is holding consultations with Japan, the United States, and other friendly countries to devise an acceptable mechanism to achieve the goal of nonproliferation in South Asia. A Foreign Office spokesman, answering a question about the foreign secretary's visit to Japan and his talks in Tokyo with Japanese officials, said that Japan has shown interest in participating in a regional conference on nuclear nonproliferation. The conference was proposed by Pakistan. He said the foreign secretary attended the sixth session of the Pakistan-Japan Joint Commission and held talks on bilateral and economic relations. The Nuclear Nonproliferation Treaty [NPT] and arms control issues were also discussed. The spokesman said Pakistan reaffirmed its stance that it is ready to ratify the NPT, provided that India is willing to do so simultaneously.
