940613 FT 13 JUN 94 / European Elections: Computer leaves Ashdown smiling through gritted teeth At the Liberal Democrats' Westminster headquarters in Cowley Street a huge crowd of mainly young party activists had gathered from early yesterday evening for what they expected would be a celebration over bottles of wine and Coca-Cola. But as the evening wore on - showing the party failing to make the big gains in the south that some opinion polls had predicted - the mood became more subdued. The switch from euphoria to silence had its most dramatic and poignant moment just after midnight. The live television coverage reported a Liberal Democrat gain in Surrey. A huge cheer broke out and Mr Paddy Ashdown and his campaign manager Mr Matthew Taylor embraced in front of the cameras. Then while Mr Ashdown was in full flow, expressing his delight at the result and predicting the beginning of the end of a long struggle to wrench control of the south from the Conservative party, the television commentator corrected the result to a Conservative win, and apologised for a computer error. With that announcement Cowley Street fell temporarily silent and Mr Ashdown, visibly shaken, walked from the room, leaving Mr Taylor to rationalise the result. Mr Taylor blamed Labour for allowing Mr Major to do better than expected against the Liberal Democrats. 'What we are seeing is the Labour party doing well enough in the south and south-west at our expense and making it possible for the Conservatives to hang on. John Major may well end up thanking the Labour party for allowing him to hang on to his job for another term and saving his bacon.' By 12.45am some spirits at Liberal Democrat headquarters were again elated by the news that after their second gain there were two further seats where recounts were taking place. Mr Ashdown was back in front of the television cameras wearing a huge grin. He predicted that the party would still emerge from the night better than anyone could have predicted a year ago. Even after the earlier disappointment of Surrey, Liberal Democrat activists took heart from the election of their first Euro MP. One party worker, Mr David Charlesworth, said: 'For us to get even one Euro MP is a breakthrough. Any more will be the cream on top of the cake.' Another worker, Mr David Cloke, said: 'Any gain is good news for us and we'll be happy if we end up with two.'
