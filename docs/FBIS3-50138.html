Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Belgrade, Jan 24 (TANJUG) -- Neither of the candidates nominated for the post of president of the parliament of the Yugoslav Republic of Serbia was returned late on Monday evening [24 January]. The candidate of the Socialist Party, Zoran Arandjelovic, secured 123 votes, two short of the necessary majority. The candidate nominated jointly by the opposition, Gvozden Rosic, was supported by 118 deputies. The vote was taken by 249 deputies to the 250-seat parliament. The procedure will be repeated on Tuesday.
