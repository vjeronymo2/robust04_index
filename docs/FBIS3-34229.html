Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Cairo, Feb 7 (AFP)--PLO leader Yasir 'Arafat is ready to meet here this week with Israeli Foreign Minister Shim'on Peres if Israel shows more flexibility toward implementing Palestinian autonomy in the occupied territories, a senior PLO source said late Sunday [6 February]. The Palestine Liberation Organization representative to Cairo, Sa'id Kamal, earlier disclosed that 'Arafat had turned down a chance to see Peres here on Monday on the grounds that Israel was delaying agreement on a final autonomy deal. He said the PLO leader would hold talks with Egyptian President Husni Mubarak on Tuesday. In Tunis a PLO source told AFP that 'Arafat would travel to Cairo on Tuesday and would meet Peres "in the event that there is progress in the Israeli position." He said the PLO was sending a delegation here on Monday headed by Yasir 'Abd-Rabbuh, a member of the organization's executive committee, to meet Peres in another bid to overcome obstacles blocking application of a September agreement between the PLO and Israel. The accord offered limited self-rule to Palestinians on the Israeli-occupied Gaza Strip and the West Bank town of Jericho. But its implementation, scheduled to start with the withdrawal of Israeli troops on December 13, has been repeatedly delayed by disagreements over security issues. In the latest attempt to iron out differences, 'Abd-Rabbuh will be accompanied by Faysal al-Husayni, a prominent Palestinian figure from the territories, Ahmad Quray', head of the PLO economic wing, and Nabil Sha'th, leader of the PLO negotiating team on security questions. In another development, Ahmad al-Tibi, a senior aide to 'Arafat, predicted Sunday in Jerusalem after discussions with Peres that a comprehensive autonomy accord would be signed in Cairo this week. "I can say an overall agreement will be signed this week in Cairo ... [ellipsis as received] Mr. Peres is committed to meeting with Mr. 'Arafat," al-Tibi declared, describing Peres as "sincere and serious in trying to accomplish an agreement very soon." A spokesman for Israeli Prime Minister Yitzhaq Rabin meanwhile refused to confirm that Peres would be in Cairo on Monday, saying: "We will issue a statement in due time." But Gad Ben Ari also said Rabin had told the Israeli cabinet he was still "interested" in having the Peres-'Arafat meeting "take place as planned." The goal of the meeting would be to "agree on how to pursue the negotiations with the PLO in order to end them as soon as possible," Ben Ari said without promising that a final accord would be reached in Cairo. The Cairo negotiations between Peres and the PLO team will focus on a list of points drawn up in the Swiss resort of Davos where Peres and 'Arafat met on January 29-30, Kamal told reporters after talks with Egyptian Foreign Minister 'Amr Musa. Both sides emerged optimistic from the Davos encounter. But while 'Arafat said he would be ready to sign a final deal in Cairo this week, Rabin said it needed three or four more weeks and the Israeli army raised its own reservations. "The security issues are the most important, but they are not the only ones," Rabin warned on Wednesday. "We will also have to agree on civilian and economic issues." The key stumbling-blocks remain the control of border crossings with Egypt and Jordan into the new autonomous areas and measures for protecting Jewish settlements in Gaza. But according to Israeli press reports, the two sides agreed in Davos on the amount of land around Jericho to be granted self-rule. A PLO statement on Sunday appealed to the United States and Russia, co-sponsors of a Middle East peace initiative launched in 1991, to "pressure" Israel into "rapidly" withdrawing its troops from the Gaza Strip and Jericho. The communique, drafted by Faruq Qaddumi, head of the PLO political department, charged that the behavior of Israeli authorities "clearly shows that Prime Minister Yitzhaq Rabin is trying to avoid a withdrawal of Israeli forces under the inadmissible pretext of security, which bears no relation to reality."
