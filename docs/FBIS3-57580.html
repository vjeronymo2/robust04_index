Language: Lithuanian Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] The Lithuanian News Agency ELTA was informed from The Hague by Nerijus Maliukevicius, press attache of the Lithuanian president, that President Algirdas Brazauskas arrived on an official visit to the Kingdom of the Netherlands this morning. A solemn welcoming ceremony took place at (Valkenburg) military airport. [passage omitted] The president was welcomed by the Dutch Prime Minister Ruud Lubbers. He presented to Brazauskas the people taking part in the welcoming ceremony. Brazauskas presented the Lithuanian delegation. [passage omitted] The Lithuanian delegation arrived at the Royal Palace in the morning. Here the Lithuanian president was received by Her Majesty Queen Beatrix. At the start, the Queen and the president conversed for half an hour and then the Lithuanian guests were presented to Her Majesty. A luncheon in honor of the Lithuanian president was held. Queen Beatrix and Brazauskas exchanged speeches during the luncheon. Brazauskas visited the Netherlands parliament in the afternoon. He met the chairmen of its lower and upper chambers. After a brief welcoming speech by the chairmen of the chambers, the president acquainted them with the priorities of Lithuanian foreign policy and replied to questions by the parliamentarians [words indistinct] the cooperation for peace program, Lithuania's economic situation and how the Netherlands state could help Lithuania. [Foreign] Ministers Povilas Gylys and Pieter Kooijmans signed two agreements in the Foreign Ministry: On marine transport and on the incentives and protection of investments. The Netherlands press focused a great deal on the visit. Two news conferences took place, one in the press center and the other in the residence occupied by the president. Journalists were mostly interested in Lithuanian-Russian relations, the stance on the document of cooperation for peace program, which is to be signed in NATO headquarters tomorrow [27 January]. The prime minister of the Netherlands gave a dinner for the Lithuanian president in the evening. The Lithuanian delegation will leave The Hague for Brussels tomorrow morning.
