931129 FT 29 NOV 93 / UK Company News: Stagecoach pays Pounds 9m for Western Travel Stagecoach, the operator of regional bus services, is to buy Western Travel, another bus company, for Pounds 9.25m, in a deal which brings to three the number of acquisitions since Stagecoach was floated in April. To finance the purchase, Stagecoach will issue 3m new shares valued at Pounds 4.75m, Pounds 4m worth of loan notes and pay Pounds 500,000 in cash. In addition it will lend Western Travel Pounds 2.1m to part-finance the payment of a special dividend of Pounds 4.5m to the vendors. Western Travel made a pre-tax profit of Pounds 1.86m on turnover of Pounds 33.9m in 1992. It had net assets of Pounds 6.5m at that date. The company operates 650 buses and employs 1,700 people. Western Travel takes Stagecoach, which is based in Perth, into three new geographical areas. Its C&G subsidiary operates in Cheltenham, Gloucester, Swindon and Stroud; Midland Red (South) is in Warwick-shire and north Oxfordshire, while Red and White Services operates in rural South Wales.
