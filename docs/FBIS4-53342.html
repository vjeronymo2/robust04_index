BFN [Text] Our country's oil minister yesterday, on the second day of his visit to South Korea, met with heads of building companies and commercial firms and oil refinery managers. In the course of these meetings, the possibility of cooperation with South Korea's oil companies and commercial firms in the fields of importing natural gas and liquified petroleum gas from Iran and their participation at the oil, gas, and petrochemical projects in the Persian Gulf were investigated, and it was agreed to exchange expert delegations in this connection. During the meeting between Mr. Aqazadeh and heads of the Korean firms, some memoranda of understanding were exchanged. The oil minister and his accompanying delegation left South Korea for Kuala Lumpur today.
