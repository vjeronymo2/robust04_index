Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [From "The Voz Andes" newscast] [Text] The Court of Constitutional Guarantees, with five votes in favor and one opposed, declared the executive decree increasing gasoline prices unconstitutional and suspended it for fundamental reasons. The Court also submitted this resolution to the Supreme Court's Constitutional Court and ruled that it be published in the official gazette. The gasoline price increase has sparked a great deal of protest in the country for the past several days. Every day the Ecuadoran people ask the president to reconsider his decision, but Sixto Duran-Ballen has said that he will not reconsider. After learning of the Court of Constitutional Guarantees' ruling, the president said that he will appeal the decision before the Supreme Court's Constitutional Court.
