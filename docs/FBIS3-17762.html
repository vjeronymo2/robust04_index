Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The first six of 35 maps of troops separation were signed in Zagreb by negotiators representing Croatia and the self-proclaimed republic of Serb Krajina in their second round of talks that started Tuesday. The Russian diplomat who broke this news to INTERFAX said, however, that "the talks are difficult and there is no telling when the remaining documents will be signed." The talks which are underway in the Russian Embassy in Zagreb center on a cease-fire and troops separation. Special envoys of the Russian and U.S. Presidents Vitaliy Churkin and Charles Redman are attending them as mediators as are officials of the UN and the International Peace Conference on Former Yugoslavia.
