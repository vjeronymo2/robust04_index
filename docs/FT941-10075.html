940211 FT 11 FEB 94 / Continued recovery predicted for engineering output Engineering output will continue to recover after declining at the end of last year, the Engineering Employers' Federation predicted yesterday, but it warned against a deteriorating trade balance, Graham Bowley writes. In an interim update to the federation's half-yearly Engineering Economics Trends report, Mr Ian Thompson, federation economic adviser, said that the recovery was being led by the electronics and vehicle sectors. Engineering output declined in the second half of last year, but the federation now expects positive growth for all major sectors except aerospace. Output from the aerospace equipment sector will continue to decline. But 'the trade deficit could be an inhibiting factor on the UK economy,' he said. The federation said the most interesting picture to emerge is a large shift in the pattern of UK exports and imports from European to non-EU trading partners. Engineering employment will continue to fall during this year, said Mr Thompson, but he emphasised that this is not necessarily a bad thing as companies improve on efficiency. Referring to the planned tax rises he said: 'The impact of the tax increase has been exaggerated and will not stop the recovery.'
