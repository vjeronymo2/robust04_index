Language: Russian Article Type:CSO [Item by Petr Karapetyan, KRASNAYA ZVEZDA] [Text] On 17 February former Commander-in-Chief of Ground Troops Valentin Varennikov testified in proceedings in the case of the GKChP [State Committee for the State of Emergency]. Calling the procuracy's charges against him in the plot against President Mikhail Gorbachev a fantasy, he declared that, being in Kiev on 18 August 1991, he did not present the leadership of Ukraine with demands for supporting the GKChP and instituting a state of emergency in western oblasts of the republic, nor did he assign any missions of any kind to the Commander-in-Chief, Black Sea Fleet regarding Belbek Airfield. Varennikov emphasized that "none of this was within my competence; I did not demand, I recommended."
