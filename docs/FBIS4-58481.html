BFN [Text] Tehran, May 3, IRNA -- Armenian Vice-President Gagik Arutyunyan arrived here Tuesday on a two-day visit to hold talks with Iranian officials on the crash of an Iranian plane in Karabakh in March and to convey a message of condolence from the Armenian president to Iranian authorities. The Armenian official was greeted at Tehran's Mehrabad International Airport by Iranian Vice President for Economic Affairs Mohsen Nurbakhsh. Speaking to IRNA upon arrival, Arutyunyan expressed regret over the incident, saying expert teams from Iran and the Commonwealth of Independent States (CIS) were still investigating the issue to find out the cause of the crash. The Iranian passanger plane was downed near Stepanakert region in Karabakh on March 17, killing all its 32 passengers and crew on board. The victims were families of Iranian diplomats in Russia and Iranian students abroad. Arutyunyan said that there were many ambigious points surrounding the crash which would be unraveled after the experts completed their invstigations. He said the staff of Armenia's control tower had done their best to prevent the plane from flying off its route. He promised "if the staff of the Armenian control tower are found guilty of negligence they will be punished." Arutyunyan added that during his stay here, he would discuss Iran-Armenia relations, expansion of bilateral ties and regional issues with President Akbar Hashemi-Rafsanjani, the Majlis vice speaker, the foreign minister, minister economic and financial affairs and his counterpart Nurbakhsh.
