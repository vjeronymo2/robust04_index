Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Riga, Jan 20, BNS -- The Latvian Government is working to strengthen the country's bounday with eastern neighbors, Defense Minister Valdis Pavlovskis told the Saeima plenary session Thursday [20 January]. Pavlovskis was asked by MP [member of parliament] Juris Janeks to comment on the issue. The government will draft new frontier regulations soon, pledged Pavlovskis. The Baltic frontier guards leadership met Jan. 19 in Jurmala, in the framework of the Baltic summit there, said Pavlovskis. They started to shape a cooperation agreement of Latvian, Estonian and Lithuanian frontier services, the minister said. The signature of the agreement is scheduled for February. The first results of this cooperation may be seen already in March, the defense minister said.
