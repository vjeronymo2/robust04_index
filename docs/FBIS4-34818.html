BFN [Text] Quito, 23 Jun (EFE) -- Approximately 100 Ecuadoran Indians are refusing to leave the cathedral in Riobamba, capital of the Andean province of Chimborazo, to protest the state of emergency declared in Ecuador on Tuesday. The spokesman for the Curia of Riobamba, a city located 188 km south of Quito, told EFE that church authorities had allowed Indians to spend the night in this church, as well as in others, over the past few days. Today, however, the Indians decided to formally occupy the cathedral. The spokesman, who asked to remain anonymous, added: "The Riobamba Curia supports the Indians, and tomorrow Victor Corral, the bishop of the dioceses, will confirm this position upon his return from Rome." The Indians began protests on 13 June in most provinces to oppose an agrarian law that was sanctioned on that same day, calling the law "illegal, racist, and ill-advised." The Confederation of Indian Nationalities of Ecuador [Conaie] and unions grouped under the United Workers Front decided the night before to combat the "state of mobilization"jointly, one of the elements of the "state of emergency" that was based on provisions in the National Security Law. Within this context, the Indians and union members scheduled joint marches and demonstrations in the days leading up to a general strike called for Tuesday. A Conaie leader said, "We have fallen back to devise new strategies," while explaining the virtual nonexistence of confrontation with the military, which the state has authorized to open roads that have been blocked by the Indians for 10 days.
