Language: Latvian Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] Estonian President Lennart Meri, Latvian President Guntis Ulmanis, and Lithuanian President Algirdas Brazauskas have circulated a joint statement in which the invitation for the Baltic states to participate in the NATO program, Partnership for Peace, is valued positively. The statement stresses that the program offers concrete possibilities for the development of cooperation between NATO and the Baltic states. The presidents also express hope that the armed forces of the Baltic states would be transformed so that they would be able to cooperate with NATO. [Second announcer] The statement also says that the task to secure the status of a member state will not be easy, and it should be carried out by all the East European states together. In addition, mutual trust and cooperation on the whole continent and in the Baltic Sea region will strengthen in the framework of the Partnership for Peace program of cooperation between all European states.
