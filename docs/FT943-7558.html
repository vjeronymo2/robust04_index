940823 FT 23 AUG 94 / Markets welcome budget plans The Swedish krona stiffened yesterday and long-term interest rates eased as financial markets gave an initial welcome to a pledge by the opposition Social Democratic Party to take tough action to curb fast-growing government debt if its wins next month's general election. The markets expect the Social Democrats, the country's biggest political party, to win the election and had driven up interest rates and speculated against the krona in the absence of a detailed opposition plan to stem the SKr200bn (Pounds 16.7bn) budget deficit. The proposal unveiled on Friday evening to strengthen the budget by SKr61bn over the next four years through a mix of tax rises and spending cuts has been criticised as 'too little, too late' by the centre-right government.
