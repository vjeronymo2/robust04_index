March 18, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

PURE GORE

I suspected that Vidal, based on his behavior I've witnessed over the years via television, was a bit pompous.

However, Harrison is the pretentious one, with her use of obscure words (jejune, probity, prescient). My favorite sentence: "Surely his woulds have had time to heal? They suppurate." I found Harrison's article revealing and entertaining, yet I got tired of having to stop so often to consult my dictionary.

CAROL CHASTANG, Reseda
