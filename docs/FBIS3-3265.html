Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Hohhot, March 2 (XINHUA) -- North China's Inner Mongolia Autonomous Region has stepped up implementation of its development strategy and a new pattern of opening-up has taken shape. The series of preferential policies and deepening of the internal reforms of the foreign trade system have effectively helped expand the scope of business activities with foreign enterprises and regions. By the end of 1993, the number of foreign trade companies, border barter-trade companies and export-oriented industrial enterprises being granted the managerial rights in imports and exports all over the 12 leagues and cities of Inner Mongolia exceeded 90, an increase of about 30 percent over the previous year. Agency services of the 90-plus privileged economic entities have channeled products of thousands of enterprises of Inner Mongolia to international markets and introduced foreign technology and products to domestic markets. Last year, trade volume of imports and exports of the autonomous region reached 1.2 billion U.S. dollars, increasing by 28.2 percent over that of the previous year. According to local foreign trade officials, more and more foreign investors have recognized the growing importance of Inner Mongolia, which set up economic ties with 82 countries and regions of the world in the year 1993. Foreign-funded enterprises have mushroomed in fields like energy, transportation, machinery, electronics, building materials and textiles with capital coming from 31 countries and regions including the United States, Russia, Japan and the Republic of Korea. International economic and technological cooperation have also been expanded. The region has signed contracts for 143 labor service and construction projects with foreign countries. Domestic enterprises and companies of the Inner Mongolia Autonomous Region have also established 83 subsidiaries or joint ventures in Russia and Republic of Mongolia.
