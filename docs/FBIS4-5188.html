BFN [Text] Phnom Penh AKP April 8 -- Four sets of anti-tank missile were seized in Siem Reap Province on April 5. Police said the four missiles known as "Bruss" were found in a boot of a taxi driving from Banteay Meanchey to Siem Reap. One man on board, suspected to be an owner of the missiles, was arrested when the police found the missiles at their checkpoint. The taxi driver Yoeun Yang, 43, said the arrested man had ordered him to pick up a well-wrapped package, in which the missiles were found, at a place in Banteay Meanchey Province. Besides the arrested man, there were one woman and two children on board. Police said the four missiles were expected to be used for destroying tanks of the Regional Military Unit 4 stationed in Siem Reap Town. Chea Sophat, the two-star police general, said he believed that the missiles, if not found, would have been used for not only destroying tanks but also for other purposes.
