Language: Japanese Article Type:BFN [By Masaru Soma] [Text] Hong Kong, 14 Dec -- A Western diplomatic source here disclosed on 14 December that Deng Xiaoping, China's most influential leader, left Beijing for Shanghai on 10 December. The source stated that during his stay in Shanghai, Deng will start working out plans to reshuffle top military personnel. The military leadership reshuffle was originally expected to take place in mid- December, but it has now become most likely that the reshuffle will take place after next February. Every year, Deng spends the season of the year's end and the new year as well as the Lunar New Year (which falls on 10 February) in Shanghai partly for the purpose of passing the winter. Citing as the basis for his observation that Deng has arrived in Shanghai, the same source points out that the Xijiao Guesthouse where Deng usually stays and its neighboring area have been put under stricter guard than usual. Moreover, an informed source in Hong Kong states that "Deng's movements have become slower than before" but that "there are no problems with his health because his thinking is clear and he has a nimble mind." At the same time, the same source points out that because senior military leaders are accompanying him on his current visit to Shanghai, it can be expected that plans for the military leadership reshuffle are being worked out now.
