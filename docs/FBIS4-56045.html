BFN [Text] Belgrade, May 25 (TANJUG) -- Yugoslav Human and Minority Rights Minister Margit Savovic, who has met with the charge d'affaires at the Hungarian Embassy in Belgrade, Andras Vincze, hailed the Hungarian proposal that the two countries' experts meet to exchange experiences on the resolution of the status of their national minorities. Savovic informed Vincze about the comprehensive preparation of a federal law on minorities in Yugoslavia, the Federal Information Ministry announced. The two officials also discussed the effects of the international sanctions on the living conditions and health of the Yugoslavs and the problems in ferrying humanitarian aid via Hungary. Vincze said that Hungary, as a member of the United Nations, is obliged to honour its decisions, but is doing everything possible to facilitate the transport of relief aid to Yugoslavia. He said that last year 12,000 trucks carrying humanitarian aid had passed through Hungary, and that there had been problems only in four-five cases due to a lack of adequate documentation.
