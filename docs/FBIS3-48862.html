Language: Japanese Article Type:BFN [Text] Washington, JIJI -- A senior U.S. Government official warned on 26 January that if the Japan-U.S. economic framework talks end in failure, the United States would use sanctions as a weapon to press Japan to open its market. President Clinton met on 25 January with other U.S. Government officials to review the Japan-U.S. economic framework talks. The same senior U.S. Government official made it clear that during the meeting, President Clinton approved of U.S. negotiators taking a tough stance and making no concessions at the bilateral economic framework talks. The same senior U.S. Government official also disclosed that during the meeting, a U.S. official, while pointing out that no substantial progress has been made in the Japan-U.S. economic framework talks, stressed the need for both Japan and the United States to work to make a success of the Japan-U.S. summit talks scheduled for 11 February, and asserted that the talks should hammer out trade accords beneficial to the United States.
