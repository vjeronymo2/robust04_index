BFN [Text] Belgrade, June 14 (TANJUG) -- Sporadic explosions could still be heard at Tuesday noon from an army ammunition dump in Lisicji Potok in Belgrade which was struck by a lightning while a thunderstorm was raging over Belgrade on Monday night. Military sources say that no one was killed in the explosion, that the situation is "under control" and that there is "no danger of the explosion spreading and endangering civilian facilities." Early on Tuesday, reporters and photographers were denied access for security reasons to the underground ammunition dump where explosives for military exercises are kept. A team of TANJUG's reporters who managed to come near the dump situated in the popular Belgrade picnic site of Kosutnjak say the explosions occur at approximately 40-minute intervals. The whole area smells of gunpowder and is covered by a cloud of whitish smoke. The road to the dump is heavily guarded by army and [word indistinct] forces who have advised the residents of Kosutnjak to avoid walking in the woods for fear that some explosives might have been blown onto the grass. Teams of military experts who are clearing up the terrain say that the rain is helping them prevent the fire from spreading. A part of the residential area near the dump was evacuated in the night between Monday and Tuesday. Widow of the late Yugoslav president Josip Broz Tito, Jovanka Broz, whose residence is located near the ammunition dump, was among them.
