Language: English Article Type:BFN ["News Release"] [Text] Tallinn, March 17 -- An Estonian weekly must pay damages and apologize publicly for publishing a list of alleged KGB informers in five articles last spring, the Tallinn City Court ruled on Thursday [17 March]. The court ruled the published data was false and the owner of the paper, AS Myyt, must in ten days apologize in the weekly "Eesti Aeg"( Estonian time) before the seven plaintiffs and play damages in the amount of 70,000 kroons ($5,000). "Eesti Aeg" in its five issues last spring published the list of alleged KGB informers claiming the list had been found in a notebook of a KGB officer. The list was quite lengthy, but only seven people decided to take legal action.
