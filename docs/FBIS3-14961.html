Language: Russian Article Type:BFN ["Decree of the Russian Federation Federal Assembly State Duma on Declaring a Political and Economic Amnesty" signed by I.P. Rybkin, chairman of the Russian Federation Federal Assembly State Duma, and dated Moscow, 23 February 1994] [Text] With a view to national reconciliation and to achieving civil peace and accord, in accordance with Article 103 Part 1 Point "f" of the Russian Federation Constitution, the Russian Federation Federal Assembly State Duma decrees: 1. All criminal cases being processed by investigators and cases not examined by the courts be terminated with respect to persons facing criminal proceedings: a) with regard to the events of 19-21 August 1991 connected with the formation of the State Committee for the State of Emergency (SCSE) and with participation in its activities; b) with regard to the clash between demonstrators and the personnel of internal affairs organs 1 May 1993 in Moscow; c) over participation in the events of 21 September-4 October 1993 in Moscow connected with the publication of the Russian Federation president's edict No 1400 of 21 September 1993 "On Stage-by-Stage Constitutional Reform in the Russian Federation" and opposition to its implementation, regardless of how these actions may be described according to articles of the RSFSR Criminal Code. 2. Persons sentenced for actions envisaged by Point 1 of the present decree are to be released from punishment. 3. The following are to be released from punishment in the form of incarceration and also from punishments not involving incarceration: a) persons sentenced for crimes envisaged by Article 88 Part 1 of the RSFSR Criminal Code; b) persons sentenced for crimes envisaged by Article 92 Part 1 and Part 2 of the RSFSR Criminal Code; c) persons sentenced for crimes envisaged by Article 170 Part 1, Article 171 Part 1, and Articles 172 and 175 of the RSFSR Criminal Code. 4. All criminal cases being processed by investigators and cases not examined by the courts are to be terminated and persons sentenced under Article 92 Part 3, Article 93 Part 2 and Part 3, and Article 93-1 of the RSFSR Criminal Code for the embezzlement of state and public property on a large or especially large scale are to be released from punishment provided that the aforementioned crimes were committed during the period before the elimination of the USSR (before December 1991, inclusive), aside from those who were sentenced for the embezzlement of state and public property by means of larceny, theft, robbery, or fraud. 5. The processing of all investigations and cases not examined by the courts regarding crimes committed by the persons listed in Point 3 is to be terminated before the entry into force of the present decree. 6. Points 3-4 of the present decree do not extend to the following convicted persons: a) persons with previous convictions for premeditated crimes and sentenced again to incarceration for premeditated crimes; b) recidivists who are deemed especially dangerous; c) malicious violators of the terms governing the serving of their sentences; d) persons previously released from places of incarceration by amnesty or pardon who have committed another premeditated crime. 7. Persons who, in addition to punishment for a crime, have been ordered to undergo compulsory treatment for alcoholism, drug addiction, or venereal diseases and who are subject to release from punishment under the present decree are to be amnestied at the end of their full course of treatment. 8. The decree applies to persons sentenced by Russian Federation courts, and also USSR courts. 9. This decree enters into force the moment it is published. Points 1 and 2 of the decree are to be implemented immediately. Points 3, 4, and 8 are to be implemented within six months under the procedure laid down by the decree "On Declaring an Amnesty in Connection With the Adoption of the Russian Federation Constitution," and Point 4 of the present decree is to be implemented by the courts. [Signed] I.P. Rybkin, chairman of the Russian Federation Federal Assembly State Duma [Dated] Moscow, 23 February 1994 No 65-1 GD
