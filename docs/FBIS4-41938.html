BFN [Text] Yerevan, June 28 (SNARK) -- Armenia is the only country in CIS where the growth of basic economic indicators is observed. According to the State Statistical Administration data published in Yerevan today, the produced national income during the five months of this year has increased as compared to the corresponding period last year by 7.5 percent and the volume of industrial output grew by 12 percent. At the same time, say specialists, such a growth indicates only insignificant improvement in the state of economy where a steep slump was observed in 1992-93. Due to the permanent blockade of communications and insolvency, Armenia, still experiences a shortage of raw materials and electricity and the life level of the republic continues to remain the lowest among the CIS countries.
