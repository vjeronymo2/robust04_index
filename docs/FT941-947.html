940328 FT 28 MAR 94 / Hardship fears for travellers A children's charity fears proposed legislation in the criminal justice and public order bill to be debated in parliament today will 'cause considerable hardship' for travellers and gypsies. If passed, the bill would give local authorities and police extensive new powers to evict families from unauthorised sites. Mr Michael Taylor, Save the Children's UK director, said it would mean the frequent eviction of travellers and gypsies who could be forced into moving constantly from one illegal stopping place to another.
