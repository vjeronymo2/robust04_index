BFN [By ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondent Dmitriy Chubarov] [Text] Moscow, 7 Jun -- The coming signing of a package of agreements with the Organization of Economic Cooperation and Development (OECD) will be a "major step on the road to implementing a strategy of creating political conditions for an equitable partnership between Russia and the leading Western powers". This was stated today by Russian Federation Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev before his flight to Paris. The head of the foreign policy department flew out to the French capital to take part in a special meeting with ministers of the member-states of the OECD which unites the most developed countries in the world. The ceremony of the signing of a declaration on cooperation between the Russian Federation and the OECD and also an agreement on the OECD's privileges and immunities in the Russian Federation will take place in Paris tomorrow, 8 June. Dwelling in this connection on Russia's relations with Europe, the minister announced that at the end of the month a big agreement might be signed with the European Union at summit level. "Thus," he added, "this summer we will already start to reap the economic harvest of our political partnership." At the request of journalists, A. Kozyrev expressed his opinion on the "nuclear problem" on the Korean peninsula and the calls by some countries for economic sanctions to be introduced against the DPRK. "In the final count," he said, "it may come to a stage-by- stage introduction of sanctions. But before that all the other possibilities should be exhausted, including the possibility of holding an international conference." Speaking of Russia's possible participation in the "Partnership for Peace" program, the Russian Federation foreign minister stressed that this program not regarded by the Russian side as a panacea. In Istanbul at the end of the week he is to take part in a session of the North Atlantic Cooperation Council. "This course of action is in keeping with our strategy for European partnership," A. Kozyrev said. He said that in the near future the Russian president will make initiatives on this, initiatives in which the central role in the coordinating the process of integration in Europe is allotted to the CSCE.
