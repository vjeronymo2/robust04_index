Language: German Article Type:BFN [Text] Bonn (DPA) -- On Thursday [10 March], Defense Minister Volker Ruehe, of the Christian Democratic Union [CDU], met Lithuanian Foreign Minister Povilas Gylys for an exchange of opinions. The Defense Ministry has stated that the two ministers discussed the situation in the Baltic republics and the CIS. In addition, they also broached issues of European security architecture and bilateral cooperation. They also talked at great length about details of NATO's Partnership for Peace offer to the countries of East Europe. Gylys had met Foreign Minister Klaus Kinkel, of the Free Democratic Party of Germany [FDP], on Wednesday. That meeting was also attended by Estonian Minister Without Portfolio Juri Luik and Latvian Foreign Minister Georgs Andrejevs. After his talks, Kinkel warned Moscow against "adding" further conditions to delay the 31 August 1994 deadline for the withdrawal of the Russian forces from Estonia and Latvia.
