Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, Feb. 25 KYODO -- Peru's prime minister and Mongolia's president separately said Friday [25 February] their countries want to become members of the Asia-Pacific Economic Cooperation (APEC) forum. In Tokyo, Peruvian Prime Minister Efrain Goldenberg, who is concurrently foreign minister, asked Japanese Foreign Minister Tsutomu Hata for support in Lima's bid to participate in APEC, Foreign Ministry officials said. Hata responded positively, saying he hopes to relay to related nations Peru's desire to take part in the regional economic group, they said. Hata was quoted as saying the Peruvian Government led by President Alberto Fujimori has promoted democratization and is making efforts to resolve difficulties. Goldenberg, visiting Japan to attend a seminar, conveyed a message by Fujimori that he is willing to invite Hata to his country, the officials said. In Bangkok, Mongolian President Punsalmaagiyn Ochirbat met Thai Premier Chuan Likphai and said Mongolia hopes to join APEC and have close relations with the Association of Southeast Asian Nations (ASEAN), Thai officials said. Ochirbat is scheduled to visit Laos and Vietnam following Thailand.
