BFN [Text] Today in Kiev, the Ukrainian-Chinese Intergovernmental Commission for Trade and Economic Cooperation held its first meeting. As Deputy Prime Minister Valeriy Shmarov, head of the Ukrainian half of the commission, noted, the body is supposed to establish new bases for developing bilateral trade and economic relations, as well as define the primary direction of cooperation. In the opinion of Valeriy Shmarov, the annual volume of trade between Ukraine and China could reach a level of $4 billion. Last year, it amounted to $670 million, with Ukrainian exports totalling $580 million.
