BFN [Announcer-read report over video; from the "NHK News" program] [Text] Prime Minister Tsutomu Hata expressed his serious regrets at the House of Councillors' Budget Committee this morning on North Korea's (the DPRK's) announcement of its withdrawal from the IAEA (International Atomic Energy Agency). He indicated that he would continue to persist in persuading North Korea to agree to hold dialogue with the international community. [Begin Hata recording] With regard to the withdrawal from IAEA, it is indeed unfortunate. However, this still does not mean withdrawal from the nuclear NPT [Nonproliferation Treaty]. In the circumstances, we still hold hopes that they [the North Koreans] will open their doors to the international community as soon as possible to eliminate suspicions about their development of nuclear weapons. [end recording] Hata thus indicated his intention to persist in persuading North Korea to engage in dialogue with the international community.
