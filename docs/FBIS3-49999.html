Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tirana, January 14 (ATA) -- The Albanian Government took recently a decision for the increase of the price of fuels which comes into force January 16, 1993. The proposal for this increase was presented by the Ministry of Finance and Ministry of Mining and Energy Resources. The price of one ton of crude oil will increase from 4,500 leks to 6,500 leks, a liter benzine [gasoline] from 34 to 46 leks and imported benzine from 62 leks per liter to 82 leks, while the price of gasoil will increase from 25 leks to 38 leks per liter and the price of natural gas from 3,000 leks to 6,300 leks per 1,000 cubic meter. According to Niqifor Rexho, director of Finance Department at the Ministry of Mineral and Energy Resources, there is a program which intends to bring the prices of oil and its by products to the level of prices in the neighbouring countries. According to Mr. Rexho, the price of crude oil is expected to amount to $80 per ton.
