Language: English Article Type:BFN [By BUSINESS-TASS] [Text] Moscow February 1 TASS -- Russian budget expenses in 1993 were close to 61 trillion roubles and comprised 37.8 per cent of the gross national product, according to the Finance Ministry. The figure for 1992 equalled 33 per cent and the 1993 increase was caused mostly by the budget article called "Other Expenses" which the ministry did not specify in its report on Tuesday. The "other expenses" grew from 13.4 per cent in 1992 to 21.4 per cent in 1993. Defence expenses dropped from 14.3 to 12.4 per cent. Although the major share of appropriations goes to the national economy, it decreased from 34.5 to 27.3 per cent. More money was allocated to "social and cultural measures" - 23.1 per cent in 1992 and 24.9 per cent in 1993. Approrpiations for science, police, governing and controlling bodies, as well as for foreign economic activities varied from 1.6 to 5.1 per cent in 1993. Finance Ministry experts said that budget spendings increased because of credits for grain purchases in Russia, for conversion and loans to CIS states.
