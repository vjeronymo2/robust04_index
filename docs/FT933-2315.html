930920 FT 20 SEP 93 / Izetbegovic defiant Bosnia's President Alija Izetbegovic at the weekend cast doubt over whether he would endorse a plan for the republic's partition after mediators failed to win concessions from Serb and Croat leaders, writes Laura Silber in Belgrade. 'Peace is everybody's dream in Bosnia-Hercegovina, but not peace at any price,' Mr Izetbegovic said. Lord Owen and Mr Thorvald Stoltenberg, the international mediators, had accelerated their efforts to clinch a deal between the three leaders of Bosnia's rival communities. But Lord Owen was quoted as saying that, if there was no sign of a breakthrough, a meeting scheduled for tomorrow might not take place.
