October 21, 1989, Saturday, Home Edition

ART AND AIDS EDUCATION

Art potentially educates. Art has played an invaluable role in the ongoing battle to fight AIDS. Now the National Endowment for the Arts "will be forced to establish internal standards to red-flag those grant applications whose contents may be controversial" ("Paranoia, Confusion Left in Wake of Arts Debate," Oct. 10).

This red-flagging will undoubtedly encompass all artistic endeavors that explore the drama of AIDS. Once again, opportunities to educate the American public about AIDS have been squelched because of insidious homophobia.

Art potentially saves lives. Censorship is a killer.

MICHAEL KEARNS, Artists Confronting AIDS, Hollywood
