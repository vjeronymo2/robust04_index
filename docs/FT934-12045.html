931028 FT 28 OCT 93 / UK Company News: Exchange benefits aid Rowe Evans CONTINUING firmness in palm oil prices and the weakness of sterling against the Indonesian rupiah helped Rowe Evans Investments achieve a 48 per cent profits increase in the six months to end-June. On turnover up from Pounds 2.42m to Pounds 2.59m profits before tax improved from Pounds 926,000 to Pounds 1.37m. Comparatives, however, included turnover of Pounds 311,000 and profits of Pounds 77,000 from discontinued operations. Earnings per share rose to 1.74p (1.13p). Sterling had, on average, been 15 per cent weaker against the rupiah during the period, the directors said.
