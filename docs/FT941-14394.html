940120 FT 20 JAN 94 / International Company News: Microsoft climbs 22% to Dollars 289m Microsoft, the world's largest personal computer software supplier, yesterday reported a 22 per cent increase in second-quarter net income, from Dollars 236m to Dollars 289m. The figures translated into earnings per share of 95 cents, up from 78 cents, and were struck on revenues 20 per cent higher at Dollars 1.22bn. The results, announced after the market closed, were in line with analysts' expectations. The consensus Wall Street forecast was for earnings per share of 93 cents. Microsoft said several factors contributed to the improvement, including an enthusiastic response to its newest editions of Microsoft Office in the US, together with the 'ongoing adoption of the Microsoft Windows operating system'. For the six months, Microsoft reported net income of Dollars 528m, or Dollars 1.74 a share, up from Dollars 445m, or Dollars 1.48, in the same period of last year. Revenues rose from Dollars 1.76bn to Dollars 2.11bn. Before the figures were announced, Microsoft shares fell Dollars 1 3/8 to Dollars 83 1/4 in trading on the Nasdaq market. However, they were quoted in after-hours trading at Dollars 84 1/8.
