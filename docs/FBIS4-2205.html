BFN [Text] Beijing, April 6 (XINHUA) -- The mainland will properly handle the boat tragedy that claimed the lives of 24 Taiwanese tourists and eight mainlanders, said a senior official from the Association for Relations Across the Taiwan Straits (ARATS). In a telephone conversation this afternoon with Chiao Jen-ho, vice-chairman and secretary general of the Taiwan-based Straits Exchange Foundation (SEF), Tang Shubei, vice-president of ARATS, said that leaders of the Chinese central government and other departments concerned attached serious importance to the accident. He said Zhejiang Province had made emergency efforts to locate and recover the remains of the dead. The local authorities had also given a lot of help to the relatives of the victims who went to Zhejiang to settle the matter. The 32 victims died on March 31 when the tourist boat they boarded burst into flames on Qiandao Lake in East China's Zhejiang Province. Tang said that he understood SEF's expectations. He said that ARATS had done everything it could to keep in touch with SEF. He told Chiao that Beijing had dispatched a team of specialists to the accident site to conduct an investigation. Tang said that such a tragedy was the last thing the two sides want to see, adding that there are more than one million Taiwan compatriots visiting the mainland every year. It was a rare accident, he said. Chiao conveyed to Tang the concerns and opinions of the Taiwan public. Tang said he will forward the information to relevant departments.
