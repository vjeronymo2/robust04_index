Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, March 18 KYODO -- The government announced Friday [18 March] it is suspending new economic aid to Nigeria, which has been under military rule since last November. Nigeria is not eligible for Japan's official development assistance (ODA) since the regime has dissolved the parliament, banned political rallies and taken other undemocratic measures, government officials said. Japan, however, will continue to provide emergency humanitarian aid to the west African country, they said. Japan will resume aid when Nigeria restores civilian rule, the officials said. By fiscal 1992, Japan had provided 87.4 billion yen in yen-based loans to Nigeria, the second largest amount it had given an African nation, next to Kenya.
