Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Report by Cecilia Pachano from "The News" newscast] [Text] According to Copei [Social Christian Party] leader Eduardo Fernandez, his party is willing to establish a national agreement to support President-elect Rafael Caldera, thus showing its support for the democratic system. Fernandez made the statement as he was leaving a breakfast meeting with President Ramon J. Velasquez during which they analyzed the country's current situation and the issues the Caldera administration will face. Eduardo Fernandez insisted that he will not return to the post of secretary general of his party, indicating that it is a role he has already fulfilled. He said Copei is a party that reaches agreements, and that is why it would endorse Caldera's request for support so he can have the political backing necessary to manage the country over the next five years. [Begin Fernandez recording] The magnitude of the country's problems demands an effort of national understanding and unity. We must foster a climate to face the country's basic problems. [end recording] Asked about the initiative to allow National Convergence to lead the Senate, he said it is one possibility that must be analyzed, adding that it is very possible it will become a reality.
