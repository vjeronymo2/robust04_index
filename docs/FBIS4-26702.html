BFN [By Bear Li] [Text] Taipei, June 24 (CNA) -- The Ministry of Foreign Affairs on Friday [24 June] said that the government has no concrete plans to set up a representative office in India. Foreign Ministry Spokesman Rock Leng made the statement in response to press queries on the possibility of a breakthrough in Taiwan's relationship with India. The two nations have had no formal contacts since India recognized Beijing in 1949. Commenting on remarks by visiting former Indian Ambassador to Beijing A.P. Venkateswara that India will establish an office in Taipei by the end of August, Leng said that his ministry needs to verify that Venkateswara's remarks reflect the policy of the Indian Government. He said that the government hopes to develop relations with India, but that developing a relationship would take time. Venkateswara, a member of an Indian delegation that arrived here last Friday, met with Vice Foreign Minister Fang Chin-yen on earlier this week, Other members of the Indian delegation include former Foreign Minister I.K. Gujral and the former Governor of India's central Bank, R.N. Malhotra. While meeting Gujral Friday morning, President Li Teng-hui said that he hoped Gujral's visit would help restart exchanges between the two nations.
