921230 FT 30 DEC 92 / Fleeing Cubans land at Miami A Cuban aircraft with dozens of people aboard landed at Miami's airport yesterday after being hijacked, Reuter reports from Miami. Up to 58 people were thought to be aboard, most of whom had asked for political asylum, according to US authorities in Miami. Customs officials were holding the aircraft pending investigations by US immigration and law enforcement agents. Two men, identified by a Miami television station as the co-pilot and a security guard, were taken from the aircraft with their hands tied after apparently trying to thwart the hijacking. Cuba condemned the hijacking, calling it a 'terrorist act,' and said the US was to blame. Prensa Latina, the Cuban news agency, quoted Cuban officials as saying the hijacking 'was encouraged by the US policy of granting asylum to all illegal emigrants.'
