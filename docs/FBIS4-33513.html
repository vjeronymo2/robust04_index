BFN [Excerpt] Brasilia, 3 Jun (AE) -- Today, one of President Itamar Franco's advisers reported that political pressure on Planalto Palace and several ministries has reduced to only three the number of state-owned companies' directors who were dismissed for converting employees' salaries into Real Value Units by the maximum percentage index. Transportation Minister Rubem Bayma Denys had estimated that at least 50 managers would be dismissed, discharged, or retired as a result of the incident. The official gazette, however, only published the name of three directors to be dismissed: Rilson Carneiro Raposo, Jose Eudes Rangel, and Roberto Vaz da Silva. They are, respectively, the president and directors of the Executive Group for the Study of Transportation Integration Policy (Geipot). [passage omitted]
