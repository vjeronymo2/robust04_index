BFN [Text] Today, Ukraine's President Leonid Kravchuk received Anton Malynovskyy, the president's representative in Zhitomir Oblast, and Ivan Kapshtyk, the president's representative in Kiev Oblast. They discussed the vital issues pertaining to implementation of the provisions of Ukraine's legislation on the social protection of the population that suffered as a result of the accident at the Chernobyl nuclear power station. During the meeting they also discussed issues pertaining to the implementation of the other enforceable enactments relating to the Chernobyl [accident]. The president instructed relevant ministries and services, together with the oblast state administrations, to submit in the shortest possible period of time specific proposals aimed at solving these important problems. The meeting was attended by Heorhiy Hotovchyts, Ukraine's minister for the protection of the population against the after effects of the Chernobyl nuclear power station, and Vasyl Rudenko, the head of the president's service in charge of territories.
