November 11, 1990, Sunday, Valley Edition

HIGH SCHOOL ROUNDUP;

OAK PARK TRIPPED, 18-3, SETTLES FOR LEAGUE TITLE TIE

Cheered on by the Carpinteria High football team, which had more than a slight interest in the outcome, Bishop Diego defeated Oak Park, 18-3, Saturday night in a Tri-Valley League game at San Marcos High.

Oak Park (6-4, 3-1 in league play) must share the league title with Carpinteria.

Bishop Diego's Benito Rivera hauled in a 34-yard pass from Richie Donati in the first quarter to open the scoring. Howie Dougherty added a touchdown later in the quarter.

The only bright moment for Oak Park came in the second quarter on a 24-yard Mike Stern field goal.

Bishop Diego scored its final touchdown when Oak Park's Wes Lones fumbled the second-half kickoff and Danny Kendrick recovered the ball at the Eagle 28. Donati, 10 of 20 for 89 yards and one interception, then passed four yards for his second touchdown. ROB SONNER
