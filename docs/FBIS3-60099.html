Language: Finnish Article Type:CSO [Finnish News Service report: "Nearly Four Years in Prison for Drug Courier in Lahti"] [Text] The Lahti Municipal Court handed a drug courier a prison term of almost four years. The 45-year-old Lahti former businessman was arrested in October by Swedish Customs authorities, with a quantity of almost 8.5 kg of hashish in his possession. A body search of the man after his arrival in Finland additionally turned up four kg of amphetamines. The man has kept silent during the court proceedings. In addition to being sentenced for two cases of aggravated drug smuggling, he was also convicted of four firearms violations. He did admit to having four illegal weapons in his possession. The man received a prison term of 46 months. He will have to reimburse authorities in the amount of 120,000 markkas. This sum will pay for a reward in the amphetamines case and the cost of a fancy rental truck he used for transporting the hashish. It will also pay the fine for the illegally obtained firearms. Lahti Municipal Court determined the value of a submachine gun to be 500 markkas, or the amount that the Armed Forces will have to pay for a replacement.
