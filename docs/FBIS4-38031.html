BFN [Text] Aden, 27 Jun (MENA) -- The self-declared presidential council in south Yemen has called for other countries to intervene to stop the constant northern Yemeni shelling of civilians and vital institutions in Aden and what it termed "the brutal acts of repression by the Sanaa regime in the occupied areas." A statement issued by the council and faxed to MENA tonight says: "The Sanaa regime has no respect for the resolutions and pleas made by the international community. It has consistently declared cease-fires, breached them, and blamed the southerners." The statement accuses Sanaa's authorities of depriving Aden's inhabitants of water, saying that wells are the only source of water following the destruction of water pipes and reservoirs and the purification station. The statement vows continuing "national resistance in the occupied areas" and says that the southerners refuse to surrender.
