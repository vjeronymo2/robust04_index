930215 FT 15 FEB 93 / Tourism survives Czechoslovakia split The split of Czechoslovakia into two independent states has not damaged the tourism industry in the Czech Republic, a Czech Economy Ministry official said yesterday, Reuter reports from Prague. 'The Czech Republic still attracts a lot of foreign tourists . . . more than Slovakia, as has always been the case,' Mr Jiri Cech, head of the ministry's tourism department, said. He said 80 per cent tourists who came to the now defunct Czechoslovakia in 1992 visited the Czech Republic and that this trend had continued in 1993.
