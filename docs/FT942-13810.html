940422 FT 22 APR 94 / World Stock Markets: Mexico recovers lost ground Mexican stocks leapt more than 6 per cent Thursday in a technical rebound after three straight trading session declines. According to preliminary official information, the Mexican market's key IPC index closed up 117.99 points at 2,075.32 - a gain of 6.03 per cent. At the end of 1993, the IPC stood at 2,602.63. Value was a moderate 1.013bn new pesos on 89.1m shares traded. Traders said there was no real reason for the recovery in the Mexcian Stock Exchange other than that investors saw opportunities to buy cheap stocks after the market had lost more than 11 per cent earlier in the week. Traders said, however, the market reacted favorably to gains by New York stocks and also to the news that peace talks between rebel guerrillas in Chiapas and the Mexican government look set to restart next week. In intra-day trading, the market broke back through the 2,000 barrier. Traders said this was a psychological boost to investors. The market fall below 2,000 on Wednesday for the first time since Nov. 9 last year. Most buying was done by foreign investors, with ADRs of Telmex leading the recovery. Telmex L series shares were up 56 centavos, or 6.48 per cent, to 9.2 new pesos. Telmex holding company Grupo Carso was up 6.67 per cent, or 1.7 new pesos, at 27.2 new pesos.
