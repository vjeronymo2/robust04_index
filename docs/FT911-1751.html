910503 FT 03 MAY 91 / UK Company News in Brief AAH HOLDINGS has increased its tally of trading units to 129 with the acquisition of Elliotts Pharmacy, a chain of seven retail pharmacies around Bristol, for Pounds 1.5m in cash and shares. ***** AEGIS GROUP has sold Siebert Head, a packaging design specialist, to its management. ***** ASPREY GROUP has acquired Rene Boivin, a Parisian jeweller. BOOKER has sold L Noel & Sons, a maker and distributor of herbs, spices, cocktail cherries and other fine foods formerly part of Fitch Lovell, for Pounds 8.5m cash. BT has acquired the 55 per cent of Westminster Cable Company it did not own. ***** ESSEX WATER has posted a circular to shareholders detailing proposals to convert status from a statutory water company to a public limited company. On conversion, voting and non-voting irredeemable stock would be converted into voting and non-voting ordinary shares, no longer subject to dividend limits. The present 8.625 per cent redeemable preference stock 1993 will be converted into 9.625 per cent redeemable preference shares 1993, holding cumulative dividend rights and preferential rights on a winding up. ***** HARTSTONE GROUP: in its 1-for-1 rights issue, acceptances have been received in respect of 28.68m new ordinary (96.4 per cent). ***** PICT PETROLEUM has agreed to earn a 5 per cent working interest in Block 115 offshore Vietnam, subject to the approval of the Vietnamese government. ***** REDLAND'S subsidiary Braas, through its Austrian company Bramac, has formed a joint venture in Czechoslovakia to make concrete roof tiles. Redland already has joint ventures in Hungary and Yugoslavia. ***** RENTOKIL GROUP has acquired the tropical plants business of City Gardens, of Boston, US, for an initial Dollars 10m (Pounds 6m) in cash, with further payments up to Dollars 3m dependent on profits.
