940809 FT 09 AUG 94 / International Company News: Chilean sale puts Millicom in black Millicom International Cellular, the Luxembourg-based international cellular communications company listed on Nasdaq, reported pre-tax profit of Dollars 28.2m for the six months to June 30. The profit was due to the sale of the company's Chilean cellular operation for Dollars 37m. Excluding extraordinary gains, the company recorded a net loss of Dollars 5.7m, a 70 per cent reduction on a net loss before extraordinary gains of Dollars 19.3m for the same period in 1993. Revenue was Dollars 34.3m, 56 per cent up on the Dollars 22m in the same period in 1993. Most of the company's 22 operations, in 16 countries, are recent start-ups, and it is expecting to bid for new cellular licences in emerging market countries, particularly in Latin America and the Asia-Pacific region. The number of subscribers rose by 40 per cent in the six months to 85,214. The company's most profitable operations were in Moscow, Sri Lanka, the Philippines and Guatemala.
