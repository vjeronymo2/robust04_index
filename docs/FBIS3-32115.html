Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [From the "Newspaper of the Airwaves" newscast] [Text] Paraguayan President Juan Carlos Wasmosy and Peruvian President Alberto Fujimori have signed a joint political, economic, and cultural declaration at the Government Palace. The document was signed during the first official trip by Wasmosy, who was democratically elected. Wasmosy arrived at the Peruvian Air Force's 8th Group in Lima at 1050. He was accompanied by the foreign affairs and industry ministers. At the 8th Group, he was welcomed with a 21-gun salute and met by Peruvian President Alberto Fujimori, ministers of state and congressmen, following which the National Police band played the Peruvian and Paraguayan national anthems. After the welcoming ceremony, President Wasmosy and Peruvian Foreign Minister Efrain Goldenberg went to the Heroes of Independence Park to place wreaths on monuments to the nation's heroes. At the park, Wasmosy extended a message of brotherhood to the Peruvian people and reiterated his pledge to strengthen Paraguay's ties of friendship with Peru. Wasmosy will lunch with Fujimori at 1330 in the formal dining room at the Government Palace. He will visit the Democratic Constituent Congress at 1600 and then he will attend a solemn session of Lima's Municipal Council, during which he will be declared an illustrious visitor and will be given the keys to the city.
