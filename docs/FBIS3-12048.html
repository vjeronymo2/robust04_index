Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Last week, the Netherlands-Cuba working group held its first plenary session. The business delegation, headed by (Coy Stork), was interested in contacting Cuban enterprises for research, coastal management and shoreline restoration in Cuba and the Caribbean, registration and joint operation of Cuban ships, construction in third countries, and joint productions in the machine tool, food, and textile industries. In exchange, the Netherlanders will supply canned milk, meat, fruit, and vegetables to Cuba. In the 1994-95 work program, the group will promote possibilities for investment in Cuba and will support the efforts of Dutch enterprises established in Havana. The creation of joint enterprises and variants of associations of mutual benefit and the introduction of the scientific-technical, biotechnical, and pharmaceutical industries to the Netherlands market were additional aspects considered in the final document, which was signed by (Van Vough), head of the Dutch section, and Ariel Maso, director of (Cubanique) and head of the Cuban section at the meetings.
