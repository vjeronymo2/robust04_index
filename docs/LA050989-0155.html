May 9, 1989, Tuesday, P.M. Final

SOVIET CLEANUP SHIP SENT HOME A FAILURE

A mammoth Soviet vessel brought in to help clean up the Alaskan oil spill can't handle the hardened, taffy-like crude and is being sent home, authorities said today.

The high-tech, 425-foot Vaydaghubsky, which has been under a $15,000-a-day monthlong Exxon contract to skim the oil spilled March 24, did not have its contract renewed.
