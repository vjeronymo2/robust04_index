BFN [Text] Australia's foreign minister, Gareth Evans, has released a statement on his efforts to help an Australian citizen detained without charge by China. Senator Evans said Australia had been assiduous in its efforts on behalf of James Pang who has been held in Shenzhen detention center since October because of corruption allegations. Senator Evans, who released the 33-point chronology listing Australia's actions on behalf of Mr. Pang since October, expressed surprise at Australia's commitment to the case has been criticized in a Melbourne newspaper report. He also released a copy of the letter that he had written to Mr. Pang's wife in Hong Kong last Friday, saying that Australia was taking the case very seriously. He has raised the issue of the Australian citizen being detained without charge with China's foreign minister, Qian Qichen, with Guangdong vice governor, Lu Ruihua, and with the first vice chairman of China's National People's Congress, Tian Jiyun.
