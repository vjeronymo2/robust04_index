Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Report by Alexandra Ciniglio from the "RPC Television News" newscast] [Text] Costa Rican President Rafael Angel Calderon met with President Guillermo Endara this morning to discuss several topics, including banana export problems. Amid tight security, a protocol ceremony was held at the stairs of the Presidency of the Republic to receive the Costa Rican president. The republican band played both national anthems to welcome Rafael Angel Calderon Fournier. [Begin Calderon recording] We are both ending our respective terms. Currently, relations between Panama and Costa Rica are excellent and this is how they should always be between two fraternal countries. We are going to talk a bit about our bilateral relations and the banana situation which is so important for both countries, including the struggle in which all the Latin American banana-producing countries are engaged. [end recording] Later the Panamanian president awarded Calderon the Manuel Amador Guerrera Order. In the presence of all his ministers Guillermo Endara recognized the neighboring country's support for Panama. President Calderon Fournier awarded the Panamanian president the Juan Mora Fernandez Order, represented by a gold plaque. Presenting Endara with the award, Calderon highlighted the role played by the president in the struggle against the military dictatorship.
