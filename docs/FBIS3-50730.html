Article Type:CSO [Editorial Report] Havana GRANMA in Spanish of 14 December 1993 on page 2 reports that, although the economic situation prohibits the building of new day-care centers, resolution 203 of November 1993 provides for day-care centers for children ages 2 through 5 to be established in sugar- and agricultural-related work centers. According to the article, State resources budgeted for food services and raw materials may not be applied to day care; rather, the work centers must provide for day care from their own resources. If a center does not have the means to establish day care, then food services will be suspended. Health services for the children include a guaranteed supply of potable and boiled water, according to the article.
