Language: Bengali Article Type:BFN [Editorial: "Pulling the United States"] [Text] There was a time when European rulers frequently used to refer to the phrase "the white man's burden." Those were the days of imperialism. By this burden they meant the black people of Africa and Asia. These colonial rulers considered themselves the guardian of the black people. It is true that these blacks were a cause of anxiety for their white masters. Although those days are gone, there is no reason to heave a sigh of relief. At present, the two neighboring countries in the Indian subcontinent, which have become engaged in a dispute over the Kashmir issue, are willing to accept the United States as a mediator to resolve the discord. This reminds us of the bygone days of imperialism. Benazir Bhutto is pulling one hand of President Clinton while the other is being pulled by Narasimha Rao. This is a matter of great delight for President Clinton as the United States wants to be the only protector of the world. What consolation or satisfaction does Islamabad or New Delhi gain by doing all this? What special gains do they expect by calling upon the United States to mediate their dispute? The finance committee chairman of the U.S. Senate and former U.S. ambassador to India, Daniel Patrick Moynihan, was told by Prime Minister Narasimha Rao that all bilateral disputes between India and Pakistan should be resolved in accordance with the Shimla Agreement. He also urged the United States compel Pakistan to sit down for a dialogue to resolve this issue. On the other hand, Pakistan is saying that the situation in Kashmir is gradually becoming explosive. It wants the United States to exert pressure upon India so that an early settlement is reached on this issue. It must be said that a big game is being played with the Kashmir issue. If New Delhi wants to inform the world community about the involvement of Pakistan in the militancy in Kashmir, it should do this work properly and stop issuing contradictory statements. Otherwise, this country will have to face a lot of miseries. It should be admitted that Indian diplomacy has failed to deal properly with the Kashmir issue and Pakistan is taking advantage of this situation. Pakistan is using the United Nations and the Islamic Conference Organization to achieve their designs. It is also approaching anti-Indian officials in the U.S. administration and pleading for the protection of human rights in Kashmir. India has done the right thing by rejecting the offer given by the Islamic Conference Organization to mediate in this issue. As Jammu and Kashmir is a state of India, the sovereignty of the country would have been violated had India accepted this proposal. It should also be realized by India that this is not the proper time to take a stubborn attitude on this issue. India should soften its stand and see whether the allegations of human rights violations is true or not. It would be wise to soften the Indian stand under international or U.S. pressure. There is nothing wrong in saying that India will not accept the illegal demands and evil designs of Pakistan. At the same time no one should be allowed to think that the attitude of India is self-contradictory. *
