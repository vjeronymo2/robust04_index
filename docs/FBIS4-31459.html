BFN [Text] Bratislava, June 7 (CTK) -- Less than a half of Slovak citizens favor the idea of Slovakia's joining NATO, according to a poll conducted by the Slovak Statistical Office in May. Only 15 percent of those questioned said it was most necessary for Slovakia to try to join NATO, while 34 percent believed that it was probably necessary. Almost one Slovak out of five (18 percent) is afraid and 11 percent are sure that such efforts are senseless. 22 percent of those polled did not answer the question. If Slovakia became a member of NATO, almost two fifths of citizens (37 percent) would expect an improvement in Slovakia's defense capability, according to the poll. Four percent of Slovaks said they would expect the defense capability to worsen.
