920429 FT 29 APR 92 / Observer: Upmanship Is there a danger that the renaissance of Sir Edward Heath, elder statesman, is being a bit overdone? Take yesterday. As Betty Boothroyd, the new speaker, led MPs in taking the oath of allegiance and signing the Commons roll, Heath was given precedence over John Major, the prime minister, and the rest of the Cabinet. The last backbencher to be so honoured was Sir Winston Churchill in 1959 when he was shepherded to the front of the queue by Harold Macmillan.
