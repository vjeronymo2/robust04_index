Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Pyongyang, March 13 (KCNA) -- Leaders of different countries referred to the question of Korean reunification when they received DPRK ambassadors. John Samuel Malecela, prime minister of Tanzania, said that the question of Korean reunification must be solved entirely by the efforts of the Korean people themselves and that Tanzania would support the stand of the Democratic People's Republic of Korea for the country's reunification. Anatole Kanyenkiko, prime minister of Burundi, said that the 10-point program of the great unity of the whole nation and formula of reunification through confederacy put forward by the great leader President Kim Il-song are peace-loving and patriotic proposals to reunify the split country and unite the divided compatriots into one through dialogue, not confrontation, and by peaceful means, nor war. Anerood Jugnauth, prime minister of Mauritius, said he supported the Korean people's efforts to reunify the country by peaceful means and expressed the hope that the nuclear issue on the Korean peninsula would be resolved peacefully.
