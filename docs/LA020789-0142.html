February 7, 1989, Tuesday, Orange County Edition

BRIEFCASE: WHEN DELIVERY RATES INCREASE, SO DOES THE INCOME OF MICRO GENERAL;

SCIENCE/TECHNOLOGY

Most businesses frown on the idea of higher shipping and mailing costs, but the folks over at Micro General Corp. in Irvine smile at such news.

Micro General's business benefits when delivery rates change, as they did Monday when general rate increases by United Parcel Service went into effect.

The Irvine company makes computerized shipping systems and postal scales for use in shipping departments and office mail rooms. Every time a major shipping company, such as UPS or the Postal Service, changes its rates, Micro General enjoys a windfall.

The reason is this: The heart of every Micro General product is a tiny computer chip on which is stored information about shipping company rates. The computerized shipping systems enable a company's mail room, for example, to comparison shop among different shipping firms to find the least costly way to send a package.

Each time there is a rate change, the microchip must be replaced. In the case of the UPS increase, most of Micro General's 15,000 customers will have to pay the Irvine firm $115 to order the new chip.

The result, Micro General says, will be a 25% windfall in its revenue for the current quarter ending in March. A company spokesman, Charles Ramsey, says the firm is not forecasting what its total revenue will be for the period.

Micro General had earnings of $202,668 on revenue of $4.4 million for the 9 months ended last September.
