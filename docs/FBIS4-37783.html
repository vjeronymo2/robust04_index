BFN [Text] The YI'UD [Independent and Democratic Israel] party's council has given its backing to negotiations with Labor to enter the coalition. Any agreement will be brought before the council. Meanwhile, Tzomet leader Refa'el Eytan said he believes that there will be legal complications with YI'UD joining the coalition. He said he was not sure whether Gonen Segev could serve as a minister or Alex Goldfarb as a deputy minister. A Qol Yisra'el reporter says the attorney general believes there is no legal problem with Gonen Segev serving as a minister, while the prime minister's legal adviser believes there is no possibility of appointing someone from a breakaway faction to the post of minister.
