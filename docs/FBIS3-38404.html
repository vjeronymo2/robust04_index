Language: German Article Type:BFN [Interview with SPD Chairman Rudolf Scharping by unidentified correspondent; place and date not given: "SPD Chief Scharping: The CDU Urgently Needs Rest..."] [Text] [BILD] Mr. Scharping, please tell us whom you would take into your government as ministers.... [Scharping] Later! In addition to good women and men in political offices and functions, we also have excellent people in industry, for example: Karl Otto Poehl, Edzard Reuter, Manfred Lahnstein, and many others. They are experts! I also like to use their technical knowledge. [BILD] What would you do differently if you were in government? [Scharping] I would keep the ministers' salaries frozen for some time to come, reduce the number of ministries, and appoint fewer state secretaries. In the Construction Ministry alone, there are three state secretaries -- this is a pure waste of money. [BILD] With which partner do you want to implement this? [Scharping] This is a question about a coalition through the back door. If the Social Democratic Party of Germany [SPD] is the strongest party, then the coalition partners will all come by themselves. The will to participate in government is in the nature of the liberals, but also of the greens. [BILD] ...and of the Christian Democratic Union/Christian Social Union [CDU/CSU]? [Scharping] I want the CDU/CSU to have a period of regeneration, which it needs urgently -- and that in the opposition...! [BILD] As things look, the next president will be Roman Herzog. Will the SPD withdraw Johannes Rau? [Scharping] Johannes Rau is the best candidate for this office. In the Federal Assembly, there are enough sovereign women and men who orient themselves toward what is best for Germany and what is the desire of the overwhelming majority. Mr. Kohl initially wanted Johannes Rau, then Mr. Heitmann, and now Herzog. The first choice was and is the best. [BILD] Must the more recent history of the SPD be rewritten after the revelations of Brandt's notes? [Scharping] No, the accusation that Herbert Wehner did the work of the other side is slander. There is no evidence of that. Willy Brandt's widow has not done herself a favor. I am sad rather than outraged about this matter. [BILD] Will Mrs. Seebacher-Brandt be expelled from the SPD? [Scharping] I do not think that this is an appropriate way to settle the conflict. There is a party disciplinary procedure underway against her, however; I have no business interfering with the responsible arbitration committee.
