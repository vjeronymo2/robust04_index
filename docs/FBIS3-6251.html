Language: English Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] Security at the Interior Ministry will be stepped up from today, with the offices of Interior Minister Chawalit Yongchaiyut being declared a "restricted zone". The offices of the five deputy interior ministers, the permanent secretary and deputy permanent secretaries have also been declared "restricted zones", where unauthorised entry is prohibited. A source said yesterday the tightening of security at the ministry was agreed on at a meeting on Friday in the aftermath of bomb attacks on the homes of Social Action Party [SAP] leader Montri Phongpanit, SAP MP Bunphan Khaewatthana, Permanent Labour Secretary Sawai Phrammani in Nakhon Ratchasima and on a locomotive in Songkhla. As part of the new measures, the source said, the offices in the Interior Ministry of the Local Administration Department, the Secretariat, the Permanent Secretary, the Welfare and Labour Protection Department and the Institute of Prince Damrong Rachanuphap are now regarded as restricted zones. Earlier, Permanent Interior Secretary Aree Wong-araya had declared his office and the area in front of it off-limits to reporters. [passage omitted]
