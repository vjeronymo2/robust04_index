October 15, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

'SEX' AND ART

O'Neill displays shortsightedness when, in his assessment of "sex," he mistakes a laconic style for shallowness. He refuses to believe that this "yuppie soap opera" could reveal the emptiness of modern life. (A stronger argument might have been that the use of yuppies to depict emptiness was too easy.)

O'Neill obviously has definite ideas about what constitutes a work of art, and anything that does not conform to those preconceived notions doesn't deserve further consideration. This is so much easier than trying to approach a work on its own terms. But that would require thought.

MICHAEL BALLARD

Long Beach
