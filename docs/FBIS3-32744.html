Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Santa Fe de Bogota, 12 Feb (EFE) -- Official sources reported today, Saturday, that Alvaro Solano, a Colombian guerrilla leader also known as "El Guajiro," who is considered to be one of the chiefs of the Maoist dissidents from the Popular Liberation Army (EPL), was captured today in Neiva, capital of Huila Department (in the south). The sources added that "thanks to some information," police arrested "El Guajiro" at Los Garzones airport in Neiva as he prepared to board a domestic flight. The EPL dissident rebel, who did not want to sign the peace agreement with the government in January 1991, is being interrogated today by authorities. Solano or "El Guajiro" is accused of rebellion, association with criminal intent, and terrorism, among other charges. Last 29 January, authorities captured another rebel from that group in Cartagena de Indias (in the north) called Guido Meneses Ruiz, the alleged author of the murder of 50 former members of his organization who rejoined civilian life through the EPL agreements in 1991.
