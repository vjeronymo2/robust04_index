Language: English Article Type:BFN ["Pool" item] [Text] Sarajevo, Mar 17 (TANJUG) -- Serbs and Moslems agreed on Thursday at Sarajevo Airport to open up so-called "blue roads" for the civilian population and humanitarian supplies in the area of the capital of the former Yugoslav Republic of Bosnia-Herzegovina ravaged by a 23-month-old war. The agreement was signed by the speaker of the Bosnian Serb parliament, Momcilo Krajisnik, and the deputy prime minister of the Moslem government in Sarajevo, Husan Muratovic, at a U.N. sponsored meeting. The agreement provides for opening the road which links Sarajevo and Visoko via Vogosca, corridors linking the Sarajevo Serb suburbs of Lukavica and Ilidza with Sarajevo's Moslem suburbs of Dobrinja and Butmir as well as the bridge linking the suburb of Grbavica with the Moslem part of Novo Sarajevo. The agreement is to take effect on March 23 and U.N. peace-keepers deployed in Sarajevo are to supervise its implementation. A ceasefire has been in force between Serb and Moslem forces in the Sarajevo area since February 10. They have withdrawn their heavy weapons from an area 20 kilometers in radius around Sarajevo or placed them under U.N. control.
