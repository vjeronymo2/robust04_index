Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] China and Iran have asked Islamabad to defer voting on the human rights resolution on Kashmir in Geneva. This was announced by the Pakistan foreign minister, Sardar Asif Ahmad Ali, at Islamabad a short while ago. He told a press conference that consequently Pakistan has authorized China and Iran to decide the best course they feel is available for it. Earlier, Beijing and Tehran approached Islamabad to postpone for three hours the voting on the resolution this evening so that further consultation could be held on certain proposals made by them. These options included either withdrawal of the resolution or its indefinite deferment. PTI, quoting observers in Islamabad, says Pakistan can agree to withdraw the resolution.
