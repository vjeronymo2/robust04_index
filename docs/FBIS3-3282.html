Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Macao, March 17 (XINHUA) -- Tangshan city of north China's Hebei Province opened a seven-day export commodities fair here this afternoon. It is the first time that the city has conducted such a large- scale trade fair overseas. The city's trade delegation brought to the fair more than 200 varieties of commodities including foodstuffs, local products, hardware, mineral products, chemicals, medicines, machinery and equipment, textile, clothes, arts and crafts and pottery and porcelain products. They also brought over 300 economic and technical cooperative programs covering machinery, chemistry, light industry, building materials and porcelain. As an important industrial city in north China, Tangshan reported a total industrial and agricultural output value of 43.24 billion yuan (five billion U.S. dollars) in 1993 and recorded 26.4 billion yuan (three billion U.S. dollars) in terms of gross domestic product and 330 million U.S. dollars in export earnings. At present, the city has more than 3,500 industrial enterprises producing over 1,400 varieties of products, some 350 kinds of products are exported to more than 90 countries and regions worldwide. Boasting more than 20 trade companies and a dozen of industrial enterprises which have the right to manage their own export and import business, Tangshan now has set up trade relations with more than 100 countries and regions worldwide. Its export commodities include coal, rolled steel, cement, porcelain, corn, peanut and Chinese chestnut.
