Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The president of the National Assembly of Burundi and acting president of Frodebu [Front for Democracy in Burundi] party, Mr. Sylvestre Ntibantunganya, has paid a one-day working visit to Uganda at the invitation of President Yoweri Museveni. Mr. Ntibantunganya, who was leading a high-powered ministerial delegation, was received at the airport by vice chairman of the NRM [National Resistance Movement] and the NRC [National Resistance Council], Al-Hajj Moses Kigongo, the Burundi ambassador to Uganda, Mr. Havyrimana, and other senior government officials. From the airport, the Burundi delegation went to Lake Victoria Hotel, Entebbe, where they held talks with President Yoweri Museveni. The talks were also attended by the vice chairman of the NRC, Al-Hajj Moses Kigongo, and the director general of the Internal Security Organization, Brigadier Jim Muhezi. A press release issued after the talks said the two delegations met in a brotherly and (?good) atmosphere and exchanged views on the current political situation in Burundi. The Burundi delegation briefed President Museveni on the political and security situation prevailing in Burundi and also the background to the crisis the country is going through. President Museveni reiterated his deep sorrow at the death of the late President Ndadaye, Pontien Karibwami, president of the National Assembly, and other high ranking officials of the Burundi Government. The Ugandan leader pledged his full support to all the people of Burundi in their efforts to re-establish peace and strengthen the process of democracy. The two delegations agreed to meet again as soon as possible to deepen their discussions on a date mutually convenient. In an interview with the UGANDA NEWS AGENCY, UNA, correspondent at the airport before departure for home, Mr. Ntibantunganya expressed his gratitude to President Museveni for his concern for the people of Burundi and said he was confident that the Ugandan leader had the capacity and experience of handling problems like that of Burundi. He said he came to Uganda to seek advice in trying to find an institutional solution to the Burundi crisis which needed all political and social groups in the country to work together. Mr. Ntibantunganya was later seen off by the NRM vice chairman, Al-Hajj Moses Kigongo, and other senior government officials.
