940408 FT 08 APR 94 / International Company News: Dow Jones posts advance of 30% in first period Dow Jones, the media group which publishes the Wall Street Journal, yesterday reported a 30 per cent rise in first quarter net income, helped by sharply improved operating earnings at all of its three divisions. The group reported net income of Dollars 40.17m, or 40 cents a share, compared with Dollars 30.95m, or 31 cents, in the same period of last year. Revenues rose 7.7 per cent to Dollars 499.2m from Dollars 463.4m. The figures included a Dollars 3m one-time after-tax charge from the adoption of a new accounting standard relating to employees' post-employment benefits. Excluding this, net income was up 39.5 per cent, the company reported. Consolidated operating income was 35.8 per cent higher than in the same period of last year, at Dollars 88.75m. The information services segment, which includes the Dow Jones/Telerate and business information services operations, saw operating income rise 39.7 per cent to Dollars 48.14m on revenues 10 per cent higher at Dollars 230m. Business publications had operating income of Dollars 41.5m, up 30.5 per cent, on revenues 7.2 per cent higher at Dollars 213.5m. The Wall Street Journal's advertising lineage rose 1.7 per cent, but on a per issue basis was even with a year earlier. Ottaway Newspapers, the group's community newspaper subsidiary, reported a 17.8 per cent rise in operating income to Dollars 4.43m, on revenues 1.3 per cent higher at Dollars 55.78m. Advertising lineage fell 1.1 per cent. The company's shares closed Dollars 1 1/4 higher on the New York Stock Exchange, at Dollars 39 1/8.
