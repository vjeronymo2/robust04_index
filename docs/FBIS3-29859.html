Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Nagasaki, Feb. 20 KYODO -- Incumbent Nagasaki Gov. Isamu Takada won a fourth four-year term in office Sunday [20 February], beating out two other contenders in the prefecture's gubernatorial election. Takada, 67, backed by the Liberal Democratic Party, Shinseito [Japan Renewal Party], the Democratic Socialist Party and the Social Democratic Party, topped Momiyoshi Shigaki, 64, and Katsumi Nagata, 40. Shigaki was supported by the Japan New Party and Sakigake (Harbinger), while Nagata was backed by the Japanese Communist Party. Voter turnout was 65.76 percent, compared with 78.13 percent in the previous election, Election Administration Committee officials said.
