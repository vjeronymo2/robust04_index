BFN [Text] Beijing, 21 Jun (ZHONGGUO TONGXUN SHE) -- While gradually removing price controls where appropriate over the next few years, China will continue to exercise direct state control over the prices of five types of commodities and services. The five types of commodities and services are: Less competitive commodities that have a great bearing on social stability and on the long-term development of the national economy, such as crude oil, natural gas, and a few rare metals; Commodities whose prices are strictly controlled by the government including some medicines and weapons; Living quarters offered to civil servants and low-income earners; Utilities where competition is inappropriate, such as public transport, urban water and gas supplies, electricity for general and production purposes, rail passenger and freight transport, the mail service, and telecommunications; Major facilities and services such as various types of social public insurance, public education, and health care.
