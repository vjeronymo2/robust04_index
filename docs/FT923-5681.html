920828 FT 28 AUG 92 / International Company News: General Cinema advances GENERAL Cinema, the retailing, publishing and theatre group which last year acquired the Harcourt Brace Jovanovich publishing business, yesterday reported third-quarter profits of Dollars 71.4m after tax, for the three months to end-July. This compared with Dollars 45.3m in the same period a year earlier, and brings the total for the first nine months of the financial year to Dollars 516.2m, including an extraordinary credit of Dollars 419.6m. The first nine months of 1990/91 saw a loss of Dollars 19.9m. General Cinema's revenues in the third quarter were Dollars 992.1m, compared with Dollars 972.2m a year ago. The company acknowledged that because of the seasonal nature of educational publishing the bulk of its operating earnings and revenues from publishing comes in this quarter. But the specialty retailing, insurance and theatre division also improved third-quarter performance, while interest and administrative expenses were reduced. General Cinema shares rose Dollars 1 1/2 at Dollars 25 7/8 on the news.
