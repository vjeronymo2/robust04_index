940506 FT 06 MAY 94 / International Company News: Profits surge at Swedish steelmaker Firmer prices and increased volumes helped SSAB, the Swedish steel group, lift first-quarter pre-tax profits sharply, to SKr478m (Dollars 62.9m) from SKr23m, writes Christopher Brown-Humes. It says profits for the full year could be nearly twice last year's SKr776m, provided current market trends continue. The group, one of Europe's few profitable steelmakers, noted that supply and demand in western Europe had improved during the spring. 'This should improve chances for certain price increases during the second half of the year,' it said.
