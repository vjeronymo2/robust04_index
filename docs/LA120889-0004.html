December 8, 1989, Friday, Home Edition

MEDFLY SPRAYING CONTROVERSY

As I lie here in bed listening to the malathion-spraying helicopters flying low overhead, I can't help thinking: How ironic it is that with all the concern for and pains taken to preserve the thousands of forms of animal life, from the huge elephant to the tiny insect in some remote desert pond, we meet with only limited success.

Yet when we reverse the procedure and attempt to exterminate one member of the animal kingdom (i.e., the Medfly) we find it near impossible.

AL MEHLMAN

Rosemead
