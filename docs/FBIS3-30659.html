Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Prague Feb 4 (CTK) -- Today's meeting between Czech Deputy Foreign Minister Alexandr Vondra and NATO's Gebhardt von Moltke discussed practical steps in the implementation of the Partnership for Peace [PFP] project. Moltke, NATO assistant secretary general in charge of the division of political affairs, arrived in Prague today along with another four NATO officials at the end of a week-long Partnership mission which had taken him to Albania, Bulgaria, Hungary and Romania. The Partnership for Peace project was adopted at NATO's Brussels summit in January. The delegation provided the Czechs with further information on the project, Moltke told CTK, adding that he had supported Czech participation in the project throughout the negotiations. NATO is ready for cooperation in military planning, training and exercises, including joint peace missions, he said.
