BFN [Text] According to a recent nationwide opinion poll conducted by ASAHI SHIMBUN on 8 and 9 May, 53 percent of respondents said "it was appropriate" for the Social Democratic Party of Japan [SDPJ] to leave the coalition administration, while 26 percent said "it was not appropriate." Thus, the approval rate of the SDPJ's decision was much higher than the disapproval rate. Among the respondents who support the SDPJ, 57 percent highly evaluated the party's secession from the coalition while 29 percent were critical of the secession. In terms of party affiliation, it is noteworthy that 76 percent of supporters for the Japan Communist Party [JCP] welcomed the SDPJ's secession from the coalition. Next to this figure, 67 percent of Sakigake [Harbinger] supporters and 57 percent of Liberal Democratic Party supporters welcomed the SDPJ's action. On the other hand, disapproval of the SDPJ's secession was conspicuously noted among supporters for the ruling coalition parties of the Tsutomu Hata cabinet. For example, the SDPJ's secession from the coalition was criticized by 42 percent of Democratic Socialist Party supporters, by 41 percent of Komeito supporters, and by 36 percent of Shinseito [Japan Renewal Party] supporters.
