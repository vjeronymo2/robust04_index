BFN [Text] Tokyo, April 19 KYODO -- The government-backed Japan National Oil Corp. said Tuesday [19 April] it has reached basic agreement to conduct geological surveys for oil and natural gas in the Republic of Kazakhstan of the former Soviet Union. The corporation and Kazakhstan authorities have been conducting negotiations since May 1993 on possible surveys in the republic, which is believed to be rich in oil and natural gas resources, officials said. They said the three-year surveys, costing 5.3 billion yen, will cover a 10,000-square-kilometer land area northwest of the Aral Sea.
