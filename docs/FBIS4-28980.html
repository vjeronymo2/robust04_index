BFN [Text] Pyongyang, June 13 (KCNA) -- Rice transplanting and humus-potted maize transplanting were all finished in the right season to be followed by weeding of paddy and non-paddy fields in the countryside of Korea. According to data available, the first-round weeding had been carried out at 83 percent in paddy fields and at 81 percent in maize fields nationwide by the 10th of June. South Hwanghae and South Pyongan Provinces, Kaesong, Pyongyang and Nampo municipalities finished the first-round weeding in the period. Now they have carried out the second-round weeding at 50 percent. The agricultural working people in South Hwanghae Province, regarding qualitative weeding in the right season as one of the basic factors of a high yield of grain, set themselves the goal of finishing the third-round weeding of the paddy and non-paddy fields within this month and are topping their daily quotas 20 percent by concentrating manpower and machines on it. Weeding is also brisk in the mountainous rural areas and on the east coast. Meanwhile, efforts are being made to do fertilizing on a scientific and technical basis and prevent damages by harmful insects.
