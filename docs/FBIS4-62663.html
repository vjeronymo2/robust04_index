BFN [Report by ITAR-TASS correspondent Vyacheslav Bantin] [Text] Tokyo 17 May TASS -- President Islam Karimov of Uzbekistan, who is on his first visit here, and Japanese Prime Mnister Tsutomu Hata have issued a joint statement, stipulating basic principles of relations between Uzbekistan and Japan. The document was signed directly before the Uzbek-Japanese high-level talks. The joint statement, in particular, says that Uzbekistan "expresses the hope" that Japan will be granted the status of a permanent member of the UN Security Council. Tashkent and Tokyo proclaim the intention to closely cooperate within the U.N. framework and in other international organizations for the sake of peace and security in the world, the document stated. According to the document, Uzbekistan has pledged to contribute to ensure stability in central Asia, and also carry out large-scale privatization aimed at a transition towards the market economy. Both parties supported the enhancing of a political dialogue between the two countries, and noted that this will help stregthen and activate their relations. After adopting the joint statement, foreign ministers of Uzbekistan and Japan signed a joint document, reaffirming that both sides regard still valid all agreements between the former Soviet Union and Japan which concern Uzbekistan. Speaking at a ceremony of signing the both documents, Japanese Prime Minister Tsutomu Hata expressed confidence that relations between the two countries, which trace their beginning to the great Silk Route era, will develop and strengthen. "The signing of the bilateral documents, in our opinion, has signalled the beginning of great cooperation between our republic and Japan," President of Uzbekistan Islam Karimov noted.
