BFN [Text] The House of Representatives convened under Speaker 'Abdallah Bin-Husayn al-Ahmar this morning. The session was attended by brothers Dr. 'Abd-al-Rahman 'Abd-al-Qadir Bafadl, minister of supply and trade; 'Ali Salih al-Salami, minister of finance; Khalid 'Abd-al-Salam Karman, minister of legal affairs; Muhammad Ahmad al-Junayd, governor of the Yemeni Central Bank; and Jamal Muhammad 'Abduh, deputy minister of transport; along with representatives of public supply corporations. Representatives of the government reported on financial, supply, and economic matters and set out ways to promote the country's national economy and put its financial and monetary affairs in order. They emphasized that the government is capable of moving and continuing to meet the public requirements for food items and basic services throughout the Republic of Yemen and those of official and private organizations to keep the developmental process going. The House then heard a brief report about the special parliamentary committee it assigned to study the developments that our country has witnessed [words indistinct] in connection with forming parliamentary delegations to be sent out to brotherly and friendly states and the other functions of the committee. House Speaker Shaykh 'Abdallah Bin-Husayn al-Ahmar then reported to the deputies about the outcome of his meeting with brother Lakhdar Brahimi, the UN secretary general's special envoy to the Republic of Yemen. The meeting was good, and Brahimi came out with the impression that the Yemeni people were wedded to their unity and resolved to stand up to the rebellious, separatist elements. What is happening in Yemen, Brahimi was told, is a domestic affair, the house speaker added. The House of Representatives is to reconvene tomorrow morning, Monday, God willing.
