BFN [By Herold-Jean Francois] [Text] Port-au-Prince, 11 Jun (EFE) -- Today, the organization known as "Capois-La-Mort," which includes high-ranking extreme right-wing soldiers, demanded strict measures against foreigners and the closure of the Port-au-Prince and Cap-Haitiene airports. In addition, they demanded an end to diplomatic relations with France, Canada, Venezuela, and the United States and declared the ambassadors of these countries persona non grata. According to their demands, no foreign airplanes should be allowed to land at the Haitian capital or at any other airport. The demands were listed in a communique issued today. Among other things, the document asks for border officials to ban the entry of foreigners crossing the Dominican Republic border. The document also demands cutting off water, power, and telephone services to foreign embassies and those "collaborating with the enemy." The military group suggests and even demands giving the French, Canadian, Venezuelan, and U.S. ambassadors 48 hours to leave Haiti. If they choose to remain in the country, the group recommends limiting their movements as well as the movements of other diplomatic representatives, consuls, and members of international agencies. They call for ambassadors and other foreign government officials to be ordered to remain within a 15 km radius of their official domicile. According to the communique outlining the special measures, in order for these people to travel outside the said 15 km perimeter, they must first get approval from the Ministry of Foreign Relations and the Armed Forces. "Capois-La-Mort" considers the strict sanctions ordered against the country and backed by the international community, as "a declaration of war against the black Republic of Haiti." The organization urges the government to approve special "war measures," to enable it to respond to the "foreign aggression." The written demands presented by "Capois-La-Mort" come while rumors of a foreign military invasion of Haiti spread. Observers see the demands as evidence that the military and its collaborators could take extreme measures.
