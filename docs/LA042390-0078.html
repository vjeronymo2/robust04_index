April 23, 1990, Monday, Home Edition

MORNING REPORT: STAGE

Final Act for Whole Theater: It's curtains for the Whole Theater, which rose to national prominence with a push from its co-founder, Olympia Dukakis. The board of trustees for the Montclair, N.J., theater said the building will have to be sold to pay off debts of about $450,000. Officials said the theater's association with Dukakis, who won a supporting actress Oscar in 1988 for "Moonstruck," may have lulled would-be donors into believing it was financially sound. GREG BRAXTON
