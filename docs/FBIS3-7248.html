Language: Japanese Article Type:BFN [From NIHON KEIZAI SHIMBUN 28 March Morning Edition] [Text] On the evening of 27 March, Prime Minister Morihiro Hosokawa issued a comment regarding the victory by Seiken Tanimoto, the ruling coalition-backed candidate, in the gubernatorial election in Ishikawa Prefecture. He said: "We plan to solidify the unity among the ruling coalition parties in the future and move toward establishing a reformed political system." Hosokawa also commented on why he thought the ruling coalition candidate had won. He said: "We have appealed to the public, stressing not only the problems peculiar to Ishikawa Prefecture, but also the significance the election holds on the people's view of whether or not it is proper to continue promoting the reformed politics of the ruling coalition parties." His remarks expressed his confidence in the future unity of the ruling coalition parties.
