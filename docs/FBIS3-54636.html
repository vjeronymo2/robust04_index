Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The Belarusian parliament's presidium has turned down UN Secretary-General Butrus-Ghali's request for sending a Belarusian battalion to Yugoslavia for a peace-keeping mission, speaker of the Belarusian parliament Stanislav Shushkevich told Interfax's Minsk branch. He said that although Belarus was well aware of the importance of its participation in the international effort to settle armed conflicts, the presidium did not even venture to raise this question in the parliament. The Belarusian people have not yet got over the Afghan syndrome, he said. He also added that during the Afghan war Belarus had lost far more soldiers than other former Soviet republics. Shushkevich said that the current psychological condition of Belarusian society and the memories of World War II during which Belarus lost one fourth of its population was another reason for refusal. He stated that other representatives of the UN secretary-general had also been informed of the presidium's unanimous position.
