BFN [Discussion between political correspondents Udi Segal, outside the Prime Minister's Office, and 'Adi Talmor, in the studio -- live] [Text] [Segal] A very brief cabinet session, which started at 0900, ended a short time ago. This session was convened to ratify the Gaza-Jericho agreement, and the agreement was indeed unanimously ratified. The results of the Histadrut elections obviously hovered in the air, but political affairs were the main issue on the agenda. Minister Yosi Sarid told us that even more significant than the ratification of the agreement was the determination of a timetable for the transfer of powers. The target date is next Wednesday [18 May]; by then all the Gaza and Jericho autonomy areas will be transferred to Palestinian authority. [Talmor] Am I correct in saying that by that date all the Palestinian police forces will have entered the area and the Palestinians will be in charge of all the powers and buildings in the area? [Segal] More than that. By next Wednesday the PLO will bear full responsibility in Gaza and Jericho. This is what Minister Yosi Sarid told us several minutes ago: [Begin Sarid recording] The cabinet ratified the agreement unanimously. We should pay attention, however, to the timetable, because now it is very specific: by Friday, two days from now, the Palestinians will take over the southern part of the Gaza Strip and all of Jericho. By next Wednesday the Palestinians will take over the entire Gaza Strip, that is, Gaza city itself and the northern part of the Gaza Strip as well. This means that by next Wednesday the Palestinians will be in charge of the Gaza Strip and Jericho, as stipulated by the agreement. The actual testing of the agreement will start at that time. [end recording] [Jerusalem Qol Yisra'el in Hebrew at 0800 GMT on 11 May, reporting on the cabinet session, adds the following: "Minister Shim'on Shetrit said in the session that the agreement signed with the PLO deviates from the Declaration of Principles. He cited several examples, such as the granting of passports to the Palestinians, the ability to legislate basic laws, radio frequencies, dialing zones, etc."]
