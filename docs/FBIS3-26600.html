Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Lusaka, February 8 (XINHUA) -- A Chinese company today handed over two Y-12 aircrafts to the Defense Ministry of Zambia at a ceremony held in an airforce base near Lusaka. The two China-made aircrafts were sold on credit by China National Aero-Technology Import and Export Corporation. Speaking at the ceremony, Zambian Defense Minister Ben Mwila said China has provided all kinds of assistance to Zambia before and after its independence. The arrival of the planes will surely strengthen the capacity of the Air Force, he added. In reply, Chinese Ambassador to Zambia Yang Zengye said in line with the principles of equality, mutual benefits, stress on practical results, diversity in forms and attainment of common progress, China will further promote the existing friendly relations of close cooperation with Zambia. The two aircrafts flew here on January 25.
