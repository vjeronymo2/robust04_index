Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The president, Mr. Farooq Ahmad Khan Leghari, has said that Pakistan attaches the highest importance to peace and security in the Indian Ocean, as the country depends heavily on the sea for trade, commerce, and other economic benefits. He was addressing the 60th midshipmen commissioning parade at the Pakistan Naval Academy [PNA], PNS Rahbar, in Karachi today. On arrival at the naval academy, the president was accorded the general salute. He reviewed the parade. The president gave prizes to the outstanding midshipmen. He gave the chairman, joint chiefs of staff committee, gold medal to Cadet Mohammad Amin Sharif, PNA dirk for runner-up to Midshipman Mohammad Waheed, and the sword of honor to Midshipman Rahim Akbar. Champion division and winner of proficiency banner was Fox-1 Division. Speaking on the occasion, the president said the Indian Ocean constitutes the lifeline of the world economy, as about one-fourth of international trade passes through it. He said Pakistan being a littoral state, its concern is perfectly legitimate; and inexplicably large naval buildup by any country in the region naturally and understandably heightens our worries about the security of our shores and the safety of our sea lanes. He said this concern can be allayed if the Indian Ocean is declared a zone of peace. Congratulating the passing-out midshipmen, the president emphasized the need for a high spirit of patriotism. The commanding officer of the academy said that the highest professional training has been given to the passing-out midshipmen. March past and salute to the president by the midshipmen. [video shows midshipmen marching past and saluting the president]. Earlier, the president visited some ships of the Pakistan naval fleet. Later talking to newsmen informally at PNS Rahbar in Karachi today, the president said that Pakistan had agreed to participate in the Indo-Pak secretary-level talks, as it sincerely desired the solution of the Kashmir dispute. The president said, moreover, it was a welcome move for Pakistan, as during the last three decades, it was for the first time that India had agreed to discuss the Kashmir issue. It was heartening to note that at least Kashmir was recognized as a legitimate dispute, the president added. The president said from Pakistan's view, it was a victory as at last India finally recognized that Kashmir is a disputed territory which needs ultimate solution. He said the resumption of Indo-Pak talks was actually a support from Pakistan to the liberation struggle of the Kashmiris, as it greatly values their just struggle. Mr. Farooq Ahmad Khan Leghari has said that Kashmir issue is a most intricate issue and one should not expect immediate results.
