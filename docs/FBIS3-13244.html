Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] The following decision has been issued by the Council of Ministers: Decision No. 128, date 25/9/1414 A.H. [7/3/1994] On the basis of the discussions in the Council of Ministers' session on the noticed constant increase in the spread of satellites which started to transmit their different TV programs to reception equipments wherever they might be. In the interest of an organizational situation which determines the necessary controls and rules for the reception of external television broadcasts via the international satellite channels in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia, the council decides the following: First, the Information Ministry assumes the task and responsibility of implementing the project of receiving external television transmission via the international satellite channels and transmitting it to viewers via the cable system inside the kingdom in accordance with the religious and social values in the country. Second, the import of dishes and their accessories which are used for the reception of foreign satellites programs is forbidden. The manufacturing of or any of their parts is forbidden inside the kingdom. The use of microwaves, decoders, or any other means for the reception and distribution of satellite transmission inside the kingdom is also banned. The manufacturing of the decoders or their parts is forbidden locally. Third, it is forbidden to transfer satellite television transmission to the viewers inside the kingdom by anyone, or any quarter by means of subscriptions, be it by using microwaves, decoders, or anything else. Fourth, whoever violates the provisions of articles 2 and 3 of this decision is punishable by a financial fine of no less than 100,000 riyals and no more than 500,000 riyals, together with the confiscation of the equipments, the subject of the violation.
