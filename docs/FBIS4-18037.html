BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Lyudmila Yermakova] [Text] Moscow April 28 TASS -- Russia's commission on human rights is preparing a report on human rights in Russia which will be presented to President Boris Yeltsin. The commission, set up by the president after the October 1993 revolt in Moscow, will inform him about human rights abuses in the country, commission chairman Sergey Kovalev said on Thursday. The commission plans to finish a national human rights programme by the end of May. The programme has been developed in cooperation with the Russian Foreign Ministry. The commission also handles complaints about violations of civil rights. Kovalev, who has been appointed by the State Duma a commissioner on human rights, said that a draft law regulating the status, rights and duties of the commissioner on human rights is now being discussed in the Duma Committee for Legislation. He believes that after the law has been adopted, the commission on human rights will have to continue working in order to keep the president informed about human rights in Russia.
