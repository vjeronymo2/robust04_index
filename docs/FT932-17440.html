930401 FT 01 APR 93 / World Trade News: UK company secures Bangkok sewage deal BANGKOK has awarded its first contract for sewage treatment and waste water to North West Water, the privatised UK water utility. The Pounds 160m contract, which covers design, construction and one year's operation of the facilities, is still subject to final ratification by the Thai government. The company said it hoped for clearance soon and construction was due to start in July. The project includes installation of about 50km of sewers and a new sewage treatment plant. It will serve 700,000 of Bangkok's 10m people.
