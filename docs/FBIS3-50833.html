Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Shortly before leaving on a tour that will take him to several African countries, Foreign Minister Roberto Robaina stated that the Cuban delegation will explain the reality of today's Cuba. The foreign minister held a news conference, during which he broached diverse international relations subjects, emphasizing his tour of Ghana, Angola, Zimbabwe, Zambia, and Namibia. Robaina said he is going to update the foreign ministers of those countries on how things are in Cuba, but will also meet with the ministers of health, tourism, education, and others. He will be received by the presidents of the nations visited. Regarding the internal situation in Angola, Robaina said that Cuba and Angola maintain excellent relations, which are experiencing a period of mutual understanding. With regard to relations with Chile, the foreign minister said they will be reestablished at the moment the Chileans consider most opportune. On Cuba's relations with the United States, Robaina stated that while the United States' language may be less aggressive; the blockade has intensified.
