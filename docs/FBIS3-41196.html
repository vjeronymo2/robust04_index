Language: English Article Type:BFN [Report by Katy Turner] [Text] Dutch Judges have been in Cyprus investigating allegations of drug smuggling involving offshore companies from Holland, it was revealed yesterday. Police spokesman Savas Andoniadhis said the judges, who were assisted by the Cyprus force, were conducting a general inquiry into smuggling. Narcotics and fabrics are understood to have been included in the investigations. The judges were on the island for ten days. The value of the smuggled goods is believed to be thousands of dollars. A seven-member team comprising the judges, lawyers, and tax inspectors investigated two unnamed Dutch offshore companies. The companies are believed to have moved huge quantities of fabric to Lebanon and Pakistan using documents relating to another country. According to reports, members of one offshore company were described as owners of boat which was bought with drugs money. A Dutch judge issued a confiscation order on the unnamed boat and any property gained through smuggling.
