May 31, 1990, Thursday, P.M. Final

ISRAELI OFFICER ACCUSED OF TRAINING COLOMBIAN CIVILIANS

A court today charged a high-ranking reserve officer and a company he heads with illegally providing military expertise and equipment to a group of civilians in Colombia.

The defendant, Lt. Col. Yair Klein, is also wanted in Colombia for allegedly helping to train fighters for cocaine cartels.

The case involving Klein and his Hod Hahanit, or "spearhead" company, has prompted wide debate in Israel over the practice of retired military officers working as mercenaries overseas.

Klein's company is defined in the charges as an organization that trains and builds counterterrorist fighting squads.

The four-page charge sheet makes no mention of the Colombian charges that Klein was involved in training assassination teams for the Medellin drug cartel. Klein has vigorously denied such allegations.
