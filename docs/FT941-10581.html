940209 FT 09 FEB 94 / Tribunal told of 'cover-up' on forex deals Concerns raised by a London bank officer when she was asked to destroy suspicious foreign exchange deal notes went unheeded by management, an industrial tribunal was told yesterday. In 1992, Adam and Co, the Edinburgh-based private bank now owned by Royal Bank of Scotland, announced a Pounds 21m loss from unauthorised foreign exchange dealing. Ms Tracey Humphreys, 30, a former assistant supervisor at the Pall Mall branch of Adam and Co, yesterday suggested to the tribunal in Croydon, south London, that the problems could have been discovered much earlier had managers heeded her warnings. Claiming unfair dismissal when she was made redundant in March 1993, she accused Mr Richard Donaldson, the director who dismissed her, of attempting to cover up for his own incompetence. The bank said Ms Humphreys was made redundant because of the transfer of foreign exchange operations to Edinburgh. She had been given a formal warning for carrying out unauthorised money transfers from her own account at the bank. Ms Humphreys said she made the transactions without knowing it was against bank procedures. Ms Humphreys, who joined Adam and Co in October 1990, said she had raised her concerns with Mr Donaldson nearly a year before her dismissal. She had also informed the then finance director Mr Jeremy Lowry, who has since left the bank. She said she was told by foreign exchange dealers to destroy deal confirmation notes because the deals had been cancelled. Mr Donaldson told the tribunal: 'The events of the foreign exchange had nothing whatsoever to do with my reasons for considering Tracey Humphreys for redundancy.' After the hearing, he said he referred Ms Humphreys to Mr Lowry as the appropriate person to deal with her concerns. 'It had absolutely no relevance to her dismissal case.'
