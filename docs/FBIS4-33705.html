BFN [Text] La Paz, 9 Jun (EFE) -- Bolivian President Gonzalo Sanchez de Lozada today announced that Marcelo Cespedes Gutierrez has been appointed the new labor minister. Gutierrez replaces Reynaldo Peters, who resigned today. Peters' sudden resignation can be added to those of Luis Lema, Bolivian ambassador to Argentina; and Jorge Otasevich, the congressional and political affairs under secretary, both of whom resigned today. Ernesto Machicao, minister of social communications, explained that the resigning government officials will replace those senators who have been objected to in the ongoing impeachment process against the suspended president and senior justice of the Supreme Court. The impeachment process against Supreme Court President Edgar Oblitas and Senior Justice Ernesto Poppe is in its final stages after an anguishing process in the Chamber of Deputies and the Senate. This is the first effort made to establish a precedent in the battle against corruption undertaken by the Sanchez de Lozada administration. The defense objected to several of the senators from the ruling Nationalist Revolutionary Movement (MNR). In view of this there was a risk that the two-thirds of the votes necessary to approve the impeachment would not be achieved. With the votes of Peters, Lema, and Otasevich, the MNR will be able to secure the majority of votes necessary to approve the impeachment process.
