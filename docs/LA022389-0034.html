February 23, 1989, Thursday, Home Edition

CONGRESSIONAL PAY RAISE

I wish to reply to the column "Wright Crumbles in Panic Politics" by David S. Broder (Op-Ed Page, Feb. 12). I think the headline on this column is just one man's opinion because House Speaker Jim Wright showed great courage in taking the stand that he did on the vote for a pay raise. He showed great leadership in standing up to all those who wanted him to protect them, yet did not have the nerve to speak out so that the people could hear them. Shame, shame on many of our government workers.

Now how can Broder say the American people are the long-term losers? We won. And, it makes me laugh when I hear the rebuttal that good people will not run for office or may think about resigning. Every time there is an election, we have many people running for the same office and spending thousands of dollars, sometimes much more than the office will pay.

Wright did take the heat, both from his own colleagues and from the people at large, and that shows a very strong man. If his "buddies" even think about removing him from the speakership it will show how hypocritical they really are.

It is time the people speak up again and demand another way for increasing the pay of lawmakers. The current method is ludicrous, and if attempted again there will be another outcry of the people.

ALPHA LEE DAY

Bellflower
