BFN [Text] Lieutenant General Khin Nyunt, secretary-1 of the State Law and Order Restoration Council [SLORC] of the Union of Myanmar [Burma], received Mr. Homoko P. M., head of the Board of Directors of the P.T. Humfort Company from Indonesia, and entourage at the Ministry of Defense's Dagon House at 0800 this morning. Together with Lt. Gen. Khin Nyunt at the meeting were Brigadier General Abel, minister of national planning and economic development; U Ohn Gyaw, minister of foreign affairs; U Tin Tun, deputy minister of energy; and U Aung Phone, deputy minister of forests. Matters on opening up Indonesia House in Myanmar and improving trade with Myanmar were coordinated and discussed at the meeting. Accompanied by Mr. Mohamed Sanusi, Indonesian ambassador to the Union of Myanmar, Mr. Ir. [Engineer] Hartarto, coordinating minister of industrial and trade affairs of the Republic of Indonesia, and entourage called on Lieutenant General Tun Kyi, members of the SLORC and minister of trade, at the Ministry of Trade at 1330 this afternoon. Frank and cordial discussions on bilateral economy and trade issues were held at the meeting. Mr. Joop Ave, minister of tourism, post, and telecommunications of the Republic of Indonesia, and party who are currently in Yangon [Rangoon] called on Information Minister Brig. Gen. Myo Thant at the Myanmar Television and Radio Department [MTRD] at 0900 this morning. The meeting was also attended by U Kyi Lwin, director general of the MTRD; U Win Sein, director general of the Information and Public Relations Department; U Aung Maung, managing director of the Printing and Publishing Enterprise; U Than Maung, managing director of the News and Periodicals Enterprise; U Tin Pe, managing director of the Motion Picture Enterprise; and responsible personnel. They held frank and cordial discussion and exchanged their television and radio work experience. With the assistance from the Ministry of Culture, the visiting minister and entourage were entertained with a dance from the Rama play and O-zi [Long drum] dance. Radio Myanmar's band and singer Nwe Yin Win also entertained the visiting minister and entourage with a popular Indonesian song. After the entertainment program, U Kyi Lwin, director general of the MTRD, and Mr. (Rali Siregar), chairman of the RCTI [Rajawali Citra Televisi Indonesia] private television company, signed a memorandum of understanding on bilateral exchange of television programs. After the signing of memorandum, Information Minister Brig. Gen. Myo Thant presented the gifts to Mr. Joop Ave, minister of tourism, post, and telecommunications of the Republic of Indonesia, and entourage. Similarly, a business mission led by Indonesian Communications Minister Dr. Haryanto Danudirto also called on U Win Sein, minister of rail transportation, at the latter's office at 0800 this morning. They held frank and cordial discussions on matters concerning Myanmar's railway and road transport for a bilateral mutual cooperation. Mr. Ir. Hartarto, coordinating minister of industrial and trade affairs of the Republic of Indonesia, and entourage visited Shwe Dagon Pagoda, Defense Services Museum, and (Bamwegon) reservoir project in the industrial zone in Syriam-Kyauktan area this morning. Mr. Ir. Hartarto, coordinating minister of industrial and trade affairs of the Republic of Indonesia, and his wife; Dr. Haryanto Danudirto, ministser of communications, and his wife; Mr. Joop Ave, minister of tourism, post, and telecommunications; senior departmental officials, and private business entrepreneurs, who paid three-day goodwill to Myanmar at the invitation of the Government of Union of Myanmar, left Yangon by special airplane this evening. The Indonesian delegation was seen at Yangon airport by Lt. Gen. Khin Nyunt, secretary-1 of the SLORC, and his wife; Lt. Gen. Tin U, secretary-2 of the SLORC, and his wife; Lt. Gen. Kyaw Ba, minister of hotels and tourism; Lt. Gen. Thein Win, minister of transport, and his wife; Brig. Gen. Abel, minister of national planning and economic development; U Soe Tha, minister of communications, post, and telegraph; U Nyunt Swe, deputy minister of foreign affairs, and his wife; U Ni Ni Than, accredited Myanmar ambassador to the Republic of Indonesia, and his wife; Mr. Mohamed Sanusi, Indonesian ambassador to the Union of Myanmar, his wife; responsible personnel, and Indonesian embassy staff.
