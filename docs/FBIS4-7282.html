BFN [From NIHON KEIZAI SHIMBUN 29 April Morning Edition] [Text] In a NIHON KEIZAI SHIMBUN interview on 28 April, Chairman Tomiichi Murayama of the Social Democratic Party of Japan [SDPJ] showed a positive attitude toward formation of a joint floor group with Sakigake [Harbinger] and some other groups. He noted the SDPJ will formally seek formation of the SDPJ-Sakigake floor group at the lower house after Golden Week recess in May, opening party head and executive talks on a regular basis. He said: "We would like to open formal talks with Sakigake after the recess in May." In the interview, Murayama stressed the need for the joint floor group with the Sakigake, saying: "We should build closer relations with Sakigake." On top of that, he hinted at his positive attitude toward a tie-up with certain quarters in the Japan New Party and the Democratic Socialist Party, noting: "In addition to Sakigake, we would like to seek cooperation with other people we can get along with."
