October 17, 1989, Tuesday, Southland Edition

CANDIDATE ANNOUNCES BID TO REPLACE LUKENS

Republican state Rep. John Boehner declared his candidacy Monday for the seat held by Rep. Donald E. (Buz) Lukens (R-Ohio), calling the congressman's sex-related conviction a tragedy that leaves voters feeling betrayed.

Boehner was the first politician to announce his intention to seek election to the seat in 1990.

Lukens is appealing his May conviction on a charge of having sex with a teen-age girl, and he has rejected demands by Republican Party leaders that he resign.
