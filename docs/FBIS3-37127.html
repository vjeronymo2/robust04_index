Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Mikhail Shevtsov] [Text] Moscow February 11 TASS -- In order to prevent a new outbreak of hostilities on the territory of Abkhazia, the republic's government has asked the president of the Confederation of Caucasian Peoples Yuriy Shanibov to send 50 observers, representing all the peoples affiliated to the confederation, who will be posted along the Inguri River, marking the border between Georgia and Abkhazia, the press service of the Abkhazian Supreme Soviet reports. The letter notes that the Russian leadership had concluded a treaty with Georgia at a time when the situation is most unstable in the region and the Georgian leadership has started to exploit it in its aggressive plans against the people of Abkhazia. The Abkhazian side claims that Georgian special services are staging subversive and terroristic acts against Abkhazian servicemen in the border Gali District.
