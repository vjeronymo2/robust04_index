920311 FT 11 MAR 92 / International Capital Markets: Bank rules alter in Philippines THE Central Bank of the Philippines announced yesterday that the period within which banks will be allowed to be in chronic reserve deficiency is to be reduced to two from four consecutive weeks, effective from March 21, AP-DJ reports from Manila. The bank said this would improve the management of reserve and base money programmes, particularly meeting targeted monetary aggregate levels. It said the reserve position of a bank and the penalty on reserve deficiency is to be based on a seven-day week and other days when there is no central clearing.
