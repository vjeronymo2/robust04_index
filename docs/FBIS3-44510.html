Language: English Article Type:BFN [By Bear Li and Lian Chun-ti] [Text] Taipei, Jan. 4 (CNA) -- Presidential spokesman Raymond Tai said Tuesday [4 January] that Premier Lien Chan is engaging in successful "vacation diplomacy" in Malaysia and Singapore. "It is encouraging, and will surely contribute to future cooperation with those countries," Tai said in a regular presidential press conference. Lien began his two-leg private visit last Thursday. He has already met with the leaders and other ranking officials of the two Southeast Asian countries. Tai said Lien's current trip is a realization of the "practical diplomacy" initiated by President Li Teng-hui. He said, however, that Li does not have plans to visit any foreign countries in the near future. According to a CNA dispatch, meanwhile, Premier Lien and his wife spent a happy Tuesday in Singapore. Lien started the day by playing golf with Singaporean Prime Minister Goh Chok Tong in the morning, and then took a ride on the Mass Rapid Transit [railway] system and toured the city in the afternoon. Lien and his wife were honored by Senior Minister Li Kuan Yew at a dinner party held at the presidential office Tuesday night. Several Singaporean cabinet ministers were also in attendance. Lien will leave Singapore Wednesday, winding up his first overseas trip since he assumed the premiership early last year.
