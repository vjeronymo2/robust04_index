BFN [Text] On 11 April, President Yuriy Meshkov of Crimea visited the Ministry of Internal Affairs of Crimea and announced a decree on releasing Oleksandr Plyuta from the official duties of minister of internal affairs of Crimea. By presidential decree, Valeriy Kuznetsov, the former commander of an army corps deployed in Crimea, has been appointed in his place. His present position is adviser to the president of Crimea on military matters. On the same day, Yuriy Meshkov visited the security service of the Republic of Crimea where he announced another decree: on releasing Ivan Kolomyystev from his official duties as head of the security service of Crimea. Volodymyr Lepikhov, deputy head of the presidential administration and former head of the 32d Army Corps' special branch has been appointed in his place. A meeting of the Supreme Council presidium of the Republic of Crimea was held on 12 April. The Central Electoral Commission reported on the results of the elections. The presidium decided to form a working group consisting of 15 newly elected deputies in order to prepare the next session. In accordance with the law, the first session of the Supreme Council of Crimea will be held one month after the elections on 10 May. Replying to a question from members of the presidium and deputies regarding the president's decrees announced on 11 April, Mykola Bahrov, chairman of the Crimean Supreme Council, said they had not been agreed with the presidium and he considers the Crimean president's actions to be illegal.
