BFN [Excerpt] Cairo, 7 May (MENA)--The vanguards of the Palestinian police and security forces left 'Ayn Jalut Palestinian forces' camp in Cairo for Rafah this morning as they prepare to enter the Gaza Strip within the next 48 hours. The forces will take charge of their tasks within the framework of the Palestinian National Authority. These vanguards include some 1,000 people, mostly young men between 16 and 26 years old who live in Egypt and Sudan, although their families still reside in Gaza. The Palestinian troops boarded buses bearing the picture of Palestinian Chairman Yasir 'Arafat. They waved their Palestinian flags and chanted patriotic Palestinian songs. Major General Yusuf Nasr, commander of the Palestinian security forces in Gaza and Jericho, stated that the troops are expected to enter the Gaza Strip on Sunday or Monday. Major General Yusuf Nasr affirmed that the Palestinian forces will complete their deployment in Gaza and the area of Jericho within 14 days, in accordance with the Palestinian-Israeli agreement signed in Cairo. The agreement provides for completing the Israeli troop withdrawal and their replacement with Palestinian troops within 14 days of 4 May. He added that Palestinian security and police forces arriving from Iraq and Jordan will head for Jericho tomorrow via the Allenby Bridge over the Jordan River to take over their tasks as soon as possible. He added that the security and police forces will be equipped with personal weapons, automatic rifles and pistols, in addition to light- and medium-machine guns. Maj. Gen. Nasr pointed out that much of the assistance promised by various states has not arrived. Only 25 vehicles donated by the United States have arrived, he said, adding that an additional 175 vehicles will arrive on 15 May. He also said that a shipment of police uniforms has arrived from Sweden. He noted that the troops going to Gaza today are self-equipped and are not reliant on foreign assistance. He added that a number of police stations in Gaza can absorb the first batch of Palestinian forces. We will need more centers and stations in coming days, he said. Maj. Gen. Nasr pointed out that the Gaza Strip and Jericho area will need stability, reconstruction, and security in the future and there is no time for disputes among citizens of different political backgrounds. We believe in democracy and in every citizen's right to express himself, and we will cooperate with everyone, he said. He added that the Palestinian security forces have developed a specific program to handle the problem of collecting arms from Gaza's citizens, but he refrained from revealing it. Marwan Kanafani, adviser to Palestinian President Yasir 'Arafat, has told MENA that the Palestinian troops will enter the Gaza Strip and Jericho as soon as they are allowed to. He explained that the Palestinians want to complete this task quietly and safely. He noted that Israeli Prime Minister Yitzhaq Rabin had stated that the Palestinian troops were not prepared to enter Gaza and Jericho. This is not true, he said, because the 9,000 troops will complete their entry in just 14 days. He noted that the majority of these troops have never seen the Palestinian land, but that the homeland is in their hearts. He affirmed that the Palestinian side is prepared to put forward new working papers and ideas when the Palestinian and Israeli negotiators get together to find a solution to a number of issues, such as the area of Jericho, the crossing points, Gaza's coastline, and others. He pointed out that the Palestinians base their work on ending the occupation. Therefore, he said, ideas will be put forward to achieve this goal. Brigadier General 'Abd-al-Hayy 'Abd-al-Wahid, commander of the Palestinian 'Ayn Jalut forces in Egypt, gave a speech to the Palestinian police and security forces in which he said that this is the moment Palestinians have been awaiting for over 27 years. You have to carry out the tasks you have been entrusted with in order to build our state and safeguard the homeland's security, he said, noting that the Palestinians have made great sacrifices for this moment. He thanked the Egyptian Armed Forces for training these troops and the Egyptian people and President Mubarak for their support for the Palestinian people and cause. [passage omitted]
