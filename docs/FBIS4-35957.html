BFN [Text] Well-informed political and military sources have spoken about the political and military dilemma in which the ruling regime in Sanaa has found itself because it insists on continuing the war against the Democratic Republic of Yemen by seeking more military supplies. These sources linked the recent visits of Yahya Husayn al-Arashi, member of the Permanent Committee of the General People's Congress, and Muhammad 'Abdallah (al-Yadumi), the third man in the Yemeni Alliance for Reform [al-Tajammu al-Yamani Li-al-Islah] to Khartoum and the visit paid to Sanaa today by the Sudanese minister of information, 'Abd-al-Basit Sabdarat, as the envoy of Sudanese President Lieutenant General 'Umar Hasan al-Bashir. The sources said the visit was linked to the question of military supplies sent from Sudan to the Sanaa regime.
