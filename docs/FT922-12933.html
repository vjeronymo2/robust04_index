920415 FT 15 APR 92 / Arts: Today's Television Never trust a programme with an exclamation mark in its title. BBC2's new arts magazine Artrageous] promises to celebrate work not because it is the best, not because it is by artists judged to be the most talented, but because it is produced by the young. Why? The frantic pursuit of yoof on BBC2 and other parts of television is becoming pathetic (7.30). At 8.00 on ITV, Inspector Morse fans are promised further personal details about the beer-swilling detective with the old motor. In this final episode of the present series, you meet his senile stepmother and his sensitive stepsister. At this rate we should have a fully rounded portrait by about 2025 AD. In the last of the Soul series (8.10 BBC2) Anthony Clare, presumably the only shrink with a licence to broadcast, challenges the idea that machines with artificial intelligence will overtake or even replace human beings. Dispatches promises shocking revelations about the mismanagement of British Rail's parcel service, Red Star (9.00 C4). Channel 4 begins Sean's Show, a new 30-minute comedy series starring the award-winning comedian Sean Hughes (10.30).
