BFN [Text] Presidential Spokesman Andrzej Drzycimski has said that, according to President Lech Walesa, the current wave of strikes may hinder the functioning of the state. The president thinks that serious negotiations between the government and trade unionists should prevent any further strikes or stop them altogether. [Begin Drzycimski recording] This strike, which is beginning to affect key security industries, is a threat not only to a particular industry but also to the whole state. That is why the president is saying that one must consider whether all of the industries should strike when this could bring a halt to the whole country. This strike is not aimed at changing political power but at finding economic solutions. The president thinks that, in such a situation, the talks must be continued because nothing can happen without them. [end recording] [Announcer] Andrzej Drzycimki said that the president has not yet decided on the excess wage increase tax [neopopiwek] law because he has received contradictory opinions on this regulation. Lech Walesa thinks that opponents as well as supporters of the excess wage increase tax have very valid arguments. [passage omitted]
