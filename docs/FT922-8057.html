920515 FT 15 MAY 92 / Survey: FT Traveller, Genoa (11) - Key facts for the business visitor Telephones Genoa dialing code 010 from inside Italy. For callers from outside Italy country code is 39, then 10 for Genoa. Hotels City centre hotels can get very full during big trade fairs, notably the October boat show, and visitors are advised to book well in advance. City, via San Sebastiano 6: Tel: 5545; Rates: single L170,000, double L240,000. Restaurants Saint Cyr, piazza Marsala 8; Tel: 886897. Behind the Jolly hotel, elegant surroundings and fine local food, notably fish. L80,000-L100,000 a head with wine. Gran Gotto, via Fiume 11; Tel: 564344. Attractive ambience near the Corte Lambruschini complex and piazza della Vittorio. Local Ligurian specialities, especially fish. Around L80,000 a head with wine. Primo Piano, via 20 Settembre 36; Tel: 540284. Stylish first floor restaurant which cultivates a clubbish style, with views over the arcardes of one of Genoa's main shopping streets. L80,000-100,000 a head with wine. Clubs Genoa has a handful of exclusive clubs. Though closed to non-members, they are worth viewing, and ideal for discreet corporate entertaining for those with membership. Circolo Artistico del Tunnel, via Garibaldi 5; Tel: 280379. Genoa's most famous and exclusive club. Its name is believed to be drawn from the opening of a new rail tunnel between the city and Sampierdarena in 1875, the year of its founding. Membership limited to 500. Il Santa Caterina, salita Santa Caterina 8; Tel: 5533119. Small but beautifully-restored premises in an ancient Palazzo close to via Garibaldi. A new club, principally for dining, which is trying to compete with its established counterparts. Yachting Club, porticciolo duca Abruzzi; Tel: 293865. As its name implies, Genoa's leading sailing club. Also exclusive. Culture The new Carlo Fenice Opera House is the city's cultural showcase for opera and classical music. Tickets are still hard to come by, as the building retains strong curiosity value for locals. Piazza De Ferrari. Box office, Tel: 589329 or 5704067. Teatro della Corte and Teatro Genovese are the city's two main theatres for drama. The former is part of the new Corte Lambruschini complex while the latter is more traditional in its design. Box office: Teatro della Corte, Piazza Borgo Pila 42, Tel: 5702450; Teatro Genovese, via Martin Piaggio 4, Tel: 8393589. Airport Christoforo Colombo airport, Tel: 2411, flight information 2415410, is four miles west of the city and has regular Alitalia and ATi flights to most big Italian cities. There are also connections to many European centres with flights by British Airways and others. Buses from town leave for the airport from Piazza Verdi and piazza Acquaverde. Media Genoa's daily newspaper is the established Il Secolo XIX, edited near Brignole station and owned by the Perrone family. All main national papers are found first thing in the morning, and some have a separate Genoa supplement. Foreign papers such as the Financial Times are widely available, but seldom arrive before mid-afternoon. Tourist information Main office (from tomorrow) via Roma 11. Tel: 541541. Other branches at Piazza Verdi 18/R (by Brignole station) Tel: 583398; Principe station Tel: 262633. There is also an office at the airport, Tel: 2415247. Open 8am-8pm. Closed Sundays.
