BFN [Unattributed article: "Previti's Assurances on Defense Policy"] [Text] (ANSA) Rome, May 13 -- Italy's new defense minister, Cesare Previti, the day after the government was sworn in, sent messages containing assurances to the secretaries of NATO and the WEU [Western European Union], as well as to the U.S. secretary of defense, to the effect that Italian policies of defense and security would keep to the firm traditional guidelines followed by previous Rome governments, the ministry information office reported this evening. In his message, Previti said that Italy was firmly anchored to the Atlantic Alliance and deeply committed both to close relations with the United States and to the building of a credible European security identity. The defense minister also emphasized the serious tensions that exist within an international framework and to which Italy, because of its geographical position, is particularly exposed.
