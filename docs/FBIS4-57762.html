BFN [Text] On 18 May in Santos (Sao Paulo State), Alcides Tapias, president of the Brazilian Federation of Banks (Febraban), said Luiz Inacio Lula da Silva's presidential candidacy "may be one of the factors that caused foreign debt certificates to drop." These certificates, which were issued by the Brazilian Government, have a nominal value of $100. On 4 May, they were negotiated for $74 and on 17 May their value dropped further to $67. Tapias could not forecast the position banks will adopt if the certificates' value continues to drop. He said he has no data on the amount of these certificates still in the hands of the national banks.
