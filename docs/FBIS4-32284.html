BFN [Text] Tirana, June 22 (ATA) -- On its way to Romania, the Albanian delegation led by the speaker of the parliament, Pjeter Arbnori, stopped over in Skopje on June 18 and had a meeting with the speaker of the Macedonian Parliament, Andov. Mr. Arbnori stressed that frequent meetings are important because they help strengthen the relations and open new ways for cooperation. Mr. Andov expressed the readiness of the Macedonian party to follow this road, with the aim that the relations between the two countries must set an example to the Balkan peninsula. Further on Mr. Arbnori expressed some concerns with regard to the red tape obstacles related with the census of the Albanian population, about which he was informed in the meetings that he had had with Albanians in Tetovo. Mr. Arbnori asked from Mr. Andov to use his authority to help in the quickest and satisfactory possible solution of the problems that have aroused in the process of the census of the population in The Former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia [FYROM]. Mr. Andov promised and guaranteed the Albanian party a quick solution. Further on, Mr. Andov focused on the promotion of the economic and trade relations between the two countries which is important for the Former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia in the conditions of the blockade imposed by Greece. Mr. Arbnori expressed the readiness and interest of Albania in this point, stressing that Albania is ready to go ahead in this direction not because the problem is related with Greece but because cooperation and the intensification of regional trade and economic relations is the only road towards stability and prosperity.
