Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] At least one Hebron resident was killed by Army fire and 14 wounded in fierce clashes in the town this morning. There was also renewed violence in the Gaza Strip. Mark Weiss reports: The clashes at various locations in Hebron erupted this morning after the Army announced a two-hour lifting of the curfew to enable residents to stock up on basic supplies. Hundreds of residents joined the violent protests, and 17-year-old Usamah Mustafa Ghazal was shot and killed by Army fire, and at least another 14 were wounded, two of them seriously. Witnesses report that a second resident, 18-year-old Nidal al-Hammumi, has also died of his wounds, but this has still not been confirmed by hospital sources. [Jerusalem Qol Yisra'el in Hebrew at 1200 GMT on 2 March reports: "In Jericho, soldiers shot and killed 'Uthman 'Ali Bargham, 20, and wounded 22 local residents in the most violent riots the city has experienced in many years." The report adds that "several masked men incited the masses in a mourning procession in the city today. The marchers began to riot, attacking the police station, at which point the troops were forced to open fire."] Clashes continued in Gaza city and the refugee camp of Jabalyah this morning. At least 20 residents were wounded, according to Palestinian sources. The curfews in towns and refugee camps in the Gaza Strip were lifted today, although the schools remained closed. The closure imposed following the Hebron massacre remains in force. The curfews on major population centers in the West Bank remain in place, although there are frequent reports of curfew breaking and isolated clashes with troops. Two border policemen were lightly wounded in stone-throwing clashes today, one in Ramallah and one in Gaza.
