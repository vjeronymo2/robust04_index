Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Naples, Italy, Feb 9 (AFP) -- Police have smashed a drug-trafficking ring involving heroin worth 50 billion lire (30 million dollars) and arrested 42 people, police sources said Tuesday [8 February]. A further 28 people already serving prison sentences were also said to be implicated in the ring, which came to light following confessions by five former members of the Camorra, the Neapolitan Mafia. According to the confessions, the drug trafficking was controlled by 12 Mafia families headed by boss Raffaele Ascione. Up to 1,000 fixes of heroin a day were being sold by the gang. Police did not say if they had seized the drugs.
