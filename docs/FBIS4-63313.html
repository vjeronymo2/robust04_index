BFN [Report by ITAR-TASS correspondent] [Text] Brussels, 20 May -- Kazakhstan is the second CIS state -- following Ukraine -- to have finished drawing up an agreement on partnership and cooperation with the European Union. This document was initialled in Brussels on Friday [20 May]. The ITAR-TASS correspondent learned in the Kazakhstan embassy that the agreement will be signed by President Nursultan Nazarbayev of Kazakhstan and the European Commission chairman in the coming months. The agreement represents a political document encompassing diverse forms of cooperation. It lays the basis for development of relations between Kazakhstan and the European Union and is designed to promote the expansion of bilateral cooperation between this Central Asian republic and each of the 12 countries of the European Union. This is essentially the first agreement between independent Kazakhstan and such a major international association as the European Union. Two more joint documents are now being drawn up to regulate relations between the sides in individual spheres of cooperation, and in particular, in issues relating to power engineering and nuclear materials.
