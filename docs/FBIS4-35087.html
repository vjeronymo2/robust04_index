BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic developments monitored through 28 June. According to figures released by the National Institute of Statistics, Geography, and Information Sciences, INEGI, the number of persons working in the country's assembly plants rose 3.9 percent during the first quarter of this year compared to the same period last year. INEGI added that up to March of this year there were 2,085 assembly plants in the country, which represents an accumulated yearly increase of 1.7 percent compared to the same period last year. (Mexico City NOTIMEX in Spanish 2144 GMT 21 Jun 94) Figures from the Finance and Public Secretariat indicate that during the first quarter of this year, Mexico's trade balance showed a deficit of $5.728 billion, which represents an increase of 22.5 percent with respect to the same period last year. During this same period, the country's exports rose 15.8 percent, whereas its imports rose 17.4 percent. (Monterrey EL NORTE (Foreign Edition) in Spanish 21 Jun 94 p 22A) Economic research conducted by the daily EL FINANCIERO, based on Bank of Mexico data, reveals that the country's international reserves reached a record level of 25.332 billion, or a 3.2 percent increase with respect to the balance accumulated until December 1993. The modest increase can be attributed to foreign investments, which rose over 10.6 percent during the first quarter of the year. (Mexico City EL FINANCIERO in Spanish 21 Jun 94 p 3A) Speaking at the closing of the 26th National Meeting of Fiscal Officials, President Carlos Salinas de Gortari announced that all state governments will receive an additional allocation of 1.2 billion new pesos, thanks to an increase in tax revenues. He added that the money will be spent on social projects. (Mexico City NOTIMEX in Spanish 0402 GMT 22 Jun 94) On 22 June, the Banque Nationale de Paris and the National Bank of Public Works and Services, Corp., Banobras, signed a loan agreement for 36.5 million francs, almost $6.5 million, with the interest rate set at 7.1 percent. Banobras will use the loan to finance the construction of new subway lines in the city of Monterrey, capital of the State of Nuevo Leon. (Mexico City NOTIMEX in Spanish 0808 GMT 22 Jun 94) A little over $8 billion entered the country from January to May through the stock market, the National Stock Exchange reported on 21 June. It added that 56.6 percent of that total was obtained by placing debt bonds abroad. (Mexico City LA JORNADA in Spanish 22 Jun 94 p 40) The Finance and Public Credit Secretariat, SHCP, reported that the Senate has approved treaties to prevent double taxation and tax evasion with Ecuador and Italy. It also approved agreements with Belgium and Switzerland to provide greater security and stability to investors from those countries. The SHCP said that an agreement between Mexico and Germany was promulgated on 10 June to prevent double taxation. (Mexico City EL FINANCIERO in Spanish 24 Jun 94 p 8) According to economic research by EL FINANCIERO, based on information from the Commerce and Industrial Development Secretariat and the DIARIO OFICIAL DE LA FEDERACION, the Law on Economic Competition is being sidelined by the predominance of foreign investment in priority areas for the country's development. It is seen that foreign capital is increasing its presence in the domestic market by forming strategic alliances. This will promote the emergence of oligopolies that will limit the free participation of Mexican enterprises. Key partnerships are appearing in the commercial area with Gigante-Fleming; Gigante-Carrefou; Liverpool-K-Mart; Comercial Mexicana-Price Club; Comercial Mexicana-Toys'r'Us; and Cifra-WalMart. The incursion of firms with foreign capital is not limited to a single sector of the economy. They also include the food, textile, and tourism sectors. (Mexico City EL FINANCIERO (ANALISIS Supplement) in Spanish 24 Jun 94 p 3a) In the first quarter of 1994, Mexican Petroleum, Pemex, netted a $44.5 million surplus in its petrochemical products' trade balance. Pemex Petrochemical sold a total of 421,700 tons of petrochemical products. In the same period, it bought a total of $18.3 million worth of petrochemical products abroad. (Mexico City UNOMASUNO in Spanish 24 Jun 94 p 16) Foreign investment in the first two months of the year was 143 percent higher than the amount registered in the same period in 1993. The National Processing Industry Association, Canacintra, indicated that 77.6 percent of that capital was directed toward the financial market. Foreign investment channeled to productive activity decreased 24.2 percent in the first two months of 1994 while in 1989, this activity received 85.8 percent of the investments. (Mexico City NOTIMEX in Spanish 0118 GMT 25 Jun 94) Foreign investment during the current administration surpassed the goals set by the government at the beginning of the term. Commerce Under Secretary Fernando Sanchez said the 1988-94 goal was $25 billion but has reached more than $42 billion. He said there is currently confidence in Mexico because there are signs that industry is on the right course, as indicated by manufacturing sector exports which have exhibited "great dynamism." (Mexico City NOTIMEX in Spanish 0218 GMT 28 Jun 94) Pemex increased its production of petroleum products 3.9 percent in the first four months of 1994, compared to the same period in 1993. It produced 1.6 million barrels a day for the domestic market. Of this amount, 498,400 barrels correspond to gasoline. (Madrid EFE in Spanish 0131 GMT 28 Jun 94)
