BFN [From the "Diplomatic Panorama" feature: By correspondents Igor Porshnev, Vitaliy Trubetskoy, and others] [Text] On May 18, in Interfax's material concerning cooperation between Russian and Pakistani secret services in the efforts to free former Soviet prisoners of war published in News Bulletins and in Diplomatic Panorama, the press secretary of the director of Russia's Intelligence Service, Tatyana Samolis, was quoted as saying that former Soviet prisoners of war still remained in Pakistan. In her Friday's interview with Interfax Samolis explained that some former Soviet servicemen taken prisoner "in the Afghan war" still remained abroad. According to Samolis, she did not mean any concrete country, in particular Pakistan. Members of Russia's Foreign Ministry explained to Interfax that former Soviet servicemen are held on Afghan soil. "Had our prisoners of war been in Pakistan, the Pakistanis would have extradited them long ago," a high-ranking Russian diplomat told Interfax's correspondent.
