Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] An official spokesman for the Foreign Ministry has stated that official memorandums have been exchanged between the Government of the Hashemite Kingdom of Jordan and the Vatican as a prelude to establishing diplomatic relations at an ambassadorial level between the two countries. The spokesman pointed out that intensive and continued contacts are under way through diplomatic channels on this issue. An announcement on taking this step will be made soon, he said. The spokesman added that final arrangements will be worked out to translate the cordial and distinguished relations between Jordan and the Vatican. His Majesty King Husayn and His Holiness Pope John Paul II are eager to entrench these relations, so they will enjoy much respect and appreciation proceeding from the historic and distinguished bilateral relations, he noted. The Vatican's official spokesman, Dr. (Navarro Valse), said Jordanian-Vatican relations are good and fruitful. In a news conference held at the Vatican, he added that the Vatican also has good relations with many regional states, and has contacts on fruitful cooperation with some of them. He particularly pointed to relations with Jordan, and asserted that Christians in Jordan enjoy unequalled privileges. Dr. (Valse) added that out of their desire to put these good relations in an institutional framework, the two sides began to negotiate the establishment of full diplomatic relations. There was an agreement on this issue. (Valse) explained that the arrangements on this issue are about to be finalized, and that they will be announced soon. He expressed the hope that this step will be a catalyst to enhance the current cooperation with Jordan that has long contributed to supporting and promoting the peace process. He indicated Jordan's great role in regional stability and security. [passage omitted]
