May 26, 1990, Saturday, Home Edition

BRIEFLY

Smaller Personal Income Gains: Americans spent more during April despite a slump in income gains, the Commerce Department said.

But personal income rose a mere 0.3% -- the smallest monthly increase in seven months -- after solid gains of 0.8% in the two preceding months. The last time there was a smaller increase was September of 1989, when incomes were up 0.2%. But spending rose at a stronger-than-forecast 0.6% last month after gains of 0.5% in February and March.
