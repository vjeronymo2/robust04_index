Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Changsha, March 4 (XINHUA) -- Central China's Hunan Province has boosted export-oriented vegetable production in the bid to increase farmers' income. The province has taken full advantage of local geographic conditions, with two cities and a prefecture bordering on Guangdong Province and the Guangxi Zhuang Autonomous Region, which are near to Hong Kong, Macao and Vietnam. It has set up sales agencies in Guangdong and Guangxi to promote vegetable exports to Hong Kong, Macao and Vietnam. Last year lingling prefecture exported over 200 million kg of garlic, sweet potatoes, tomatoes and radishes to Hong Kong and Vietnam, double the total of the previous year. Tinned chillies from the Jianghua Autonomous County of the Yao nationality sells well on world markets. The product was the winner of a gold medal for new products at the fifth Chinese Commodity Fair. Changsha, Yueyang and Hengyang cities exported 1,300 tons of tinned mushrooms last year, and more mushroom-growing villages and towns have emerged. Guidong, Pingjiang and Lengshuijiang cities have succeeded in growing non-polluted vegetables for export. Now the province has built up a number of export-oriented vegetable-growing bases with priority on developing more varieties and improving quality. Meanwhile, advanced storage, processing and package techniques are being stressed.
