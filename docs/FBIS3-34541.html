Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tehran, Feb. 8, IRNA -- The Islamic Republic of Iran and Turkey are studying export of Iran's natural gas to Europe through a pipeline via Turkish Territory. Turkey is expected to import 5,000 million cubic meters of Iranian gas annually, and is also willing to go ahead with purchase of Iranian oil. Turkish Minister of State for Economy, Cemil Erhan, and Iran's Oil Minister Gholam Reza Aqazadeh-Kho'i in a meeting in Tehran expressed satisfaction with consolidation of Tehran-Ankara relations. The two officials referring to historical bonds between the two nations, called for expansion of cooperation in all areas. Erhan and Aqazadeh agreed to follow up the accord through dispatch of a mission to Turkey from the Iranian Oil Ministry. Erhan arrived in Tehran Monday [7 February] at the invitation of Minister of Heavy Industries, Mohammad Nejad-Hoseynian.
