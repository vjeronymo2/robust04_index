921103 FT 03 NOV 92 / Letter: Reject the impassioned lobbies Sir, Your leader, 'One cheer for the chancellor' (October 30), affirms the chancellor's right to insist on the maintenance of economically valuable capital spending in a recession. However, not all capital projects currently vying for support fall into this category. One notable example is London's Jubilee line, which has a negative return measured on a cost benefit basis. The credibility of the government's economic policy is to be judged by its willingness to reject such cases despite impassioned lobbying. David Starkie, Garden Cottage, Benham Park, Newbury, Berks RG16 8LX
