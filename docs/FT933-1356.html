930924 FT 24 SEP 93 / Arts: Today's Television Are gardens created by women so very different or more distinctive to those created by men that BBC2 is justified in making a series called An Englishwoman's Garden? We can find out at 8.30 when the series begins with a look at four gardens - or can we? Sybil Spencer's garden was originally created by her son and husband and Frieda Brown's garden in Leeds is also the responsibility of Joe Brown. This is an intriguing start for the series. ITV begins another of those series which seek big ratings and a socially useful purpose - 'Crimewatch' is an obvious example. Missing (9.00) kicks off with the somewhat startling fact that 250,000 people in Britain disappear each year. In trying to track down missing persons the programme will, like 'Crimewatch', use 'dramatic reconstruction'. It is difficult to guess whether fans of opera or of animation will want to watch BBC2's new series Animated Arias. The series will provide 12 five-minute films, shown in pairs. Today, two familiar lollipops: 'Vesti la giubba' from 'Pagliacci' and 'La donna e mobile' from Rigoletto (9.50).
