BFN [Text] The forum Towards a New Accord which was attended in Moscow by representatives of 17 parties and 30 movements from all former USSR's republics except Turkmenistan called on the leaders of all the new independent states to refute territorial claims on one another and publicly say so. Igor Smirnov, chairman of the International Movement for Democratic Reforms ruling body, Arkadiy Volskiy, President of the Russian Union of Industrialists and Entrepreneurs, Gennadiy Zyuganov, leader of the Communist Party of the Russian Federation, Nikolay Ryzhkov, former Soviet prime minister, and State Duma and Federation Council members were among the participants of the forum. The forum supported the idea of a Eurasian Union that had been put forward by Kazakh President Nursultan Nazarbayev and called on the CIS heads of state to consider it from the viewpoint of what is good for their peoples. They supported the proposal made by Ryzhkov that a CIS parliament had to be set up.
