Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Phnom Penh AKP January 24 -- Prince Norodom Sirivut, vice prime minister and minister for foreign affairs and international cooperation, met here on Saturday [22 January] Mr. Michael Kirby, special UN envoy for human rights, who is now on a visit to Cambodia. Australian Michael Kirby, appointed last November as the United Nations special representative for human rights in Cambodia, arrived in Phnom Penh on Friday [21 January] and is due to begin looking into Cambodia's human rights situation as part of a week-long fact- finding mission aimed at strengthening human rights education. Speaking to his guest, Prince Norodom Sirivut welcomed the Cambodia's visit by Mr. Michael Kirby on his human rights mission in the country, saying the issue of human rights in Cambodia [word indistinct] to be improved. He thanked the United Nations for its concern and assistance to the improvement of human rights in Cambodia and pledged to support and create favourable conditions to the UN Human Rights Center to fulfill its mission in Cambodia. Mr. Kirby informed the prince that his mission in Cambodia was to keep contact with the government and people of Cambodia on human rights issue and help the latter to respect the human rights. Mr. Kirby will to make a report on the human right situation in Cambodia at the UN [words indistinct] the 50th meeting of the UN Human Rights Committee. He also asked Prince Sirivut to send a high-power delegation of Cambodia to participate in the (?meeting to be) held in Geneva in (?February).
