Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Santa Fe de Bogota, 11 Jan (AFP) -- The Colombian Government acknowledged on Tuesday the presence of U.S. troops on its national territory, which -- it explained -- "are carrying out various tasks within the framework of current treaties" signed between the two countries. A press communique issued by the National Defense Ministry reported there are 259 U.S. soldiers carrying out different tasks in various regions of the country. The official communique seeks to calm the strong controversy among country's politicians and media over the recent arrival of U.S. Marines in the area of the Colombian Pacific area, where -- according to the government -- they are participating in the construction of civil projects in the poor area of Juanchaco.
