930224 FT 24 FEB 93 / Observer: Keep Cuba Tidy Could it be that the lack of competition in today's Cuban election is designed to keep the streets clean? According to Fidel Castro, sparse resources are not being squandered on littering streets with election literature and dissenting candidates. The island's first election since the 1959 revolution will be 'the purest in the world', he says. It is not going to be an exercise in 'cheap politics and demagogy . . . which you see today in a large part of the world'.
