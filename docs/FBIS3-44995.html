Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, January 11 (XINHUA) -- China will gradually increase its investment in large-scale agricultural development projects, which reached two billion yuan last year. Speaking at a national meeting on comprehensive agricultural development, State Councillor Chen Junsheng said China will continue to increase the output of grain, cotton and other farm produce and more investment will be used for their development. Chen, who is in charge of China's agriculture, said that priority will be given to production of grain, cotton, edible oil and meat, transformation of grassland and development of large areas of wasteland. People in the central and western parts of China, which accounted for about 60 percent of the total investment during the five years, will be given more money for comprehensive agricultural development, he added. He said that nearly 20 billion yuan investment in comprehensive agricultural projects during 1988-1992 had helped China produce more grain, cotton, edible oil, sugar and meat. The official said that a total of about seven million ha [hectares] of low-yield and medium-yield farmland had been transformed during the five years and about one million ha of wasteland suitable for farming have been developed. In addition, about one million ha of land have been afforested and 300,000 ha of grassland improved. Thanks to the development, China has produced an additional 17 million tons of grain, 250,000 tons of cotton, 700,000 tons of edible oil, nearly 10,000 tons of sugar and 620,000 tons of meat. Priority of the project has been given to five provinces in the valleys of the Yellow River, Huaihe River and Haihe River, including the provinces of Hebei, Shandong, Anhui, Henan, Jiangsu, plains in northwest China and some areas in south and central China. "There are about 66 million ha of low-yield and medium-yield farmland to be transformed," the official said. He added that the land already transformed made up only one tenth of the total. He said the productivity of 80 percent of China's 400 million ha of grassland will be doubled if it is transformed. Grain produced by transformed farmland in east China's Anhui Province accounted for about 60 percent of Anhui's total and in coastal Shandong Province and north China's Hebei Province, 33 percent of their grain came from transformed land. Liaoning Province, which is known as a large industrial province in China and had to buy 1.5 million tons of grain from other parts of the country, has since 1991 become self-efficient in grain, eggs, milk, meat and some other farm produce, after a five-year-long comprehensive agricultural development.
