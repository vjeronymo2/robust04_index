BFN [Text] Tokyo, June 24 KYODO -- Nippon Telegraph and Telephone Corp.'s purchases of equipment from foreign companies in fiscal 1993 ended last March were up 20.4 percent from the previous year to 119 billion yen, the telecommunications giant announced Friday [24 June]. On a dollar basis, such purchases increased 39.3 percent to 1,092 million dollars. NTT's procurement from abroad accounted for 16 percent of its total purchases in the fiscal year under review, the company said, with those from U.S. firms making up 93 percent. Among U.S. equipment purchased by NTT were digital exchanges, optical-fiber cables and personal computers, it said.
