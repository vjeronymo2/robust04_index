Language: Slovene Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] In light of some newspapers writing about the alleged replacement of U.S. Ambassador Alan Wendt [as heard], the Slovene Government, the U.S. Embassy, and the Defense Ministry have issued statements: In its statement, the Slovene Government emphasizes that it has no knowledge of any replacements and that it favors strengthening good relations with Washington in all spheres. The hitherto good cooperation is, to a great extent, also a result of the work and efforts of the U.S. Embassy in Ljubljana. [passage omitted] The claims of the U.S. Embassy were also supported by our Defense Ministry. A brief statement says that Lieutenant (Leach) has, from 3 November 1993, lead very successfully a liaison group within the Army to Army program, which is aimed at exchanging experiences in the military sphere. Claims that Lieutenant (Leach) has been recalled is insulting to Mr. Leach -- who has, in the meantime, become a great friend of our country -- and harms diplomatic efforts, the international reputation of Slovenia, and cooperation of our ministry with the U.S. Defense Ministry, the Defense Ministry statement says.
