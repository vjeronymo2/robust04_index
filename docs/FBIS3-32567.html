Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [From the "National" newscast] [Excerpt] President Violeta de Chamorro brought a message of peace and reconciliation to the municipalities of San Francisco, Santo Tomas, and San Pedro de Lovago in Chontales Department, where she inaugurated various projects. Mrs. Chamorro issued an appeal to the irregulars, urging them to lay down their weapons and work in peace and tranquillity. During the ceremony at Santo Tomas inaugurating six blocks which had been newly paved, President Chamorro said her government is currently studying the proposal presented by poet Pablo Antonio Cuadra and Cardinal Obando y Bravo in connection with the situation prevailing in the northern part of the country. She said similar proposals were presented in due time by the government and, in fact, it has offered various amnesties. Nevertheless, she said, certain groups are still up in arms. [Begin Chamorro recording] I ask them to lay put down their weapons and seek a way to work in peace and tranquillity. [end recording] Referring to the Army's presence in the northern part of the country, Mrs. Chamorro said that farmers have requested this. [Begin Chamorro recording] I know many of the farmers -- for example, local farmers -- have asked me to send the Army, to send people to control the situation so they may work, produce, ship their coffee harvest, and ship everything. That is why the Army is there and that is why the police and everyone else are there -- so that farmers may ship their harvests. [end recording] [passage omitted]
