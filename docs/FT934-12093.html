931028 FT 28 OCT 93 / Technology: Buzzwords - Software at Work GEOGRAPHICAL INFORMATION SYSTEMS use maps or locations on-screen. They match other data, such as demographic or financial, to a map or other representation of physical space. DATA CAPTURE, sometimes known as data take-on, presents specific difficulties for a GIS. Data cleansing - the process of resolving discrepancies or omissions in data - is a large part of the task. Sheffield reports that it found relatively few omissions.
