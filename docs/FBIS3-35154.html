Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Cairo, 21 Feb (MENA)--Palestinian and Israeli negotiators started in Cairo today a new round of talks on the final arrangements for temporary Palestinian self-rule in the Gaza Strip and Jericho. The talks began with a private meeting between Dr. Nabil Sha'th, political adviser to Palestinian President Yasir 'Arafat, and General Amnon Shahaq, Israeli deputy chief of staff, to draw up the agenda of the talks. Afterward, the meeting of the Security Affairs Committee began under Dr. Sha'th and General Shahaq. The Civilian Affairs Committee also met under attorney Jamil al-Tarifi representing the Palestinian side and Gadi Zohar representing the Israeli side. Ya'qov Neriah, political adviser to Israeli Prime Minister Yitzhaq Rabin, is participating in the talks for the first time. Palestinian sources said Y'aqov Neriah's participation in the talks means the Israeli side is serious about reaching an agreement on the points of differences still under discussion. The sources noted the positive atmosphere and the great progress made during the last round of talks in Taba. Hasan 'Asfur and Y'aqov Neriah told journalists that the talks will be held in secret and that the press will not be allowed to cover them. The Palestinian and Israeli negotiators will hold intensive talks until Wednesday, when Sha'th and Shahaq will announce the results at a news conference.
