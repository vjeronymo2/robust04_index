July 6, 1989, Thursday, Home Edition

MORNING REPORT: STAGE

Actor Woody Harrelson, who tends bar on the NBC series "Cheers," will be re-opening tonight in two one-act plays at the Court Theatre in Hollywood, where he also toiled last fall. One play is "2 on 2," which he also wrote, and the other is Edward Albee's "The Zoo Story." Others returning from the earlier production are Clint Allen, Brad Blaisdell, Rob Moran and director Larry Gilman. Even though he will be going back to work on "Cheers" early in August, Harrelson says he will complete the scheduled run of the one-acts through Aug. 13, having successfully managed to do both last fall. "Doing 'Cheers' and the plays simultaneously is exhausting, but what it means for me is being able to hear people laugh day and night, and that is sweet, sweet music," says the actor. ALEENE MacMINN
