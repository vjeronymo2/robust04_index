September 16, 1990, Sunday, Valley Edition

DIGEST / LOCAL NEWS IN BRIEF: FIRE BURNS 100 ACRES OF BRUSH IN AGUA DULCE

A fire burned 100 acres of brush in Agua Dulce on Saturday afternoon before firefighters extinguished the blaze, the Los Angeles County Fire Department reported.

The fire burned in the 32000 block of Agua Dulce Canyon Road, north of the Antelope Valley Freeway, said Los Angeles County fire dispatcher Teresa Rios. It broke out at about 2:30 p.m. and was controlled in less than two hours, said Fire Department spokesman Kerry Brisson.

No structures were threatened and no one was injured, Brisson said. The cause of the fire was under investigation.
