BFN ["News Release"] [Text] Estonian Foreign Minister Juri Luik signed, together with Council of Europe member states foreign ministers, in Strasbourg, on Wednesday [11 May], European Human Rights Convention 11th protocol, spokeswoman of the Ministry Mari-Ann Rikken told a press conference on Friday [13 May]. The protocol prescribes foundation of a permanent European Human Rights Court, which would receive complaints from persons, groups of persons, non-governmental organisations and states. The Court includes one judge, elected for six years, from each Council of Europe member state. The protocol comes into force after ratification. Estonia, however, has to ratify the convention and its first 10 protocols first.
