Language: English Article Type:BFN [By KAZTAG correspondent Ivan Zakharchenko for TASS] [Text] Almaty January 31 TASS -- "We welcome Vietnam's initiative to set up a joint commission to streamline economic relations. This will help expand cooperation in all spheres," Kazakhstan's Prime Minister Sergey Tereshchenko said. The statement was made at the meeting with visiting Vietnamese Foreign Minister Nguyen Manh Cam in Almaty on Monday. Tereshchenko stressed that Vietnam has accumulated rich experience in combating inflation, strengthening currency relations and developing the light industry. Kazakhstan and Vietnam could cooperate in establishing joint ventures to process agricultural produce and produce gas and oil, the prime minister added. Kazakhstan has skilled geological cadres and could help Vietnam with gas and oil prospecting and training specialists, the premier said. The Vietnamese minister recalled that Vietnam was one of the first countries to have recognised the independence of Kazakhstan. He believes further development of relations would be beneficial to both countries.
