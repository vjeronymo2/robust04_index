921102 FT 02 NOV 92 / Business backs Maastricht pact MOST UK companies believe adoption of the Maastricht treaty will be beneficial to them, although few appear to understand precisely what is involved, according to a survey for Ernst & Young, the accountants and management consultants. The Mori survey of 131 companies found that 56 per cent thought adoption of the treaty would benefit their companies while 13 per cent said it would be detrimental. A further 27 per cent said it would have no effect and 5 per cent had no opinion. Mr Michael Gardiner, the Ernst & Young partner in charge of European issues, said that support for Maastricht from respondents 'appears to be based on a positive attitude to Europe rather than an understanding of the detail'. The Institute of Directors has urged the government to clarify what it sees as the uncertainty created by the treaty. The IoD, which has 42,000 members, has written to ministers and MPs saying there are at least 20 questions that need to be answered. They include the areas over which European Community member states will retain competence and the extent to which governments will control economic policy. Mr Peter Morgan, IoD director-general, said: 'Since the (European exchange rate mechanism) is in disarray, with many countries only hanging on by imposing capital controls, while Germany seems unlikely to give up the Deutsche Mark, what is the urgency for ratification?' By contrast, the Confederation of British Industry said failure to ratify the treaty would damage confidence. Sir Michael Angus, CBI president, last week told the Conservative backbench European committee: 'If the Maastricht treaty is not ratified, the UK runs the risk of losing the benefits of the single market.'
