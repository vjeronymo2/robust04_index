BFN [From the "RPC Television News" newscast] [Text] The Presidency Information and Public Relations Office of the Republic has released a communique announcing that by executive decree signed by President Guillermo Endara Galimany, educator German Vergara has been appointed education minister. Professor Vergara has been appointed due to the retirement of current Education Minister Marcos Alarcon, effective 1 July. The communique adds Professor Vergara is the current Health Ministry secretary general. The executive decree will become effective on 1 July. RPC tried to obtain reactions from Education Minister Alarcon as well as from Professor Vergara, but both declined to comment. The two men are members of the Arnulfist Party. Meanwhile, teachers union leaders have expressed hope the new appointment will foster a better understanding between teachers and the Education Ministry. Efrain de Leon, of the Association of Independent Teachers, has commented on the issue: [Begin de Leon recording] He represents the executive branch; therefore, he represents a political party and will function with political guidelines. We also know that working against him is his term of almost two months. We hope he will try during this period to correct the many mistakes Alarcon has made and reach an agreement, for the first time during this period of democracy, between the Education Ministry and the teachers unions. [end recording]
