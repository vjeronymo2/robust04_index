Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on regional guerrilla activities monitored through 23 March.

Colombia
In the early morning of 15 March, presumed FARC members dynamited a police roadblock near the 2 km marker of the Southern Highway in Neiva, Huila Department. No casualties were reported. (Santa Fe de Bogota EL TIEMPO in Spanish 17 Mar 94 p 9A) On 19 March, FARC kidnapped a doctor and a shopkeeper in Sabanas de Pedro, a rural area in Los Palmitos, Sucre Department, where 18 people have been kidnapped so far in 1994. Three FARC guerrillas died during a confrontation with the Narino Battalion troops in southern Bolivar Department on 19 March. A member of the National Liberation Army (ELN) was killed in Rionegro, Santander Department. An ELN guerrilla was killed in a confrontation with the anti- guerrilla Granaderos Battalion in Campamento, northern Antioquia. Two soldiers were injured in a confrontation with FARC members in the rural area of Turbo in Uraba, Antioquia Department. (Santa Fe de Bogota EL TIEMPO in Spanish 21 Mar 94 p 8A) On 22 March, 60 Socialist Renewal Movement guerrillas promised in Cali to demobilize and turn in their weapons. On 23 March, they will move from Valle del Cauca, Narino, and Tolima to the peace camp in Flor del Monte. Rebels set off explosives in the police station of Angostura, northern Antioquia Department, killing two policemen. (Santa Fe de Bogota Inravision Television Cadena 1 in Spanish 0130 GMT 22 Mar 94)
Guatemala
Presumed guerrillas on 16 March kidnapped the drivers of two oil tankers on a highway between Tucan and La Ceiba, Peten Department. (Guatemala City El GRAFICO in Spanish 17 Mar 94 p 2)
