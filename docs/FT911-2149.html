910501 FT 01 MAY 91 / Parliament and Politics: Pledge on brewing industry THE LABOUR Party promised a 'fresh start' review of competition, consumer choice, and prices in the brewing industry, Philip Rawstorne writes. Government orders introduced after the Monopolies and Mergers Commission report on the industry, had 'failed to stem the tide of takeovers and closures,' said Mr Doug Henderson, a Labour trade and industry spokesman. Government changes had done nothing to to cut the price of a pint or give consumers greater choice of beers. Retail prices had increased by 142 per cent between March 1979 and March 1991. Beer prices rose by 222 per cent over the same period, he said.
