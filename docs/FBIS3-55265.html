Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] In line with the recent decision by the head of the local administration Vladimir Yelagin, the Orenburg Region will have its own government. The resolution on the issue says that the Cabinet is to be formed proceeding from provisions of Yeltsin's decrees of October 22 and December 22, 1993 on measures to improve the activities of local executive bodies of power. The staff of the administration head, which will exist in parallel with the local government, is to be cut by 20 percent. The similar two-level executive structure was first created in 1992 by the former governor of the Sverdlovsk Region Eduard Rossel, who was dismissed by Yeltsin's decree. Now Rossel is the president of the Association of Economic Interaction of the Ural Region. He is also elected deputy to the new Russian Parliament.
