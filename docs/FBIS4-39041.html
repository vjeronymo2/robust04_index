BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Oleg Velichko] [Text] Moscow June 5 -- A government delegation led by First Vice-Premier Oleg Soskovets will fly to Malaysia for a two-day visit this afternoon. The main event of the coming trip is the signing of a contract on June 7 to sell Malaysia 18 Russian MiG-29 fighters. The Russian delegation will discuss a package of bilateral economic issues with Malaysian Premier Mahathir Mohamed and his cabinet members in the Malaysian capital Kuala Lumpur. It is also planned that the delegation will be received by the Malaysian supreme head of state. ITAR-TASS learnt at the Russian Government's office that the above-mentioned contract has been agreed upon. It is worth 615 million U.S. dollars. According to Malaysian representatives, 20- 25 per cent of the sum will be paid with palm oil, necessary for various industries. The first fighter will be delivered next April. The implementation of the entire project is planned for a 25-year period and provides for creating infrastructure in Malaysia, necessary for the operation of MiG fighters. The two countries plan to build in Malaysia a technical centre to service aircraft.
