BFN [Report by ITAR-TASS correspondent Boris Maynayev] [Text] Bishkek, 2 May -- The presidents of Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, and Uzbekistan devoted their meeting on 29-30 April on the shore of Lake Issyk-Kul to the deepening of economic links in Central Asia and the CIS, says a communique on the results of the summit meeting released today by President Askar Akayev's press service. The document notes that the meeting was held in a spirit of mutual understanding and trust. The sides exchanged views on the current situation in the region and the Commonwealth, and also on a whole range of international problems. The presidents of the three countries signed a treaty on the creation of a single economic space, representing a new level of integration for the economies of the Central Asian states, the communique stresses. The sides noted that the time has come to remove all the obstacles standing in the way of economic interaction and to eliminate customs barriers, double taxation, and complications with the passage of payments; and that the treaty is fully in accordance with the provisions of the agreement on the creation of a CIS economic union, and is open for joining by all the states of the Commonwealth.
