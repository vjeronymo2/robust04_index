March 24, 1989, Friday, Southland Edition

THAILAND HONORS 165 SLAIN SECURITY MEN

King Bhumibol Adulyadej and Queen Sirikit presided over a mass cremation Thursday of 165 soldiers, police and defense volunteers killed in Thailand border defense and anti-insurgency incidents last year. The victims included 116 soldiers, 40 defense volunteers, six policemen, two navy personnel and one civilian volunteer, military-run television reported.

In the annual rite, the king lighted a ceremonial flame during the cremation of the 163 Buddhists at a temple on the outskirts of Bangkok. The two Muslims were honored in a ceremony in the south, where there are large Muslim populations.
