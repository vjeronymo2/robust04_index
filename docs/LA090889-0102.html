September 8, 1989, Friday, Home Edition

GOSSEN, BROUSSARD LEAD WASHINGTON ST. TO 46-41 VICTORY OVER BRIGHAM YOUNG

Brad Gossen passed for 317 yards and two touchdowns, and Steve Broussard caught a 66-yard scoring pass and ran for three other touchdowns as Washington State defeated No. 24-ranked Brigham Young, 46-41, Thursday night.

Gossen threw scoring passes of 64 and 66 yards in the first half to Tim Stallworth and Broussard.

Washington State (2-0) bounced back from a 21-17 halftime deficit as Broussard scored twice on one-yard runs in the third quarter and Jason Hanson kicked a 58-yard field goal, breaking the school record of 57 yards set by Paul Watson in 1977.

Hanson also had field goals of 22, 52 and 46 yards, tying a school record for the most field goals in a game, and extended his string of successful conversions to a school-record 54.

Ty Detmer of BYU (1-1) completed 34 of 53 passes for 537 yards and four touchdowns, but he had three passes intercepted.
