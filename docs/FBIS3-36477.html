Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Sergey Ostanin] [Text] Moscow Feb 4 -- Russia and the United States will conduct joint military exercises in the Far East in July to train troops for operations conducted by peacekeeping forces. The exercises are expected to be held for a week on Russian territory, with the participation of a few units belonging to land troops, ITAR-TASS learned from a well-informed source in the Russian Defense Ministry today. The possibility of conducting joint military exercises was discussed last year during a working meeting between Russian and U.S. defense Ministers in Bavaria in summer, and during the visit by the Russian defense minister to the United States in autumn. A final agreement on joint exercises was reached at the January U.S.- Russian summit in Moscow.
