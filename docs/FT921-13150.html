920123 FT 23 JAN 92 / Parliament and Politics: Labour accuses government of raising tax burden LABOUR yesterday accused the government of raising the tax burden, squandering at least Pounds 11bn on under-priced privatisation share offers, and re-emphasised Britain's poor economic record with those of its European competitors. Mr Jack Cunningham, Labour's campaigns co-ordinator, produced figures to show that a couple with two children on average earnings have seen their tax rise from 35.2 per cent to 37.5 per cent in the 13 years of Tory government. Mr Paddy Ashdown, the Liberal Democrat leader, called on the government to end the uncertainty about the timing of the election. 'If the government can announce the Budget today, they should be prepared to announce the general election today,' he said, warning that in failing to end speculation, Mr John Major was putting party interests before the interests of the country.
