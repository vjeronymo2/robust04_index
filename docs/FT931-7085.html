930226 FT 26 FEB 93 / Deficit on trade outside EC falls sharply THE UK'S trade deficit with countries outside the European Community shrank by Pounds 324m last month, according to official figures released yesterday. The deficit on visible or merchandise goods was Pounds 1.02bn in January, compared with December's record Pounds 1.34bn deficit. The last time there was a surplus on UK trade with non-EC countries - which accounts for less than half of total overseas trade - was in the fourth quarter of 1986. Imports and exports remained at historically high levels with the value of exports reaching a record Pounds 4.29bn in January. The value of imports at Pounds 5.31bn was the second highest level ever recorded. The record high was in December when import values were Pounds 5.44bn. The underlying deficit, which excludes oil and erratic items such as aircraft, silver and precious stones, also narrowed to Pounds 776m in January, from Pounds 1.04bn in December. The Central Statistical Office gave no figures for trade with the EC as a new system called Intrastat is being introduced for measuring intra-EC trade, in line with the single market. The CSO said it expected to present the first results of Intrastat towards the end of July. In December, the last month for which a full set of figures is available, the UK's visible deficit with the EC was Pounds 541m and with the whole world Pounds 1.88bn. Statisticians stressed that it was not possible to infer from the latest non-EC trade figures what was happening to trade in the community. The CSO said devaluation of sterling had yet to make much impact on exports. The government said in the Autumn Statement in November that it did not expect exports to benefit from devaluation until the first quarter of this year. In the three months to the end of January the value of exports to non-EC countries grew by 6 per cent, compared with the previous three-month period. Import values grew by 14 per cent. Trade volumes showed a similar trend. Last month import and export volumes reached record levels. A breakdown of trade by area shows that the UK has a negative balance of trade with all areas outside the EC except the oil exporting countries. The deficit on visible trade with the US and Canada widened to Pounds 182m in January from Pounds 123m in December. In the latest three months the deficit was Pounds 367m compared with Pounds 44m in the three months to October. The 'real' or constant price estimates of the UK national accounts and other economic statistics will be re-based on the year 1990 in August this year instead of 1985 as at present, the Central Statistics Office said yesterday.
