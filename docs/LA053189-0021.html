May 31, 1989, Wednesday, Home Edition

MORNING REPORT: ROMANCE

Wedding March: Oscar-winning composer Marvin Hamlisch and independent television producer Terre Blair were married Monday in an outdoor ceremony on the banks of the Hudson River in New York. The marriage was the first for Hamlisch, 44, who has won three Academy Awards and a Pulitzer Prize, as well as Grammy, Tony and Golden Globe awards. Two of his Oscars were for "The Way We Were," and one for "The Sting." It was the second marriage for Blair, a native of Columbus, Ohio. Liza Minnelli, Ann-Margret and Carly Simon were among the guests, along with actor George Segal, who serenaded the newlyweds on the banjo, and singer Roberta Flack, who performed "The First Time Ever I Saw Your Face." The couple planned to leave Tuesday for a honeymoon in Europe. ALEENE MacMINN
