Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Text] Knin, 26 Feb (TANJUG) -- The republic of Serb Krajina and the Republic of Croatia today signed a document extending the cease-fire agreement until 31 March. Apart from the extension of the cease-fire, the warring sides agreed to continue talks on reaching a general cease-fire. This is the third time the cease-fire agreement between Krajina and Croatia -- originally signed on 17 December 1993 -- has been extended. The cease-fire has been holding, apart from sporadic provocations on the part of the Croatian Army, especially in northern Dalmatia and Lika. In the presence of Colonel Wood and UNPROFOR [UN Protection Forces] Commander Jean Cot the [extension] document was signed by Colonel Kosta Novakovic, assistant commander of the Serb Krajina army, in agreement with the civilian and military authorities of Krajina.
