BFN [Text] Istanbul, May 23 (A.A) -- Fourteen terrorists including two women were captured during operations by security forces in Istanbul, it was reported on Monday. A statement by security headquarters said among those captured were also military leaders of the terrorist organization. Automatic rifles, handguns, hand grenades and material used in making bombs were also seized in searches of their houses and hide outs. The statement said that the operations were started on May 10, adding that those captured were responsible for activities such as the killing of one police officer, the bombing of two coffee houses, a furniture shop, an official building in different districts of the city. It was also determined they had taken part in the bombing of an election office and two private offices. One of the terrorists is a military leader of the terrorist PKK [Workers Party of Kurdistan] organization and was trained in Greece, following which he came to Turkey, the statement also said. Another terrorist, a woman, was a secretary at the office of the now-banned, pro-Kurdish DEP [Democratic] Party, it was reported. Police said the investigation continues.
