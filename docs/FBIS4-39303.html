BFN [Text] Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev said that his country is coordinating its efforts with the Cyprus Government on possible new approaches in finding a solution to the Cyprus problem. Speaking after a meeting in Moscow with Foreign Minister Alekos Mikhailidhis, Kozyrev also said that the Russian Government wants a solution to be found for the Cyprus problem as soon as possible, based on UN decisions on the safeguarding of the Cyprus Republic's territorial integrity and unity. Kozyrev added that he has discussed with Mikhailidhis the possibility of new approaches to the Cyprus issue. In just a few words, he said, we coordinated our watches on how we should continue working on the issue by keeping in mind that Russia is a permanent member of the UN Security Council. Foreign Minister Mikhailidhis said that the discussion he had with Kozyrev centered on the next steps that will follow on the Cyprus issue, after the UN secretary general's report to the Security Council. Mikhailidhis continued his contacts and this afternoon met with the Russian Parliament President Ivan Rybkin. Later today, he will be received by Patriarch Aleksey.
