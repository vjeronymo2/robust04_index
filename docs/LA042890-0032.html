April 28, 1990, Saturday, Home Edition

LITHUANIA'S STRUGGLE FOR INDEPENDENCE

As one of the participants in the April 11 White House conference with Baltic-American leaders, my impressions were somewhat different from those conveyed by your story of April 12 (Part A).

The meeting itself was a signal that the Bush Administration is seriously considering various options that go beyond present rhetoric in responding to Soviet escalation of the Baltic situation.

The fact that Secretary of State James Baker, National Security Adviser Brent Scowcroft and White House Chief of Staff John Sununu attended the meeting along with President Bush helped emphasize this. The further fact that a meeting which was scheduled to last half an hour continued for more than an hour indicated that the proposals which were made were not dismissed out of hand.

No one in the Baltic delegation expected President Bush to announce a major policy shift based on our discussions. What we hoped for and received was the President's unequivocal endorsement of the right of the people of Estonia, Latvia and Lithuania for self-determination. We also received an invitation to continue providing input into the Administration's Baltic policy decision-making process.

JAAK TREIMAN

Canoga Park
