Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Unattributed editorial: "King Husayn's Successful Visits to the Sultanate of Oman, Qatar"] [Text] The successful visits His Majesty King Husayn made to the Sultanate of Oman and to Qatar, and his meetings with Sultan Qabus Bin-Sa'id and His Highness Shaykh Khalifah Bin-Hamad Al Thani have been the culmination of his majesty's continuous efforts to restore cohesion to the Arab ranks in order to confront the dangerous stage through which our nation is passing. Jordan, Oman, and Qatar have identical positions on Arab solidarity, joint Arab action, and the need to begin a new Arab stage that overcomes the negative elements of the past and takes into consideration the Arab nation's interest and future. However, some Arab positions are still hostage to these negative elements and fail to overcome them. Furthermore, these positions adhere to weak arguments and advance stands that are based on deceptive imaginations and facts which aim to implement the biggest conspiracy that targeted Iraq and the Arab nation as a whole in what was called the Gulf crisis. His majesty the king's statements to the Qatari media and Jordanian journalists reflect Jordan's firm and principled commitment to the higher pan-Arab interests and eagerness to maintain the minimum joint Arab action. The statements also demonstrate the efforts with sincere brothers to overcome the disaster and to start laying down new bases for Arab solidarity, based on mutual respect, eagerness to maintain the nation's rights and interests, and coordination of positions on peace negotiations, the status of Jerusalem, and support for the steadfastness of the kinsfolk in the occupied territories in face of occupation and in their just struggle to restore their legitimate rights, led by restoration of occupied Palestinian territories and the right to self-determination. Undoubtedly, His Majesty King Husayn's emphasis on the status of Jerusalem as part of the occupied territories, as stipulated in all international resolutions, and on linking the fate of the holy city to the current Arab situation is a renewed call on Arab leaders to meet, have frank discussion, evaluate what happened so far in peace negotiations, and coordinate Arab positions on Jerusalem in particular, and the Arab causes in general.
