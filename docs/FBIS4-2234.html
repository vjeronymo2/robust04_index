BFN [Text] Beijing, April 7 (XINHUA) -- The development of relations between Mongolia and China enjoys a promising prospect, said visiting Mongolian Minister of External Relations Tserenpiliyn Gombosuren. In an exclusive interview with XINHUA here this afternoon, Gombosuren voiced his satisfaction over the talks with Chinese Foreign Minister Qian Qichen yesterday and meeting with Premier Li Peng this afternoon. He said there would be "two important events" in Sino-Mongolian ties this year. One is Chinese Premier Li peng's Mongolia visit at the end of this month, and the other the upcoming China visit by Chairman Nachagyn Bagabandy of the Mongolian State Great Hural. The high-level exchange of visits between the two countries would be of major significance in the development of bilateral links, he added. Gombosuren noted that the two countries' economic and trade relations have been expanding in recent years. Last year Mongolia's trade volume with China, in which border trade played an important role, accounted for one-fourth of the country's total trade volume. He pointed out that there exist great potential and a bright future in the cooperation between the two countries in such fields as fuel, power and transportation. He stressed that it is the priority of Mongolia's foreign policy to develop friendly relations with China and Russia, two neighbors of Mongolia. Gombosuren and his party arrived here yesterday. In addition to Beijing they are also scheduled to tour Tianjin and Nanjing.
