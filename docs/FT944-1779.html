941219 FT 19 DEC 94 / Socialists lead in Sofia polls The Bulgarian Socialist party of former communists was leading the race for votes in the third general election since Bulgaria embraced democracy, preliminary estimates showed after the voting yesterday. Three pollsters gave the socialists between 42 and 44 per cent of the vote and the Union of Democratic Forces around 24 per cent. In the last elections in October 1991, the Socialists won 106 seats, second to UDF's 110, with neither holding a majority.
