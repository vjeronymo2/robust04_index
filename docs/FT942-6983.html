940527 FT 27 MAY 94 / Arts: Today's Television Time was when the sense of release prompted by the ending of the working week could be expressed by wearing a tie decorated with the letters 'TGIF': Thank God It's Friday. Now television does the job for us by organising a Friday night schedule which seems intended to drive us out of our homes. If you simply have to stay indoors you could always watch comedy repeats such as Open All Hours with Ronnie Barker playing the la-la-lascivious grocer, Arkwright, whose mission in life is to bed the buxom Nurse Gladys (8.00 BBC1); or Red Dwarf in which Kryten discovers he has just 24 hours to live .well, to exist shall we say, given that he is a robot (9.00 BBC2). Television's only Friday night treat is Have I Got News For You, a startling improvement on its radio ancestor, 'The News Quiz'. Guests are now faced with the virtually impossible task of matching the recent performance of Bob Monkhouse. He delivered a lesson in topical comedy to team captains Ian Hislop and Paul Merton who usually have such an easy time dominating the proceedings (10.00 BBC2).
