BFN [By Jose Otero] [Text] Foreign Minister Jose Mulino requested yesterday the support of countries in the Nonaligned Movement (NAM) for Panama to be given the seat of the next NAM heads of state summit in 1999, to "show to the world the country that is to manage the Canal in the next century." Mulino made this request during his main speech before the 11th NAM Foreign Relations Ministers meeting currently being held in Cairo, Egypt. "I hope my successors at the foreign ministry will support and honor Panama's request to be the seat of the NAM heads of state summit in 1999," Mulino emphasized. The foreign minister added in his speech that, if this request is granted, Panama will secure its most important commitment to the world. Mulino called on all NAM member countries to prepare and back the initiative unanimously as a homage to the Panamanian people. "We advance, without having any time to loose or any margin for errors, toward the historic commitment of receiving the Panama Canal on 31 December 1999," Mulino added.
