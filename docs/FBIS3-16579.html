Language: Ukrainian Article Type:BFN [Text] The press service of the president of Crimea issued a statement today in connection with the 12 March 1994 resolution by the Central Electoral Commission on the elections to the Supreme Council of the Crimea Republic. The statement indicates that in his decree "On Conducting a Poll Among the Citizens of the Crimea Republic on 27 March," President Yuriy Meshkov was guided by the electors' will and also took into account the refusal of the Supreme Council of Crimea to lift a moratorium on holding an all-Crimean referendum. In setting the date of the poll, the president was guided by the fact that with the assistance of the Central Electoral Commission, significant organizational and material losses could be avoided in the course of conducting the poll and electing deputies to the Supreme Council of Crimea. The document underlines that the president will continue taking into account the Crimea citizens' will while solving the most important state issues.
