BFN [Text] Bucharest ROMPRES, 4/4/1994 -- "We have spent 48 hours in Romania and we have noted that you have all the necessary elements for success. You have a big potential but a greater support is needed from the part of the Romanians living abroad, especially in the United States, said the minister of foreign affairs of El Salvador, Miguel Angel Salaverria, after a round of talks with the head of the Romanian diplomacy, Teodor Melescanu, on Monday, April 4. One of the main theme approached during the talks referred to the concrete cooperation in the economic field between the two countries, said the Romanian minister of foreign affairs, Teodor Melescanu. The Romanian party underscored its economic potential in several fields of interest for El Salvador, especially the textiles, electric energy, petro-chemistry, and expressed its readiness to participate, once the issue of the financial sources is solved, said Melescanu. In the context, the two parties analysed the idea of opening a Romanian economic mission in El Salvador. The two parties debated issues concerning the regional relations in Eastern Europe as well as in Central America. A second round of talks between the two foreign ministers is scheduled for Monday afternoon when an accord is going to be signed thus canceling the requirement of visas on diplomatic and service passports. The two ministers will sign also a memorandum concerning a consultations mechanism to be established between the two foreign ministries. The minister of foreign affairs of El Salvador, Miguel Angel Salaverria, was received on Monday by Romanian President Ion Iliescu. The Salvadorean guest conveyed to the Romanian head of state "a warm message" from the part of Salvadorean President Alfredo Cristiani, expressing the desire for strengthened cooperation between El Salvador and Romania. The Salvadorean dignitary, who is making a several days long private visit in Romania, was pleasantly impressed by the contacts he had had here. The democratic future of Romania is secure, said the Salvadorean minister. The Salvadorean minister referred also to the future of the diplomatic relations between the two countries -- a Salvadorean ambassador for Romania is planned to be based in Rome -- as well as to the possibilities for joint actions in the economic field to which Romania could participate with technology while El Salvador with work force and capital.
