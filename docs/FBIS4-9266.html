BFN [Text] Bucharest ROMPRES, 20/4/1994 -- Foreign Ministry Spokesman Mircea Geoana made a few points on news items in the Romanian media on the Romanian-Hungarian treaty, during a news conference he held in Bucharest on 20 April. The news items claimed that "sources at the Foreign Ministry and the presidency said that, pursuant to U.S. and NATO pressures, the basic Romanian-Hungarian political treaty will be signed by 1 July, 1994." Mircea Geoana denied the existence of such an understanding and said that as far as Romania was concerned, it was interested to conclude the political treaty with Hungary the soonest. "Even before the Paris conference or 26-27 May, when the `stability' pact initiated by Balladur is launched, we would like to do it," said Mircea Geoana. He added that Hungary has not stated its stand on the proposals advanced long ago by the Romanian Foreign Ministry regarding the outstanding issues. "If we got an answer and were able to agree on a new round of negotiations, this would be a positive element in the actuation of the Romanian-Hungarian political dialogue," he said. Regarding the issue of lifting the embargo against the Federal Republic of Yugoslavia, "from what we know this is not envisaged for now" -- Foreign Ministry Spokesman Mircea Geoana said, referring to the "official launch" of the joint demarche of Greece, Bulgaria and Romania in this respect. The Foreign Ministry spokesman said such a demarche ought to be made at the right time, when the process of political negotiations reaches a congenial stage. "In keeping with the evolution of the negotiations between the Bosnian conflicting parties, a decision will be analyzed and made by all parties concerned", he said, adding that Hungary showed interest in the demarche, while Ukraine did not state its stand. The idea of a joint demarche of Greece, Bulgaria and Romania for the lifting of the embargo on the Federal Republic of Yugoslavia was launched during the Bucharest visit by the Greek foreign minister a few weeks ago. The initiative pursues the easing of the negative effects emerged following the application of the UN sanctions by the countries neighbouring ex-Yugoslavia. The prime minister of the Grand Duchy of Luxembourg, Jacques Smiter, will make an official visit in Romania over April 24-26 at the invitation of the Romanian Prime Minister Nicolae Vacaroiu, the Romanian Foreign Ministry spokesman also said. The agenda of the visit includes official talks with the Romanian premier, interviews with Romanian President Ion Iliescu, Senate President Oliviu Gherman, Chamber of Deputies Speaker Adrian Nastase. On the occasion of the visit, an inter-governmental agreement of cooperation in the fields of culture, education, science and sport will be signed. The postponement of the signing of the basic political treaty between Romania and the Federal Republic of Yugoslavia, first scheduled to take place while Yugoslav Federal President Zoran Lilic was in Romania for the Crans Montana Forum, stirred the interest of the journalists who attended the Foreign Ministry briefing on 20 April. Confirming President Lilic would not attend the Forum because of the tense situation in Bosnia, Mircea Geoana said the official document would be signed only after the two parties agreed on all issues. "It is an important political document with a neighbour state, that has to fall in line with the whole of the political treaties Romania is concluding with other states of the world," he added. "It is not to be achieved upon conjunctural bases, statuting instead the format of bilateral relations in all spheres." Referring to the conflict in Gorazde, the Romanian Foreign Ministry spokesman reiterated Romania's constant stand relying upon the principle of using the whole range of political-diplomatic means to induce the conflicting parties to go on negotiating. "We follow with worry the events in Gorazde and see the high rate of contacts and statements by all parties concerned, which leaves room for optimism," Geoana said.
