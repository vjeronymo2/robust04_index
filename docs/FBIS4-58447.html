BFN [Text] Doha -- Qatari Foreign Minister Shaykh Hamad Bin-Jasim Bin-Jabr Al Thani opened the fifth multilateral meeting of the Middle East Arms Control and Regional Security working group at the Doha Sheraton Hotel this morning. The four-day meeting is attended by 220 representatives from 43 countries. His Excellency Muhammad al-Za'abi, United Arab Emirates [UAE] ambassador to Qatar, heads the UAE delegation to the meetings. In a speech opening the meeting, the Qatari foreign minister said that he hopes that the meeting will succeed, and that this meeting confirms the desire to effectively participate in the Middle East peace process. He said this meeting falls within the framework of Qatar's eagerness to render this peaceful process a success and of its continued participation in the multilateral talks, which basically deal with the post-peace stage. He explained that the aspired peace is one that restores all the occupied Arab territories to their owners, foremost holy Jerusalem, as well as restoring the Palestinians' legitimate national rights, including the right to self-determination.
