920915 FT 15 SEP 92 / World Stock Markets (Asia Pacific): Nikkei average gains 2 per cent but in low volume FUTURES-LED trading left shares higher yesterday with the Nikkei average 2 per cent ahead, but volume was low ahead of today's public holiday, agencies report from Tokyo. The 225-share average gained 363.71 at 18,471.40 after recording a day's high of 18,552,79 and low of 18,149.34. Advances outnumbered declines by 589 to 399, with 153 issues unchanged, although turnover was estimated at only 230m shares, compared with 700m on Friday. The Topix index of all first section stocks improved 13.26 to 1,383.11, and in London the ISE/Nikkei 50 index put on 5.50 to 1,113.48. News that Germany was to cut interest rates spurred activity, with the financial sector very active. Sumitomo Bank moved ahead Y30 to Y1,730 in volume of 1.47m shares, and Mitsubishi Bank firmed Y10 to Y2,290 with some 1.28m shares traded. Nomura Securities advanced Y70 to Y1,700 and Nikko Securities ended Y45 higher at Y786, both in heavy volume. Blue chip electricals encountered bargain hunting. Hitachi rose Y9 to Y792, Toshiba Y19 to Y629, Sony Y100 to Y4,210 and Pioneer Y140 to Y3,360, but NEC slipped Y10 to Y802. Cement companies were in demand on speculation that they will benefit from government spending plans. Nihon Cement strengthened Y32 to Y790, Sumitomo Cement Y13 to Y572 and Onoda Cement Y14 to Y563. Bio-technology issues were mostly firmer, Green Cross adding Y10 at Y1,730 and Meiji Milk Y10 at Y1,180, but Nippon Zeon receded Y13 to Y723. Roundup GERMANY'S announcement that it was to reduce its interest rates lifted many of the region's markets yesterday. AUSTRALIA saw its largest one-day rise for five months. The All Ordinaries index put on 35.50 to 1,536.70 in turnover of ADollars 249.8m. News Corp fell 78 cents to ADollars 22.54 on news, after Friday's close, that it plans to raise ADollars 2.27bn in fresh capital. Foster's Brewing climbed 11 cents to ADollars 1.59 after Mr John Elliott and Mr Ken Biggins of International Brewing Holdings resigned from the board. TAIWAN, shut last Friday for a public holiday, dropped 2.7 per cent to a 20-month low after the detention of Oung Ta-ming, the textile tycoon whose Hualon group is one of the largest in the market, in connection with a stock scandal. The weighted index ended 100.46 lower at 3,685.04 in turnover of TDollars 17.44bn (TDollars 15.77bn). Hualon preferred weakened TDollars 7.50 to TDollars 102.50 and other Hualon-related stocks, Chia Hsin Live and Min Shin Cotton, also fell sharply. HONG KONG remembered its link to the US dollar again as the Hang Seng index appreciated 69.86 to 5,607.15, but turnover was down from HKDollars 1.82bn to HKDollars 1.39bn. HSBC was the most active issue, rising HKDollars 1.50 to HKDollars 55 on European buying. NEW ZEALAND closed 1.23 per cent ahead. The NZSE-40 index gained 17.92 at 1,479.09 in turnover of NZDollars 21m. The breweries sector gained ground, Lion Nathan adding 16 cents at NZDollars 4.46 and Magnum putting on 5 cents at NZDollars 1.07, but volume remained light. Air New Zealand, which announces its annual figures soon, moved forward 6 cents to NZDollars 2.70. BANGKOK remained positive following Sunday's general election. The SET index rose 16.05, or 2 per cent, to 817.95 in turnover of Bt14.73bn. It was the largest daily turnover since February 13. SINGAPORE saw late buying of blue chips, which left the Straits Times Industrial index 11.16 stronger at 1,380.40. MALAYSIA's composite index advanced 2.95 to 583.74 in turnover of MDollars 133.5m. BOMBAY strengthened in late trading and the BSE index finished 54.93 higher at 3,274.85.
