Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Gambian President Dawda Kairaba Jawara and his wife left Nouakchott today after a three-day official visit to our country. [passage omitted] Earlier, President Jawara had answered journalists' questions. He expressed his sorrow at leaving this beautiful country after a pleasant and useful visit that enabled us, the president said, to have fruitful talks and to sign agreements between our countries. The Gambian president added that the trade ministers signed a trade agreement today aimed at strengthening exchanges in that field between the two countries. The Gambian president said: As you have seen from the joint communique, we have specified other areas of cooperation in the energy, fishing, maritime, and air transportation fields. I think that all these fields will strengthen existing cooperation between our countries. Nothing remains except for me to thank my brother and friend, President Maaouiya Ould Sid'Ahmed Taya, for the warm welcome and generous and brotherly hospitality that I and my accompanying delegation received. President Jawara held a one-on-one session with President Taya this morning. After the session, the two presidents signed the final communique, which reflected their identical points of views on all issues discussed. They expressed their will to develop and strengthen the friendly relations linking the two countries. [passage omitted]
