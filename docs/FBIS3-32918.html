Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] San Jose, 14 Feb (DPA) -- Costa Rican President Rafael Angel Calderon said today that his government is still standing firmly behind Foreign Minister Bernd Niehaus' candidacy for the OAS secretary generalship. He concurrently warned that Washington may eventually exert pressure on the small countries that support Costa Rica's top diplomat. "My government will not withdraw or change Niehaus' candidacy under any circumstance," added Calderon, recalling that he had rejected a request from Mexico that he seek the position. Calderon said he has not received any information to the effect that the United States, which supports Colombian President Cesar Gaviria, is exerting pressure on small countries to force them to withdraw their support for Niehaus, a 52-year-old expert in international relations. "If pressure was exerted we would denounce it and all the countries on the continent would regret it," Calderon said. The president made these remarks in response to questions concerning reports of an alleged weakening of Niehaus' candidacy in light of the emergence of Gaviria, whose nomination is not official as yet. Meanwhile, Fernando Naranjo, a ranking spokesman for the social democratic National Liberation Party (PLN), who has been mentioned as the next foreign minister, spoke today in favor of Niehaus' nomination. Local newspapers have suggested over the past few days that support for the Costa Rican foreign minister is deteriorating. Within the framework of these rumors, several names were mentioned, including that of Oscar Arias, a former president and Nobel Peace Prize winner; Sonia Picado, a judge at the Inter-American Human Rights Court; Gonzalo Facio, the Costa Rican ambassador to Washington; and former Foreign Minister Rodrigo Madrigal as possible alternatives for Costa Rica in the event of Niehaus' withdrawal. Costa Rican President-elect Jose Maria Figueres said shortly before the 6 February elections that his government would support the current foreign minister. Meanwhile, President Calderon announced that Niehaus today initiated a tour of Caribbean nations to confirm the support offered to him by 13 nations in that region. The foreign minister claims to have 23 votes, five more than are needed to win the position in the first round, which is scheduled for 27 March. Niehaus asserts that he is the leader of a Central American candidacy and believes that it would only be fair for this area to have an OAS secretary general because thus far all of the secretaries have been from large South American countries. The principle of rotation must be observed, he recently asserted during an interview granted to DPA.
