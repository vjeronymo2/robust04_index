Language: Japanese Article Type:BFN [Text] The outline of Prime Minister Hosokawa's policy speech to the Diet was firmed up on 23 February. In the speech, Prime Minister Hosokawa will indicate that his government has entered its second term of "changes." Hosokawa will make clear his government will focus its efforts on "administrative reform" and "economic reform" -- the new major policy tasks following political reform. The prime minister also will announce his government is to put administrative reform at the top of its domestic agenda. He will express his eagerness to vigorously promote deregulation, decentralization, and the freedom of information. The government will submit a budget bill for fiscal 1994 to the Diet on 4 March for deliberation. Hosokawa is scheduled to deliver his policy speech to the Diet on 7 March. The policy speech will also stress the achievements of the coalition government over the six months since it was inaugurated last August. These include the partial opening of the Japanese rice market, the enactment of political reform bills, and a package of stimulus measures which includes a cut in income tax. Hosokawa will also unveil plans to: 1) Promote administrative reform, especially deregulation; 2) aid the recovery of the economy, open Japanese markets, and manage the economy and finance; 3) make sweeping changes of the current tax system to prepare for a society of senior citizens; and 4) make international contributions.
