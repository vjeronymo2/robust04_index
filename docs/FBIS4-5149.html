BFN [Text] Foreign Minister Han Sung-chu said that it is hard to consider the North Korean nuclear issue a South-North issue because it has developed into an issue at the level of international cooperation since the UN Security Council took issued the president's statement. While giving a briefing on the result of President Kim's recent visit to Japan and China at the Democratic Liberal Party's office today, Minister Han Sung-chu said the above and added that we should try to resolve the nuclear issue between South and North Korea through efforts between the International Atomic Energy Agency and North Korea and between the United States and North Korea. Minister Han also said we should make efforts to remove the belief at home and abroad that the Korean peninsula is facing a crisis to prevent adverse influence upon our economy, including tourism, investment, and trade. He added that U.S. Secretary of Defense William Perry's recent remark that the United States would not hesitate to bring about war has been confirmed to be an exaggeration by reporters. Regarding the Russian proposal for eight-party talks on the nuclear issue, Minister Han said that he basically welcomed [kibonjoguro hwanyonghanda] Russia's interest in the issue and that he would consult with countries concerned, watching their reactions.
