CSO [Article by Eduardo Chimely Chimely] [Text] Guadalajara, Jalisco, 30 March--Some 12,493 psychotropic pills (the largest number in the local jail's history) were confiscated by guards at the state crime prevention prison. This announcement was made today by its head, Maricela Gomez Cobos. According to the information furnished, the inmates Jaime Bugarain Barajas, Juan Gallegos Hernandez, Ricardo Gonzalez Almaraz, Felipe de Jesus Esparza Lopez, and Oscar Ortiz Gonzalez, confined in cell 77 of that jail's third dormitory, had concealed a box containing 12,493 pills of the Roche brand, each of two milligrams. They were being sold inside the prison for between 5,000 and 10,000 new pesos. The drugs were placed at the disposal of the Federal Public Prosecutor's Office, and a new trial will be opened against the culpable inmates. All of them have been remanded for theft, assault, and crimes against health, according to the aforementioned prison authority, who described this as the largest confiscation in the institution's history.
