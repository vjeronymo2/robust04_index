May 14, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

TRIBUTE TO A SLAIN ATHLETE

The 2,000 students of John Glenn High School in Norwalk filled the school's quad Thursday for an emotional memorial service for Juan Enriquez, 18, a senior football and wrestling star who was killed Tuesday in a drive-by shooting near his south Norwalk home. Students carried red, white and blue balloons and the football players wore their red jerseys.

"Only a few days ago he was playing out here with everyone who was close to him," Principal Elias Galvan said, speaking at a podium decorated with Enriquez's letterman's jacket. "He always had a smile and friends around him. He always had a sense of pride and dignity. He did things to improve the image of John Glenn High School."

Anger was expressed at gang activity.

Mayor Grace Napolitano said: "I'm speechless. It can't happen to us, but it can and it did. We have had enough, guys, enough of the violence, enough of the drugs. All of you have a responsibility to this (Enriquez) family, to yourself and to the community to cure what the heck is going on in this community. Until you wake up and start making a difference, who's going to be next?"

Ernie Gutierrez, a football player, directed his comments at any gang members who might have been in the audience: "Chaka wasn't even in a gang . . . what do you think the chances are for you?" Football Coach Willie Norman announced that Enriquez's football jersey, No. 77, would be retired. The jersey he wore last season was given to his mother, Theresa Enriquez, who sat on the stage with her family and Caprice Fimbres, who had been Enriquez's girlfriend.

Tearful students expressed their feelings for Enriquez by sharing their memories of him and by reading poems they had written.

Students also placed wreaths on the sidewalk at Horst Avenue and Algardi Street where Enriquez was gunned down. Football and wrestling jerseys with memorial messages written on them were left at the scene. Students also scribbled messages on the pavement.

Members of Enriquez's family, including his mother, also visited the student memorial.
