BFN [Report by Majdi Hilmi] [Text] The Sudanese opposition National Democratic Grouping has affirmed that the Sudanese regime's policy is to divide Sudan and to foment disturbances among its sects and people. In a memorandum sent to Arab foreign ministers, the grouping pointed out that the regime has obstructed the Constitution, dissolved parties and democratic unions, and launched ethnic war against the south. This, the grouping added, threatens Sudan's unity and invites foreign intervention. The grouping accused the regime of harboring and training terrorists in Sudan. It said that the regime is providing terrorists with weapons, funds, and passports. The grouping maintained that it has information that terrorists come from various Arab countries to training camps in Sudan. In its memorandum, the Sudanese grouping urged the Arab League to intercede and stop the ongoing war in southern Sudan. The grouping also called for removing the regime and setting up a democratic authority that would respect the right of citizenship; separate religion from politics; recognize religious, cultural, and ethnic diversity; and recognize the independence of the judiciary and the legal profession. The grouping voiced its support for the unity of Sudan and its people.
