BFN [Zafer Kiraz video report from Washington on 14 April -- recorded] [Excerpt] [Passage omitted] Ambassador Volkan Vural, chief adviser of the Prime Minister's Office, briefed the press on Prime Minister Ciller's meeting with President Clinton today. According to Vural, during the meeting, Clinton congratulated Ciller on the election results. Ciller, in turn, briefed the President in detail on the economic stability package. Pointing out that the economic program demands sacrifices of everyone in Turkey, Ciller noted, however, that the package is vital and the coalition government will implement it successfully. The prime minister stressed that it will be easier to implement the economic stability program with foreign support. Vural reported that during the meeting, Clinton described the measures adopted in Turkey as an important step. According to Vural, Clinton pledged that the United States will extend all possible assistance to Turkey in its implementation of the program. In accordance with President Suleyman Demirel's request, during the meeting, Ciller also briefed Clinton on the recent situation in Azerbaijan. Pointing out that she expects Washington to undertake an initiative to stop Armenian attacks, Ciller also expressed the view that with regard to Bosnia-Herzegovina, NATO air operations against the Serbs should be conducted whenever necessary. The Cyprus question was also discussed at the meeting. Ciller said that Turkey is in favor of reaching an agreement on the confidence-building measures [CBM's] package. Ciller added that no changes should be made to the CBM's. [passage omitted]
