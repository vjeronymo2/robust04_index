BFN [By ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondent Vladimir Solntsev] [Text] Moscow April 28 TASS -- The Russian Foreign Ministry positively appraised the appointment of Koji Kakizawa on Thursday to the post of Japanese foreign minister. A high-ranking representative of the Russian Foreign Office noted in an interview with ITAR-TASS that Kakizawa "was long known as a man who actively participated in implementing Japanese foreign policy in many spheres, including the problem of ensuring security and settlement in Cambodia. As for relations with Russia, the diplomat noted, "We proceed from a premise that they will constructively develop in the spirit of the positive tenor which is characteristic of the bilateral dialogue after the Tokyo visit by President Boris Yeltsin last October". Kakizawa who was parliamentary deputy of the Japanese foreign minister in the past, repeatedly pointed to the need for "serious efforts to establish closer and normal relations between Japan and such important neighbour as the Russian Federation", stressing that these should be "long-term relations of friendship" turned to the 21st Century.
