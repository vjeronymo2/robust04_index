BFN [Text] Russia and China ended talks in Moscow on Tuesday by signing an agreement for cooperation in the area of communications. The two sides are committed to help improve their communications networks and postal services, ensure the transit of all types of communications and postal dispatches through each other's territory and provide better legal regulation. Russia and China are planning to increase the number of telecommunication links from 30 to 90 by the end of this year and largely reduce the need for third countries in this sphere, Russian Communications Minister Vladimir Bulgak told reporters in Moscow. The number of links, telephone and other, will eventually be increased to 250 to meet the two countries' needs, he said. The two countries reaffirmed the intention to let Beijing hook up to Russia's transiberian fiber optic communications due to go into operation in 1996. For its part, China is to install communications from Harbin to Khabarovsk and Nakhodka and from Heihe to Blagoveshensk.
