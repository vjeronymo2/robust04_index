Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Chongqing, January 12 (XINHUA) -- The economic section under the Sichuan intermediate court, with the task of handling economic disputes involving overseas clients, was set up today in Chongqing, the largest economic center in southwest China. This overseas-related economic court is the first of its kind founded in Sichuan Province. Along with the expanding of reform and opening, more and more economic disputes have surfaced involving foreign-funded enterprises and Sino-foreign joint ventures as well as investors from Hong Kong, Macao and Taiwan. By the end of last year, the city had 1,300 overseas-funded businesses. Early in 1977, China's first economic court was set up in Chongqing. The Sichuan intermediate court began to prepare for the foundation of an economic court to handle disputes involving overseas clients April last year. During the preparatory period, the court took up 19 economic cases, involving 37 million yuan and 2.3 million U.S. dollars.
