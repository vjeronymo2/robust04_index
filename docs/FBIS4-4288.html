BFN [By reporters Bata Ercang (1572 1044 1422 0221), Lu Guodong (0712 0948 2767), and Chang Wanlong (1603 8001 7127)] [Text] Ulaanbaatar, 28 Apr (XINHUA) -- At the invitation of Mongolian Prime Minister P. Jasray, Premier Li Peng arrived here by a special plane at 1630 local time on 28 April to begin a two-day official visit to Mongolia. When Li Peng walked down from the ramp, Jasray stepped forward to cordially shake hands with him and then extended a warm welcome to him. A group of Mongolian children dressed in colorful national costumes presented flowers to the Chinese guests. A solemn welcoming ceremony was held at the airport. The band played the national anthems of China and Mongolia, followed by a 19-gun salute. Accompanied by Jasray, Li Peng viewed an honor guard from the Mongolian People's Army. Welcoming Li Peng and his party at the airport today were also Mongolian Deputy Prime Minister Choyjilsurengiyn Purebdorj and Foreign Minister Tserenpiliyn Gombosuren. Chinese Ambassador to Mongolia Pei Jiayi and others also went to the airport to welcome Li Peng. Those who accompanied Li Peng on the visit were: Zhu Lin, Li Peng's wife; Ismail Amat, state councillor and chairman of the State Nationalities Affairs Commission; Qi Huaiyuan, director of the Office of Foreign Affairs under the State Council; Wuliji, chairman of the Inner Mongolia Autonomous Regional People's Government; Wang Mengkui, deputy director of the State Council Research Office; Deputy Foreign Minister Tang Jiaxuan; and Liu Shanzai, deputy minister of foreign trade and economic cooperation. Li Peng issued a statement at the airport. He said: China and Mongolia are two countries linked by mountains and rivers, and there has been traditional friendship between the people of the two countries. We are happy to see the gratifying expansion of Sino- Mongolian friendly relations and cooperation in all fields in the past few years thanks to the concerted efforts by both sides. The maintenance and development of a friendly and good-neighbor relationship between China and Mongolia conforms with the common wishes and fundamental interests of the two peoples, and it will benefit peace, stability, and development in the Asia-Pacific region as well. Today in Ulaanbaatar, the grassland capital, the spring breeze was warm and the sun was bright. (Buyintuho) International Airport was decorated to welcome the guests. The national flags of China and Mongolia were fluttering in the square in front of the place where the plane was to park. In front of the lounge there was hung a huge banner on which was written in Chinese and Mongolian, "A warm welcome to the respected Premier of the State Council of the People's Republic of China Li Peng and Madam Zhu Lin." This is the first visit by a Chinese premier in 34 years since Premier Zhou Enlai visited Mongolia in 1960.
