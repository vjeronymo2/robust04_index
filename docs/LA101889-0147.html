October 18, 1989, Wednesday, Home Edition

METRO DIGEST LOCAL NEWS IN BRIEF: FATHER, SON TO BE TRIED IN RECORD COCAINE BUST

A father and son team who allegedly oversaw the Mexican leg of a major Colombian smuggling ring were ordered Tuesday to stand trial in Los Angeles in connection with a record cocaine seizure at a Sylmar warehouse.

After a preliminary hearing, federal Magistrate Lawrence Leavitt found probable cause to order Carlos Tapia Ponce, 68, and his son, Hector Tapia Anchondo, 38, held for trial in U.S. District Court in Los Angeles.

The two Mexican nationals were arrested Sept. 29 at the Flamingo Hilton Hotel in Las Vegas, one day after Drug Enforcement Administration agents raided the Sylmar warehouse and seized 21.4 tons of cocaine, the largest such seizure in history.

Four men are being held in Los Angeles in the case.

No date was set Tuesday for Tapia Ponce and Tapia Anchondo to appear on the charges.

The government's sole witness at the preliminary hearing was DEA agent William Sherman, who testified that Tapia Ponce and Tapia Anchondo were identified as two key players in the massive drug smuggling operation.

The identification was made by another man who was arrested in the case, James McTague, 41, who is married to Tapia Anchondo's sister. McTague, who was arrested along with three others on Sept. 28, has been identified as the manager of the Sylmar warehouse, where authorities found the cache of cocaine and $12.2 million in cash.
