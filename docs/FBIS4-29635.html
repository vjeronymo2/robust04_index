BFN [Text] Pyongyang, June 18 (KCNA) -- Former U.S. President Jimmy Carter called a press conference at the Thongil House [as received] in the north side portion of Panmunjom today before leaving the DPRK. He arrived in Pyongyang on June 15. He told local and foreign reporters that his trip to the Democratic People's Republic of Korea was positive. He said, "All my discussions with President Kim Il-song were very open, frank and without restraint and I think the result is positive." He added that direct talks between the DPRK and the United States can be resumed. He said North Korea's graphite moderated reactor system will be replaced by a light water reactor system. "Overall questions about nuclear weapons can be resolved," he said. He said he thinks "sanctions" are definitely a serious mistake. "If the things continue to go well there is no need to consider sanctions any more." "I have never thought sanctions are effective or necessary," he said. "Hope now my government will agree that the crisis is over and the sanctions are no more necessary for consideration." [sentence as received] Giving his impressions of Korea, he said the most exciting impression was made when he witnessed the performance of children in Pyongyang yesterday. "This was the most remarkable performance I have seen in my life," he added. "Hope the whole world will see the children of North Korea as we appreciated yesterday," he said. [sentence as received] He said he will strive with sincerity for harmony between the two countries.
