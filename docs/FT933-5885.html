930901 FT 01 SEP 93 / Arts: Today's Television Today's BBC Prom is an all-Mozart affair. The chamber orchestra The English Concert, conducted by Trevor Pinnock, plays two symphonies, the Paris (K297) and the Prague (K504) and, in between, three sections from Mozart's Vespers (K339): Dixit Dominus, Laudate Dominum, and Magnificat (7.30 Radio 3). With an eye to the opening next week of the world championship chess match between Gary Kasparov and Britain's Nigel Short, BBC2's On The Line investigates the notoriously odd relationship between chess and politics in Russia (8.30). For the handful of people who were not rooted in front of the set when they were first transmitted, C4 is repeating five of the Inspector Morse dramas, starting with a story about the murder of a retired policeman (8.30). BBC2 begins a three part drama about politics, centred upon Lou Larson, a female Labour MP who is heavily into battered women and women's centres, but has a 'passionate past' which, it is feared, may be discovered by the press. Phyllis Logan plays Lou (9.25).
