930402 FT 02 APR 93 / People: Jackson, new controller for BBC2 Michael Jackson, who became the youngest ever BBC head of department in May 1991, has now been appointed the second youngest controller of BBC2. Jackson is 35 - the first controller, Michael Peacock, was 34 - and takes over from Alan Yentob, who became controller of BBC1 last month. A graduate in media studies from the Polytechnic of Central London, now the University of Westminster, Jackson was a successful independent producer before joining the BBC in 1987. He had launched the Media Show on Channel 4 and earlier was organiser of the Channel 4 group which lobbied successfully for independent access to the channel before its launch in 1982. Head of music and arts at the BBC, he was the founding editor of The Late Show on BBC2 and was involved in programmes ranging from Moving Pictures, Takes from Prague and the Rembrandt season to themed events such as TV Hell, Granadaland, A Night in with Alan Bennett and last month's live broadcast of Verdi's opera Stiffelio.
