940205 FT 05 FEB 94 / Financial Times Round the World Ski Expedition: Win a skiing holiday in Courcheval or Verbier - Competition In the second of our 'Round the World' competitions, simply answer three questions below, and estimate how many miles Arnie Wilson will ski during February. The winner will receive a four-day skiing holiday for two in Courcheval or Verbier provided by FlexiSki, and two runners up will each receive a case (6 bottles) of Singleton malt whisky. QUESTIONS 1. What do double black diamonds on a US ski map mean? a. Glacier b. Most Difficult Run c. Slalom Finish 2. Today we use wax - but what was commonly used on skis in Finland 1,500 years ago to make them go faster? a. Tree Bark b. Animal Hides c. Leaves 3. Which country is hosting this year's Winter Olympics? a. France b. Sweden c. Japan d. Norway And how many miles will Arnie ski this month? THE PRIZES FlexiSki (tel: 071-352 0044) specialise in flexible breaks in Courcheval and Verbier. Their prize features scheduled flights from Heathrow, private transfers to a chalet, ski guiding and meals. The Singleton is an award-winning Speyside malt whisky, made mellow by the spring water from Dorie's Well and part-maturation in sherry casks. HOW TO ENTER Send your answers, plus your estimate of Arnie's mileage, with your name and address on a postcard to: Round the World Competition, Marketing Department, Financial Times, Number One Southwark Bridge, London SE1 9HL. JANUARY COMPETITION: ANSWERS AND WINNERS Answers: President Ford skied at Vail; Pistes are called Trails in the US; the Four Hills is the famous ski jump competition. Winner: Mrs E Payne of Petts Wood, Kent wins a week's holiday for 2 in Zermatt with Ski Scott Dunn. Runners up: David Blodgett of Voorheesville, New York, and Freddie Avis of London W2, each receive a case of magnums of Mercier champagne. TERMS AND CONDITIONS 1.
