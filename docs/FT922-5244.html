920601 FT 01 JUN 92 / Arts: Today's Television The cleverest man I have known, and one of the wisest, was Sir Peter Medawar. On the subject of human population he told me there was only one certainty: people would worry, either because there were too many births or, as in France after the Fist World War, too few. His wife, however, had no doubts about the perils of population explosion: she ran the International Planned Parenthood Federation. It was bumping into my headmaster, the father of umpteen children, at one of her IPPF functions (inviting him had been my idea of a joke) which led eventually to my expulsion from school . . . but I digress. Horizon (8.10 BBC2) considers global population growth and asks the crucial questions: when will the population reach the carrying capacity of the planet and what happens then? Few current affairs programmes have achieved as much pre-transmission ballyhoo as today's World In Action (8.30 ITV). It had better be good. The first of two parts, it investigates the members of the International Olympic Committee and especially the president, Juan Samaranch. Panorama ponders the forthcoming Earth Summit in Rio (9.30 BBC1).
