Language: Portuguese Article Type:BFN [Text] Sao Jose dos Campos -- The Brazilian Aeronautics Company (Embraer) has sold the first two units of the 50-seat regional transport EMB-145, which is based on the [EMB-120] Brasilia plane. The Australian airline company Flight West, which intended to purchase four planes, now has decided to purchase two planes and place two firm options to purchase two more planes. The contract's cost has been estimated at $60 million. The contract was signed by Flight West Chairman Dennis Buchanan. Flight West has been operating both [EMB-110] Bandeirante and Brasilia planes for many years. The EMB-145 is Embraer's most daring project and is still in its development stage. The EMB-145's initial flight has been scheduled for April 1995. Budgeted at $300 million, this project has a potential $1 billion market in the next 15 years. Each plane is sold for $13 million. Fourteen customers from eight countries have confirmed 136 purchase orders.
