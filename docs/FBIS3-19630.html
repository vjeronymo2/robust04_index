Language: Turkish Article Type:BFN [Announcer-read report over video] [Text] Polish National Defense Minister Piotr Kolodziejczyk, who is currently on an official visit in Turkey, conducted contacts in Ankara today. President Suleyman Demirel received the Polish minister at the Cankaya Mansion. In a statement during the meeting, Demirel said that the friendly relations between the two countries date back 579 years and the first friendship agreement was signed on 23 July 1923. Pointing out that businessmen from the two countries can cooperate in Turkey as well as in third countries, Demirel explained that Poland is successfully managing the process of democratic transformation and transition to a free market economy within the framework of the reforms it is implementing. Turkey is according the necessary support to Poland on the international and bilateral levels, Demirel remarked, adding that the Partnership for Peace project proposed by NATO constitutes an important move. In his statement, Kolodziejczyk conveyed to Demirel a friendship message from Polish President Walesa and expressed gratitude for Turkey's support and contribution to the Partnership for Peace project with regard to membership in NATO. The Polish defense minister noted that his country wishes to benefit from Turkey's recent achievements, especially from its experience in the defense industry field.
