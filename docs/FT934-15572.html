931011 FT 11 OCT 93 / International Company News: Amdahl forecasts deeper loss AMDAHL, the struggling US mainframe computer manufacturer, said it expected to report an operating loss for its third quarter 'considerably larger' than its Dollars 23.7m second-quarter loss, writes Louise Kehoe in San Francisco. The company blamed continuing 'depressed conditions' in the mainframe computer market, together with 'intense competitive pressure on pricing' for its losses. Industry analysts said that International Business Machines, Amdahl's chief competitor in the mainframe market, has been deeply discounting prices of its mainframe computers in a bid to boost sluggish demand. Amdahl's anticipated losses have raised raised concerns about IBM's performance.
