BFN [Text] Baku (April 11) TURAN -- "Nobody can force us to agree on the locating of foreign military bases on the territory of the republic if it [the placement of bases] is not in the interest of Azerbaijan", said Azerbaijan's Defense Minister, Mamedrafi Mamedov. Mamedov opposes the placement of Russian border troops on the Azerbaijani-Iranian and Azerbaijani-Turkish frontier. "Azerbaijan can protect its own southern borders without external help", said the Defense Minister. Earlier, Russian Defense Minister, Pavel Grachev, said that Russia intends to place one military base on Azeri territory Azerbaijan and representatives of Russian military commandants have repeatedly stated that they are interested in deploying Russian border troops in the southern region of Azerbaijan. Sources in the Russian Ministry of Foreign Affairs told Turan that Moscow is dissatisfied with Azerbaijan's refusal. A Russian diplomat, speaking on condition of anonymity, told a Turan correspondent that "if Baku refuses, relations between Russia and Azerbaijan will worsen. This will cause serious economic difficulties in Azerbaijan that will lead to further dissatisfaction among the population".
