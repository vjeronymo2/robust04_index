BFN [Text] Buenos Aires, April 20 (XINHUA) -- Senior official of the Chinese Communist Party (CPC) Hu Jintao held talks here today with high-ranking officials of Argentina's ruling Justicialista Party on ways of promoting bilateral relations. In the talks the two sides briefed each other of the performance of the two parties and the situation of the two countries. Both sides held that contacts between the two parties over the past many years have played a positive role in promoting friendly relations between the two countries, hoping that bilateral ties will be further strengthened in the future. Hu, Standing Committee member of the Politburo of the CPC Central Committee, said China and Argentina, though far apart and having different social systems, have similar experiences in history and common tasks at present, which will help bring them into reliable partnership. The head of the Chinese party delegation expressed his confidence that there are broad prospects for closer cooperation between the two parties. Hu conveyed greetings from Chinese leader Jiang Zemin to Argentine president and leader of the Justicialista Party Carlos Menem. Meanwhile, the senior officials of the Justicialista Party welcomed the Chinese delegation and said Hu's visit will further strengthen the cooperation between the two parties. On the same day, President of the Argentine Chamber of Deputies Alberto Pierri and former Argentine President Raul Alfonsin met with the Chinese delegation on separate occasions. Hu arrived here this morning from Uruguay to begin the second leg of his three-nation Latin American tour, which will take him to Brazil next.
