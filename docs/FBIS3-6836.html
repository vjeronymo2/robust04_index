Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, March 22 KYODO -- The Japanese Government decided Tuesday [22 March] to donate 750,000 dollars of emergency aid to the United Nations Children's Fund (UNICEF) to assist millions of Angolans caught in the crossfire of internal conflicts, government officials said. The Japanese aid is targeted at improving social infrastructure in medical care and sanitation in the civil war-torn nation, they said. UNICEF has called for nations around the world to assist their relief program for sufferers of the Angolan civil war, the officials said.
