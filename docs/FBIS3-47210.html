Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, Jan. 11 KYODO -- Japan will cosponsor an eminent persons' meeting on the subjects of population and development on January 26 and 27 in Tokyo, Foreign Ministry spokesman Terusuke Terada said Tuesday [11 January]. The meeting will formulate its recommendations to a UN-sponsored conference in Cairo next September. Some 20 high-level scholars, academics and political leaders who are well-versed in the problems of population will attend the meeting at the UN University in Tokyo, Terada said. Participants will include such prominent figures as Norwegian Prime Minister Gro Harlem Brundtland, former Mexican President Miguel de la Madrid, former World Bank President Robert McNamara and Taro Nakayama, former Japanese foreign minister. The meeting is to be held under the cosponsorship of the U.N. Population Fund (UNFPA), the UN University and the Foreign Ministry. Japanese Prime Minister Morihiro Hosokawa will address the opening ceremony. The participants are expected to adopt a Tokyo declaration on the closing day. The deliberations at the meeting will be recommended to the U.N.-sponsored International Conference on Population and Development, which is to be held in Cairo in September to discuss measures for problems related to the rapidly increasing world population. Terada said Japan is by far the largest donor country for UNFPA, donating 59 million dollars in 1992 and as much as 63 million dollars in 1993.
