931210 FT 10 DEC 93 / Car chief sees small sales rise Ford, the leader of the UK new-car market, yesterday forecast only modest growth in demand next year with registrations expected to rise by about 4.5 per cent to 1.85m. Ford does not expect the UK market to recover the ground lost during the recession for at least five years. Mr Ian McAllister, chairman of Ford of Britain, forecast an increase in new-car sales to 2.1m in 1998 from 1.78m this year. UK new-car sales peaked at 2.3m in 1989, then plunged to only 1.59m in both 1991 and 1992. Registrations have risen by about 12 per cent this year. Car sales to fleets (operators of 25 cars and above) remained relatively stable during the recession at about 630,000 a year, said Mr McAllister. The recovery depended on retail and small-business customers coming back into the market.
