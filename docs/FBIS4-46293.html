BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Sergey Ostanin] [Text] Moscow, 28 May -- An attempt was made on the life of Chechen President Dzhokhar Dudayev on 27 May. An ITAR-TASS correspondent learned this from Chechnya's press and information ministry today. The attempt occurred at 1900 hours along the road to Groznyy when the motorcade in which the president was travelling passed a Lada car parked on the side of the road. The blast from the powerful car bomb killed Interior Minister Magomet Eldiyev, his deputy Saidali Batayev, and the driver of their car, who were accompanying the president. The car in which the Chechen president was riding was thrown 10 meters by the explosion, but the president was not hurt. ITAR-TASS learned from Shirlai Pashayev, Chechnya's deputy minister for information and the press, that components of a remote control triggering device were found at the scene of the attempt. According to Pashayev, similar devices have not been used before on the territory of the Chechen Republic. An investigation is under way. A decision was adopted at an emergency sitting of the Chechen cabinet of ministers today to declare Sunday, 29 May, a day of mourning.
