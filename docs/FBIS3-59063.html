Language: French Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Francois Mitterrand, who visited Nantes today, said he is concerned about unemployment and the decrease in social security benefits. [passage omitted] In a speech at the Town Hall, President Mitterrand called for national unity and solidarity to protect the weakest citizens against the strong, and he gave a warning to the international community about Bosnia: [Begin Mitterrand recording] We need an international law to settle all this. (?We will see) Sarajevos everywhere if the law of might is the international rule, if the United Nations is not able to make its voice heard, if the civilized nations are not in a condition to establish peace and reconciliation. What an immense retreat that will be after we had all shouted together: Long live freedom! Those who waited for it for half a century, three quarters of a century, who suffered in concentration camps, those who saw their nearest ones, their parents, their brothers, their children, die in pain and under torture, will turn away from freedom, and will stop believing in it. [end recording]
