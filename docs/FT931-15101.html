930114 FT 14 JAN 93 / International Company News in Brief: Credit Lyonnais buys Chase unit CREDIT Lyonnais yesterday said it had reached an agreement in principle to acquire Chase Manhattan Bank Austria, the Austrian unit of Chase Manhattan Bank, AP-DJ reports from Paris. Terms were not disclosed. The French state-owned bank said that the acquisition, which must be approved by Austrian banking authorities, would strengthen its presence in Austria. Until now, Credit Lyonnais has had a representative office in the country. Credit Lyonnais said Chase wwould retain a representative office in Vienna. Chase Austria is capitalised at Sch20m (Dollars 1.75m) and had assets almost Sch2.5bn at the end of 1991. The Chase unit specialises in lending and services to corporations and selected private customers. The French bank said the acquisitions fits in with its recent acquisition of BfG Bank of Germany. ***** SEARS, Roebuck, the large US retail and financial services group, is selling its business centres division to InaCom, a Nebraska-based company which markets computer systems and services, for an undisclosed sum, writes Nikki Tait in New York. ***** COLD Storage, a Singapore-listed subsidiary of Australian food group Goodman Fielder, is to buy a 70 per cent stake in Standard Confectionery, a Malaysian fresh and frozen cakemaker, for about MDollars 15m (USDollars 5.8m), Reuter reports from Singapore. The investment was 'part of Goodman Fielder's strategy to transform Cold Storage into a leading regional consumer-food manufacturing and distributing company,' CSH said.
