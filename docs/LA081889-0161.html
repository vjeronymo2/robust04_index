August 18, 1989, Friday, P.M. Final

THE SIDELINES;

SOCCER CLUB SOLD AT RECORD PRICE

Manchester United, one of the world's most famous soccer clubs, was sold today in a surprise deal worth more than $31 million, a record for a British club.

A consortium led by Isle of Man-based businessman Michael Knighton paid United Chairman Martin Edwards $15.8 million for his controlling interest in the English First Division club.

The buyers said up to $15.6 million would be spent on redeveloping the club's famous Old Trafford ground.

Edwards, chairman for 9 1/2 years, will stay on as vice chairman and chief executive.
