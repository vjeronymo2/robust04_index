BFN [Text] Heavy fighting continued between Khun Sa's army and Burmese troops in Shan State close to the Thai border. According to a recent report, Burma sent 10 battalions more of reinforcements in an attempt to retake Mong Chot. A reliable source reported to MATICHON on 23 May that, during the night of 22 May, 10 battalions of the 23d brigade, with its stronghold west of the Salween River and about eight kilometers from Mong Chot, mounted an offensive against Khun Sa's soldiers and managed to take back the military stronghold at Mong Chot at dawn on 23 May. There was no report on the casualties of both sides, but Khun Sa's men reportedly pulled back from Mong Chot toward Doi Na Mu and Doi Nam Cham. As for the situation in Chiang Mai, MATICHON's correspondent reported intense fighting at the border in Wiang Haeng, Mae Ai, and Fang Districts in Chiang Mai. There was also fierce fighting opposite Muang District of Mae Hong Son Province, about 10 kilometers from the Thai-Burmese border, with the two sides pounding artillery fire on each other continuously. The source reported that Khun Sa's troops advanced toward Thachilek-Napong and, in the past two days, launched three attacks on Burmese Government troops, killing and wounding many. They apparently wanted to capture Thachilek. The Burmese border pass at Thachilek opposite Mae Sai in Chiang Rai was closed, as the Burmese military was recruiting men to be porters and soldiers. It also wanted to black out news about the situation in Burma to keep it from leaking to the Thai side. Meanwhile, Mr. Kansai Chaiyen, a leading member of the Shan State Restoration Council, said the Burmese Government proposed a cease-fire negotiation after three weeks of fighting. Burma claimed that several factions of Khun Sa's army had already agreed to a cease-fire negotiation. However, the Shan State Restoration Council made it clear that there would be a negotiation only when Burmese troops had pulled out from Shan State, and that the negotiation be conducted in a country other than Burma.
