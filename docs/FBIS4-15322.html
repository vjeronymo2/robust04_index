BFN [Text] Tbilisi, April 7 (SNARK)--No less than two months will be necessary to organize the return of refugees to Abkhazia, told today in his interview to SNARK the chairman of Georgian Human Rights and International Relations Committee Aleksandr Kavsadze. He told that tomorrow, April 8, the four-lateral committee will sit to discuss the specific aspects of expected peoples' return to their homes. Kavkadze observed that the process will have a step-by-step character. Meanwhile, told the committee chairman, the principles of peacekeepers' dislocation in the Georgian-Abkhazian conflict zone will be considered on April 12 in Geneva. Georgian side suggests the forces to be disposed on the whole territory of Abkhazia, while the Abkhazian side insists on their presence only along the Inguri River, known to separate the fighters, said Kavsadze.
