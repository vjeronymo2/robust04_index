BFN [Text] Tokyo, April 4 KYODO -- Prime Minister Morihiro Hosokawa and South Korean Foreign Minister Han Sung-chu agreed Monday [4 April] that China must play a larger role in encouraging North Korea to remove suspicions about its nuclear program. Hosokawa told Han that China's role will be crucial in tackling growing threats arising from Pyongyang's alleged nuclear weapons development program, a Foreign Ministry official said. The premier also spoke of the need to seek cooperation from Beijing in persuading Pyongyang to allay international concern that it is developing nuclear weapons capability at its facilities in Yongbyon, 90 kilometers north of the capital, the official said. China is North Korea's last major ally. During the 35-minute meeting, Han remarked that China appears ready to play a more active role than before, the official said. Han told Hosokawa that in addition to close contact among Japan, the United States and South Korea, the roles of China, Russia and other members in the UN Security Council will be important in helping resolve the issue. On Sunday, Han agreed with Japanese Foreign Minister Tsutomu Hata that China has a vital role in resolving the North Korean nuclear issue. They also confirmed their intention to monitor how Pyongyang responds to the UN Security Council's call for it to accept full inspections of its suspected nuclear sites. The Security Council on Thursday unanimously adopted a statement urging North Korea to allow the International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA) to complete checks of its nuclear facilities. The IAEA had reported that it was not allowed to carry out certain measures at a North Korean radiochemical laboratory. After emerging from the session with Hosokawa, Han declined to reveal exactly what he discussed with the premier on North Korea's suspected nuclear program. "I cannot make a simple statement now," he said. Han will return home later Monday at the end of a three-country trip to discuss the North Korean nuclear issue. Before flying to Tokyo, he visited China and the U.S. to seek cooperation on the issue.
