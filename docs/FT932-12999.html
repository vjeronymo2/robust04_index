930426 FT 26 APR 93 / Business leaders welcome package BUSINESS leaders not easily impressed by political rhetoric said they were encouraged by Mr Heseltine's message, although significant doubts remain over his ability to deliver. Shortly before the trade secretary made his presentation to the FT, four business organisations were invited to DTI headquarters in London to hear the same message. Mr Neil Johnson, director-general of the Engineering Employers' Federation, said he hoped the unique overture offered 'a clear punctuation mark' in Mr Heseltine's tenure at the DTI - the early stages of which had been hijacked by the coal crisis. Mr Johnson said: 'There is no better industry champion in cabinet than Michael Heseltine. He is showing signs of warming to the task and we should encourage him. He must be demanding of his officials and they must be demanding of us.' A more cautious note was introduced by Mr Peter Morgan, director-general of the Institute of Directors, who welcomed Mr Heseltine's efforts to create a 'department for the market' but stressed that some of the preconditions he had spelled out for long-term economic strength were well outside the minister's remit. 'What he is trying to do is useful but, although he sees the total picture, he can only influence part of it. We will have to see how successful he is in getting other departments to join in.' Mr Christopher Stewart-Smith, president of the British Chambers of Commerce commented: 'We greatly welcome the constructive approach. Business definitely feels that an important change in attitudes is under way. 'We have been understandably sceptical, given the history of the DTI, but there is a real sense of partnership beginning to develop. As the economy recovers, it is very encouraging to see a change in attitudes within government, which will also help improve confidence.' Mr Howard Davies, director-general of the Confederation of British Industry, said Mr Heseltine and the DTI were 'clearly moving in the right direction, albeit rather slowly. The DTI has been more active in advancing industry's interests in Whitehall, which is good.'
