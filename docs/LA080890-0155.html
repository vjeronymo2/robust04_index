August 8, 1990, Wednesday, P.M. Final

OIL FLOW FROM IRAQ HALTS

q's oil exports came to a complete halt today as a world embargo took hold after its invasion of Kuwait.

Oil industry sources said no Iraqi oil was leaving Turkey or Saudi Arabia, which carry nearly 90% of Baghdad's exports by pipeline, nor was any oil leaving Iraq by sea.

Oil is Iraq's main source of foreign exchange, and a prolonged embargo could destroy its economy. It was exporting 2.7 million barrels per day -- 5% of world output.
