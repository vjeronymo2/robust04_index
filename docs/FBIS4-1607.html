BFN [Text] Lagos, March 14 (XINHUA) -- China and Nigeria are working on proposals for holding the third session of the China-Nigeria Economic and Trade Joint Commission in Beijing, China, a senior official from the Chinese Embassy in Nigeria said today. Huang Zhaohong, the Chinese economic and commercial counselor, said that they have proposed the third quarter of this year as the time for the session. They are now waiting for a decision by the Nigerian Foreign Ministry, he said. The proposed session is expected to discuss ways to promote bilateral trade and economic ties, Huang said. The second session of the joint commission was held in Lagos in 1985. China and Nigeria have a lot in common as both are developing countries, he said, adding that "we need each other not only for economic reasons."
