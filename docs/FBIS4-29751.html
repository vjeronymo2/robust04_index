BFN [Text] Tokyo, June 21 KYODO -- The chairman of the second largest opposition Social Democratic Party (SDP) [of Japan, SDJP] said Tuesday his party will not submit a no-confidence motion against Prime Minister Tsutomu Hata's minority government pending talks on the SDP's possible return to the ruling coalition. Tomiichi Murayama said he will seek a meeting with Hata as soon as Wednesday to discuss the realliance issue. Murayama, who has demanded voluntary resignation by the Hata government, also said he will try to settle realliance and resignation talks simultaneously. The SDP left the coalition in April and has threatened to join the largest opposition Liberal Democratic Party (LDP) in seeking a no-confidence vote if Hata refuses to step down together with his ministers.
