Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tallinn, Jan 07, BNS -- The main Russian combat unit still stationed in Estonia, the 144th armored infantry division, keeps on taking its equipment out of Estonia, officials at the Estonian Defense Ministry say. According to information obtained by BNS, the division headquarters applied to Estonian authorities for 80 different transport permits in December. The division took a total of 96 vehicles and 1,800 tons of property out of the country on 16 railway trains last month. The division hired 8 trains to take 57 vehicles and 2,800 tons of property out from Estonia in November. As much as 254 vehicles and 5,300 tons of army property were taken out of Estonia by the same unit on 42 trains in July. The division, stationed in bases in Tallinn, as well as at Keila and Klooga to the west of the capital, was still about 1,000-strong in December. The division's tank regiment, stationed in Keila, until now comes for shooting exercise to Manniku on the outskirts of Tallinn. The division's ammunition depots at Klooga and Manniku contained about 3,500 tons of ammunition at the end of 1993.
