Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Pakistan has proposed to India that future bilateral talks should aim at finalizing the modalities for holding a plebiscite to ascertain the wishes of the people of Jammu and Kashmir. The proposal was conveyed by the foreign secretary, Shaharyar M. Khan, to the Indian high commissioner, Mr. S.K. Lambha, in Islamabad today. Pakistan has also asked the Indian Government to take visible steps in the Indian-held Kashmir so that a propitious climate is created for the peaceful resolution of the Jammu and Kashmir dispute. Pakistan has asked the Indian Government for an early response to the proposals, which are aimed at focussing on essential issues relating to Kashmir and to giving a meaningful and purposeful direction to the talks. Pakistan's high commissioner to India will hand over the proposal to the Indian foreign secretary, Mr. J.N. Dixit, later today.
