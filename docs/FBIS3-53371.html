Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Lieutenant General 'Umar al-Bashir, president of the Republic, acquainted himself in his office at the general command today with the results of the unofficial meetings between a government delegation and a committee of ministers from the Intergovernmental Authority on Drought and Development, IGADD, which were recently held in the Kenyan capital, Nairobi, under the chairmanship of the Kenyan foreign minister and in which the Sudanese Government took part with a delegation led by Mr. 'Abdallah Deng Niyal, minister of peace and rehabilitation. In press statements following the meeting Mr. Niyal confirmed the government's readiness to negotiate with the rebel movement. He added that he conveyed to the meeting of the IGADD ministers the government's commitment to what was mentioned in the recommendations and resolutions of the national dialogue conference for peace as a basis for negotiation. He pointed out that the government's stand on a cease-fire is part and parcel of the comprehensive agreement. His excellency restated the government's readiness to sit at a negotiations table with the rebel movement at any time or place specified by the committee of ministers of the IGADD states. He proclaimed the necessity of stopping the fighting, explaining that it affects the progress of negotiations between the two sides.
