May 6, 1990, Sunday, San Diego County Edition

A PARK IN THEORY

I have been observing the progress of the San Dieguito River Valley Regional Park with great interest and frustration.

This 43-mile, sea-to-mountain park from Del Mar to Julian is exciting and vital to preserve some of the stunning natural landscape of lagoon, wetland, valley, mountain diversity, which is rapidly disappearing.

I hear about the park repeatedly in the press and from political persons as if it were a reality. However, the park exists only as a proposal.

No land has, as yet, been acquired. Until land is in public ownership, it is not protected.

The bureaucratic structure for the park is in place. There's the joint powers authority with representation from the six communities involved; there is a Citizens Advisory Committee. There is even $10 million available for land acquisition, but why hasn't it been spent?

Meanwhile land costs escalate, wetlands disappear -- 90% of the wetlands in California are already gone.

The longer the delay, the higher the cost of acquisition and less will be purchased with funds available.

Will this be a paper park or the real thing -- and when?

SYLVIA TROY

Del Mar
