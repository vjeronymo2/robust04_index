February 7, 1989, Tuesday, Home Edition

GRIPES ABOUT AIRLINES RISE 50% IN JANUARY

Consumer complaints against major airlines rose 50% in January and airlines reported increases in late flights and mishandled baggage at the end of last year, but on-time performance was still well ahead of a year earlier, a government report said Monday.

The Transportation Department, in its monthly consumer report on air travel, said consumer complaints against the industry reported to the department went from 724 in December -- the lowest level in four years -- to 1,086 in January. But the new total was still a 70% improvement over the 3,674 complaints received in January, 1988.

Complaints covered a wide variety of problems, including flight cancellations, ticketing mistakes, difficulty in getting refunds, lost baggage, rude employees, problems with smoking restrictions and claims of misleading advertising.

More Mishandled Baggage

The nation's 13 largest domestic carriers reported that 76.1% of their flights were on time in December, compared to 77.7% a month earlier. The rate was still nearly 10 points better than December, 1987, when one-third of all flights were late.

A department news release blamed the busy Christmas-New Year holiday season for an increase in mishandled baggage reports filed against the carriers in December. The reports averaged 8.58 for each 1,000 passengers, compared to 6.17 in November.

The airlines covered in the monthly consumer report account for about 90% of domestic flight revenue. Carriers are required to report on operations in and out of the nation's 27 largest airports.

For the third month in a row, Eastern Airlines reported the best on-time record, with 88.1% of its flights arriving within 15 minutes of posted time. Earlier this year, Eastern was consistently among airlines with the most delayed flights. The figures do not count fights delayed because of mechanical problems.

Eastern was followed by Southwest, with 79.6% on time, and Delta, with 79.3%.

Piedmont had the worst record for mishandled baggage, 12.27 for each 1,000 passengers, and Southwest had the best, 4.59 for each 1,000.

Pan American had the most ticketed passengers denied seats on overbooked flights, with 10.54 for each 10,000 boardings for the first nine months of 1988.
