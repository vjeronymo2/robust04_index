BFN [Text] Beijing, 28 Apr (XINHUA) -- A spokesman for the Ministry of Foreign Affairs expressed the belief that following the establishment of Japan's new government, China and Japan will continue to maintain their close coordination and cooperation and will work together to promote the long-term and steady development of the two countries' relations. The spokesman made the statement while answering a reporter's question at a news conference held this afternoon. The reporter asked: Prime Minister Tsutomu Hata is now forming his cabinet, what is your comment? What do you expect from the new Japanese Government? The spokesman said: Premier Li Peng has sent a congratulatory message to Prime Minister Tsutomu Hata. Mr. Hata is a statesman with whom the Chinese people are familiar. "We appreciate the zeal and foresight he has demonstrated in strengthening Sino-Japanese friendship and cooperation, as well as the great deal of work he has accomplished for this purpose. Ruling and nonruling parties in Japan share a common understanding toward the development of reciprocal and cooperative relations between China and Japan in all fields. We believe that, following the establishment of the new Japanese Government, China and Japan will continue to maintain their close coordination and cooperation and will work together in promoting the long-term and steady development of the two countries' relations."
