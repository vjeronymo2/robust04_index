Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Hungarian President Arpad Goncz is in Madrid as the first Hungarian head of state in our country. Following an official reception at the Pardo Palace, Goncz's official residence during the visit, the Hungarian president and his wife are having lunch with the King and Queen of Spain at the Zarzuela Palace. Pedro Fernandez Cespedes reports: [Begin recording] [Cespedes] President Arpad Goncz's visit fits in with the Spanish government policy of paying special attention to central and eastern Europe and coincides with a good period in Spanish-Hungarian relations. These [relations] are without problems; trade has increased three-fold over the last two years. This is a fact underscored by President Goncz recently at [Madrid's] Barajas airport where he said that his visit had a strong economic component. [Goncz in Hungarian with superimposed Spanish translation] Our political relations are satisfactory and successful but I have brought with me a very big delegation--virtually an entire army--of businessmen. I hope that thanks to them the economic component of this visit will be enhanced. [Cespedes] The trade balance currently favors Spain by five billion pesetas, that is a surplus of 153 percent. Spanish exports went from 4.8 billion pesetas in 1991 to 14.108 billion in 1993. Spanish investment is planned in Hungarian wine companies. After the sale of 76 trains, by the Kaf company, there are possibilities that the Talgo [Spanish high speed train] will link Kiev, Budapest and Vienna. The situation in Bosnia will be an important topic during the political talks that Arpad Goncz and [Spanish Prime Minister] Felipe Gonzalez will hold this afternoon. The Hungarian president has said that he hopes that the conflict will be solved without having to carry out the heralded air strikes. [end recording]
