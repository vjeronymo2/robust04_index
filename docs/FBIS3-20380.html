Language: Turkish Article Type:BFN [Announcer-read report over video] [Text] Nationalist Action Party [NAP] leader Alparslan Turkes has said that the NAP emerged from the local elections with great success because of its consistent and stable policies focused on unity and the integrity of the country. At a news conference he held in parliament, Turkes said that even though certain circles expected the NAP to weaken because some deputies had left it, the party increased its votes in municipal councils by 250 percent and the number of its mayors by 300 percent, to 155. Turkes said that the citizens in eastern and southeastern Turkey showed in the elections that they do not want to secede. He added: The election results are a good lesson to those who try to interfere in Turkey's domestic affairs. Turkes pointed out that the NAP received good results in eastern and southeastern Anatolia. He said: The elections were held in a mature way, with a high rate of participation. They resulted in a victory for democracy. The NAP leader stated that the citizens voted in favor of maintaining the coalition, adding: The election results show that the people do not want chaos, or the state business to be delayed through frequent elections. They show that the people want the present coalition to remain power. That is my conclusion.
