921119 FT 19 NOV 92 / Housing market continues to tumble THE PLANNED pit closures, the demise of Dan-Air and the sterling crisis in September continued to unnerve the housing market, a survey of 150 estate agents indicates today. The survey by the Royal Institution of Chartered Surveyors found that house prices fell again in spite of a reduction in interest rates of almost a third in the past two months. Three quarters of the agents surveyed in England and Wales reported a further fall in prices during the three months to the end of October. The rest said prices had stayed mainly static. Worst affected was southern England, with the problems of Dan-Air - now taken over by British Airways - affecting the area around Gatwick airport particularly hard. Prices in East Anglia have been further undermined by the repossession by banks and building societies of homes, including some previously owned by Names at the Lloyd's insurance market. Mr Michael Knight, a consultant employed by Jackson-Stops & Staff in Newmarket says: 'Every time a home is repossessed it knocks another 20 per cent off the price.' Estate agents in northern England said that the announcement of pit closures had adversely affected housing markets in mining areas such as Pontefract and Chesterfield. The institution hopes that the housing market may have reached the bottom. Mr Peter Miller, its housing spokesman, said: 'The market is waiting at the traffic lights showing amber; whether they turn red or green hangs on perceived economic stability. 'Fear of redundancy is the most commonly cited reason for potential buyers not committing themselves.'
