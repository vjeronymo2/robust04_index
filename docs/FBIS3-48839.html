Language: French Article Type:BFN [Text] Phnom Penh 28 Jan (AKP) -- The Cambodian Government has decided to waive entry visas for Cambodia for visits of two weeks or less. This decision was made on Wednesday, 26 January, at a Council of Ministers meeting chaired by Prince Krompreah Norodom Ranariddh and His Excellency Hun Sen, first and second prime ministers of the Royal Government of Cambodia respectively. The decision will be effective as of 1 March 1994. At present, foreigners must pay $20 for an entry visa at Pochentong Airport. Concerning agricultural development for the period of 1994-95, the meeting decided to impose a ban on the export of logs and timber, but it still authorizes the exploitation of wood for local needs. With regard to rubber exploitation, the government has decided in principle to privatize rubber production. It also examined the project for palm oil cultivation, saying it favored foreign and local investments in that sector. The government has also adopted [number indistinct] May as Water Policy Day. On that day, all citizens should participate in work to construct irrigation systems. They should work on these projects for 15 days per year.
