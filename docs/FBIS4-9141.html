BFN [Text] Political and diplomatic figures continue to arrive in Bucharest for tomorrow's Crans Montana Forum opening. Two prominent figures arrived about an hour ago: Catherine Lalumiere, acting secretary general of the Council of Europe [CE], who was welcomed at Otopeni International Airport by Ambassador Virgil Constantinescu, general director at the Romanian Ministry of Foreign Affairs, and Georg Reisch, general secretary of the European Free Trade Association [EFTA]. According to the schedule, Lalumiere will chair, together with President Ion Iliescu and Forum Foundation President Jean Paul Carteron, the inaugural session of the Crans Montana Forum in Bucharest on 21 April. Upon his arrival at Otopeni Airport Reisch made the following statement to our radio station: [Begin Reisch recording, in English with superimposed translation into Romanian] I am very glad to be here again as the head of EFTA. We want to have close ties with Romania. We have an accord on free trade exchanges and I am looking forward not only to presiding over a Forum session on the importance of free trade for international security, but also to meeting with Romanian officials to discuss details on the cooperation between Romania and EFTA. [end recording] We remind you that Otar Patsatsia, prime minister of the Republic of Georgia, also arrived in Bucharest this evening. According to our correspondent Nicu Popescu, the following guests are also expected to arrive tonight: Mechyslaw Hryb, chairman of the Supreme Council of Belarus, and Shim'on Peres, Israeli minister of foreign affairs.
