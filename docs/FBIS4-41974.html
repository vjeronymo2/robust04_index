BFN [By ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondent Andrey Bychkov] [Text] Moscow, 30 Jun -- "There are no differences between Russia and India, not on any issue" -- with these words Russian President Boris Yeltsin opened the talks between Russian and Indian delegations in the Kremlin today. He had just completed a one-to- one conversation with Indian Prime Minister Narasimha Rao. "This applies both to bilateral issues and to international affairs," the Russian president said. Turning to Narasimha Rao, he then said the intergovernmental Russo-Indian commission on commercial, economic, scientific, and technological cooperation commission "regrettably has not functioned, although there has been enough time for that." Boris Yeltsin asked that commission to "do real and actual work." While the leaders of the two countries were talking in St. Catherine's Hall of the Great Kremlin Palace, top representatives of Russia and India were signing a package of bilateral documents. The agreements provide specifically for the development of cooperation in science and technology, information, and tourism. Both sides also intend to work together in environmental protection, space research, and the exploitation of space for peaceful purposes. The intergovernmental agreement providing India with credits to finance long-term programs in the field of technological cooperation and special purchases. The Russian president and the Indian prime minister are expected to sign a declaration on further development and deepening of cooperation between the Russian Federation and the Republic of India today, as well as a Moscow declaration on protecting the interests of multiethnic states.
