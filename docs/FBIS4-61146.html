BFN [Text] Tbilisi, 29 April (SNARK) -- The head of Georgian state, Eduard Shevardnadze, issued an order according to which the number of state-funded facilities will be reduced since the second quarter of 1994. The order also reads that, with purpose of fast transition to free prices, the state subsidiaries for bread, electric power and gas are planned to be cut. At the same time, VAT [value-added tax], customs taxes and excise duty for petrol will be increased. The 8-percent tax for products export will be removed. Also the obligatory currency sale to government by the enterprises is no more valid. The order, as think the government specialists, has been developed according to the IMF and IBRD [International Bank for Reconstruction and Development] recommendations.
