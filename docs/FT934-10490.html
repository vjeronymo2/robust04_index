931105 FT 05 NOV 93 / World Trade News: Bus joint venture set up in China HINO Motors, the Japanese maker of trucks which is affiliated with Toyota, has set up a joint venture company in China to manufacture and market buses, the company said, writes Michiyo Nakamoto. The joint venture, Long Ri Bus, has been formed with one of China's big five bus manufacturers and is the first of its kind with foreign participation. It is capitalised at Y245bn (Pounds 1.54bn) with Heilongjiang Bus contributing 65 per cent, the Heilongjiang Import and Export Corporation 10 per cent, and Hino Motors and Mitsui 12.5 per cent each. Production, initially 100 units a year rising to 500, will start this month. Hino will provide components such as engine transmissions. The chassis will initially be Chinese-made.
