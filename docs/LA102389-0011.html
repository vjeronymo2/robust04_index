October 23, 1989, Monday, San Diego County Edition

RAIN MAY CONTINUE THROUGH TUESDAY

After a mostly cloudy and sometimes drizzly weekend it finally feels like fall in San Diego.

The rain that fell over the weekend may continue through Tuesday, when San Diego weather forecasters have predicted mostly cloudy skies and a slight chance of light rain, according to National Weather Service forecaster Wally Cegiel.

San Diego County rainfall totals varied from a trace in some areas to .09 of an inch in Chula Vista to 1.2 inches on Palomar Mountain during the 24-hour period that ended Sunday at 4 p.m., according to the National Weather Service.

The temperature forecast for all San Diego areas today is partly cloudy with highs ranging from 67 to 74 and lows in the upper 50s.
