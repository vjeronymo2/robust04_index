BFN ["Paid Publicity" by the Permanent Commissions of Representatives of Guatemalan Refugees in Mexico (Comisiones Permanentes de Representatives de Refugiados Guatemaltecos en Mexico--CCPP) issued in May 1994 in Guatemala City] [Text] The CCPP, in view of the government's noncompliance with the agreements signed on 8 October 1992, which permit the voluntary and dignified return of our brothers, would like to inform national and international public opinion: First: That we announce the return of our brother refugees from Mexico to the farm La Quetzal, La Libertad Muncipality, Peten Department, will take place on 27 June, according to the government and CCPP agreement. In view of the government's lack of respect and irresponsibility in connection with the signed agreements, we hold the government responsible for any attack or incident against the personal safety of those returning as well as against those who offer their solidarity by accompanying them. Second: That the CCPP, always respecting what has been agreed, presented to the CEAR [Special Commission for Assistance to Repatriated People] on 14 April a plan of operation for the return of the refugees, thus complying with the agreements in which it is stated the refugees may return 60 days after the plan is presented, meaning in our case the return to Guatemala can legally take place after 15 June. Third: That one of the seven agreements dated 8 October establishes that the refugees may decide how, when, and where they will return. We have decided to return to El Peten, specifically to the farm "La Quetzal," which is private property, and an agreement has already been reached for its purchase from current owners. To this avail CONAP [National Council for Publicity] has issued an opinion that at least 20 percent of this farm may be used for settling. Nevertheless, afterward, and to block our return, CONAP contradicted itself and has said it is illegal to settle in "El Quetzal," because it is a part of the Maya Environ. CONAP has always opposed our return, despite the fact they and several institutions have made studies indicating only 80 percent of the farm lies within the environ and the number of families that will settle will not endanger forest conservation or the environment. Fourth: We call on the national and international community to speak up regarding this situation and demand the Guatemalan Government comply with the agreements signed on 8 October 1992, without further delays or pretexts. Fifth: We ask the verification board to issue the report requested on 24 March 1994. Sixth: Last, we summon the accompanying forum [foro de acompanamiento] to establish a permanent assembly to accompany the movement's return to El Peten, which, we repeat, will take place on 27 June. [Issued] Guatemala City [Dated] May 1994. The return is a right of all Guatemalans, and it is essential to the national process of reconciliation. Return is struggle, not resignation Permanent Commissions of Representatives of Guatemalan Refugees in Mexico, CCPP
