Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The special envoy of the prime minister, Mr. Asif Ali Zardari, had a meeting with the Iraqi prime minister, Mr. Ahmad Husayn Khudayyir, in Baghdad and discussed with him bilateral relations. He stressed the need to establish fruitful cooperation in various fields once the impediments are removed. The two leaders also discussed humanitarian issues of direct concern to the two countries, including the release of three Pakistanis undergoing imprisonment in Iraq. They also exchanged views on development problems in the Third World and underlined the need for South-South cooperation. Mr. Asif Zardari conveyed to the Iraqi prime minister greetings of Prime Minister Benazir Bhutto and through him to the Iraqi people. He also conveyed goodwill feelings and humanitarian concern of the people of Pakistan for their brethren in Iraq. The Iraqi prime minister recalled the close relations that Iraq traditionally enjoyed with the government and people of Pakistan and particularly expressed his high regard for Ms. Benazir Bhutto. He said she is held in the highest esteem both by the government and the people of Iraq.
