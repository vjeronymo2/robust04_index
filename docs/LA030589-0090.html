March 5, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

COMPANION OMISSIONS

Thank you for writing about the strange omissions in the Oxford Companion to English Literature (Book Review, Feb. 12).

While I realize that your comments were directed toward a comparison between the Oxford book and the Cambridge Guide to Literature in English, it might interest you to learn that there is no mention of Isabel Colgate and Roald Dahl, both proper British subjects as far as I know. This seemed so odd that I simply stopped looking for more quirkiness.

Also, in Oxford's rush to drown us in printed words and imperil our forests, they have produced several volumes of collected articles such as the ones on female jazz singers that did not even dignify the effort with an index. I recall a time when such fine bookstores as the old Pickwick would not even order books such as this that came without an index.

Anyway, your comments were salutary and might, I would hope, help to bring this rampant spinning of words gently but firmly to heel.

JAMES D. HIGSON

NEWPORT BEACH
