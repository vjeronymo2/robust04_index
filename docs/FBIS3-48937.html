Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Belgrade, Dec 31 (TANJUG) -- Miners at Serbia's biggest coal mine Kolubara on Friday ended a three-day strike after negotiating a settlement with the authorities in the night between Thursday [30 December] and Friday. Early on Friday afternoon, the Belgrade city transport was back to normal after its workers ended their several-hour stoppage. Like the workers in Kolubara, they went on strike demanding higher wages and better living standards. The strike of the 17,000 Kolubara workers in Lazarevac near Belgrade ended after the miners on Friday morning verified the agreement reached with the republic's electricity board and the government on Thursday night. The strikers demanded to receive their wages in German marks because of the galopping inflation which hit one million percent in December alone. The disturbances in coal deliveries resulted in the suspension of operation of the thermal power plant Nikola Tesla in Obrenovac, which produces 50 percent of Serbia's overall electricity, due to which the Serbian electricity board started with several-hour brownouts on Thursday. Although the miners' strike has ended, it is not certain whether power supply in Serbia will be back to normal on Friday because of the disturbances in coal deliveries and electricity production or whether some parts of the republic will celebrate New Year's Eve in candlelight. Dissatisfied with low wages, workers in the Belgrade city transport struck on Friday morning. The Serbian railways has been on strike for several days, along with some hospitals and gravediggers in Belgrade. A drastic fall in the living standards and record-high hyperinflation have been provoked by draconic U.N. sanctions introduced against Yugoslavia in late May of 1992. Three-fourths of Serbia's industrial facilities have been standing still for months, while the remaining one-fourth operate at reduced capacities although they are not on strike. The average salary in Serbia is below 15 German marks, while one kilo of meat sells for 40 marks.
