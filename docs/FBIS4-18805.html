BFN [Announcer-read report over still of Cetin, Christopher] [Text] Foreign Minister Hikmet Cetin has said that the United States should intensify its initiatives concerning the Greek Cypriot side if progress is to be achieved in the Cyprus talks. Cetin replied to a letter on the Cyprus issue he received from U.S. Secretary of State Warren Christopher. According to information obtained by a Turkish Radio and Television correspondent, in his reply letter Cetin said that one should remain loyal to the original package the UN secretary general issued on 1 July, 1993, regarding the confidence-building measures. He pointed out that the UN secretary general announced that Kliridhis, leader of the Greek Cypriot administration, accepted the package in question. In his letter, Christopher said that a significant point has been reached on the Cyprus talks, adding that a great opportunity has been created. He further thanked Turkey for the efforts it exerted on the issue and noted the benefits of encouraging the negotiating process.
