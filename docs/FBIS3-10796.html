Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Ximena Iturralde Sanchez de Lozada, the Bolivian first lady, visited the new Chilean President, Eduardo Frei, and his wife Marta Larraechea after his inauguration yesterday. President Frei said that the Bolivian sea coast claim is a pending issue, because international treaties currently in force are involved and that the way to approach this issue will be found. Frei said he hopes to have smooth relations with Bolivia during his administration. Yesterday Bolivian Foreign Minister Antonio Aranibar thanked President Frei on behalf of the Bolivian people for his comments and wished him success in his administration. [Begin Aranibar recording] The Foreign Ministry on behalf of the Bolivian Government and people expresses its satisfaction over the comments made by Arturo Frei Ruiz-Tagle, president of the Republic of Chile, in response to the message carried by the Bolivian first lady, Ximena Iturralde de Sanchez de Lozada, on the occasion of her visit to Santiago. The Bolivian Foreign Ministry upon reiterating to President Eduardo Frei its wishes for his success in his administration expresses its desire to continue this new and fruitful communication between the two countries. [end recording]
