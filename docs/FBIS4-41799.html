BFN [Text] Kiev, 28 June (AFP) -- Former Ukrainian premier Leonid Kuchma, challenging President Leonid Kravchuk in presidential elections here, will contest the validity of the first-round election result with an allegation of vote-rigging, a Kuchma aide said Tuesday [28 June]. Pyotr Lemek accused several local electoral committees of falsifying votes to give Kravchuk an edge over Kuchma. A press conference of the central electoral committee scheduled for 1100. (0800 GMT) Tuesday was delayed for several hours. "Kuchma garnered 34 percent of the votes and Kravchuk 36 percent, and the difference is not eight percentage points as (Kravchuk) aides maintain," he said.
