931109 FT 09 NOV 93 / Cartoons call the tune A WELSH broadcaster, the BBC and a Russian film company aim to benefit from the market for cultural cartoons. Operavox, a series of six animated classic operas condensed to 30 minutes, is the result of a collaboration between the Welsh-language channel S4C, BBC2, and the Russian production company Christmas Films. The group's previous venture, Shakespeare - The Animated Tales, has been sold to 50 countries and recently won two Emmy awards in the US. A second series of Shakespeare films is in production for next autumn. Two of the operatic films, The Magic Flute and The Barber of of Seville, were produced in Moscow and the other four - Carmen, Rigoletto, Turandot and Rhinegold - were made in the UK. Music for the films, which are aimed at a non opera-going audience, has been recorded in Welsh and English by the orchestra and chorus of the Welsh National Opera. Transmission in the UK is set for next Easter. The group also announced that it had begun pre-production work on six half-hour episodes for a series called The Old Testament - The Animated Tales, which is expected to be ready for transmission in autumn 1995. Mr Chris Grace, director of animation at S4C, said: 'European and American broadcasters are attracted by our commitment to using the art of animation in the European vein to explore our common cultural heritage.'
