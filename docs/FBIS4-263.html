BFN [Text] In Gabon, Prime Minister Casimir Oye-Mba's fourth government assumed office officially in Libreville this afternoon about 10 days after it was formed. Addressing the 36 members of his cabinet, Prime Minister Oye-Mba did not fail to point out the difficulties that might crop up during their terms of office. Let us listen to Mr. Oye-Mba: [Begin Oye-Mba recording] Apart from the pride generated in you by your appointment, you should know that being a cabinet member is mainly a question of office and responsibility. I inform you, particularly the newcomers, that the president of the Republic and I did not call on you to attend a picnic. We called on you to assume heavy and difficult responsibilities. I sincerely congratulate and encourage you. I assure you of my personal support which will help you do your work in accordance with the head of state's general guidelines and the instructions that I will give you. [end recording]
