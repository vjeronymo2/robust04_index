921029 FT 29 OCT 92 / Arts: Today's Television ITV brings yet another batch of Rumpole Of The Bailey, one of the most memorable characters ever created specifically for television. However, much as we all wish him to carry on, would it not be a good idea if, once in a while, creator John Mortimer could produce a script which did not rely upon coincidental themes at work and at home? Today there is collusion to replace Rumpole in chambers while at home Hilda suspects she is about to be replaced by another woman (9.00). BBC2's sequence of documentaries about life in Britain today, Present Imperfect (9.30), turns to that peculiar British institution, the transport 'caff'. What with motorway service stations and Little Chefs, 'caffs' are becoming fewer and further between, and a good thing too, some will say. Channel 4 is showing three short American TV dramas, Women And Men which, for reasons best known to themselves, they have collectively labelled a 'film' (10.00). The scripts are adapted from stories by Mary McCarthy, Dorothy Parker and Ernest Hemingway, and the directors are Frederic Raphael, Ken Russell, and Tony Richardson (yes, all British).
