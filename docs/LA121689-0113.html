December 16, 1989, Saturday, Home Edition

AIRLINE SEVERS TIE WITH CBS IN DISPUTE

Because CBS refused to cut stories about air crashes and other airline problems from the daily in-flight news shows it provides American Airlines, it is losing American's business on Feb. 28, a CBS official said.

Thus, in March, only taped CNN newscasts -- without pictures of airline crash stories -- will continue on the airline, thanks to an unusual contract that Turner Home Entertainment said it expects to sign next week with American.

American's rationale may be to avoid alarming its passengers, but "it's like passing out newspapers with holes cut in them," said David Berman, a vice president of CBS Broadcast International.

"The agreement is limited to (stories about) air crashes," said Steve Chamberlain, vice president of Turner Home Entertainment, which merchandises programs of Cable News Network, part of Ted Turner's cable empire.

"There will not be any other wholesale editing of our product," he adds. "Above all, we have to preserve the journalistic integrity of our network."

Although he said air crash stories would be edited from CNN's in-flight news packages for American, Mary O'Neill, a spokeswoman for the airline, said Thursday that this doesn't mean a complete blackout of such stories.

"What the contract with CNN calls for is that CNN will not show the video," she said by phone from American's headquarters in Dallas. "They will talk, in the audio portion, about these kinds of incidents. But they will not show the video."

She called this an arrangement worked out after American's surveys showed that its passengers complained that pictures of airline crash scenes disturbed them deeply.
