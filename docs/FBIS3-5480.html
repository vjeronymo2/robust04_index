Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, March 8 KYODO -- Sony Corp. said Tuesday [8 March] it has agreed to license its 16-bit microcontroller unit (MCU) technology to Texas Instruments Japan Ltd. Under the agreement, the Japanese subsidiary of the U.S. semiconductor manufacturer will develop, produce and market MCUs using Sony technology, Sony said. The MCU forms the brain of a wide range of electronics products, including videocassette recorders and personal computers. The companies will also study the possibility of joint 16-bit MCU development and technology sharing in the area of MCU peripheral circuitry, Sony said. In addition, the companies will discuss the incorporation of MCUs developed by Texas Instruments Japan in Sony's audiovisual products, Sony said.
