BFN [Text] President Itamar Franco has met with his ministers and decided to keep the Federal Police buildings under Army control today. Late this afternoon, the president will again meet with the ministers of strategic sectors to review the decision on the Army presence at Federal Police facilities. During last night's meeting at Planalto Palace, the president, the military ministers, Justice Minister Alexandre Dupeyrat, and Federal Administrative Secretary Ronaldo Canhim analyzed the Federal Police strike for two and a half hours. President Franco decided to maintain the security measures that were adopted on Wednesday until today, Friday. The measures are considered essential for the rendering of essential services and to preserve public property. Late this afternoon, the president will again meet with the ministers to reassess the strike and a proposal to withdraw the military troops, which was made by the national defense committee of the Chamber of Deputies. The president will then decide whether he will maintain Federal Police buildings and stations under Army control.
