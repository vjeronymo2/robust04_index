BFN [Text] Pyongyang, June 17 (XINHUA) -- The President of the Democratic People's Republic of Korea (DPRK) Kim Il-song met with former U.S. President Jimmy Carter and his wife a second time at noon here today. Kim's wife Kim Song-ae also joined the meeting this time, according to local press reports, which said Kim and Carter had sincere talks over issues of common concern. No other details were disclosed. Carter is due to cross the demarcation line at Panmunjom on Saturday [18 June] on his way back to Seoul, where he is expected to brief South Korean President Kim Yong-sam on the results of his talks with Kim Il-song. Carter's visit to DPRK is aimed at facilitating exchanges of views on the nuclear issue among the United States, South Korea and the DPRK. He had talks with South Korean leaders earlier this week before arriving in Pyongyang on Wednesday. Kim Il-song had his first meeting with carter on Thursday and they talked in a cordial atmosphere, according to local press reports.
