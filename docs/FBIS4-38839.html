BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Konstantin Pribytkov] [Text] Geneva, 2 June -- Bosnian Serb leader Radovan Karadzic plans to visit Moscow next week. The Serbian leader, who is also well known as a poet, said today in an exclusive interview with an ITAR-TASS correspondent in Geneva that the trip is connected with his being awarded the literary Mikhail Sholokhov Prize by the Russian Union of Writers. In addition, Radovan Karadzic intends to make use of his stay in Moscow to have talks about the rapid achievement of peace in Bosnia and Herzegovina. According to the Serbian leader, the trip is planned for about 8-12 June.
