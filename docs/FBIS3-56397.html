Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Semen Ivanov] [Text] Moscow January 17 TASS -- The launch of the U.S. "Discovery" space shuttle on which a Russian cosmonaut is to make the first flight was postponed from January 20 to February 3 because of malfunctions in the craft's engines, TASS learned from reliable sources in the Russian space business on Monday. 35 year-old Sergey Krikalyov is the first Russian cosmonaut to fly on board the U.S. space shuttle, according to a U.S.-Russian agreement. Krikalyov made two space flights on the Russian "Mir" space station, the first lasted for 151 days in 1988-1989 and the second - 312 days from May 1991 to March 1992. Krikalyov who was trained for the shuttle flight in the United States said the Russian training programme is better in many aspects. Krikalyov, who is a computer engineer, said the computers installed on the "Discovery" had been designed long ago and are considerably inferior to the computer centre onboard the "Mir". He also complained about an inconvenient work schedule in orbit - in two shifts. There is only one sleeping-bag for two crew members and they have to sleep in turn, according to him. Krikalyov was surprised that after the training programme was completed astronauts did not have to pass any exams or tests. It is the captain who bears all responsibility for the flight preparations. This is the second delay in the launch of the space shuttle. Initially it was planned for last November 10, but was also postponed.
