Language: English Article Type:BFN [By GIA-TASS] [Text] Tbilisi January 20 TASS -- Chairman of the Georgian parliament Eduard Shevarnadze received a delegation from Moscow on Wednesday which arrived to practically implement agreements reached between the mayor's offices of Tbilisi and Moscow at recent talks. Under the agreement, in the first quarter of 1993, Georgia is to receive 3,000 tonnes of potatoes, 5,000 tonnes of flour, 1,000 tonnes of sugar, butter, vegetable oil and canned food in exchange for Georgian tea, champagne, mineral water "Borzhomi" tobacco and citrus fruits. The first batch of foodstuffs is expected to arrive from Russia in the near future. The Moscow delegation is led by Vice-Premier of the Moscow government, Aleksey Peshkov.
