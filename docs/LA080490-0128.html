August 4, 1990, Saturday, Home Edition

ROBERT J. SCHMORLEITZ; ATTORNEY DEFENDED UNITARIAN CHURCH

Robert J. Schmorleitz, 77, an attorney who represented the First Unitarian Church when its nonprofit status was jeopardized during the anti-communist investigations of the 1950s.

Schmorleitz went to court to help the church keep its nonprofit status after Unitarian leaders refused to sign a loyalty oath declaring that they weren't communists. Schmorleitz received his law degree from Southwestern University in Los Angeles and after World War II service in the Merchant Marine set up a private law practice in North Hollywood. In Los Angeles on July 26 of cancer.
