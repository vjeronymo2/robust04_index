Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities in Peru monitored through 27 January. Source is listed in parentheses following each item: The Inter-American Development Bank, IDB, has approved new loans for Peru totaling $300 million, which will be earmarked for health and education projects as well as for a program to grant property deeds to poor peasants. This information was made public on 9 January by Manuel Estela, the Peruvian representative to the IDB. He reported that this is the first part of $400 million in loans the IDB intends to grant to Peru 1994. The Financial Development Corporation will be in charge of administering the funds. (Lima LA REPUBLICA in Spanish 10 Jan 94 p 7) The Netherlands' Government has officially notified Economy Minister Jorge Camet Dickmann of the partial forgiveness of Peru's debt to the Netherlands for the granting of loans on concessionary terms, which amounts to $1.9 billion. The Netherlands canceled a total of $6.7 billion of Peruvian debt in 1993. (Lima EL COMERCIO in Spanish 14 Jan 94 p A4)
