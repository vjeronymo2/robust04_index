Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, Feb. 4 KYODO -- The Ministry of Finance [MOF] unveiled new administrative guidance Friday [4 February] to help mergers of financial institutions burdened with nonperforming loans. Ministry officials said the guidance is designed to help banking and nonbanking firms liquidate bad loans as quickly as possible to promote economic recovery. Financial institutions were urged to actively utilize Cooperative Credit Purchasing Co. to rid themselves of bad loans or high-risk loans, the officials said. The company is a joint venture established by about 150 commercial banks and finance firms to liquidate bad loans. The ministry also urged financial institutions to consider transferring claims for which they have agreed to reduce or exempt interest payments to affiliates. The guidance will be incorporated in a comprehensive package of pump-priming measures to be announced by the government in the near future, the officials said.
