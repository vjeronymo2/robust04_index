920810 FT 10 AUG 92 / Councils 'fail to exploit' volunteers LOCAL authorities are failing to exploit volunteers as a resource in developing their community care programmes, according to Community Service Volunteers, a charity that organises voluntary effort. A survey by the charity of half of all local authorities in England and Wales found that in spite of rising demand for community carers, only 14 per cent of councils had a clear strategy for recruiting and deploying volunteers. Barely two thirds even consider volunteers. The charity noted that although all authorities mention the voluntary sector in their plans, many refer only to services bought in from professionals employed by voluntary organisations. The charity reports an increase of nearly a third in the number of young people putting themselves forward under the organisation's full-time volunteer scheme. A recent survey by the organisation showed that up to 50 per cent of older people would give time for organised voluntary work if they were asked. Ms Elisabeth Hoodless, director of the charity, said: 'Councils should develop clear strategies for involving volunteers - a flexible, cost-effective and highly motivated resource.'
