July 21, 1990, Saturday, Home Edition

LIMITING SALES OF SPRAY PAINT TO CURB GRAFFITI

Concerning the editorial regarding graffiti I would like to point out a few other people and organizations also working on this problem.

We must congratulate Sen. Torres for his legislation.

Locking up the cans is probably where it will go. Most of paint used for graffiti is stolen.

There is another bill in the Legislature sponsored by Assemblyman Richard Katz (D-Sylmar) which will put a tax on spray paint and this revenue will be used for graffiti cleanup and educational work related to gangs.

Many Los Angeles officials have worked together with VOICE over the past year on a major anti-graffiti campaign. Valley Organized in Community Efforts (VOICE) is a coalition of churches and synagogues in the San Fernando Valley. The City Council is proposing to lock up spray paint cans and use non-jail sentencing to clean up graffiti.

Hopefully, all this work will come to fruition very soon.

SANFORD DU ROFF

Tarzana
