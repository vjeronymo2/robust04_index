930319 FT 19 MAR 93 / Arts: Today's Television If you are more interested in the arts than in American movies, American comedies, or steam train drama (a repeat of All Creatures Great And Small on BBC1, and another episode in the new series of Doctor Finlay on ITV) there is only one programme for you on television tonight: Arena. This, too, has an American subject: writer Philip Roth, still best known to British readers for his 1969 novel, Portnoy's Complaint. Previously Roth has tended to stay off the promotion circuit but now, having written a new book, Operation Shylock, he is putting himself about like all the others (9.30 BBC1). On radio, however, there is much wider choice. On Radio 2's Arts Programme (10.00), Austin Mitchell visits Durham Cathedral which has been declared a World Heritage Site. Radio 3 offers Strange Meeting in which Russell Davies considers the achievements of Wilfred Owen who was born 100 years ago and died at the Sambre Canal in 1918 one week before the armistice (10.45). And on Radio 4 in Kaleidoscope Roddy Doyle talks about his new book, Paddy Clarke Ha Ha Ha which draws on his childhood in the 1960s (9.15).
