March 7, 1990, Wednesday, Home Edition

DR. NEIL SOLOMON: LOOKS AND HEALTH

Question: Anyone who can read should know by now that sunbathing not only does not make a person more attractive but can do damage to the skin.

Are there any other possible dangers to our appearance that we should be aware of?

Answer: Some of the factors that influence our appearance are beyond our powers to control. Age, for example, is one such factor. Others include heredity, skin type, and our general facial skeletal structure.

However, other factors are within our control. These include exposure to sunlight, which you mention, as well as tobacco and alcohol use. In general, I believe that the greatest contribution to good looks is good health.
