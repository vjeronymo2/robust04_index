920111 FT 11 JAN 92 / International Company News: First Chicago registers Dollars 15m loss FIRST Chicago, the holding company for First National Bank of Chicago, posted a fourth-quarter loss of Dollars 15.1m, or 40 cents per share, compared with earnings of Dollars 57.1m or 75 cents per share a year ago. Annual earnings for 1991 were Dollars 116.3m, or Dollars 1.15 per share, down by more than half from Dollars 249.3m, or Dollars 3.32, in 1990. The fourth-quarter results were hit by a Dollars 150m provision for credit losses, primarily for two loans in retailing and communications. Another Dollars 30m was set aside as loss provisions for other real estate assets. Non-performing assets fell by Dollars 300m at the end of 1991 from their peak of Dollars 1.6bn. The bank reduced its quarterly dividend to 30 cents from 50 cents.
