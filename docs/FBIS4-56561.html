BFN [Text] Barbados Foreign Minister Branford Taitt stated to PRENSA LATINA that the presence of Cuban President Fidel Castro at the First International Small Island [Nations] Conference has greatly contributed to defining the objectives and strategies of the conference. Following a brief meeting with Cuban Foreign Minister Roberto Robaina, Taitt said he had been excited to learn that Fidel Castro was to attend the Bridgetown conference. Taitt pointed out that Fidel Castro's address to the plenum was brilliant, and that he personally believes Castro's presence lends greater importance to the conference and contributes to defining fundamental elements in the strategies of sustainable development of the small island nations. Taitt added that the process of integrating Cuba into the Caribbean Community's economic mechanisms would not suffer delays due to external pressures.
