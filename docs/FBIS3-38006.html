Language: English Article Type:BFN [From the "Diplomatic Panorama" feature: reports by Diplomatic Correspondents Dmitriy Voskoboinikov, Igor Porshnev, Vitaliy Trubetskoy, and others] [Text] President Yeltsin has urged the Federation Council to endorse the dispatch of an additional military contingent numbering 300 men to join the U.N. peace-keeping forces in former Yugoslavia "with a view to ensuring control over the implementation of an agreement on a peaceful solution to the conflict, and over the unconditional observance of the agreement to cease fire in the area of Sarajevo by the warring sides". The Russian president made this appeal in connection with a request from the U.N. Secretary General. The relevant draft resolution instructs Russia's government to resolve the issues connected with the forming of a corresponding volunteer military contingent. The Federation Council decided to consider this issue in the framework of its defence and security committee Friday morning, and discuss it at its evening session the same day.
