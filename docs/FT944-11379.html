941107 FT 07 NOV 94 / Employers urged to stamp out workplace bullying The white-collar MSF union will today call for measures to deal with workplace bullying by establishing counselling for victims and disciplinary procedures, Motoko Rich writes. According to a recent survey up to 78 per cent of employees have witnessed workplace bullying and 51 per cent have been victims. The union defines bullying as an 'abuse of power', but differentiates bullying from harassment, which it says is normally 'based on race or sex'. It adds that there is very little legal redress for victims and advises them to keep records of any incidents. The union will campaign for employers to recognise bullying as a serious problem and to support staff through complaints and disciplinary procedures, and counselling for both victims and bullies. The Health and Safety Executive said it was preparing guidance for managers on stress in the workplace which highlights the importance of providing complaints procedures for victims of bullying. It added: 'Where an individual employee flags up a case of bullying it should be taken seriously by the management. Bullying could affect an individual's mental or physical health.'
