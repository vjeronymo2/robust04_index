May 17, 1989, Wednesday, Home Edition

THE NATION

Three Americans in four now think of themselves as environmentalists, according to a new Gallup Poll. Public concern about the environment tends to center on ecological problems that are tangible and may be directly observed in people's daily lives. Large majorities say they worry a great deal about pollution of rivers, lakes and reservoirs (72%), contamination of soil and water by toxic waste (69%) and air pollution (63%). After the wreck of an Exxon tanker that spilled more than 11 million gallons of oil off Alaska, 41% of Americans say they would seriously consider joining a boycott of Exxon's products; 7% claim they already have done so. Almost as many (46%) are opposed to a boycott. Asked to name the most important problems facing the nation, 4% now cite environmental issues; 34%, various economic problems, and 27%, the drug crisis.
