BFN [Text] Cairo, 4 Apr (MENA)--Dr. Nabil Sha'th, political adviser to Palestinian President Yasir 'Arafat and head of the Palestinian side to the Gaza-Jericho Committee meetings, has stressed that the main objective of the Security Committee meeting today is to complete the negotiations concerning the establishment of the Palestinian police force, the timetable for the force's entry, and the date the vanguards will arrive in the Arab territories. In statements before starting the Palestinian-Israeli negotiations today, Dr. Sha'th said the Legal Affairs Committee is expected to begin its work today after legal advisers arrive from the West Bank and Gaza. Sha'th said several Palestinians from the occupied territories are expected to arrive today to take part in the meetings of the committee in charge of transferring power. The committee started its work in Cairo today. In his statements, Dr. Sha'th said the delegates will also discuss the possibility of drawing up a timetable for the release of the Palestinian prisoners and detainees. He noted that the committee stemming from the Gaza-Jericho Committee will meet today and will continue working day and night to achieve an agreement as soon as possible. He said several Norwegians from the international observer force will arrive in Cairo today. General Amnon Shahaq, Israeli deputy chief of staff and head of the Israeli side to the negotiations, said this round of talks will continue until Wednesday evening. He said he hoped that positive results would be achieved as soon as possible. Brigadier General 'Abd-al-Razzaq al-Yahya, head of the Palestinian side to the Security Committee, expects that an agreement will be reached very soon, noting that if progress is achieved in the talks, the negotiations concerning the Palestinian police force will be concluded today. Brig. Gen. al-Yahya affirmed that Palestinian President 'Arafat will arrive in Cairo tomorrow, Tuesday.
