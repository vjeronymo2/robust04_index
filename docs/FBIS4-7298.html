BFN [Text] Thailand and Vietnam signed a memorandum of understanding on intellectual property, the first of its kind between the two developing countries in this fast-growing region of the world. The document was signed by representatives from Thailand's Ministry of Commerce and Vietnam's Ministry of Science, Technology and Environment during the visit by Deputy Minister of Commerce Churin Laksanawisit to Vietnam during April 22 to 24, 1994. The agreement is aimed at making Thailand a center for intellectual property registration and claims for Indochina. Deputy Minister Churin said that Vietnam is in agreement in Thailand's proposal. The memorandum of standing signed by the two sides provided opportunities for both countries to exchange information on laws, procedures in registration of patents, trademarks, and copyrights, registrated marks and patents, and cooperation in personnel development. Churin said that the signing of the memorandum of understanding with Vietnam will benefit Thai business people and investors, facilitating the registration of their trademarks and protecting their intellectual property rights.
