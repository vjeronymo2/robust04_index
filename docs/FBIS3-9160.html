Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] Norwegian Foreign Minister Godal has hosted a lunch for Mate Granic, vice president of the [Croatian] government and foreign minister. In his toast he stressed the shared responsibility of the international community for resolving the problems of the former Yugoslavia and expressed appreciation for Croatia's way of resolving humanitarian problems. He also offered further humanitarian aid and accepted with pleasure the Croatian minister's invitation to visit our country. Speaking about the agreement reached in Washington, he said that it was very good and that it would receive positive reactions in Norway. Croatian Minister Mate Granic then held a news conference at which he explained the importance of the Washington agreement. He added that within the next 10 to 15 days negotiations on Bosnia-Herzegovina's organization will also start with the Bosnian Serbs. At this news conference, which attracted a great deal of attention among Norwegian media, Minister Granic also explained Croatia's standpoints regarding the resolution of occupied areas in the Republic of Croatia. He stressed that the Croatian authorities are willing to give local autonomy to the Serbs in the protected areas as well as all rights in keeping with international laws. Granic stressed the importance of normalization of relations between Zagreb and Belgrade and mutual recognition among all the states of the former Yugoslavia. He added that this would be impossible without the reintegration of the protected areas into Croatia. [passage omitted]
