Language: Japanese Article Type:BFN [From MAINICHI SHIMBUN in Japanese 22 January Morning Edition] [Text] On 21 January the Liberal Democratic Party [LDP] submitted to House of Representatives Speaker Takako Doi two no-confidence motions against Tsutomu Hata, deputy prime minister and concurrently foreign minister, and Eijiro Hata, agriculture minister, in connection with their handling of the opening of the rice market under the new multilateral trade negotiations (Uruguay Round). The lower house Committee on Rules and Administration will discuss how to deal with the LDP-submitted motions at a board of directors meeting on 24 January. As reasons for the no-confidence motions against them, the LDP cited the following: Foreign Minister Hata "neglected to take part in the negotiations on his own initiative and to brief the Diet on progress in the negotiations; as a result he ruined the Diet resolutions." Agriculture Minister Hata "lied to the people in replying to Diet interpellations."
