910514 FT 14 MAY 91 / International Company News: Contigas plans DM900m east German project CONTIGAS, the German gas group 81 per cent owned by the utility Bayernwerk, said yesterday that it intends to invest DM900m (Dollars 522m) in the next four years to build a new gas distribution system in the east German state of Thuringia. Reporting on its results for 1989-1990 the company said that the dividend would remain unchanged at DM8. Sales rose 9.4 per cent to DM3.37bn, but post-tax profit fell slightly from DM31.3m to DM30.7m. In the first half of the current year sales rose 23 per cent. Mr Jurgen Weber, currently vice-chairman of Lufthansa, the German airline, is today expected to be named as the successor to the chairman Mr Heinz Ruhnau who retires at the end of 1992. Mr Weber is currently the technical director on the Lufthansa board.
