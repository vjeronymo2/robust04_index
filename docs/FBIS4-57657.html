BFN [Report by Juan Carlos Santoyo from the "ACIR Radio News" newscast] [Text] Manuel Camacho Solis, commissioner for peace and reconciliation in Chiapas State, met today with permanent members of the Chamber of Deputies' Congress Commission, who are helping with the investigations in connection with the Chiapas conflict. Camacho Solis made a very important announcement to several legislators who closely follow the talks. He said that in less than three weeks, a new plan to consolidate peace in Chiapas will be disclosed. Camacho Solis also said that right now the most important thing is continuing the dialogue, which he stressed is going well. This meeting resulted in the issuance of a communique by the Senate and signed not only by [words indistinct] but by (Rubi), which says that right now legislators view the negotiations favorably. Nevertheless, they stress the need to implement this peace program as quickly as possible and establish the necessary mechanisms to ensure that peace is achieved.
