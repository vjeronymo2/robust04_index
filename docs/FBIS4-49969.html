BFN [By Benjamin Yeh] [Text] Taipei, May 11 (CNA) -- The Taiwan High Court on Wednesday [11 May] upheld the nine-year jail term meted out to Shih Yuebo by the Taipei District Court earlier this year for hijacking a passenger airliner from Mainland China to Taiwan on Aug. 10. Shih pled not guilty, claiming he hijacked the plane to seek political asylum mere. Shih, 30, of Hebei Province, forced the Air China Boeing 767, which was en route from Beijing to Indonesia, to land in Taiwan after he threatened the crew with what he said was a bomb but that later turned out to be sulfuric acid in a bottle. Nobody was injured during the incident, the third of 11 hijackings of mainland Chinese planes to Taiwan last year.
