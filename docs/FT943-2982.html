940916 FT 16 SEP 94 / Improved GMTV avoids sanctions The Independent Television Commission decided yesterday not to impose sanctions on GMTV, the commercial television breakfast station, because there had been a 'demonstrable improvement' in its service. The ITC had warned that unless there was an improvement by this month formal sanctions might be imposed, which could include fines. As part of its review of GMTV's first year on air the ITC had judged its performance to have been unsatisfactory. The commission said there had been significant shortfalls in the required amount of news, current affairs and children's information. There was also concern about the quality of some programming. GMTV is owned by Granada, Carlton Communications, the Guardian Media Group, Scottish Television and the Walt Disney Company. Following the introduction in February of an early morning news programme produced by Reuters and the addition of other current affairs output, the ITC said yesterday it was satisfied that the company was meeting licence requirements. It added: 'Despite these improvements, the commission will be looking for further evidence of sustained quality in GMTV's children's programmes during the remainder of the year.'
