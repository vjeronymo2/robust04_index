921014 FT 14 OCT 92 / Banks row stifling Russian oil output A ROW between western bankers has blocked finance for desperately needed equipment to help Russia arrest its rapid decline in oil production. The European Bank of Reconstruction and Development has a plan to finance purchases of oil and gas equipment through credits from western commercial banks and the US Exim bank. Oil output has plunged from 570m tonnes in 1987 to about 395m this year and an estimated 340m-350m in 1993. However, the entire project has been held up by objections from the World Bank. The Russians aim to pay back the credits with revenue from the extra production which the new equipment will bring. However, under the World Bank's 'negative pledge' rule, it will not permit other lenders to be treated more favourably than it in the repayment of credits - and sees a pledge of future oil output to the EBRD, when no such pledge has been made to it, as more favourable treatment. The World Bank is providing more than Dollars 1bn of credits for various projects. Former Soviet president Mikhail Gorbachev was said last night to be planning a trip to Italy, after the Russian authorities lifted a travel ban to enable him to go to Germany for the funeral of former West German Chancellor Willy Brandt. Russia's constitutional court imposed the ban to try to force Mr Gorbachev to appear at hearings to decide the fate of the Communist party. The Russian Foreign Ministry said Mr Gorbachev's passport was only valid for travel to Germany.
