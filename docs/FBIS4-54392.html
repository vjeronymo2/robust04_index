BFN [Text] Sofia, April 27 (BTA) -- Bulgarian Prime Minister Lyuben Berov today submitted before parliament a declaration on the cabinet's programme by year's end. Providing income adjustments after prices nearly doubled in late March and early April and offering national security guarantees were pointed as the major tasks facing the cabinet at that particular moment. The declaration also proposed that deputy prime ministers be put in charge of the social policy, structural adjustment in economy and foreign policy, and a minister, on foreign investments. Prof. Berov's declaration was prompted by the demands of the pro- cabinet Bulgarian Socialist Party (BSP) which last week set April 25 as the deadline for the prime minister to come up with his proposals for changes in the social and economic departments of the cabinet and May 15 to submit a report on a new socioeconomic policy. The cabinet's first task is to restore the lost prices-incomes balance and curb the rising inflation, Prof. Berov told the MP's. He proposed that an interdepartmental council on prices and incomes headed by a deputy prime minister be set up with the Council of Ministers to this end. He further vowed to bring the trade unions back to the negotiating table within the framework of the Tripartite Council by the end of this week and said the cabinet is ready to start serious negotiations. Bulgaria's two major labour organizations backed out of the tripartite negotiations after the cabinet declared it freezes the salaries in the state-financed sector. Prof. Berov said a deputy prime minister should be appointed to coordinate and oversee the structural adjustment in economy. This step is aimed to overcome the cabinet's failure to make a headway with privatization, a situation which, "among other things, was brought about by a rift between the deputy prime minister in charge of privatization and the rest of the ministers", Prof. Berov's declaration read without naming any ministers. Privatization- related matters are now within the powers of Deputy Prime Minister Valentin Karabashev. A second deputy prime minister is needed to take over the foreign policy issues and coordinate the work of the different institutions in this sphere, according to the prime minister. He also said it is necessary to appoint a minister on trade and another one to take the lead of the Foreign Debt Management Committee and control the foreign investments. Prime Minister Berov told the MP's he was considering changes in the Defence Ministry leadership. An updated programme will be submitted within two weeks to specify the cabinet's activity in two stages: the first one ending in September and the second one, with year's end. It will also specify the structural and personnel changes to be made in the cabinet. An attempt of the opposition Union of the Democratic Forces (SDS) to put Prof. Berov's declaration to debates was voted down by the other parliamentary factions. SDS floor leader Stefan Savov asked what this declaration in fact sought to achieve, saying it proposed no specific personnel or structural changes, all the more that the cabinet had already had one programme on which it was formed in late 1992. Mr. Savov further reiterated SDS' stand that this Cabinet can no longer remain in office and cabinet overhauls would do no good. "The assessments Prof. Berov's declaration makes are identical with those of the Socialist Party but what matters here is the prime minister's actions and the effect they will have," BSP floor leader Zhan Videnov said. According to Mr. Videnov, the personality of the those to enter the cabinet is of primary importance. He gave the cabinet a couple of weeks' time to make the effect of changes perceptible. "We expected to see a more specific programme and proposals to underlie the forthcoming structural and personnel changes in the cabinet," ethnic Turks' Movement for Rights and Freedoms spokesman Yunal Lyutfi told a radio correspondent. Defence Minister Valentin Aleksandrov, whom the declaration indirectly pointed to in connection with future changes among senior Defence Ministry officials, said in the parliamentary lobbies he did not intend to resign adding that Prime Minister Berov was obviously subject to political pressure. The MP's accepted a SDS motion that parliament tomorrow discuss the foreign-currency reserves and the cabinet's foreign-currency policy.
