BFN [Excerpt] Tokyo, May 16 KYODO -- The schedule for the July summit of the Group of Seven (G-7) economic powers in Naples has now taken shape, Foreign Ministry sources said Monday [16 May]. The sources said that according to a provisional schedule worked out among the sherpas, or the personal representatives of the G-7 leaders, the summit leaders will begin their dialogue with a working dinner on the evening of July 8. The leaders will consider economic issues in a formal session on July 9, a day which will also see the release of an economic declaration, as well as separate meetings among the foreign ministers and the finance ministers of the G-7. Russian President Boris Yeltsin will make his first appearance at the Naples summit on the evening of July 9, when he attends a banquet. On July 10, Yeltsin will join in political deliberations among the G-7 leaders on specific topics such as the former Yugoslavia, the Middle East and Eastern Europe. But the sources said there remain other topics to be discussed only among the G-7 countries themselves. Later in the day, the G-7 countries will issue a chairman's summary and a political declaration. Along with Russia, the economic powers will also issue a political statement regarding the former Yugoslavia. In the case of previous summits, the Kremlin leader only joined the G-7 leaders after their talks were already finished for a special "G-7-plus-one" session. [passage omitted]
