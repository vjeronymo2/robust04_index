920527 FT 27 MAY 92 / UK Company News: Drayton English decreases DRAYTON ENGLISH and International Trust has seen its unquoted portfolio suffer badly from the UK recession, leading to a number of write-offs and provisions. Its net asset value fell from 108.4p to 66p over the 12 months ended April 5 1992, although that had improved to 71.5p by the end of April. Gross income for 1991-92 dropped from Pounds 6.07m to Pounds 4.4m, and net revenue from Pounds 2.69m to Pounds 1.69m. Earnings per share were 0.71p (3.16p) and the final dividend is cut to 0.6p (2.3p) for a total of 1.2p (3.2p). Resources were being focused into the UK quoted smaller company sector, which currently accounted for 60 per cent of the portfolio. Two other Drayton trusts have also reported their results: Drayton Recovery, which began trading last October, produced gross revenue of Pounds 931,000 for the period to April 30 1992, and net revenue of Pounds 594,000 for earnings per share of 7.19p. An interim dividend of 2p is declared; a special of 2p has already been paid. Net asset value at April 30 was 98.9p. Drayton Blue Chip, which began trading in March 1991, finished with gross revenue of Pounds 1.8m for the period ended March 31 1992. Net income reached Pounds 1.12m for earnings per share of 8.88p, and the final dividend is 5.495p to make 8.4. Over the six months to March 31 1992, net asset value fell from 89.4p to 60.5p.
