Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Russia regards the NATO program Partnership for Peace as a phase in the establishment of a new collective defense system in Europe, Russian Defense Minister, Pavel Grachev, emphasized at a meeting of the military command in Moscow on Friday [25 March]. Grachev said Russia did not intend to integrate with NATO military structures but would seek more cooperation with the alliance. Speaking on the participation of Russia in the NATO program, Grachev stated that Russia would seek the appropriate status for itself according to its position and role. He stressed that any attempts to resolve European problems would have no future without Russia. Development of the Russian Armed Forces during the first three months of this year was considered at the meeting. The military command also discussed issues concerning military and mobilization preparations of the army, accommodation of Russian servicemen withdrawn from other states and financing of the Armed Forces. The Russian Defense Ministry's board will meet in Moscow on March 26 to discuss issues on the levy.
