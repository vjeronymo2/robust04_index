920311 FT 11 MAR 92 / CBI rejects plans for Scotland THE Confederation of British Industry in Scotland, which last month said it wanted improvements in the way in which Scotland is governed, has decided it likes none of the proposals from political parties for constitutional change north of the border. Mr Alasdair MacCallum, chairman of the CBI in Scotland, said there was 'no evidence to suggest that the proposed alternatives for governing Scotland offer any improvement in the stability and confidence which are necessary to improve the indigenous industrial base'. Neither independence nor devolution would 'stimulate Scottish companies to compete on price and quality in international markets or to secure higher levels of inward investment', Mr MacCallum added. The aims of Scottish business were likely to be best achieved by 'an improvement in the current administrative arrangements within the UK'. Mr McCallum said after a meeting of the council of the CBI in Scotland last month that constitutional change was inevitable. Last night Mr David MacLehose, the CBI's Scottish director, said administrative improvements favoured by the the CBI included a single tier of local government.
