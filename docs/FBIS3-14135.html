Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Ba'labakk, Lebanon, March 22 (AFP)--Syrian soldiers arrested at least three people carrying weapons in this eastern al-Biqa' Valley town on Tuesday [22 March] amid a security crackdown, witnesses here said. A Syrian military source meanwhile declared illegal any attempt to carry weapons in the town. Witnesses said the soldiers set up checkpoints in Ba'labakk, searching cars and arresting anyone with weapons. The measure came 10 days after some 300 armed members of the Iranian-backed Hezbollah movement deployed in Ba'labakk as part of a demonstration by thousands of people in support of Jerusalem Day, in response to a call by Iran. Lebanon's military prosecutor Nasri Lahhud denounced the presence of armed Hezbollah militants and issued warrants for their arrest, saying they had violated the law. Some 35,000 Syrian troops are deployed in Lebanon.
