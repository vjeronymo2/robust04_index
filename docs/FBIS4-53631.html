BFN [From MAINICHI SHIMBUN 21 May Morning Edition] [Text] Prime Minister Tsutomu Hata touched on the outlook for economic recovery at a 20 May House of Councilors Budget Committee session and said: "We would like to watch the economic situation carefully while hoping to see a certain, clear sign of recovery this fall or after." Prime Minister Hata indicated that the economy would be on the track for full-fledged recovery as early as fall. In reply to Diet interpellations, government officials have so far reiterated that "the economy is expected to be on track for full-fledged recovery by the end of fiscal 1994" (as stated by Yoshio Terasawa, director general of the Economic Planning Agency). Prime Minister Hata's remark was the first government view that this fall is the goal of full-fledged economic recovery. Touching on concrete measures to stimulate the sluggish economy, Prime Minister Hata also stated that "it is important to deal with" the execution of the budget "while studying the possibility of front-loading spending" after the Diet passes the budget. Moreover, he stressed the need for promotion of deregulation and the stability in the yen's exchange rate to the dollar.
