BFN [Text] Seoul, June 11 KYODO -- The foreign ministers of Japan and South Korea confirmed Saturday that planned U.N. Security Council sanctions against North Korea over the nuclear standoff are aimed at encouraging Pyongyang to hold dialogue for a negotiated solution. The confirmation came at a meeting between Japanese Foreign Minister Koji Kakizawa and his South Korean counterpart Han Sung-chu held in Seoul, officials said. The two sides also pledged close bilateral cooperation in dealing with North Korea's suspected nuclear arms development program.
