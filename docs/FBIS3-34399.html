Language: Czech Article Type:BFN [Text] In Jerusalem today, Czech Prime Minister Vaclav Klaus and Israeli Prime Minister Yitzhaq Rabin signed a joint statement on the promotion of economic relations between Israel and the Czech Republic. According to Prime Minister Klaus, the document carries a decision on immediate preparation for a further step toward the liberalization of bilateral trade. This step should result in an agreement on a free-trade zone between the Czech Republic and Israel in the very near future. At the same time, the Czech Republic and Israel have succeeded in concluding the two-year long discussion on the text of the bilateral agreement on the protection of investment and on profit repatriation. At a news conference, Prime Minister Klaus returned to the possibility of Czech arms exports to the so-called sensitive countries. He repeated that arms exports were carried out on the basis of a government license and in line with the law.
