BFN [Text] Washington, June 8 (XINHUA) -- The World Bank approved a loan of 380 million U.S. dollars here today to support the national highway project in China. The loan, the bank said, will be used to build the 340 kilometer superhighway between Shijiazhuang and Xinxiang in central China, which is part of the divided, four-lane Beijing-Shenzhen expressway. The part of highway supported by the bank, according to senior bank transport specialist Hatim Hajj, is one of the most heavily traveled corridors in China. Highway traffic in China grew 14.1 percent between 1982 and 1992. China is now planning to construct a 25,000 kilometer national highway system equivalent to the U.S. interstate system, the bank said. Bank lending to the highway sector in China since 1981 totals over one billion dollars.
