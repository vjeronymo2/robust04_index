Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tirana, January 6 (ATA) -- As reported financial police has discovered cases of contraband fuels to Montenegro through Shkodra Lake. Recently the police detected cases of smuggling fuels into Montenegro through Buna River flowing from Shkodra Lake into the Adriatic. Major Muhamet Gradeci, head of Shkodra Financial Police, told ATA that the police in cooperation with police of order, discovered December 21, 1993 in the coastal village of Pulaj near Buna, 30 kilometers from Shkodra, pipes which were used for smuggling fuels from Albania into Montenegro. The pipes were some 20 meters long and connected the coasts of Buna. The pipe line is larger than the one discovered a month ago almost in the same place, Major Gradeci said and added that the other pipe line was some 10 centimeters broader and operated through pumps which carried fuels from tankers into Montenegro. Mr. Gradeci said he had suspicion that other pipes might exist in other areas of Buna River and added that financial police has been working to discover them. The pipe lines have been installed by peasants of nearby zone who transport fuels which is provided to them by smugglers. The pipes were destroyed right after they were discovered but this was done only in the Albanian territory. Smuggling with fuels is result of cooperation among Albanian and Montenegrin smugglers. Financial police is working to cut the threads of fuels smuggling. A private fuels station in Velipoja, near Buna River flow, has been a good shelter for smugglers for a long time. Financial police has already blocked the station and every tanker which passes this way is suspicious of smuggling. Mr. Gradeci explained that 50 per cent of condraband fuels is carried out through the fuels station in Velipoja. Attempts to smuggle with fuels increased especially by the end of last year and the beginning of the new year. Financial police and that of public order blocked December 30 evening 11 tankers loaded with oil and the amount of fuels was not usual. After verification of documents the police controlled the destination the fuels were headed to. On January 4, 1994, the police took possession of lorries and cars carrying 130 barrels of fuels. Half of the barrels carried kerosene which is not allowed to be transported by citizens but only through vehicles having the authorization of the government.
