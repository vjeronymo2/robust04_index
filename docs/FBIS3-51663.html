Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities in Brazil monitored through 20 January. Tax revenues were $4.541 billion in December, $1.009 billion more than in December 1992. Federal government revenues totalled $46.3 billion in 1993, an increase of 25.8 percent compared with 1992. Average monthly revenues were $792 million than in 1992. (Sao Paulo GAZETA MERCANTIL in Portuguese 10 Jan 94 p 3) On 12 January, Brazil shipped 13,000 metric tons of orange juice to Japan. This represents 81 percent of the 16,000 metric tons exported by Brazilian industries to Japan in the second half of 1993. This is the second shipment to the port built to receive the product. The port was inaugurated in April 1992 when Brazil sent the first shipment of 13,000 metric tons. (Sao Paulo GAZETA MERCANTIL in Portuguese 12 Jan 94 p 15) The Brazilian Coffee Exporters Federation reported on 11 January that 1993 coffee bean exports totalled 15,083,499 sacks valued at $988.46 million, an increase of 5.5 percent compared with 1992. (Sao Paulo GAZETA MERCANTIL in Portuguese 12 Jan 94 p 15) A study by the State Companies Coordination Department shows that the five major state-owned companies failed to transfer $1.8 billion in dividends to the federal government in 1992. Only $233 million of the $2.081 billion estimated by the government were transferred to the Treasury. (Sao Paulo FOLHA DE SAO PAULO in Portuguese 12 Jan 94 Section 1 p 9) A report delivered on 12 January by Central Bank President Pedro Malan to the Monetary Policy Subcommission, which analyzes the economic plan, shows that in 1993 banks had profits of $8.9 billion, 2 percent of the GDP, because of inflation. (Rio de Janeiro O GLOBO in Portuguese 13 Jan 94 p 19)
