Language: Japanese Article Type:BFN [Text] On 11 January, Foreign Minister Tsutomu Hata rejected U.S. demands in the framework trade talks that Japan try to increase the share of foreign products and foreign firms in its domestic market to the level of that in other major industrialized (G-7) nations. At a news conference following a regular cabinet meeting the same day, Hata said: "It is senseless to say that it is unfair that certain products are not selling well in a certain country although they are selling well in other countries. It is not proper to look at one figure and insist that things ought to be a certain way."
