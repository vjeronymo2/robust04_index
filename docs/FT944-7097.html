941126 FT 26 NOV 94 / Court reprieve for Tapie Mr Bernard Tapie, the French businessman-politician, yesterday won a few days' reprieve when the Paris commercial court postponed to Monday its examination of whether his companies should be placed under court administration, a possible first step to bankruptcy. Credit Lyonnais, Mr Tapie's main banker, forced the issue last week by blocking the account of Bernard Tapie Finance, the holding companies which controls La Vie Claire, a chain of health food shops, and Testut and Terraillon, which makes industrial weighing machines.
