Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] A Chinese delegation has arrived in Amman for trade talks on bolstering commercial exchanges between the two countries. The delegation, led by deputy chief of the Chinese Council for International Trade Development, Ye Peng, will meet private and public sector economists. The trade balance between the two countries is still in China's favor. Jordan's imports of Chinese goods surged from $29 million in 1991 to $53 million in 1992. Jordanian exports to China, mainly potash, rose from $18 million in 1990 to 32.5 million dinars [as heard] in 1991.
