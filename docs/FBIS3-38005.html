Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Text] Grigoriy Karasin, director of the Information and Press Department of the Russian Foreign Ministry, expressed confidence that the West would support the Russian President's idea of holding a meeting with the leaders of the United States, Britain, France, and Germany on the Yugoslav conflict. He stated this during a conversation with journalists in Moscow, RIA reports. Karasin noted that at present, Russia thinks that the main thing in the search for a settlement in Yugoslavia is not to lose impetus. As has already been reported, this week the Russian president invited the leaders of the United States, Britain, France, and Germany to hold a joint meeting. It should result in the signing of a joint document that would put an end to bloodshed in the former Yugoslavia.
