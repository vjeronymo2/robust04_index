BFN [Text] Buenos Aires, 13 Apr (NA) -- Cuban Foreign Minister Roberto Robaina stressed this afternoon that the debt his country has not yet settled with Argentina "is not an issue that has been frozen." In this regard, he expressed his government's willingness to seek alternative repayment methods. The diplomat, who is paying a 48-hour official visit to Argentina, also denied negotiations for a meeting between President Carlos Menem and Cuban leader Fidel Castro during an upcoming Ibero-American summit in Cartagena de Indias. In remarks made in the Senate, Robaina added: "The possibilities are always open. It depends on the activities each one of them has during the summit. My visit's goal, however, is not to prepare a meeting. Thus far, there is nothing on this issue."
