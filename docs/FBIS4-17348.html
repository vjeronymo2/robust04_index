BFN [Text] The National Salvation Front [NSF] insists on early presidential elections, NSF leader Ilya Konstantinov told his organization's National Council in Moscow on Sunday. He said that the National Salvation Front would sign the Memorandum on Civic Accord only if this demand was fulfilled. Ilya Konstantinov, Gennadiy Sayenko, Mikhail Astafyev and Mikhail Titov criticized the Civic Accord pact and announced that they would not sign it, although they would take part in its discussion on April 26. Konstantinov also said that the NSF's main task now was to find and support a candidate representing the opposition. Member of the NSF's Political Council Sazhi Umalatova stated that former Soviet prime minister Nikolay Ryzhkov would suit all opposition forces. Members of the NSF's National Council sharply criticized the government and president.
