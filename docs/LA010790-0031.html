January 7, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

SHADOW OF A DOUBT

It is interesting that much is made of the Johnsons' class status. Would this story have been done if Mr. Johnson had been a welder instead of a "professional"? A Mexican-American immigrant gardener? The fact that the Johnsons own a home with a view where they have lovely barbecues for their friends only serves to call attention to what is true in many dysfunctional families (and which seemed very important to Siegel): Appearance is everything. Although I feel certain that Siegel knows that sexual abuse of children knows no class or economic boundary, the maintenance of Mr. Johnson's innocence seems to hinge largely on his professional status and his good income (and his own businessman-style aggressiveness).

PATRICIA HUNT

El Segundo
