Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Cairo, 27 Mar (INA)--Iraqi Foreign Minister Muhammad Sa'id al-Sahhaf met with Egyptian Foreign Minister 'Amr Musa late last night. The two ministers discussed the latest Arab developments and exchanged views on the topics listed on the agenda of the 101st session of the Arab League Council, particularly those related to enhancing joint Arab action. An INA correspondent in Cairo said al-Sahhaf briefed his Egyptian counterpart on the latest developments between Iraq and the UN Security Council and the results of the latest review [of sanctions], which echoed positive attitudes by some Security Council members toward Iraq. Dr. Nabil Najm, Iraq's permanent representative to the Arab League, and Dr. Muhammad Nu'man Jalal, Egypt's permanent Arab League representative, attended the meeting. Muhammad Sa'id al-Sahhaf also met this evening with Arab League Secretary General Dr. 'Ismat 'Abd-al-Majid. Talks dealt with the latest Arab developments, the Arab League agenda, and effective means to reactivate joint Arab cooperation in order to confront the challenges facing the Arab nation. The INA correspondent in Cairo said the secretary general hoped the Security Council and other states will understand Iraq's position and lift the embargo as soon as possible in order to end the suffering of the Iraqi Arab people. Dr. Nabil Najm, Iraq's permanent representative to the Arab League, attended the meeting.
