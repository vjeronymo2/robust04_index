Language: Hungarian Article Type:BFN [Text] A total of 129 employees of Hungarian Radio have been dismissed or pensioned off. According to a statement by [Hungarian Radio Vice President] Laszlo Csucs, their names will not be published to respect their personal rights. In his statement issued by MTI, the vice president supported the idea of unbiased public service activity. We quote: The transformation of the system is under way in Hungary and Hungarian Radio cannot avoid this either. The State Audit Office's investigation, which was carried out in 1992, drew top officials' attention to several shortcomings, though it found financial matters in order. Hungarian Radio prepared a plan of measures that included a plan to reduce overly high staff numbers, which stood at over 2,000 people. According to Csucs's statement, decisions on redundancies were not based on political, but professional and ethical criteria; that is, Hungarian Radio dismissed those who did not meet these criteria, the vice president's statement reads.
