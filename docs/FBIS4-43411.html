BFN [Announcer-read report over video] [Text] Prime Minister Tansu Ciller has said that all the targets envisaged by the 5 April economic measures have been met so far and the rate of inflation has started to drop fast. The prime minister visited the Turkish Union of Chambers and Stock Exchanges and met with Yalim Erez, the chairman of the Union, and other officials. In a statement before the meeting, Prime Minister Ciller said a large portion of the work envisaged by the 5 April economic measures has been carried out. She pointed out that the measures received great support, both from international organizations and from the domestic public. The first positive results are being received, she said, adding that the mechanism is now at work that will seriously improve the exports that had reached a deadlock and the current transactions deficit that was getting larger and larger. As a result of a series of measures that had to be taken, Ciller explained that, on the one hand, while the capital and interest on loans are being paid, one the other hand, getting further loans is being kept at a minimum. In this way, she said,the amount of money paid in interest from tax revenues has dropped significantly for the first time since 1987.. The prime minister pointed out that the budget deficit, which had reached 32 trillion Turkish lira, was 4.5 trillion lira last month. This drop will continue, she stressed.
