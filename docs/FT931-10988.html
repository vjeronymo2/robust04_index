930205 FT 05 FEB 93 / Major refuses consideration of aid for Daf MR JOHN MAJOR yesterday brushed aside calls for government financial aid to Leyland Daf as 'simply not realistic' in spite of mounting pressure from Labour to rescue the truck maker, Ivo Dawnay writes. During angry exchanges in the Commons Mr Major refused to make any commitment to match any intervention in the company from the Netherlands or Belgium. He told Mr John Smith, the Labour leader, that the receivers had made clear that parts of the business could be saved without state funding. 'I believe that is the way to proceed,' he said. Mr Smith refused to accept the decision, arguing that it was 'vital' to British manufacturing industry that the company should not be allowed to collapse. Earlier the cabinet had heard a report from Mr Michael Heseltine, the trade and industry secretary, on the company.
