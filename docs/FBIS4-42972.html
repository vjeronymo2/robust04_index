BFN [Text] Foreign Minister Alekos Mikhailidhis will go to New York this afternoon to brief the UN secretary general and the members of the Security Council on the positions of the Cypriot Government on the Cyprus issue. In a statement after meeting President Glavkos Kliridhis this morning, Mikhailidhis said his meetings with the Security Council members will begin on Monday [13 June], adding that he expects to meet Butrus-Ghali on Thursday or Friday. He said the goal of these meetings is to convey the positions of the Cypriot Government on the current state of the Cyprus issue as it has evolved since the UN secretary general submitted his report. Mikhailidhis added: It is well known that negotiations are being held among the Security Council permanent members on future steps for the Cyprus issue. On his way to New York, the foreign minister may stop in Athens to meet his Greek counterpart, Karolos Papoulias. Mikhailidhis said he and Papoulias will exchange views on the handling of the Cyprus issue. He will also meet Greek Foreign Ministry officials to discuss the European Union-Cyprus fourth financial protocol.
