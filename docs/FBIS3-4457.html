Language: Chinese Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, 26 Mar (XINHUA) -- The 19th session of the Sino-Portuguese Joint Liaison Group has released a press communique. The full text reads: The 19th session of the Sino-Portuguese Joint Liaison Group took place in Beijing 22-25 March. The two sides had a long discussion on issues concerning the localization of civil servants, the official status of the Chinese Language, and translation and localization of laws; and studied the progress in dealing with these issues. The two sides signed the minutes of talks concerning the aviation agreements which the Macao Government had reached with the Governments of Belgium, Luxembourg, and the Netherlands; and they underscored the significance of these agreements for the future Macao international airport. The Portuguese side furnished the Chinese side for study the text of the aviation agreement which Portugal had initialled with Brazil. With regard to international organizations, the two sides took note of the requests about Macao's access to the World Organization of Meteorology, which the governments of the two countries had officially submitted in Geneva. The two sides also took note that the International Association of Navigation Marks had revised its charter, and the two sides reaffirmed their attention to the process Macao will go through in joining the organization. The two sides also studied the progress made by the working panel of the Bank of China and the Atlantic Bank on their participation in issuing currency, and instructed the panel to proceed with its work. The two sides also discussed major issues relevant to Macao's economic development and social stability, as well as the issue concerning the nationality of some residents in Macao. The meeting proceeded in an amicable and cooperative atmosphere. The 20th session of the PRC-Portuguese Liaison Group will take place in Lisbon beginning 5 July.
