941014 FT 14 OCT 94 / Observer: Fringe benefits Much to the alarm of Tory officials at the conference in Bournemouth, they were asked to sanction a fringe meeting entitled 'Let's kill all the lawyers'. The sponsor - the Law Society - explained that the offensive words were a quotation from Shakespeare's Henry VI, part two; so that's all right. Other useful Shakespeare quotes: 'Bump off all the accountants' (Measure for Measure); 'See off all the ad men' (Love's Labour's Lost); 'Let's see how much these quangos really cost' (The Winter's Tale); and 'Portillo's for the chop' (King John).
