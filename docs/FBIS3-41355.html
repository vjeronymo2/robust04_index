Language: English Article Type:CSO [Article: "Narcotics Worth Rs 10 Crore Seized"] [Text] Bombay, January 4: The Narcotics Cell of the Bombay police arrested nine persons and seized heroin, mandrax and hashish worth Rs 10.50 crores and recovered a mandrax-making unit. A police inspector, Mr Daljeet Parmar, received information that Mohamad Yunus (30) was bringing drugs between 4 p.m. and 4.30 p.m. near Ganesh Bhavan, 3rd Pasta Lane, Colaba, on New Year's Day. He was arrested by the police with 18 kg of hashish, which was in the shape of balls and sticks and was of Indian origin. In the second case, a total of 28 kg of mandrax tablets were seized by another inspector, Mr Pradeep Sharma, and his staff. Initially, 10 kg of mandrax tablets were seized from Shaikh Akhtar Shaikh (32) and Sayed Javed Abdul Aziz Sayed (28). They revealed that they had made the mandrax tablets at a factory in Khirani Road, Saki Naka. This place was raided and 18 kg more of mandrax tablets and 10 kg of methaqulone and a tabletting and drying machine were seized. In the third case, three alleged women drug-traffickers, Phullu Farida Sayyed Ahmed, (27), Akhtari Begam Mulshidas Parulkar (40) and Almari Marya Kajitan Denis (35) were arrested with Hanif Mohamed Fakir (32), and 2 kg of heroin seized.
