BFN [Report by ITAR-TASS and KAZTAG special correspondents] [Text] Tokyo 8 April, TASS -- The West "should help Russia in the process of reforms, democratisation and stabilisation, nor for remarks by concrete politicians," President Nursultan Nazarbayev of Kazakhstan told a news conference here on Friday. [sentence as received] Both Moscow and all its neighbours in the east and west are interested in the success of Russian transformations, emphasised Nazarbayev, who is currentlty in Japan on an official visit. Nazarbayev believes that there is nothing surprising in the complex processes taking place in the former Soviet Union. The majority of current problems have been brought about by the disintegration of the Soviet Union's economic system and break-up of the common ruble area, and should have been foreseen by politicians who were busy producing a Commonwealth of Independent States in Belovezhskaya Pushcha forest, he said.
