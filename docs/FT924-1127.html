921219 FT 19 DEC 92 / International Company News: BA warning over US jobs BRITISH Airways yesterday accused Washington of putting nearly 100,000 US jobs at risk by linking its Dollars 750m offer for 44 per cent of USAir to liberalisation of the UK-US aviation market. The warning coincided with a last minute effort by Mr John Major, the prime minister, to win Washington's approval for the deal during weekend talks with President George Bush. Sir Colin Marshall, BA's chief executive, claimed the US government's apparent determination to make approval of the bid conditional on UK liberalisation was 'entirely to the benefit of the three major US airlines'. American Airlines, United Airlines and Delta Air Lines have led the campaign to block the BA-USAir deal unless the US government can secure greater access for US carriers into the London market. Sir Colin said BA hoped the bid would be approved. But BA was disappointed it appeared to hinge on bilateral issues which had no connection with the commercial deal between the two airlines. 'For the US government, the upholders of belief in freedom of capital and freedom of movement, to be rejecting this deal, which clearly puts at risk the future of USAir, its 47,000 employees, and a similar number who are providers of services and products, boggles the mind.'
