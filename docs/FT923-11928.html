920722 FT 22 JUL 92 / International Company News: Insurance side spurs Sears, Roebuck SEARS, Roebuck, the US retail and financial services group, increased second-quarter net income by 15.5 per cent to Dollars 345.8m from Dollars 299.4m a year ago. The company's insurance, brokerage and real estate divisions all gained solidly during the quarter, but its struggling merchandise group suffered a 50 per cent drop in income to Dollars 78.5m from Dollars 156.9m. Merchandise group revenues fell 1.1 per cent to Dollars 7.57bn from Dollars 7.65bn. Per share earnings for the period ending June 30 were 90 cents, against 87 cents last year, reflecting the dilutive effect of a stock offering. Revenues rose 1.3 per cent to Dollars 14.27bn from Dollars 14.09bn. Mr Edward Brennan, Sears chairman, said the slower pace of the US economic recovery and intense retail competition resulted in lower gross margins. The merchandise group also lost revenues following a scandal involving its automotive repair shops and accusations of unneeded repairs. The matter is being investigated in several states and Sears has had to expend considerable funds on advertising to explain its side of the story. The merchandise group's results were also affected by decreases in and changes to the company's catalogue business, including a Dollars 20.2m pre-tax expenses to close catalogue pick-up counters in its stores. Second-quarter income at Allstate Insurance rose 68.3 per cent to Dollars 235.3m from Dollars 139.8 last year. Revenues were up 4.4 per cent to Dollars 5.02bn from Dollars 4.81bn. The Dean Witter Financial Services Group reported income of Dollars 97.9m, up 8.1 per cent from Dollars 90.6m. Revenues were up 4.9 per cent to Dollars 1.28bn from Dollars 1.22bn. Coldwell Banker, which handles residential real estate, reported income of Dollars 10.3m, turning round a year-ago loss of Dollars 3.1m. Revenues were up 4 per cent to Dollars 455m from Dollars 437.7m. For the first six months, consolidated net income rose 24.4 per cent to Dollars 667.6m from Dollars 536.6m last year, partly reflecting the company's success in cost cutting. Earnings per share were Dollars 1.79 against Dollars 1.56.
