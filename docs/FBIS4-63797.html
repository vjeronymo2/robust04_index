BFN [From "News Bulletin" No. 506] [Text] Vilnius, 24 May, ELTA -- President Algirdas Brazauskas sent on Tuesday [24 May] a congratulation telegram to the newly elected President of Germany Roman Herzog. "I would like to take this opportunity to convey my heartfelt congratulations on the victory in the elections of the president for the first time held in the unified Germany. I earnestly hope the relations between our two countries will further expand under Your Excellency's good offices. I wish you success and the best of luck," Brazauskas said in his telegram. Brazauskas also sent a telegram to the former German President Richard von Weizsacker. He said in it: "On the occasion, I would like to thank you for the attention and understanding shown to our state. ``Germany, one of the largest European states, did not refuse to help Lithuania during the crucial historical period. Traces of your last-year visit in Lithuania have remained significant in strengthening the democracy, tolerance, mutual understanding of various nations. ``I am sure the newly elected President Roman Herzog will be a worthy successor and relations between Lithuania and Germany will be further developed. ``I hope you will not forget Lithuania in future. Therefore, I would like to invite you as my personal guest to visit our country once more,'' Brazauskas said.
