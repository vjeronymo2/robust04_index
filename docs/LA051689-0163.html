May 16, 1989, Tuesday, P.M. Final

HORSE TRAINER, IN PRISON FOR MURDER, DIES OF CANCER

Howard (Buddy) Jacobson, a one-time top horse trainer convicted of killing his former girlfriend's lover in a highly publicized New York City trial, died early today.

Jacobson, 58, died at Erie County Medical Center where he had been under treatment for cancer, said a spokesman for Attica state prison, where Jacobson had been serving his sentence.

Top Horse Trainer

Jacobson, the top horse trainer in the United States in 1963, also accumulated a fortune in modeling agency and real estate ventures.

He was serving 25 years to life for second-degree murder in the Aug. 6, 1978, killing of John Tupper. Prosecutors claimed that Jacobson killed Tupper in jealousy over Tupper's affair with model Melanie Cain.

Tupper, 33, was shot six times, stabbed and beaten. His body was found in a dump in the Bronx.

Cain, who appeared on the covers of several national magazines, including Cosmopolitan and Red Book, had lived with Jacobson for four years, but the relationship ended 22 days before Tupper's death.

Jacobson was convicted in 1980.

In May, 1980, he escaped from a Brooklyn jail by changing places with a man posing as his lawyer. He was captured 40 days later in Manhattan Beach, Calif.
