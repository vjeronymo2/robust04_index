930621 FT 21 JUN 93 / Construction Contracts: Improving the coastal environment ALFRED McALPINE SERVICES AND PIPELINES has been awarded contracts worth Pounds 20m since April. The largest of the contracts is worth Pounds 15m and involves the construction of foul and storm water outfalls for Southern Water Services at east Worthing. The work is being carried out to comply with an European Community directive for the improvement of the coastal environment. On completion in December the foul water outfall will be one of the longest in Europe. British Gas has awarded Alfred McAlpine two contracts with a total value of Pounds 5m. The first, worth Pounds 3m, is to lay a 900mm diameter overland pipeline between the gas offtake installation at Huntingdon and two new installations to be constructed as part of these works at St Neots and Little Barford in Cambridgeshire. The work is due to be completed in October. The second is for the construction of a 9km long pipeline connecting Horndon on the Hill to the gas offtake installation at Tilbury in Essex.
