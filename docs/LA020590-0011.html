February 5, 1990, Monday, Home Edition

SAVING HISTORIC LEONIS ADOBE

I would like to clarify some of the statements made in Al Martinez's column "An Itch in Old Calabasas" (Jan. 16) regarding the historic Leonis Adobe and Caltrans' proposal for the Valley Circle Boulevard interchange with the Ventura Freeway.

None of the historic Leonis Adobe property is in any way threatened by Caltrans' proposal.

No property will be taken from either the National Register property (which contains the Adobe, barn and other structures) or from the adjacent Leonis Adobe Assn. property.

The present right-of-way line that runs between the Ventura Freeway and a portion of the Leonis Adobe Assn.'s property (containing the corral, the adobe area, and part of a nearby restaurant's parking lot) will be maintained.

The proposed project would require approximately 0.5 acres of property held by the Leonis Adobe Assn. Most of the property in question is located between Calabasas Creek and the Valley Circle Boulevard/Mulholland Drive intersection.

Caltrans is working closely with the Leonis Adobe Assn. on plans to construct a sound wall along the present right-of-way line to reduce the level of freeway noise in and around the adobe structure.

Caltrans is also working on plans to enhance the area with new landscaping and to relocate and replant some of the existing oak trees located on the property.

JERRY B. BAXTER

Caltrans District Director

Los Angeles
