October 15, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

JUST RELEASED IN PAPER

FICTION

PALM LATITUDES by Kate Braverman (Penguin: $8.95). The fateful existence of three Latina women in a Los Angeles barrio.

HEAVEN'S PRISONER by James Lee Burke (Pocket: $3.99). A plane crash in Louisiana thrusts Vietnam vet, ex-detective, ex-drinker Dave Robicheaux into an unwanted morass.

GREEN CITY IN THE SUN by Barbara Wood (Fawcett Gold Medal: $4.95). Dr. Deborah Treverton is summoned back to Africa to settle the family coffee business -- and a medicine woman's curse.

STORIES IN AN ALMOST CLASSICAL MODE by Harold Brodkey (Vintage: $12.95). Eighteen stories written over 30 years about the contradictions within families.

WHEN I AM AN OLD WOMAN I SHALL WEAR PURPLE: An Anthology of Short Stories and Poetry, edited by Sandra Martz (Papier-Mache Press: $10). Writings about the later seasons in a woman's life by daughters, sons and significant others.

NONFICTION

CHILD STAR: An Autobiography by Shirley Temple Black (Warner: $5.95). Once America's tiniest star, Black reflects on her gigantic impact.

DEADLY MEDICINE by Kelly Moore and Dan Reed (St. Martin's: $4.95). Genene Jones seemed to be the perfect nurse, always there in an emergency -- until she was convicted of 30 cases of infanticide.

BROTHERS: Black and Poor -- A True Story of Courage and Survival by Sylvester Monroe and Peter Goldman (Ballantine: $4.95.) Newsweek correspondent returns to his childhood home, Chicago's Taylor Court projects, and discovers a microcosm of the plight of black men in America.

THE CLUSTERING OF AMERICA by Michael J. Weiss (Perennial: $10.95). You are not where you live, but your zip code can reveal a lot about you.

SELF-HELP / REFERENCETHE OLD FARMER'S 1989 ALMANAC by Robert B. Thomas (Old Farmer's Almanac: $2.95) Since 1792, this little book has presented an eclectic mix of planting tables, weather forecasts, recipes and astrological secrets.

LIFE AFTER THE CITY: A Harrow-smith Guide to Rural Living by Charles Long (Camden House: $12.95). Ruminating chapters on earning a living, transition strategies, social adjustments, and maintaining friendships.

VERY LA: The Native's Guide to the Best of Los Angeles by David Hoffman (Chronicle: $9.95). If you decide to stay, here's how to make the experience a little more enjoyable.
