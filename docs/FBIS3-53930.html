Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Cairo, 20 Jan (INA)--Iraq has once again protested to the Arab League over the continued violations of the Iraqi airspace by U.S., British, and French aircraft, which are provided with facilities by the rulers of Saudi Arabia and Kuwait. These violations pose a grave threat to Iraq's security, stability, and national unity. In a letter to Arab League Secretary General Dr. 'Ismat 'Abd-al- Majid, Iraq's permanent envoy said on 14, 15, and 16 January, these aircraft continued their violations of the Iraqi airspace and conducted 129 hostile sorties by flying from bases in Saudi Arabia and Kuwait. The Iraqi envoy asked that the letter be distributed to the Arab member states. A list detailing these violations was enclosed.
