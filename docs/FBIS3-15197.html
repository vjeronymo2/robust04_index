Language: Turkish Article Type:BFN [Text] The Russian Army is reported to have entered Azerbaijani territory this morning. Russia is said to have entered Azerbaijan in accordance with an agreement concluded with Baku, and to have established security zones. [Begin unidentified reporter recording] According to a report in the Russian newspaper IZVESTIYA, the aforesaid agreement was concluded following talks between the Azerbaijani President Geydar Aliyev and Vladimir Kazimirov, Russia's special representative on Nagorno-Karabakh. The agreement envisages the establishment of 35 control points in the Armenian-Azerbaijani conflict zones by the Russian troops. The Karabakh Armenians will also have observers at these control points. The agreement also provides for the phased withdrawal of the Armenians from the occupied lands. In his previous statements, Azerbaijani President Geydar Aliyev had said that Russian soldiers would definitely not be allowed to enter Azerbaijan. [end recording]
