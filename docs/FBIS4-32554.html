BFN [Text] The National Council of the Union of Labor [UP] has made a decision on joining the opposition to Prime Minister Waldemar Pawlak's government. UP has accused the government coalition of failing to implement the policies announced during the election campaign and of continuing the policies of the previous governments. UP has also criticized the government's Strategy for Poland program. According to UP, this document assumes a further exacerbation of the uneven distribution of the social costs of the transformation of the state. The UP National Council has not yet decided whether Industry Minister Marek Pol, who represents UP in the government, should leave the Council of Ministers.
