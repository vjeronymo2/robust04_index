921203 FT 03 DEC 92 / UK Company News: Dry dock effect lowers LOF to Dollars 1m Loss of hire while London Spirit was in dry dock in Singapore was behind the profits decline to Dollars 1m (Pounds 540,000) at London & Overseas Freighters in the six months to September 30. Announcing the 19.5 per cent fall from a previous Dollars 1.24m the tanker company pointed out that these results related to the period before the change in domicile. Under a scheme of arrangement a new vehicle, London & Overseas Freighters was recently re-established in Bermuda. It was also expanded to include Scoresby, a fellow company in the Kulukundis family stable. In the six months under review net freights and hire income was little changed at Dollars 5.8m (Dollars 5.77m) and vessels' operating costs rose to Dollars 2.8m (Dollars 2.44m). Provision for deferred repairs took Dollars 365,000 (Dollars 279,000). Earnings per share translated to 4.3p (5.89p).
