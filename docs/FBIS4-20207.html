BFN [Unattributed article: "NATO: Italy Hosts `Dynamic Impact' Exercises"] [Text] (ANSA) Rome, April 26 -- NATO forces will stage "Dynamic Impact" exercises in the central western Mediterranean, May 4-19, aimed at training convention forces to deal with crisis situations, the supreme headquarters of allied forces in Europe (SHAPE) announced today. SHAPE said operation Dynamic Impact was an exercise which employed the use of land, sea, air and amphibious forces and command centers. The exercise includes naval and air operations in the central-western Mediterranean and land and amphibious operations in Italy-Friuli, Tuscany and Emilia-Romagna with the employment of armed forces from eleven nations -- Belgium, France, Germany, United States, Netherlands, Portugal, Britain, Greece, Italy, Spain and Turkey. France and Spain, while not fully integrated into NATO's military structure, will participate with land, sea and air forces in the framework of normal training exercises with the allies. In order to enhance security and avoid or at least reduce inconveniences for civil air traffic, all activities have been coordinated between civil and military authorities in the framework of the NATO Committee for European Air Space Coordination (CEAC). Operation Dynamic Impact, will be carried out under the command of the new chief of allied forces in southern Europe, Admiral Leighton Smith.
