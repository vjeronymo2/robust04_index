940716 FT 16 JUL 94 / World Stock Markets (Asia Pacific): Hong Kong ahead with gain of 3.5% The Nikkei average rose for the third consecutive session helped by stability in the currency markets, writes Emiko Terazono in Tokyo. The 225-issue index closed up 52.11 at 20,770.15, for a week's gain of 1.2 per cent, after rising to the day's high of 20,877.98 in the morning, before falling to a low of 20,718.14 on profit-taking and position adjustment ahead of the weekend. Volume was 288m shares against 292m. Most investors were reluctant to accumulate positions ahead of the weekend. The Topix index of all first section stocks rose 3.95 to 1,668.47, while the Nikkei 300 edged up 0.56 to 303.03. Advances led declines by 579 to 393, with 200 issues remaining unchanged. In London, the ISE/ Nikkei 50 index was up 2.62 at 1352.88. Some retail stocks were sought on restructuring efforts and the recent return in consumer confidence. Sogo, a department store, gained Y70 to Y821. Analysts said that although department store stocks had gone up substantially already, there was still room for a rally over the next few weeks. 'They're good for the short term trader, but for the fundamental investor, clothing wholesalers are better investments,' said Mr Paul Heaton, retail analyst at Baring Securities. Electric cable makers were higher on reports of government investment in fibre optic projects as a part of the government's information superhighway development. Furukawa Electric rose Y18 to Y731 and Nippon Hume Pipe gained Y52 to Y777. Profit-taking depressed some issues which had rallied on Thursday: Aisin Seiki lost Y60 to Y1,450 and Sony Y100 to Y5,950. Nippon Telegraph and Telephone rose Y6,000 to Y859,000 and East Japan Railway gained Y5,000 to Y510,000. In Osaka, the OSE average put on 79.14 to 23,227.55 in volume of 29m shares. Roundup The steadier US dollar and interest rate outlook allowed markets in the region to accentuate the positive. HONG KONG rose 3.5 per cent on the day and 8.1 per cent on the week, the Hang Seng index closing 308.74 higher at 9,117.02. Foreign funds poured back into the market, and turnover rose from HKDollars 3.84bn to HKDollars 5.11bn. Banks, about to report earnings, saw keen rotational buying interest, HSBC rising HKDollars 2.75 to HKDollars 87.50, its Hang Seng Bank unit by HKDollars 1.75 to HKDollars 54.50 and Bank of East Asia by HKDollars 1.50 to HKDollars 33.40. Properties continued their rebound. SYDNEY noted a a continuing futures rally as the All Ordinaries index closed 50.3, or 2.5 per cent better at 2,058.0, 4.7 per cent up on the week in turnover of nearly ADollars 600m. In futures, the September share price index contract rose 58 to 2070 after a gain of 48 points on Thursday. BANGKOK projected good second quarter finance and bank results and the SET index jumped 42.80, or 3.3 per cent to 1,344.17, 4.4 per cent better on the week. Turnover was Bt15.2bn, its highest since February 1. Thai Farmers Bank topped the active list, Bt7 to Bt133 after a 56 per cent rise in net profits. WELLINGTON ended with the NZSE-40 index 38.41 higher at 2,012.51, up 2.9 per cent on the week. TAIPEI saw late buying of financials and electronics push the weighted index up by 102.89, or 1.6 per cent to a six-month high of 6,407.50 in heavy trade, 3.5 per cent up on the week, turnover rising to TDollars 88.03bn from Thursday's TDollars 84.86bn. MANILA was lifted by the Central Bank's move to trim the level of required reserves on bank deposits by three percentage points. The composite index ended 47.07 up at 2,604.90, 0.8 per cent up on the week. SEOUL, in contrast, fell on local news reports that banks had been told by the central bank to refrain from stock investment. The central Bank of Korea (BOK) denied the reports, but selling continued throughout the afternoon and the composite index closed 8.49 lower at 950.23, almost flat on the week.
