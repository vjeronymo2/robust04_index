2. Results We have used the actuarial model to estimate the change in payment for capital-related costs from FY 1994 to FY 1995. To show the effect of the capital prospective payment system on low capital cost hospitals and high capital cost hospitals, we are presenting separately in Table IV the results of our simulation for these hospitals. We consider a hospital to be a low capital cost hospital if, based on a comparison of its initial hospital-specific rate and the applicable Federal rate, it will be paid under the fully prospective payment methodology. A high capital cost hospital is a hospital that, based on its initial hospital-specific rate, will be paid under the hold-harmless payment methodology. If the redetermined hospital-specific rate is greater than the adjusted Federal rate, these hospitals will be paid under the hold-harmless payment methodology.
