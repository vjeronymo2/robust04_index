Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Summit talks between Presidents Ilyas al-Hirawi and Hafiz al-Asad are still under way. According to our Damascus correspondent, the two presidents met for three and a half hours today. Vice President 'Abd-al-Halim Khaddam attended part of the talks. Our correspondent cites sources close to the talks as saying that the two presidents discussed Lebanese-Israeli talks, the conditions made by Israel, and Israel's position concerning UN Security Council Resolution 425. The Lebanese and Syrian presidents also reviewed U.S. views concerning Lebanese-Israeli talks and the U.S. message to Lebanon, which warns of the repercussions of the volatile situation in the south. Al-Hirawi and al-Asad stressed the need for coordination between their countries in matters of domestic, foreign, diplomatic, and security policy. The two presidents met again this afternoon. Sources close to the talks say that various domestic issues will be discussed in that session.
