941114 FT 14 NOV 94 / Arts: Today's Television The great British public are turning as one to BBC 2's Martin Chuzzlewit tonight - or are they? Last week's opener tingled with a sense of occasion, redoubtable British actors who are correctly hatchet-faced in Pinter going wildly OTT at the nuzzle of a wing-collar or the swing of a corkscrew curl. Thesps love Dickens and he loved them; no quarrel with the relish of Tom Wilkinson's magnificent Pecksniff or Paul Scofield's double act as the ill-matched Chuzzlewit brothers; and watch out for a touching John Mills tonight. But irritatingly nudging film music and a cautious narrative pace suggest slower-witted overseas markets are targeted for this slice of English heritage. It even has a honking case of adenoids, in the form of young Martin who swallows his words and breathes them out through his nose (9.30). From costume capers to Granada's launch of The War Machine, ITV's week-long look at the arms trade. Tonight's World in Action coasts in on the scummy crest of the sleaze wave, promising revelations of corruption among the men from the ministry and a major British company (8.30).
