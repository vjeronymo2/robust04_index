Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Vladas Burbulis] [Text] Vilnius, February 15 (TASS) -- It is a year today since Algirdas Brazauskas was elected president of Lithuania. In the country's first universal democratic elections 60.03 percent of voters cast their ballots in his favour. Responding to a question from the country's most popular daily LIETUVOS RITAS about what successes the president feels proud of, Brazauskas said that when he won the elections, news was circulated all over the world that Lithuania was again ruled by communists. "Since that time Europe, and not Europe alone, has come to realize that all power institutions in the republic were elected democratically, that reforms continue and that Lithuania is pursuing a predictable foreign policy. No one in Europe can say that some uncertain types were in power. It was an important task for as to change the image of Lithuania in the eyes of the world community", Brazauskas said. "The adoption of Lithuania in the Council of Europe confirms the fact that our country has won international recognition. We see good relations which have been formed lately with the European Union, NATO and other international organizations as our great achievement," the Lithuanian president said. "In the field of foreign policy we managed to avoid major conflicts through planning, and the Russian Army was withdrawn on time," Brazauskas said. He also said that his successes as president also included better relations among people. "Suspiciousness is vanishing and there is no more search for internal enemies," Brazauskas stressed.
