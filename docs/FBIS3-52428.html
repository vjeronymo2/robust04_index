Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] The 11th session of the Arab Interior Ministers' Council opened in Tunis this morning. The session will be held from 4 to 6 January. Abdallah Kallel, the Tunisian minister of state and interior minister, read [Tunisian] President Zine El Abidine Ben Ali's speech at the opening session. In his speech, President Zine El Abidine Ben Ali said in particular that duty calls on all Arab countries to arm themselves with the required initiative to overcome difficulties and spread amity and unity of word, stressing that the Tunisia of the new era is committed to follow that correct path. He said that as far as Tunisia is concerned, security must be all or nothing. [passage omitted] In his speech, the head of state said that the strength of Arab society requires intensive and concerted efforts in order to prevent society from being influenced by dubious ideological trends and deviant behavioral patterns. The most dangerous of these are those which use religion as a pretext and Islam as a vehicle to resort to violence and terrorism with a view to achieving personal objectives, which are not unknown to any perceptive analyst. [passage omitted] The agenda of the 11th session of the Arab Interior Ministers' Council, chaired by the Syrian interior minister, includes 18 items. Combating narcotics will be the most prominent among them. [passage omitted]
