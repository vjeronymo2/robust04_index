940513 FT 13 MAY 94 / Imports from EU fall to a three-year low Imports from the European Union into the UK fell to their lowest level for almost three years in February, amid the continuing recession in western Europe, seasonally adjusted trade figures from the Central Statistical Office showed yesterday. The value of imports from EU countries to the UK fell to Pounds 5.1bn during February, down from Pounds 5.4bn the previous month. Coupled with fairly flat levels of UK exports to the EU, the fall in imports meant that the UK's trade deficit with the rest of the EU also fell in February, down to Pounds 74m from Pounds 97m the previous month. This was its lowest level for five months, and markedly lower than the UK's trade deficit with the rest of the world. Part of the reason for the improvement in the EU trade balance was that imports from Germany fell for the fifth consecutive month in February, dropping to Pounds 1.48bn - leaving the trade deficit with Germany narrower at Pounds 226m. The trade deficit with France was also lower in February, down to Pounds 91m from one of Pounds 137m - again largely due to falling imports. Meanwhile, yearly figures show that the UK ran a trade surplus with the Benelux countries for the second year in succession. The UK's trade deficit with non-EU countries slightly dipped in February, after rising earlier in the year. The trade balance with the US fell to a deficit of Pounds 12m, compared with a surplus of Pounds 38m in January. On a three-monthly basis, however, the balance showed a growing trade deficit with the US, with one of Pounds 145m during the three months to February, compared with Pounds 4m during the previous
