BFN [Text] Cairo, 6 Jun (MENA)--The northern al-Jizah Prosecution Office continued its interrogation of Muslim Brotherhood General Guide Hamid Abu-al-Nasr today. After four hours of questioning, the prosecution decided to resume the interrogations Thursday. Abu-al-Nasr told his interrogators that the Muslim Brotherhood has had no official or structural organization since the decision to dissolve it in 1954. He said he is assuming the post of the group's general guide to shoulder his responsibility in filing lawsuits demanding that the dissolution decision be repealed. He noted that this is currently being examined by the judiciary. Abu-al-Nasr said that as a general guide he issues statements expressing the Muslim Brotherhood's views on various issues. He stressed that he respects the Constitution and the laws and that he expresses his opinion through legitimate means allowed by law.
