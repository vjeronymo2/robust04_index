920918 FT 18 SEP 92 / Commodities and Agriculture: Prospect of free trade in coffee between Mexico and Canada THE prospect of free trade in coffee between Mexico, the fourth largest producer, and Canada, which imports around 2.3m bags a year, has further muddied the waters of next week's ICO negotiations, writes David Blackwell. The agreement between the two countries appears to be enshrined in an annex to the North American Free Trade Agreement (Nafta). Mr Lawrence Eagles, analyst with GNI, the London futures broker, pointed out yesterday that even if consuming countries operated a penalty process for coffee outside a quota system, Mexico would be able to export its full quota to any ICO consumer and send the rest to Canada, which is not in the ICO.
