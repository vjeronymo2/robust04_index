Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following reports were monitored on the heads of state attending the funeral of late President Felix Houphouet-Boigny in Yamoussoukro on 7 February. Paris AFP in French at 0751 GMT on 7 February reports that President Paul Biya left Yaounde this morning for Yamoussoukro to attend the funerals of Ivorian President Felix Houphouet-Boigny, according to the national radio. Abidjan Radio Cote d'Ivoire Chaine Nationale-Une Network in French at 1245 GMT on 3 February quotes an official of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs as saying that the Kenyan ambassador resident in Lagos; the Zambian high commissioner in Lagos; and Alioune Blondin Beye, the UN special representative to Angola, have already arrived. Abidjan La Chaine-Une Television Network in French at 2000 GMT on 6 February reports that the following heads of state are already in Yamoussoukro to attend the funerals of President Felix Houphouet- Boigny. They are Fredirico Mayor, the UNESCO director general; Presidents Idriss Deby of Chad; Pascal Lissouba of Congo; and Ange Patasse of Central African Republic. Paris AFP in French at 1016 GMT on 5 February reports that President Omar Bongo of Gabon will attend the funerals of President Felix Houphouet-Boigny on 7 February in Yamoussoukro. Bamako Radiodiffusion-Television du Mali Radio in French at 0700 GMT on 7 February reports that President Alpha Omar Konare left Bamako this morning for Yamoussoukro to attend the funerals of President Felix Houphouet-Boigny. Paris AFP in French at 1442 GMT on 5 February reports that Sao-Tomean President Miguel Trovoada will attend the funerals of Ivorian President Houphouet-Boigny on 7 February. He will use the opportunity to meet representatives of Portuguese-speaking African countries and UNITA Leader Jonas Savimbi. Abidjan La Chaine Une Television Network in French at 2000 GMT on 7 February reports nearly 20 African heads of state were present at Yamoussoukro to demonstrate their compassion and express the pain caused by the disappearance of Felix Houphouet-Boigny. The video shows the following heads of state: Presidents Lansana Conte of Guinea, Joao Bernardo Vieira of Guinea-Bissau, Alpha Oumar Konare of Mali, Mahamane Ousman of Niger, Nicephore Soglo of Benin, Blaise Compaore of Burkina Faso, Gnassignbe Eyadema of Togo, Oumar Bongo of Gabon, Monteiro Mascarenhas of Cape Verde, Abdou Diouf of Senegal, Jerry John Rawlings of Ghana, Sani Abacha of Nigeria, Paul Biya of Cameroon, Mobutu Sese Seko of Zaire, Teodoro Obiang Nguema Mbasogo of Equatorial Guinea, Sir Dawda Jawara of the Gambia, and Ilyas al-Hirawi of Lebanon. Also shown are President Francois Mitterrand and Prime Minister Edouard Balladur of France. Italy was represented by Senate Speaker Giovanni Spadolini, and Great Britain was represented by the Duchess of Kent.
