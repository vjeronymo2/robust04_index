September 12, 1990, Wednesday, Home Edition

YOUNG ADULTS' INABILITY TO BUY OWN HOME

Kudos to Robert Kuttner ("The Great Generational Depression," Commentary, Sept. 3) and the few other souls in the (economic) wilderness for pointing out a recent American phenomenon: the hard fact that a young family, by itself, will never be able to own their own home.

Kuttner cites some (all too familiar to me) data, but what impressed me the most was his theory: that the reason why "the political system is paying so little attention to this remarkable generational economic distress . . . is because the distress has yet to reach the opinion-leader class in a serious way."

The fact today is that if you're young, and don't have a great deal of personal wealth, or at least parents of means, then the only way you're gonna own a home is to be a highly paid professional -- at least $65,000 a year. Then marry a similar young professional and combine your incomes toward the purchase of your home. You may be able to afford a small place in a not-too-objectionable part of town. And whatever you do, don't make the mistake of having children! This will put one-half of the mortgage-paying team out of commission for several critical months! I mean, what do you want, a family or a home?

You tell the older generation this, and they'll never believe you. If you ask the government for help, you are sent a list of no-money-down fixer-uppers. My parents nod in sympathy, but, you see, they're financially strapped because of that condo in Maui.

TODD WAITE

Van Nuys
