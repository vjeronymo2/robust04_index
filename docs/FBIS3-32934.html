Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on Guatemalan economic developments monitored through 15 September. Guatemalan Institute of Tourism Director Jose Miguel Gaitan has told reporters that last year 562,000 tourists visited the country and spent $265.4 million. Gaitan indicated tourism is the country's second best source of foreign currency. (Panama City ACAN in Spanish 1834 GMT 9 Feb 94) The government's austerity plan went into effect yesterday in accordance with Government Agreement 40-94. Consequently, the agencies of the executive branch, as well as the decentralized and autonomous entities participating in the agreement, must implement measures to rationalize public spending. Among the provisions contemplated in the aforementioned agreement is a ban during the current fiscal year on the purchase of automobiles, with the exception of ambulances, patrol cars, and similar public services equipment, provided that the corresponding budget appropriations have been made. (Guatemala City PRENSA LIBRE in Spanish 11 Feb 94 p 115) The Central American Bank of Economic Integration (BCIE) and the Banco de la Construccion have signed an agreement to provide an additional 5,810,570 quetzals to finance loans for small and medium-sized industries within the PAPIC [expansion unknown] program. To date, the BCIE has provided a total of 55 million quetzales in loans to finance programs for small and medium-sized businesses. (Guatemala City PRENSA LIBRE in Spanish 13 Feb 94 p 18)
