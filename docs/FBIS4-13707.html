BFN [Text] Tabriz, E. Azerbaijan, April 21, IRNA -- Volume of Trade exchanges between Iran and Australia reached dlrs. [dollars] 400 million last year, Australian Ambassador to Tehran Nicolas Warner told IRNA here Wednesday [20 April]. Warner said during the period Iran's non-oil exports to Australia stood at dlrs. 25 million, up by 40 percent as compared with the amount of the preceding year. The exported commodities mainly included dried fruit and carpet, he added. Australia has made some efforts to reduce the negative balance in the two countries' trade exchanges through organizing trade fairs and seminars for the Iranian exporters in several Australian cities. Noting that Iran mainly imported wheat, meat and telecommunication equipment from Australia, Warner said his country had allocated dlrs. 750 million in credit for the Iranian importers in an effort to further consolidate mutual industrial and trade cooperation. The ambassador visited this northwestern Iranian city to explore the grounds for the promotion of commercial cooperation between the provincial units and country.
