BFN [Article by Virginia Silveira in Sao Jose dos Campos, Sao Paulo State] [Excerpts] From 30 August to 4 September, the Army Air Command (Comavex) [Comando de Aviacao do Exercito] will host in Taubate (Sao Paulo State) the first International Helicopter Fair in Latin America (Hexpo/94). GPM Events, Promotions and Informatics -- the company responsible for organizing the fair -- has already sold 45 of the 90 stands available at the fair, where equipment and aircraft worth more than $600 million will be on display. Large manufacturers of helicopters and suppliers of aircraft parts and equipment have already confirmed their attendance at the fair, which is expected to attract more than 200,000 people. Among those who will be attending Hexpo/94 are Eurocopter (a merger of the French Aerospatiale with the German MBB), which is represented in Brazil by Helibras [Brazilian Helicopters, Inc.]; Bell, which is represented by Lider Taxi Aereo; Rolls Royce, Celma, Motortec, TAM, Petrobras [Brazilian Petroleum Corporation], Bank of Brazil, and others. GPM President Jose Fernando Portugal said his company is investing $160,000 in organizing the fair. In addition to the exposition, the fair includes a seminar on flight safety, shows, flying demonstrations, and the first Brazilian helicopter flying competition. The fair will devote an area of 5,800 square meters to aircraft exposition, and 3,800 square meters will be earmarked for exposition of equipment and aircraft of the Aeronautics, Army, and Navy Ministries. There will be 90 stands, the sizes of which will range from 6 to 144 square meters. [passage omitted] The Taubate-based Army Air Command, which was built in 1986, houses the largest helicopter fleet in Brazil. Its current fleet consists of 52 helicopters, 36 of which are Panthers (two turbines and 12 seats) while 16 are Esquilo models (the light type, with six seats).
