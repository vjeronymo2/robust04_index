February 25, 1989, Saturday, Orange County Edition

SOUND OFF!

Caltrans' division of aeronautics has established guidelines to control the amount of noise generated over residential areas by aircraft using the state's airports. Under those guidelines, any area exposed to an average Community Noise Equivalent Level (CNEL) of more than 65 in a year is considered to be noise-impacted.

To comply with those regulations, John Wayne Airport has set up nine stations at locations in Newport Beach, Santa Ana and Tustin to monitor noise levels. Some stations are on private property and others on public land, at the following locations:

The stations monitor the noise levels on a daily basis. The average daily levels, which are also averaged for each month, are "the result of a computer analysis based on county-provided data including flight tracks, actual traffic

counts, average SENELs (Single Event Noise Exposure Levels) by aircraft model and recorded CNEL values for each monitor station," according to the quarterly report from the airport's Noise Abatement Office.

As of the end of September, a total of 50 homes in Newport Beach and Santa Ana Heights are in a noise-impacted area. The state has several remedies for property owners in the area: the homes can be insulated, they can be purchased by the county, or the sites can be rezoned for "other non-noise sensitive uses,"

according to Lynn Dosheery, a county planner who serves as the Santa Ana Heights project manager. For instance, the zoning in the area along Acacia and Birch streets in Santa Ana Heights has been changed from a general agricultural use to a business park, she said.

TELEPHONE COMPLAINT CALLS

July-September, 1988

The airport's Noise Abatement Office logs noise complaints from local citizens. During the third quarter of 1988, the office received a total of 1,322 noise complaints from local citizens, a 103% increase from the number of complaints received the previous quarter. The increase can be traced to the fact that in the summer months, "windows are open, people are outside, and are just generally more sensitive to noise," said airport spokeswoman Kathie Rutherford.

Also, Balboa residents had been publicly promoting noise complaints as a part of their unsuccessful effort to have jet liners rerouted so that departures occur as much over Tustin as they do over Newport Beach.

Flights generally depart southbound over Upper Newport Bay, except when air traffic controllers reverse the pattern due to wind conditions. According to Assistant Airport Manager Jan Mittermeier, there was no significant change in the number of departures over Newport Beach.
