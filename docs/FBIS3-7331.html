Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Hanoi VNA March 28 -- Philippine President Fidel V. Ramos's official friendship visit to Vietnam expected here today is a significant event in the friendly and cooperative relations between the two countries, says NHAN DAN in an editorial today. The editorial continues: Vietnam and the Philippines have had time-honoured culture and trade exchanges. Over the past recent years, there have been positive changes in the cooperative relations between the two countries. Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet's official visit to the Philippines in 1992 is a great landmark in the promotion of mutual understanding and trust, creating and impetus to their economic-trade cooperation. The two-way trade value increased to nearly USD [U.S. dollars] 50 million in 1993 from USD 12 million in 1988. The Philippines has 11 investment projects in Vietnam with a total capital of USD 60 million, ranking 18th among foreign countries investing in Vietnam. 'As neighbour, Vietnam and the Philippines have a common aspiration for each country's socio-economic progress, for peace, stability, cooperation, and development in the region. The Philippines and Vietnam have had a common voice in seeking peaceful solutions, conforming to their interests as well as international laws and pratices, to solve problems left behind by the history and foster the friendly and cooperative relations between the two countries'. The paper wishes President Ramos's visit a success, thus contributing to further promoting the Vietnam-Philippines relations for happiness and prosperity of the two nations, and for peace, stability, cooperation and development in the region and all over the world.
