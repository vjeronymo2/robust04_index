BFN [Report by ITAR-TASS correspondent Tigran Liloyan] [Text] Yerevan 17 May TASS -- A statement of the Armenian parliament presidium circulated here today expressed concern over the terrorist act in the Armenian drama theatre in Tbilisi last Sunday [15 May]. The statement says that this encroachment upon ethnic Armenian residents of Georgia is not the only one. It provokes alarm over the safety of the Armenian population of Georgia. The parliament heads hope the Georgian authorities will do their best to find the criminals in the near future and severely punish them, as well as work to prevent similar actions in future. The Armenian parliament leaders expressed sympathy with the explosion victims and their families. Georgian State Head Eduard Shevardnadze telephoned Armenian President Levon Ter-Petrosyan today in connection with the incident. He assured the Armenian leader that the Georgian authorities will do their best to reveal the terrorists and punish them, sources at the Armenian president's press service report. A statement of the Armenian Foreign Ministry has been made in connection with the terrorist act. The ministry accused of the incident "the forces hampering regional stabilization and striving to turn the Karabakh confrontation in a regional conflict and involve Armenia in the war." In his turn, Levon Ter-Petrosyan described the terrorist act as a provocation against the traditional Armenian-Georgian friendship.
