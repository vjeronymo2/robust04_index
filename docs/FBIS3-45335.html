Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Khartoum, January 13 (XINHUA) -- Chinese Vice-Premier and Foreign Minister Qian Qichen arrived in Khartoum today on a three-day official visit. He said upon arrival at the Khartoum International Airport that China and Sudan have strong, traditional and friendly ties. These friendly relations have been strengthened and developed in the 35 years since the establishment of diplomatic relations between them. He said he will meet with Sudanese leaders and have talks with Foreign Minister Husayn Abu-Salih on bilateral relations and international issues of common interest. His talks will also deal with the situation in the region, he said, expressing his confidence that his current visit will contribute to increasing friendship and understanding between the two peoples and enhancing cooperation between them. Sudan is the third leg of Qian's six-nation African tour. He has visited Ethiopia and Eritrea and will continue on to Kenya, Madagascar and Mauritius.
