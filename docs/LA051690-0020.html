May 16, 1990, Wednesday, Home Edition

STATE OFFICIALS CONTINUE AERIAL SPRAYING AGAINST PESKY MEDFLY

It is very interesting that the decision to resume the Malathion spraying was announced the day after a bill that had passed the Assembly didn't get the two-thirds majority required to pass in the Senate (Part A, May 4).

This bill by Mike Roos (D-Los Angeles) would have allowed citizens injured by the spraying the right to sue the manufacturers and suppliers of malathion and the government entities which authorized the spraying.

It is also interesting to note that our government is spraying a large urban population with a toxic chemical repeatedly while the country that we want to buy our fruit has outlawed aerial spraying of malathion because of permanent irreversible optic nerve damage found among children in a sprayed area in that country.

And the California taxpayer picks up the tab!

JUDD B. CUSHING

Los Angeles
