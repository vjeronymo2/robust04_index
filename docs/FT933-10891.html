930802 FT 02 AUG 93 / The ERM Crisis: Chequered history of ERM 1979 March: Start of the European monetary system and the exchange rate mechanism 1981 October: Lira and French franc devalued by 3 per cent 1983 March: D-Mark revalued upwards by 5.5 per cent 1986 April: D-Mark and Dutch guilder revalued upwards by 3 per cent 1987January: D-Mark and Dutch guilder revalued upwards by 3 per cent and Belgian franc by 2 per cent 1990 January: Lira moves to narrow band and devalues 3.7 per cent October: Sterling joins the ERM 1992 April: Portugal joins the ERM September 13: Lira devalued by 7 per cent September 16: UK quits the ERM; Spain devalues peseta by 5 per cent; lira suspended from system until September 22 September 22: Lira quits system indefinitely November 21: Spanish peseta and Portuguese escudo devalued by 6 per cent 1993 January: Irish punt devalues by 10 per cent May: Spain and Portugal devalue by 8 per cent and 6.5 per cent respectively July 30: French franc, Belgian franc and Danish krone pushed to ERM floors against D-Mark amid heavy speculative selling
