Language: Latvian Article Type:BFN [Text] A working group, including officials from the Economics Ministry, the Foreign Affairs Ministry, and the Bank of Latvia has returned from a visit to Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. A Latvian-Kyrgyz Government agreement (for indefinite time) on the principles of trade and economic cooperation, which provides for a mutually favorable procedure, was initialled in Kyrgyzstan. In addition, a protocol and its supplements were signed on reciprocal supplies of goods for this year. Kyrgyzstan wishes to receive from Latvia minibuses, spare parts for motorcars and tractors, shoes, synthetic threads, as well as consumer goods. For its part Kyrgyzstan will supply to Latvia washed wool, woolen yarn, fermented tobacco and other goods, and raw materials. The text of an agreement on the principles of trade and economic cooperation, and a protocol of supplies were coordinated with Kazakhstan. Supplements to the protocol were signed. This agreement will be initialled in the very near future. Latvia will receive from Kazakhstan coal from Karaganda, pig iron, tin plate, which is needed for producers of tinned fish and dairy products, and rolled nonferrous metals. After additional coordination by the governments of the two countries, the supply of wheat from Kazakhstan is also possible. A visit of the working group to Uzbekistan, as well as to Tajikistan and Turkmenistan to coordinate and sign protocols of reciprocal supplies are scheduled for the beginning of March.
