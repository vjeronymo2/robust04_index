BFN [By Debbie Kuo] [Text] Taipei, May 13 (CNA) -- Taiwan will work with Southeast Asian countries to increase regional intellectual property rights (IPR) protection, the Board of Foreign Trade (BOFT) said Friday [13 May]. Taiwan is paying equal attention to consultations on both IPR protection and agricultural imports as part of its effort to join the General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade (GATT), BOFT officials said. The efforts include exchanges of information and personnel between Taiwan and members of the Association of Southeast Asian Nations (ASEAN). Besides IPR protection consultations, the officials said, BOFT is seeking bilateral recognition of national laboratories and cooperation in product verification and quality control certification with ASEAN members. Details of the issues will be further discussed in an October Asia-Pacific meterology conference to be held in Taipei, BOFT said.
