BFN [Text] The first round of the inter-Tajik talks on national reconciliation that were held under the aegis of the United Nations Organization has completed its work in the city of Moscow of the Russian Federation. The participants in the talks signed a protocol on setting up a joint commission on the problems of Tajikistan refugees. The protocol says that the participants of the inter-Tajik talks on national reconciliation under the aegis of the United Nations Organization arrived at the following agreement: To set up a joint commission on the problems of the Tajik refugees on an equal basis between of representatives of the Government of the Republic of Tajikistan and the opposition, in particular, the Umed Foundation [Tajik opposition fund to render assistance to compatriots abroad] from 19 April 1994. The joint commission will work within the framework of the course of talks and coordinate its activity with the United Nations High Commission for Refugees in those countries the Tajik refugees are. The joint commission could set up working groups and bring in experts when it is necessary. The participants of the inter-Tajik talks on national reconciliation asked the United Nations to render financial assistance to the joint commission. A joint communique was adopted on the results of the first round of the inter-Tajik talks. The joint communique says that the first round of the inter-Tajik talks on national reconciliation took place in the city of Moscow under the aegis of the United Nations, with participation of observers from Afghanistan, Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, Iran, Pakistan, Russia, and Uzbekistan from 5-19 April of the current year. The Tajikistan Government delegation was led by Shukurjon Zuhurov, the minister of labor and employment. The opposition delegation was led by Otakhon Latifi, the chairman of the Coordinating Council of the Democratic Forces of Tajikistan in the Commonwealth of Independent States. Ambassador Ramiro Piriz-Ballon, the United Nations secretary general's special envoy was a mediator in the course of talks. The talks were held in a working, open, and mutual understanding atmosphere. Both sides (?showed themselves really in favor of) a solution to the package of difficult political, military, humanitarian, social, and economic issues that appeared as a result of political conflict and fratricidal war in the Republic of Tajikistan. The talks participants have approved and adopted the agenda of talks during all rounds that are as follows: 1. Measures on the political settlement of the situation in Tajikistan. 2. Solution to the problems of refugees. 3. Issues relating to the construction of constitutional structures and strengthening of the state structure of the Republic of Tajikistan. The sides decided to discuss all three groups of issues as a whole and to express their opinion on all the issues, in particular the agenda of talks in the working documents. The sides and the United Nations secretary general's special envoy approved the joint communique of the inter-Tajik talks and adopted a decision to urge the Tajikistan Government and the Tajik opposition forces not to jeopardize the talks. The sides have signed a protocol on setting up a joint commission on resolving the problems of Tajik refugees. The sides have expressed gratitude to all the states that rendered assistance to the Tajik refugees, as well as to the United Nations High Commission for Refugees and other international organizations. The sides expressed their readiness to achieve national reconciliation only through political dialogue. With this purpose, on the basis of the previous agreement on changing the venue of talks, the sides decided to ask the United Nations secretary general's special envoy to hold meetings on the venue and date for the second and third rounds of the inter-Tajik talk. The sides have expressed gratitude to the Russian Federation Government for the hospitality, assistance, and cooperation in organizing and holding the first round of talks in Moscow. Similarly, the sides have expressed gratitude to the United Nations secretary general and Ramiro Piriz-Ballon, his special envoy, as well as to the representatives of those states who acted as observers at the talks for their assistance and cooperation in organizing and holding the inter-Tajik talks on national reconciliation.
