BFN [Report by UKRINFORM correspondent Lev Ryabchikov for TASS] [Text] Simferopol, 22 May -- The Crimean-Ukrainian parliamentary talks on unblocking the situation on the peninsula following the adoption of the law on restoring the Crimean Constitution of 6 May 1992 will be held in the next few days. On Monday [23 May] or Tuesday Simferopol and Kiev will hold consultations to finally agree the venue for the talks. It has become known from trustworthy sources in Kiev that the Ukrainian delegation will be led by Boris Oleynik, who has expressed readiness to go to Simferopol. Crimean President Yuriy Meshkov is currently in the Crimea after coming back from a working trip to the Kuban region on Friday. On Saturday evening for three hours he attended the extraordinary session of the Crimean Parliament, which was held behind closed doors. The internal political situation and the problems of settling the situation with Kiev around the Crimean Constitution of 6 April [as heard] 1992 were the center of attention.
