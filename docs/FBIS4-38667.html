BFN [Unattributed report from "Inform" column: "Georgia Has Its Own Special Forces"] [Text] A Georgian State Security Service special-purpose, assault-landing brigade has been reviewed in the village of Mukhrovani in East Georgia. Talking to journalists, Igor Giorgadze, chairman of Georgia's State Security Service, said that this subunit has been trained at the Russian special services' training centers. The Russian Armed Forces have provided the new Georgian special-purpose forces with new modern arms and equipment free of charge. The assault-landing brigade comprises tanks, armored hardware, artillery, a crack "Alfa" subunit, and an antiterrorist squad. The latter has been trained, armed, and equipped by the U.S. CIA. Newspapers in Tbilisi reported that the upkeep of the Georgian special forces will cost the Republic 5 billion Russian rubles a year.
