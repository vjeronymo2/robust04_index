920526 FT 26 MAY 92 / Arts: Today's Television Television produces precious few programmes which last as little as five minutes, but today sees the start of Dance House (8.25 BBC2), a series of 12 short films within the 'Commissions and Collaborations' season 'designed to show the diversity of British dance today.' Today's opening offering is 'Mosaic,' a monologue written by Anthony Minghella, with choreography by Jonathan Lunn, featuring dancer Lauren Potter and actress Juliet Stevenson. There is another collectively awful party in the final episode of ITV's superb Anglo-Saxon Attitudes (8.30), this time given by Robin and Marie-Helene, and gate-crashed by .. Well, wait and see. Rear Window, Channel 4's series about arts and race, features the work of children's writer, Geoffrey Trease, who, at 88, is about to finish his 102nd novel (9.00). Some of the lines in BBC1's 60-minute comedy-drama series, Boys from the Bush (9.30) are a bit weak, but the situations, such as Arlene's wedding in Delilah's brothel last week, are often very funny. BBC2's highly informative documentary series, The Racing Game , looks at horse-breeding (9.50).
