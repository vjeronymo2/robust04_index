930728 FT 28 JUL 93 / International Company News: Result from Tenneco disappoints Wall St TENNECO, the US conglomerate which has been extensively restructuring, yesterday reported more than doubled second-quarter earnings from continuing operations, but net income fell 28 per cent compared to the same period of 1992. The figures were towards the low end of analysts' expectations, and Tenneco shares fell Dollars 2 1/2 to close at Dollars 48 on the New York Stock Exchange. Income from continuing operations totalled Dollars 111m, or 63 cents a share, against Dollars 50m, or 32 cents, in the same period of last year, on sales of Dollars 3.48bn, compared with Dollars 3.43bn. Net income totalled Dollars 88m, or 50 cents, after a Dollars 23m, or 13 cents, extraordinary loss. That was down from Dollars 123m, or 84 cents, in the same period last year, while operating income was slightly ahead at Dollars 303m, compared with Dollars 296m. The J I Case agricultural and construction equipment unit reported operating income of Dollars 59m, up Dollars 48m from the same period last year, while revenues dipped slightly to Dollars 1.1bn. The company attributed the improvement to 'better margins as a result of a richer product mix, Case's value-based pricing strategy, strong manufacturing performance and operating cost reductions'. For the six months, Tenneco reported net income of Dollars 162m, or 96 cents, against a loss of Dollars 543m, or Dollars 3.87, after accounting changes in the same period of last year.
