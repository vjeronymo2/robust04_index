BFN [Text] Washington, April 24 KYODO -- Japanese Finance Minister Hirohisa Fujii left Washington for home Sunday after attending a meeting of finance ministers and central bankers from the Group of Seven (G-7) industrialized nations. Fujii will miss the twice-yearly gatherings of the International Monetary Fund and the World Bank through Tuesday in Washington because of a tight schedule at home involving formation of a new cabinet. Fujii is expected to arrive in Tokyo on Tuesday. Bank of Japan Governor Yasushi Mieno will represent Japan at the IMF and World Bank meetings, along with Kosuke Nakahira, vice finance minister for international affairs.
