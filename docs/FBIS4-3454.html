BFN [Text] Beijing, April 20 (XINHUA) -- Chinese President Jiang Zemin inspected the Beijing International Telecommunications Center here today, urging concerted efforts to make telecommunications work better serve the country's economic development. Jiang, general secretary of the Central Committee of the Chinese Communist Party (CPC), said that the CPC Central Committee and the State Council are pleased at the rapid development of China's telecommunications services in recent years. Situated on the southern side of Beijing Sanyuan fly-over, the Beijing international telecommunications center is an important link in China's national telecommunications network which transmits international telephones, telegraph, and data business into or out of China. Jiang arrived at the center this morning, accompanied by Chen Xitong, party secretary of Beijing, and Wu Jichuan, minister of posts and telecommunications. At a TV conference room, he talked by video telephone with the head of Shanghai Telecommunications Bureau -- about supplies of vegetables, traffic conditions and development of telecommunications in Shanghai. Jiang urged Shanghai and Beijing telecommunications bureaux to learn from and compete with each other. At the international telephone control room, he urged the operators to devote efforts to their study of foreign languages. In a meeting room, Jiang listened to a report from Beijing telecommunications bureau and the Ministry of Posts and Telecommunications. He said that the establishment of a socialist market economic structure demands a highly developed telecommunications network. He called on various departments within telecommunications and posts services to enhance cooperation and use state investment to its best advantage.
