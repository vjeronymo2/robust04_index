921008 FT 08 OCT 92 / Jagan 'sure of winning presidency' in Guyana Opposition leader Dr Cheddi Jagan seemed assured last night of the presidency of Guyana, having what local politicians called an unassailable lead for his People's Progressive Party (PPP), Canute James reports from Georgetown. With 61 per cent of the votes from the Monday general election counted, the PPP had three in every five, all but a handful of the rest being taken by the ruling People's National Congress (PNC) party of President Desmond Hoyte. Dr Jagan, a 74-year-old former marxist who now talks like a free marketeer, was expected to be sworn in at the weekend, ending 28 unbroken years of rule by the PNC. Mr Jimmy Carter, US former president and leader of the foreign observers at the election, has declared the polling free and fair. 'The conduct of the elections was excellent,' he said.
