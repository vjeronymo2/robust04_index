920501 FT 01 MAY 92 / Observer: Redressed Problem. Minutes before the chairman of Britain's biggest bank addresses his last annual general meeting, somebody pours white paint all over him. What do you do? How the officials of Barclays Bank answered the said challenge yesterday would certainly win a distinction in any Institute of Bankers exam. Loyal company servant John Moulding swiftly offered the chairman his own suit, then shivered in his underpants while replacement pinstripes were rushed in from Marks and Spencer. Meanwhile an apparently perfectly tailored Sir John Quinton stepped on stage for his final bow. What his thoughts were on the incident, nobody knows. But Barclays non-executive director and ICI chairman Sir Denys Henderson reportedly joked: 'I hope it was Dulux.'
