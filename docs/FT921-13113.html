920123 FT 23 JAN 92 / International Company News: Provisions soar to Dollars 2.6bn at SecPac SECURITY PACIFIC, the loss-making Los Angeles-based bank being merged into Bank of America, unveiled an 80 per cent leap in 1991 bad debt provisions, to Dollars 2.6bn. The bank also recorded a 75 per cent jump in 1991 loan write-offs, to Dollars 1.4bn. SecPac revealed, as expected, a 1991 loss of Dollars 774.5m, a sharp deterioration from the Dollars 161.3m net profit recorded in 1990. The bank's fourth-quarter loss was Dollars 409.2m, compared with a loss of Dollars 357.6m in the same period of 1990. Shawmut, the Connecticut-based bank, also reported a 1991 loss. The bank, which last week abandoned merger talks with the Bank of Boston, lost Dollars 170.7m in 1991, which compares with a Dollars 133m loss in 1990. The fourth quarter saw a tiny net profit of Dollars 2.4m, against a Dollars 215.7m loss in the last quarter of 1990.
