BFN [Text] The government will carry out a five-year program from next year to build up the nation's technology infrastructure in a bid to maximize its technological competitiveness. The program is aimed at not only developing strategic technologies but also enhancing the effectiveness of their distribution to cope with the post Uruguay Round era which is expected to put major emphasis on technological innovation in economic competition in the world. A total of 706.5 billion won will be poured in to the project, 417.9 billion won of which will be committed by the government and the remaining 286.6 billion won by the private industrial sector, the Ministry of Trade, Industry and Energy said yesterday. Next year, 40 projects will be undertaken at a cost of 145.8 billion won, 59.6 billion won of which will be provided by the government and 86.2 billion won by the private sector. They include the fostering of technicians to design ASIC (application specific integrated circuit) chips and to develop technologies for high tech electronic games, the establishment of a software industrial technology information center and the setting-up of an industrial technology database. Among others are the establishment of a textile industry information network, a pilot plant for fine ceramics, and the creation of a base for joint research for the development of automotive parts.
