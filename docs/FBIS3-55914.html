Language: English Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Brussels, Jan 11, BNS - Lithuanian moved Tuesday towards accepting NATO's offer of a "Partnership for Peace," taking a lead among former Soviet states. President Algirdas Brazauskas instructed his government to start "immediate preparation" to sign a partnership document with the Western alliance. In a statement distributed by the Lithuanian embassy in Brussels, Brazauskas said partnership "paves the way to the full integration of Lithuania into the NATO political-military structures." [passage omitted] Brazauskas said the Baltic republic, which borders Russia, would identify "facilities, resources and forces," that could be used in the partnership.
