BFN [Excerpt] Magdeburg (ADN-lSA) -- Two weeks after the European elections, the official final results for Saxony-Anhalt are now available. The turnout on 12 June was 66.07 percent of those entitled to vote, of whom 30.13 percent voted for the CDU [Christian Democratic Union]. The SPD [Social Democratic Party] gained 383,337 votes or 27.94 percent, returning officer Paul-Uwe Soeker announced in Magdeburg at the weekend. The PDS [Party of Democratic Socialists] gained third place with 18.9 percent. The FDP [Free Democratic Party] failed to clear the five percent hurdle, gaining only 4.71 percent of the votes. The Greens were given 77,907 votes or 5.68 percent. The Republicans gained 2.77 percent and thus played an insignificant role. [passage omitted]
