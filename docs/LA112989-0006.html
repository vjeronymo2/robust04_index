November 29, 1989, Wednesday, Home Edition

CUTTING U.S. DEFENSE SPENDING

The unfolding major decline in worldwide communism should strikingly improve the military security of the United States. However, even if we receive a "peace" dividend because of reduced military expenditures, our country might face tougher economic competition.

Some of those countries converting from communism to capitalism would become far more competitive in a global economy.

At the end of World War II, the U.S. dominated the world economy, but has seen its share of the world gross national product slip with the rise of other capitalist economies out of the ashes of war.

Aren't we in for a new wave of hard-nosed competitors out of the ashes of communism?

What if a number of these new capitalists follow Japan's post-war philosophy of high savings and investment rates, in order to favor a rapid buildup in production capacity? If so, are we willing to curtail our profligate consumer borrowing and spending habits in order to compete under the new reality?

FLOYD A. OLIVER

Los Angeles
