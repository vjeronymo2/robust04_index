January 29, 1989, Sunday, Valley Edition

COLLEGE BASKETBALL;

CSUN WOMEN TRIP DOMINGUEZ HILLS, 63-51, DESPITE ABSENCE OF CAVALIN

The Cal State Northridge women's basketball team suffered through one of its worst shooting performances of the season, but even without their best player the Lady Matadors defeated Cal State Dominguez Hills, 63-51, Saturday night in the Toro gym.

Chris Cavalin, who is averaging a team-leading 16.6 points and 10.1 rebounds a game, missed the game because of a pulled groin muscle. She is expected to play Thursday when Northridge plays host to second-ranked Cal Poly Pomona.

Northridge (15-4 overall, 3-1 in the California Collegiate Athletic Assn.) is a game behind Pomona in the conference standings.

Bridgette Ealy had a team-high 22 points and 12 rebounds to lead the Lady Matadors, who shot just 33.9% from the field. Vicki Mallett added 13 points for CSUN and Julie Arlotto had 11 points.

Kathy Goggin had 16 points to lead Dominguez Hills (5-11, 1-3). MIKE HISERMAN In other women's games: Cal Lutheran 82, Christ College 71 -- Christ College started the game with only 5 players and played the final minutes with just 3 after 2 had fouled out of the Golden State Athletic Conference game at Christ College. Heidi Griffith led the Regals (5-12, 2-3) with 21 points and Brenda Lee had 18 points and 11 rebounds. Cuesta 90, Canyons 72 -- Tressie Millender scored 32 points and grabbed 10 rebounds, but it wasn't enough for Canyons in the Western State Conference game at Cuesta. Cuesta center Vanessa Hornbuckle, a graduate of Newbury Park High, scored a game-high 41 points and pulled down 12 rebounds. Teammate Karen McConnell added 25 points and 10 rebounds for Cuesta (11-11, 3-5). Debbie Barnard had 12 points and a game-high 13 rebounds for Canyons (3-17, 2-6).MEN Cal Lutheran 88, Christ College 75 -- Dave Rowlands came off the bench to score 12 points and spark a second-half rally that saw the visiting Kingsmen outscore Christ College, 49-32, in the GSAC game. Center Mike Demeter, who along with Steve deLaveaga led Cal Lutheran with 26 points, scored 17 points in the first half. Jeff Logsdon had 12 points for Cal Lutheran (8-12, 2-4) and Charles Jackson had 10 points.
