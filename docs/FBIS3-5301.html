Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, March 4 KYODO -- The government decided on Friday [4 March] to designate the lead and zinc mining and smelting industry as a recession-hit industry eligible for government subsidies from March 9, 1994 until June 30, 1995. The industry is now in serious recession as a result of reduced demand from the automobile and steel industries and the yen's appreciation. When a certain industry is designated as a specific slump-hit enterprise, businesses in the industry will be given subsidies, to help them take steps to prevent unemployment and help workers laid off find new employment.
