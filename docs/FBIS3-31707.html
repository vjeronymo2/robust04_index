Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Text] Belgrade, 21 Feb (TANJUG) -- Two radars for the detection of the location of artillery fire have been flown to Sarajevo airport, TANJUG learned today from Belgrade UNPROFOR [UN Protection Forces] headquarters. The radars will "cover" Mt. Igman and the territory northeast and southeast of Sarajevo and will be able to locate the positions from which the cease-fire is violated, it is learned at the Belgrade UNPROFOR headquarters. UNPROFOR radio reports that after a long period of tension a certain sense of relief is felt today in Sarajevo and that General Michael Rose has said that the warring parties in the former Yugoslav Republic [of Bosnia-Herzegovina] have placed 268 artillery pieces under the "blue helmet's" control at nine locations.
