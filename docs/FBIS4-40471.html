BFN [Text] The State Duma's Security Committee has unanimously described the Russian president's edict on urgent measures to protect the population from gangsterism and other manifestations of organized crime as contradicting the Constitution and existing legislation. This was stated on Thursday in an interview to Interfax by Viktor Ilyukhin. He noted that this fact was reflected in the State Duma's decision, which had been prepared on this matter. The lower chamber plans to discuss the president's edict at a plenary meeting on Friday 17 June. Ilyukhin stressed that in conditions when the Constitutional Court is temporarily not operating, only the president can halt the operation of the edict. A recommendation to Boris Yeltsin to do this is also set out in the draft State Duma decision.
