930127 FT 27 JAN 93 / International Company News: Nortel posts 24% advance in final term SHARES IN Northern Telecom surged yesterday after the Canadian telecommunications equipment maker reported a surprisingly strong 24 per cent rise in fourth-quarter earnings. Fourth-quarter earnings climbed to USDollars 253m, or Dollars 1.02 a share, from Dollars 204m, or 83 cents, a year earlier. Revenues rose 10 per cent to Dollars 2.54bn. Order backlog at the end of the year was a record Dollars 3.9bn, up 19 per cent from December 1991. For the year as a whole, earnings climbed to Dollars 548m, or Dollars 2.17, from Dollars 515m, or Dollars 2.03. Revenues advanced to Dollars 8.41bn from Dollars 8.18bn. On the Toronto stock exchange yesterday, Nortel's shares jumped by CDollars 3 7/8 to CDollars 57 7/8 at the close. Dr Paul Stern, chairman, said income from almost all product lines improved last year. Slimmer margins in the highly competitive market for office switches was offset by stronger volumes.
