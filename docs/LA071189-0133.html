July 11, 1989, Tuesday, P.M. Final

NATION;

MET PERFORMERS ARRIVE IN BEIJING

A company featuring artists from the New York Metropolitan Opera arrived today in Beijing for the first prominent American cultural event since the bloody military crackdown on pro-democracy demonstrators last month.

"What happened in China is tragic for all concerned," Joann Grillo, managing director of Ambassadors of Opera and Concert Worldwide, said in an interview.

"However, China is a reality. The world must deal with her," she said, explaining the company's decision to proceed with its three performances in Beijing despite the May 20 imposition of martial law and the June 4 military crackdown in which hundreds or perhaps thousands of protesters were killed.

The Ambassadors of Opera and Concert Worldwide is an independent company that features performers from the New York Metropolitan Opera. Since 1983, it has staged operas and musicals throughout the world.

"I don't want to sound presumptuous, but if our concerts can bring some beauty into the lives of the people in Beijing, then we will have served our purpose," Grillo said.
