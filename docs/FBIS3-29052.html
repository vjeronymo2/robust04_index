Language: Korean Article Type:BFN [By Yu Sung-u] [Summary] North Korea has reportedly requested that ROK businesses invest capital into the development of Port Chongjin and is preparing for increased North-South trade by opening, within this year, a sea route between Chongjin and Pusan that is to serve twice a week. According to authorities concerned on 14 February, Chairman Li Tiehu of the Xianhu business group of Jilin Province, China, visited the ROK in early February to discuss this issue and conveyed the North Korean authorities' intention that "Rather than China or any other country, North Korea is now counting on the South Korean side for the necessary funds to develop Port Chongjin that is estimated to exceed $100 million," and added that "North Korea is gearing up efforts to open sea routes between Pusan and Chongjin, and Inchon and Nampo as part of a plan to devote all of Chongjin to free economic zones." [Citing a Chinese source, Seoul HANGUK ILBO in Korean on 15 February 1994 p 7 adds: "North Korea reportedly launched a feasibility study on opening Chongjin and Nampo as economic zones" and "North Korea once reviewed opening Sinuiju as an economic zone, only to withhold a final decision."] Xianhu Business has been learned to have signed an exclusive contract with the North Hamgyong Provincial Administrative and Economic Committee, and the Economic Cooperation Co. of North Korea to build a wharf for containers and cranes, a road connecting Chongjin and Horyong, and to expand an open storage yard to 237,000 sq. yds.
