Language: English Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] The ANC [African National Congress] has again called on the government to intervene in Bophuthatswana. ANC Secretary General Cyril Ramaphosa said that he expected the Transitional Executive Council to implement a range of steps aimed at bringing President Mangope's government to heel. [passage omitted] However, Foreign Affairs Minister Pik Botha has said that the government will not consider military intervention in Bophuthatswana while South African interests and nationals are not threatened. Mr. Botha was responding to a statement during Radio Today by a senior ANC official who said that it was the government's responsibility to deal with the present unrest situation in the state.
