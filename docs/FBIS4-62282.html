BFN [Text] Kiev -- Ivan Kyshko, member of the Central Electoral Commission for the election of the president of Ukraine, informed a UNIAN correspondent on 11 May, (the last day for submitting lists of signatures in support of contenders for the presidential election) that documents from district electoral commissions on the results of collecting signatures have been received by the Central Electoral Commission. According to the documents, Ivan Plyushch, Leonid Kravchuk, Leonid Kuchma, Oleksandr Moroz, Volodymyr Lanovyy, and Petro Talanchuk have gathered the required number of signatures (100,000). According to Ihor Kyshka, Valeriy Babych, Ivan Valenya, and Mykola Rud, who were unable to gather the required number of signatures within the established time limit, have withdrawn from the election campaign. With regard to the candidacies of Volodymyr Lanovyy and Petro Talanchuk, the Central Electoral Commission is clarifying information concerning the demands of the law on collecting 1,500 signatures in 18 oblasts. As a UNIAN correspondent learned from informed sources, the final number of signatures gathered is as follows: Leonid Kravchuk -- 270,000; Leonid Kuchma -- 210,000; Ivan Plyushch -- 130,000; Petro Talanchuk -- 120,000; Oleksandr Moroz -- 120,000; Volodymyr Lanovyy -- 100,000.
