BFN [By ITAR-TASS special correspondent] [Text] Bonn May 11 TASS--"Germany remains Russia's major foreign economic partner," Russian President Boris Yeltsin told a joint news conference he gave here together with German Chancellor Helmut Kohl on Wednesday. He reported that "issues of economic cooperation were also in the focus of attention at today's talks in Bonn". Under conditions of a complicated transition period Russia is presently going trough, it is difficult to avoid certain decline in economic ties. "Moreover, most far-sighted economic partners do not scale down their activity despite the temporary difficulties confronting Russia. I am confident that we will manage to overcome them", the president stressed. "During the talks, we agreed to pay most attention to such fields of cooperation as power engineering, machine-building, conversion, support of small and medium-sized business, especially in the production sphere. We suggested using such effective instrument of economic relations as non-interest bearing commercial credit, or the so-called `swing'", Yeltsin said, adding that "the discussion of trade and economic relations between Russia and Germany will be continued on the second day of his visit".
