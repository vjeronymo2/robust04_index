BFN [From NIHON KEIZAI SHIMBUN 23 April Morning Edition] [Text] On 22 April, a Foreign Ministry source indicated that Finance Minister Hirohisa Fujii and Treasury Secretary Lloyd Bentsen are expected to hold Japanese-U.S. finance ministerial talks in Washington prior to a meeting of finance ministers and central bank governors from the Group of Seven (G-7) major industrialized nations which is to be held there. Although a concrete schedule for the Fujii-Bentsen meeting has not yet been decided on, on 23 April both sides were coordinating the schedule. During the meeting with Bentsen, Fujii is expected to indicate the government's intention to maintain in the future its basic position on Japan's policies toward foreign countries and its domestic economic measures, as well on following a policy accord reached by the ruling coalition parties. Meanwhile, Bentsen is expected to call for additional economic pump-priming measures by Japan, claiming that "Japan should make voluntary efforts to achieve an economic recovery led by the expansion of domestic demand to lead a world economic recovery."
