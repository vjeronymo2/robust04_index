930518 FT 18 MAY 93 / Second building society backs PIA BRADFORD and Bingley, the UK's seventh largest building society, has welcomed plans to form the Personal Investment Authority, the controversial self-regulatory body for retail financial services, Norma Cohen writes. The endorsement by Bradford and Bingley, the only big building society to remain a dispenser of independent financial advice, follows a similar decision by Halifax, the market leader. Earlier this month it became the first society to back the authority. The authority is intended to bring all retail financial services providers, including banks and building societies, under the umbrella of a single self-regulatory body. But in the authority's planning stages, banks and building societies had baulked at joining, and life insurance companies had said they would not join unless the banks did as well. Mr Geoffrey Lister, Bradford and Bingley chairman, said: 'The PIA would bring consistent rules and standards of supervision to the whole industry.'
