Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Oslo, Jan 22 (AFP) -- Israeli Foreign Minister Shimon Peres and PLO Chairman Yasir 'Arafat concluded talks in Oslo Saturday night [22 January] aimed at jumpstarting the stalled Palestinian self-rule negotiations. The two leaders left agreeing to meet in Davos, Switzerland next Saturday, Foreign Ministry officials said. 'Arafat and Peres said they had made progress in the discussions, but did not go into any details. Acting Foreign Minister Bjoern Tore Godal was pleased with the talks. "I'm more optimistic now than I was a few days ago," Godal said. "'Arafat and Peres have a deep personal commitment" to the discussions, he added. 'Arafat and the Palestinian delegation left the meeting for the airport, due to fly to Saudi Arabia, while Peres was due to leave Sunday morning.
