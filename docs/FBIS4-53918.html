BFN [Text] Hanoi VNA May 21 -- A symposium on investment and business in Vietnam was held in Washington (USA) on May 19-20 in the presence of a Vietnamese delegation led by Deputy Prime Minister Tran Duc Luong who arrived in the US on May 18. Mr. Luong was accompanied by the cabinet minister in charge of cooperation and investment, and some vice ministers in charge of economic branches and businessmen. During the symposium which was sponsored by the `World Economic Development Congress' organization, Ms V. Foot, president of the US-Vietnam Commerce Council delivered an introdution on Deputy Prime Minister Tran Duc Luong's biography. Speaking at the symposium, Mr luong briefed the economic achievements recorded by the Vietnamese people in recent years. He noted that Vietnam's sound external policy in recent years has made important contributions to the renovation cause and economic achievements in Vietnam, at the same time integrated Vietnam into the international community. He also said that Vietnam was ready to join ASEAN as full member, welcomed the development of the relationship with APEC [Asia-Pacific Economic Cooperation], prepared for its accession to WTO (GATT) and broadened its economic ties with the European Union. On the US-Vietnam relations, he said, the two sides agreed in principles to settle technical issues relating to the exchanges of representative offices with regulations of general consulate. He reinterated that the Vietnamese Government and people expresssed their sympathies over the issue of Americans missing in action (MIA) and would continue their efforts to settle this humanitarian issue. During his stay in Washington, Deputy Prime Minister Tran Duc Luong met with Secretary of State W. Christopher and Senators J. Kerry, J. McCain and B. Johnston. Mr. Luong also called on the headquarters of World Bank and the International Monetary Fund in Washington.
