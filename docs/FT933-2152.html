930921 FT 21 SEP 93 / Turning point for UN, says Boutros THE PROCLAIMED renaissance of the United Nations after the end of the cold war may lack reality and the organisation remains at a turning point, according to Mr Boutros Boutros Ghali, the secretary general, writes Michael Littlejohns in New York. In the introduction to his annual report to the general assembly, which begins a new session today, he criticised members that pushed UN troops into conflicts 'where major powers are not willing to venture themselves.' At the same time, the UN was struggling to deal with problems of the poorest states in which rich nations were no longer so interested now that decades of ideological competition for their votes were at an end.
