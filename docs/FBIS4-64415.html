BFN [Text] Ankara, May 2 (A.A.) -- Iranian Foreign Minister First Assistant [title as received] Mohammad Hashemi arrived here for an official visit on Monday [2 Monday]. Hashemi was welcomed at the airport by Foreign Ministry Under Secretary Ozdem Sanberk. Sanberk said that Hashemi's visit was very important to improve better understanding and to resolve problems between Turkey and Iran at a time of ambiguity in the region. He added that Hashemi was to hold contacts in Ankara under a semi-annual political consultation mechanism set up between the two countries. Sanberk stressed that Iran was a friendly and brotherly country and that the border between the two countries remained stable. He stressed that Turkey and Iran had mutual interests. During Hashemi's visit, the Armenian invasion of Azerbaijan, the latest situation in the Caucasus, Bosnia, and Iraq will be discussed. Sanberk added that high-level mutual visits between the two countries were to continue and "thus, relations between Turkey and Iran will be furthered." Hashemi said that the situation of the region and the world necessitated furthering relations and consultations between the two countries and that he was very pleased to be visiting Turkey. He said that the region in which Turkey and Iran are face various problems and his current visit to Turkey was related to these turbulences and wars. He pointed out that he was paying his first visit to Turkey as the first assistant to the foreign minister and that this showed the importance Iran attached to Turkey. "This visit is an opportunity for us," Hashemi said.
