BFN [Text] Wataru Kubo, secretary general of the opposition Social Democratic Party of Japan [SDPJ], held a news conference at a hotel in Hachinohe City, Aomori Prefecture, on 4 June. During the news conference, he touched on the issue of imposing economic sanctions against North Korea for its suspected development of nuclear weapons and stated: "At a time when the United Nations has yet to make any decision, Japan, the United States, and South Korea must be cautious about the economic sanctions against North Korea. The SDPJ takes a position against the economic sanctions on North Korea." In this way, Kubo made it clear that the SDPJ will oppose any economic sanctions imposed by Japan, the United States, and South Korea against North Korea. During the news conference, Kubo also said that, through an SDPJ delegation which is scheduled to leave Japan for Pyongyang on 10 June, the party intends to urge North Korea to accept nuclear inspections by the International Atomic Energy Agency [IAEA] and hold dialogue with the United States and South Korea.
