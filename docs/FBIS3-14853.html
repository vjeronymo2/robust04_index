Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] A high-ranking officer of the Russian special service stressed that certain circles in the U.S.A. intended to make a pause in the bilateral relations due to the case of Ameses in order to examine what was happening in Russia. [sentence as received] According to the same source, there was definitive pressure exercised on U.S. President Bill Clinton and his Aide Strobe Talbott in this direction. Commenting on possible response of the U.S. to expulsion of U.S. Minister-Counsellor James Morris from Russia, the high-ranking officer told Interfax that "escalation was undesirable. However, Interfax it is to take place it will be bilateral." He said Moscow was unwilling to surrender its positions. He also emphasized that, in his opinion, the lay off of the Federal Intelligence Service's Director General, Nikolay Golushko, had nothing to do with the Ameses.
