940323 FT 23 MAR 94 / Talks 'mishap' sparks cod row Denmark and Sweden have clashed over Baltic cod quotas following Sweden's accession negotiations with the European Union. Under the agreement, Sweden's share of the cod quota will rise from 35 to 40 per cent. Fishermen on the Danish island of Bornholm are urging Denmark to vote against Swedish EU membership unless they renounce the extra 5 per cent. But Mr Carl Bildt, the Swedish prime minister, said: 'A deal is a deal.' The extra quota was awarded to Sweden in the final, hectic phase of the negotiations, unknown to Danish negotiators. 'It was a technical mishap,' said Mr Niels Helveg Petersen, the Danish foreign minister.
