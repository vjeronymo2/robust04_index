January 8, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

OUTTAKES: CAMERA ANGLES

Sources tell us that Kevin Costner will make his directorial debut in March when Island Pics shoots "Dances With Wolves" in Mexico. The 19th-Century romantic adventure, adapted by Michael Blake from his novel, centers on the relationship between a white man and an Indian woman in the Southwest.

Reps for Costner and Island wouldn't comment.

A source said that Costner, who's been searching for a project to direct for about a year, zeroed in on "Wolves" partly because it could be made economically. He'll have the only major English-speaking role, with the other principal parts written in Indian and Spanish dialects.

Also signing up for his first directing job is "Moonstruck" writer John Patrick Shanley. The project is "Joe vs. the Volcano" at Warner Bros., a comic fantasy with Tom Hanks as a man who sells his soul to the devil. Scheduled to shoot next summer. Leonard Klady
