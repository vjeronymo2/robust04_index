February 4, 1989, Saturday, San Diego County Edition

CLAYTON'S THREE-POINTER LIFTS SAN PASQUAL PAST ESCONDIDO

Jimmy Clayton made his only shot of the night -- a three-pointer with 5 seconds remaining -- count to lift San Pasqual past Escondido, 56-54, in an Avocado League boys' basketball game Friday at San Pasqual.

Escondido could not get a shot off in the final seconds as San Pasqual improved to 9-9 and 6-4. Escondido is 9-9 and 4-6.

With San Pasqual leading by nine with 4:30 left, Erik Meek (17 points, 16 rebounds) fouled out of the game. Escondido's Chris Heintschel (14 points) then hit three three-pointers to tie the game.

San Pasqual had taken a 16-7 lead after 8 minutes and increased that to 40-30 by the end of the third quarter.

Brooks Barnhard scored a game-high 18 points for Escondido. Bryan Banner and Andy Loveland scored 12 each for San Pasqual.

In other Avocado League games:

El Camino 83, San Marcos 49 -- Travis Sweitzer led all scorers with 23 points, but El Camino had four players in double figures. Travis Gilley (17), Todd Swanson (14), Shaun Scurry (13) and Glen Ankton (12) led the Wildcats (16-5, 9-1). El Camino outrebounded San Marcos, 52-40. Swanson had 14 assists and 4 steals, Boyer added 16 rebounds and 6 blocks, and Ankton had 4 steals and 7 rebounds. San Marcos is 5-14, 1-8.

Ramona 59, Carlsbad 52 -- Sophomore Todd Dick made two free throws and a layup down the stretch, and Ramona held Carlsbad scoreless over the final 3 minutes at Ramona. Leading, 53-52, with 3 minutes left, Tony Newman (26 points) and Dick took control. Newman added 11 rebounds, and Dick and Troy Wood each had 9. Calvin Coleman scored 18 points for Carlsbad. Ramona is 8-12, 5-5.

Oceanside 62, Rancho Buena Vista 60 -- The Longhorns, down by nine with a minute to go, hit two three-pointers but it wasn't enough as Oceanside held on. Jerry Garrett led Oceanside with 14 points. Todd Fellars' 23 points led Rancho Buena Vista (9-12, 5-5).

City Western League

USDHS 66, Kearny 60 -- Peter Ellis led all scorers with 28 for USDHS (9-8, 2-4). Andre White hit four in a row in the third quarter to put Kearny (11-12, 2-4) up by one. USDHS took control in the fourth, converting 10 of 10 free throws in the final 3 minutes.

Clairemont 78, University City 66 -- Ray McDavid scored 41 points and had 12 rebounds and 10 assists for Clairemont (10-13, 2-4). Clairemont jumped to a 24-8 lead in the first quarter. University City is now 12-8 and 4-2.

Mission Bay 57, La Jolla 49 -- Brett Grebing had 10 points and 11 rebounds as host Mission Bay won its fourth game in a row to create a three-way tie for first place in the City West. Mission Bay (12-6, 4-2) made 6 of 9 first-quarter shots to take a 16-5 lead it never relinquished. Peter Wilson had 13 points and seven assists for Mission Bay, which was defeated by La Jolla (4-2) by five in the league opener. In that game, La Jolla controlled the boards. But this time, Mission Bay had a 42-23 rebound advantage. David Berteaux scored 16 points for La Jolla.

City Eastern League

Patrick Henry 75, Madison 65 -- Terence Hamilton scored 22 points and Jason Lonsdale and Bart Brandenburg had 17 apiece for Henry (12-8, 4-2). Brad Eaddy's 25 points led Madison (12-9, 4-2).

Grossmont 3-A League

John Pressler and Andy Merideth had 14 points each for Helix (15-5, 6-1). John Russell scored 12 points, and Scott Georgens added 10 before fouling out with a minute left for Granite Hills (14-7, 3-2).

Palomar League

Castle Park 73, Montgomery 58 -- Brad Grady scored 21 points and Gerardo Gomez added 10 for Castle Park. Albert Maldonado had 19 for Montgomery.

Chula Vista 34, Marian 31 -- Sean Desmond (12 points) hit a 12-foot jump shot and made two free throws with 5 seconds left to lead Chula Vista (11-13, 8-3) at Marian.
