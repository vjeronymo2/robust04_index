920109 FT 09 JAN 92 / Deutsche Bank employees fired Deutsche Bank, Germany's largest bank, has dismissed two employees and filed criminal charges against them after discovering irregularities during the course of a routine investigation, writes David Waller in Frankfurt. The bank said yesterday that the employees were back-office staff involved in the processing of stock-market transactions at the Frankfurt branch, and that the head of the relevant department had been suspended pending further inquiries. It stressed that the irregularities had nothing to do with trading activities.
