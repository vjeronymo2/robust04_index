BFN [Text] Seoul, May 19 (YONHAP) -- The South Korean Government will propose joint research to industrialize Russia's advanced technology in the machinery, materials and aircraft fields as well as making better use of scientific brainpower over the long term. While demanding overdue payment of 7 million U.S. dollars for Korean exports, Seoul is considering letting Moscow pay in resources to make up for its lack of cash. The government wants to construct an exclusive port for Korean vessels in Vostochnyy near Vladivostok and is also seeking to use a harbor in the nearby Tuman (Tumen) River. It will present its wish list at the first Korea-Russia Economic Cooperation Committee meeting, slated to run two days beginning Friday in Seoul. Korea will negotiate to set up a research base in Russia and study the former Soviet republic's latest technologies as well as pursue joint research. It will mull joining hands with foreign companies to explore Russia's vast resources while encouraging local firms to form consortiums to take part in resource development. The government will offer support by putting up funds. Korea will seek Russia's cooperation on the resumption of fishing in the Okhotsk sea with a catch quota of 300,000 tons of pollack in the upcoming talks. They will also discuss laying optical submarine cables linking Korea, Russia and Japan, and possibly inviting North Korea to take part in the project.
