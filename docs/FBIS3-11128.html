Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Caracas, 12 Mar (EFE) -- Members of an Army intelligence unit detected and dismantled a network of concealed microphones at Miraflores Palace, seat of the Venezuelan Government. The Army is now seeking the "brains" of the espionage system, the local press reported today. Unofficial sources at Miraflores Palace remarked that the microphones had been placed in the main rooms of the building, including the office of President Rafael Caldera and the room where the Council of Ministers meets regularly. Although it has not yet been confirmed, it is believed that, through this high-technology system, those responsible for telephone espionage also had access to the main offices of the Ministry of Defense. A report published by the daily "EL GLOBO" states the system's connections reportedly linked the network to the private offices of former President Carlos Andres Perez and the main office of Banco Latino, in which the government intervened last January and where a sophisticated espionage center was discovered. Following the bank intervention, congressional investigators asserted that hundreds of telephones of prominent Venezuelan political and business personalities were wiretapped from that office, but the wires leading to hidden microphones were not discovered. However, it has not been ruled out that the investigation will follow this course, especially if it is confirmed the network discovered at Miraflores leads to terminals located at the main office of Banco Latino, whose former President Gustavo Gomez Lopez is accused of planning a coup d'etat. The location of Gomez Lopez, for whom an arrest warrant was issued over the bankruptcy of Banco Latino, is not known. It is only known that he is abroad. However, on several occasions Gomez Lopez has contacted local media to reject the accusations. Even so, Venezuelan political leaders view Gomez Lopez as the main ideologist of a coup d'etat, planned with former Defense Minister Radames Munoz. This coup attempt was reportedly aimed at preventing the electoral victory of Rafael Caldera last 5 December. Those who made accusations against Gomez Lopez argue the intentions of the banker and Munoz were definitely foiled after the military officer disclosed his plans to U.S. Government officials, who warned they would not go along with an attempt against the Venezuelan democratic regime.
