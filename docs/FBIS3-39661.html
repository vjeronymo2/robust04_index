Language: German Article Type:BFN [Text] Bonn (DPA) -- On Friday [18 February], Federal Chancellor Helmut Kohl had another telephone conversation with Russian President Yeltsin to discuss recent developments regarding the withdrawal of all heavy arms from the city of Sarajevo, which is being besieged by the Serbs. Government Spokesman Dieter Vogel said in Bonn that the main point of the conversation was to urge the Serbs to withdraw their heavy arms or put them under UN control still before the ultimatum expires on Monday. Foreign Minister Klaus Kinkel, of the Free Democratic Party of Germany, is also involved in Kohl's talks with the Russian leadership. According to Vogel, the Federal Government paid tribute to Russia's efforts to ease tensions in Sarajevo. The Serbian side is now expected to finally raise the siege of the Bosnian capital.
