Language: English Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Jakarta, Jan 17 (AFP) -- Two senior United Nations officials will visit jailed East Timor independence movement leader Xanana Gusmao in his Jakarta prison, sources close to the delegation said Monday. [passage omitted] The UN envoys, Tamrat Samuel and Francesco Vendrell, arrived in Jakarta Sunday and had talks with Indonesian Foreign Minister Ali Alatas. Alatas would not confirm that the envoys would visit Gusmao, but told AFP there was a "possibility" they would do so. He said his talks with the envoys had centered on new discussions on East Timor he was scheduled to have with Portuguese Foreign Minister Jose Manuel Durao Barroso on May 6, under the auspices of UN Secretary General Butrus Butrus-Ghali.
