940113 FT 13 JAN 94 / UK Company News: Hoskins holders back transformation plan Mr Howard Hodgson, the ebullient former funeral director, has received shareholders' backing to transform a tiny USM-traded brewery into a tobacco accessories and airport shops supplier. A special meeting yesterday approved the change of name from Hoskins Brewery to Halkin Holdings. Trading in both the existing shares and those issued to fund the metamorphosis is set to begin on the Official List this morning. Some 25m new shares at 68p by have been issued by way of a placing, subscription and rights issue to raise Pounds 15m. The issue price compares with a price of 78p on Tuesday when the shares were suspended. Shareholders supported all the resolutions put forward. These included the Pounds 10m cash acquisition of the business of Ronson, a manufacturer of cigarette lighters and other smokers' requisites and the Pounds 9.5m partial cash alternative offer for LGW, the airport shops supplier. The Ronson acquisition, upon which the LGW acquisition is conditional, is expected to be completed today.
