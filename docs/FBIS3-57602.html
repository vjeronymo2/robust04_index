Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Baku (January 25) TURAN--Armenia asked Russia to help in solving the problems regarding displacement of Russian troops at the borders of Azerbaijan with Iran. This problem was briefly discussed during the talks between Russian and Armenian Ministers of Foreign Affairs Andrey Kozyrev and Vahan Papazyan on January 20 in Moscow, a reliable source from Russian Ministry of Foreign Affairs reported. Yerevan believes, that "being cut off from Iran by a Russian buffer, Azerbaijan will be more complacent at the negotiation table, and at the fronts as well". The same source informed, that "the Russian party has the impression, Armenian side is looking for a respite after the serious defeats it was incurred during the recent battles." According to the plan, Azerbaijan is to give broad political autonomy to mountainous Karabakh, "to offer as a gift", Shusha to the Armenians and to work out a plan for "humanitarian corridors" from Armenia to mountainous Karabakh via the towns of Lachin and Kelbadzhar. This plan of Andranik Mihranyan, Russian president's adviser also envisions the creation of a demilitarized buffer zone, along all the borders of mountainous Karabakh. The responsibility for administrative-legal control in the zone will be carried out by the Russian peacemaking forces. Representative of so-called RMK [Republic of Mountainous Karabakh] Arkadiy Gukasyan, as a member of delegation from the Republic of Armenia was participating in negotiations with Kozyrev. However, according to the official report of Russian Minister of Foreign Affairs Andrey Kozyrev met Papazyan and Gukasyan separately.
