931222 FT 22 DEC 93 / Observer: Switched on W Edwards Deming, who died this week, may have been one of the most influential figures in modern industrial management, but that is not what sticks in the mind of one of Observer's colleagues. His enduring memory is of a conference in Detroit where an awe-struck audience became increasingly puzzled by a rambling and somewhat disjointed speech by Deming, then in his 70s. It was not until question time that the man who taught the Japanese Total Quality Management admitted to having been beaten by one of life's simpler challenges. He had not been able to read his prepared text because he could not figure out how to switch on the light on the lectern.
