April 14, 1989, Friday, Southland Edition

2 TRAINS COLLIDE IN JAPAN; 118 INJURED

Two trains collided head-on Thursday at a station in central Japan, injuring 118 people, most of them schoolchildren, Kyodo News Agency reported.

The agency said a two-car train with 220 passengers rammed into the front of a stationary three-car train carrying 330 people at Minami Minowa, about 100 miles west of Tokyo. It quoted police as saying the accident was caused either by a fault in automatic switching or driver error.
