BFN [Interview with Bulgarian Socialist Party leader Zhan Videnov by Rozalina Evdokimova; place and date not given: "Urgent Changes in Government Are Needed"] [Excerpts] [Evdokimova] Mr. Videnov, do you see a coincidence between the interview President Zhelev gave to the BBC British radio station and the arrival of observers of the Parliamentary Assembly of the Council of Europe in Bulgaria, who will analyze the political situation and "recommunization" in Bulgaria? [Videnov] I do not think that there is such a connection, however, it is obvious that ideological statements and scholastic disputes, as well as deliberately fanning confrontation rather than discussing the real socioeconomic problems of the Bulgarians today, are a basic issue for President Zhelev, and for people who do not know our country's reality. It is not strange that foreigners assume such a position. They are far away from the country's reality and not especially concerned with its real problems. However, the position of the president is absolutely inadmissible for any head of state. [Evdokimova] Mr. Zhelev devoted a lot of space in his interview to the Bulgarian Socialist Party [BSP] and the situation in our country. Speaking about the BSP's support for the cabinet, Mr. Zhelev said that this is nothing else but a road leading to new elections, already in the fall. What could you say about this? [Videnov] The fact that the cabinet relies on the BSP despite the fact that Berov's cabinet contains many BSP opponents from the near and remote past, implies that the BSP is a highly responsible, state-responsible, and competent party, capable of participating in the government (unlike the other parties, which regardless of the people's vote results, are unable of translating this vote to governing). I include among these parties the Union of Democratic Forces [SDS] that President Zhelev likes so much, and even the president himself, as former SDS leader. Within the framework of the confidence received by the people in 1991 the BSP helps the country to resolve its most urgent problems. It is the success or failure of these efforts that will determine when Bulgaria will head toward new elections. It must be pointed out that these are the efforts of the entire country, rather than the efforts of a single party or the government. [passage omitted] [Evdokimova] The BSP is being ever more frequently accused of being responsible for the economic and social situation that emerged in the country, and for supporting a cabinet that cannot do its job. What could you say about this? [Videnov] The situation could be corrected through early parliamentary elections. If someone thinks that he is ready for such a step toward decisively improving the situation in the country, let him convince the people that they should participate in such a step. However, the people are not convinced of this. It is not clear what early elections could contribute to the country. As long as the cabinet remains within the framework of its program and tries to move things forward, it could rely on a certain parliamentary consensus. This support will be denied when the cabinet fails. [passage omitted] [Evdokimova] Will you demand personnel changes in the cabinet? [Videnov] Of course. They are absolutely necessary and should have been effected immediately after the budget was adopted. The changes have been postponed because of the prime minister's illness, however, they will become topical again, as soon as next week.
