BFN [Editorial Report] Sarajevo Radio Bosnia-Herzegovina Network in Serbo-Croatian at 0800 GMT on 31 March reports fighting in the following parts of the country: The correspondent from Gorazde, Enes Musovic, reports that "the chetniks have been pounding all lines of defense of the Bosnia-Herzegovina Army fighters with artillery of the largest calibers the entire night." As he continues to say, "the situation is extremely fierce on all lines of Gorazde's defense on the right bank of the Drina River" while "on the left bank of the Drina River the chetniks are attacking from Ustipraca and Mesici at Kopaci and Trovrh." "From 1900 last night to 0800 this morning one person died and eight sustained serious injuries. Yesterday and the day before, 15 people were killed and 40 injured." "The activities of the Serbian aggressor against the Bihac district have continued overnight and this morning," correspondent Mirza Sadikovic says. "Yesterday, in the early evening, Karadzic's terrorists attacked the center of Bihac [words indistinct] as well as the hospital. New material damage was caused and, according to incomplete reports, there are injured civilians. In the course of the night, the intensity of shelling subsided a little because of low flights by NATO aircraft." "In other areas," Sadikovic goes on, "antiaircraft arms' fire and sniper fire were observed in the course of the night and since early morning." "On the separating lines with the paramilitary formations of the so-called Kladusa autonomists, there have been provocative attacks with mortars and hill guns at various intervals, launched from the area of Krivaja and [word indistinct] in the Kladusa commune."
