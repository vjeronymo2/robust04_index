BFN [Text] Tokyo, June 22 KYODO -- Former U.S. President Jimmy Carter will make an unofficial visit to Japan from July 2 to 9 at the invitation of private organizations, government sources said Wednesday [22 June]. The government is arranging meetings between Carter, and Prime Minister Tsutomu Hata and Foreign Minister Koji Kakizawa to hear from Carter about his recent meeting with North Korean President Kim Il-song, the sources said. During the eight-day visit, Carter is scheduled to attend an international symposium on development of local communities in Yamagata Prefecture, northern Japan, and meetings of economic organizations in Tokyo, they said.
