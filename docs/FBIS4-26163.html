BFN [Text] New Delhi, June 18 (XINHUA) -- A Sino-Indian conference on the five principles of peaceful coexistence will be held on June 27 to 28. The five principles of peaceful coexistence in the contemporary world first formulated by China and India in 1954 would be the main theme of the conference, officials here said today. It is learned that Indian Prime Minister Narasimha Rao would inaugurate the conference and eminent academics, diplomats and intellectuals from the two countries would attend the conference. The main theme would be discussed in three sessions-- international relations and diplomacy, world trade and economic systems in connection with challenges and opportunities, and lasting peace and security issues.
