BFN [Text] Tokyo, April 1 KYODO -- A delegation from the largest party in Japan's ruling coalition left Friday [1 April] for Beijing for a three-day visit there for talks with Chinese Communist Party officials. The group led by Issei Inoue, vice chairman of the Social Democratic Party [of Japan] (SDP) [SDPJ], will discuss with Chinese officials preparations for a meeting of the socialists international, a grouping of socialist parties from some 60 countries, set for May in Tokyo. Inoue is expected to ask China to exert its influence over North Korea to accept full inspections by the International Atomic Energy Agency, SDPJ officials said.
