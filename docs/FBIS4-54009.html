BFN [Text] The ruling Democratic Socialist Party [DSP] and the ruling New Japan Party are advocating breaking up the unified parliamentary group "Kaishin" [Innovation]. Commenting on the two ruling parties' move at an 18 May meeting of Komeito legislators, Komeito Secretary General Yuichi Ichikawa said: "If the two ruling parties, which formed "Kaishin," have altered their policies, their leadership cannot escape the responsibility." In this way, he indicated that if the "Kaishin" is dissolved, Keigo Ouchi, chairman of the DSP; Morihiro Hosokawa, representative of the Japan New Party; and Ichiro Ozawa, secretary general of Shinseito [Japan Renewal Party], would need to step down.
