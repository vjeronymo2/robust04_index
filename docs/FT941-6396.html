940302 FT 02 MAR 94 / Reforms at TUC win wide backing Mr David Hunt, employment secretary, was joined by other political parties, employers' organisations and industry leaders in welcoming the Trades Union Congress's announcement yesterday of a 'relaunch' of its aims. The TUC said it wanted to broaden its links and exert influence across the political spectrum and not just inside the Labour party. Mr Hunt said he saw the modernisation as 'a welcome and necessary step if unions are to market their services to their individual members more effectively in the workplace and help employers to become more competitive'. Mr John Smith, Labour leader, acknowledged that 'the TUC . . . should have the widest possible influence'. Mr John Monks, TUC general secretary, said the congress wanted to 'signal that we want to work as partners with employers who will work with us to maximise job and income security'. Mr Howard Davies, director-general of the CBI, said he believed the new approach would 'help to develop even better understanding between employers and unions'. Sir Denys Henderson, chairman of ICI, said: 'It is important the TUC should be able to function efficiently and effectively.'
