BFN [Text] Tallinn, Apr 12, BNS -- The second session of a joint commission of the European Free Trade Association [EFTA] and Estonia on April 11 in Tallinn was informative, Foreign Ministry Deputy Chancellor Priit Kolbre told BNS. The participants surveyed their countries' economic situation and cast a glance on the future. The session underlined the importance of the free-trade agreements Estonia has concluded with EFTA member states. The Baltic republic's major trade partners among EFTA members are Finland and Sweden, but contacts are maintained also with Norway and Switzerland, Kolbre said. The session was attended by representatives from six EFTA member states as well as the EFTA secretariat. The trade organization was officially represented by Ambassador Veijo Sampovaara from the Finnish Foreign Ministry. EFTA Secretary General Georg Reisch also attended the meeting. The relations between EFTA and Estonia rest on a cooperation declaration signed in 1991 in Geneva, which affirms mutual readiness to create conditions for commercial and economic cooperation with the eventual aim of establishing a free trade regime. The EFTA-Estonian joint commission first met in 1993 in Stockholm.
