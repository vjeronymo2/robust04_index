930217 FT 17 FEB 93 / International Company News: Profits up at Coca-Cola bottler COCA-Cola Enterprises, the world's largest bottler of Coca-Cola products, reported an increase in operating income in the fourth quarter, although special charges and other considerations led to a per share loss. Operating profit, excluding required accounting charges, rose to Dollars 181m, up from an adjusted Dollars 121m a year earlier. For the year the operating profit came to Dollars 725m excluding one-time special charges, up from Dollars 667m. Fourth quarter sales were Dollars 1.3bn, up from Dollars 1.2bn. For the year, sales were barely changed at Dollars 5.1bn, compared with Dollars 5bn. The bottler, 44 per cent owned by the Coca-Cola Company, underwent extensive restructuring in 1992, trimming administrative staff and decentralising operations. It said the weak US economy and unusually cool, wet weather, cut soft drink demand. Mr Summerfield Johnston, chief executive, said: 'We view a 9 per cent increase in cash operating profit as tangible evidence of the company's progress.'
