Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Prague Jan 4 (CTK) -- Losses in Czech agriculture in 1993 can be roughly estimated at between 8.1 and 8.3 billion crowns (about 275,000 USD), the daily HOSPODARSKE NOVINY writes today. The data, obtained from the director of the Research Institute for Agricultural Economics, Josef Kraus, resulted from the institute's analysis and are preliminary. Of the loss, 4.14 billion crowns was generated by state farms and 2.24 billion crowns by agricultural cooperatives. Private farmers showed the best results: They showed a profit when compensation for the work of the farmer and family members are not included. Businesses involved in agricultural primary production saw a loss of 384 million crowns, while other agricultural companies, such as school farms, ended up with 1.2 to 1.4 billion crowns in aggregate losses. Debts owed by the farms worsen the situation. They total 42.2 billion crowns, more than twice the amount of claims -- 19.7 billion crowns.
