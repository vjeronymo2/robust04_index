Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Seoul, Dec. 2 (YONHAP) -- Chon Pung-il, a leading nuclear scientist, became the first South Korean elected to a high-level post with the International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA) on Thursday. IAEA Director-General Hans Blix announced at the opening of the Board of Governors meeting in Vienna that Chon had been elected director of the Nuclear Power Division. Chon graduated from Seoul National University and has a Ph.D. from Carnegie Mellon University in the United States. He is deputy director of the atomic industry center of the Korea Atomic Energy Research Institute (KAERI). He will be in charge of consultation and technical assistance to IAEA members on nuclear reactor construction, safety management, repair and cost analysis as well as running the power reactor information system, which covers over 420 reactors and 72 under construction. Chon assumes the three-year post early next year.
