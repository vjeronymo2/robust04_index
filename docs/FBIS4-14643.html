BFN [From 4 April PAEVALEHT, HOMMIKULEHT, p 1; "News Bulletin" No. 63/94] [Text] Chief of staff of the Defence Forces GHQ Colonel Ants Laaneots has announced his resignation which is to come in force on April 18. Colonel Arvo Sirel, currently head of the GHQ I section, is to take over. Sirel, 49, has served in the Soviet missile forces and graduated from the artillery academy in Moscow. He was awarded the rank of colonel in December 1993. Laaneots, 45, has also served in the Soviet Army, graduate of the armoured forces academy in 1981. He commanded a tank regiment and was a chief of staff of an armoured division. He was Soviet military advisor in Ethiopia in 1987-1989, from September 1989 served as military commissar of Tartu. Prime Minister Edgar Savisaar appointed Laaneots government advisor and acting head of the Defence Forces GHQ in 1991. Colonel Laaneots declined from commenting the reasons of his retirement, adding that he intends to rest first. The last two and a half years have been exhausting, he said. Chairman of the Riigikogu [parliament] defence commission Rein Helme expressed his regret and added that "an atmosphere of accusations and suspicion has developed at the GHQ, which obstructs normal work."
