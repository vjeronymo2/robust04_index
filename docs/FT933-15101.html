930710 FT 10 JUL 93 / UK Company News: Hawtin surges and paying interim Hawtin is returning to paying interim dividends after an absence of 20 years as half-time profits have expanded 51 per cent. For the six months ended March 31 1993 the pre-tax outcome was Pounds 783,000 (Pounds 518,000) from turnover of Pounds 13.1m (Pounds 13.9m). Earnings per share were 0.75p (0.49p) and the interim payment is 0.25p. Operating profit rose to Pounds 1.15m (Pounds 980,000) while interest charges were reduced by Pounds 83,000 to Pounds 383,000. Mr Phillip Dovey, managing director, was 'particularly pleased' with operating profit in the building products division in view of the difficult trading conditions. That operation contributed Pounds 122,000 (Pounds 17,000). In other activities leisure and fitness accounted for Pounds 650,000 (Pounds 672,000), textiles Pounds 316,000 (Pounds 229,000) and property Pounds 329,000 (Pounds 219,000).
