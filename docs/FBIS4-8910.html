BFN [Text] Forty-three people, including 6 children, were killed and 112 were wounded in Gorazde yesterday, UNHCR spokesman Peter Kessler said on Wednesday [20 April]. A UNHCR building in Gorazde was hit twice at about 1800 yesterday evening, but there were no casualties among the staff, Kessler said, quoting reports by UNHCR personnel on the ground. The shelling of Gorazde continued on Wednesday morning, but less intensely, Ron Redman, UNHCR spokesman in Geneva, said after talking to Gorazde ham operators at 0700 this morning.
