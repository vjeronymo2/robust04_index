BFN [Text] La Paz, 28 Apr (AFP) -- President Gonzalo Sanchez de Losada spoke on Thursday [date not further specified] of the danger of a "narco-guerrilla" and powerful drug cartels like those of Colombia developing in Bolivia if a general drug enforcement policy is not quickly defined. Sanchez de Lozada said that this new policy, which he will present to Congress next August, must not be imposed from abroad. He said this to a local newspaper, alluding to the problems created in Bolivia by U.S. pressure to radicalize drug enforcement and to eradicate coca plantations in El Chapare, the largest cocaine raw material producing zone in the world. The chief of state said: "Trafficking is a very serious problem for all sectors of society and it affects Bolivian relations with the rest of the world." The president said he was worried about the lack of a consistent drug enforcement strategy. He stated that most of the coca produced in El Chapare--approximately 60,000 tonnes per year--ends up in drug production. "Nobody chews coca in El Chapare; coca produced in Yungas region is taken to El Chapare, and all goes to produce drugs. The atmosphere in the region favors the establishment of drug cartels or narco-guerrillas," Sanchez de Lozada said. Regarding the policy being prepared by his administration, Sanchez de Lozada mentioned the creation of a National Drug Enforcement Council [Consejo Nacional de Lucha Contra el Narcotrafico] presided by Foreign Minister Antonio Aranibar and comprising Justice Minister Jorge Blattman, Sustainable Development Minister Guillermo Justiniano, and Minister of Government German Quiroga. This council must find solutions in Bolivia and not solutions imposed from abroad, the president said, adding that this reorganization includes defining a policy toward the United States. "This is far too important for them (the United States) because after many years they have recognized the need for shared responsibility. It is our obligation to generate a proposal that will really solve this problem," Sanchez de Lozada said.
