BFN [Text] Bucharest, ROMPRES, 22/6/1994 -- The vice president of the State Duma (Parliament) of the Russian Federation, Valentin Kovalev, told a press conference held at the Romanian Parliament building that an improvement of the customs regulations, blended with the growth of investment in international trade would boost by some 15 per cent the import-export activities between the countries affiliated to the Black Sea Economic Cooperation Organization. Besides these steps, the Russian Federation contemplates other means as well for the economic development in this area, such as a Black Sea Cooperation Bank, a consolidated infrastructure for transports, including a circular auto route around the Black Sea, and a unitary power grid in the entire zone.
