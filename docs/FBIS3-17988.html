Language: Georgian Article Type:BFN [Text] Tbilisi, 31 Mar -- According to IBERIA news agency, today employees of the Tbilisi Police Department gathered at the Parliament building. About 500 of them remained outside the building while approximately 300 entered the parliament building. After confirmation by Parliament of Shota Kviraya as the interior minister of the Republic of Georgia, the police employees started knocking at the door, demanding that the head of state come outside. There were clashes between the head of state's security guard and police when Shevardnadze came out of the parliament building. Tbilisi police department chief David Zeikidze accused Shevardnadze of breaking his promise and demanded that the case of the interior minister be revised. Negotiations were held between the head of the Georgian state and the representatives of the Tbilisi Police department. A certain agreement has been reached. Negotiations are still continuing (by 1345).
