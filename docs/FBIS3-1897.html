Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, March 2 (XINHUA) -- Parliamentarians, experts and scholars from 14 Asian countries arrived here today on separate occasions to attend the 10th Asian parliamentarians' meeting on population and development, scheduled to open tomorrow. The participants including representatives of the United Nations and other international organizations will also take part in the second session of the fourth executive committee of the Asian Forum of Parliamentarians on Population and Development (AFPPD). The 10th Asian parliamentarians' meeting on population and development will be held according to a proposal made by the executive committee of the AFPPD at its fourth meeting held in Kuala Lumpur, capital of Malaysia, last October. This is also the first meeting to be held in China sponsored by the Asian Population and Development Association (APDA). The meeting is aimed at further enhancing exchanges and cooperation on population among parliamentarians of these countries. The main topics of the two-day meeting are about the issue of population and development and women in the 21st century -- strategy for prosperity and peace. Zhao Dongwan, leader of the delegation of China's National People's Congress (NPC) and chairman of the NPC Education, Science, Culture and Public Health Committee, will address the meeting at the opening ceremony. Since its founding in 1982, the APDA has provided financial aid to meetings of the AFPPD. It also sponsored nine Asian parliamentarians' meetings on population and development, all of which were attended by delegations from the NPC.
