940804 FT 04 AUG 94 / Adams optimistic over peace prospects Mr Gerry Adams, Sinn Fein leader, spoke last night of 'guarded optimism' over the prospects for peace in Northern Ireland after discussing a ceasefire with the IRA. Speaking 10 days after Sinn Fein in effect rejected the Downing Street declaration, Mr Adams said: 'The peace process remains firmly on the tracks and it is my view that it will not be derailed, and, in fact, that it can move forward.'
