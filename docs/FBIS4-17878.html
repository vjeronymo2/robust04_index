BFN [Text] Russia and the United States now have no fundamental differences on problems of an Arab-Israeli settlement, Russia's Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev stated after his meeting in Geneva with U.S. Secretary of State Warren Christopher. In the past week Moscow was the center of the Middle East peace process, he said: it was visited by PLO leader Yasir `Arafat and Israel's Prime Minister Yitzhaq Rabin. Now Washington is, as it were, taking over the baton from us, and it is very important for our actions to be coordinated, Kozyrev stressed. The Russian minister had in mind the visit by the U.S. Secretary of State to the Near East, which starts today. Christopher will visit Saudi Arabia, Egypt, Israel and Syria.
