Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Quotation marks as published] [Text] "The mere fact Magistrate Guillermo Marquez Amado has strangely delayed the ruling on Mayin Correa's case for an illogical period of time clearly proves the official's high degree of evident partiality and disqualifies him for that post," it was stated yesterday by Jaime Padilla Beliz, president of the EL SIGLO Publishing Company. The judicial scandal provoked by this clever decision will have continental repercussions, he added. The statements were given within the context of a meeting among various parties to consider the measures and actions that will be adopted when the unfair and absurd ruling occurs; and it will undoubtedly threaten the elections scheduled for 8 May. Padilla Beliz has explained he attended most of the sessions, which were extended for two weeks, and no documents or any other evidence were presented to prove Mayor Mayin Correa had committed the electoral crimes with which she was charged. The only thing done was to present depositions signed by her sworn enemies, without any proof or evidence, and it was proved most of them lied. This was personally admitted in public by Magistrate Guillermo Marquez Amado (Pico), who was in charge of the proceedings. Padilla Beliz continued: "Despite all this, Magistrate Marquez Amado insists on sentencing her, and he has prepared a ruling of approximately 32 pages, sentencing and disqualifying her for public posts for several years. He also suspended her prison sentence, given the lack of a criminal record, as was done in the case of former Attorney General Rogelio Cruz." No decent person in the country will accept such a monstrosity, Padilla Beliz has said. "This is a country where lowly feelings and shameful cowardice abound. Politicians know Mayin is right, and she is already the city's mayor-elect with 74 percent of the votes, according to the latest poll. Nevertheless, they do not dare protest because they are afraid of the Electoral Tribunal, and they state cunningly: "They are the judges, and you cannot go against them." They are also the ones who will tally the votes, and they can really screw us if we oppose them in Mayin's case." That is the reasoning used by men who have no courage, honor, pride. I would rather die defending the truth and reason then to humiliate myself and kneel before the powerful." It is known a magistrate had stated he will not sign the document, because he attended the trial and no one proved Mayin was guilty of anything." Padilla Beliz asked: How, then, can Mayin be sentenced? It was learned the president of the Republic has personally threatened one of the magistrates, saying he will fire the magistrate's wife from a position to which she was recently appointed, if he rules in favor of Mayin. This happens because a degenerate midget who was one of General Manuel Antonio Noriega's intimate friends and who changed sides when he realized the gringos would oust him has become director of First Lady Ana Mae Diaz de Endara's campaign for mayor. He recently fabricated a poll that shows the president's wife leads in the polls. Consequently, the president believes his wife will be mayor of the capital if he eliminates Mayin. Padilla Beliz concluded: "They are toying with the people's patience, however, and anything -- for example, the Christian Democratic magistrate's ruling -- could unleash violence and the cancellation of the elections. "I ask the Supreme Electoral Tribunal magistrates to meditate well what they can do. Set aside personal and partisan hate and lead the country to honest and clean elections."
