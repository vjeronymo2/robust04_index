940614 FT 14 JUN 94 / Arts: Today's Television Those of us who did not see An Evening With Gary Lineker on stage but did see BBC1's comedy drama 'Fair Game' on television on Saturday will be interested to discover how far the BBC production resembles its better known predecessor when ITV screen their television version tonight (8.30). Both dramas plait together the twin strands of World Cup football and the emotional/sexual relationships of a band of onlookers. BBC2 shows the final episode in Quantum Leap, part of a rich seam of American fantasy which gives the lie to the common claim that American television only makes crime series. The central character in Quantum Leap is Sam Beckett who travels through time, becoming a different person in each episode (9.00). Little Napoleons sounded like yet another of Channel 4's misconceived series aiming for something multiracial but merely achieving separatism. However, in the opening episode Saeed Jaffrey and Norman Beaton were so good as competitive solicitors who both become councillors that you could forget the race element altogether (10.00).
