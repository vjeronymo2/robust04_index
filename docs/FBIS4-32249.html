BFN [Text] The Constitutional Court reviewed the case initiated by 49 people's deputies of the Union of Democratic Forces [SDS] in connection with the parliament's vote on the structural changes proposed by the prime minister on 20 May. Constitutional Court Judge Aleksandur Arabadzhiev reports the court's decision: [Begin Arabadzhiev recording] The prime minister has no constitutional obligation to submit his cabinet's resignation when the National Assembly rejects one of his proposals to establish new ministries or make changes in the membership of the cabinet. [end recording] The prime minister has to resign when the National Assembly votes no-confidence in the cabinet or in him personally, or when the cabinet does not receive the vote of confidence it had demanded. The rest, Mr. Arabadzhiev added, is a matter of political culture and tradition.
