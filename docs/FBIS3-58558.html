Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Ankara, Jan 14 (AA) -- Foreign Minister Hikmet Cetin said on Friday [14 January] that Turkey will continue to support Georgia to overcome the problems it faced. Cetin told at a meeting with his Georgian counterpart Alexandr Chikvaidze that he was happy to see Chikvaidze in Turkey. Cetin said during Georgian President and Parliament Speaker Eduard Shevardnadze's visit, 10 agreements, protocols and solidarity documents were signed adding that these two countries have similar views on problems. He said a Black Sea Economic Cooperation (BSEC) meeting will be held in Tbilisi at the beginning of summer adding that a summit will be held between heads of states and governments in case the other BSEC member countries accept it. Chikvaidze thanked for Turkish Foreign Ministry's preparations for Shevardnadze's visit adding that Turkey and Georgia completed infrastructure of the bilateral relations in political, economical and cultural fields.
