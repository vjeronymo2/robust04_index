BFN [Excerpts] Another round of extended talks was held this morning between the brother leader of the revolution and Egyptian President Husni Mubarak. [passage omitted] JANA's correspondent in Sirte has reported that all aspects of bilateral cooperation between the two fraternal countries as well as other Arab and international topics of mutual interests were reviewed at this round of talks. He added that the meeting also dealt with the situation in Yemen and the Egypt's role, which ought to protect Yemeni unity and face up to the colonialist schemes aimed at creating schism and weakening the Arab nation, beginning with Yemen and followed by Saudi Arabia, Egypt, Syria, and the rest of Arab countries. In this respect, Egypt's leading role in the Arab homeland and in the world, given Egypt's weight, civilization, and huge sacrifices for Yemen in the sixties, was stressed. As for Egypt's pan-Arab role, it was reiterated that the Israeli schemes were aiming at harming Egypt, because the program of the Zionist movement was aimed at establishing the so-called state of Israel from the Nile to the Euphrates. As far as the Lockerbie problem was concerned, it was asserted that concocting such a problem was aimed at keeping Egypt busy with secondary issues and distracting it from playing its pan-Arab role.
