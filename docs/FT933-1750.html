930922 FT 22 SEP 93 / UK Company News: Southern Newspapers ahead at Pounds 7.5m BOOSTED BY a disposal surplus and lower interest charges, Southern Newspapers, the regional newspaper group, lifted pre-tax profits to Pounds 7.51m for the 53 weeks ended July 3 1993, against Pounds 7m for the previous year. In line with the continuing difficult trading conditions on the south coast, advertisement revenue fell by over 2 per cent, but this was offset by gains in contract printing revenue and newspaper sales revenue to give an overall lift in turnover from Pounds 69.55m to Pounds 71m. Earnings per share were higher at 22.73p (17.75p) and the total dividend is stepped up to 11.8p (11.25p) with a final payment of 8.8p (8.25p). Operating profits were lower at Pounds 6.8m (Pounds 7.23m) but a Pounds 693,000 (Pounds 168,000) surplus on the disposal of investments and lower interest charges of Pounds 143,000 (Pounds 580,000), left the pre-tax figure ahead of the previous year. Mr John Salkeld, chairman, stated that since the year end there had been an improvement in trading levels of both the newspaper and contract printing divisions which, with firm cost control, 'augurs well for the future.'
