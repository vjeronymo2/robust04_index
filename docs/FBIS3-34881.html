Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tehran, Feb. 15, IRNA -- Georgian Foreign Minister Aleksandr Chikvaidze heading a delegation arrived here Tuesday and was welcomed by his Iranian counterpart 'Ali Akbar Velayati. Upon arrival at the airport he told IRNA that the main purpose of his visit was deepening and expansion of bilateral ties and cooperation in all areas. He said that during the recent visit of the Georgian President Eduard Shevardnadze to Iran the two countries had reached considerable understandings on light industries, machine building mining, and steel mill. Velayati also said that the Georgian embassy in Tehran would be inaugurated shortly, and that the future visit of President Akbar Hashemi-Rafsanjani to Tblisi would bring about a basic development in the two countries' relations. Iran and Georgia inked eight agreements in economic, cultural, commercial, gas, and transportation fields during the previous meetings of the two countries officials, which are to be followed up during Chikvaidze's visit.
