January 13, 1990, Saturday, Home Edition

THE DECLINE OF DAN RATHER'S RATINGS AFTER THE BUSH INCIDENT

Bush's 1988 attack on Rather was in the same execrable taste as his presidential campaign. Rather, after all, was not running for office, and his walking off a TV broadcast set (an act pointed out by Bush) is not comparable to lying or backing illegal and immoral activities.

Rather is not a news announcer -- he is a reporter, and a reporter's job is to get to the truth. Unfortunately, many people can't stand truth when it hammers on their petrified beliefs.

Some viewers may be alienated by Rather's deadpan (yet dramatic) style that calls attention to program content, not to himself. This is in the best tradition of TV journalism reverberating from Edward R. Murrow.

Viewers who abandoned Rather because he "abused" Bush forget that the role of the press in a free society is to probe events, even when it embarrasses government officials, and in so doing serve as the watchdog of democracy and the conscience of government.

BARBARA K. SNADER, Los Angeles
