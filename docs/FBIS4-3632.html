BFN [Text] Beijing, April 21 (XINHUA) -- Chinese Vice-Premier Zou Jiahua is to pay an official visit to the United States from April 27 to May 8 at the invitation of the U.S. Government. The purpose of the visit is to maintain the momentum of high-level exchanges between the two countries, to enhance mutual understanding and to facilitate the bilateral co-operation. This was announced by Chinese Foreign Ministry spokesman Wu Jianmin here this afternoon at a weekly press conference. Replying to a question, Wu said that since the official meeting between Chinese President Jiang Zemin and U.S. President Bill Clinton in Seattle in the U.S. last November, the bilateral exchanges and contacts on various levels and in all fields have been on the increase. During the visit, he said, Vice-Premier Zou Jiahua will engage in extensive contacts with U.S. leaders, government officials, and industry and business personages, and would exchange views with them on Sino-U.S. ties, Sino-U.S. economic cooperation and trade and other issues of common concern, in order to promote the improvement and development of bilateral relations.
