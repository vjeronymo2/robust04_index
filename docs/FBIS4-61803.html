BFN [Text] Vilnius, May 09, BNS -- Inflation dropped to this year's record low of 1.6 percent in April, the Lithuanian Statistics Department said. Inflation ran at 3.3 percent in March, 2.9 percent in February and 4.8 percent in January. But prices for personal care products and medical services went up by 6.9 percent, transportation and communication prices jumped by 6.7 percent. Food, liquor and tobacco prices edged up by 0.4 percent. Household operation expenses increased by 5.5 percent, clothing, education and recreation expenses went up by 2.7 percent. Prices for other goods and services increased by an average of 2.5 percent. Inflation slowed down considerably during the first four months of this year, as compared to last year. Inflation reached 25 percent in April last year, the overall rate of inflation in 1993 was 188.7 percent. But experts suppose that inflation will jump again because of the value-added tax that came into effect May 1.
