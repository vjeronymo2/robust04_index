940216 FT 16 FEB 94 / International Company News: Microsoft in bid for Montreal company Microsoft, the big US software group, is buying Canada's leading animation software company in a deal worth USDollars 130m. Softimage, based in Montreal, is best known internationally for the animation technology that helped to create the lifelike dinosaur scenes in Steven Spielberg's Jurassic Park film. Its range of software products is used by film studios, editing houses, broadcasters, graphic artists and product designers in North America, Europe and Asia. Microsoft is acquiring Softimage in a share exchange deal which, with adjustments, values Softimage shares at between Dollars 21 and Dollars 24. Its stock traded in New York on Monday at around Dollars 15. Daniel Langlois, who owns about 25 per cent of the company, and the rest of the board have accepted the Microsoft offer and urge other shareholders to do the same. Microsoft said Softimage will continue to be autonomous. It will develop specialised software for the creation of high quality digital content for multimedia services.
