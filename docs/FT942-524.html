940629 FT 29 JUN 94 / Colombian president-elect appoints experienced finance minister Colombia's president-elect Ernesto Samper has appointed his first five cabinet ministers, all of them Liberal party men active in his election campaign. Mr Guillermo Perry, an economist and former minister of mines and energy, has been named as finance minister. He has been closely associated with the management of Colombia's economy over the last 15 years and has also headed Fedesarrollo, an independent economic think-tank. Colombian exporters have welcomed Mr Perry's commitments to a slowing of the revaluation of the peso and to the promotion of exports. The new justice minister, Mr Nestor Martnez, has promised a thorough evaluation of the controversial surrender policy for drug-traffickers. He is a lawyer and economist, working at the International Development Bank. The other three cabinet appointments are those of Mr Fernando Botero at defence, Mr Rodrigo Pardo, a former ambassador to Venezuela and a specialist in trade and integration issues, at the foreign ministry, and Mr Horacio Serpa, a former attorney-general, as interior minister.
