920619 FT 19 JUN 92 / World Trade News: EC ministers water down procurement row clause EC MINISTERS have watered down a third-country retaliation clause in new public procurement legislation which could have aggravated trade friction with the US, Andrew Hill reports from Luxembourg. Internal market ministers yesterday agreed a directive which will force utilities to put big service contracts, ranging from maintenance and cleaning to accountancy and insurance, to open tender. The directive is the last in a series designed to liberalise EC public procurement from the beginning of next year. The US is threatening trade sanctions over an earlier directive on utility equipment contracts. That includes a clause allowing utilities to favour EC bids over those from third countries which have failed to open their own markets to competition. The US says this so-called 'reciprocity' clause discriminates against non-EC suppliers, particularly in the telecoms sector, and should be changed before it comes into force on January 1, 1993. EC ministers have agreed a more relaxed regime for the services directive. This will let the Commission investigate alleged discrimination against EC companies by third countries. Any retaliatory measures will have to be approved by member states.
