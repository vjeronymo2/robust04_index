June 15, 1990, Friday, Home Edition

U.S. ADMINISTRATION'S THREAT TO CUT OFF TALKS WITH ARAFAT

At last, in your editorial, you seem to be coming to grips with the thought that there may be no justification for the United States to continue negotiating with the PLO. If Yasser Arafat does control the terrorist organization and its affiliates, then he is responsible for the recent attack by the Palestine Liberation Front, one of the PLO's affiliates, on the Israeli coast.

If he cannot control these territories, there is no purpose in negotiating with him because he will not be able to enforce any commitment he may make.

Arafat insists that he did not know about the terrorist operation, but this hardly seems possible since the planning and training for it went on, according to Abul Abbas, the terrorist leader of the Palestinian Liberation Front, for two full years. This is unbelievable. In any event, the goal of the raid, according to one of the captured terrorists, was "to kill civilians in the (Sheraton) hotel area of Tel Aviv. . . . We were supposed to land and kill anyone we came across."

The Israeli government has already supplied our State Department with documented evidence about more than a thousand terrorist attacks against Israel since Arafat's pledge in December, 1988, that the PLO was ending such attacks. Should it not be clear by now, even to Secretary of State James Baker, that Arafat is an unconscionable liar and cannot be considered a credible negotiating partner?

You say that whether the acts of the PLO "justify an end to the U.S. dialogue with the PLO remains to be determined." But every relevant fact indicates that the terrorist PLO has not given up its aim of destroying Israel. Isn't that the bottom line?

SOL MODELL

Woodland Hills
