February 3, 1989, Friday, Home Edition

MORNING REPORT: STAGE

The Ensemble Studio Theatre, beset by financial difficulties, will leave its East Hollywood home at the end of February. Vicki Williams, a spokeswoman for the theater, says the group can no longer afford the "more than $3,000 in rent and other costs" and is looking for a new theater. The news follows the premature closing Sunday of "West Memphis Mojo" at the Ensemble, the result of a dispute between the Ensemble and the show's producers, Larry Riley and Carmen Hayward-Stetson, over the running costs of the production. ALEENE MacMINN
