Language: Portuguese Article Type:BFN [Text] The demobilization of troops from the government and the Mozambique National Resistance [Renamo] currently found in assembly areas will begin on 1 March. This decision was made today during a meeting of the Supervision and Control Commission [CSC] of the General Peace Accord. A total of 5 percent of troops from both sides will be demobilized in a first phase. Until 1 March Finance Minister Eneas Comiche will create conditions to immediately pay the subsidies to the demobilized troops and to speed up solving the problems connected with their integration into civilian life. Aldo Ajello, special representative of the UN secretary general in Mozambique, said although there is a considerable delay in the process, the demobilization process could continue until June. During today's CSC meeting the government, Renamo and the UN Operations in Mozambique signed an agreement on criteria on the selection and training regulations of the special forces of the army.
