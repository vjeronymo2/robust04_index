940915 FT 15 SEP 94 / World Stock Markets: Gold and mining stocks retain gains Gold and mining-related shares held on to gains on buying ahead of today's futures expiry, but some late selling took them off the session's highs. The gold price held above Dollars 390 an ounce and expectations continued that a push to Dollars 400 lay ahead after short-term consolidation. In comments before the parliamentary finance committee, Reserve Bank governor Mr Chris Stals again warned of a possible rise in interest rates and said that the country could not support an instant abandonment of foreign exchange controls. The overall index put on 15 at 5,873, industrials firmed 1 to 6,545 and the gold shares index advanced 36 to 2,453. De Beers moved up 65 cents to R110.15, while in golds Vaal Reefs was R5 higher at R475.
