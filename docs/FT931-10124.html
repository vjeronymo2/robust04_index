930210 FT 10 FEB 93 / Observer: Anticipating marriage The honeymoon threatens to be over before the wedding for London's young Securities Institute and the Institute of Investment Management and Research which, in essence if not name, is 36 years older. Merger talks are due to culminate this month in plans by a joint working party. But there were reputedly ructions at IIMR's council meeting this week with members 'hopping mad' over the year-old SI's campaign to recruit members. Seeking to add 500 to its present 5,000, who're mainly former Stock Exchange members, the Securities Institute has been beating the drum about a 'window of opportunity' because, until March 31, experienced securities industry practitioners can become members without passing exams. That is seen as premarital infidelity by some of the 3,000 members of the IIMR, exclusively exam-entry since 1979. 'They're promoting the merger as a saleable event before it has been agreed,' sniffed one. 'It's a bit naughty.'
