931207 FT 07 DEC 93 / Ford, Vauxhall see strong performance in November Ford and Vauxhall, leaders of the UK market for new cars, both performed strongly last month in an overall market that rose by 19.4 per cent year-on-year, Kevin Done writes. Ford had suffered an erosion of its sales earlier in the year, however, and its share in the first 11 months has slipped to 21.7 per cent from 22.5 per cent a year ago. The biggest gains among the leading manufacturers have been achieved by Fiat of Italy, Renault and Citroen of France, and Nissan and Toyota of Japan. Fiat, which has struggled for several years in the UK, has increased its sales by 38 per cent this year following heavy investment to improve its dealer network.
