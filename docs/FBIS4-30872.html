BFN ["Pool" item] [Text] Tivat, Yugoslavia, May 31 (TANJUG) -- The Yugoslav naval command has prepared and performed a several-day tactical exercise with the use of live ammunition, the information service of the Yugoslav Army General Staff said on Tuesday [31 May]. The exercise was carried out within the realisation of the tasks of this year's combat training aimed at checking the combat and operational-tactical readiness and degree of training of the Yugoslav naval command and its units in the defence of the Yugoslav part of the Adriatic coast. Tested were the organisation and performance of the anti-combat defence of the Yugoslav part of the coast in case of an aggression on Yugoslavia (Serbia/Montenegro), with the support of the military air force and anti-aircraft defence, it was said. Acccording to its contents and dynamism, the exercise represents one of the most complex forms of testing the degree of combat readiness of navy units for fighting targets at sea, land or in the air. At the end of the exercise, the navy performed shooting exercises with torpedoes, artillery and anti-submarine ammunition. The final part of the exercise was watched by the Yugoslav president and the president of the Supreme Defence Council, Zoran Lilic, Montenegrin President and member of the Supreme Defence Council, Momir Bulatovic, the Yugoslav Army chief of general staff, Colonel General Momcilo Perisic and the commanders of the Yugoslav army strategic groups. Lilic, Bulatovic and Gen. Perisic agreed that the degree of training of the fleet and coastal defence was high, as well as their readiness to successfully deal with all kinds of threats to Yugoslavia's territorial integrity and sovereignty.
