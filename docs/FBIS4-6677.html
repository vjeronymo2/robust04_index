BFN [Text] Ulaanbaatar, April 19 (OANA-MONTSAME) -- The Council for National Security of Mongolia has discussed the current political situation established due to the hunger strike at the D. Suhbaatar and Erh Choloonii Squares and their possible aftermath and taken a decision in this connection which provides for that it would be expedient to keep the Great State Hural informed about the political situation settled in the country with hearing during its discussion at the parliament's session of the demands put forth by those participating in the hunger strikes. Then it goes on to say that the demands by the participants of the hunger strike at the Suhbaatar Square are not to be accepted as being groundless. Taking into account that the hunger strike and the following political crisis might be of adverse consequences for the national interests the council considers it is necessary to call upon the hunger strikers to stop their actions. Then the council also recommended to submit for consideration to the Great State Hural for approval the draft law on procedures of holding demonstrations and meetings. In this connection it is thought to be necessary to work out in immediate future proper law on the press and until its coming into force it recommended to the government to carry on the radio and television "Tribune of Political Parties" programme as well as to settle urgently all the topical issues of concern to be regulated in terms of the press.
