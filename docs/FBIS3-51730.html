Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following ia a compilation of reports on economic activities in Brazil monitored through 20 January. According to the Getulio Vargas Foundation, the 1993 inflation rate measured by the Average General Price Index, IGP-M, was 2,567.46 percent, the highest ever in Brazil. The December inflation rate was 38.32 percent, 2.17 percentage points higher than the 36.15 percent in November. This is the highest rate since March 1990, which was a record 81.3 percent. (Rio de Janeiro JORNAL DO BRASIL in Portuguese 30 Dec 93 Economic and Business Section p 1) According to the Brazilian Institute of Geography and Statistics, the 1993 inflation rate measured by the National Consumer Price Index, INPC, was 2,489.11 percent. The Expanded Consumer Price Index, IPCA, was 2,474.15 percent. (Rio de Janeiro JORNAL DO BRASIL in Portuguese 14 Jan 94 Business and Finance Section p 1)
