BFN [Text] Brussels, June 3 KYODO -- The European Union (EU) seeks cooperation with Japan in dealing with energy and environmental issues, according to the EU's vice president in charge of science and technology attending next week's joint forum in Tokyo. Antonio Ruberti said Thursday [2 June] he looked forward to Japan-EU comprehensive cooperation socially and economically to deal with a number of issues, including global warming and the reduction of carbon dioxide emissions into the atmosphere. He indicated he would also take up the issue of Japan-EU cooperation in ensuring the safety of nuclear energy when he confers with Japanese Government officials in Tokyo. Ruberti is scheduled to arrive in Tokyo on Tuesday for the forum on cooperation on science and research that was established last October on the basis of a joint declaration in 1991 by Japan and the European Community, as the EU was then called. Among the Japanese scheduled to attend the forum opening Wednesday are Mikio Omi, director general of the Science and Technology Agency, Education Minister Ryoko Akamatsu and leaders of the Federation of Economic Organizations (Keidanren).
