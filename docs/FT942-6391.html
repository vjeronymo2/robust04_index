940531 FT 31 MAY 94 / Howard defends justice strategy Mr Michael Howard, the home secretary, last night defended the government's criminal justice strategy after Mr John Bartell, chairman of the Prison Officers' Association, warned that Britain's overcrowded prison system was 'spiralling rapidly out of control'. A report by the Prison Reform Trust showed that the number of prisoners awaiting trial had risen to a record 12,100 in January from 9,281 in January last year. The total prison population rose 13 per cent last year, to 47,000. Mr Howard said that people released on bail committed at least 50,000 offences every year. 'That is at least 50,000 victims of crime, and the public deserves to be protected.'
