BFN [Text] Bonn, April 8 (A.A.) -- Turkey's ambassador to Germany Onur Oymen had a talk today with German Federal Foreign Ministry State Secretary Dieter Kastrup, on the suspension of German arms supply to Turkey. After the meeting, Oymen told the Anatolian Agency, "we asked from Germany to resolve the matter at the soonest, through diplomatic means." During their talk the German side told Oymen that they have received reports on Turkey's violation of the Turkish-German agreement and that Turkey was using German arms it received within the NATO, for domestic security reasons. They added that Germany has suspended arms supply to Turkey until their investigation on the matter is concluded. Oymen told German officials that Turkey has not violated any agreement it signed so far and that these claims were fabricated by circles supporting the illegal terror organization. "Friendly and allied countries should trust each other," Oymen said. "The claims have no basis. We expect Germany to resolve the matter at the soonest, through diplomatic channels," he said.
