BFN [Text] In Cairo, Israeli and Palestinian delegations led by General 'Uzi Dayan and Dr. Nabil Sha'th today resumed negotiations on Palestinian autonomy in Gaza and Jericho. Gen. Dayan, who heads the IDF [Israel Defense Forces] Planning Branch, told reporters that Israel hoped the negotiations would be completed quickly. Nabil Sha'th told Qol Yisra'el's correspondent in Cairo Arye Gus the PLO want to conclude the agreement by the 13 April deadline in the Declaration of Principles. [Begin Sha'th recording] We are starting the negotiations on the withdrawal plan from Gaza and Jericho, which is really the completion of what we had started and stopped just because of the massacre. We hope to finish as soon as possible the withdrawal plan, the deployment of the Palestinian police force, and the remaining factors of security in the Gaza and Jericho. The entry of the Palestinian police force will start with first a small group of maybe seven or eight people to go to make the arrangements and then be followed by a larger number to take over some of the camps and to start preparing within a week at most, maybe Thursday. There will be a detailed schedule that will coincide with gradual withdrawal of the Israeli Armed Forces, so that by the end of this period the Palestinian police force will be fully deployed. [end recording]
