BFN [Unattributed report: "Russia Hides no Data on Chemical Arms"] [Text] Allegations that Russia is hiding some sort of data about chemical weapons are "absolutely groundless," stated Grigoriy Karasin, Russian Federation Foreign Ministry information and press department director, in connection with the accusations heard in the course of hearings in the U.S. Congress. He advised that the Russian Federation and the United Stated exchanged data on their military-chemical potentials on the basis of an intergovernment memorandum on chemical weapons. "These data are being studied and analyzed by both sides, following which Russia and the United States will each carry out five inspections at each other's installations. This is provided for so that the sides can make sure the received information is trustworthy," the Foreign Ministry official stated.
