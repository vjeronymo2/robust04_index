930325 FT 25 MAR 93 / Arts: Today's Television Given the tremendous success of Tim Hunkin's series The Secret Life Of .. it would be just like television to try with him, as previously with people such as James Burke and Magnus Pike, to turn him into a more general purpose presenter/entertainer. Let's hope it does not happen because his great strength is his passionate interest in his subject, which is domestic or popular machinery. The suspicion arises because today (8.30 C4) he investigates the office. Technology is still involved, but it doesn't sound the same as showing how a photocopier works. Also presenting the last of her series The Goldring Audit is Mary Goldring whose subject is the electricity industry. She finds that the monopoly position of the distribution companies has led to a large rise in profits without any benefit to the consumer (9.00 C4). Having begun my life in a London under attack by doodlebugs I shall watch the BBC2 series Labour Of Love with even more interest than usual. This episode tells of the struggle to keep children safe and fed (9.30). In Disguises Adam Holloway takes on the identity of a Bosnian refugee (9.30 ITV).
