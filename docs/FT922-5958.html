920528 FT 28 MAY 92 / Plan on late debts backed MORE THAN 20 trade associations and small business groups have expressed an interest in a plan to develop pilot schemes to tackle late payment of debt, the Department of Trade and Industry said yesterday. Mr Norman Lamont, the chancellor, announced in his March Budget that the government would provide up to Pounds 90,000 for three trade organisations to run one-year pilot schemes such as late-payment 'hotlines' for their members. Trade associations have until June 15 to submit detailed proposals for schemes, said Baroness Denton, minister for small businesses. Initial interest has come from trade bodies representing the building industry, printing, paint manufacturers, electrical contractors and computer services. Three other groups, the Federation of Small Businesses, the Forum of Private Business and the Confederation of British Industry's smaller firms council, have also backed the idea. Lady Denton said: 'Trade associations are often the first port of call for small firms because they understand the nature of their business and its market. Some are already working on payment issues but we want to expand their number and the range of support they offer.'
