940218 FT 18 FEB 94 / Indonesia keeps trade rights Indonesia will keep US trade privileges for at least six months because of progress in ensuring the rights of workers, Mr Mickey Kantor, the US trade representative, said, AP reports from Jakarta. In a statement released by the US Embassy in Jakarta, Washington is suspending a review of labour rights in Jakarta for six months. Jakarta will retain duty-free privileges under the Generalised System of Preferences, under which tariff cuts and expanded quotas are granted to encourage exports in some developing countries.
