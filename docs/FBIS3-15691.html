Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Riga, Mar 05, BNS -- Latvia considers Russian gas export concern GAZPROM prices for gas, 80 U.S. dollars for 1000 cubic meters, to be too high, Latvian Prime Minister Valdis Birkavs informed Friday [4 March] GAZPROM board's Deputy Chairman Valeriy Remizov and Director General Pjotr Rodionov. The representatives of GAZPROM arrived Friday in Riga to sign a new contract with Latvia for year 1994. Latvia considers the price for gas should be smaller because Russia uses Incukalns underground gas repository. In return Russia demands that Latvia did payments for gas beforehand. BNS already reported that GAZPROM breached February 11, 1993 a contract on gas supply with the three Baltic states and GAZPROM branch company LENTRANSGAZ. During the meeting the representatives of GAZPROM named Latvia's 23 million U.S. dollars debt to the organization and necessity to centralize all payments to be the main reasons for the breaching of contract. Birkavs informed that Latvian Government has given permission to use Japanese export-import bank's loan (which is part of G-24 credit given to Latvia) for paying back the debt. Lithuanian state enterprise LIETUVAS GAZE signed Thursday agreement on cooperation with GAZPROM. According to the agreement, Lithuania will receive 2.7 billion cubic meters of natural gas paying 75 U.S. dollars for one thousand cubic meters of gas.
