Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [From the "Novosti" newscast] [Text] [Correspondent] Prime Minister John Major has spoken highly of the results of his visit to Moscow and his talks with President Yeltsin and Prime Minister Chernomyrdin. Asked what he thinks of the prospects for future development of the relationship between the two countries, John Major said: [Begin recording] [Major, in English with superimposed Russian translation] I think it will continue to develop further. I have invited President Yeltsin to come to Britain again this year on a reciprocal visit. I have also invited the prime minister. I hope they will come. We are now opening doors to political cooperation. We should also be opening doors to economic relations even wider. We have enormous opportunities here. First of all, it is necessary to increase trade and mutual investments -- this will be beneficial for Russia as well as for Britain. [end recording] [Correspondent] The British premier has also spoken highly about the progress of Russian reforms. He noted that, in his opinion, many people underestimate how far the Russian reforms have already progressed.
