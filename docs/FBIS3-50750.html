Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities in Brazil monitored through 6 January. The Mineral Resources Prospecting Company has discovered small deposits of platinum in Para, Rondonia, and Minas Gerais States for the first time since the company began prospecting in 1991. This increases the possibility of Brazil becoming a platinum-producing nation. (Sao Paulo GAZETA MERCANTIL in Portuguese 29 Dec 93 p 13) The National Confederation of Industries reported that Brazilian industry will register between a 8.5-9 percent "exceptional" increase late in 1993. This performance will contribute to increasing the GDP 4.5 percent compared with 1992. (Sao Paulo GAZETA MERCANTIL in Portuguese 30 Dec 93 p 9) Labor Minister Walter Barelli on 30 December reported that as of 31 December the minimum salary will be 32,882 real cruzeiros, nearly $100, with a readjustment 75.28-percent higher than the December salary. (Sao Paulo O ESTADO DE SAO PAULO in Portuguese 31 Dec 93 Economy Section p 1) The Brazilian Institute of Geography and Statistics, IBGE, on 29 December said the unemployment rate in six Brazilian metropolitan regions -- Rio de Janeiro, Sao Paulo, Belo Horizonte, Salvador, Recife, and Porto Alegre -- was 4.57 percent in November. This percentage was lower than October's 4.89 percent and November 1992's 5.82 percent. The IBGE also said the number of employed people in the six regions grew from 15,428,279 to 15,474,639 in October. (Sao Paulo GAZETA MERCANTIL in Portuguese 30 Dec 93 p 8)
