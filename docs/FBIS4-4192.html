BFN [Text] Ulaanbaatar, April 29 (XINHUA) -- China and Mongolia issued a press communique on Chinese Premier Li Peng's two-day official visit to Mongolia, which ends this afternoon. During the visit, President P. Ochirbat and Chairman N. Bagabandi met with Premier Li Peng. Prime minister P. Jasray held talks with Premier Li. During the talks and meetings, the two sides briefed each other on the domestic situation and foreign policies of the two countries. They exchanged views on ways to further expand Sino-Mongolian relations and on international issues of common concern, according to the communique. The talks and meetings proceeded in a sincere and friendly atmosphere, and comprehensive agreement was reached between the two sides, the communique says. During the visit, the two sides signed a treaty on friendly relations of cooperation between China and Mongolia, and agreements on cooperation in culture, economy and technology between the two nations. The two countries also signed agreements on the protection and utilization of water in the border areas, on cooperation in the quarantine of animals as well as veterinary work and on guarantee and attestation of the quality of import and export commodities. The two sides are satisfied with smooth development of Sino-Mongolian relations in recent years, and agreed to further such friendly ties of cooperation based on the five principles of peaceful coexistence with a view towards the 21st century, the document says. This fully conforms with the fundamental interests of the two peoples and will benefit peace, stability and development of the Asia-Pacific region, the communique says. Also, the two sides agreed that the Sino-Mongolian treaty on friendship and mutual help signed in 1960 played an important role in expanding bilateral ties. The new treaty signed on the basis of revising the previous document will provide new legal guarantee for further opening up the future of bilateral good-neighborly and friendly relations of cooperation. The communique says. This new treaty defines the basic principles for developing Sino- Mongolian relations, the communique adds. The Chinese side reiterated that it respects Mongolia's independence, sovereignty, territorial integrity and its position as a nuclear-free-zone, according to the communique. Mongolia stated its policies on not allowing the station of foreign troops, or deploying and passing of nuclear weapons and other weapons of mass destruction, in or through the territory of Mongolia, the document says, adding that China expressed respect for that. The Mongolian side reiterated that the Government of the People's Republic of China is the sole legitimate government representing China, while Taiwan is an inalienable part of the territory of the People's Republic of China, the communique says. Both sides agreed that Premier Li Peng's visit has achieved complete success, and is of great significance for further expansion of Sino-Mongolian friendly relations. Li Peng invited Jasray to visit China when is convenient to him and the latter accepted the invitation with pleasure, the document says.
