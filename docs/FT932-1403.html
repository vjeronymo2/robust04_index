930624 FT 24 JUN 93 / Foreign holdings may cause burden on German bond market There are grounds for concern that 'massive' foreign holdings may one day become a burden on the German bond market, the Bundesbank has warned, writes Christopher Parkes. In the four months to the end of April, non-German buyers accumulated a net DM94bn (Pounds 37.7bn) of German paper, including DM65bn in public issues. Public bonds were especially favoured by large institutional investors interested less in long-term holdings than in the highest possible short-term yields, the bank said in its latest monthly report. In the light of their tendency to make the most of capital and foreign exchange market movements, 'it is all the more important to maintain confidence in the internal and external value of the D-Mark,' the bank noted.
