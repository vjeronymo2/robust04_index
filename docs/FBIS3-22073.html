Language: Urdu Article Type:CSO [News Report: "Accused Admits Smuggling Drugs to Saudi Arabia"] [Text] Peshawar (Staff Reporter)--Mohammed Hanif son of Itaul Rehman has exposed the black deeds of his uncle who sends naive villagers with heroin to Saudi Arabia falsely promising them wealth and jobs. The Narcotics Police had presented the accused in Magistrate Afsar Khan's court after initial questioning and the accused admitted in the court that his uncle Rehman was a fisherman in Karachi until recently. His luck changed and he went to Saudi Arabia and earned money as a driver in addition to working as a fisherman. The accused further said that his uncle upon his return home started the business of sending people abroad. He had already sent nine villagers to Arab countries. His uncle had arranged for 19 persons including his nephew to go abroad. He charged each person 30,000 rupees. The accused said that his uncle had prepared him to go with the other persons. One sack full of floating balls used for fishing were given to every two persons to carry with them. The names of these persons were written on those sacks to help pass the customs check points. His other uncle also had come to see them all off. At the airport the floating balls were found to be filled with heroin and all of these people were arrested. The accused said that they were innocent and that his uncle had deceived all the passengers as he owned that heroin.
