BFN [Report by ITAR-TASS correspondents Viktor Demidenko, Mikhail Melnik] [Text] Kiev 10 June TASS -- Over 27 per cent of voters would like Leonid Kravchuk to become Ukrainian president again. Ex-Premier Leonid Kuchma is supported by 20 per cent of voters, the second rating. Speaker of the Ukrainian Parliament Aleksandr Morozov is third, being backed by eleven per cent of voters. The fourth and fifth places are held by Vladimir Lanovoy, a prominent Ukrainian economist favouring the market, and Ivan Plyushch, former chairman of the Supreme Soviet. They are backed by four to 6 per cent of voters. Entrepreneur Valeriy Babich and Education Minister Petr Palanchuk are supported by only one per cent of voters. This information was supplied to ITAR-TASS by the Kiev International Institute of Sociology and the sociology chair of the Kiev-Mogilyanskiy Academy which conducted a selective representative survey in Ukraine. As many as 1,004 people were polled in 126 cities and villages in 24 regions of Ukraine and the Republic of Crimea. There is information that 73 per cent of voters now intend to participate in the elections. Ten per cent are yet undecided. 17 per cent will not vote. The poll also showed that Leonid Kravchuk is supported mostly by Ukrainian population, while Leonid Kuchma is supported by Russian-speaking population.
