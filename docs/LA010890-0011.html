January 8, 1990, Monday, Home Edition

SEN. CRANSTON

The right wing is certainly out to get Sen. Cranston.

Sure (Lincoln Savings & Loan President Charles), Keating contributed to Cranston's election campaign.

Keating gave to both parties. He gave to Republican Gov. George Deukmejian. Wasn't there a state official to check on savings and loans? If so, where was he?

What about M. Danny Wall, appointed by President Reagan to keep an eye on federal savings and loans? Obviously he didn't. And he didn't need campaign money. He was appointed.

Senators need campaign money. It's the American way -- the only way senators and presidents ever get elected.

Wall probably knew that President Reagan was against government checking up on business.

DICK WORSFOLD

Culver City
