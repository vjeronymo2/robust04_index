BFN [Text] Tallinn, Apr 20, BNS -- Estonian Defense Minister Indrek Kannik and a delegation that includes the head of the parliamentary defense commission began a three-day visit to Ukraine today. Kannik is to meet his Ukrainian counterpart, Army Gen. Vitaliy Radetskyy in the afternoon. The ministers will sign a cooperation agreement between the two Defense Ministries in the evening. Radetskyy will also give a dinner in honor of the Estonian minister. Kannik is scheduled to have meetings with Ukraine's Deputy Defense Minister, Col. Gen. Oliynk, the chief of the country's army supply division, Col. Gen. Mykhaylychenko, as well as with the ministers of Mechanical Industry and Industry. The Estonian minister will lay a wreath to the tomb of the Unknown Soldier in Kiev later in the day. Friday's [22 April] agenda of the visit includes meetings at the Ukrainian Institute of Land Forces and the General Staff. Kannik's delegation includes the chairman of the Estonian parliament's defense commission, Rein Helme, the country's charge d'affaires in Ukraine, Andrei Birov, Defense Ministry Chancellor Tarmo Molder, Defense Ministry and army Central Staff officials.
