Language: English Article Type:BFN [Press release from the UK Prime Minister's Office: "Bank of England Appointments"] [Text] 4 Mar -- The Counsellors of State, acting on behalf of The Queen, have approved the appointment, under the Bank of England Act 1946, of Sir David James Scott Cooksey and Ms. Sheila Valerie Masters as Directors of the Bank for a period of four years from 1 March 1994 in place of Sir George Adrian Hayhurst Cadbury and Mr. Gavin Harry Laird CBE [Commander of the most noble order of the British Empire] whose terms of office expired on 28 February 1994. The Counsellors of State have also approved the re-appointment of Sir David Gerald Scholey CBE as a Director of the Bank, on the expiry of his present term of office, for a period of four years from 1 March 1994.

Notes for Editors
Sir David Cooksey (53) is Chairman of Advent Ltd and Chairman of the Audit Commission for Local Government and the National Health Service in England and Wales. Ms. Sheila Masters (44) is a Partner with KPMG [Klynveld Peat Marwick Goerdeler]. Sir David Scholey (58) has been a Director of the Bank of England since 1981. He is Chairman of the SG Warburg Group plc and holds a number of other directorships.
