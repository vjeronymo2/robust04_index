Language: Greek Article Type:BFN [Text] Foreign Minister Alekos Mikhailidhis said the government is not prepared to discuss any changes to the provisions in the confidence-building measures [CBM's] for the return of the entire fenced-off area of Famagusta. Speaking after a meeting with the Famagusta mayor and municipal counselors, Mikhailidhis said the government is not discussing the contents of the measures package, but its implementation. He added: These measures very clearly refer to Famagusta's fenced-off area. Asked about the safety of Famagusta's residents, Mikhailidhis said this is one issue that will be discussed. He said: We want enough security so that whoever returns to Famagusta will feel that they are returning to their homes permanently. Mikhailidhis said the implementation of the CBM's would allow 15,000-16,000 residents to return to Famagusta. Famagusta Mayor Andreas Pouyiouros said the citizens of Famagusta number 60,000, adding that the Municipal Council's position is that the whole town should be returned in accordance with UN Security Council Resolution 550. Pouyiouros described a UN experts' report on the implementation of the CBM's as unacceptable and intolerable, adding that the world's powers and the United Nations see the measures as the first step toward an overall Cyprus solution.
