Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Russia and Japan agree that their territorial dispute should be solved "step by step taking the Tokyo declaration as a basis," Japanese Vice Prime Minister, Foreign Minister Tsutomu Hata commented on the results of his visit to Moscow. As the main result of his visit, Hata pointed out the two sides' proven commitment to preserve Russo-Japanese relations on the level achieved after Yeltsin's visit to Tokyo last October and strictly observe the provisions of the Tokyo declaration signed during the visit. Hata pointed out that his present visit to Russia had produced a number of openings which could lead to a conducive environment for the resolution of the territorial issue. In particular, the contacts between Japan and the four disputed islands will be further enhanced. The Japanese minister said he was satisfied with Russia's commitment to the course of reforms. "The main thing now is to train personnel for the future Russian economy. This is why we agreed on Japan constructing a business college at Moscow University and setting up a Japanese management center there," Hata was quoted as saying. He also said the Japanese government planned to set aside $50 Mn to stimulate Russia's privatized businesses. Hata pointed out that Russia and Japan had exchanged opinions on a wide range of global-scale issues. "Russia has proved its weight in international affairs, and its involvement in the resolution of international issues is very important," Hata said. According to the Japanese minister, the two sides reached a principal agreement to hold permanent consultations on defense and security issues. Commenting on North Korea's refusal to admit IAEA experts and its threat to withdraw from the Non-Proliferation Treaty, the Japanese minister pointed out that he had touched on the issue during his talk with his Russian counterpart, Andrey Kozyrev, and that he could confidently establish that the two countries were unanimous on the issue. Hata added that both Russia and Japan were deeply concerned over the North Korean issue, and "we demand this country admit IAEA experts and resume exchange of envoys with South Korea."
