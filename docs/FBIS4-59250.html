BFN [Text] Cairo, May 11 (AFP)--PLO chairman Yasir 'Arafat has nominated five leading figures from the occupied territories to the 25-member Palestinian Authority due to take over in Gaza and Jericho, the PLO's chief autonomy negotiator said here Wednesday [11 May]. Nabil Sha'th named the five as: - Zakariya al-Agha, a doctor and head of 'Arafat's mainstream Fatah faction in the occupied Gaza Strip, - Furayh Abu-Middayn, president of the Gaza Bar Association and a member of the Palestinian delegation to the autonomy talks with Israel, - Riyad al-Za'nun, a doctor and head of the higher health council in the Gaza Strip, - Ilyas Furayj, mayor of Bethlehem in the West Bank, - 'Abd-al-'Aziz al-Hajj Ahmad, a Palestinian deportee who returned to the occupied territories. Earlier a source at PLO headquarters in Tunis said Zakariya al-Agha would be put in charge of Gaza and autonomy negotiator Jamil al-Tarifi would take charge of Jericho. In Cairo, Palestinian officials said Sha'th had also been nominated to the authority, but Sha'th refused to comment when asked to confirm the report. Yasir 'Amr, a member of the PLO executive committee, said Tuesday the Palestinian Authority would consist of 15 figures from the occupied territories and 10 from the Palestinian diaspora. 'Arafat, who is due to head the authority, will resume discussions on its composition once he returns from South Africa, where he attended the swearing-in of President Nelson Mandela. The PLO has accused Israel of linking the deployment of Palestinian policemen in Gaza and Jericho to the formation of the team. "I'm awaiting from the other side honest implementation of what has been agreed upon. It is not the case," 'Arafat told Israeli television from South Africa.
