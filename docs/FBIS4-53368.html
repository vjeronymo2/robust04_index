BFN [Text] Seoul, May 10 (YONHAP) -- A civil organization was formed Tuesday to support North Koreans who have fled to China or who are working as loggers in Siberia. The "Headquarters of Movement for Helping Brethren Fleeing North Korea" was officially inaugurated in a founding rally attended by nearly 300 persons including lawyer O Chae-to, Chong Nam-yol, president of the Association of Protestant Orders, and Song Won-yong, a former lawmaker, at Kodang (Cho Man-sik) Memorial Hall in central Seoul. The group said, "North Koreans who fled to China to avoid human rights suppression and adversities and North Korean loggers in Siberia do not receive any adequate protection from the Chinese Government and the Russian Government, let alone the South Korean Government. "It's about time we, citizens, began a pan-national movement to bring them and help them settle here." The organization said it will begin with a fact-finding survey of the North Koreans in flight in Russia and China, ask the Russian and Chinese Governments to provide humanitarian assistance to them and start gathering funds to held them settle here.
