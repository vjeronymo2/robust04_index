April 11, 1989, Tuesday, Home Edition

NEWSWIRE

A 20-year-old woman who told police she was raped in the Oklahoma University athletic dormitory in January testified that she could not identify the men who assaulted her because the room was dark.

The woman testified at a Norman, Okla. preliminary hearing that will determine whether Oklahoma football players Nigel Clay, Glen Bell and Bernard Hall should stand trial for the Jan. 21 incident.

The victim said she went to Norman for a double date. She testified they went to a room in the athletic dormitory, then to a liquor store and returned to the dorm. She said she had some drinks and then went to the bathroom.

The woman said someone grabbed her by the shoulders and moved her into the bedroom as she left the bathroom.

Clay, 20, of Fontana, and Hall, 22, of Detroit also face two counts each of giving alcohol to a minor. All three have been free on bond since shortly after their arrest Feb. 10. Bell, 20, is from Muskogee, Okla.
