BFN [Text] Havana, 4 Jun (PL) -- The Cuban Foreign Ministry confirmed today the recent release from prison for humanitarian reasons of Mexican citizen Marcelo Garcia Ruvalcaba, who had been sentenced by a local court for the crime of terrorism. The foreign minister told PRENSA LATINA that Garcia Ruvalcaba has a serious kidney problem, and in view of the situation, the Cuban Government made the extrajudicial decision to release him for humanitarian reasons. The Mexican citizen was sentenced to 10 years in prison by the People's Provincial court in this capital on 28 February when it was proven that he had brought into the country hand grenades and counterrevolutionary pamphlets aimed at carrying out terrorist activities. Garcia Ruvalcaba established ties in the Caribbean country with individuals hostile to the revolution. He admitted his guilt in the public trial attended by representatives of the Mexican Embassy and relatives of the defendant. The Mexican citizen is an employee of Continental Airlines of the United States. He has a residence in Los Angeles where he traveled after his release. The Cuban Government's decision on 26 May was previously passed to the Mexican Foreign Ministry through the proper diplomatic channels.
