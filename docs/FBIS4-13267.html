BFN [Text] Tehran, April 18, IRNA -- Sudanese Deputy Foreign Minister Yusuf 'Umar Baradah arrived here Monday on a three-day official stay and was welcomed by his Iranian counterpart Mohammad Hashemi at the airport. Talking to reporters upon his arrival, Barideh described Tehran-Khartoum ties as "excellent" and said his government was for further expansion of the "cordial bilateral relations" in various fields. Baradah, who carries a written message from Sudanese Foreign Minister Husayn Sulayman Abu-Salih for his Iranian counterpart 'Ali Akbar Velayati, said Iran and Sudan are the target of adverse propaganda for their efforts to implement Islamic laws. He said his government was proud of Iranian and Sudanese officials' adoption of an independent political and economic path which has led to growing ties between the two countries. Hashemi referred to the age-old cultural, historical and religious commonalities between Iran and Sudan and underscored expansion of bilateral relations.
