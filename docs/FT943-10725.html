940803 FT 03 AUG 94 / UK Company News: Vivat down after exceptional After an exceptional Pounds 1m charge to cover the closure costs of its Marseilles distribution centre, pre-tax profits at Vivat Holdings fell from Pounds 1.84m to Pounds 1.06m in the six months to end-June. Turnover declined by 12 per cent to Pounds 33.6m (Pounds 38.3m). The drop in sales was because of organisational difficulties at the jeans manufacturer's French subsidiary, but was partly offset by good performances in Belgium, Spain and the UK. The company also recently became aware of a long-standing under-statement of stock in its Tunisian subsidiary. The Pounds 3.5m under-statement has been dealt with as a prior year adjustment to reserves, and has had no effect on the profit and loss account since 1990. Mr Christopher Burnett, chairman, said 'further clarification of the circumstances is being sought'. Earnings per share were 1.3p (2.4p).
