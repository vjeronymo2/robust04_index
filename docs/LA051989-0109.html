May 19, 1989, Friday, Home Edition

THE WORLD

Thousands of protesters demonstrated in 11 South Korean cities on the anniversary of the 1980 uprising in the southwestern city of Kwangju, burning effigies of President Roh Tae Woo and calling for the withdrawal of U.S. troops from the country. In Kwangju, an estimated 100,000 people rallied, while in Seoul and other cities, thousands joined marches, protests and religious services. The 10-day Kwangju uprising, which killed nearly 200 people by official count, hundreds more by unofficial reports, occurred after Roh's predecessor, Chun Doo Hwan, declared martial law and jailed opposition leaders.
