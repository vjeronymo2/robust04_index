BFN [Text] Chinese Vice Premier Zou Jiahua has begun his visit to Canada by saying his country can't grow in the next century without help. Speaking in Vancouver, Mr. Zou urged a business audience to invest more in China, especially in the transport, energy, agriculture, communications, and mining sectors. Trade between Canada and China is worth more than $4 billion a year. Prime Minister Jean Chretien will visit China in November, becoming the first Canadian leader to do so since the 1989 massacre of pro-democracy activists in Beijing. Mr. Chretien has said that human rights questions should not deter closer trade links between the two countries.
