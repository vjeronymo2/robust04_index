BFN [By ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondents Vladimir Taranov and Boris Krivoshey] [Text] Moscow, 12 Apr -- The forthcoming Moscow meeting of leaders of the CIS states will be a significant event in the development of the Commonwealth. This was declared at a briefing today by Grigoriy Karasin, director of the Russian federation Foreign Ministry's Information and Press Department. Those attending the meeting on 15 April will discuss a group of issues concerning implementation of the Treaty of Economic Union and will examine draft agreements on Ukraine's joining the Economic Union as an associate member, on the creation of an Interstate Economic Committee, and on the creation of a free-trade area. During discussion of a number of military-political problems, Grigoriy Karasin noted, an update will be given by the council of commanders of the Commonwealth's border troops on the progress of the talks between the Russian Federation and the Central Asian and Transcaucasian states on cooperation with guarding their borders with third countries. Those attending the meeting are to endorse a new version of the Statute on the Council of Defense Ministers. They will consider a draft agreement on cooperation with mobilizing the economy of the Commonwealth countries, the spokesman for Russia's foreign-policy department said. A package of issues relating to the situation in and around Tajikistan will occupy a special place on the Moscow summit agenda, Grigoriy Karasin emphasized.
