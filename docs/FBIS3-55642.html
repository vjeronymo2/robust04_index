Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [BY ITAR-TASS correspondents Vladimir Akimov, Amangeldy Akhmetalimov] [Text] Tashkent, 11 Jan -- Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan have demonstrated goodwill. The negotiations between the two countries' official delegations, headed by Presidents Nursultan Nazarbayev and Islam Karimov, ended late yesterday evening in a spirit of complete understanding. As a result, a number of important documents directed at intensifying integration processes have now seen the light of day. Among other things, a treaty on the creation of a single economic space between Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan has been signed, along with agreements on intensifying cooperation between the two countries in culture, healthcare, science, education, tourism, and sport, on settlement of mutual payments in accordance with results of trade and economic cooperation in 1992 and 1993, and on the organization of payments in 1994. This morning, Nursultan Nazarbayev and Islam Karimov set off for the town of Nukus, capital of Karakalpakistan, to participate in the conference of heads of state of the Central Asian region on the problems of lake Aral.
