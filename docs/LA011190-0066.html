January 11, 1990, Thursday, Home Edition

SAN GABRIEL VALLEY DIGEST: GLENDORA;

NEW BONDS FOR OLD DEBT

The City Council voted Tuesday to issue about $25 million in new bonds to take advantage of current lower interest rates in paying off the Glendora Community Redevelopment Agency debt.

The agency owes $10.4 million from three bond issues with interest rates of more than 9%. The new bonds, which will be retired in 20 years, will be at 7.7%. The new bonds will also generate $5.3 million for capital projects, $3.2 million to repay loans from the city, and $4.5 million toward buying property for low- and moderate-income housing.
