941025 FT 25 OCT 94 / Arts: Today's Television Three cheers for Channel 4 and television's best culture commentary series, Without Walls, which begins a new run with a programme I would have said was impossible on today's politically correct television in Britain: Sun TV critic Gary Bushell, who is nobody's fool but decidedly right wing, argues in favour of Alf Garnett, the monstrous working class Tory bigot who made Till Death Us Do Part such a huge success in the 1960s. Bushell extends the argument to criticise the stranglehold achieved by Islington trendies over so much of television these days (9.00). We shall need our VCRs since the fifth episode in BBC2's odd but endearing, and wholly engrossing, drama series, Takin' Over The Asylum, also begins at 9.00. I still think the attitude towards mental patients is sentimental, but perhaps that was necessary. BBC1 begins a new series of 'factual dramas', Against All Odds, containing testimony from the real protagonists woven into the scripts. Today's story tells how Linda McNeill went to Turkish Cyprus to snatch her three children from her violent husband (9.30).
