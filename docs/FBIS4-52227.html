BFN [Text] Pyongyang, May 6 (KCNA) -- Silk production is increasing in Korea. The Korean Silk Association overfulfilled its April plan by 5.7 percent, according to the data available. The association fulfilled its first quarterly year plan ten days ahead of the schedule. Korea with a long history of silk production is one of the famous silk production countries in the world. Many large and small silk and silk-textile mills and workshops have been built in various places of the country. Among them are Pyongyang silk mill, Hamhung silk mill, Yongbyon, Pakchon and Kumya silk-textile mills. Modern silk processing centres have been built to turn out 2,000 kinds of silk cloth, thread and clothes including Yaksan silk, brocade and Kuryong silk, special products of Korea. Most of them are exported to foreign countries and are gaining favour at world market. The export output value doubled in recent five years. Especially, brocade took two gold medals at an international exhibition. The prospect of silk production is very good in Korea. In recent years silk production capacity greatly increased with the promotion of expansion projects and the introduction of modern equipment. Joint venture with foreign countries is encouraged. Mulberry fields are expanded year by year and breeds of silkworms and feeds are developed, providing favourable conditions for development of sericulture.
