Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Violence broke out again in south Lebanon at 1630 today when Israeli forces directed a concentrated, fierce bombardment against villages and towns in the south. Our correspondent 'Ali Dawud has the details. [Begin Dawud recording] The occupation forces have stirred up the security situation and increased their attacks. The occupation forces artillery shelled numerous villages in al-Nabatiyah and the central sector in the morning and in the afternoon. The shelling of al-Tamiriyah killed one citizen and inflicted extensive damage to houses and property. The occupation forces escalated their attacks and artillery fire against southern villages at 1600 today. Shells fell in Zawtar al- Gharbiyah, Zawtar al-Sharqiyah, Qa'qa'iyat al-Jisr, Frun, and al- Ghanduriyah towns and on the suburbs of Kafr Rumman, Tulin, Majdal Silm, al-Sawwanah, Qabrikha, al-Tamiriyah, and Safad al-Battikh. These areas came under bombardment from various large-caliber and long-range guns and scores of shells landed on them this evening. The fierce shelling coincided with helicopter flights over the al-Litani River basin. It was continuing at 1715. Before noon today, the occupation forces' artillery stationed in al-Shumariyah and al-Zaffatah shelled the outskirts of Zawtar al- Gharbiyah and Zawtar al-Sharqiyah towns and al-Litani River basin. Shelling in the morning and the afternoon covered Majdal Silm, al- Sawwanah, Qabrikha, Wadi al-Hujayr, Khirbat Silm, and Tulin. Citizen Hasan 'Ali Amin 'Awali was killed in al-Tamiriyah and many homes were damaged in the villages. Bra'shit, Shaqra, and Majdal Silm towns and Wadi al-Saluqi came under bombardment this morning. The resistance men retaliated this afternoon by attacking the militia [South Lebanese Army] positions in 'Alman and al-Shumariyah and detonated an explosive charge under a Lahdist patrol on the road leading to these two positions. The resistance men this morning attacked positions of the enemy and its agents in Bra'shit, Bayt Yahun, Saydun, and al-Suwayda'. Casualties among the personnel manning these posts were not known because the occupation forces' security cordon around the area. [end recording]
