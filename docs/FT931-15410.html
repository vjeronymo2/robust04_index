930113 FT 13 JAN 93 / Observer: Anyone for qat? The organisers of the UN-sponsored Somalia peace talks that ended in Addis Ababa yesterday did their best to put the bitterly divided delegates at their ease. Warlords attending the UN cocktail party in a grand ballroom at the Hilton Hotel rubbed shoulders with the secretary- general, dipped into huge trays of smoked salmon and hors d'oeuvres, and made polite noises about a huge ice sculpture in the centre of the room depicting not a swan, but the letters 'UN'. But most appreciated was a homely touch when business got under way. Qat, the leaf which when chewed releases a mild amphetamine, is to Somalia what tea is to Britain or coffee is to Europe. Every day large quantities were purchased in Ethiopia, wrapped up in large banana fronds, and delivered into the conference room by waiters in white tails.
