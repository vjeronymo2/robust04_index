BFN [Text] The golden jubilee session of the Economic and Social Commission for Asia and the Pacific [ESCAP] begins in New Delhi tomorrow. At a joint press briefing with the ESCAP executive secretary in New Delhi today, the commerce secretary, Mr. Tejinder Khanna, said India will voice its concern at the meeting over any form of neoprotectionist measures in the post-Uruguay round of Trade and Tariff Agreement. He said we would not like any alibis to be trotted out to serve as a roadblock to global trade. The ESCAP executive secretary, Mr. Rafiuddin Ahmad, described the new GATT deal as a package of mutual benefit. He, however, said the member countries have to be watchful against any kind of protectionism and ensure that the agreement is implemented in letter and spirit.
