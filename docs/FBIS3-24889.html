Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Lagos, 2 Feb. (NAN/PANA)--The Nigerian Government has appointed Colonel Mohammed Mawa to be liaison officer at the United Nations, UN, headquarters in New York on military issues involving the world body and Nigeria. Announcing the appointment at a news briefing, the director of the directorate of Army public relations, Col. Okon, said that the colonel would address and facilitate issues relating to the welfare of Nigerian troops in UN peace keeping operations. Col. Okon said that the officer's deployment to the UN headquarters was aimed at eliminating the frequent bureaucratic bottlenecks that had often caused delays in the payment of the allowances to Nigerian troops in UN operations, particularly those in Somalia. Col. Mohammed Mawa was previously governor of Borno State.
