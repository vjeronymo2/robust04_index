920316 FT 16 MAR 92 / Appointments: First among rivals David Hagan, former founder and managing director of Tullet & Tokyo Equities, is taking a non-executive seat on the board of his old rivals First Equity, another equity interdealer broker, after 'falling out' with Tullets a year ago. 'Tullets was immensely successful and this in itself can lead to stresses and strains,' says Hagan who, since he left, has been working on a lengthy but abortive attempt to buy money brokers Exco from the British & Commonwealth administrators. Now the offer for Exco - an Pounds 85m bid backed by Electra and the fund management arm of Phoenix Securities which would have put Hagan in as Exco chief executive - has been declined and he is looking around for other opportunities. 'At 45 I am not quite old enough to embark on a career as a non-executive director.' He calls First Equity, which was set up in February 1987 five days before the equity inter-dealing broking arm of Tullets, 'a very impressive little outfit. They faced pretty mighty opposition, including from me, at the beginning. But there is now no clear market leader.' He was also attracted by the company's 'interesting institutional shareholders' - which happen to include Electra. Paul Henry, joint managing director of First Equity, says of his old rival's appointment: 'Attitudes have matured a lot. We have a healthy respect for one another now.'
