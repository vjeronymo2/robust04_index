Language: English Article Type:BFN [Report by Evgeniya Drumeva: "Opposition SDS on Foreign Exchange Market and Government Economic Policy"] [Text] Sofia, March 29 (BTA) -- The economic situation in this country is rapidly deteriorating, and one of the main reasons is the government's failure to attain its economic and financial objectives, the National Coordinating Council (the top leadership) of the opposition Union of Democratic Forces (SDS) found at its meeting today. According to SDS, because of the crisis of political confidence in the government the lev lost nearly half its price against the U.S. dollar in the last three weeks. As long as this crisis of political confidence is deepening, nothing could stabilize the financial and foreign-exchange markets, said Ivan Kostov MP of SDS, former finance minister in two successive governments, in 1991 and 1992. Back when the cabinet of independent economist Lyuben Berov was voted into office at the end of 1992, SDS declared itself against the "cabinet of independent experts" formula. Since that time, the opposition coalition has repeatedly pressed for early parliamentary elections and a politically committed government. A week ago, SDS moved at the National Assembly a question to the government about its economic and financial policy and declared that after it hears the reply, it would demand the National Assembly to take a stand. "I expect parliament to criticize the cabinet for its actions and inactions which disrupted the country's economic system," Mr. Kostov told reporters today. In case the majority votes down such a stand, SDS will move yet another vote of no confidence in the cabinet. The ex-Communist Bulgarian Socialist Party (BSP), which holds the majority in parliament at present, takes a softer line on the performance of the government. A week ago BSP called for faster and more resolute measures to end speculation on the foreign-exchange market and the lev depreciation. At the same time, however, the Socialists' leader Zhan Videnov said that "the foreign debt negotiations, rather than the dollar rate, are the government's top priority now". According to the Socialists, no personnel changes could be made now either in the government, or in the institutions concerned with the foreign debt negotiations. SDS, however, believes that the cabinet has failed to reach a favourable agreement with the London Club creditor banks. SDS economists argue that Poland received a better offer in principle on a settlement of its loan. According to Ivan Kostov, this is further confirmed by "the fact that the foreign-exchange reserves and the expected financial grant and loan aid from abroad will not be sufficient for the government to attain its two goals: To close a deal with the London Club and keep the rate of the Bulgarian currency stable". "In the present currency market conditions, the depletion of foreign-exchange reserves makes financial stability practically impossible. All measures taken by the cabinet and the National Bank of Bulgaria proved counterproductive with their influence on the financial and foreign-exchange market. Instead of achieving stability and halting the Bulgarian lev depreciation, in reality we witness just the opposite. We believe the main reason is that this government has already lost political credibility with almost everybody in this country", Mr. Kostov believes.
