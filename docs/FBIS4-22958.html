We have obtained complete NMR parameters for all Lewisite components (Table 1) as synthesized by method [3]: 1 + 2, b.p. 55C (10 torr); 3, b.p. 90C (10 torr); 4 + 5, b.p. 130C (10 torr). A new isomer 5 was isolated from a mixture of 4 + 5 using chromatography on silica gel (cf. ref 4), eluent - C[.sub]6[/]H[.sub]6[/], b.p. 127 C (10 torr) and the element analysis (C, H) characterized. In the spectrum for compound 5, the [.sup]1[/]H signal intensity ratio for trans/cis chlorovinyl groups was equal to 2. The [.sup]13[/]C signals are referenced on the basis of [.sup]1[/]J[.sub]CH[/] values, since for the number one carbon atom the chlorinated vinyl group is always [.sup]1[/] J[.sub]CH[/] 195 Hz. Thus, the high pole [.sup]13[/] C signal corresponds to C(2), while the weak pole corresponds to C(1). This reference is similary confirmed by the presence of observed weak pole [.sup]13[/]C inter-chain signals, whose values are characteristic for R[.sub]3[/]As (R = Me (4.2 Hz), Et (3.0 Hz) (see ref. 7). Furthermore, two dimensional correlational spectrum of [.sup]1[/] H[.sup]13[/]C COSY demonstrated that the high pole [.sup]1[/]H signal corresponds to H(2), and the weak pole signal to H(1). Based on the above data, it now becomes possible to unequavically determine the presence of compound 5 in _g_-Lewisite from published NMR spectra (see ref. 2). Therefore, technical grade Lewisite is not a four-component substance as previously presumed, but rather a five-component mixture of isomers having a general formula of (ClCH=CH)[.sub]m[/]AsCl[.sub]3-m[/]. We also determined that the KCCB of carbon in the CN-group to be [.sup]3[/] J[.sub]CAsCH[/] = 5.0 Hz for PhAs(CN)Me as synthesized by method (ref. 8). Thus, this KCCB may be utilized as a structural test in the chemistry of arsenic-organic compounds containing a contiguous carbon of sp-, sp[.sup]2[/]- , or sp[.sup]3[/]-hybridization.
