930114 FT 14 JAN 93 / Chernomyrdin agrees to roll back price controls in Russia Mr Viktor Chernomyrdin, Russian prime minister, has agreed to roll back controversial price controls. The economics ministry said yesterday it was working on 'significantly' shortening the list of goods affected by restrictions on enterprises' profit margins, writes Leyla Boulton. The announcement follows moves by Mr Boris Fyodorov, the new deputy prime minister for economics and finance, to convince Mr Chernomyrdin that the decision sent the wrong signals politically about the government's determination to pursue market reforms. Mr Fyodorov, who was brought into the government to handle macroeconomic policy and to reassure domestic and foreign opinion that Russia remained committed to radical reforms, announced earlier this week he was retaining Prof Jeffrey Sachs and other western economists as advisers.
