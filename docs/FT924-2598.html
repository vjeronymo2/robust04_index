921211 FT 11 DEC 92 / UK Company News: Polly creditors lose last tranche of frozen funds THE FINAL tranche of funds frozen in English accounts of the Central Bank of Northern Cyprus at the request of administrators of Polly Peck International has been recovered by the bank, its solicitors said yesterday. The move means creditors of the collapsed electronics, fruit and leisure company have lost all security over the funds. Mr David Janney of Theodore Goddard, the solicitors acting for the bank, said yesterday that the remaining Pounds 4.7m had been called down by the Central Bank in the ordinary course of its business. The PPI administrators had originally succeeded in obtaining a court injunction freezing the British accounts of the Central Bank. However, in subsequent litigation the so-called Mareva injunctions, named after the first case in which one was imposed, were relaxed and subsequently discharged.
