BFN [Text] The Thursday sitting of the Russian Government recognized that ministries and departments do not satisfactorily fulfill instructions of the Cabinet of Ministers and the president. This issue was being considered behind the closed doors. The first deputy head of the cabinet's press service Valeriy Grishin reported to journalists that the premier sharply criticized federal ministries and departments "in stringent tones" due to the fact that they do not fulfill instructions of the president and the government and underlined that he "would not let any delays in fulfillment of instructions." In Grishin's words, the worst executive discipline is within the Economics and Finance Ministries, and in the State Property Committee which have not fulfilled the greatest number of instructions. Sources close to the Cabinet told Interfax that the following data were mentioned at the sitting: As of May 1, ministries and departments have completely fulfilled 156 of 296 instructions, six have not been fulfilled at all and the rest are on the preparation stage. The government has recommended to release one of the deputy chairman of the State Property Committee from his post and two deputies Ministers, their names are not being voiced yet. High-ranking employees, including leaders of several departments in the government have received severe reprimands and warnings.
