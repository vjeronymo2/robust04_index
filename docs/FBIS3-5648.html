Language: Japanese Article Type:BFN [Text] At a news conference following a meeting of the cabinet on 25 February, Manae Kubato, director general of the Economic Planning Agency [EPA], opposed the idea of setting targets for reducing Japan's current-accounts surplus. She said, "The current-accounts surplus is the most difficult economic factor for the government to control." She also said, "I understand that Japan has maintained the principles of free trade in the Japanese-U.S. economic framework talks." In this way, Kubota indicated that setting any kind of numerical target could lead to managed trade.
