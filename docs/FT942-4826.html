940608 FT 08 JUN 94 / Channel 4 chief proposes alliance Mr Michael Grade, chief executive of Channel 4, yesterday proposed an alliance with the BBC in competition to ITV and satellite broadcasters, Raymond Snoddy writes. Mr Grade, a former director of programmes at the BBC, has turned his attentions to the corporation after failing to persuade ITV to work with Channel 4. He suggested that Channel 4 and the BBC jointly make and distribute films, produce drama and buy programmes from Hollywood. He said: 'We are natural partners. We are not threats to each other's revenues and as public service broadcasters we exist only to spend money on British programmes.' Mr Grade is trying to wean the BBC away from a growing relationship with Sky Television, a venture in which Pearson, owner of the Financial Times, has a significant stake. He believes the BBC and Sky have jointly been buying programmes in the US in addition to its Premier League football deal. He said: 'I feel that the relationship between the BBC and Sky will end in tears because there is a conflict. In the end a popular and successful BBC will prevent Sky making a breakthrough.'
