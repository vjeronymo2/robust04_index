Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Mikhail Lashch report: "Rumors About Attempt on President's Life. There Has Been No Attempt"] [Text] Yesterday rumors spread in Moscow about an attempt on President Boris Yeltsin's life. KOMMERSANT-DAILY's correspondent turned to the Russian Main Security Administration [MSA] for comment. According to MSA information, what gave rise to the rumor was the following event. Andrey Oligov, press secretary of the president's Main Security Administration, said that on 15 March security officers at the president's summer house detained a 45-year-old local resident who tried to sneak into the compound. The intruder said that he entered the guarded territory because he was looking for his missing son, a serviceman. This information was checked and proved to be correct; afterward the detainee was released. According to KOMMERSANT-DAILY information, yesterday at 1530 Aleksandr Korzhakov, chief of the presidential security service, called the Kremlin from Sochi to say that no other incidents have occurred on the territory of the presidential residence in Sochi. The president continues his vacation in Sochi; his health is normal.
