BFN [Text] Currently, at the UDMR [Democratic Union of Hungarians in Romania] headquarters in Cluj, a news conference is taking place organized by the ethnic Hungarians' associations and organizations, as well as representatives of the Roman Catholic and Reformed Churches. Among the audience there are distinguished authors members of the UDMR, among whom Sandor Fodor, the deputy chairman of the PEN Club of the Hungarian authors living in Romania, Kalman Csiha, the Cluj Roman Catholic bishop, Laszlo Tokes, the Oradea Reformed Church bishop, and experts in various domains of history and culture, our correspondent, Florin Zaharia, reports. The goal of today's news conference in Cluj is to present the stance of the UDMR opposing the beginning of the archaeological excavations in the center of that municipality.
