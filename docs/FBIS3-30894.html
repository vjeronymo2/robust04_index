Language: English Article Type:BFN ["Pool" item] [Text] Geneva, Feb 10 (TANJUG) -- Co-Chairman of the International Conference on the Former Yugoslavia, Lord Owen on Thursday said he believed chances were good that the agreement on a ceasefire and the withdrawal of artillery from positions around Sarajevo and its placing under the control of U.N. peace-keepers would be honoured. Bosnian Serb and Muslim military representatives on Wednesday reached a ceasefire agreement through the mediation of U.N. Protection Force (UNPROFOR) Commander for the Former Yugoslav Republic of Bosnia-Herzegovina, General Michael Rose. The ceasefire agreement is to go into effect at noon on Thursday. The two sides also agreed to place their artillery weapons under UNPROFOR control while the ceasefire agreement is in force. Lord Owen said the NATO ultimatum for Serbs to pull back their artillery to lines 20 kilometers from the centre of Sarajevo or else air strikes against them would be launched had had a strong impact on the Serbs. Owen said in a statement to BBC Radio he hoped that, at the next round of the peace talks on Bosnia-Herzegovina, opening on Thursday afternoon, the parties would reach agreement to appoint a U.N. administrator-designate for Sarajevo in preparation for the city's demilitarization.
