920918 FT 18 SEP 92 / Manx report can be released THE long-awaited report into the Pounds 42m collapse of the Savings and Investment Bank on the Isle of Man 10 years ago can now be published, a judge ruled yesterday. The 500-page Chadwick report into the bank's crash was shelved until the trial of two of its senior executives on fraud charges had been completed. The report is likely further to embarrass the island's government over the past standards of regulation of its offshore financial industry. Publication is expected to open the way for ex-gratia payments to SIB depositors. The government hopes that will enable the island - which now has one of the toughest regulatory regimes in the world - to put the affair behind it. Two years ago a Bank of England report into general supervisory standards on the island was severely critical of Manx treasury officials. The Chadwick report is also expected to criticise the officials. Publication is expected on Tuesday after deletions ordered yesterday by Deemster John Corrin, the Manx High Court's senior judge. He said the deletions were minor and would not affect the thrust of the report. He had received only one representation - from Fryers Bell, the island accountants - that its name be erased from the report. Depositors welcomed the decision, but criticised the Pounds 5,000 maximum level of individual payments likely from a government fund of Pounds 4.45m. Depositor protection schemes introduced on the island since the collapse mirror those of the UK and allow for a Pounds 15,000 maximum individual claim, with money levied from all other holders of banking licences on the island.
