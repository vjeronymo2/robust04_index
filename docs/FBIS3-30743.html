Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Text] In the presidential palace today Dr. Franjo Tudjman, president of the Republic of Croatia, received Janez Drnovsek, prime minister of the Republic of Slovenia, who had previously held talks with the Croatian Government delegation about relations between the two countries. Agreements on bilateral cooperation were also signed, the president's office has announced. The two prime ministers, Janez Drnovsek and Nikica Valentic, informed President Tudjman about the agreements the two delegations signed during their talks. Both prime ministers agreed that their meeting today signified a major step in the further rapprochement between the two states and in building confidence and trade cooperation, in particular. The views of the two sides on a number of outstanding problems such as the border issue and Ljubljanska Banka [the Ljubljana Bank] converged even further today. These issues are expected to be resolved soon for the benefit of both sides. At the end of the meeting President Tudjman and Prime Minister Drnovsek spoke about the situation in Bosnia-Herzegovina and the possibilites for resolving the crisis in the area of former Yugoslavia. The Slovene prime minister was accompanied by his Foreign Minister Lojze Peterle and Slovene Ambassador to Croatia Matija Valesic. In addition to Prime Minister Valentic, on the Croatian side the talks were attended by Jure Radic, head of President Tudjman's office; Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of Foreign Affairs Mate Granic; Croatian Ambassador to Slovenia Miljenko Zagar; President Tudjman's foreign policy adviser Zeljko Matic, and his aide-de-camp Brigadier Kresimir Kaspar.
