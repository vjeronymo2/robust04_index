BFN [Text] Chinese Vice Premier and Foreign Minister Qian Qichen began a visit to Belarus on 29 June. Belarusian Foreign Minister Pyotr Krawchanka said at a news conference that during talks with his Chinese counterpart -- held on the first day of the visit -- bilateral cooperation and cooperation within the framework of international organizations were discussed. The Chinese side expressed great interest in the extension of economic relations between the two states. As of today, Belarus and China have signed 11 bilateral agreements concerning all spheres of cooperation. An agreement to sign new pacts in the immediate future on bilateral cooperation in such areas as communication and information systems, air traffic, and criminal extraditions was reached. A principal decision to draft a political agreement between the two states was also adopted. All these documents could be signed in late 1994 during a planned visit by top Chinese officials to Minsk. Practical issues connected with the establishment of joint Belarusian-Chinese ventures -- primarily the setting up of a joint venture dealing with the assembling and servicing of BELAZ trucks on Chinese territory -- were also discussed. Mr. Qian Qichen pointed out in his speech that China favors the strengthening of Belarusian-Chinese cooperation in all fields. The Chinese foreign minister sees the key goal of bilateral relations in the implementation of the existing 11 agreements.
