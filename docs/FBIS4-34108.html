BFN [By special envoy Aissa G. Garcia] [Text] Cartagena de Indias, 15 Jun (PL) -- In addition to attending the Fourth Ibero-American Summit, Cuban President Fidel Castro has conducted an intense program of bilateral meetings that surpasses those of previous summits. PRENSA LATINA has learned that the Cuban president met with the leaders of Spain, Mexico, Colombia, and Honduras. For Cuba, these meetings have been useful and appropriate. The situation in Cuba and in other countries, as well as the contents and purpose of the Ibero-American summits, were the topics that prevailed during these meetings. It was learned that the Cuban president and his Honduran counterpart, Roberto Reina, met early this morning and that the meeting was very good. Cuba and Honduras broke diplomatic relations in 1961 and since then only some sports and commercial exchanges, as well as exchanges at the parliamentary level, have been conducted. The leader of the Cuban Revolution, the center of attention during this summit, also met with Paraguayan President Juan Carlos Wasmosy.
