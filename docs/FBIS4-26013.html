BFN [Text] Moscow, June 16 (XINHUA) -- Vietnamese Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet, who is in Moscow on a three-day official visit, remains optimistic about prospects of signing a bilateral treaty with Russia, though the sides failed to sign it during Thursday's [18 June] talks. Commenting on his talks with Russian Prime Minister Viktor Chernomyrdin, the Vietnamese premier told reporters: "Talks with the Russian side are going on productively. The new interstate treaty has been in general agreed upon and it is very important." He said the document would be signed after certain improvements. The signing of the treaty, which was originally scheduled to take place at mid-day on Thursday, was put off till the evening. A sources close to the Russian Government said the sides had failed to eliminate various disagreements during the talks between the two premiers. Sources from the Vietnamese delegation explained that the disputed issues include prospecting and production of oil and natural gas by the joint "Vietsovpetro" venture, the problems of the Vietnamese community in Russia, as well as the Russian naval base in Cam Ranh. To explain the delay of the signing of the documents, Chernomyrdin said that "matters of technical presentation" requiring specialists' attention cropped up during discussion. "Essentially, there have been no differences," he noted. "We are convinced that Russia and Vietnam have every opportunity and potential to step up traditional cooperation in the commercial, economic, technological, scientific and other areas," he said. However, the Vietnamese premier said the two sides differed on the following matters: the problem of paying off Vietnam's debt to Russia inherited from the former Soviet Union, terms of the operation of Cam Ranh base by the Russian Navy, and issues about the stay of Vietnamese citizens in Russia.
