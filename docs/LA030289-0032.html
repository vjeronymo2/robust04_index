March 2, 1989, Thursday, Home Edition

DELAHOUSSAYE WINS FEATURE ON GORGEOUS

Gorgeous, ridden by Eddie Delahoussaye, scored her second victory in three career starts Wednesday, beating My Glamorous One, with Chris McCarron aboard, by three-quarters of a length in the $80,200 Santa Ysabel Stakes at Santa Anita.

April Mon, ridden by Gary Stevens, finished third, another four lengths back, in the 1 1/16-mile race for 3-year-old fillies.

Delahoussaye kept Gorgeous along the inside, and the filly set the pace most of the way, then held off My Glamorous One in the stretch drive.

The 8-5 wagering favorite of the crowd of 21,488, General Charge, never threatened and finished fourth in the six-horse field, 12 lengths behind of the winner.

Gorgeous, who carried 116 pounds and was clocked in 1:42 2/5, paid $9.20, $5.40 and $3.40. My Glamorous One, carrying 117, returned $8 and $4.60. April Mon, 114, paid $4.60 to show.

Trained by Neil Drysdale, the daughter of Slew O' Gold earned $46,450 for owner Robert N. Clay.
