930120 FT 20 JAN 93 / Commodities and Agriculture: Nickel mine runs out of ore THE NAMEW Lake nickel mine in Manitoba, Canada, which started production in 1988, is to close permanently at the end of this year because it has run out of ore. The mine, which had been producing 9,000 tonnes of nickel in concentrate a year, is 60 per cent owned by Hudson Bay Mining and Smelting, a Minorco subsidiary, and Outokumpu, the state-owned Finnish group, owns the rest. Namew employs 170 and some will be moved to other mines.
