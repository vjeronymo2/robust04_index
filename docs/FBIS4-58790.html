BFN [Text] Tunis, 8 May (MENA)--PLO Executive Committee member Samir Ghawshah has stated that no decision has been made yet on the names of the members of the National Authority Council, which will take over self-rule responsibility in Gaza and Jericho. In a statement to the MENA correspondent in Tunis, Ghawshah said consultations are under way with the Palestinian leaders in the occupied territories to obtain their agreement to join the council. He added some leaders have agreed in principle to participate in the council, others have asked for more time to think about the subject, while many have declined to participate for various reasons. Ghawshah said consultations are continuing between Palestinian president Yasir 'Arafat and the diaspora Palestinian leaders, including members of the PLO Executive Committee and the Fatah Central Council, to choose 12 members for the council from the diaspora. The 12 other members will be chosen from the occupied territories. Ghawshah said he believes the formation of the council and the announcement of the names of its members will not be completed before Wednesday, 11 May, the date when 'Arafat will deliver to Israeli Prime Minister Yitzhaq Rabin a list of the names of members of the National Authority Council and the self-rule establishments. 'Arafat continued consultations last night and this morning with the members of the PLO Executive Committee and the Fatah Central Council and the representatives of the Palestinian factions. 'Arafat will continue his consultations today before leaving for Johannesburg tonight to attend the inauguration of Nelson Mandela as president of South Africa. Palestinian sources have stated that a number of prominent Palestinian leaders in the occupied territories have expressed a desire not to participate in the National Authority Council. They include Faysal al-Husayni, Fatah representative in the West Bank; and Dr. Sa'ib 'Urayqat, chief Palestinian negotiator at the Washington talks. Regarding the Palestinian personalities in the diaspora, it has become certain that Faruq Qaddumi, head of the PLO Political Department, will not participate in the council. The PLO Political Department will continue to operate from Tunis. Mahmud 'Abbas, also known as Abu-Mazin, head of the PLO National and International Relations Department, will also remain abroad to follow-up the talks on the final phase of the solution of the Palestinian issue.
