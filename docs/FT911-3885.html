910423 FT 23 APR 91 / Teaching standards criticised ONE IN three primary school pupils is not being taught to read and write properly, the schools' inspectorate said yesterday, writes Andrew Adonis. In a report on the implementation of the national curriculum, the inspectorate spoke of widespread teacher shortages, cramped conditions, few schools with adequate libraries and insufficient instruction in basic reading and writing skills. Only in two thirds of schools was the teaching and learning of English held to be satisfactory. English Key Stage 1, HMI, HMSO Pounds 2.75.
