931004 FT 04 OCT 93 / Observer: Corker Whilst lunching alone at a restaurant near the House of Commons, shadow industry spokesman Robin Cook was interrupted in his musings by the arrival of a bottle of fine wine at his table. Its provenance? A man sitting nearby promptly introduced himself as a director of a company recently acquired by Anglo-American conglomerate Hanson. Infuriated by what he saw as the new owner's policy of cutting investment, he had naturally relished the opportunity to wine, if not dine, the senior Labour figure - at the expense of one of the Tory party's more generous donors.
