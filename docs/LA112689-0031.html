November 26, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

A 'REAL' TV WOMAN

There has been much criticism of the portrayal of women in the new crop of TV shows this fall, but there is one show I want to commend.

That show is "Peaceable Kingdom." Somehow, in the midst of America's TV wasteland, this show successfully portrays: a woman in a leadership position who is competent at both management and parenting; realistic conflict among adults; a message of ecological concern and value; real-life child-rearing issues that interest kids of nearly all ages; no unrealistic or gratuitous violence and sex, just reasonably healthy relationships and normal motivations.

Wher else on the major networks can we find so much to admire -- and enjoy -- in one hour?

PAUL M. DU BOIS

San Rafael, Calif.

"Peaceable Kingdom," which stars Lindsay Wagner as a zoo director, has been canceled. It will disappear Dec. 6.
