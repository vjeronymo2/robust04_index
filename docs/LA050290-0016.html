May 2, 1990, Wednesday, Home Edition

NEW FORMAT ON EDITORIAL PAGES

It's difficult to tell one's wife that her new outfit doesn't quite suit her, or one's mother that her home-cooked meals are too salty, or indeed to tell the editors of The Times that their new editorial page format (introduced April 23) sucks.

But I've done it, and I'm doing it.

There is no newspaper west of the Hudson River that has had two pages of opinion more readable, informative and deserving of respect than yours; why change its dress? The varying type styles heading the editorials are confounding and demeaning, making them appear as news stories of differing import; the movement of the editorials from the dignified and traditional left column to a scattering across the top half of the page shocked me as much as the publishers of "new" Bibles did when they abandoned the double columns of the old St. James version; the maze of horizontal and vertical lines between pieces are as unseemly as they are unnecessary.

You may think the changed format will gain some new readers; please consider its offense to the old.

RAYMOND E. PONATH

La Mesa
