Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS parliamentary correspondent Lyudmila Yermakova] [Text] Moscow February 25 TASS -- "I favour the formation of a democratic consensus in foreign policy to prevent any extremist pronouncements. The consensus should proceed from the Russian president's initiative on the Bosnian settlement," Deputy Russian Foreign Minister and presidential envoy to the conflict zone Vitaliy Churkin said here today. "We do not need war and confrontation, we need a strong foreign policy to be based on democratic consensus," said the diplomat commenting on ITAR-TASS request at today's decision of the Federation Council to assign additional troops to the U.N. forces. The decision taken on request of Boris Yeltsin will give hope to the consensus, he said. In the words of Vitaliy Churkin, the question of expanded Russian presence in other areas has not been raised. "We still have to analyze the crisis around Sarajevo -- the behavior of Russians and their Western partners, members of NATO," said Churkin. "Our attempts to peacefully settle problems of the crisis have not always been supported," he noted. "We should clearly outline our stand and keep to it," said Churkin.
