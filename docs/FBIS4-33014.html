BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities monitored through 26 May. The Central Bank has reported that the monetary base grew by a nominal 37 percent in April; 3.9 percent less than in March. It also reported that in April the Brazilian international net reserves totaled $38.3 billions while its short term reserves totaled $32.3 billion. During the same period, the monetary base grew 49 percent by the peak concept and 45 percent by the daily average concept. (Sao Paulo O ESTADO DE SAO PAULO in Portuguese 20 May 94 p B4) Alexandre Loloian, Labor Ministry Employment and Salary Policies secretary, has reported that during the first quarter the national labor market registered 70,981 new jobs, of which 48,796 were created in March -- 0.22 percent up on February. (Sao Paulo O ESTADO DE SAO PAULO in Portuguese 21 May 94 p B5) Based on a study of the daily quotation of 1,300 products sold in 11 supermarket chains, Abilio Diniz, vice president of the Pao de Acucar supermarket chain, foresees that supermarket prices will have to be adjusted before the implementation of the real because there is a 15 to 20 percent price difference between sale and stock replacement prices. (Sao Paulo FOLHA DE SAO PAULO in Portuguese 24 May 94 S2 p 9) Household appliance sales, which had increased considerably during the first quarter of the year, dropped by 20 percent in May compared with the same month last year. This led several companies to plan collective vacations for their employees. Most of the 4,000 Arno company employees will have a 5-week vacation as of June, the company president announced. Lourival Kicula, who coordinates household appliances industries, says all companies currently have a 15 to 30-day stock which is beyond the desirable stock of 5 days. (Sao Paulo FOLHA DE SAO PAULO in Portuguese 24 May 94 S2 p 1) The Sao Paulo State Federation of Industries has reported that the unemployment level increased by 0.25 percent during the second week of May with the dismissal of 5,893 employees in Sao Paulo. The accumulated unemployment rate this year is 1.8 percent or 43,800 dismissals. The accumulated unemployment rate for the past 12 months is 2.82 percent following the dismissal of 66,837 workers. Of the 46 industries studied, most workers were dismissed in the textile, railway equipment, paper, cardboard, and rubber industries. The industries with greatest job hirings were marble, granite, metal frames, doors, windows, and construction materials. (Sao Paulo GAZETA MERCANTIL in Portuguese 25 May 94 p 2) The attempt by the Espirito Santo government to privatize its 23,17 percent participation in the Espirito Santo Power Plants, Escelsa, through an auction at the Rio de Janeiro Stock Exchange on 24 May failed completely due to the lack of bidders. A new auction should be held next 1 or 6 June. The base price of each Escelsa share was quoted at $100. (Sao Paulo GAZETA MERCANTIL in Portuguese 25 May 94 p21) The Brazilian Federation of Bank Associations today made public a report on the 1993 banking social balance which shows that the banks made a $2.9 billion profit on net assets which amount to $24 billion. Profits amounted to 12.3 percent of their assets. The dividends paid by the banks to their shareholders amounted to $730 million. The report shows that during 1993 the banks paid $22 billion in salaries to their 674,500 employees and $2.3 billion in taxes and social contributions. The Brazilian banking system consists of 244 banks with more than 31,000 branches, holding more than 47 million savings accounts and 44 million checking accounts, 3.7 million of which correspond to companies. The report shows that the average monthly volume of credit operations amounts to $95 billion, their portfolio of financial applications and securities amounts to $25 billion, 80 percent of which is made up of government papers. (Sao Paulo AGENCIA ESTADO in Portuguese 2021 GMT 26 May 94)
