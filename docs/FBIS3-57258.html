Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Baku (January 24) TURAN--A seminar of CIS national departments of Interpol took place in Baku during January 15-17, chief of Azeri National Bureau of Interpol Captain Tofik Murshudlu told TURAN news agency. Representatives of national bureaus of Interpol from nine CIS states participated at the seminar. Representatives of Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, Tajikistan and Turkmenistan could not take part in the seminar for technical matters. Armenian representatives did not participate at Baku seminar as well, though they sent their proposals to the organization committee of the forum. Baltic Interpol departments were presented by the chief of Latvian Interpol Department. A resolution on the results of the seminar is adopted. The resolution states the necessity of development of the work on bringing the laws in the former USSR republics in conformity with the international norms, working out principals of cooperation and interconnection in the struggle against crime and narcotic drugs smuggling. The importance of improvement the communication means and exchange of information is also stressed.
