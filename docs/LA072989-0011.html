July 29, 1989, Saturday, Home Edition

THE GOSPEL ACCORDING TO MURRAY NOT FOUND IN THIS GOOD BOOKIE

Gee, the pulpit's still warm. Jim Murray must have just left.

Why not throw a few bucks Oregon's way? Because of some long-ago ignored edict? Do we, the people, bury our heads in the sand and wish away gambling?

I have a cousin who happens to be a Catholic priest. Each year, he and I wager on who will finish higher in the standings, the Angels or Chicago White Sox.

Is he a sinner now, morally inept? Does Father Murray proclaim himself more righteous than my cousin?

My point is, gambling is a victim-less "crime." There are quite a few of us who are able to function every day and keep betting in its place.

Why should Oregonians gamble and see their monetary losses driven out of the state to Nevada. This way, it all stays home and the losses help the state colleges.

I excuse the vicar of The Times for calling me a low life.

GREG MURPHY

Pacoima
