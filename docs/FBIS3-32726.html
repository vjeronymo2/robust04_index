Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] A Foreign Ministry communique states the Republic of Panama established diplomatic ties with the Hashemite Kingdom of Jordan on 7 February. The two countries are establishing diplomatic ties inspired by the desire to foster, strengthen, and develop friendship, harmony, and mutual cooperation based on the principles and objectives of the UN Charter. The diplomatic ties, which will be at the level of concurrent ambassadors, are established based on provisions of the 1961 Vienna Convention. The Panamanian and Jordanian Governments, represented by Carlos Arosemena and 'Adnan Abu-'Awdah, respectively, signed the agreement at UN Headquarters in New York City. The date when the two governments will appoint ambassadors was not disclosed during the signing ceremony.
