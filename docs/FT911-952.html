910509 FT 09 MAY 91 / International Company News: Big investor attacks APM accounting A BIG institutional investor yesterday attacked the paucity of information in the accounts of Denmark's largest privately-owned business, the AP Moller shipping, oil and gas production group, writes Hilary Barnes in Copenhagen. Mr Jeppe Christiansen, representing Lonmodtagernes Dyrtidsfond (LD Fund), the trade union pension fund, claimed the accounts failed to give a fair picture of the company. He was speaking at the annual shareholders' meeting of D/S1912, one of the two parent companies for the APM group. He called for more information to be published with next year's accounts. The APM group's two partnerships and two parent companies reported profits after tax of DKr1.14bn (Dollars 174m) last year, a a decline of 29 per cent.
