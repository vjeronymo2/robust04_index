BFN [By ITAR-TASS special correspondents Albert Kochetkov and Mikhail Shevtsov] [Text] Tbilisi, June 10 (TASS) -- Russian Defense Minister Pavel Grachev's mission on the settlement of the Georgian-Abkhazian conflict has been a success. After holding talks this morning with Georgian leader Eduard Shevardnadze and receiving his consent for Russia to begin a peacekeeping operation in Abkhazia, the minister left for Gudauta where he met with Vladislav Ardzinba and achieved the same result. Taking part in the talks in Gudauta along with the Defense Minister Pavel Grachev were Ambassador of the Russian Federation to Georgia Vladimir Semskiy and Commander of Airborne Troops of the Russian Defense Ministry Colonel-General Gennadiy Podkolzin. A final decision on the beginning of a peacekeeping operation will depend on the upper house of the Russian parliament. The defense minister expressed the hope that a session of the federation council scheduled for June 14 would approve Russian President Boris Yeltsin's decree on the participation of Russian peacekeepers in the settlement of the Georgian-Abkhazian conflict. According to the defense minister, a peacekeeping operation in Abkhazia will begin three-five days after a political decision is adopted. He expressed the hope that along with Russia other Commonwealth countries would take part in the operation, particularly states - parties to the treaty on collective security. In accordance with the worked out plan of the operation in Abkhazia, the defense minister explained, the first to start it will be units of the 345th Airborne Regiment stationed in Gudauta, as well as three battalions from the group of Russian troops in Transcaucasian region. The order on their formation has been already given. In the future, Pavel Grachev noted, it is planned to bring into the zone of disengagement along the Inguri river the main forces -- three battalions formed on the basis of peacekeeping divisions from the Leningrad and Privolzhskiy military districts. The personnel and materiel will be delivered by military transport aircraft to airports in Sukhumi and Gudauta. On his return from Gudauta tonight, Pavel Grachev told journalists that a final calculation of all aspects of the peacekeeping operation and all means and forces to be involved in it would be made during this night. It is exactly with this aim that from the Tbilisi airport he set for the headquarters of the group of Russian troops in the Transcaucasian region where all details of the oncoming beginning of the peacekeeping operation will be discussed, according to informed sources from the government circles in Tbilisi.
