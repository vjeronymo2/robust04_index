December 23, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

WORLD IN BRIEF;

COSTA RICA;

QUAKE CUTS POWER, FRIGHTENS SHOPPERS

A series of earthquakes shook Costa Rica, shattering windows and sending panicked Christmas shoppers into the streets.

There were no reports of injuries. The strongest of at least six temblors -- with a magnitude of 5.7 -- crumbled the wall of an empty prison, loosened chunks from the facade of a bank building and briefly cut off telephone and electric service. A 1,000-foot-wide landslide blocked a road west from San Jose.
