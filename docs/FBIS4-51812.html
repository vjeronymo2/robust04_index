BFN [Text] Seoul, April 24 (YONHAP) -- South Korea's corporate investment in foreign countries in the first quarter amounted to 466 million U.S. dollars, up 250 percent from a year earlier, according to the Finance Ministry. The sharp increase was attributed to greatly relaxed rules governing foreign investment. The manufacturing sector accounted for 70 percent of the amount, and trade, transportation, construction and fisheries the rest. North America was the top investment market for South Korean industries, representing 46 percent, or 214 million dollars, thanks to brisk investment in computer parts factories in the area. Asia placed second with 32 percent and Europe third with 19 percent. Investment by small- and medium-sized businesses also increased 55 percent from the same quarter of last year, indicating that small-amount overseas investment was steadily increasing.
