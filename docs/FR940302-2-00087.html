Notice is hereby given that, pursuant to the Paperwork Reduction Act of 1980 (44 U.S.C. 3501 et seq. ), the Securities and Exchange Commission (Commission) has submitted to the Office of Management and Budget the following requests for extension of previously approved forms and rules under the Investment Company Act of 1940 (1940 Act). Form N&hyph;6F permits a company that has lost its exclusion from the 1940 Act because it intends to make a public offering as a business development company, but is not ready to file Form N&hyph;54A, to remain exempt from the Act for up to ninety days. It is estimated that Form N&hyph;6F takes .5 hours per response. Form N&hyph;8B&hyph;2 is the registration statement form used by unit investment trusts currently issuing securities to register under the 1940 Act. It is estimated that Form N&hyph;8B&hyph;2 takes 1,626 hours per response. Form N&hyph;54A is the notification of election to be regulated as a business development company. It is estimated that Form N&hyph;54A takes .5 hours per response. Form N&hyph;54C is used to notify the Commission that a company withdraws its election to be regulated as a business development company. It is estimated that Form N&hyph;54C takes 1 hour per response. Rule 24f&hyph;1 permits certain investment companies which have inadvertent oversales of their shares to register such shares. The reporting burden under Rule 24f&hyph;1 is estimated to be 2 hours per response. Rule 30a&hyph;1 requires every registered investment company to file a semi-annual report with the Commission. The burden of meeting the requirement of this rule is the burden of filing Form N&hyph;SAR, the reporting form prescribed under the rule. Approval for Form N&hyph;SAR has been given separately. General comments regarding the estimated burden hours should be directed to Gary Waxman at the address below.
