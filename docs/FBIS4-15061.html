BFN [Text] Moscow, 6 Apr (ITAR-TASS) -- Russian Federation President Boris Yeltsin sent a message to the participants in the international symposium "The Lessons of the Holocaust and Present-Day Russia." The text of the message, released by the Russian Federation president's press service, follows: "To the participants in the international symposium: "The Lessons of the Holocaust and the Present-Day Russia" "For the first time Russia is marking, together with all the civilized countries, the International Day of Commemoration of the Jews who fell victim to Nazism. "At present, when political extremism is rearing its head with increasing brazenness all over the world, it is particularly important that we should remember the victims and not forget the guilty. We are bowing are heads before the tragedy of the people against which Fascism perpetrated one of the most hideous crimes. "On the eve of the 50th anniversary of the great victory, we are paying tribute to the exploits of the liberating army that saved European Jews from total extermination. "We are bowing low to thousands of our compatriots who were risking their own lives by sheltering Jews, their neighbors. "I wish the participants in the congress success in their work."
