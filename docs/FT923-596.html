920928 FT 28 SEP 92 / UK Company News: Sphere raises Dollars 4m and wins its independence SPHERE, the California video game company controlled by the late Robert Maxwell through a Liechtenstein charitable trust, has won its independence. The fast-growing company that has produced popular games, such as an F-16 flight simulator, has raised Dollars 4m from new investors for additional marketing and development and buying out shares formerly held by Swico, a charitable trust which was in effect its parent. Earlier this year it emerged that Sphere, which trades under the name Spectrum HoloByte, appeared to be a private Maxwell company beyond the reach of Arthur Andersen, administrators to Mr Maxwell's private interests. The negotiations over the future of Sphere have involved Arthur Andersen. As a result in return for the Swico shares Dollars 2.3m was paid into an escrow account and a further provision of Dollars 4m in preferred shares set aside to cover any further unexpected liabilities. Before the re-financing package was put together all Maxwell appointees to the company were removed. Mr Gilman Louie, president and chief executive of Spectrum HoloByte, said: 'This financing positions the company to work with our new partners in bringing products and technologies to the industry.'
