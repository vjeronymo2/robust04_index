931112 FT 12 NOV 93 / Arts: Museum of London music festival A world music festival celebrating the capital's abundance of traditional music is to be held on November 20-21 at the Museum of London. A part of the museum's new exhibition, The Peopling of London, it includes music from Irish, Jewish and Somali groups. On the Saturday Taxi Pata Pata present jazz from Zaire in an evening concert. On Sunday afternoon, broadcaster Andy Kershaw presents traditional London music which includes Dishari, an East End ensemble playing Bengali folk music and Royte Klezmores, an all woman East European trio playing wedding music. Entry on Sunday is free. The main exhibition, which runs until May 15 1994, looks at London's current ethnic and cultural diversity in the historical perspective of 15,000 years of settlement from overseas. The Peopling of London, Museum of London, London Wall, London EC2.
