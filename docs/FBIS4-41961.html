BFN [Text] Presidential candidate Aleksandr Lukashenko who is firmly in the lead after the first round of presidential elections in Belarus and three of his campaign officers were attacked by police outside his office at the Government House in Minsk on Monday [27 June]. Yesterday, the House Management handed Lukashenko the keys to his office where the anti-corruption commission he led had worked before. However, the office was again closed this morning. Interior Minister Aleksandr Danko issued an order to provide Lukashenko with security guards of the chief state security department who accompanied him to his office on Tuesday. In an interview with INTERFAX, Lukashenko said that he was told by members of another security unit of the department outside the Government House that police were ordered not to let him into the building. Lukashenko spoke of a police assault on himself and his campaign officers outside the House. All four received minor injuries, a fact confirmed by ambulance workers. Lukashenko himself has formally appealed to the Prosecutor General for investigating the incident provoked, as he put it, by "Interior Minister Aleksandr Danko." Lukashenko who won the biggest number of votes in the first round now faces a run-off against Prime Minister Vyacheslav Kebich.
