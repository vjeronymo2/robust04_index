Language: Greek Article Type:BFN [Text] The National Council met in a three-hour session last night and dealt with the current developments following their determining that the process of promoting the confidence-building measures has for the time being stalled due to [Turkish Cypriot Leader] Rauf Denktas' insistence on unacceptable positions. During the meeting, President Glavkos Kliridhis briefed the National Council members on a draft letter he had prepared for UN Secretary General Butrus Butrus-Ghali giving the position of the Greek Cypriot side. This letter is to sent to the secretary general following a similar letter sent to Butrus-Ghali by Denktas. Government Spokesman Ioannis Kasoulidhis said that President Kliridhis does not consider Denktas' answer on the confidence-building measures to be positive, stressing that it contains the old demands in disguise. Speaking after the National Council meeting, DISI [Democratic Rally] Chairman Ioannis Matsis noted that Kliridhis' letter to Butrus-Ghali deals with all the positions Denktas presented in his own letter to the secretary general, which are just as negative as they were in New York. Responding to a question on whether we are considering disengaging from the confidence-building measures and discussion of the substance of the Cyprus issue, Matsis said that all issues are now at the forefront. AKEL [Restorative Party of the Working People] Secretary General Dhimitrios Khristofias said he estimates that Denktas will not change his positions, but added that he does not consider that Denktas' letter proposes unacceptable terms. Unfortunately for our side, Denktas' letter is framed in a diplomatic way. Khristofias expressed the view that our side will be in a difficult position. DIKO [Democratic Party] Chairman Spiros Kiprianou repeated that he considers the confidence-building measures to be dangerous, and that the best possible development would be to disengage from these measures. Denktas' letter, he said, reveals that the Turkish side wants to use the confidence-building measures to gain even greater benefits in its effort to promote its goals. Socialist EDEK [United Democratic Union of Cyprus] Chairman Vasos Lissaridhis said that Kliridhis' letter to the secretary general offers a golden opportunity to deal with the substance of the Cyprus issue. If this golden opportunity is not used, he said, there is a danger of getting involved in indirect talks on the implementation of the confidence-building measures, which, he added, constitute a de facto solution of the Cyprus issue.
