Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Text] Alija Izetbegovic, president of the Bosnia-Herzegovina Presidency, held a meeting in Sarajevo today with the top military leadership and the top representatives of the Ministry of Internal Affairs led by Army General Delic and Interior Minister Alispahic. The meeting was also attended by General Enver Hadzihasanovic, Bosnia-Herzegovina Army chief of staff brigadier, corps commanders of the Bosnia-Herzegovina Army, and the commander of special units of the Ministry of Internal Affairs. The following issues of importance for further development of the armed forces of the Republic of Bosnia-Herzegovina were examined at the meeting: -- The organizational set-up of the army of the federation of the Republic of Bosnia-Herzegovina in line with the Washington agreement and military agreements signed in Split, Zagreb, and Sarajevo; -- Material support for the development of the armed forces and the search for appropriate solutions for training and strengthening all segments of defense of the federation of Bosnia-Herzegovina; -- Resolution of the status of war invalids and families of fallen soldiers. The military top leadership and the representatives of the Ministry of Internal Affairs gave their full support to Alija Izetbegovic, president of the Bosnia-Herzegovina Presidency, in his further political activities aimed at the founding of the federation of Bosnia-Herzegovina. They unequivocally supported the standpoint that territories taken by force and ethnically cleansed areas where genocide was perpetrated must be returned. Other current issues were discussed at the meeting, on which the General Staff of the Bosnia-Herzegovina Army and the Ministry of Internal Affairs expressed the same views.
