BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities in Uruguay monitored through 9 June. The National Institute of Statistics, INE, has reported that the inflation rate totaled 2.76 percent in May. The consumer price index totals 14.7 percent after the first five months of the year, which represents a 6.9 percent reduction as compared with its position in May 1993. According to INE data, clothes and shoe prices accounted for the largest increase, namely 6 percent, while education accounted for the lowest increase of 0.5 percent. (Montevideo LA MANANA in Spanish 2 Jun 94 p 3) UTE, General Administration of State Electric Power and Telephones, sources have told EL OBSERVADOR ECONOMICO that the UTE board of directors has approved a 6.5 percent average increase in UTE rates as of 1 June. UTE rates are increased every two months. According to UTE estimates, UTE rates have declined, in real terms, by 33 percent between March 1990 and December 1993. (Montevideo EL OBSERVADOR ECONOMICO in Spanish 2 Jun 94 p 8) Stock Exchange operations declined by 34.3 percent in May as compared with May 1993. May operations, which totaled $38.2 million, were 22.3 percent under those reported for April. During the first five months, stock exchange operations totaled $212.4 million, which represents a 23 percent decline as compared with operations for the same period last year. (Montevideo EL OBSERVADOR ECONOMICO in Spanish 2 Jun 94 p 1)
