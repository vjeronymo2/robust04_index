BFN [Excerpt] Chetumal, Mexico, 22 Apr (NOTIMEX) -- The Mexican Navy rescued 94 Cubans after the Cubans' boat, which had drifted for several days, ran aground on the Banco Chinchorro reef area in southern Quintana Roo State. Roberto Erales Jimenez, under secretary of the state government, said the group, composed of 47 men, 27 women, and 20 children, sailed from Puerto Tuna, Cuba, on 15 April aboard the 30-foot boat "Casilda 27." Governor Mario Villanueva Madrid reported that the Cubans were rescued by Navy members, who helped them get to the village of Mahahual, some 45 km east of Chetumal, where they were given medical assistance and food. The governor said the Mexican immigration authorities issued them a permit to stay on Mexican territory until next Sunday. [passage omitted]
