Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Tunis, 14 Mar (MENA)--Palestinian President Yasir 'Arafat met again tonight with the Israeli delegation led by Jacques Neriya, the Israeli prime minister's adviser, who arrived here earlier today. This morning the Israeli delegation met with 'Arafat and held intensive talks with him. Yuri Savir, director general of the Israeli Foreign Ministry, and General 'Uzi Dayan, head of planning in the Defense Ministry, took part in the talks as well as PLO Executive Committee member Yasir 'Abd-Rabbuh; Abu-Ala' [Ahmad Quray'], head of the PLO's Economic Department; and Akram Haniyah, 'Arafat's adviser on the occupied territories affairs. Following this meeting, the Israeli delegation telephoned Israeli Prime Minister Yitzhaq Rabin and briefed him on the results of the talks with 'Arafat. The Israeli delegation arrived in Tunisia last night aboard a private Israeli plane but the Tunisian authorities refused to let it land. The delegation then returned to Rome where it boarded the plane that carried Dennis Ross, U.S. coordinator on the Middle East peace process, from Rome to Tunis.
