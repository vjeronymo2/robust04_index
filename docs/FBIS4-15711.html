BFN [By UKRINFORM correspondent Viktor Demidenko for TASS] [Text] Kiev, 10 Apr -- "I think that today, despite the imperfection of the law on elections which is a relic of the former Soviet system, we will elect a new parliament which, guided by the law, will take government into its hands and will work for reforms, for democracy, for an independent state," Ukraine President Leonid Kravchuk told journalists at a polling station today immediately after voting. Speaking of the presidential elections on 26 June, Leonid Kravchuk noted that he is personally not against them being held. However, laws on the status of the president, on his powers, on the structure of bodies of power must be adopted first. Leonid Kravchuk stressed that unless these legislative acts are adopted, he will not stand for the post of head of state. He noted, moreover, that if only representative functions are delegated to the president, then, in his opinion, such a head of state is not necessary.
