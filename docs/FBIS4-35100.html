BFN [Editorial report] The following is a compilation of reports on Panamanian economic developments monitored through 28 June. Delia Cardenas, planning and economic policy minister, said yesterday the Venezuelan Government has sent a letter to Panamanian authorities asking for the resumption of negotiations to pay Panama's oil debt with that country. Cardenas said that Panama has made several proposals to the Venezuelan Government to pay its debt, which is approximately $131 million, but that they have been rejected. (Panama City EL PANAMA AMERICA in Spanish 22 June 94 p 1) According to a report by the Comptroller General's Office, Panamanian exports increased by 34.17 percent during the first quarter of 1994 compared to the same period in 1993. Official statistics have also revealed bananas, with 38.51 percent, and shrimp with 8.51 percent, remain Panama's leading exports. (Mexico City NOTIMEX in Spanish 2058 GMT 23 June 94) President Guillermo Endara announced today the government will implement a policy of "restraint on public spending" in light of an imminent $270 million budget cut following a decision by the International Financial Institutions to withhold disbursements to Panama. (Panama City LA PRENSA in Spanish 24 June 94 p 2 A)
