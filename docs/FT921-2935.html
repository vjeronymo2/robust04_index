920317 FT 17 MAR 92 / Hopes dim for survivors of Turkish quake RESCUE teams said yesterday that there was little hope of finding more survivors from a powerful earthquake which hit eastern Turkey, Reuter reports from Erzincan, Turkey. Officials said 376 bodies had been recovered since the quake measuring 6.8 on the Richter scale battered the eastern city of Erzincan last Friday. They said 688 people had been injured. 'We cannot estimate how many people are still missing. We will have the final figure only when the debris is cleared,' Erzincan's provincial governor, Mr Recep Yazicioglu, said. Mr Rami Eren, the Turkish-born director of a Swiss disaster relief team. He said freezing temperatures in had reduced the possibility of people surviving for up to five days.
