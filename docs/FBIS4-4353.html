BFN [Text] Hanoi VNA March 30 -- Philippine President Fidel Ramos today left Ho Chi Minh City, concluding a 3-day official visit to Vietnam from March 28-30. On the afternoon of March 29, President Fidel Ramos left Hanoi for Ho Chi Minh City. In Ho Chi Minh City, the Philippine president and his party were received and given a banquet by the Mayor of Ho Chi Minh City Truong Tan Sang. During the meeting, Mayor Truong Tan Sang welcomed the Philippine president's visit to Vietnam which, he said, brought the neibhouring friendship of the Philippine people to the Vietnamese people including the Ho Chi Minh City people. President Fidel Ramos, for his part, expressed his honour to visit Vietnam and Ho Chi Minh City and highly appreciated the achievements recorded by the people of the city in the national renovation construction which he considered them favourable conditions for the mutual cooperation between the two countries. President Fidel Ramos hoped that the friendship and cooperation between the Vietnamese and Philippine peoples will be further developed. While staying in Ho Chi Minh City, the Philippine president attended the opening ceremony of the Philippine International Commerce Office and called on some historical relics in Ho Chi Minh City .
