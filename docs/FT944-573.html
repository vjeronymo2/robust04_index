941224 FT 24 DEC 94 / Arts: Answers to the FT Christmas Arts Quiz 1. Baroness Detta O'Cathain prematurely left her post in charge of the Barbican Centre, and Canova's Three Graces stayed in Britain. 2. Michael Dobbs, in his second series about the machiavellian Francis Urquhart. 3. Vikram Seth's A Suitable Boy won the WH Smith Award. 4. Christopher Bruce's ballet Land entered the Rambert repertory. 5. The relaunched Radio Five Live. 6. Bass-baritone Hans Hotter made his Prom debut on the opening night as the speaker in Schoenberg's Gurrelieder. 7. Deborah Warner's production of Don Giovanni. 8. The Gielgud. 9. Damien Hirst with his artistically preserved sheep. 10. An Absolute Turkey, Peter Hall's hit production of Feydeau's Le Dindon. 11. Peter Sellars' execrated production of The Merchant of Venice at the Barbican. 12. W. H. Auden, after he was quoted in Four Weddings and a Funeral. 13. The subject of the movie 32 Short Films about Glenn Gould. 14. Schindler's List, the film of Schindler's Ark. 15. Novelist Jeannette Winterson rushed round to a critic's house to confront them personally. 16. Munch's The Scream was stolen in Oslo (later recovered). 17. Alan Partridge (Steve Coogan), egregious host of Knowing Me Knowing You, moved from radio to TV. 18. Respectively to complain about critic Brian Sewell and to demand more new plays. 19. Kurt Cobain of the group Nirvana shot himself. 20. Faye Dunaway was sacked during the rehearsals of Sunset Boulevard. 21. Sir Harrison Birtwistle's Gawain was barracked by the self-styled 'Hecklers' at the Royal Opera House, Covent Garden, but supported by counter-demonstrations. 22. Dora Bryan, in Harold Pinter's The Birthday Party at the National Theatre. 23. The camera that filmed an orgasm from the inside in Desmond Morris' The Human Animal. 24. Alain Resnais' film Smoking, adapted from Alan Ayckbourn, failed to spark, disappointing the fans of both.
