BFN [Report by Nadim Kawash] [Text] Abu Dhabi, June 12 (AFP) -- The French Giat Industries is pressing ahead with a major offset weapons deal with the United Arab Emirates (UAE) and local officials said it will reap benefits for their oil-reliant economy. Officials at the Giat Offset in Abu Dhabi said the multi-billion- dollar tank deal signed with the UAE last year was proceeding without hurdles and several projects would be set up in the Gulf state. "There are no problems at all. We are going ahead with the deal and there will be several civilian projects here," one official said. He declined to elaborate but UAE offset officials said the projects covered farming, banking, industry and other sectors. They said they would be set up jointly with national investors. Giat won the tank deal after strong competition from US and British companies participating in an international defence show in Abu Dhabi in 1993. Under the deal, worth between three billion and four billion dollars, it will supply 436 Leclerc main battle tanks from the start of 1995. Experts said Giat was awarded the contract after it made a more attractive offset offer, including investments yielding nearly 60 percent of the deal's value. It was the UAE's biggest ever offset deal. Giat President Pierre Chiquet said after the deal was signed that projects in the UAE would generate more than two billion dollars in 10 years and their turnover in the long run could exceed the value of contract. Early this year, the semi official daily Emirates News said Giat had decided to restructure its offset programmes in the UAE, revising some projects and cancelling a contract with the French Offset Partners to supervise projects in the UAE because of contractual disagreements. "The deal is going smoothly and there is no doubt it will have a positive impact on the economy," a UAE offset official said. "It will help us in our efforts to obtain technology and diversify the economy." Giat has already launched a project under the offset deal, a gas- powered central air-conditioning fabrication facility. Quoting Giat sources, the UAE daily Khaleej Times said on Sunday the company would soon embark on a project to build a residential complex in Abu Dhabi to accommodate Giat offset staff and their families. A Giat spokesman refused to comment on the report, which said the UAE authorities had agreed to allot a plot of land for the project. "The project includes 80 villas to house 300 French families expected to come to the country from June next year," Khaleej Times said. "They will live in the UAE during the entire duration of the deal -- 10 years." It said some of them would be assigned to carry out military programmes, including training, maintenance and logistic follow up while the others will join the offset members already sent to oversee civilian projects. France is the UAE's top arms supplier, which has joined other wealthy Gulf Arab states in bolstering their armed forces following the 1990 Iraqi invasion of Kuwait. Officials said the UAE would likely sign military deals worth one billion dollars a year until 2000. "They all will be on an offset basis," one official said. "I think many companies will be interested because our offset programme is flexible." The UAE introduced offset programmes five years ago in a bid to attract technology needed for its drive to build an industrial base to lessen reliance on unstable oil earnings. The programme stipulates that arms suppliers re-invest in projects that should yield up to 60 percent of the deal's value.
