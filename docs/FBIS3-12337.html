Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Statement by Information Minister Michel Samahah, followed by a question and answer session, with unidentified correspondents in Beirut 27 February--live or recorded] [Excerpts] [Samahah] At the invitation of his excellency the prime minister, the Council of Ministers held an extraordinary meeting this evening. His excellency the president attended and chaired the meeting. The Council of Ministers devoted its extraordinary meeting to the attack against state security, which took the form of an explosion at the Sayyidat al-Najat Church in Zuq Mikha'il this morning. At the beginning of the meeting, his excellency the president briefed the ministers on his contacts with Syrian President Hafiz al-Asad, who denounced the attack and placed Syria's resources at the disposal of Lebanese authorities to discover the perpetrators. He also briefed the ministers on his contact with his majesty the king of Jordan for the same purpose. His excellency the president also briefed the Council of Ministers on all his contacts from within the country and abroad denouncing the attack. The Council of Ministers then examined the implications of the crime and its intention to weaken confidence in the security situation and the economic and social stability in Lebanon, fan sectarian fanaticism in the country, and divert the attention of world public opinion from the massacre committed by Israel in the Ibrahimi Mosque in Hebron in the occupied Palestinian territory. The Council of Ministers praised the overwhelming reaction through which the Lebanese expressed comprehensive national unity in the face of this attack that was directed against the country's security and unity. The Council of Ministers decided to refer the attack against the state security in the Sayyidat al-Najat Church in Zuq Mikha'il to the Judicial Council. Following the statement issued by his excellency the president, the speaker of the Chamber of Deputies, and the prime minister this morning calling for public mourning tomorrow, the Council of Ministers also decided to call for a general strike tomorrow.
