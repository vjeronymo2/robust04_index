920814 FT 14 AUG 92 / UN to place troops in Somalia The United Nations, moving swiftly to follow up a breakthrough agreement with Somalia's warlords, proposed last night to send in 500 Pakistani troops to ensure the safe delivery of food and other aid to millions of starving people in Somalia, Michael Littlejohns reports from the United Nations in New York. Mr Boutros Boutros Ghali, UN secretary-general, formally notified the Security Council about the plan and officials said its assent seemed routine. The accord with the Somali leaders calls for the deployment of UN soldiers within 21 days but Mr Kofi Annan, UN official in charge, said it was hoped the Pakistanis could arrive in the country within a fortnight. Deploying them, though, might take 'several weeks'.
