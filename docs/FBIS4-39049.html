BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Valeriy Shanayev] [Text] Vladikavkaz June 5 TASS -- In recent days, the situation has become relatively stable in the part of North Ossetia where state of emergency rule was extended for another two months, from May 31 to July 31. According to military sources, no conflicts on ethnic grounds broke out in the zone over the past twenty-four hours. Russian peacekeeping troops, in close coordination with local law-enforcement bodies, are overseeing the state of emergency rule. Officials of federal ministries and agencies continue working in the region, a spokesman for the North Ossetian president and government told TASS on Sunday. They examine the situation in the conflict zone and address issues of housing, communications, economic facilities in North Ossetia's Prigorodny District, the hotbed of the armed conflict. North Ossetian mass media gave on Sunday wide comments on the statement by the republic's President Akhsarbek Galazov, saying the North Ossetian leadership is ready to comply with the Russian president's decree on the return of refugees to places of their permanent residence. However, the problem of refugees should be solved in complex, on the basis of documents signed in Kislovodsk. The final word is to be said here by commissions of conciliation.
