Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Rocio Bernal, chief editor of Channel 7, has prepared an important report summarizing the main aspects of the meeting between Bolivian President Gonzalo Sanchez de Lozada and Argentine President Carlos Saul Menem in Buenos Aires: [Begin Bernal recording] I have important news from Argentina. An important energy agreement was signed by President Sanchez de Lozada and Argentine President Carlos Menem. The main aspects of the agreement are the sale of 6.1 million cubic meters of natural gas per year at an initial price of $1.21 per cubic meters until 1996. As of 1996, the price will be increased annually based on international prices. For the first time Bolivia will sell gasoline to Argentina. This will allow Bolivia to obtain an income of $4 million a year. Regarding light crude oils, the cubic meter will be sold to Argentina at $162.50 delivered at the Palmasola plant. The official Bolivian mission that went to Argentina headed by President Sanchez de Lozada also was able to obtain an investment of $5 million for the next two years as of the agreement's signing. An investment of $15 million for the next four years also was obtained. The contract signed with Argentine Government Oil Deposits is an important step for the country's benefit, and from the quality and quantity viewpoint it is better than the previous contract. President Sanchez de Lozada expressed by phone his complete satisfaction with the result of his visit to Argentina because the No. 1 issue, the sale of natural gas to Argentina, was excellent for Bolivia after difficult negotiations. Also, the presidents of Argentina and Bolivia reviewed international, regional, and bilateral issues within the framework of cordiality and friendship. They pointed out the excellent level of relations between the two governments, and verified the importance of the issues discussed during the recent visit of the Argentine foreign minister to Bolivia included in the La Paz Joint Declaration on 22 December 1993. With the purpose of pointing out the positive results of the current visit, the presidents signed a joint declaration that says they reaffirmed their commitment to contribute to the consolidation of democracy in the continent as the essential basis for keeping peace and developing social justice to protect human rights and implement economic development. The two presidents agreed that the invitation made by the four Mercosur [Common Market of the South] countries to Bolivia to have government representatives participate as observers in the meetings of subgroups will contribute to strengthening the mechanisms of appropriate relations for joining the Mercosur integration process. Regarding the Bolivian maritime claim, which is one of the main components of Bolivian foreign policy, the Bolivian president expressed his satisfaction over the possibilities now existing for sincere dialogue among all the parties involved. The two presidents also expressed their conviction that the 14th meeting of the Intergovernmental Committee of the Paraguay-Parana Waterway to be held in Formosa [Argentina] from 21 to 25 March will strengthen cooperation between the states on the waterway program and will be progress in studying the related issues that will facilitate regional integration. Within the framework of economic cooperation between the two governments, the Argentine Government gave Bolivia the equivalent of $84 million, of which $54 million is for local projects and will be disbursed from April to December. The remaining $30 million will be used to finance Argentine and Bolivian goods, installations, and equipment for a broad health program in the border regions of Argentina and Bolivia. To this effect, the two countries' health officials will prepare the adequate projects of basic infrastructure and health. Within 60 days, a trade agreement will be signed establishing free transit, without tariffs or other types of fees, on goods between the two countries. This is for the competitive fabrication in Bolivia of products for the Argentine and Bolivian markets, for exporting to third countries, and for offering incentives for the investment of Argentine and Bolivian capital to this effect. The two presidents expressed their satisfaction with the signing of the agreement on reciprocal promotion and protection of investments. Upon concluding his state visit, constitutional Bolivian President Gonzalo Sanchez de Lozada thanked Argentine President Carlos Saul Menem for his expressions of sympathy and friendship and for the warm hospitality of the Argentine Government and people. [end recording]
