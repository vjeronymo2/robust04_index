Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Veronika Romanenkova] [Text] Moscow January 10 TASS -- The Soyuz TM-18 spacecraft docked with the Mir orbital complex at 14.50, Moscow time, today, as was planned, ITAR-TASS learned at the press centre of the Russian military-space troops. The Soyuz TM-18 spacecraft brought to the complex the crew of the 15th main expedition, made up of Viktor Afanasyev (mission commander), Yuriy Usachev (flight engineer) and Valeriy Polyakov (cosmonaut researcher and physician). The docking was carried out under the guidance of Colonel General Vladimir Ivanov, commander of the military-space troops and chairman of the interstate commission for ensuring flights and exploitation of the Mir orbital complex. Two crews will work jointly at the Mir complex for three days. They will conduct a number of biotechnological experiments, whose results will be taken to the Earth by Vasiliy Tsibliyev and Aleksandr serebrov (the 14th main expedition). They are expected to return on board the Soyuz TM-17 spacecraft at 11.25, Moscow time, on January 14. Afanasyev, Usachev and Polyakov will spend 179 [as received] at the Mir orbital complex. They will conduct numerous medical, technical and astrophysical expediments and research. Polyakov, a physician, will remain at the orbital complex after his work-mates return to the Earth. His flight will last 429 days. He will work together with the crews of the 16th and 17th main expeditions (the crew of the latter will include a representative of the European Space Agency).
