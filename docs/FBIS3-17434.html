Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [From the "Vesti" newscast] [Text] At the sitting of the government's presidium today, Viktor Chernomyrdin said that the state administrative apparatus had unjustifiably swollen in recent years. He said that in 1991, during the existence of the Soviet Union, executives at all levels had numbered 715,000, while now there were 921,000 state employees in the Russian Federation. There were 350,000 bureaucrats in the district administrative team alone. Viktor Chernomyrdin criticized the Ministry of Finance which had complied with requests from the regions to finance the bloated adminstrative bodies.
