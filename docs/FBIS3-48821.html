Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Kuala Lumpur, Jan 31 (OANA-BERNAMA) -- Malaysia and Indonesia have agreed to study whatever fresh evidence is available in their bid to resolve the overlapping claims on the Sipadan and Ligitan Islands, Malaysian Foreign Ministry Secretary-General Ahmad Kamil Jaafar said Monday. He said both countries were agreed that the issue of overlapping claims on the Islands off Sabah and northeast Kalimantan could be settled through negotiations. Whatever new documents and ideas can be presented for study before the third round of talks in Jakarta in May, he told reporters after the presentation of Japanese Government cultural aid to Malaysia at the Foreign Ministry here. He was confident that the two sides could reach a concrete consensus at the talks although the issue was complicated. Malaysia and Indonesia had studied all the documents and maps that were presented at the second round of talks here last week, he said. He said both sides shared the view that all international legal aspects be resorted to if necessary to resolve the issue. Both parties were confident that the issue would not spark off something that is to be regretted in the future.
