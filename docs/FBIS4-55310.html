CSO [Article by the press spokesman of the Ministry of Industry, Mining, and Energy Sources: "The Chrome Industry Has Prospects for Development in the Market Economy"] [Text] Representatives of the Independent Trade Union of Miners in Albania, led by Asllan Myshku; the trade union of the chrome corporation, led by Sabah Pata; the "Albkrom" corporation; and the Ministry of Labor, Emigration, and Social Assistance, along with the deputy minister of industry, mining, and energy resources, Albert Brokaj, have held a meeting in Bulqize, in the largest mining center of the chrome extraction and processing industry. During this meeting representatives of these trade unions, which belong to the Union of Independent Trade Unions of Albania, led by President Valer Xheka, brought up some important issues related to the collective labor contract, which they said was signed in a unilateral manner. They also raised issues related to production, the opening up of new jobs, the financing of new projects dealing with the development and future of the chrome extraction and processing industry, the urgent need to update technology, the creation of a joint enterprise, and possibilities for privatization. They wanted to know why workers in the corporation have not received 20-percent wage increases in accordance with the government decision. They were concerned about the sale of chrome, chrome concentrate, and ferrochrome, about the consequences of the embargo [against Yugoslavia] in the chrome extraction and processing industry and about the satisfaction, as soon as possible, of demands for housing and for the improvement of the miners' living and working conditions. The participants in the meeting raised a number of other issues regarding the organization and management of work in the corporation and stressed the need for a bitter struggle against possible signs of corruption and for the further improvement of social assistance. In their response, Deputy Minister Brokaj and the representatives of "Albkrom" and the Ministry of Labor, Emigration, and Social Assistance said that the government has drawn up a strategy for the chrome industry, which is a valuable national asset, and, therefore, the government has demonstrated and will continue to demonstrate concern for its continued development. It was pointed out that this corporation has had a higher hard currency income than any other corporation or production unit in the country and that the greater part of this income is used for the needs of the corporation. There are good possibilities for increasing this income. It was stressed that the ministry must seek compensation from the United Nations for the serious consequences which this industry has suffered in the northeastern part of the country because of the embargo. At the end of the meeting the trade unions strongly stressed that a resolution containing the following demands should be issued: -- the collective contracts should be re-signed, with the participation of all the trade unions, especially the trade union of the corporation, which represents about 46 percent of the employees; -- the financial situation of the corporation should be improved by forgiving the taxes for 1992 and 1993; -- workers should receive a 26-percent wage increase; -- negotiations for the creation of a joint enterprise should be accelerated; -- additional funds should be sought from the government for the construction of the Klosi traverse bank, the ramp, and shaft No. 7, which are linked to the future of the mining industry; -- the price of energy for the chrome corporation, the largest consumer of energy, should be reduced; -- the headquarters of the corporation should be transferred to Bulqize and the retirement age for certain categories of workers should be lowered. The demands in this resolution will be presented to the government. It was decided that the collective contract would be re-signed on 15 April 1994 at the corporation's headquarters in Tirana.
