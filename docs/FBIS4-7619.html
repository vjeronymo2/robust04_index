BFN [Excerpts] Knin, 4 Apr (TANJUG) -- A cease-fire agreement between the Croatian Army and the Serb Krajina army covering a 1,600-kilometer-long front came into effect at 0900 today. [passage omitted] ISKRA reports, quoting a statement by Lieutenant Colonel Marinko Gajic, the assistant commander of the Serb Krajina army, that Serb Krajina army units have started withdrawing their heavy weapons and forces, and that on the Krajina side "there will be no problems in fully complying with the provisions of the Zagreb agreement." The same source said that UNPROFOR [UN Protection Forces] representatives reported that the Croatian side is also withdrawing its manpower and equipment.
