Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [By unidentified ITAR-TASS correspondent] [Text] Moscow, 12 Feb -- Vladimir Kazimirov, roving ambassador of the Russian Foreign Ministry, and Morteza Bank, director general in the Iranian Foreign Ministry expressed deep concern over the sharp escalation in military activities in Nagorno-Karabakh. Russian-Iranian talks on settling the Karabakh conflict have taken place in Moscow. The sides expressed particular disquiet at the growing scale of combat actions, their worsening severity and the increasing bloodshed, which is intensifying the inhuman nature of the conflict. As ITAR-TASS was told at the Department of Information and the Russian Foreign Ministry press center, both sides noted that the increasing internationalization of the conflict represents a great danger to the cause of peace and regional stability. It carries the threat of a major conflict with unpredictable consequences for the region and for the whole world. During the consultations it was acknowledged that only by relying on peaceful political means and on the capability of the sides to reach a sensible reciprocal compromise will it be possible realistically to lay down a firm and long-term basis for a settlement. The Russian side stressed that the main priority is to reach an immediate cease-fire, the cessation of all combat actions, and the conclusion of an agreement on this. The sides confirmed their conviction that it was possible and essential to reach a bloodless political solution to all the problems of the conflict exclusively by peaceful means, including the liberation of territories, the restoration of transport and power links, and the return of refugees. The participants in the consultations noted that Russia and Iran support the efforts of the world community to settle the conflict. Russia and Iran are in direct proximity to and have interests in achieving a settlement of this conflict and they have frequently put forward initiatives toward reaching a peaceful resolution and they are continuing their efforts and maintaining constant contact with each other.
