Language: Persian Article Type:CSO [Text] Tehran, 22 Azar [15 Dec] (KEYHAN-E HAVA'I News Service) -- A new Iranian school has opened in Damascus, admitting Iranian, Afghan, Pakistani, and Arab students. This school was opened on Woman's Day in Damascus near the grave of Her Holiness Zaynab (peace be upon her) on the birth anniversary of Her Holiness Fatimeh Zahra (peace be upon her) through the efforts of officials of the Islamic Republic of Iran's Ministry of Education and Training and the office supervising Iranian schools outside the country in Syria and Lebanon. At the opening ceremonies for this school, the supervisor of Iranian schools outside the country said: The establishment of these schools is an important step in the spread of the real Islamic culture, and participation in these schools by friends of the Islamic Republic of Iran increases every day. Studying in the new school are 130 Iranian, Afghan and Pakistani students and students from several Arab countries in the region.
