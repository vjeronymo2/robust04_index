Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Interview with Air Force Marshal Yevgeniy Shaposhnikov, former commander in chief of the CIS Armed Forces, candidate of Russian Democratic Reform Movement (RDRM) in the 12 December elections: "Second RDRM Congress -- Realignment of Forces?"; place and date not given] [Text] The second congress of the Russian Democratic Reform Movement will open in Moscow (at the Sociopolitical Center on Tsvetnoy Boulevard) on Saturday. In order to find out its aims and objectives our correspondent turned to a long-time RDRM supporter, Air Force Marshal Yevgeniy Shaposhnikov, former commander in chief of the CIS Armed Forces, a political figure who ran on the movement's ticket in the 12 December elections. [KURANTY] Yevgeniy Ivanovich, what are the main aims of the RDRM's second congress? [Shaposhnikov] First: We need to sum up the results of our work in the period between the congresses and during the election campaign. To make an analysis. Second: to determine our program for the immediate future. The program should be specific. Metaphorically speaking, our aims and objectives should be more "down-to-earth." [KURANTY] Can organizational changes be expected? [Shaposhnikov] I am not a leader of the movement. To some extent I would like some changes to take place, but without the movement's evolving into a party. I categorically oppose a strict hierarchy or party centralism. [KURANTY] What are the main methods the RDRM employs to raise its effectiveness? [Shaposhnikov] We have a fairly large number of sympathizers. At present, to pursue our political line we need to strengthen our vertical structures somewhat.
