BFN [Text] Paris, April 14 (AFP) -- Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev on Thursday [14 April] welcomed a French proposal for relaunching peace negotiations on former Yugoslavia, the French Foreign Ministry said. Spokesman Richard Duque said French Foreign Minister Alain Juppe spoke by telephone with Kozyrev on Thursday to discuss the situation in the besieged Moslem enclave of Gorazde in Bosnia. Kozyrev was "very positive" on a French proposal for a "partnership" of the European Union, Russia and the United States to restart the Bosnia peace conference, currently chaired by the EU [European Union] and the United Nations, Duque said. Juppe also discussed a French draft resolution at the UN Security Council on Gorazde, demanding a ceasefire and deployment of UN peacekeepers in the enclave, the spokesman said.
