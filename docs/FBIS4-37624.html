BFN [Text] The military court in Nabulus has handed down 10 life sentences to 'Abd-al-Rahim Hasan Shadid, a 24-year-old Fatah member from a village near Nabulus. He was convicted of eight counts of murder of Palestinians who were said to have cooperated with Israel and of wounding four others, as well as three counts of attempting to attack Israeli soldiers. Rifles, a machine gun, pistols, and grenades were found in his home. Shadid confessed and said he had no regrets about carrying out the violence. Meanwhile, Fatah members serving life prison terms have been freed recently in the framework of the Cairo accord. One of them was sentenced to nine life terms for the murder of at least nine Palestinians on the grounds they cooperated with Israel. [Tel Aviv YEDI'OT AHARONOT in Hebrew on 23 June on page 16 carries a report by David Regev adding: "Thirty-eight Palestinian terrorists who were released in the last few months as part of the agreement with the PLO and who resumed their terror activities have once again been imprisoned by the security forces. ["Sources said the security forces are furious about the releases and their inability to keep track of the discharged prisoners, many of whom resume their terror activities."]
