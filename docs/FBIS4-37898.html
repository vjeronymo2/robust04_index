BFN [Text] The Sri Lankan President, Mr. D.B. Wijetunga, has dissolved the country's parliament and called for general elections on the 16th of August -- six months ahead of schedule. A presidential proclamation in Colombo said the parliament stands dissolved from midnight tonight and the election process will be set in motion from next week. The presidential election will follow some time in October-November. Our Colombo correspondent reports that the present parliament elected in 1989 was scheduled to continue till February next year. The main opposition, Sri Lanka Freedom Party, has described the president's decision to advance parliamentary elections as political hari-kari, but said it was fully prepared to face the electorate.
