Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Accra, 10 Jan (PL) -- Captain Kojo Tsikata, member of Ghana's Council of State, today voiced his concern over the situation in Angola and the future of the ruling party, the Popular Movement for the Liberation of Angola (MPLA). During an interview with Cuban Foreign Minister Roberto Robaina, the old politician charged that Western governments -- without mentioning names -- are exerting pressure to accommodate in power Jonas Savimbi, the leader of the armed UNITA [National Union for the Total Independence of Angola]. Tsikata criticized the support some governments are still giving the Angolan armed rebel group that was defeated in the September 1992 first-ever multiparty elections considered valid and just by the United Nations. In another part of his conversation with the Cuban foreign minister -- who arrived yesterday in this city for an official two- day visit -- Tsikata regretted the tensions prevailing in Liberia, where Ghana has sent troops to contribute in the search for a political solution to the crisis in this West African state. Likewise, he encouraged the Nigerian leaders to prevent an outbreak of violence in that country.
