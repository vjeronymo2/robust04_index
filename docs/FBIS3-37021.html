Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Gennadiy Temnenkov] [Text] Bonn February 10 TASS -- The finance ministers and chiefs of issuing banks of the Group-of-Seven highly industrialized countries are due to gather in a regular meeting in the town of Kronberg, not far from Frankfurt am Main, on February 26, the German Finance Ministry said in a press release on Thursday. Participants in the meeting are expected to discuss urgent problems of the world economic development, as well as the current economic situation in Russia, prospects for continuation of reforms in this country and possibilities of further assistance to it from the G-7 countries and international financial organizations. With this in view, senior officials of the International Monetary Fund and the World Bank have been invited to the kronberg meeting. The G-7 finance ministers and chiefs of issuing banks will also hold talks with the Russian delegation. Invited to the talks are Russian Economics Minister Aleksandr Shokhin and acting finance minister Sergey Dubinin.
