930510 FT 10 MAY 93 / Ireland cuts interest rates The Central Bank of Ireland is cutting two key interest rates, from today, AP reports from Dublin. The central bank said over the weekend that it was it was reducing to 8.50 per cent from 8.75 per cent the short-term rate it charges on loans to commercial banks. It will also cut its overnight deposit rate to 5.25 per cent from 5.50 per cent. That rate is applied to surplus money-market funds that the central bank lends out overnight to commercial banks.
