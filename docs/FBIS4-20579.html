Boron Oxide and Boron Nitride 43070061J Tokyo ISBB'93 in English Aug 93 p 49--FOR OFFICIAL USE ONLY 43070061J Tokyo ISBB'93 English CSO [English abstract of article by Yasuji Muramatsu, Tom Scimeca, and Masaharu Oshima, NTT Interdisciplinary Research Laboratories; Jun Kawai, Institute of Physical and Chemical Research (RIKEN); and Hiroo Kato, Photon Factory, National Laboratory for High Energy Physics] [Text] Recently, several X-ray emission threshold experiments have been attempted to elucidate satellite structures in X-ray emission spectra using monochromatized synchrotron radiation.[.sup]1,2[/] The satellite structure in BK_a_ emission spectra of boron oxide and boron nitride is particularly interesting because the origin of the high- energy satellites is not predicted from DV-X_a_ molecular orbital calculations.[.sup]3[/] The aim of this study is to investigate the satellite structure in the BK_a_ emission spectra of the boron compounds from threshold experiments using highly brilliant undulator radiation as an excitation probe. Spectroscopic measurements were carried out on a fluorescent X-ray spectrometer of sophisticated design[.sup]4,5[/] installed on an undulator beamline (BL-16U)[.sup]6,7[/] at the Photon Factory. A prominent increase in the high-energy satellite peak heights was first observed when the excitation energy was tuned to the narrow pre-edge peak in the B1s absorption spectrum corresponding to the transition from B(1s) to an unoccupied anti-bonding orbital (p*). It is therefore confirmed that the high-energy satellites originate from resonant reemission due to a B(1s)-p* -B(1s[.sup]-l[/]) transition. This resonant reemission phenomenon reflects the electric structure of the localized anti-bonding p* orbital in trigonal-planar-structured boron compounds. References 1. R.D. Deslattes, R.E.
