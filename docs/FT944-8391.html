941119 FT 19 NOV 94 / Weekend Money: Finance Week BUSINESS Ireland was pitched into political crisis after the prime minister, Albert Reynolds, and his Fianna Fail cabinet colleagues resigned. Camelot, the operator of the UK's first national lottery for more than 150 years, said it was 'absolutely delighted' with early sales of tickets. Ready-made meals comprised almost 35 per cent of the average household's food bill last year, a rise of nearly 10 per cent since the early 1980s, according to the 1993 National Food Survey. The Post Office is close to a deal with a leading insurer that would enable it to sell travel insurance through many of its 20,000 branches. Better than expected figures for UK inflation, unemployment and the public finances provided chancellor Kenneth Clarke with a strong base for a confidence-building Budget. Independent gas companies hoping to enter the lucrative market for supplying Britain's 18m households were relieved to learn from the Queen's speech that legislation for removing British Gas's monopoly will be introduced soon. About 7m people who have mortgages with building societies would be given new rights to vote on issues affecting societies if proposals put forward by the sector's trade body are accepted by the government. MARKETS In one of the most frenetic days ever on the London Metal Exchange, prices soared on Wednesday as wave after wave of investment fund and speculative buying took copper, aluminium and lead to fresh four-year peaks and nickel and zinc to their highest levels for two years. Many large pension funds are close to including commodities in their investment portfolios as a way of diversifying away from traditional financial holdings, according to Steven Strongin, vice-president and commodities strategist at Goldman Sachs, the US investment bank. The US Federal Reserve surprised the financial markets with an increase of three-quarters of a percentage point in short-term interest rates intended to keep inflation under control. Plans to launch a pan-European stock market for fast-growing companies were unveiled by a group of European venture capitalists, the Paris Bourse and the US Nasdaq market. Lazard Investors is to launch an Indian equity fund next week in conjunction with BK Birla, the Indian business group, and the Bombay-based merchant bank Creditcapital Finance Corporation, in which Lazard Investors holds 40 per cent. Rising interest rates are encouraging more gold mining companies to hedge their future production, and this appears to be putting a 'cap' on any price rise above Dollars 400 a troy ounce, according to the World Gold Council. COMPANIES British Aerospace has increased its bid for VSEL. The latest offer is a 3.3 for one share swap, worth around Pounds 14.70 for each VSEL share, with a Pounds 14 cash alternative matching GEC's Pounds 14 cash offer. Shares in cash-rich De La Rue, the bank-note printer, hit an all-time high as the market responded to a sparkling set of interim results. Land Securities, the UK's largest quoted property group, tumbled 17p to 606p on Wednesday on turnover of 7.5m shares - the busiest session for 18 months -after a warning about the second half. The third-quarter figures season for the insurance composites ended with a whimper of disappointment as General Accident's results included shock paper losses from its market investments, particularly in the US. Sam Chisholm, the chief executive of British Sky Broadcasting, could receive up to Pounds 3m this year in salary and bonuses, the pathfinder prospectus for the satellite television venture revealed. British Sky Broadcasting believes it lost around 50,000 existing and potential new customers through pirate devices. GWR, the expanding Bristol-based commercial radio group, has had its radio licences acquisition plan blocked by the Radio Authority, even though a similar scheme involving Emap and Trans World Communications was accepted. Attwoods described the increased bid of Pounds 391m from Browning-Ferris Industries of the US as meagre and cynical, and urged shareholders to reject the offer in favour of the proposed break-up plan. British Steel, Europe's second largest steel-maker, is raising its interim dividend from 0.5p a share to 2p after a surge in half-year pre-tax profits from Pounds 27m to Pounds 159m. Automotive Financial Group Holdings, one of the biggest UK motor retailer groups and controlled by Octav Botnar, has been sold to the Barclay brothers, the reclusive UK entrepreneurs, in a deal understood to be worth about Pounds 200m. Shares in Towry Law, the independent financial advice group, dropped 44p to 86p on Wednesday after the group, which floated last November, warned that it expected losses of Pounds 1.25m at the half year, compared with pre-tax profits of Pounds 565,000. RJB Mining expects to have cleared all debts incurred in its bid for the English regions of British Coal by the end of 1998, and is looking for pre-tax profit margins of about 18 per cent in 1999. Acquisitions following its 1993 rights issue helped Great Portland Estates, the UK's sixth largest property company, announce interim pre-tax profits ahead from Pounds 16m to Pounds 21.4m. Boots is to end 80 years of prescription medicine manufacture with the sale of its drugs business to BASF of Germany for 'about Pounds 850m'. Boots later spent Pounds 508m on buying back its shares on the stock market, which will leave Boots' management free to concentrate on acquisitions in over-the-counter (non-prescription) drugs, probably in Germany. Barclays Bank also reduced excess capital by announcing that it would buy back Dollars 500m (Pounds 300m) of non-cumulative preference shares. BrightReasons, the owner of Pizzaland, has become the latest new-issues casualty. The company announced that its share offer would be 'delayed'. PowerGen is considering joint ventures with other European companies as a means of honouring a commitment to try to dispose of 2,000MW of plant. Gartmore is to launch an investment trust to track a new index covering the smallest 1 per cent of UK equities by market capitalisation. Foreign & Colonial's offer of C-shares in its Emerging Markets investment trust was almost fully subscribed, raising Pounds 113.7m, to add to its existing Pounds 150m of funds. Fidelity's launch of the new Fidelity Special Values investment trust was less successful, pulling in Pounds 45.4m against early hopes of up to Pounds 125m. Most of the money came from private investors through financial advisers. Microsoft announced plans for a world-wide, on-line information service for personal computer users. Carriage House Motors, Manhattan's Rolls-Royce dealership, closes its doors on East 61st Street next month.
