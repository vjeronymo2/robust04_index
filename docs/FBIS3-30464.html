Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Bucharest, ROMPRES 3/2/1994 -- A North-Atlantic delegation, headed by NATO undersecretary for political affairs, Gebhardt von Moltke, arrived in Bucharest on February 3 for consultations with the Romanian party on the next stages of the Partnership for Peace Romania has to traverse. Gebhardt von Moltke told the press upon arrival at the Otopeni Airport that Romania was "very active" in the close work together with the NATO Cooperation Council. Heads of state and government of the NATO countries have decided to take an important step ahead in the military cooperation, he said, stressing that the Partnership for Peace "gives Romania the possibility to strengthen this cooperation." He said he came here to get more information and get this process started, being very interested in the talks that would follow. He added Romania was a partner of the North-Atlantic Alliance and the Partnership for Peace gave it the possibility to structure this cooperation to its benefit.
