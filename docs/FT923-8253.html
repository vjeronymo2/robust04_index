920812 FT 12 AUG 92 / Georgian interior minister kidnapped MILITANT supporters of ousted President Zviad Gamsakhurdia took Georgia's interior minister hostage yesterday along with a group that included a top adviser to Mr Eduard Shevardnadze, AP reports from Tbilisi. Mr Shevardnadze, the former Soviet foreign minister who now heads the ruling state council, told of the kidnapping in the western city of Zugdidi on Georgian television. The area has been a strong base of support for Mr Gamsakhurdia, ousted from power in January. Mr Shevardnadze warned he was prepared to take 'extreme measures,' including the use of force, to win the release of Interior Minister Roman Gventsadze and the other hostages, including his adviser on national security.
