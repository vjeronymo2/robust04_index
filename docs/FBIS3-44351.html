Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Colombo, December 30 (XINHUA) -- China's Guangdong International Economic and Technological Cooperation Company today took over the management of the Palwat Sugar Factory, one of the biggest sugar producers in Sri Lanka. According to the contract signed by the two sides today in Colombo, the management of the sugar factory with an annual production of 4,600 tons will be undertaken by 16 Chinese managers and experts. The signing ceremony was attended by Sri Lankan Minister of Plantations Rupa Karunatilleke, Agricultural Minister R.M. Dharmadasa Banda and Chinese Ambassador Zhang Chengli. The factory, set up in the southeastern Uwa Province in 1986, had been managed by a British company which has come under attack by local people and press reports for environmental reasons before the Chinese take-over, the first of its kind in economic cooperation between Sri Lanka and China.
