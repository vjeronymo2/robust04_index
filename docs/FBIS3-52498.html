Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Heavy fighting resumed in Kabul today after a brief lull because of snowfall. Reports say government forces are besieging General Dostam's stronghold of Microryan and have inflicted heavy casualties. Fighting was concentrated near an important Dostam base. Clashes have also been reported from a nearby sports stadium. Witnesses said the fighting resumed around noon when the fog lifted. In the eastern district of Kabul, Golboddin forces allied with Dostam troops are reported to be advancing on Qila Zaman about 3 km from the capital Kabul. The fighting has left several hundred dead or wounded and thousands homeless. The International Committee of the Red Cross in Kabul said essential supplies have become scarce. Foreign Minister Sardar Asif Ahmad Ali's reaction on the Afghanistan situation. [Begin Asif Ali recording] We are all actually very very disturbed at what is happening there, because precious Muslim blood is being shed. That is what becomes even more painful to us. I was about to leave for Afghanistan today, but for this fighting that has erupted there. I am still prepared to go and I have conveyed to Afghan Government and brother Afghan leaders that if they want us and even under these conditions, I am prepared to go to Afghanistan and make an offer of our good offices so that the fighting can end and the bloodshed can end. Because, the people of Afghanistan, they deserve to get peace in that country now. [sentence as heard] [end recording]
