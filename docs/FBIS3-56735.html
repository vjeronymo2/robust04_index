Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Text] INTERFAX has reported the end of the meeting between the Russian president and the head of the government, at which the question of the make-up of the new cabinet of ministers was discussed in particular. The meeting lasted six and a half hours, but no decision on the personnel composition of the government was taken. From the Kremlin, Boris Yeltsin and Viktor Chernomyrdin went off to their dachas. Deputy Prime Minister Aleksandr Shokhin, INTERFAX reports, told journalists today that Viktor Chernomyrdin had offered him a choice of two posts in the new government -- minister for CIS affairs or economics minister. Shokhin is going to announce his decision tomorrow. Today too, Sergey Shakhray, another deputy prime minister, stated, according to the POSTFAKTUM agency, that he had been offered the post of minister of nationality affairs and regional policy. It is not yet known whether he has accepted the offer. Meanwhile Boris Fedorov's press secretary denied a report that he has resigned. In a RIA interview, the press secretary said, literally, the following: Boris Grigoryevich has not accepted the prime minister's proposal that he should retain just the post of finance minister, but neither has he resigned.
