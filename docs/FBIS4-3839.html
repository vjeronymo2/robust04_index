BFN [Text] London, April 22 (XINHUA) -- NATO today threatened the use of air power against the Bosnian Serb Forces around the Muslim town of Gorazde unless they pull back three kilometers from the center of the town by 00:01 GMT on Sunday [24 April], according to the BBC. The ultimatum, issued after a meeting of NATO ambassadors at the NATO headquarters in Brussels, also asked Serbs to stop shelling Gorazde immediately and give U.N. personnel access to the town, one of the six U.N.-designated "safe areas" for Muslims in Bosnia. NATO said that if all conditions were not met, it would consider air strikes on Serb positions within a 20-kilometer radius of Gorazde.
