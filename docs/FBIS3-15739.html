Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] A second train, carrying 60 nuclear warheads of strategic rockets stationed in Ukraine, is expected to leave the Khmelnitskiy railroad station for Russia at 20:20 Moscow time Wednesday. An informed source in the Russian Defense Ministry told Interfax that the warheads would be sent to a factory in the Sverdlovsk Region for dismantling. The first trainload of 60 warheads had left the city of Pervomaisk for Russia on March 4. A total of nearly 2000 nuclear warheads remain in Ukraine in the hands of the 46th Rocketry Division stationed in Pervomayisk and the 43rd Rocketry Army stationed in Khmelnitskiy. Meanwhile, officials in the Russian Nuclear Energy Ministry told Interfax that 120 heating elements had been sent by one of the Elektrostal, Moscow Region, factory to the Chernobyl nuclear power station at the expense of the Russian government.
