941213 FT 13 DEC 94 / World Commodities Prices: Tea The good general demand was sustained, reports the Tea Brokers' Association. Coloury Assams met strong competition and prices ruled fully firm to often dearer. Plainer North Indians also sold well at generally higher levels. The few brightest East Africans on offer tended 2 to 5 pence easier although pd's were a good feature and prices advanced. Coloury mediums remained unchanged but lesser mediums lost 2 to 4 pence. Ceylons were about steady. Offshore: fairly good demand at around last levels. Quotations: best available 160p/kg, good 135p/kg, good medium 128p/kg, medium 108p/kg, low medium 80p/kg. The highest price realised this week was 161p/kg for a Kenya pd.
