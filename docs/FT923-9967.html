920801 FT 01 AUG 92 / Arthur Andersen sued in S&L case THE US federal government yesterday filed a Dollars 400m (Pounds 209.4m) civil lawsuit against accountants Arthur Andersen, accusing the Chicago-based firm of accounting malpractice, negligence and breach of contract in connection with audits it performed in the 1980s of a failed Texas savings and loan. The suit, filed in an Illinois court, claims Arthur Andersen was negligent during audits between 1984 and 1987 of the failed Benjamin Franklin Savings Association of Houston. The S&L failed in 1989, costing US taxpayers Dollars 900m. The suit was filed by the Resolution Trust Corporation (RTC), the federal agency in charge of dealing with failed S&Ls. In Chicago, Arthur Andersen denied any wrongdoing and said it would 'vigorously defend this lawsuit and is confident that our work was performed in accordance with professional standards.'
