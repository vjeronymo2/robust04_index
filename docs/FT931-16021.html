930109 FT 09 JAN 93 / Chairman of health region resigns SIR JAMES ACKERS has resigned as chairman of West Midlands Regional Health Authority, which has been the subject of criticism for inadequate financial controls. A National Audit Office report in October found that a consultancy exercise intended to generate Pounds 50m of savings had cost the authority some Pounds 4m. After the report was compiled Sir Roy Griffiths, deputy chairman of the National Health Service policy board, was sent to the West Midlands authority to strengthen management controls. Sir James, 57, had been chairman of the authority - England's biggest - since 1982 and received Pounds 20,925 a year for the part-time role. He said in his resignation letter to Mrs Virginia Bottomley, health secretary: 'Now that the task of rectifying matters is almost complete, I am quite certain that it is in the interests of the regional health authority to have a clean sheet and a fresh start.'
