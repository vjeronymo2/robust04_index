920916 FT 16 SEP 92 / B&Q criticised on price claim B&Q, the DIY chain, has been reprimanded by the Advertising Standards Authority about price claims after complaints from a village shop which said its prices were lower. B&Q's spring 1992 catalogue said it used its buying power to make bulk purchases and pass savings on to customers with lower prices. The ASA ruled that: 'While a price promise was a useful protection to customers, it did not necessarily mean that the claim to offer lower prices through bulk-buying power was accurate.'
