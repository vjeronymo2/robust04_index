931112 FT 12 NOV 93 / UK Company News: Lloyd's trusts approach limit of capacity TWO MORE Lloyd's investment trusts were launched yesterday amid further indications that the limit of institutional investor interest in the new Lloyd's schemes is being reached. Angerstein Underwriting Trust, an investment trust sponsored by NatWest Markets and Stace Barr, the Lloyd's agency, announced that it aimed to raise Pounds 75m through a placement and offer for subscription, while a smaller vehicle, Corporate Membership, also issued its prospectus. Corporate Membership, formed by individual members of Lloyd's and sponsored by Peel, Hunt, the stockbrokers, is seeking to raise Pounds 20m through an offer for subscription. However, SG Warburg and Chartwell, which planned to raise between Pounds 100m and Pounds 150m for New London Capital, are understood to be planning to scale back their fund to between Pounds 75m and Pounds 85m. At Angerstein, Mr Charles Phillips of NatWest Markets said Pounds 67.5m had been placed with institutions, although it was aiming to raise Pounds 23m from retail investors. Angerstein will provide Pounds 111m of capacity on syndicates recommended by Stace Barr, its independent Lloyd's adviser. Corporate Membership said it had secured in excess of Pounds 16m of secured capacity from syndicates and had identified a further Pounds 15m of syndicate capacity.
