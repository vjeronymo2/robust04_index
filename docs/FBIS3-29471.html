Language: Cambodian Article Type:BFN ["News Flash"] [Text] According to the Khmer Royal Armed Forces General Staff, a passenger train traveling from Phnom Penh to Pursat and Battambang Provinces was blown up at 1145 on 15 February by mines planted by the Khmer Rouge at kilometer marker 103, in an area between Krang Sna and Kdol stations in Toek Phos District, Kompong Chhnang Province. Four cars were derailed. The train convoy supervisor's left leg was severed, while three passengers were killed and eight others seriously wounded. The wounded were sent to the Pursat provincial hospital. At 1300 on the same day, a locomotive was blown up by a Khmer Rouge mine at the Sereisaophoan railway station in Banteay Meanchey Province. Two train drivers and five passengers were seriously wounded in the explosion.
