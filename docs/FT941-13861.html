940122 FT 22 JAN 94 / Finance and the Family: Fixed rate mortgages Lenders have been issuing new fixed rate mortgages. Abbey National's range includes 7.15 per cent (7.5 APR) fixed until the end of April 1999, 6.75 per cent (7.1 APR) fixed until April 30 1997 and a fixed rate of 6.39 per cent (6.7 APR) until April 30 1996. The booking fee is Pounds 300 for the five year fix and Pounds 250 for the others. There are early redemption penalties of between 60-150 days loss of interest. First-time borrowers are being offered 6.25 per cent (6.5 APR) until the end of April 1996 and 6.59 per cent (6.8 APR) to April 30 1997. The booking fee for both mortgages is Pounds 90 and early redemption penalties are 60-90 days loss of interest. The mortgages are not linked to compulsory insurance-related products. Nationwide building society has released a new two-year fix of 5.85 per cent (7.7 APR) for first time buyers for a Pounds 95 fee. The rate for other borrowers is 5.99 (7.8 APR) and the fee is Pounds 200. Borrowers will get a free valuation and Pounds 250 'cashback'. The early redemption penalty is 3 months. There is no compulsion to take out insurance-related products. TSB is offering a 10-year fix at 7.89 per cent (8.4 APR) for an arrangement fee of Pounds 250 but borrowers have to take out the bank's buildings and home contents insurance. All the mortgages are portable.
