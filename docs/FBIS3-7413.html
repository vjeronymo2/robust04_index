Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Vientiane, March 28 (KPL) -- Deputy Prime Minister Khamphoui Keoboualapha on March 26 received a visiting delegation of the National League of Families of the U.S.A. The delegation was headed by Ms. Ann Mills Griffiths, executive director of the league. At the meeting, the head of the delegation informed Lao Deputy Prime Minister Khamphoui Keoboualapha, who is also president of the Committee for Planning and Cooperation, about the outcome of its current visit to Laos. Khamphoui Keoboulapha talked with the delegation about several important issues, especially the state of social and economic development in Laos during the renovation process. He reaffirmed the consistent policy of the Lao PDR [Lao People's Democratic Republic] government concerning its relations with all countries in the world, with emphasis on the United States of America. He said the Lao Government always desires to promote the good relations of friendship with the U.S.A., reflecting in the issue of MIAs. The deputy prime minister also pointed out that the Lao Government and people conduct the humanitarian policy providing good cooperation to the U.S. side (?in searching) for the remains of Americans missing in action during the war time. On the same day evening, the American delegation also visited Foreign Minister Somsavat Lengsavat, who also discussed with the delegation about the MIAs issue. The Lao foreign minister confirmed the good-will and sincerity of the Lao Government in implementing its humanitarian policy, especially its good cooperation with the U.S. side in searching for MIAs.
