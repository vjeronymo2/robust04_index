BFN [Text] Tokyo, April 19 KYODO -- The Export-Import [ExIm] Bank of Japan announced Tuesday [19 April] the signing of agreements to establish untied credit lines equivalent to up to 100 million dollars to the Baltic countries to help support infrastructure projects. The loans consist of 30 million dollars each to Estonia and Latvia and 40 million dollars to Lithuania, the governmental bank said. The bank said the loans will be provided in collaboration with the European Bank for Reconstruction and Development (EBRD) under a government loan program for developing countries mapped out last June. The bank extended a total of 100 million dollars to the Baltic states last year in co-financing with the World Bank.
