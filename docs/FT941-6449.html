940302 FT 02 MAR 94 / Australian budget priorities outlined Australia's budget priorities in May would be 'to improve the prospects for the long-term unemployed, to promote industry and regional development and to implement the Native Title legislation', Mr Ralph Willis, Australian treasurer, said yesterday. These measures, coupled with the government's commitment to cut the budget deficit to about 1 per cent of gross domestic product by 1996-97, meant 'little scope for further substantial initiatives this year'. His comments came as he unveiled the government's initial budget platform, contained in a 'fiscal framework' document. Publication is the starting point for talks on budget details with the Labor party caucus and parties holding the balance of power in the Senate. The government wants to avoid a repeat of last year's fiasco when its finance package became stalled in parliament. A defined period for negotiations has been set this year, and the budget date moved forward to May. The fiscal framework provided few new projections about the economy. But it did play down the likelihood of a 'jobs levy' to fund the unemployment measures. 'A jobs levy. . . has been suggested as a means of accommodating increased expenditure pressure without prejudicing the deficit reduction programme,' it said. 'The government ..would prefer to meet its aims without having to introduce such a measure.'
