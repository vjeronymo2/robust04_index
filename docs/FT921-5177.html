920305 FT 05 MAR 92 / Appointments: Non-executive directors HILLSDOWN HOLDINGS continues to acknowledge the wishes of institutional shareholders with the appointment of another outside director. Sir Roy Griffiths, who retired as non-executive deputy chairman of J Sainsbury last year, joins the Hillsdown board in a non-executive capacity. Sir Roy became deputy chairman of Sainsburys in 1975 and was managing director between 1979 and 1988. The diversifed food group chaired by Sir Harry Salomon had not appointed any outside directors until Sir John Nott was invited onto the board last September. Tony Brice, formerly an executive director, is also a non-executive director. ***** Sir Leslie Porter at HELENE. ***** Ian Irvine, deputy chief executive of Reed International, at MEPC. ***** John Eccles, chief executive of the Commonwealth Development Corporation, at COURTAULDS TEXTILES. ***** Bill Farrer, recently retired solicitor and governor of the Burghley House Preservation Trust, at SOTHEBY'S. ***** Sir Ronald Grierson, formerly vice-chairman of GEC, at HOSPITAL CORPORATION INTERNATIONAL. ***** Geoffrey Williams at STANDARD CHARTERED, having ceased his executive duties. ***** Jean Peyrelevade, Brian Gilbertson and Alan Romanis at TRANSATLANTIC HOLDINGS. ***** Robert Paine at CONDER. ***** Tom Robson has retired from HICKSON INTERNATIONAL. ***** Alain Segui, executive vice-president of Source Perrier's international division, is joining United Distillers, the Guinness spirits company, as regional director for central Europe. Segui, who joined Perrier in 1979, has been responsible since 1986 for building the mineral water business in all markets except France and the US. He helped increase turnover from FFr 750m to FFr 2bn in 1990. At United Distillers, Segui, 44, will be responsible for the overall business management of operations in France, Italy, Netherlands, Belgium, and eastern Europe. He will be based in Paris. He succeeds Claude de Jouvencel who has been promoted to business development director based at United Distillers' headquarters in London.
