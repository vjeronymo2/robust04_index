BFN [Text] Tokyo, April 9 KYODO -- Kazakhstan's President Nursultan Nazarbayev said Saturday [9 April] that Prime Minister Morihiro Hosokawa's resignation will not alter Japanese policy or affect agreements signed between the two countries. Nazarbayev told reporters at a Tokyo hotel that bilateral agreements will remain valid and projects planned between Japanese corporations and his country will remain unchanged. Nazarbayev said that Prime Minister Morihiro Hosokawa's announcement Friday that he will resign was unexpected but did not come as a surprise. He said he received no prior indication that Hosokawa would announce his resignation when he met the prime minister on Thursday. But Nazarbayev said he is aware that "when a large number of parties make up a coalition, the problems usually become complex. "From an international perspective, it (Hosokawa's resignation) is nothing to be surprised about," he said. Nazarbayev arrived in Japan on Wednesday for a four-day visit. In addition to his talks with Hosokawa, Nazarbayev met Emperor Akihito and Empress Michiko on Friday.
