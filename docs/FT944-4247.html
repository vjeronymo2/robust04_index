941209 FT 09 DEC 94 / TUC plea over deaths at work A new offence of 'manslaughter at work' should be created to target negligent company executives responsible for workplace safety, the TUC urged today. More than 400 people die at work every year due to poor safety standards but manslaughter charges are rare even when unions prove that an employer is negligent, said the TUC. Mr John Monks, TUC general secretary, said: 'Prison should be the final deterrent to careless, recalcitrant employers who cut corners for a quick profit.'
