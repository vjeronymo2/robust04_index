BFN [Text] Moscow 4 April, TASS -- President Yeltsin received U.N. Secretary-General Butrus Butrus-Ghali in the Kremlin today. They had a constructive and businesslike discussion of a wide range of problems dealing with world politics, and tasks in the sphere of consolidating world security. Butrus-Ghali told Yeltsin about the steps taken for the purpose of making the work of the U.N. more quick and efficient. The Russian side supported the strengthening of the U.N. and the taking of measures for improving its work, so that it will be able to tackle topical problems of the 21st century. Yeltsin stressed Russia's interest in cooperation with the U.N. in connection with the advance of the Russian reform. The U.N. secretary-general said this was in line with his intentions and actions. They discussed in detail problems of peace-keeping activities and humanitarian diplomacy. The sides were unanimous in their opinion that those spheres of the U.N. activities, which are becoming central in world politics, should be given greater attention and considerable resources. Special attention was devoted to conflict situations on the CIS territory and the observance of human rights in the former USSR republics. Yeltsin stressed Russia's openness to cooperation with the U.N. for purposes of ensuring peace in the hotbeds of tension, situated on the CIS territory. Butrus-Ghali praised Russia's peace-keeping role and reiterated the U.N. intention to actively promote the attaining of a durable political solution to the crises, specifically in Georgia and Tajikistan. Butrus-Ghali asked President Yeltsin to attend a summit meeting on social problems, due to take place in March 1995, and to address the U.N. General Assembly within the framework of preparations for the 50th anniversary of the United Nations Organisation. The invitations were accepted, ITAR-TASS learned from the press service of the Russian president.
