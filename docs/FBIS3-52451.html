Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] In the Foreign Ministry's regular briefing this morning, Foreign Minister Dr. Husayn Sulayman Abu Salih reviewed Sudanese-Eritrean relations. [passage omitted] The minister also talked about Sudanese-British relations, pointing out that these relations are historic and all the problems between the two countries could be solved through dialogue. The minister referred to the wrong behavior by the British ambassador to Khartoum and asked Britain to send a new ambassador who would work toward strengthening relations between the two countries. The minister also talked about the archbishop of Canterbury's visit to the rebel areas. He said the visit was seen as support for mutiny, a violation of national sovereignty, and a call to war. He also said that the visit contributed to the deterioration of Sudanese-British relations. He described the archbishop's action as a political one on whose basis the thesis of mutiny would be built.
