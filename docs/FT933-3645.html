930913 FT 13 SEP 93 / Science foresight plan launched THE GOVERNMENT'S ambitious 'technology foresight' programme, aimed at picking areas on which the UK should concentrate research funding, will be launched today. Scientists, industrialists and civil servants are meeting in Edinburgh in the first of a series of eight seminars to be held in different cities this autumn. They will lay the foundations for the full-scale foresight exercise next year. Mr William Waldegrave, the minister for science, hopes that this will involve several thousand people in science and business giving their views and 'building a consensus on the most important generic technologies for the future'. The aim is to match the most promising scientific fields with the country's research resources and UK companies willing and able to exploit the results of the research. By 1995 the priorities identified in the foresight exercise will start to influence the way the government allocates public spending of about Pounds 5bn a year on research and development. The technology foresight steering group, chaired by Professor William Stewart, the government's chief scientific adviser, held its first meeting last week. Prof Stewart added that group had yet to decide about the technologies on which to concentrate and the procedures that would be used for choosing them. Prof Howard Newby, chairman of the Economic and Social Research Council and a member of the steering group, said: 'The most important objective in the early stages must be to create a sense of common purpose between government, industry and academics.'
