September 5, 1989, Tuesday, Valley Edition

VALLEY PEOPLE

Delphi Information Systems named Donald L. Lucas as chairman of the Westlake Village computer concern, replacing Walter F. Bauer, who retired but remains a Delphi director.

Bauer stepped down because he turned 65 and was at the end of a three-year contract with Delphi, said Delphi President Richard R. Janssen. Bauer's departure was not related to Delphi's recent poor performance, Janssen said.

Delphi, which sells computer systems to property-casualty insurance brokers, lost $710,000 on revenue of $20.5 million in its fiscal year that ended March 31.

Lucas, a venture capitalist and a Delphi director since 1983, also is chairman of two computer software companies, Oracle Systems and Cadence Design Systems.
