BFN [Text] The Foreign Affairs and Security Committee of the Latvian Cabinet of Ministers today approved an interim agreement between the Latvian and Russian Governments on travel from Latvia to Russia, and return to places of permanent residence in Latvia of citizens of the former USSR who are neither citizens of Latvia nor citizens of Russia nor citizens of a third country. According to the interim agreement, the above-mentioned people will be able to travel to Russia without visas and return to Latvia showing documents stating their identity. The passport of the former USSR, which has an entry in it about a person's place of permanent residence in Latvia, will ensure visa-free return to Latvia. Apart from this, the Committee also approved an agreement between Latvia and Russia on the work of border representatives, which envisages nomination of plenipotentiary representatives of the two states for settling incidents that have occurred on the border and for tackling problems related to the border. The agreement provides that each side nominates one plenipotentiary border representative and two deputies, who will consider problems arising on the border at conferences after a proposal from either side.
