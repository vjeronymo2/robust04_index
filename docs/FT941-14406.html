940120 FT 20 JAN 94 / International Company News: Ipsco to build Dollars 400m steel mill in Iowa Ipsco, western Canada's leading steelmaker, has chosen south-eastern Iowa for a USDollars 400m mini-mill to produce hot-rolled coil and plate. The mill, with 1m tonnes annual capacity, will be built under a turnkey contract by Germany's Mannesmann Demag. It will double Ipsco's steelmaking capacity. It will make coil up to 96 inches wide and 3/4 inch thick and plate up to 120 inches wide and two inches thick. The workforce will total 300 people and will start production in 1996. Mr Roger Phillips, president, said Ipsco chose Iowa because of an abundant supply of scrap, low taxes and state aid.
