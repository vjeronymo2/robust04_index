December 31, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

TERM LIMITS FOR ELECTED OFFICIALS

Your editorial is marching to a different drummer. The majority of the public did not elect officials for life so that they could pocket perks, pensions and personal mistresses.

As a lifelong Republican, many of my associates agree with Atty. Gen. John Van de Kamp that two-term limits are a necessity. We will vote "yes" for this nonpartisan issue.

Two terms was good enough for the founding fathers, and it should be good enough for today's politicians.

ROBERT WAGNER

Former Mayor Pro-Tem

South Pasadena
