BFN [Excerpts] The former president of the lower chamber, Deputy Antoine Joseph, called on the Army and the private sector on 20 April to take a stand against the clean sweep policy and the implementation of Article 149. Deputy Joseph deems the demands of some political sectors that demand the return of President Aristide 10 days after the appointment of a new prime minister unrealistic. [Begin Joseph recording, in Creole] Certain solutions that have been proposed are supported by some sectors. I can put myself in the shoes of the private sector. I very much doubt that the private sector would have an interest in supporting the implementation of Art. 149. This is also my private position. Personally, I think the country is not ready to face the political and financial consequences of the implementation of Art. 149. [passage omitted] [end recording] Furthermore, Deputy Joseph reaffirmed his support for the initiative of the president of the Chamber of Deputies to begin consultations with the various sectors involved in the crisis. [passage omitted]
