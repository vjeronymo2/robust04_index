BFN [Text] Russia is Kyrgyzstan's major partner and its strategic ally, Kyrgyz President Askar Akayev declared during his meeting with Chairman of Russia's Federation Council Vladimir Shumeyko on Wednesday [4 May] in Bishkek. In the course of their conversation Akayev pointed out that Kyrgyzstan and Russia maintain traditional friendly and partnership relations. However, in his opinion, there are several problems in the republic related to migration of the Russian-speaking population. Akayev underlined, that Kyrgyzstan does its utmost to solve this problem. In his words, it needs Russia's assistance. He indicated that the Slavic University formed in Bishkek in which the Republic's Russian-speaking population can receive an education represents "one of the examples of the Russian-speaking population's strengthening." As Akayev said, it is necessary to create other institutes and organizations so as to lower the level of Russian migration from Kyrgyzstan. He also pointed out that in the near term the Republic's government would adopt a state program in order to keep its Russian-speaking population from leaving. Shumeyko underlined that Russia is ready to render economic assistance to Kyrgyzstan, in particular, "to support the Republic's regions, which have suffered from natural disasters."
