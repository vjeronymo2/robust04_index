Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [From the "Global TV News" newscast] [Text] As had been announced this afternoon, the military court of the Supreme Council of Military Justice handed down sentences on the military personnel accused of the kidnapping and murder of nine students and one professor from La Cantuta University. The sentences ranged from one to 20 years in prison. The military court also decided to acquit Colonel Manuel Guzman Calderon when it was established that he had been transferred from the command of the university's civic action before the crime. At the end of one hour, the length of the hearing at the Simon Bolivar military quarters in Pueblo Libre, the following verdict was read: Brigade General Juan Rivero Lasso, five years in prison and a fine of 50,000 new soles as civil compensation in favor of the state; Colonel Federico Navarro Perez, four years in prison, payment of civil compensation for the same amount; Major Santiago Martin Rivas, 20 years prison; and Major Carlos Pichilingue Guevara, 20 years prison. Both officers jointly will have to pay 500,000 new soles as civil compensation. Captain Jose Adolfo Velarde was only sentenced to one year prison; this will have been served by 14 July taking into account time already served. Velarde will have to pay 10,000 new soles as civil compensation. Noncommissioned officers Pedro Suppo Sanchez, Julio Chuqui Aguirre, Nelson Carbajal Garcias, and Jose Antonio Sosa Saavedra were sentenced to 15 years in prison each and ordered to pay a collective civil compensation of 2 million new soles to the victims' legal inheritors. The military court resolved to file the legal case against Lieutenant Aquilino Portela who is at large. Meanwhile, the military prosecutor and the district attorney did not agree with the verdicts, indicating they will file an appeal. The sentenced officers, one by one, indicated they would appeal their sentences.
