Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] [passage omitted] Awaiting the next round of multilateral meetings, the Movement of the Islamic Society [HAMAS] announced today that it would participate in the upcoming national dialogue conference. Mr. Mahfoud Nahnah, head of the movement, expressed his hopes in an interview published today in L'OPINION that the date of that conference would not be postponed. On the project of the national consensus platform drawn up by the National Dialogue Commission, Mr. Mahfoud Nahnah said it contained a number of proposals put forward by parties involved in the dialogue. He stressed that, on the whole, there seemed to be an acceptable consensus even if it remained limited. The head of HAMAS reiterated his call to include representatives from the dissolved Islamic Salvation Front [FIS] and said he considered the success of the national conference to be linked to the participation of the largest possible number of tendencies. On the other hand, the National Constitutional Rally announced its continued participation in the next rounds of dialogue attended by all sides. The National Constitutional Rally, which is a coalition of 12 political parties, expressed in a statement the necessity of adopting dialogue as a means and method to contain the political crisis. The rally stressed its determination to continue dialogue with the National Dialogue Commission to reach a consensus on the concord platform, and to exert all efforts to find safe ways to resolve and to contain the political crisis in the presence of all sides in the dialogue without marginalization, exclusion, outbidding, or imposed leadership.
