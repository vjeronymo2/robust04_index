July 23, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

HOT WEATHER CAN CAUSE LAWN TROUBLE

Hot weather can mean trouble for lawns. Good watering habits, like watering more deeply and less frequently, will make your lawn look better. Dry, brown spots or an entire unhealthy lawn can be caused by the lawn moth, packed soil, poor watering methods or fertilizer burn.

Never fertilize wet grass because it is likely to cause burns, even when the correct amount is used. Find out from a certified nurseryman how to tell when your lawn needs water depending on the needs of your particular grass.
