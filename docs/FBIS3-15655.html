Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Konstantin Marchenko report under "From Our Correspondents and News Agencies" rubric: "Did Kiev Sell Arms to Tehran?"] [Text] Kiev -- At a news conference in Kiev organized by the Iranian Embassy in Ukraine to mark the 15th anniversary of the Islamic Revolution, Iran's first deputy ambassador to Ukraine, Agadzhani [name as transliterated], drew the press's attention to an item in THE WASHINGTON POST. It reported that an exchange of Iranian oil for Ukrainian artillery mounts took place in 1992. According to the Iranian diplomat, his state exchanged oil neither for artillery mounts nor even for tank spares. Earlier, Ukrainian Defense Minister Vitaliy Radetskyy had stated that, while he is an advocate of the arms trade, nevertheless Ukraine should not for the moment sell off its arsenals; it is necessary first to create a closed production cycle.
