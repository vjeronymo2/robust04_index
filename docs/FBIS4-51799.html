BFN [Text] Seoul, April 20 (YONHAP) -- The export-import [Exim] Bank of Korea will offer relending worth 10 million U.S. dollars to Vietcom Bank to facilitate Korean exports to Vietnam. Exim Bank Governor Chae Hong-sik and Vietcom Bank Vice President Ha Hui-song signed a contract Wednesday for the relending service covering three to 10 years and carrying annual interest rates of about 6 percent. Under Korea's first subleasing service to the Indochinese country, Vietnamese firms importing Korean industrial products will be able to borrow money from Vietcom with the Exim Bank funds at interest rates running around 7 percent. Including its facility to Vietcom, Exim Bank extends relending to 26 foreign financial institutes worth 264 million dollars.
