Language: English Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] The minister of state for internal affairs, Mr. Tom Butime, has assured members of the joint international observer group, who have started arriving in the country for the Constituent Assembly elections which will take place on Monday [28 March] that the government has put everything in place to ensure that the elections take place unhindered. Mr. Butime, together with the Constituent Assembly commissioner and officials of the UN independent secretariat and the UNDP [National Union for Democracy and Progress], were briefing the observers on the situation on the ground at a pre-deployment meeting, which took place at the city hall this afternoon. Mr. Butime told them that it is in the interests of the government to ensure that the elections on Monday succeed, because the stakes are high and everything has been finalized to ensure their freedom of movement and access to the areas that they will want to visit. He assured them that the security of Uganda is good and that nothing will hinder the elections on polling day. [passage omitted]
