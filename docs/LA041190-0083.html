April 11, 1990, Wednesday, Home Edition

NEWSWIRE

Quarterback Jack Trudeau of the Indianapolis Colts was released on his own recognizance after his early-morning arrest on a charge of drunken driving.

Trudeau, 27, of Carmel, Ind., was stopped by a Carmel police officer for speeding and erratic driving about 3 a.m. He recorded a blood-alcohol content of 0.19%, nearly twice Indiana's legal limit of 0.10%.
