Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Andrey Varlamov] [Text] Tokyo, Nov 16 TASS -- One million U.S. dollars worth of humanitarian aid will be rendered by Japan to Belarus to overcome the Chernobyl accident aftermath, in accordance with today's decision of the Japanese Government. The first batch of the humanitarian cargo to include high- precision medical equipment and diagnostics instruments will be sent to Belarusian hospitals in mid-December. The other will be supplied in Minsk by February 1994. According to a report of the Japanese Foreign Ministry, the action will be done within the framework of a 100 million dollar program of assistance to former Soviet republics. It was adopted by an international conference on assistance to new independent states -- 12 former soviet republics with the exception of the Baltic states -- in October 1992 in Tokyo.
