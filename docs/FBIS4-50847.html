BFN [Text] Beijing, May 22 (XINHUA) -- Qinghe County in north China's Hebei Province is now processing and dealing in more than 4,000 tons of cashmere annually, which accounts for two thirds of the country's total or half of the world's total. The county has become the country's largest base for production of cashmere. Every year, it exports over 3,000 tons of cashmere to more than 20 countries and regions, such as Japan, Italy, Britain, Germany and the United States. Many foreign businessmen commented that the world's cashmere is mainly in China and China's cashmere is mainly in Qinghe. The county started to develop the industry of cashmere in 1984. After ten years of efforts, 18 out of the total 20 townships of the county have established the industry of cashmere. There are now more than 100 industrial enterprises of cashmere in the county with over 7,500 wool-carding machines and 40,000 employees. Among them were three Sino-foreign joint ventures. Helped by the research institute of the Tianjin Textile College, enterprises of cashmere in Qinghe County managed to developed a series of unique technology for processing cashmere, which raises the rate of extracted cashmere by 20 percent and reduces the rate of length-damaged cashmere by 12 percent compared with that of its imported technology and equipment. The county has established perennial business relations with over 30 domestic foreign trade enterprises and more than 100 overseas cashmere firms across the world.
