Chihuahua 94WB0034C Mexico City EXCELSIOR in Spanish 27 Mar 94 STATES Section pp 1, 4 94WB0034C Mexico City EXCELSIOR Spanish CSO [Article by correspondent Manuel Cabrera Garcia] [Text] Ciudad Juarez, Chihuahua, 26 March--Drugs worth $4.5 million were seized by the Federal Judicial Police (PJF) detailed to Chihuahua State, during a counternarcotics operation in which 1.5 tons of marijuana were intercepted, a warehouse was seized, and a presumed drug dealer was remanded for crimes against health in all their forms. This announcement was made here today by the PJF deputy commissioner, Javier Norona Guerrero. He explained that, during the operation, headed by the force's commander in the zone, Armando Alarcon, and backed by a special group from the Office of the Attorney General of the Republic (PGR), 131 packages of the aforementioned drug were intercepted moments before they could be shipped to the United States. Norona Guerrero added that the investigation conducted by the PGR personnel ended with the capture of Ramon Moreno Gomez, aged 27. Seven PJF agents participated in the operation, called "Lightning," backed by an agent from the Federal Public Prosecutor's Office, successfully intercepting the drugs. According to Eduardo Mancera, group chief of that police force, this dealt a severe blow to the international drug Mafias. He noted that the PJF agents seized the drugs from a 1978 Dodge pickup truck without license plates, as it was leaving a warehouse located in the Colonia Industrial, at 2379 Montevideo Street. The vehicle was confiscated by the PGR, which took it to that office's facilities in Ciudad Juarez. He claimed that, if the marijuana had reached the United States, it would have supplied 200,000 addicts. The subject under arrest and the drugs were placed at the disposal of the authorities.
