940812 FT 12 AUG 94 / International Company News: Czech bank rallies after heavy loss Agrobanka, the Czech Republic's fifth biggest bank, posted a loss of Kcs3bn (Dollars 107m) last year after making a big increase in loan-loss provisions, to Kcs4bn. However, the bank said it had recovered in the first half of the current year with a profit of Kcs99m. The 1993 loss announced yesterday wiped out the bank's capital, leaving it with a negative equity of Kcs400m and with a negative capital adequacy ratio of -0.8 per cent. The Czech National Bank, the central banking authority, has set a minimum adequacy ration of 8 per cent.
