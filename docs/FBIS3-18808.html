Language: German Article Type:BFN [Text] Wiesbaden (DPA) -- In 1993, the economic performance in eastern Germany measured by the GDP rose by 7.1 percent in real terms compared with the year before. Thus, the economic growth in the new laender and in the former East Berlin was 0.8 percentage points above the preliminary value published in January. This was announced by the Federal Statistical Office in Wiesbaden on Wednesday [9 March]. During the second half of 1993, the GDP rose by 7.4 percent compared with the second half of 1992. During this period it amounted to about 149 billion German marks. As a result, eastern Germany has a share of 9.2 percent in the overall German economic performance.
