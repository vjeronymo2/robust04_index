BFN [Text] Wellington, April 26 (XINHUA) -- Western Samoa Head of State Malietoa Tanumafili II and Prime Minister Tofilau Eti Alesana met Monday [25 April] respectively the visiting delegation of the Chinese National People's Congress (NPC), according to a report reaching here today from Apia. In his meeting with Tian Jiyun, Malietoa expressed his gratitude to China for its assistance to Western Samoa, while Tian, leader of the delegation and vice chairman of the NPC Standing Committee, briefed the head of state on the domestic situation in China and China's independent peace foreign policy. Prime Minister Tofilau told Tian that Western Samoa enjoys good relations with China which are based on mutual respect and understanding. He reaffirmed that the Western Somoa Government abides by the relevant resolution of the United Nations and holds that there is only one China in the world, that is the People's Republic of China. Taiwan is part of China, he said, adding Western Samoa has no official relations whatsoever with Taiwan. Tian expressed appreciation of the position declared by Tofilau on the Taiwan issue. He said China and Western Samoa have good relations in the political, economic, cultural fields, and there are broad prospects for their cooperation. He proposed the governments, parliaments and business circles of China and Western Somoa increase exchange of visits to promote mutual understanding and the development of trade and economic cooperation between the two countries. On Saturday, Western Samoa Parliament Speaker Afamasaga Fatu Vaili also met the NPC delegation. The delegation arrived in Apia on Saturday morning on the second leg of its four-nation south Pacific tour, which also covers New Zealand, Papua New Guinea and Australia.
