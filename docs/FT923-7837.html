920815 FT 15 AUG 92 / International Company News: Daily News unions fail to agree on a new owner THE FUTURE of the Daily News, the New York city tabloid, seemed more precarious than ever yesterday afternoon when its unions failed to agree on a new owner after three days of negotiations. The cash-strapped paper, rescued by Mr Robert Maxwell last year, has been under Chapter 11 of the US bank ruptcy code since the month after Mr Maxwell's death. A creditors meeting has now been rescheduled for Monday, the day bankruptcy court judge Tina Brozman set for the paper to produce its reorganisation plan. Earlier this week, the creditors committee had said it would announce its own choice among the three potential owners on Friday if the unions failed to reach an agreement. The paper has received bids from Mr Conrad Black, the Canadian publisher who owns the Daily News in London, and Mr Mortimer Zuckerman, owner of US News and World Report magazine and The Atlantic Monthly. There is also a so-called 'stand alone' plan under which Silver Screen, a group of film financiers, would join forces with the unions, giving them a stake in the paper. The creditors are reviewing revised offers from both Mr Black and Mr Zuckerman on debt payments and a new printing plant. In spite of yesterday's apparent impasse, Mr Fred Drasner, president of US World and News Report, said: 'We are reasonably encouraged by the outcome because we think we have the support of the majority of the unions.'
