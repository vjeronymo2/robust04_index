930513 FT 13 MAY 93 / Unions back Labour links THE LEADERS of six of Britain's largest trade unions stressed yesterday that they want to avoid any confrontation with Labour leader Mr John Smith at this autumn's party conference over the issue of union-party links. The union leaders, who met yesterday to try to hammer out an agreement on principles for the future relationship between the unions and the Labour party, came from the Transport and General Workers, the GMB general union, the Public Employees, MSF the technicians union, UCW the communications union and RMT, the main rail union. The union leaders do not accept one member one vote for the selection of Labour parliamentary candidates. They are meeting again next week to attempt to find an alternative. All agreed to back a demand by Mr Bill Morris, the TGWU's general secretary, for the publication of a joint union statement to be sent to all Labour constituency parties and affiliated associations emphasising why the union links are essential for the Labour party. Other union leaders, however, are concerned about a draft of the proposed statement Mr Morris wants to have approved at today's executive meeting of Trade Unionists for Labour (TUFL), the party's trade union funding body. The draft statement says 'Labour's individual membership is small' and those that belong to the party are 'unrepresentative of Labour voters as a whole'. The statement says it is 'the affiliated unions that give the party a much broader, more representative base and a wider appeal among electors'. It says that trade unions are popular, adding: 'It would be foolish to alienate and lose this base support in the quest for floating voters.' The statement adds: 'When the media, other parties, business interests and the establishment have tried to kill off the Labour party the trade unions have provided the stability which helped us survive.' One senior union source said of the statement: 'It is unhelpful to Mr John Smith and potentially divisive.'
