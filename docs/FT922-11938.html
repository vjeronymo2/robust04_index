920423 FT 23 APR 92 / World Trade News: Philip Morris buys into Czech cigarette maker PHILIP Morris of the US has won a bid to buy into Czech cigarette manufacturer Tabak Kutna Hora, acquiring 30 per cent of the stock for Dollars 104.4m, (Pounds 58.9m), the Hospodarske Noviny (Economic News) daily reported yesterday, Reuter reports from Prague. Czech deputy prime minister Mr Jan Strasky said the company would invest Dollars 140m in updating Kutna Hora's obsolete technology. The Czech government would retain 35 per cent of the stock, and 27 per cent would be distributed to the population under Czechoslovakia's scheme of coupon privatisation, the daily said. This year, about Dollars 9bn of assets will be handed over to Czechs and Slovaks against government-issued coupons, making some 8.5m citizens shareholders in former communist state property. Consumption of cigarettes in Czechoslovakia, where 38 per cent of the population are regular smokers, has been steadily rising and is now at just over 2,000 cigarettes per capita a year.
