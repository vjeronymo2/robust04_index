BFN [Text] The Presidents of Latvia, Lithuania, and Estonia are meeting at the Latvian resort of Jurmala today. The agenda of the presidents' closed meeting includes: the Russian-Latvian agreement on the withdrawal of Russian troops and future policy toward Russia, the Polish-Lithuanian treaty on good-neighborly relations, and other political matters. The presidents will meet in private without even their advisers present. A Baltic News Service correspondent learned in Jurmala, where the presidents are holding the meeting, that they are not going to hold a news conference, although a joint statement could be released. Final arrangements for the meeting and the topics for discussion were made by Presidents Guntis Ulmanis, Algirdas Brazauskas, and Lennart Meri by phone yesterday. The meeting was provisionally planned for an earlier date, but it was decided to hold it following the visit to Moscow by Latvian President Guntis Ulmanis.
