Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Report by PRENSA LATINA correspondent Eduardo Rodriguez from Harare] [Text] Foreign Minister Roberto Robaina delivered a message from President Fidel Castro to Zimbabwean Acting President Simon Muzenda. Muzenda and Robaina held a short meeting at the Zimbabwean Executive Offices in Harare. With this meeting, Robaina began the third and last day of his official visit to Harare. Robaina arrived in Harare on Saturday from Angola. This visit is part of a two-week tour of the African continent. The head of Cuban diplomacy will travel to Lusaka, Zambia tomorrow and from there to Namibia, the last leg of his tour, which also included Ghana and Angola. Foreign Minister Robaina also met today with Zimbabwean Foreign Minister Nathan Shamuyarira. Shamuyarira described relations between Cuba and his country as excellent and expressed his hopes that bilateral exchange will be strengthened. The Zimbabwean foreign minister recalled that Cuba had done much for Africa, and that now the African countries must offer their support to a country facing difficult times. The Zimbabwean foreign minister encouraged an increase in contacts between the ruling ZANU-PF [Zimbabwe African National Union-Patriotic Front] and the Communist Party of Cuba and expanded cooperation and commercial exchange in other areas.
