Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] In a sign of complications in Yemen's political crisis, the Yemen Socialist Party said it seized antiaircraft missiles being moved by its northern rivals toward the south. A party spokesman said: An Army vehicle carrying 40 antiaircraft missiles from the north and heading south was seized by a southern brigade. He did not say when the missiles were seized, but said this was a new proof of the escalation of the political and the military tension in the country. The announcement came hours after Planning Minister 'Abd-al-Karim al-Iryani blamed Vice President 'Ali Salim al-Bid's party for delaying the signing of an accord to end the political crisis paralyzing the country. Al-Iryani said: There are no arrangements for the signing of the accord neither in Amman nor inside Yemen as new conditions have been set by the Yemen Socialist Party. He said: The YSP wanted constitutional legal and security conditions not included in the accord. He did not give details, but political sources said the YSP was apparently seeking guarantees for its officials from security services run by the president's supporters.
