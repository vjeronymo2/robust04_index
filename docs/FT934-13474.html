931021 FT 21 OCT 93 / UK Company News: Losses at Whinney Mackay-Lewis rise In the worst recession in the construction industry 'in living memory' Whinney Mackay-Lewis, the USM-quoted architect and planner, saw pre-tax losses for the year to April 30 double from Pounds 435,000 to Pounds 950,000. Turnover fell from Pounds 5.68m to Pounds 3.45m. However, the company said that the level of enquiries had risen rapidly in the last two months and had reached a point where it was needing to take on new staff. Operating losses were Pounds 477,000, compared with profits of Pounds 205,000. Interest charges fell to Pounds 412,000 (Pounds 477,000) and there was an exceptional charge of Pounds 61,000 (Pounds 163,000). As part of the reduction in fixed costs it sold a property for Pounds 3.8m, which allowed repayment of borrowings. Losses per share were 12.1p (5.1p).
