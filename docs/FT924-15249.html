921006 FT 06 OCT 92 / Abolish top police ranks, say councils SENIOR POLICE ranks should be abolished and replaced with flexible local management structures, councils argue in radical proposals submitted to the government's inquiry into policing yesterday. They propose that all ranks between sergeant and chief constable should be replaced with functional titles such as manager and commander. Mr Kenneth Clarke, home secretary, is awaiting the results of the inquiry headed by Sir Patrick Sheehy into police rewards and responsibilities next spring before deciding on ways of restructuring the service. The council associations' evidence is important because their members form the police committees responsible for overseeing the service. They say: 'The very existence of clear and detailed national rank and pay structures may effectively discourage some local managers from thinking on a clean-sheet basis about what organisation structure they actually need.'
