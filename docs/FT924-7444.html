921117 FT 17 NOV 92 / Poland starts Lot airline sell-off The Polish government began privatising the state-owned airline, Lot, yesterday, paving the way for an eventual foreign partnership for the company, Reuter reports from Warsaw. The deputy transport minister, Mr Boguslaw Liberadzki, said an order had been signed to transform Lot into a treasury-owned joint-stock company. The airline is looking for a strategic partner to take up to 49 per cent of the company, the other 51 per cent remaining in state hands. The announcement followed a surprise decision by the ministry to dismiss Lot's managing director, Mr Bronislaw Klimaszewski, and replace him with his deputy, Mr Jan Litwinski, because of the airline's financial problems.
