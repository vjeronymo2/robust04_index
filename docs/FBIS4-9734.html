BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities in Brazil monitored through 31 March. The Sao Paulo Commercial Federation reported that February sales in Sao Paulo increased 12.28 percent compared with the same month in 1993, and that this has been the best level of sales in the country in the last three years. (Brasilia Voz do Brasil Network in Portuguese 2200 GMT 25 Mar 94) A merger between Brastemp and Consul, both of which belong to the Brasmotor Group, was announced on 24 March by the holding company's president, Hugo Miguel Etchenique. The new company, whose merger must still be approved at an April stockholders' meeting, will be called Multibras, Inc. Household Appliances. The new company's sales will initially total $800 million per year with exports of $100 million. Multibras will have 10,000 employees. Etchenique said he will not shut down any factories or cut manufacturing. (Sao Paulo O ESTADO DE SAO PAULO in Portuguese 25 Mar 94 Economy Section p B12) Fuel prices will be increased 19.25 percent as of 26 March, the Finance Ministry announced on 25 March. Average fuel price increases totaled 42.5 percent in March as of 24 March. (Sao Paulo GAZETA MERCANTIL in Portuguese 25 Mar 94 p 16) Finance Minister Fernando Henrique Cardoso and Sao Paulo Governor Luiz Antonio Fleury Filho on 25 March signed contracts to refinance the Sao Paulo Government's debts to the Bank of Brazil, the Treasury's agent. Three billion dollars, involving debts contracted with the Federal Savings Bank, the National Bank for Economic and Social Development, and the Bank of Brazil and allocated for investments, have been refinanced. The debt's rollover will be indexed using the IGPM, Market Prices General Index, or the TR, Referential Interest Rate. The contracts call for 240 monthly installments. (Sao Paulo O ESTADO DE SAO PAULO in Portuguese 26 Mar 94 Economy Section p B5) Sao Paulo, Rio de Janeiro, and Parana States lose $560 million per year from a commonplace maneuver in oil and oil by-products: fabricated interstate fuel sales to dodge ICMS, Goods and Services Movement Tax, collection. This type of fraud totals $1 billion throughout our country. Illegal trade operations involve 35 percent of total oil by-products sales in our country. (Sao Paulo O ESTADO DE SAO PAULO in Portuguese 27 Mar 94 Economic Section p B11) Braspetro, Petrobras International, Inc., is negotiating to prospect for and produce oil and natural gas in Peru and Bolivia. In 1993, Braspetro produced an average of 22,900 barrels of oil per day in five countries: Angola, Argentina, England, the United States, and Ecuador. In Colombia, Braspetro will begin producing 600 barrels per day in the Llanos basin, while it will perform exploratory work in Libya and Norway. In 1994, Braspetro will expand its production to 31,000 barrels per day, investing $160 million. In Argentina, Braspetro reduced its participation in the San Julian maritime basin from 53.5 to 33.5 percent. (Sao Paulo GAZETA MERCANTIL in Portuguese 29 Mar 94 Special Section p 3) Brazilian oil reserves stand at 3.8 billion barrels, 5.5 percent higher than at the end of 1992. This reserve will last 16 years based on average production of approximately 700,000 barrels per day. (Sao Paulo GAZETA MERCANTIL in Portuguese 29 Mar 94 Special Section p 4) Agriculture Minister Synval Guazzeli and Nidero Maki, vice president of Japan International Cooperation Agency, on 29 March signed a contract for financing the third phase of the Brazilian-Japanese Cooperation Program for the development of the Cerrado region -- the Prodecer III project. The project involves agricultural development of 40,000 hectares in Maranhao and Tocantins States. The Prodecer III project will get an investment of $138 million. Japan will provide $83 million and Brazil $55 million. (Sao Paulo O ESTADO DE SAO PAULO in Portuguese 30 Mar 94 p B4)
