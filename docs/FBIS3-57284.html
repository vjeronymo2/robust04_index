Language: Lithuanian Article Type:BFN [Text] Adolfas Slezevicius, the prime minister of the Republic of Lithuania, met Carl Bildt, the prime minister of Sweden, in Stockholm today. The talks lasted for an hour and a half. Bilateral regional problems were discussed. The plan Partnership for Peace, security problems of the Baltic Sea region, the possibilities for the development of cooperation between Lithuania and Sweden, and economic relations between the two countries were discussed by the two prime ministers. Kazys Bobelis, chairman of the Seimas Foreign Affairs Committee, Deputy Foreign Minister Algimantas Januska, Romualdas Kalonaitis, Lithuanian ambassador extraordinary and plenipotentiary in Sweden, Lars-Aake (Nilsson), state secretary at the Swedish Foreign Affairs, Ministry, Tom Werterman, head of the CSCE Sector at the Swedish Foreign Affairs Ministry Political Department, Lars Magnuson, Swedish ambassador extraordinary and plenipotentiary to Lithuania, and Jonas [name indistinct], Carl Bildt's adviser on international relations, were also present at the meeting.
