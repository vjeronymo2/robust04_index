BFN [Text] More than 100 leading academics yesterday made public an open letter demanding the government and the opposition jointly set up a special committee to draft a new, more democratic constitution. One hundred and thirteen intellectuals from institutions across the country -- including Chulalongkon, Chiang Mai, Songkhla Nakharin, Khon Kaen, Mahidon and Thammasat universities -- signed their names to the letter. Organizers said it would be submitted to House Speaker Marut Bunnak on Monday. The letter said all politicians should cooperate to resolve the political impasse fuelled by activist Chalat Worachat's hunger strike as soon as possible, before a national crisis develops. The academics accused the Democrat Party of being insincere in its effort to ease the current political tensions. Efforts to end Chalat's hunger strike should not be used as a bargaining chip by any political group seeking to take advantage of the situation. All politicians, and in particular government MPs, should be decisive in resolving the impasse, the letter said. Opposition MPs had already declared their full support for Chalat's demand for a House committee to draft a new charter. Surichai Wankaeo, deputy dean of Chulalongkon University's Political Science Faculty, said the letter was aimed at prompting the government to introduce more democratic changes. He said if democracy was enhanced Chalat may decide to quit his hunger strike. Academic Suchai Trirat said the people should not consider Chalat's hunger strike "pressure outside Parliament" as Chalat's purpose was to help develop the parliamentary system. "Politics does not mean only the people inside Parliament," he said.
