BFN [By ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondents] [Excerpts] Moscow, 14 Apr -- Han Sung-chu, foreign minister of the Republic of Korea, today ended the official part of his visit to the Russian capital. He described as "constructive" the Russian proposal to convene a multilateral conference on security problems in the Korean Peninsula, and agreed in general the program for the upcoming visit to Moscow on 1-4 June by President Kim Yong-sam. Today he held talks with Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev and met the chairmen of both chambers of the Federal Assembly. According to Han Sung-chu, South Korea, together with all involved parties, will continue to actively analyze and study the Russian initiative that intends to gather at one table representatives of both Korean states, Russia, the United States, China, Japan, and also the United Nations and the IAEA. [passage omitted] The head of the Russian foreign relations department said, on the whole, relations between Russia and the Republic of Korea were on a rising trend, as it had been envisaged during President Boris Yeltsin's visit to Seoul, and the upcoming visit by President Kim Yong-sam to Moscow demonstrated this. A solid package of agreements will be prepared for it, inter alia a political document. Han Sung-chu told journalists it was expected that during the upcoming Russian-South Korean summit issues of bilateral cooperation and international problems, including the situation in northeast Asia and in the Korean Peninsula, would be discussed. The heads of state, he said, will exchange opinions regarding cooperation not only on the bilateral basis, but on the multilateral basis within the framework of international organizations. Han Sung-chu arrived in Moscow on Wednesday from where he will leave for London on Friday.
