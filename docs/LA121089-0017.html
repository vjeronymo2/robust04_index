December 10, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

DR. BOGGS

I read with interest "The Rise and Fall of Dr. Boggs," by Doug Smith (Oct. 29), and was not wholly surprised at the response from some of his former patients. It amazes me that the public continues to revere the physician as some sort of abstract God; unfortunately, many physicians also view themselves thusly. As someone who works with MDs on a daily basis, I adhere to the belief that these are moral men and women who run the gamut of ethical agendas and technical expertise; corrupt individuals can be seen operating in the same forum as those whose respect and admiration is well-deserved.

When choosing a physician, as when dealing with any consumer issue, it would be advisable not to trust blindly, and remember that no allegiance to any individual need be permanent.

CHERYL BROWN, R.N., San Dimas
