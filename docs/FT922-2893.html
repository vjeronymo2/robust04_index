920615 FT 15 JUN 92 / Israel to rent out surplus apartments THE Israeli government yesterday decided to rent out thousands of the surplus apartments it holds as a result of over-estimating immigrant demand to purchase, writes Hugh Carnegy in Jerusalem. In doing so it has ignored arguments from the Bank of Israel that the move runs contrary to a pressing need to reduce the state's role in the economy. The cabinet voted to make more than 4,600 of some 12,000 empty units available for rent to the poor and the elderly. Under the pressure of mass immigration from the former Soviet Union, the government ordered thousands of apartments to be built in outlying areas, with a guaranteed buy-back clause. But immigrants' unwillingess to take on even subsidised mortgages in areas without jobs, and a decline in the rate of immigration, has left the Housing Ministry facing a loss estimated at up to Dollars 1bn (Pounds 543m).
