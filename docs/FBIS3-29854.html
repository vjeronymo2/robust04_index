Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, Feb. 23 KYODO -- A top Postal Ministry official on Wednesday [23 February] reversed his position on high-definition television (HDTV) development, saying the ministry will continue to support an analog HDTV system developed by Japan Broadcasting Corp (NHK). Akimasa Egawa, director general of the Broadcasting Administration Bureau at the Ministry of Posts and Telecommunications, told reporters Tuesday his ministry will promote the development of a digital HDTV system to replace NHK's hi-vision system. The remark stirred protests from Japanese consumer electronics companies, which are already selling hi-vision television sets. Egawa said Wednesday the ministry supports the continuation of NHK's experimental hi-vision broadcasts, to be followed by full broadcasts.
