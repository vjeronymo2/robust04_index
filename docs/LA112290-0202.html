November 22, 1990, Thursday, Orange County Edition

ORANGE COUNTY NEWSWATCH

BAD NEWS: Agencies providing holiday food to the needy say they expect many more hungry mouths this year. . . . The Orange County Rescue Mission, which served 1,800 turkey dinners last Thanksgiving, is expecting to serve as many as 3,000 today.

The Salvation Army also reports increased demand. . . . But there's good news too. Despite recession worries, donations have not decreased. "I don't think I've experienced more calls from people to volunteer," says a Salvation Army spokesman. Carla Rivera and Anne Michaud
