BFN [Text] Parliamentary delegations from Ukraine and Crimea have ended talks in Simferopol on the settlement of the Crimean problem. In a final document, the participants noted that proposals for coordinating the two sides' legislations should be prepared by 15 June. At the same time, the delegations agreed that the Ukrainian constitution has supremacy over other laws on the territory of Crimea. It was decided to set up a joint working group which will deal with the delineation of authorities between Ukrainian and Crimean state structures. At the same time amendments will be made to Ukrainian laws. The delegation from the Ukrainian Supreme Soviet asked President Leonid Kravchuk not to take any measures against the Crimean authorities. The Ukrainian delegation also addressed a similar request to President Yuriy Meshkov. INTERFAX reports that Oleksandr Moroz, chairman of the Ukrainian parliament, plans to arrive in Simferopol on Wednesday.
