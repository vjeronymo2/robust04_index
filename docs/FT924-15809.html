921002 FT 02 OCT 92 / Arts: Today's Television It is hard to imagine that the spectacle of detumescent Scottish nationalism can be anything like as gripping as blood-feuding Albanians who provided the excellent opener to BBC 2's 'Under the Sun' trilogy on European tribalism. All the same, it should be worth watching gloomy kilt-wearers analyse the Scottish Nationalists' election route in the concluding film, Ninety-Minute Patriots (9.30). Feuds and hanky-panky within the bosom of another tribe continues to prove gripping entertainment in Behind the Lines, BBC 1's acclaimed drama series about the police (9.30). Otherwise it is a dullish evening unless you pant for the merry laughter and effervescent wit of the folks on Terry Wogan's Friday Night on BBC 1 (10.20.) By an extremely strange coincidence, inhabitants of Ilfracombe in North Devon can see their dear little town twice over. First it appears as the location for BBC 1's cringingly idiotic Challenge Anneka (8.00) and then, on an altogether higher plane, Ilfracombe's gardens are scrutinised in Garden Club on C4 (9.00).
