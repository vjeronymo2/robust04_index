BFN [Text] Pyongyang, May 6 (KCNA) -- A human evolution history research team of Kim Il-song University recently unearthed in and around Pyongyang 42 pieces of 20 fossil men, 298 pieces of stone implements, 109 pieces of bone implements, two bone sculptures and 34,702 pieces of fossil animals belonging to the Paleolithic age and 15 pieces of six fossil men, 23 pieces of stone implements, six pieces of bone implements, 43 pieces of earthenware, one piece of ornament and 1,047 pieces of fossil animals belonging to the Neolithic age and 12 pieces of five fossil men, one piece of earthenware and two pieces of stone implements belonging to the Bronze age. In this connection, a symposium for reading papers on the scientific research into fossil men and relics recently unearthed in and around Pyongyang took place at Kim Il-song University Thursday. Published at the symposium were data about a profound scientific and theoretical analysis of the archaeological significance of the fossil men and relics discovered in Chungri cave, Yonggok cave No. 1 and Taehung caves Nos. 1 and 3 and the features of the bones of ancient Koreans and the results of the research into the systematic evolution of Koreans in Pyongyang. According to them, scores of pieces of relics unearthed from the first and second cultural layers of the Paleolithic age in Chungri cave show that the cultural traditions of primitive men who left the Komunmoru relics were carried forward by their descendants in Pyongyang. The geologic time of Yonggok cave No. 1 where ten fossil men were unearthed on the second, third and fourth cultural layers of the Paleolithic age ranges from the beginning of the Pleistocene epoch to the Holocene epoch of the Quaternary period and that of Taehung cave No. 1 where three fossil men were unearthed on the cultural layer of the Paleolithic age ranges from the end of the Pleistocene epoch to the beginning of the Holocene epoch of the Quaternary period. In Taehung cave No. 3 fossil men, 43 pieces of earthenware and 65 fossil bones of animals were discovered. The earthenware used by ancient Koreans in Pyongyang represents their wisdom and creative talent and noble esthetics. It is patent proof that the common features of the culture of the homogeneous Korean nation were carried forward in the Neolithic age, too. The fossil human bones and relics which have been unearthed and studied prove that the evolution of Koreans took place in Pyongyang in regular order. This is because the features of Koreans were formed in due sequence with Pyongyang as the centre, the culture of ancient Koreans was developed systematically with Pyongyang as the centre and Pyongyang was the best place for the life of mankind. The participants in the symposium said the symposium proved that the evolution of Koreans took place in this land, notably in Pyongyang in regular order and that Pyongyang had sufficient natural conditions and paleontological conditions for the origin and development of ancient Koreans.
