Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities in Uruguay monitored through 10 February: The World Bank granted a $76 million loan to Uruguay to implement agricultural projects and promote industrial development. (Montevideo EL OBSERVADOR ECONOMICO in Spanish 1 Feb 94 p 8) The gross foreign debt was $7.735 billion and the net foreign debt $2.242 billion through 30 September 1993. The gross foreign debt rose $38 million during the first nine months in 1993 as a result of the public sector's debt, which increased $68 million, and private debt, which dropped $30 million. The public sector's foreign debt totalled $7.095 billion. (Montevideo EL PAIS in Spanish 2 Feb 94 Market Indicators Supplement p 1) The National Statistics Institute on 1 February reported that the consumer price index increased 3.41 percent in January compared with the previous month, bringing the inflation rate for the last 12 months to 51.99 percent. Housing registered the highest increase with 8.08 percent; food, 3.28 percent; and beverages, 1.2 percent. (Montevideo LA MANANA in Spanish 2 Feb 94 p 40) On 3 February, the Central Bank of Uruguay reported that its net foreign reserves, RIN, increased $22.4 million in January. The total RIN is now $1.2253 billion. (Montevideo EL OBSERVADOR ECONOMICO in Spanish 4 Feb 94 p 7)
