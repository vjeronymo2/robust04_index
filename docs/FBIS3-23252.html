Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Under the supervision of the public prosecutor, Counselor 'Abd-al-Sami' Sharaf-al-Din, the Higher State Security Prosecution is conducting extensive investigations into the seizure of explosives bound for terrorists in Asyut. The explosives consisted of approximately 100 kg of gunpowder used in making bombs and explosive devises. The prosecution is also conducting investigations to identify the source of these explosives. In releasing this information, Counselor Sharaf-al-Din said the haul was found in the possession of two truck drivers. The two suspects admitted that a man, whose first name and description were disclosed, gave them two sacks of explosives, each containing about 50 kg of gunpowder, claiming that they contained organic fertilizer. The suspects said that he asked them to deliver the sacks to another man in Asyut after giving his description and fixing a rendezvous point and gave them a large sum of money. The Higher State Security Prosecution ordered the arrest of the man described by the drivers and efforts are now under way to detain him. The two gunpowder sacks were delivered to the criminology laboratory for a technical report.
