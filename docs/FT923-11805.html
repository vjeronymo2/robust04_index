920723 FT 23 JUL 92 / Britain criticises state help for Iberia airline BRITAIN yesterday attacked a European Commission decision to approve a Pta120bn (Pounds 655.7m) capital increase and restructuring plan for Spain's state-owned airline Iberia, write Andrew Hill in Brussels and Daniel Green in London. Brussels gave its approval after Madrid promised the funds would be used to renew Iberia's fleet rather than to buy stakes in other Community airlines. It will also alter the loss-making airline's statutes in line with the Commission's air transport liberalisation plans. The Commission insisted this should be the last such subsidy. Lord Caithness, UK aviation minister, called the EC decision 'most regrettable'. He said Mr Karel Van Miert, transport commissioner, had accepted last week that state aid could be allowed if it might equally have come from a commercial organisation. 'Yet within days the Commission has approved a vast injection of state aid to Iberia which surely no private sector company would have undertaken,' said the minister.
