930203 FT 03 FEB 93 / International Company News: SocGen closer to takeover of securities house SOCIETE Generale has bought out the remaining 19.7 per cent of Societe Generale Strauss Turnbull, the London-based securities house which it has been trying to build into a broad-based trading, research and derivatives firm. The purchase, understood to have cost less than Pounds 4m, (Dollars 6.04m) was described by SGST executives as part of a long-term move by the bank progressively to take on full ownership of the firm. It did not signal any change in direction, said Mr Paul Tisch, chief executive. The bank's stake has grown since it first took 55 per cent of Strauss's Eurobond arm in 1980. In the mid-1980s, it took the maximum permissible 29.9 per cent in the the firm's equity operation. Mr Derek Strauss, son of Mr Ronald Strauss, one of the founders of the original Strauss Turnbull partnership and himself holder of 5 per cent of SGST, said he would remain with the firm .
