940117 FT 17 JAN 94 / Observer: Windy words Bill Foggitt, the weather sage from Thirsk, thinks Britain may face a heat wave this summer. Unmoved by recent heavy rainfalls - 'it hasn't been much different than normal in Thirsk' - he reckons August may be unconventionally steamy. 'We had a good summer in 1990. That coincided with the Gulf War. It was a beautiful day when war broke out in September 1939 and we had a very hot summer in 1940. Forebodingly, he adds that 'Russia could be on again. I could see the Bosnian Serbian conflict overflowing into Macedonia and into Russia. It could lead to nuclear war.' Was he forecasting Armageddon? 'No, that's the name we give an old lady who goes into the Three Tuns.'
