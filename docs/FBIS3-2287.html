Language: English Article Type:BFN [By Lilian Wu] [Text] Taipei, March 8 (CNA) -- The Israel Economic and Trade Office formally opened Monday [7 March] in Taipei. Israeli representative Jacob Fass, 46, told reporters he was honored to be here. Fass, a career diplomat, explained that his office would work to promote trade, investment and tourism relations between Israel and Taiwan. The office will also handle visa applications, most of which can be processed in two days, Fass said. Trade between Israel and Taiwan totaled US$500 million in 1993, with Taiwan enjoying a trade surplus of US$100 million. Taiwan's major export items to Israel include personal computers, textiles and motorcycles, totaling US$300 million, while Israel exported US$200 million worth of goods to Taiwan last year, mainly hi-tech products, such as telecommunications, medical, aerospace and computer items. In June, a large delegation representing some of the biggest companies and banks in Israel will visit Taiwan to explore possible business opportunities here, Fass said. A Taipei Economic and Trade Office opened in Israel last May.
