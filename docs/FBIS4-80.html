BFN [Text] Pretoria March 30 SAPA -- Ninety-six per cent of potential voters already have identification documents enabling them to vote in next month's election, the Department of Home Affairs said on Wednesday [30 March]. In a statement the department said 21,850,116 of an estimated total of 22,709,152 voters as determined by the Central Statistical Services had their documents by last Friday. During the preceeding week 153,520 ID documents and 152,301 temporary voters' cards had been issued and the department would continue its efforts to sustain this trend. The department reminded the public that its regional and district offices would be open on Family Day (April 4) and Founders Day (April 6) enabling them to apply for the necessary documents. "Reports from various quarters indicate that the rush at the department's offices is diminishing." The department warned that the loss or theft of ID documents constituted a "very real" security risk.
