BFN [Text] The heads of state and government participating in the fourth Latin American summit are taking advantage of their meeting in Cartagena de Indias to hold bilateral meetings. Cuban President Fidel Castro has expressed his interest in meeting with Felipe Gonzalez. Juan Luis Diaz Prats reports from Colombia: [Diaz Prats] King Juan Carlos and Prime Minister Felipe Gonzalez have held separate meetings with other leaders attending the fourth Latin American summit. The king received Chilean President Eduardo Frei, Uruguayan President Luis Alberto Lacalle, and Costa Rican President Jose Maria Figueres. King Juan Carlos was interested in the situation in Chile and Costa Rica and in Central America. He said he regretted having to delay until next year his official visit to Uruguay, a country which has begun the electoral process. Gonzalez held meetings with the presidents of Colombia, Uruguay, and Honduras. The summit agenda was discussed during these meetings, but so was the situation in Cuba. Cuban President Castro has asked to speak to Gonzalez at 0700 Colombia time, which is 1400 Spanish time. Castro was the last leader to arrive in Cartagena, after 2300 local time. The Uruguayan president confirmed to Gonzalez that his government would continue to collaborate in the fight against terrorism, restricted only by respect for judicial decisions. Gonzalez also spoke about cooperation with the three Latin American leaders. In the specific case of Colombia, the Spanish prime minister offered to continue aid to the areas devastated by the floods in Cauca and Huila, which will be visited at the end of the summit by Jose Luis Dicenta, secretary of state for international cooperation and Latin America, who is responsible for drawing up a Spanish aid plan for southwest Colombia.
