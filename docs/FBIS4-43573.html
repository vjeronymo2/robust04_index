BFN [Excerpts] The Socialist Party this evening is looking for a new prodigy able to embody its ideas and to remotivate the electorate less than one year before the presidential elections. [passage omitted] The bitterness of Mr. Rocard and a strange day, as related by Ghislaine Chenu and Roland Sicard: [Sicard] This is the first picture of Michel Rocard as a rank-and-file member of the Socialist Party. He has just been beaten, he is no longer the first secretary. His immediate reaction: [Begin Rocard recording] This morning I suggested to the Socialist Party national council that it should go beyond factionalism. I suggested we should build a political movement which could meet the demands of the French people. Finally, I suggested that a team of gifted and sincere young politicians -- including Martine Aubry -- should join the national leadership. All this was rejected. This is why I resigned. It is now up to members to express themselves. As for me, I remain, I still am a fighter for hope. [end recording] [passage omitted]
