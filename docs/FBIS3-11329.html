Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [From "The News" newscast] [Text] Luis Eduardo Giusti Lopez has been appointed the new president of Venezuela Petroleum Inc. [Petroven] to replace Gustavo Roosen, who will continue to head the intervention committee at Banco Latino. Giusti entered the oil industry in 1966 through Shell of Venezuela, which preceded Maraven, S.A. He has held numerous positions in the areas of production, oil bed engineering, and planning both in Shell and Maraven. In the announcement about Giusti's appointment, it was reported that 2,170,000 barrels of crude oil per day, at an average price of $13.34 a barrel, were exported from Venezuela in 1993. In other words, the volume exported in 1993 exceeded 1992 exports by 116,000 barrels daily. It was also said that the sales of gasoline produced by the subsidiary (?Cyclop) Petroleum in the United States increased by 7 percent. In the refineries located on Venezuelan territory, 950,000 barrels of oil were processed daily. The Amuay refinery processed 440,000 barrels daily; the Cardon refinery, 265,000; the Palito refinery, 100,000; and the Puerto la Cruz refinery, 141,000 barrels daily. The production of diesel with 0.05 percent sulfur began in the Amuay refinery during this period. In addition, the Palito refinery started producing oxygenated gasoline to meet the new environmental demands stipulated by the U.S. market. In addition, Rossen, head of the Banco Latino intervention board, said that a plan to refloat the bank is under way. He asked the bank's depositors to keep calm. [Madrid EFE in Spanish at 2340 GMT on 18 March reports that, according to Energy and Mines Minister Erwin Arrieta, President Caldera on 18 March removed the entire Petroven board of directors and appointed Giusti].
