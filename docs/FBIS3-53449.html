Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] President Zine El Abidine Ben Ali this morning received at Carthage palace Mr. Manuel Marin, the first vice president of the European Commission in charge of cooperation policy for development. Following the meeting, the European official said that his talk with the president was important and extremely useful and was characterized by openness, pointing out that this was due to Zine El Abidine Ben Ali's clear and far-sighted political views towards Tunisia's relations with the Mediterranean region, in general, and with the European Union [EU]. He expressed happiness for the forthcoming start of negotiations with Tunisia over the new partnership agreement between our country and the EU, hoping that during the second half of this year, a final partnership formula would be reached. Mr. Manuel Marin made clear that technical consultations will take place between the two sides in order to look into "the terms appropriate to the Tunisian economy which we should respect," saying that "if the final formula is the free exchange which has great importance to the Tunisian economy and its future, we should also acknowledge the need for the Tunisian economy to be adequate to the new framework of cooperation in the best of ways. In conclusion he expressed confidence for the partnership agreement between Tunisia and the EU to acquire the utmost possible importance and use for both sides.
