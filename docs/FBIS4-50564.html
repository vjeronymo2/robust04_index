BFN [Text] Warsaw, May 12 (XINHUA) -- Polish President Lech Walesa told a visiting Chinese general today that his nation would like to develop relations of cooperation and partnership in all areas with China. Walesa met this morning with General Zhang Wannian, member of the Military Commission of the Central Committee of the Communist Party of China and chief of the General-Staff of the Chinese People's Liberation Army, at the presidential palace. Walesa said that his nation needs new friends and new partners, and that he was eager to visit China. General Zhang said that relations between China and Poland have been developing rapidly in recent years, and that there is great potential for the furthering of ties. He also said that he had a deep impression in Poland of social stability and steady economic development. On the Polish military build-up, General Zhang said that Polish military forces are well-trained and well-equipped and that China could learn from Poland. General Zhang arrived here last Saturday [7 May] after visiting Romania.
