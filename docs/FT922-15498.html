920401 FT 01 APR 92 / UK Company News: Johnston Press produces 8.5% rise to Pounds 7.6m JOHNSTON PRESS, the Edinburgh-based company which publishes paid-for and free newspapers in England and Scotland, increased pre-tax profit by 8.5 per cent, from Pounds 6.74m to Pounds 7.6m, in 1991. The rise was achieved despite a 2 per cent fall in sales to Pounds 61.9m, and stemmed from cost control measures and the shedding of production staff as a result of new technology. Mr Fred Johnston, chairman, whose family controls 52 per cent of the shares, said the newspapers had generally suffered a decline in advertising volumes. Gains in Scotland, where recession had been less severe, was offset by falls in the English newspapers. Earnings were 18.7p (16.7p). The final dividend is 3.5p, making 5.5p (5p).
