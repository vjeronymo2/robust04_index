Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Buenos Aires, 14 Mar (NA) -- Vice Foreign Minister Fernando Petrella said today that if Argentina has no medical patents law "it will be very difficult to get investments and import advanced technology." Petrella said: "If we want to introduce the country into a market such as the U.S. market, we cannot go on without a patents law." In this regard, U.S. Trade Representative Mickey Kantor reportedly told President Carlos Menem in Chile on 11 March that if no progress on that law is made in Congress by 30 April, Argentina would be eliminated from the list of candidates to join NAFTA. Regarding the request made by UN Secretary General Butrus-Butrus Ghali that the Argentine Government send more Blue Helmets to Bosnia, Petrella said this is "highly gratifying for our foreign policy and the Armed Forces, which are the participants in this peace process." In comments made in the "Puzzle" program of the Rock and Pop FM station, Petrella said our country "conveyed to the United Nations that we will only participate if we get weapons similar to the ones the other units have."
