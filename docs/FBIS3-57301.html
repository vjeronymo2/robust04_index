Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Among other issues, the Russian and Chechen sides discussed the possibility of a meeting between Russian President Boris Yeltsin and Chechen President Dzhokhar Dudayev at the bilateral talks in Moscow last week, Chechen Prime Minister Mairbek Mugadayev [title as received] told a press conference in Groznyy today. He said his two-hour meeting with Russian Prime Minister Viktor Chernomyrdin focused on a wide range of issues related to the concluding of a treaty on the principles for economic cooperation between Russia and the Chechen Republic. The Chechen official pointed out that "all the issues the Chechen delegation raised before the Russian prime minister were understood and approved. The two sides came to the conclusion that a political settlement and a meeting of the heads of state were needed for the resolution of all economic disputes." "In turn, V.Chernomyrdin hoped that such a meeting could be held in the near future," Mugadayev said. The Chechen prime minister said the meeting touched on the possibility of freeing the former speaker of the Russian Supreme Soviet, Chechen citizen Ruslan Khasbulatov accused of organizing the armed uprising in Moscow in early October.
