Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Shanghai, February 19 (XINHUA) -- The Shanghai Municipal Government has decided to build four special centers for returned Chinese students to apply what they have learned overseas. The four centers will be located within the Zhangjiang High-Tech Industrial Zone in the Pudong New Area, the Xinxing Technological Development Zone at Caohejing, the Zone of Technological Development at Jiading and the Chedun Economic and Technological Development Zone in Songjiang County. According to information from the Shanghai Municipal Service Center for returned Chinese students, about 100 returned students who have acquired doctor's or postgraduate's degrees in the United States, Japan and Canada have applied for starting their businesses in the Zhangjiang High-Tech Industrial Zone, where returned Chinese students can enjoy many preferential policies. The Chedun Economic and Technological Development Zone, covering an area of 20 hectares, was set up recently by the shanghai University of Industry and Songjiang County. At present, construction of basic facilities has been completed in the zone. Officials with the Chedun Economic and Technological Development Zone said they hoped to build the zone into a base for carrying out experimental application of newly-developed scientific and technological achievements and for industrial mass production. The zone will offer a series of services to attract more returned Chinese students to launch businesses. The services include handling visas for the students, providing residential housing and workshops at perferential terms, offering legal consultancy, and helping find cooperation partners.
