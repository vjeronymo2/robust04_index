BFN [Text] The prime minister, Mr. P.V. Narasimha Rao, has called for the involvement of Panchayats [rural bodies] to disseminate information about developmental activities. Inaugurating the 21st conference of state ministers of information and cinematography in New Delhi today, he said Panchayat leaders should form part of a vital link in the two way communicational structure that should be established. This, he said, is very essential as a large number of beneficiaries of social welfare schemes are ignorant of them. Drawing attention to the revolution that is taking place in the field of information technology, Mr. Rao said we should preserve our cultural identity to prevent our society from becoming directionless. Ruling out banning of satellite television channels, the prime minister said that Doordarshan [Indian Television] and All India Radio have a lot to learn from foreign media though they should not emulate them. He called for more attention to developing the programs that will be presented to the people. In his keynote address, the information and broadcasting minister, Mr. K.P. Singh Deo, called for better coordination between the central and state governments to make mass communication more effective. He said his ministry is having a fresh look at the composition and terms of inter-media publicity coordination committees that are already working at the state level. The meeting is discussing in three sessions the subjects of entertainment tax and films, interpersonal media, and the electronic media.
