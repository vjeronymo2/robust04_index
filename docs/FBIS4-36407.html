BFN [Text] Brother Lieutenant General 'Ali 'Abdallah Salih, Presidential Council chairman, today received brother Lakhdar Brahimi, personal envoy of UN Secretary General Dr. Butrus Butrus-Ghali. During the meeting, the two sides discussed issues related to Lakhdar Ibrahimi's fact-finding mission in our country. The president welcomed the UN secretary general's envoy. He reiterated our country's compliance with UN Security Council Resolution No. 924 on the situation in the Republic of Yemen, and its readiness to extend all possible support to guarantee the success of the mission of the UN secretary general's envoy. The Presidential Council chairman also stressed that the forces of unity and legitimacy are fully abiding by the cease-fire, but the gang of rebellion and secession deliberately refuses to respond to and violates UN Security Council Resolution No. 924 in an attempt to internationalize the internal crisis in our country. He pointed out that the only thing that could prove the gang of rebellion and secession has responded to the resolution is for it to stop firing artillery shells at the positions of our Armed Forces at the entrances of the city of Aden, and pledge not to use what remains of the warplanes this secessionist gang has used to bombard the forces of unity and legitimacy along all axes. The brother president said he has instructed our Armed Forces to observe a cease-fire as of 1800 [1500 GMT] today in implementation of UN Security Council Resolution No. 924, and to give a third chance to the rebellious and secessionist gang, hoping that this gang will not repeat its violations of the cease-fire, and will not continue its aggression against our Armed Forces. He emphasized that the forces of unity and legitimacy will fully and decisively comply with the cease-fire at all axes. Brother Lakhdar Brahimi has expressed appreciation for the cooperation of the brother president and the officials in our country, for their care to make his mission a success, for complying with UN Security Council Resolution No. 924, and for doing all that will restore stability and security to Yemen. Attending the meeting were Muhammad Salim Basindwah, minister of foreign affairs, and ambassador Muhammad Hadi, director of the Arab Department at the Foreign Ministry.
