BFN [Text] The House of Representatives met today under its speaker Shaykh 'Abdallah Bin-Husayn al-Ahmar. At the outset of the meeting, the people's representatives reviewed and approved the minutes of the previous session. The people's representatives then resumed their discussions, deliberations, and comments on the contents of the report of the House of Representatives' committee which is composed of the defense and security committee and a number of representatives, concerning the incidents which took place between the 3d and the 1st Armored Brigades in the 'Imran region. The report was submitted for discussion by the representatives, who stressed that the House of Representatives should make a decision to stop the military repercussions, especially since these repercussions only claim the lives of the sons of the one homeland, leave widows, bereaved women, and orphans, and cause immense and grave material losses. Several representatives also called on the House of Representatives to make a decision to stop media reactions, particularly the exchange of media vituperations carried on channels 1 and 2. Some people's representatives suggested that the House of Representatives form a parliamentary salvation government from among the House of Representatives, which is elected by the people. This government should be entrusted with the implementation of the accord document in letter and spirit and should remain unbiased, so that the country will not be exposed to fragmentation and splits, and in order to safeguard Yemen, its achievements, unity, democracy, security, and stability. In their discussion, the representatives stressed the need for the military commanders to respect the Islamic months during which fighting is forbidden [words indistinct] so that will mark the beginning of the road to entrenching security and stability, ending the military repercussions of the crisis, and bolstering national unity. Dr. 'Abd-al-Salam Khalid Karman, minister of legal and House of Representatives affairs attended the meeting. The House of Representatives will resume its meetings on 4 May, God willing.
