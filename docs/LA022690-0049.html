February 26, 1990, Monday, Home Edition

NEWSWIRE

The president of soccer's governing body wants rule changes to accommodate television for the 1994 World Cup, the first to be played in the United States.

Joao Havelange, president of the International Federation of Association Football, was quoted Sunday in Rio de Janeiro as saying games should be divided into four 25-minute quarters instead of two 45-minute halves to allow more commercials.
