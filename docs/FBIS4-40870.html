BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Galina Kuchina] [Text] Riga June 20 TASS -- Russian troops are being pulled out of Latvia according to schedule, the press centre of the North-Western Army Group reported on Monday. There will be not a single unit or military installation here after August 31, apart from the radar station in Skrunda. Air and naval forces, border troops and air defence units have already left this Baltic state. Out of 177 units, over 50 percent were redeployed, three-quarters of combat materiel and 95 percent of ammunition were carried away. Less than ten percent of material resources are to be loaded. There were 361 military and 34 housing compounds in Latvia. Russian troops have now less than a third of military and about 50 percent of housing compounds. The rest were handed over to local authorities.
