940527 FT 27 MAY 94 / Major switches policy on animals The Conservatives attempted yesterday to soften their 'hunting-and-shooting' image - making made animal welfare a central policy in their European campaign alongside farming, fishing and the countryside. They launched a dossier listing government action on animal experiments, whaling, elephants, wildlife, stray dogs and farm animals. In the foreword, Mr John Major, the prime minister, writes: 'The welfare of animals is no longer a fringe issue. 'Most people feel that there is room to improve the way animals are treated. No other government - in Britain or abroad - can match the record of this Conservative government on animal welfare.
