Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, March 29 (CNS)--Four financial laws and regulations including a "Law for the People's Bank of China" will come into force, according to a meeting held by the People's Bank of China on work on national financial laws and regulations. The Vice President of the central bank Mr. Yin Jieyan said that the draft laws and regulations namely the "Law for the People's Bank of China", a "Banking Law for the People's Republic of China", an "Insurance Law" and a "Law on Notes", all of which were drawn up by the central bank have been submitted to the State Council for appraisal and discussion before going for deliberation and approval by the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress. It is expected the laws and regulations will come into force in the second half of this year. The "Law for People's Bank of China" is aimed at safeguarding the stability of the currency and ensuring smooth operation of the financial market in a bid to improve the financial system to serve economic development. The law states that the central bank will set up a committee for monetary policy in order to stabilize the value of the currency and prevent inflation. The "Banking Law for the People's Republic of China" is aimed at governing the commercial banks in a bid to make them operate smoothly.
