920922 FT 22 SEP 92 / Museums gear up to business methods MUSEUMS AND art galleries increasingly see themselves as businesses that must sell their services to the public, according to the latest edition of Cultural Trends, published today. The government remains the most important source of income to museums and galleries in the UK, contributing more than Pounds 260m in 1991-92. But for the 11 English museums and galleries sponsored by the Office of Arts and Libraries, self-generated income rose from Pounds 28.6m in 1988-89 to Pounds 46.7m in 1991-92: 21.4 per cent of total income.
