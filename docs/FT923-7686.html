920815 FT 15 AUG 92 / Finance & The Family: Directors' Transactions ON THE first anniversary of its debut on the UK stock market, directors of Eurocamp have been taking profits. All five of the executive directors each sold 50,000 shares at 315p. Shares in the tent and the mobile home rental group have outperformed the market by almost 40 per cent since they were floated last year. Interim results announced last month covered the period to the end of April and, as such, give little away because of the strong seasonal nature of the business. Southern Business Group is another stock to have outperformed the market by 40 per cent over the past year. Paul Roberjot has sold 150,000 shares at 94p in order to meet a personal tax liability. He bought 100,000 shares in April last year at 102p. BPB Industries has featured frequently in this column. Alan Brooks, the joint managing director, has bought 22,000 shares at 159p. A week earlier, his colleague Jean-Pierre Cuny had purchased 10,000 at 137.5p. Together with Alan Turner, the chairman and chief executive, these three have been actively building up their personal holdings since November last year. Malcolm Dagul, chairman of Southend Property Holdings, has been accumulating shares steadily since the company's final results were announced on 22 July 1992. In total he and his colleague John Main have acquired 271,000 shares at prices ranging from 40.5p to 43p.
