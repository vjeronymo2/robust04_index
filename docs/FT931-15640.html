930112 FT 12 JAN 93 / Parliament and Politics: Asylum measure nearer to law THE ASYLUM and Immi-gration Appeals Bill, aimed at accelerating and simplifying the decision-making process in asylum cases, was moving towards completion of its remaining stages in the Commons last night. During the debate, Mr Kenneth Clarke, the home secretary, told MPs that only one in 20 of the applications for political asylum in Britain dealt with in 1992 proved to be well founded. In the committee stage last month the government bowed to pressure from Tory backbenchers by agreeing to water down proposals which would have removed the current right of appeal for relatives of British residents refused entry into Britain for short visits.
