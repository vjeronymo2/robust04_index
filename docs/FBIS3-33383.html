Language: Portuguese Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Brasilia -- The government has decided not to continue waiting for the Constituent Congress. It has reported on its plans to control, through a draft bill, the use of the 150-km-wide strip of territory along the Brazilian border with other countries. In accordance with the project the Strategic Affairs Secretariat sent to congress, any initiative for the border strip will have to be discussed first with the Executive Secretariat of the National Defense Council. [passage omitted] The draft bill sent to Congress by the government establishes that the National Defense Council will have to make all decisions about the border strip area: be it about the purchase of buildings by foreigners, the demarcation of indigenous land, scientific expeditions, the installation of television and radio services, and the admittance or work of religious missions and nongovernmental organizations.
