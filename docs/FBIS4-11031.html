BFN [Text] Buenos Aires, 19 Apr (NA) -- The Chilean national defense chief of staff, Vice Admiral Jorge Patricio Arancibia Reyes, stated today in this capital that Chile will continue to purchase armaments because "the technological level that has been acquired, which is related to the level of a country's development, cannot be neglected." "A country must have the armed forces it is capable of maintaining, both technologically and financially, in a close relationship between security and development," Arancibia Reyes said. Notwithstanding, the admiral denied that Chile is embarked on an arms race, and said "materiel only has been renewed" in recent times. Asked about the appearance of insurgent groups in several parts of the continent, such as the group that revolted in Chiapas, Mexico, he maintained that today "they have an international structure behind them that keeps their concern about these movements on the alert." [quote as received] The Chilean Army chief [title as received], who is visiting the country on an official visit, today met Argentine Defense Minister Oscar Camilion. During the meeting, in which the Argentine joint chief of staff, Lieutenant General Mario Candido Diaz, participated, the Chilean officer handed a letter to Camilion from Chilean Defense Minister Edmundo Perez Yoma, who proposed a meeting between the two ministers some time this year. Regarding the Mapuche conflict that has emerged in his country, Arancibia Reyes stressed that "a solution is in the works," and that "the concerns expressed by the Mapuches are being well channeled." Finally, Arancibia Reyes highlighted "the greatness of the Argentine Armed Forces" within the Latin American context.
