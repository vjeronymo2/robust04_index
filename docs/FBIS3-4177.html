Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, March 28 (XINHUA) -- Kim Yong-sam, visiting president of the Republic of Korea, said here today that the ROK and China should establish economic and trade relations which are mutually complementary and look towards the future. He said that the close ROK-China economic and trade ties will not only contribute to the common prosperity of the two countries, but also to the active development of economies in Asia and in the world at large. He made the statement in a luncheon in his honor hosted by the China Council for Promotion of International Trade (CCPIT) and China Chamber of International Commerce (CCIC). Kim said that while continuing the two-way trade, the two countries should strengthen their joint development of new technology and establish more joint-ventures, in order to realize mutual complementary economic and trade relations with an eye to the future. He expressed his hope that ROK enterprises should be more active in the Chinese market and develop along with their Chinese counterparts since there are factors conducive to their becoming good partners. Zheng Hongye, president of both CCPIT and CCIC, said in his speech that he was confident that Kim's current visit will exert a positive influence on the promotion of bilateral trade and economic and technological cooperation. Before the luncheon, Chinese Vice-Premier Li Lanqing met with ROK President Kim and his party at the Diaoyutai State Guesthouse. During the meeting, the two sides reviewed the development of bilateral economic and trade ties since the establishment of diplomatic relations. They shared the view that the two governments should make greater efforts to promote these ties.
