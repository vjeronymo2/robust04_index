BFN [Report by Enrique Gutierrez from the "Early Morning" newscast] [Text] Nearly 100 members of the Party of the Democratic Revolution [PRD] have occupied the Coyuca de Benitez Municipal Office in Guerrero State to demand, among other things, clean municipal elections and lower water and electricity rates. Members of the so-called Peasant Organization of the Sierra Madre of the South [Organizacion Campesina de la Sierra Madre del Sur], affiliated with the PRD, occupied the Coyuca de Benitez Municipal Office in Guerrero State on the morning of 11 April. The PRD members, headed by Bartolo Guzman Martinez, held Mayor Jose Serna and four other municipal employees hostage. Municipal Council Chairman Jesus Herrera Velez continues to work from a private home while the PRD members demand that he come to the municipal offices. The demonstrators are demanding fair municipal elections as well as a decrease in water and electricity rates.
