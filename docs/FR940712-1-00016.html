The Proposed Amendment Accordingly, pursuant to the authority delegated to me by the Administrator, the Federal Aviation Administration proposes to amend part 39 of the Federal Aviation Regulations (14 CFR part 39) as follows: PART 39_AIRWORTHINESS DIRECTIVES 1. The authority citation for part 39 continues to read as follows: Authority: 49 U.S.C. App. 1354(a), 1421 and 1423; 49 U.S.C. 106(g); and 14 CFR 11.89. §39.13 [Amended] 2. Section 39.13 is amended by removing both AD 87&hyph;04&hyph;04, Amendment 39&hyph;5529, and AD 89&hyph;16&hyph;02, Amendment 39&hyph;6273, and by adding a new airworthiness directive to read as follows: British Aerospace, Regional Aircraft Limited: Docket No. 94&hyph;CE&hyph;02&hyph;AD; Supersedes AD 87&hyph;04&hyph;04, Amendment 39&hyph;5529, and AD 89&hyph;16&hyph;02, Amendment 39&hyph;6273. Applicability: HP 137 Mk1, Jetstream Models 200, 3101, and 3201 airplanes (all serial numbers), certificated in any category. Compliance: Required within the next 50 hours time-in-service (TIS) after the effective date of this AD, unless already accomplished. To prevent failure of the flap torque shaft assembly, which could result in asymmetric flap deployment and loss of control of the airplane, accomplish the following: (a) Install an improved torque shaft assembly in accordance with Part 1 of the ACCOMPLISHMENT INSTRUCTIONS section of Jetstream Aircraft Limited Service Bulletin (SB) No. 27&hyph;JA 920340; or modify the existing torque shaft assembly in accordance with Part 2 of the ACCOMPLISHMENT INSTRUCTIONS section of Jetstream Aircraft Limited SB No. 27&hyph;JA 920340, which incorporates the following pages:

(b) The life limit requirements of the torque tube shaft assembly required by AD 87&hyph;04&hyph;04, and the repetitive inspections of the universal joints and universal joint rivets required by AD 89&hyph;16&hyph;02 remain mandatory until the effective date of this AD or upon accomplishing the installation or modification referenced by paragraph (a) of this AD, whichever occurs first.
