Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Ulaanbaatar, March 22 (OANA-MONTSAME) -- At the secondary schools of Mongolia there are studying some 370 thousand children. This is approximately by ten thousand less than during the last year. More than 92 thousand schoolchildren have stopped attending their lessons for the current schooling year. Such a weakening children's interest in study is being promoted by a number of objective and subjective factors. Among them one can see such as a formalistic attitude to the teaching in schools, low level professional skill of some teachers and at last financial difficulties arising in the transition period to a market. Last winter some 86 schools had to cease their studies due to the absence of heating supply to their schoolbuildings. For many of schools in the countryside there is a serious lack of teaching staff.
