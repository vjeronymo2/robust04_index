930923 FT 23 SEP 93 / Liberal Democrats at Torquay: Steel leads calls for UN reform LIBERAL Democrats backed a call by Sir David Steel, the party's foreign affairs spokesman, for a shake-up in the structure of the United Nations. Delegates agreed there should be reform of the permanent membership of the UN security council, but rejected a proposal requiring automatic seats for the European Community and Japan. A motion calling for the UN to develop peacemaking as well as peacekeeping machinery was endorsed as was the establishment of an international criminal court with a human rights commissioner within the UN. Sir David said: 'We must ensure that the UN itself is fully accountable for its actions or inactions.'
