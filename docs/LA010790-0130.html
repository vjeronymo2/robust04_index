January 7, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

BUCHANAN ON MONROE DOCTRINE

In trying to explain the United States illegal break-in of the Nicaraguan envoy's residence, President Bush said, " . . . Makes you wonder what our young men are up against down there" (Part A, Dec. 31). This from a man who sent 24,000 soldiers, armed with the most advanced killing tools, to a foreign country, which primarily accomplished the killing of Panamanians.

This is kinder and gentler? It sounds like something Adolph Hitler might have said when he sent his troops to Czechoslovakia.

RICHARD F. GETZ

San Diego
