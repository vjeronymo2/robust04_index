930907 FT 07 SEP 93 / Arts: Today's Television A PACKED evening, starting with lots of chess. On BBC2 Peter Snow reports on both World Chess Championships, the Short-Kasparov challenge beginning today in London under their own breakaway organisation, the Professional Chess Association, and the Netherlands match under the old FIDE (7.20). Channel 4, which calls itself 'host broadcaster' for the London match, provides coverage in World Chess at 8.00 and 11.45. BBC1 begins The Skipper a six-part documentary series about Roger Nowell, Newlyn fisherman 'and modern day buccaneer'. C4 begins a season devoted to DW Griffith: Father Of Film. Kevin Brownlow and David Gill, world's greatest champions of silent cinema, are showing restored versions of Birth Of A Nation, Broken Blossoms and Intolerance but they start tonight with a 60-minute documentary about Griffith himself. BBC2 begins Cracking The Code an eight-part series about the history of genetics (9.30) and at 10.20 shows the first in this year's 10 x 10 series of new short films. First Tuesday reports on a new age 'enlightenment' holiday on a Greek island (10.40 ITV).
