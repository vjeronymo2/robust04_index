Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities in Brazil monitored through 3 February. Sao Paulo industry grew 11.2 percent in 1993. The Sao Paulo State Federation of Industries reported that the Industrial Activity Index in December was 13.1 percent higher than the same month in 1992, and 1.2-percent higher than in November. The number of employed people in the industrial sector dropped 3.8 percent compared with the previous year. (Sao Paulo O ESTADO DE SAO PAULO in Portuguese 27 Jan 94 p B5) The Average General Price Index, IGP-M, which measures inflation according to the financial market, ended January with an inflation rate of 39.07 percent. The index is 0.75-percent higher than the December rate. The January inflation rate is the highest since March 1990. (Rio de Janerio Rede Globo Television in Portuguese 2200 GMT 31 Jan 94) The Bank do Brasil, BB, had a net profit of $388.8 million in 1993, lower than 1992's $446.7 million. The BB plans to auction its shares in 83 companies after Carnival on the Rio de Janeiro stock exchange. (Sao Paulo GAZETA MERCANTIL in Portuguese 1 Feb 94 p 1)
