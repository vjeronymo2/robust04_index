BFN [Text] San Jose, 27 Apr (ACAN-EFE) -- Jose Maria Figueres, the Costa Rican president-elect, rejected the possibility the country may enter the Central American Parliament (Parlacen) as long as differences on the issue still exist in his country. He also questioned Parlacen's "concrete results" in the almost three years it has been operating. In an interview published today in the newspaper LA REPUBLICA, Figueres, who is 37 years old and will assume control of the government on 8 May, said Parlacen is "an issue that has divided the country," and as long as this situation continues "I do not think it is prudent to continue the controversy." "We can do many things to become closer to the rest of the Central American countries without thinking about this parliament," said the next Costa Rican chief of state. He also said regional unity can be achieved "by fighting for just causes." According to Figueres, Central American unity does not depend on Parlacen but, rather, on a "frank, open, friendly, and unitarian" position of the countries in the isthmus, because "it is easier to be heard as a region than as a sole country." Figueres added, however, "the negative opinion many Costa Ricans have about Parlacen could change when we see concrete results," and he asked "what are the concrete results" of this parliament since it started operations? Parlacen, headquartered in Guatemala, was installed in October 1991 with deputies from Guatemala, Honduras, and El Salvador. Nicaragua, which has already approved its entry into the forum, has still not elected its deputies. Costa Rica and Panama have not even ratified the proposal to enter this organization.
