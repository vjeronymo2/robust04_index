920124 FT 24 JAN 92 / Parliament and Politics: Onslaught by Meacher over poverty IN A BLUNT address during an Opposition debate on poverty Mr Michael Meacher, the shadow social security secretary, tried to portray the government as one that neglected the poor, David Owen writes. For the first time since 1945, he said, more than 10m people were living at or below the income support level. It would be surprising if child benefit survived the end of a fourth Tory term in government, he continued. For the government, Mr Tony Newton, the social security secretary, pledged that the value of child benefit, retirement pensions and disability benefits would be protected against inflation. He forced an admission from Mr Meacher that a few individuals earning less than Pounds 21,000 a year might pay more in national insurance contributions under Labour. That might arise in cases where there were marked fluctuations in weekly income levels, Labour admitted. However, Mr Newton found it difficult to cite the social security select committee to support his claim that people at all levels of income distribution had become better off under the Tories. Mr Frank Field, Labour chairman of the committee, emphasised that the smallest gains under the Conservatives had gone to the poorest individuals.
