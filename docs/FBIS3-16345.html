Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Lyudvig Chibirov, the leader of South Ossetia (a self-proclaimed republic in Georgia -- Interfax) said that among the priority political problems facing the new parliament is holding talks with Georgia to define South Ossetia's political status. "We are ready to hold talks at an international level the way talks between Georgia and Abkhazia are held," he told Interfax on Tuesday. As to the idea of uniting South and North Ossetia, supported by the population of South Ossetia during the referendum in 1992, this question will be discussed in Moscow not in Tskhinval, he said. North Ossetia is part of Russia.
