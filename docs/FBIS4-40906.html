BFN [Text] Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev has told Interfax that he will sign not only the NATO Partnership for Peace program but also a protocol to another program, Russia-NATO, worked out by Russian officials in Brussels on June 22. Kozyrev will leave for Brussels on June 21. Kozyrev said his main purpose in Brussels was to sign the protocol to the Russia-NATO program. Kozyrev may leave Brussels for Corfu instead of Moscow in order to join Russian President Boris Yeltsin who will participate in the EU summit and sign an agreement on partnership and cooperation between Russia and the EU. A high-ranking official from the Russian Foreign Ministry's All- European Cooperation Department told Interfax that the Russia-NATO program transmitted to the NATO headquarters a month ago was a "sound document reflecting our vision of relations with NATO, Russia's place in Europe and in the world."
