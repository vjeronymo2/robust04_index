Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Chisinau, BASAPRESS, 1/2/1994 -- Alexandru Buruiana, chairman of Parliament Commission for Foreign Relations, back from an official visit to Strasbourg, told BASA that Council of Europe representatives he had met support the idea of a national poll in Moldova on its independence. "Experts of European structures believe that, though without juridical effect, such poll would not allow Tiraspol leaders invoke the reason of `eventual unification,'" Buruiana asserted. The Moldovan delegation met with Catherine Lalumiere, Secretary General of Council of Europe and discussed the Ilascu group problem, working out future steps for releasing the political prisoners. At meetings with top European dignitaries between January 19th and 29th, questions regarding Moldova's adherence to the Council of Europe were examined. Recommendations were issued, also regarding the enhancement of Moldova legislation in accordance with European standards, separation of state powers. The participation of Council of Europe observers in February 27th parliamentary elections was discusses, states a Parliament Press Service communique.
