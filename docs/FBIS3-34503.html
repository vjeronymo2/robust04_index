Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] New York, 8 Feb (INA) -- Iraq has informed the UN General Secretariat of U.S. aircraft violations of its airspace on provocation and reconnaissance missions. The aircraft, breaking the sound barrier, are also dropping hostile leaflets and flares. In a letter to UN Secretary General Butrus Butrus-Ghali, Iraq's permanent UN representative said between 21-31 January, the U.S. aircraft violated Iraq's airspace, conducting 719 sorties over wide areas in northern and southern Iraq. He pointed out that a red drone was seen in the Umm Qasr region, heading from west to east. Another drone, coming from Saudi Arabia, dropped leaflets between al-Shu'aybah and al-Wahdah Airport. He also confirmed that on 25 January, a drone was observed heading toward al-Dayr region. On 27 January, another drone was seen falling in the al-Barjasiyah region. Iraq's permanent UN representative added that two reconnaissance TR-1 planes were detected over al-Basrah, al-'Amarah, al-Nasiriyah, al-Diwaniyah, and southern al-Najaf, which then headed for Saudi territories. He added that on 28 January, a drone violated Iraq's airspace over al-Basrah, Safwan, al-Shu'aybah, Jabal Sinam, and returned to Kuwait again on the same day.
