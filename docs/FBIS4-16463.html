BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Igor Ignatyev] [Text] St. Petersburg April 17 TASS--The EBRD [European Bank for Reconstruction and Development] will grant a 10.3 million dollar credit to the Russian space industry which will be used for launching into space the European "Inmarsat" satellite by the Russian "Proton" booster rocket. The ceremony of signing this credit agreement in St. Petersburg was attended by Russian Prime Minister Viktor Chernomyrdin. The EBRD President Jacques de Larosiere described this agreement as an important and symbolic act. He pointed out that the agreement is an example of integration of Russia's high technologies into the international market. During the signing ceremony Russian Prime Minister Viktor Chernomyrdin described the agreement as the beginning of "a long road". "St. Petersburg is a window to Europe opening the way for our partners. We realize that much will depend on us, but we must create conditions to enable them to work normally," Chernomyrdin said.
