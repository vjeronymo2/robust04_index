Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Prague, March 16 (CTK) -- Professor Juraj Hrasko, member of the Slovak Academy of Sciences and an expert in agriculture, was today appointed environment minister of the new Slovak Government. He was proposed by the Democratic Left Party (SDL), of which he is a member. Hrasko was born on May 14, 1931 in Tocnice, south Slovakia. He graduated from the High School of Agriculture in Kharkov, former USSR. From 1955 he worked as a scientist in Czechoslovakia's Central Institute of Agricultural Inspection and Testing, in 1960 he became head of the Pedology Laboratory of the Czechoslovak Academy of Agricultural Sciences. Since 1968 he has directed the Bratislava-based Pedological Research Institute. In 1980 he was appointed a member of the Slovak Academy of Sciences. Hrasko focuses on comprehensive research, classification and development of soils, and organizes training of experts. In 1950 he joined the Communist Party of Czechoslovakia (KSC), now he is a member of the SDL, for which he was elected a deputy to the Slovak Parliament in the June 1992 elections.
