BFN [Text] Jordanian and Palestinian officials have resumed talks on the joint economic working plan agreed upon during yesterday's sessions between the both sides. The minister of information Dr. Jawad al-'Anani who is heading Jordan's delegation to the meetings said that the two sides will sign the economic plan agreement tomorrow. He added that the heads of the two sides will later hold a press conference about the results of their meetings. The joint committee met at the prime minister's office last night. Dr. al-'Anani told reporters after the meeting that the two sides discussed a number of issues dealing with banking, trade, customs, and tourism. The head of the Palestinian side to the meetings Ahmad Quray' said that subcommittees were set up and they will commence their work on Saturday to follow up the implementation of the agreement. He added that Jordan will be the first in basic reference due to the distinguished relations between the two brotherly countries.
