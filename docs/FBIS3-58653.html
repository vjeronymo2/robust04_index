Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] [Announcer] Foreign Minister Javier Solana is continuing his Far East tour. After visiting Japan, he is now in South Korea, where he has signed five economic cooperation agreements, two extradition agreements, and a cultural relations agreement. Thailand and Malaysia will be his next destinations. In statements to ``Spain at Seven'' [this newscast], the minister highlighted Spain's interest in this region because of its trading importance: [Begin Solana recording] We have made a package of agreements with a country that is of increasing importance in this region, that is growing greatly, and whose trade with Spain is also growing. In the last three years we have practically doubled our export rate to these countries, and to this particular country, to Korea, and we must maintain that pressure because they are, (?as I said,) countries that are of increasing importance in international trade. [end recording]
