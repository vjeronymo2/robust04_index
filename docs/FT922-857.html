920625 FT 25 JUN 92 / Lloyd's losses top 2bn LOSSES for the 1989 underwriting account increased to Pounds 2.06bn compared with Pounds 510m in 1988, according to preliminary unaudited figures released yesterday by Lloyd's, Richard Lapper writes. Underwriting losses in 1989 were Pounds 1.37bn, while Names' personal expenses amounted to Pounds 292m. An extra Pounds 398m was earmarked to strengthen reserves for claims expected to emerge on business underwritten in earlier years. Syndicates in the non-marine and marine market were worst hit with losses climbing to Pounds 1.76bn (Pounds 1.14bn) and Pounds 1.32bn (Pounds 821m) respectively. Syndicates in the aviation and motor markets made profits of Pounds 281m (Pounds 16m) and Pounds 597m (Pounds 52m) respectively. The worst losses were heavily concentrated among a minority of syndicates specialising in catastrophe reinsurance business. Five syndicates (255, 290, 298, 540 and 847) accounted for 34 per cent of the overall loss and two agencies accounted for 37 per cent of the loss - Gooda Walker with 25 per cent and Feltrim with 12 per cent. Lloyd's also gave details on the strength of its asset base last year. The premium trust funds - into which insurance premiums are paid - rose from Pounds 9.80bn to Pounds 12.25bn.
