BFN [Text] Cairo, April 18 (AFP)--The PLO has rejected an Israeli compromise proposal on the scope of Palestinian jurisdiction in parts of the occupied territories slated for Palestinian self-rule, the chief PLO negotiator said early Monday [18 April]. Nabil Sha'th was speaking at the opening session Sunday [as received] of the latest round of Israeli-PLO negotiations aimed at implementing Palestinian autonomy on the Gaza Strip and in the West Bank town of Jericho. He said Israel had proposed that Palestinian jurisdiction extend to all foreigners -- non-Israelis and non-Palestinians -- in Gaza and Jericho, apart from those who travel there to visit Jewish settlements. In another offer, according to Sha'th, Israel suggested that a Palestinian charged with a crime against an Israeli be tried before a Palestinian court if he is arrested in the autonomous areas and before an Israeli court if he escapes and is captured in Israel. Israel had initially wanted to maintain jurisdiction over all foreigners as well as over any Palestinian charged with a crime against an Israeli. "They tempered their first proposal, which I have called a horror," Sha'th said. "It is now a horror on a minature scale but still it is a horror." He charged that Israel's position on jurisdiction was "a clear violation of the Declaration of Principles," referring to an autonomy agreement signed last September by Israel and the Palestine Liberation Organization. "From our point of view, the only (areas) out of our jurisdiction are Israelis and settlements." He disclosed that an electronic surveillance system will likely be used to resolve the issue of Palestinian movement between Gaza and Jericho. The proposed route linking the two sites would extend from Eretz, bordering the Gaza Strip, and then pass through Ashqelon in southern Israel and Hebron on the West Bank, entering Jericho south of Jerusalem. Sha'th also said that Israeli and PLO negotiators had begun discussions Sunday on water sharing but declined to release details. But he added: "It's not really that much of a problem. It can be solved." Sha'th stressed that that PLO was still pressing for a general amnesty for all Palestinian prisoners held by Israel, estimated to number 10,000. Israel announced last Tuesday that it would agree to free 5,000 prisoners following the approval of a final autonomy accord, excluding Islamic militants in the Hamas movement. Hamas has claimed responsibility for two recent bomb blasts that killed 12 Israelis. "What we are discussing is a general amnesty," Sha'th said. "We cannot accept that Israelis tell us that people who are imprisoned because of their resistance in the past to an illegal occupation should remain in jail indefinitely, even after an agreement is signed."
