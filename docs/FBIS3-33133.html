Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] La Paz, 8 Feb (ANF) -- Social Communications Minister Hermann Antelo said today the fact that the government was unable to meet the hopes of the Bolivian people during the first six months of its administration is "quite explainable." He said: "The hopes the population had on the administration of President Gonzalo Sanchez de Lozada were so high when he was inaugurated last August that it was impossible to satisfy them all. Even if we brought in a magician to run the country, it would have been impossible to satisfy the demands immediately." A recent poll by the Bolivian Catholic University, UCB, shows the people's confidence in the government is decreasing as the government administration goes on. This situation also affects President Sanchez de Lozada, but to a lesser degree. Antelo said the first stage in any government is to bring order and make preparations. He added: "The harvest comes later. The people want immediate results; however, they also want immediate well-being, and that is impossible to do." He added that government actions will be recognized when the people are able to see the results of the great measures for change being implemented and under debate in Congress. Antelo said: "We understand the people's reaction. It is valid from their perspective. We believe we are doing out best to prepare the grounds for changing that perspective, not only in appearance but through the implementation of measures." According to the UCB poll, 62 percent of those polled have a bad impression of the government. Last October the bad impression was only 44.5 percent, which means that the discontent increased by 17.5 percent.
