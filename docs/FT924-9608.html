921105 FT 05 NOV 92 / The Maastricht Vote: Business welcomes outcome of vote MANY business organisations last night welcomed the government's narrow victory. Sir Michael Angus, president of the Confederation of British Industry, said: 'We welcome the outcome of the vote, which is what the business community wanted. 'We now look to the Edinburgh summit for further clarification of the important principle of subsidiarity. 'We hope that formal ratification by the House of Commons can proceed as soon as possible to resolve uncertainty about our position in Europe.' Mr Richard Brown, director of policy for the British Chambers of Commerce, said: 'Business will be breathing a huge sigh of relief tonight. 'The past two months have been very unsettled for the business community and this should put us back on a stable course for Europe and the single market.' But Mr Peter Morgan, director general of the Institute of Directors, sounded a note of caution: 'As it stands, the treaty is an imperfect document of many contradictions. There is an urgent need for more information about the practical effects of the treaty on UK business.'
