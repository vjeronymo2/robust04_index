Language: Belarusian Article Type:BFN [Text] I will either not vote at all or vote against it, because nobody has defined the subject of the voting, former Supreme Soviet Chairman Stanislav Shushkevich told an RID correspondent immediately after receiving his ballot for voting on three chapters of the Belarusian draft constitution. The point is that this is the wrong way to vote. It would be better to vote on separate chapters, and even better on separate articles. If the voting concerned separate articles, I would approve about 20 of them, the ex-speaker added. Asked why he did not join the statement signed by 40 deputies who refused to participate in the voting, Stanislav Shushkevich responded that he was simply not informed.
