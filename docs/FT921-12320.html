920128 FT 28 JAN 92 / Cost plea by steelmakers SHEFFIELD'S steelmaking and engineering companies have asked the government to cushion the shock of what they say will be 20 per cent increases in electricity prices from April, Ian Hamilton Fazey writes. Some say they have experienced 40 per cent rises, and higher costs are forcing them out of tightening markets. In an open letter to Mr John Major, the prime minister, manufacturers in a 30-mile radius of Sheffield suggest that the government could relieve Sheffield's intensive industrial electricity consumers of the fossil fuel levy. Professor Stephen Littlechild, the generating industry's regulator, has criticised some aspects of pricing.
