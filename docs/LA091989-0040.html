September 19, 1989, Tuesday, Home Edition

MORNING REPORT: LEGAL FILE

A former television anchorman in Erie, Pa., has filed suit against his station, charging he was demoted because of his age. Hyle Richmond, 62, filed suit in U.S. District Court against WICU-TV and its parent company, Great Lakes Communications Inc., seeking reinstatement to his job as an on-air newscaster and anchorman. The suit claims that Richmond, after more than 30 years on the air, was demoted to a reporter's job on Sept. 28, 1987, and was replaced by Lisa Adams, who is in her early 30s. It further says the station also fired or demoted three other on-air newscasters or anchors who were more than 40 and replaced them with younger employees. Richmond is still employed by the station. ALEENE MacMINN
