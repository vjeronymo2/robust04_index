BFN [Text] Zagreb, April 7 (AFP) -- A series of explosions ripped through a munitions dump outside the Croatian capital Thursday [7 April], prompting the authorities to place the surrounding suburbs under alert and stop all traffic. The dump was ablaze and shells were exploding every few seconds, eyewitnesses said. Residents near the munitions depot in Sesvete, about 12 kilometres (seven miles) outside Zagreb, were told to take refuge in bomb-shelters. There were no immediate reports of casualties but Sesvete police told AFP they expected that people had been injured.
