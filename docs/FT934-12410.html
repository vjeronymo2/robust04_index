931027 FT 27 OCT 93 / Civil servants agree day of action FIVE civil service trade unions have voted for a one-day strike to protest at the government's market testing and privatisation policies. The unions, which represent about 300,000 staff, said the day of action on November 5 would be the biggest seen in the civil service since 1981. The action is likely to disrupt, but unlikely to halt, a wide range of activities, from unemployment benefit offices to Customs checks. Unions said yesterday that the objective was not so much to disrupt services as to draw the public's attention to market testing. The unions claim market testing, which compares the cost of services provided by the public and private sectors, is the first step towards full privatisation and a central part of the government's plans to 'sell off as much of the civil and public services as it can'. Leaders of the unions - the CPSA, NUCPS, the Inland Revenue Staff Federation, the Northern Ireland Public Services Alliance and the Scottish Prison Officers Association - described the vote in favour of action as 'overwhelming'. The combined total was 105,560 in favour and 64,987 against. The Treasury, which is responsible for civil service pay and management, said: 'We would regret any decision by the unions to proceed with this unnecessary action.'
