BFN [Text] The former vice-president Aleksandr Rutskoy was summoned to the office of the Moscow Prosecutor on Thursday. This was reported to Interfax by a member of the Duma and the leader of the People's Party of Free Russia Vasiliy Lipitskiy. The office of the Prosecutor General has given instructions to the Moscow Prosecutor to check the lawfulness of a number of statements and calls by opposition leaders during mass actions on May 9 in Moscow, including calls to destroy the current regime, and to evaluate the burning of the Russian flag in public. Other summons have been sent to the leader of Working Russia Victor Anpilov, the head of the National Salvation Front Ilya Konstantinov who together with Rutskoy were released from jail last February under an amnesty of the Duma.
