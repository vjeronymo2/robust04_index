920527 FT 27 MAY 92 / Sweden forecasts a slow recovery Sweden has reached the bottom of its three-year slump in manufacturing output, but the country's economic recovery is going to be slow and weak, according to the latest forecast published yesterday by the Confederation of Swedish Industries, writes Robert Taylor in Stockholm. The employers' organisation said it would take Swedish industry until the end of the decade to return to the levels of production it achieved in 1989. Yesterday, Sweden's central bank announced a sharp deterioration in the country's balance of payments deficit in March to SKr3.3bn.
