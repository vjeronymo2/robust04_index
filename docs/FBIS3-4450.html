Language: Chinese Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, 22 Mar (XINHUA) -- While talking about his impressions of his trip to China, Japanese Prime Minister Morihiro Hosokawa said today at a social gathering attended by his cabinet members after a cabinet meeting that his meetings with Chinese leaders are of great significance. Hosokawa said that, during his visit to China, he not only discussed with Chinese leaders the issues of bilateral relations between Japan and China, but also conducted significant meetings with them on a wide variety of subjects such as U.S.-China relations and human rights. He stressed that, through this trip, he has made China understand that the current Japanese Government attaches importance to China. Hosokawa pointed out that China has achieved remarkable development, and its market is very important to Japan's economy. Meanwhile, he held that Japan is relatively slow in entering this great market in China.
