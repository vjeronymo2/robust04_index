September 11, 1990, Tuesday, P.M. Final

LIZ SMITH: GEOGRAPHY SEPARATES BARBARA WALTERS, MERV ADELSON

IT IS WITH great sadness that Barbara Walters and Merv Adelson are announcing that they will be having a trial separation. For the last five years, this glamorous and very nice couple has lived a high-intensity, bi-coastal wedlock, with each traveling from coast-to-coast at least once a month.

But now, Merv's business interests require his spending most of his time in Los Angeles and Barbara's television assignments, instead of lessening, seem to have multiplied so that she is traveling all over the world or operating primarily from New York. The twain meets -- but the effort is gigantic.

Merv has always had, and continues to have, total respect for Barbara's career and has never suggested she give it up. But when he sold his company, Lorimar Telepictures, to Warner Brothers, his business requirements changed and he was not in New York as much as he had been in the past.

The many friends of the couple on both coasts know that Merv and Barbara are still deeply devoted to each other. It sounds like a cliche, but I believe they will remain the closest of friends.

And, being the incurable romantic that I am, I personally hope they will find that their separation isn't worth the separation.

PATTI DAVIS isn't going to give up life just because she isn't Mommy and Daddy's pet kid. The daughter of Nancy and Ronald Reagan has just signed with Carol Publishing for her second novel. She is about three-quarters of the way through the new fiction. It will tell the story of a woman's struggle with the issue of motherhood (does she want it or not?), and it will contain flashbacks of the protagonist's own childhood. "Smoke Signals" is the working title for this, and it is due next year.

Patti has also written a book for children coming from Lowell House this fall. "Blue and Darby" is about two dolphins. Conventional wisdom has it that the Reagans have more or less washed their hands of their rebellious Patti precisely because of her pro-dolphin/anti-tuna nets crusade, which seemed critical of her father's policies when he occupied the White House.

So maybe you'd like to buy Patti's book if you are a dolphin lover -- as a gesture of loyalty. Remember, it's tough being an orphan, especially when your parents are living.

ENDQUOTE: Sandra Bernhard gives lots of "attitude" in her recent profile by People magazine. And her fans like it that way, say the editors. Asked constantly throughout about her friendship with Madonna, Sandra gets rather definite: "If Madonna and I were really having an affair, I don't think we would be talking about it in public. She's a nice friend, and I have a great time with her. She has a lot of money and takes me out to dinner. . . . If I was really having an affair with her, do you think I'd be here? I'd be in Japan spending all her money. If I'm with a man, it's because I'm with a man. If I'm with a woman, you know, every woman that I'm seen with I'm not sleeping with. And I'm not sleeping with every man, either. Anything goes in my life. I'm an adventurous person, and I'm also a very sexual person, and I like interacting with a lot of people. I think it's boring to be stuck in one genre.

"I'm not a lesbian, and I'm sick of being called one. I'm not, and I want to set the record straight. . . . I'm going to blow everybody's minds. I'm going to get married and belong to Hadassah!"
