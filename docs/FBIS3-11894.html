Language: Portuguese Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] Rio de Janeiro JORNAL DO BRASIL in Portuguese on 18 March publishes on page 4 a 700-word unattributed article on the results of a survey showing current voting preferences for the different presidential candidates. The article notes that the Brazilian Public Opinion and Statistics Institute, Ibope, interviewed 2,000 voters throughout the country between 18 and 22 February. According to the results of this poll, Luis Inacio Lula da Silva leads the pack with 29 percent of the votes; Paulo Maluf and Fernando Henrique Cardoso come in tied for second with 11 percent of the votes each; Leonel Brizola and Antonio Carlos Magalhaes are third with 8 percent each; and Orestes Quercia and Alvaro Dias come in fourth with 4 percent of the votes. The article also notes that Lula "has well-distributed support, in cities and rural areas, in the north and south, and among rich and poor." The article also points out that Lula has a high rejection rate, while Cardoso has the lowest rejection rate among all presidential candidates, "which will become very important in the runoff election."
