September 15, 1990, Saturday, Home Edition

FACES . . . : CHACONAS, CABLE'S KING OF TACK, BRINGS 'MR. PETE' TO THE MASSES

Peter Chaconas started his television career on a dare from friends.

Paying $35 per half-hour show, Chaconas signed up for the minimum nine-show run on Century Cable's public access channel in 1987, and launched "Take a Break With Mr. Pete." The Mr. Pete persona has gained wider exposure ever since, with appearances by Roseanne Barr and Steve Allen highlighting the cable program. Mr. Pete covered the Emmy awards show for a San Francisco TV station and the Zsa Zsa Gabor trial for Pat Sajak's defunct late-night talk show.

Chaconas' first broadcast television program, "The Late Mr. Pete Show," airs Sunday nights at 11:30 on KTLA-TV Channel 5. The show, which runs for three more weeks, is a testing ground for Chaconas' goal of national syndication. The first attempt to sell it will happen early next year at the National Assn. of Television Program Executives' syndication marketplace in New Orleans. For Chaconas, the operative word in his public access career has been tacky.

"The old shows are really incredibly tacky, which public access television is," he said. "It's just total tack. I just proclaimed myself the king of public access television, the king of all this tack."

The KTLA shows, although slicker and free of the mistakes that made for "half the fun" of the public access shows, are consciously offbeat. The set is a garish mix of orange and checkerboard black-and-white, the house band plays zydeco music, and Mr. Pete wears a plaid dinner jacket, sunglasses and a bolo tie with his mustachioed face on it.

Keeping to his pledge of being nothing more than a regular guy on television, he announces his address on the air and plugs the Authentic Cafe, where he still works as a waiter.

"I get recognized all over town these days, and people tell me they feel I'm going to be famous," he says. "I get that all the time; it's a weird thing to have strangers say that. And it's a weird thing to be able to say about yourself." PHIL WEST
