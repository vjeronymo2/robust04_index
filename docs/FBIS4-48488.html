BFN [Excerpts] N'Dalatando is facing a critical situation. The Popular Movement for the Liberation of Angola [MPLA] is bent on decimating the residents of N'Dalatando, using its air force and long-range artillery pieces. About 28 air raids were carried out on 18 May. The MPLA used TC- 7's, MiG-23's, and Su's. More than 70 bombs with substances banned internationally were being dropped on the city and outlaying areas daily. [passage omitted] A terrible MPLA military offensive is under way in Cuanza Sul. The four-pronged offensive was launched from Waku-Kungo and Gabela and is designed to capture the District of Ebo. The offensive, which began on 16 May 1994, has been characterized by massive air force attacks and artillery shelling. Violent clashes have been taking place with the UNITA [National Union for the Total Independence of Angola] armed forces defending the people from the MPLA adventure. People's Armed Forces for the Liberation of Angola [FAPLA] units have been left to their own devices. A number of FAPLA members have surrendered to UNITA. Cadet Officer Daniel Caieba, a native of (Cassongue), surrendered to UNITA on 17 May. [passage omitted] The communist regime of Jose Eduardo dos Santos is bent on resuming war in the city of Bie. Lethal equipment for MPLA troops was unloaded in Bie at approximately 0625 on 18 May. Flying from Luanda, a IL-776 military aircraft dropped eight parachutes, but all of them fell in the sector of the city controlled by UNITA. Five parachutes had grenades, large quantities of AK-47's, AGS 17 ammunition belts, and AK-47 ammunition boxes. The other three parachutes had Racal-30 communications radios. [passage omitted]
