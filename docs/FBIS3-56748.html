Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Oleg Velichko] [Text] Moscow, January 19 (TASS) -- Arkadiy Volskiy, president of the Union of Industrialists and Entrepreneurs, whose Civic Union Bloc lost parliamentary elections completely, accused Yegor Gaydar, the architect of Russian reforms, of trying to escape responsibility for the failure of reforms. The resignation of Gaydar "is an attempt to avoid responsibility for failures in economic reforms and a desire to put the burden on the shouldres of those people who could not allow themseleves the luxury to abandon the leadership in the difficult moment", Volskiy told a meeting of regional leaders of his union on Tuesday. He also said that the December 12 elections had shown that the Russian people "do not accept unreasoned experiments in politics and economy". According to Volskiy, Russia now faces two alternatives: whether to join industrially developed nations or remain their raw materials appendage with cheap resources and skilled manpower. First Deputy Prime Minister Oleg Soskovets addressed the meeting and said he would "strengthen constructive cooperation" with the union."
