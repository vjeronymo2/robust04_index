BFN [Text] Bucharest ROMPRES, 18/4/1994 -- "The talks between the foreign ministers of Romania and Armenia pointed to the necessity of concluding the soonest a basic political treaty between the two countries," said Romanian Foreign Ministry Spokesman Mircea Geoana at the close of the first round of talks between Teodor Melescanu and Vahan Papazyan. It is hoped that in a near future that important document will be signed during a meeting of the two countries' presidents. The parties informed each other on the situation in Trans- Caucasian and the conflict in former Yugoslavia. Papazyan referred to the conflict in Nagorno Karabakh and outlined the latest developments and initiatives for its settlement. Teodor Melescanu outlined Romania's main foreign policy options. He presented the stage of Romania's relations with the NATO, the European Union, and the neighboring states. During the talks the parties agreed to expand the relations of cooperation in the international bodies, especially the CSCE and the UN, and at a subregional level, with reference to the Black Sea area.
