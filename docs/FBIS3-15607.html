Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Oleg Kuzmin] [Text] Tehran March 7 TASS -- The leaders of the Tajik opposition stationed on Afghan territory have agreed to direct negotiations with the Dushanbe government in Moscow, given guarantees of their personal safety and presence of observers from the United Nations, Russia, Afghanistan, Iran and Pakistan. This was announced by opposition delegation leader Khodzhiakbar Turadzhonzodah [name as received] told ITAR-TASS after winding up talks with Russian First Deputy Foreign Minister Anatoliy Adamishin here. The Tajik opposition leader said he and other opposition leaders were prepared to leave for Moscow to meet with Dushanbe representatives. Anatoliy Adamishin confirmed what Turadzhonzodah said and stressed that talks in Tehran "resulted in a substantial step" towards the political settlement of the conflict in Tajikistan. Negotiations on the political settlement of the conflict in Tajikistan were the main objective of Anatoliy Adamishin's visit to the Islamic Republic of Iran.
