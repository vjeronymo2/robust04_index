Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS parliamentary correspondent Ivan Novikov] [Text] Moscow March 9 TASS -- Parliamentary hearings, a form of parliamentary work which withstood the test of time and without which lawmaking in reformed Russia cannot exist, are being held increasingly often by deputies of the Russian State Duma. In mid-March, Russian lawmakers are scheduled to hold debates on problems of reform of the local government in the Russian Federation. Apart from lawmakers, parliamentary hearings will be attended by representatives of the president's administration, the Central Electoral Commission, bodies of state power of the subjects of the Federation and local governments. Parliamentary hearings on "efficient social policy under conditions of economic reforms" are expected to be held at about the same time and will be attended by members of the government, heads of ministries and departments, representatives of trade unions and scientists. Parliamentary hearings on the concept of "Russia's transition to a model of stable development" are scheduled for the second half of March. These debates will be held by parliamentary committees for the use of nature and natural resources and ecology. Russian mps are expected to hold parliamentary debates on the results of privatisation of state-run and municipal enterprises in the Russian Federation in the period between 1992-1993. During these debates Russian lawmakers will discuss a state programme of privatisation of state and municipal enterprises in Russia. These debates will be organized by the Duma Committee for Property, Privatisation and Economic Activities.
