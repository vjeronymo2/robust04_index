940504 FT 04 MAY 94 / Survey of Greater Philadelphia (12): To complete the Philadelphia story KEY FACTS: A GUIDE FOR THE VISITOR Airport: Philadelphia International Airport tel (215) 937-6800 8 miles south-west of downtown. A Septa (Southeastern Pennsylvania Transport Authority) rail line connects the airport with three Center City stations. The trip takes approximately 25 minutes and costs Dollars 5. Airport shuttles and limousines take 20-40 minutes to downtown, and fares start at Dollars 8. Call ahead to make reservations. Airport Limelight Limousine (215) 342-5557, Deluxe Transportation Company (215) 463-8787, Philadelphia Airport Shuttle (215) 969-1818. A taxi ride to downtown will cost about Dollars 20 excluding gratuities. Airlines: Those serving Philadelphia include: American Airlines (800) 443-7300, British Airways (215) 492-2460, Continental Airlines (215) 592-8005, Delta Airlines (215) 928-1700 and United Airlines (800) 241-6522. Car Hire: Alamo (215) 492-3960, Avis (215) 365-3600, Budget (215) 492-9442, Dollar (215) 365-2700 and Hertz (215) 492-7200. Taxi Services: Cabs in Philadelphia tend to be more expensive than in many US cities. They can be found at designated taxi stops or hailed on the street. The main cab companies are Quaker City Cab (215) 728-8000, United Cab (215) 238-9500, and Yellow Cab (215) 922-8400. Rail: Philadelphia is served by Amtrak at 30th Street station (at Market Street), Suburban station at 16th street and JFK Boulevard and Market East station at 11th and Market streets (215) 824-1600. Septa's regional rail lines provide service to the outerlying areas of the city and the suburbs from the same three center city stations.
