BFN [Text] Istanbul, June 9 (A.A.) -- President Suleyman Demirel on Thursday [9 June] said that Azerbaijan has suffered great losses from the Armenian invasion, adding responsible countries should not remain spectators to this situation. He called it a crime against humanity that the country should be left in this situation adding that 1.2 million people have become refugees and that a very harsh winter has led to heavy losses. Demirel said that it is unacceptable that a country seizes another country's territories by force and in this situation it is impossible that Turkey establish good relations with the invading country, Armenia. He said Turkey will take steps to bring peace in the region and will continue to voice the injustices done to Azerbaijan, adding he will take up the issues during his meeting with U.S. Secretary of State Warren Christopher. Azerbaijani President Geydar Aliyev said that a number of agreements have been signed with two friendly countries Turkey and Azerbaijan and that bilateral high level meetings are also contributing to the development of relations.
