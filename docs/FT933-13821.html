930716 FT 16 JUL 93 / Parliament and Politics: Caterer settles libel action DOWNING STREET caterer Ms Clare Latimer settled her libel action against Scallywag magazine yesterday, leaving the prime minister to continue his legal battle alone. Scallywag immediately threw down the gauntlet and challenged Mr John Major to a full-scale libel trial. Mr David Price, the magazine's solicitor, said: 'The first law of libel is that you should not start something you cannot finish. We will see if Mr Major is prepared to meet my clients in court.' Miss Latimer said the defendants could not bring themselves to apologise and they had no means to meet any claims for damages or costs. She added: 'In the circumstances, as they agreed to undertake to the court not to repeat the allegations, I am content to have reached a settlement.' Mr David Hooper, Mr Major's solicitor, confirmed that the prime minister's action against the magazine was unaffected by the settlement with Miss Latimer and was continuing.
