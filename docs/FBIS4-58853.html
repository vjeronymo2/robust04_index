BFN [Text] A responsible source at the Aden branch of the Ministry of Interior and Security has said in a statement to the YEMENI NEWS AGENCY, SABA'', that life in the economic and commercial capital, Aden, is proceeding normally, in spite of a few desperate attempts to subject the city to air bombardment from time to time -- in order to attack certain vital economic installations and civil institutions -- which have been unleashed by aircraft belonging to the bloody gang. The source stressed that the Aden branch of the ministry had taken all necessary measures and precautions to assure the freedom and safety of the citizens, and to ensure their security and livelihoods. These include civil defense measures and the securing of sources of water and supplies. The source called on all the citizens to adhere to security and stability, and to persevere with their normal daily activities. The source said that public and private supplies and consumer installations would continue to render services. Such installations include restaurants, gas stations, butane gas depots, and the like. The source went on to say that the Aden branch of the Ministry of Cooperatives, the municipality, and other relevant quarters would apply stern measures against anyone attempting to incite citizens, hoard foodstuffs or consumer goods, or stir up disputes concerning supplies. Such measures would include the withdrawal of trading licenses from business concerns, bakeries, or restaurants which closed their doors to the needs of the citizens to prevent them from having access to their products. Concluding the statement, the source called on all citizens to cooperate with the police and to report any phenomena liable to undermine the stability of their security or livelihood.
