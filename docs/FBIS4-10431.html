BFN [From the "Early Morning" newscast] [Text] The meeting of Inter-American Development Bank [IDB] governors has begun in Guadalajara. They talked about economic growth and the fight against poverty as requirements for political stabilization and a more just society. Patricia Gutierrez has filed a report: [Begin recording] [Gutierrez] As the 20th century comes to an end and as we approach the threshold of a new millennium, no country is immune to events that shape politics and economics at a world level. This is what President Carlos Salinas de Gortari said as he opened the IDB governors assembly. [Salinas] As we look forward, we must start facing the challenges common to all nations: the head-on fight against poverty; a solution to the world dilemma of creating and ensuring employment; the construction of a strong and lasting democracy; the protection and preservation of our natural and cultural resources; and the endless fight against a criminal force--drug trafficking. [Gutierrez] Before the 46 IDB governors and hundreds of representatives from financial institutions and governments of many nations, the Mexican head of state stressed his commitment to contributing to the creation of more just societies, more efficient economies, and stronger and more democratic institutions. Pedro Aspe, finance and public credit secretary who also attended the meeting, said there are no deviations from the path the country has chosen to follow. He said: We believe investors will continue to have a long-term vision to ensure the viability and permanence of economic recovery. He maintained national unity prevailed during the painful, difficult, and unexpected situations Mexico experienced during the first few months of the year. [end recording]
