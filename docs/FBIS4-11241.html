BFN [Report by Cecilia Pachano; from "The News" newscast] [Text] Doctor Gustavo Roosen has assumed the task of revitalizing the Venezuelan banking system, after being sworn in today at Miraflores Palace as presidential commissioner for the reform of the financial system. Roosen will exercise the office simultaneously with his current position as chairman of the Board of Trustees of Banco Latino. In a lengthy news conference held after being sworn in, Roosen presented his six-point plan to rescue the national banking system: reaching a solidarity accord with private banks, strengthening the Superintendency of Banks, reorganizing Fogade [Deposit Guarantee and Banking Protection Fund], reducing nominal interest rates and the differential between nominal and real interest rates, defining new financial instruments to replace the zero-coupon bonds, and establishing a plan of rehabilitation or major surgery for banks assisted by Fogade. [Begin Roosen recording] If we are able to strike a pact of solidarity regarding what I mentioned in my initial explanation, the country will see a change and will see a restoration in confidence, which was key in the case of Banco Latino. I am convinced by the conversations I held yesterday morning and last night, which prompted me to accept this difficult task, that the government is ready, the Central Bank is ready, and, as a result of my contacts with fellow bankers as chairman of the Board of Trustees, I am convinced the banks are also ready for this solidarity pact. This is key to eliminating the uncertainty that affects the financial system at this time. [end recording] Dr. Gustavo Roosen said that meetings with the private banks will begin this weekend in an attempt to sign the solidarity accord. A 72-hour deadline was set for its signing.
