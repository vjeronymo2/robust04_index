BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondents Tamara Zamyatina and Vladimir Mostovets] [Text] Helsinki, 18 Apr -- Russo-Finnish relations, prospects for Russia's entry in the Council of Europe, and settlement of the Bosnia-Herzegovina conflict were topical at today's meeting between Russia's State Duma Chairman Ivan Rybkin and Finnish Foreign Minister Heikki Haavisto. Ivan Rybkin is visiting Finland at the head of a Russian parliamentary delegation. During the discussion the two sides expressed concern over mounting tensions in Bosnia and voiced their common viewpoint view that the conflict in former Yugoslavia must be settled through negotiations and not through the use of force. Ivan Rybkin stressed that any use of military force can only be permitted with UN approval and following mandatory consultations with all permanent members of the UN Security Council. While discussing Russia's entry into the Council of Europe, the State Duma chairman and the Finnish foreign minister noted that this step would be in the interests of the entire region, since Russia cannot be sidelined in European integration process. Heikki Haavisto reaffirmed Finland's commitment to contribute toward Russia's speedy acceptance into the Council of Europe.
