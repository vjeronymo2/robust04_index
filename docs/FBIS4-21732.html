BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on insurgent activities in Peru compiled from Peruvian media monitored through 31 March. Security forces from the Political-Military Command of the Fifth Security Sub-Zone arrested 18 alleged Shining Path ``terrorists'' in Puno Department. Some of the main leaders under arrest are Luis Mamani Alvarez, ``Comrade Cleto;'' Leovigildo Efrain Jara Hualla, ``Comrade Carlos;'' Estanislao Flabio Mamani Gutierrez, ``Comrade Cesar;'' Serapio Aguilar Pari, ``Comrade Raul;'' Juan Pedro Quispe Mamani, ``Comrade Jorge;'' and Natividad Quispe Butron. (Lima EL COMERCIO in Spanish 19 Mar 94 p A12) One soldier was killed and three civilians seriously wounded in a 17 March clash between a military patrol and more than 100 Shining Path members who attacked the Puente Duran settlement, Huanuco Province, which is located 65 km south of Tingo Maria. A military spokesman said the subversives tried to take over the settlement to get food. (Lima LA REPUBLICA in Spanish 20 Mar 94 p 19) About 186 peasants who were recruited five months ago by Shining Path members and were staying in the ``Sello de Oro'' camp decided to surrender to the Army in Santa Rosa, Ayacucho Department. They are being held in the Pichari garrison in the Apurimac River valley about 220 km northeast of Ayacucho. (Lima EL COMERCIO in Spanish 24 Mar 94 p A14)
