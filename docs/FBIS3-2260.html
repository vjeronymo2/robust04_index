Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Belgrade, March 7 (XINHUA) -- The first group of the UN Protection Force (UNPROFOR) soldiers backed by tanks today took control of Tuzla military airport which is expected to be opened soon. Some 100 Norwegian soldiers riding in 17 armored personnel carriers and six Danish tanks, entered the base to replace Bosnia's Muslim troops, who are to officially hand over control of the airport at noon Tuesday. Located 15 kilometers from Tuzla city, the airport has been the biggest military one in former Yugoslavia with two runways, one of which is 2,400 meters long. The airport is capable of receiving all types of aircraft. The airport has been closed for more than 10 months. Under an accord reached by Bosnian Serb and Russian leaders, the Serbs agreed to reopen it on condition of using it only for humanitarian purposes. About 300 Swedish soldiers will be deployed there to protect the runways.
