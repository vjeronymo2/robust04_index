March 5, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

CURRENT PAPERBACKS: THOUSAND PIECES OF GOLD, A BIOGRAPHICAL NOVEL BY RUTHANNE LUM MCCUNN (BEACON PRESS, BOSTON: $8.95)

Ruthanne Lum McCunn has woven an enthralling work of fiction from the true life story of Lalu Nathoy (known later as Polly Bemis), a Chinese pioneer who settled the American Northwest.

Lalu was her father's favorite, his qianjin, or "thousand pieces of gold." But, after his crop of winter wheat fails because of drought in 1871, he sells the teen-age Lalu to a band of raiding bandits for two sacks of soybeans.

The bandit chief subsequently sells her to a brothel in Shanghai for 30,000 in taels. "Normally, I would not be able to buy a scrawny, dark-skinned girl with feet like dragon boats," the madam tells Lalu, "but today I have a special buyer who does not care about such things, someone who will take you to America."

Traveling from San Francisco to Portland, Lalu is then brought by mule to the mining community of Warrens: Hong King, an aging Chinese saloon-keeper, has bought her to draw business, and renames her Polly.

Later the white saloon-keeper next door, Charlie Bemis ("no one dares mess with him, he can keep a tin can dancing in the air with his six-shooter"), becomes her protector and eventually wins her from Hong King in a poker game -- and gives her her freedom.
