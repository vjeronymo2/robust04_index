Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Speaking to reporters at Nandial yesterday, Mr. Narasimha Rao said the government is taking all steps necessary to combat terrorism in Jammu and Kashmir and that elections will be held there once a conducive atmosphere prevails. [Begin Rao recording] I said we will have to deal with terrorism, transborder terrorism, exported terrorism, all that is going on there. We have to control it, create conditions of law and order so that we could proceed toward a democratic process, which that particular state has not had for some time. [end recording] Political activity has gained momentum in Jammu and Kashmir with the former chief minister, Dr. Farooq Abdullah, returning to the state after a stay in the United Kingdom. On his arrival in Jammu today, Dr. Abdullah held detailed discussions with senior party colleagues and reviewed the political situation. Talking to newsmen, Dr. Abdullah said that he will reactivate the Jammu and Kashmir National Conference in the state. He said New Delhi has succeeded in exporting to the international community Pakistan's role in aiding and abetting terrorism in the state. Dr. Abdullah, however, said a lot still needs to be done to fail Pakistan's designs on Kashmir.
