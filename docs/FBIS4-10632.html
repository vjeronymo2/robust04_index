BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities in Paraguay through 14 April. According to the Central Bank, BCP, the drop in March's inflation rate, which stood at 1.9 percent, shows that inflation will continue its downward rate. The BCP believes the measures it adopted, namely a strict control of monetary policy, and the adjustment policy implemented by the government were instrumental in curbing the inflationary spiral. (Asuncion ABC COLOR in Spanish 6 Apr 94 p 16) Paraguay owed more than $566 million to the Paris Club by 31 December 1993. This represents 46.5 of Paraguay's foreign debt. Paraguay's largest creditor in the Paris Club is Japan, with which the debt totals about $260 million. Paraguay's second-largest club creditor is Germany, with more than $134 million, followed by France with $63 million. Fifty-one percent of the foreign debt involves multilateral organizations. The Inter-American Development Bank is the largest creditor, with $429 million, followed by the World Bank, with about $150 million. Paraguay's foreign debt totaled $1.217 billion by late 1993. (Asuncion ABC COLOR in Spanish 10 Apr 94 Economic Supplement p 3) According to information from the General Customs Directorate, Paraguayan exports totaled $172 million during the first quarter this year, which represents a 32 percent increase compared with the same period last year. Soybean exports totaled $78,477,750; lumber, $17,169,061; leather and hides, $11,905,205; cotton, $8,596,638; frozen meat, $8,067,565; essential oil, $2,027,979; meat, $1,014,856; and coffee, $526,500. Paraguay's main purchasers were the Netherlands, with 32.6 percent; Brazil, 23.5 percent; the United States and Argentina, 7.6 percent; and Bermuda, 4.4 percent. (Asuncion ULTIMA HORA in Spanish 14 Apr 94 p 21)
