Language: Russian Article Type:BFN ["Draft Decree of the Federal Assembly State Duma on an Amnesty in Connection With the Adoption of the Russian Federation Constitution"; published under "Official Section" rubric] [Text] In connection with the adoption of the Russian Federation Constitution, and guided by the principle of humanity, the Federal Assembly State Duma decrees: 1. That the following be released from penalties taking the form of imprisonment, as well as from penalities other than imprisonment: a) convicted persons who have served in field armies or combined units or participated in combat actions in defense of the motherland or in the course of performing service duties connected with this; b) women; c) men over 60 years of age; d) first and second category invalids. 2. That persons sentenced for crimes of negligence to terms of imprisonment up to and inclusive of five years who have served at least one-third of the specified term of punishment be released from penalty. 3. That male minors condemned to terms of imprisonment up to and inclusive of three years and who have not previously served time in corrective-labor colonies be released from penalty. 4. That persons on probation, persons on suspended sentences, and persons sentenced to punishments other than imprisonment be released from penalty. 5. That persons sentenced for premeditated crimes to terms of imprisonment up to and inclusive of three years who have served at least one-third of the specified term of punishment be released from penalty. 6. That all criminal proceedings pending examination by the courts should be terminated where they relate to crimes committed prior to the entry into force of the decree by: a) persons listed in Paragraph 1, and persons who do not fall within the remit of Paragraph 8 of the decree; b) persons who have committed a crime for which punishments other than imprisonment are envisaged; c) male minors who have committed crimes for which a punishment of no more than three years' imprisonment is stipulated and who have not previously served time in corrective-labor colonies. In cases concerning crimes for which punishment in the form of imprisonment is envisaged but which were committed before the entry into force of the decree, it is decreed that the court, if it deems it necessary to specify a penalty other than imprisonment, release the convicted person from penalty. 7. That time yet to be served be reduced: a) by one-half for male minors who do not fall within the remit of Paragraph 1, 2, or 3 of the decree, and for persons convicted to a term of imprisonment for crimes of negligence who do not fall within the remit of Paragraph 2 of the decree; b) by one-third for persons sentenced for premeditated crimes to terms of imprisonment up to and inclusive of five years and who do not fall within the remit of Paragraphs 1, 3, and 5 of this decree; c) by one-quarter for persons convicted of premeditated crimes to terms of imprisonment of over five years, and who do not fall within the remit of Paragraph 1 of the decree. 8. That the validity of Paragraphs 1 through 7 of the decree does not extend to the following convicted persons: a) those who have committed crimes under Articles 64, 66, 67, 68, 74, 77, 77.1, 79, 86, 87; Section 2 of Article 88; Sections 3 and 4 of Article 89; Sections 2, 3, and 4 of Article 90; Article 91; Section 3 of Article 92; Sections 2 and 3 of Article 93; Articles 93.1, 95, 102, 103, 108; Sections 2, 3, and 4 of Article 117; Section 1 of Article 125; Articles 125.1, 126.1; Sections 3 and 4 of Article 144; Sections 2, 3, and 4 of Article 145; Article 146; Sections 2 and 3 of Article 147; Article 148; Section 2 of Article 149; Section 2 of Article 171; Articles 173, 174, 174.1, 176, 176.1, 176.2, 177, 178, 179, 191.2, 193; Section 3 of Article 206; Articles 212.1, 213.2, 218, 218.1; Section 2 of Article 224; Articles 224.1, 224.2, 226.1; and Paragraph (c) [v] of Article 240 of the Russian Soviet Federated Socialist Republic Criminal Code; b) those previously convicted for premeditated crimes and again condemned to a term of imprisonment for premeditated crimes; c) those deemed particularly dangerous recidivists; d) those who are malicious violators of the prison regime [rezhim otbyvaniya nakazaniya]; e) those previously released from their places of detention prior to completion of the sentence specified by the court within the confines of an amnesty or a pardon who have again committed premeditated crimes. 9. That for persons subject to release from penalty under this decree but in respect of whom measures of compulsory treatment for alcoholism, drug addiction, or venereal diseases have been specified alongside the penalty for the crime committed, the act of amnesty be applied on completion of the full course of treatment. 10. That persons convicted by courts of the Russian Federation and also by USSR courts fall under the remit of this decree. 11. That this decree shall enter into force from the moment of publication and shall be subject to implementation over the course of six months. [Signed] The Chairman of the Federal Assembly State Duma
