BFN [From "The News" newscast] [Text] President Rafael Caldera has emphasized that constitutional amendments must include provisions for an impeachment referendum, that is, the possibility that people could impeach any government official, including the president, who fails to perform his duties. He made these statements at the opening of a play called "Venezuela, Political Crisis, and Constitutional Reforms." He also said that participative democracy must be the main aspect of the amendments to ensure that people will have a say in the nation's important sociopolitical issues. He also rejected all doubts that the community will not be able to understand the amendments due to the large number of articles. [Begin Caldera recording] People will not discuss the amendments article by article; they will merely reply yes or no, as is done in every democratic country. They will ratify or reject the amendments drafted by the National Congress. [end recording] He also said that it is necessary to give the Venezuelan people a greater opportunity to participate and more power in view of the situation the country is currently experiencing.
