May 25, 1990, Friday, Home Edition

DR. NEIL SOLOMON: BETA-CAROTENE SOURCE

Question: I have learned quite a bit about vitamins, minerals and other nutrients from your column, and my family's diet has improved considerably as a result.

But now I've been reading about beta-carotene. Is it a vitamin? What foods is it found in?

Answer: Beta-carotene is one of the carotenoids, a group of compounds related to Vitamin A. Beta-Carotene is found in deep-yellow and dark-green vegetables.
