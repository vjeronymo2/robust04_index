BFN [Excerpts] We are going to start by repeating what we have time and again said here over the past few days: We are going through a tense social situation, which is marked by not entirely successful attempts to set up roadblocks. There is, above all, an atmosphere of uncertainty and insecurity. Workers have clashed with government forces on the streets using the technique of organizing in cell-style picketing groups. There are talks under way aimed at starting negotiations between the government and the Bolivian Labor Federation [COB], but no progress has yet been made in addressing substantive issues. While the situation is not new, it is worrisome just the same. Violence took hold on the streets of La Paz this afternoon. Union leaders, policemen, and even common criminals participated in acts of violence. The COB rally scheduled for this afternoon in San Francisco Square was not held. Police took positions over the entire city of La Paz, and prevented every form of street protest. As soon as a group of workers began to convene, police were already dispersing them. Demonstrators moved toward the northern part of the city, where they continued to fight police personnel. Dozens of people were arrested. The situation was altogether different at the Lima police precinct. There the ones who were surrounded were the policemen. The actions there were in no way related to the COB social struggle, let alone with the struggle waged by the unions of rural and urban teachers, who in recent times have often taken to the streets of La Paz. In this case, common criminals surrounded the Lima precinct to release their fellow criminals who were being held at the precinct. [passage omitted] The criminals managed to open the cell, but there was only one prisoner there. Their next move was to try to hang the policemen, who locked themselves up in the precinct office. [passage omitted] Finally, reinforcements managed to rescue the Lima precinct police personnel. Disturbances in La Paz continued until very late. The demonstrators damaged several buses. You are now watching a clip of (Higuino Square), where the police dispersed one of the many groups of demonstrators and cleared the street for traffic. No one was spared from the acts of violence on Peru Avenue, not even reporters. Three journalists and a press car were attacked this afternoon by rural teachers on Peru Avenue, where window panes were shattered and the windshields of cars and buses were broken. [passage omitted] This time the demonstrators acted in small groups, giving no quarter to police. The clip you are watching took place near the court house, where the air was filled with tear gas. In the meantime, the last eight people still under arrest for breaking windshields and windows during the rural teachers demonstration on 22 April requested that the Fourth Criminal Court judge set them free during a habeas corpus hearing. Judge Lidia Molina upheld their request, and ordered that they all be released. [passage omitted] The rural teachers have announced that they will file another habeas corpus petition to obtain the release of those arrested this afternoon.
