920521 FT 21 MAY 92 / Commodities and Agriculture: Kazakhstan expected to sign mining deal PRESIDENT Nursultan Nazarbayev of Kazakhstan is expected to sign an agreement with Rothschild North America today, aimed at developing Kazakhstan's mining and metal processing, Reuter reports from New York. The deal with Rothschild, a subsidiary of the British merchant bank NM Rothschild, is meant to stimulate investment by western companies and has a potential value of several hundred million dollars, according to Mr Vladimir Scherbakov, an adviser to President Nazarbayev. The deal comes three days after Kazahkstan signed a 40-year, Dollars 20bn (Pounds 11.2bn) deal with Chevron to exploit the country's large Tengiz oil field. Mr Scherbakov said Kazakhstan would sign at least two other deals before the president's delegation leaves the US on Saturday. One is an industrial development deal with Creditanstalt bank of Austria and the other is a link between the New York Stock Exchange and Kazakhstan's stock exchange.
