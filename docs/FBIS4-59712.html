BFN [Text] Rabat, May 16 (MAP) -- Mauritanian Prime Minister Sidi Mohamed Ould Boubaker left Morocco over the week-end after a several day visit during which he conveyed a message to King Hassan II from President Maaouiya Ould Sid'Ahmed Taya. In a departure statement, the Mauritanian premier voiced satisfaction over the outcome of his visit to Morocco and hailed the sound cooperation and secular ties binding the two countries. During his stay, Ould Boubaker conferred with Moroccan peer Mohamed Karim Lamrani on ways of boosting bilateral cooperation in all fields. The two sides agreed to convene next June in Rabat a meeting of the follow-up cooperation commission to prepare the agenda of the high joint commission in charge of economic, technical and cultural cooperation.
