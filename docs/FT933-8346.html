930817 FT 17 AUG 93 / Factions reach deal on Sarajevo A PLAN to put Sarajevo under temporary United Nations control was agreed 'in principle' last night by the leaders of Bosnia's three warring factions. The city's fate would be put on ice during negotiations on a three-way carve-up of the rest of Bosnia on ethnic lines. Earlier Mr Alija Izetbegovic, Bosnia's Moslem president, had insisted that ending the siege of Sarajevo must top the agenda of the latest talks on the country's 17-month conflict. The accord came just hours after Serb, Croat and Moslem leaders resumed negotiations for the first time in two weeks. However, officials warned that the agreement was not yet finalised. Mr John Mills, spokesman for the international mediators, said: 'The devil is always in the details.' Report, Page 16 Italy's offer of beds, Page 2
