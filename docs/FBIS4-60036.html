BFN [Report by Shirin Dahruj from Port Said] [Text] Dr. Usamah al-Baz, first Foreign Ministry under secretary and director of the President's Office for Political Affairs, has denied that the Gulf states are helping the extremist groups in Egypt. He stressed that the Gulf countries know that any harm to Egypt's security will bring harm to them. Speaking at a seminar at the Education College in Port Said, al-Baz noted that any outside military intervention in Yemen will further complicate the crisis. He stressed that Egypt will not intervene militarily in Yemen but will hold intensive political and diplomatic contacts to end the conflict. Al-Baz cautioned against conflicts among Arab states. He noted that such conflicts would give a bad impression of the Arab nation worldwide. Al-Baz pointed out that the notion of a Middle East market was first raised by Israeli Foreign Minister Shim'on Peres. He pointed out that before establishing a Middle East market, an Arab world must be established first.
