BFN [Text] The debts of Ukrainian enterprises owed to their counterparts in other former USSR countries are rising steeply. According to a report by the economy directorate of Ukraine's Cabinet of Ministers, credit indebtedness increased by 65.3 percent in the first two months of this year alone, and was 20.5 trillion rubles on 1 March 1994. Debit indebtedness increased in the first two months of the year by 16.2 percent, and reached 6.5 trillion rubles on 1 March 1994. In particular, Ukrainian enterprises were 5.4 trillion rubles in debt to their trading partners in Russia in January-February, and now owe them 15.5 trillion rubles. (KIYEVSKIYE VEDOMOSTI, 7 May 1994)
