BFN [Text] Today, as part of the sixth joint economic commission of experts from Cuba and Spain, negotiations began for the establishment of an agreement that will promote and protect reciprocal investments. At the opening, Ernesto Melendez, minister of Foreign Investment and Economic Cooperation, described the relations with Spain as precious and important to Cuba. Apolonio Ruiz Ligero, Spanish secretary of state for foreign trade, likewise pointed out that the information received on Cuba's economy is enlightening and that Madrid is attending this meeting with the hope of working with Cuba on problem solving. Melendez and Ruiz Lisgero head the specialized teams from their respective countries, which will analyze the possibilities for cooperation as part of the commission's work.
