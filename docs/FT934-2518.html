931214 FT 14 DEC 93 / Shadow role for Lange Mr David Lange, former New Zealand prime minister, was appointed foreign affairs spokesman in the shadow cabinet by Ms Helen Clark, opposition Labour party leader yesterday, writes Terry Hall in Wellington. Mr Lange is widely held responsible for a rift with the US, Britain and Australia from 1984 when he was foreign affairs minister and premier. Then, New Zealand banned visits by nuclear-armed vessels, legislation over which led to the break-up of the post-war Anzus treaty.
