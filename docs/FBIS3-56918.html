Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Vilnius, Jan 20, BNS -- Lithuanian and Polish Government delegations signed on Thursday [20 January] a protocol of the first round of talks on state border delimitation and revision. Lithuania and Poland both recognize the inviolability of the present border. The delegations, led by Vladislovas Domarkas, Lithuanian deputy minister of foreign affairs, and Eugeniusz Zuziak, representative of the Polish border guard chief commandant's headquarters, meet in Vilnius Jan. 17-21. The Eastern Europe Division of the Lithuanian Foreign Ministry said the first round goes smoothly and without any major disputes. Reportedly, an agreement on state border and legal relations in the border zone will be completed during the second round of talks. The delegations are scheduled to meet again in Warsaw in March or April.
