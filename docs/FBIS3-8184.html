Language: Czech Article Type:BFN [Text] Georgian President Eduard Shevardnadze made a brief stopover in Prague today on his way to an official visit to the United States. More from our reporter David Stahlavsky. [Stahlavsky] Vaclav Havel received the Georgian president for an approximately hour-long meeting at Prague Castle and welcomed this opportunity to discuss bilateral relations. For example, Georgia will open an embassy in Prague that will also deal with other Central European countries. For its part, the Czech Republic will open a Consulate General in Tbilisi, and both diplomatic representations will be preparing a system of treaties to secure wider cooperation. At their meeting, both presidents exchanged their views on the situation in Georgia, which is being troubled by civil and ethnic war, in Russia, and in other parts of the world. [passage omitted]
