940202 FT 02 FEB 94 / World Stock Markets (Asia Pacific): Hong Kong up 3% as Tokyo volume climbs Monday's sharp rise attracted active trading, and the Nikkei average closed moderately higher as volume jumped to 950m shares, writes Emiko Terazono in Tokyo. The 225-issue index gained 187.22 at 20,416.34 as corporate investors sold into strength, supported by buying by overseas investors and domestic individuals. The Topix index of all first section stocks added 13.88 at 1,643.10, but inLondon the ISE/Nikkei 50 index retreated 12.61 to 1,346.17. The Nikkei fell to the day's low of 20,237.52 in the morning, and peaked at 20,509.95 inthe afternoon, rising above 20,500 for the first time since October 26 last year. Selling pressure from institutional investors, who had bought shares in orderto support the Nikkei index last year, was especially strong at those levels. Traders said the Nikkei's recovery past the 20,000 level had boosted investor confidence. 'Monday's rise was more effective in supporting the market than an income tax cut, since it increased the Tokyo market's capitalisation by Y20,000bn,' said Mr Yasuo Ueki at Nikko Securities. Buying ahead of Thursday's announcement of the government's economic stimulus package may drive the index above the next resistance line of 21,148 reached in September. Nippon Telegraph and Telephone put on Y32,000 at Y1.01m, recovering the Y1m level for the first time since April 19 last year. Other telecommunication and multimedia related issues were also higher, with Fujitsu rising Y20 to Y1,040 and Fujikura, a cable maker, gaining Y21 at Y1,010. The yen's strengthening hurtexport-oriented sectors. Pioneer Electronic fell Y40 to Y2,980 and Hitachi declined Y3 to Y902. Steelmakers were weak, with Kawasaki Steel slipping Y9 to Y372. Honda Motor receded Y40 to Y1,700 following BMW's acquisition of Rover. Some banks were lower on profit-taking. Industrial Bank of Japanshed Y20 to Y3,340. In Osaka, the OSE average added 256.82 at 22,121.65 in volume of 83.8m shares. Roundup The region was mainly stronger on renewed foreign demand for equity. Kuala Lumpur was closed. HONG KONG rose sharply on overseas buying ahead of the Chinese new year, encouraged by approval of a funding plan for the new airport. The Hang Seng index advanced 339.87, or nearly 3 per cent, to 11,826.89. Turnover expanded to HKDollars 9.2bn from Monday's HKDollars 7.1bn. Expectations of strong corporate results aided sentiment, brokers added. Among the blue chips, HSBC Holdings topped the active list, surging HKDollars 5 to HKDollars 120, while Hang Seng Bank rose HKDollars 1.50 to HKDollars 77. Cheung Kong put onHKDollars 2 at HKDollars 50.50. SEOUL climbed to finish at a 52-month high in spite of selling pressure from the market stabilisation fund, which placed sell orders worth Won400bn. The composite index moved up 14.39, or 1.52 per cent, to 960.10. Turnover amounted to Won1,350bn. TAIWAN was supported by buying of stocks in the construction sector, which has lagged behind the market's recent rise. The weighted index ended 61.79 higher at 6,176.91 in turnover of TDollars 101bn, after Monday's TDollars 89.25bn. Textile stocks remained firm on expectations of a recovery in the industry and higher product prices. Shinkong Synthetic Fibres moved forward TDollars 1.20 to TDollars 30.20. MANILA slipped back following worries over bomb attacks on three oil companies and fears of further protests against a 15 per cent increase in fuel prices. The composite index surrendered 39.07to 2,835.49 in turnover of 1.14bn pesos. SINGAPORE was firmer, helped by foreign demand for selected stocks. The Straits Times Industrial index rose 9.98 to 2,349.08. NEW ZEALAND gained fresh impetus from economic data which showed an improvement in growth. The NZSE-40 capital index added 25.7 at 2,423.62. Telecom, which has been leading the market higher in recent sessions, advanced a further 11 cents to NZDollars 4.96. PAKISTAN rose on the first day of the new account and the KSE 100 index closed 28.00 stronger at 2,272.07. Brokers said they expected share prices to continue to recover after heavy selling pressure last week.
