BFN [Text] Geneva, 7 Jun (TANJUG) -- Srpska republic president Radovan Karadzic says that there is a possibility of a document marking the end of the war being signed this afternoon in Geneva's Palais des Nations. Following his talks with Yasushi Akashi, UN special envoy for the former Yugoslavia, Karadzic said at a news conference: "We have conducted difficult but constructive talks. Our suggestion about a general 12-month cease-fire was not accepted." Momcilo Krajisnik, president of the Srpska republic assembly, said to a TANJUG correspondent: "We expect that a document will be signed on an all-encompassing four-month cease-fire, but we still have to wait for the Muslim side's reply. Yasushi Akashi is expected to meet the Muslim delegation this afternoon. Afterwards, a plenary session may take place and, eventually, documents may be signed. Everything is still uncertain, though."
