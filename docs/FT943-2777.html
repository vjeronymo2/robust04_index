940917 FT 17 SEP 94 / Broadcasters welcome end of ban The end of the ban on broadcasting Sinn Fein members' voices was welcomed last night by broadcasters. Mr Michael Grade, Channel 4 chief executive, said: 'The lifting of the ban ends Britain's most embarrassing attempt to censor coverage of the most important domestic political story of post-war years.' Mr John Birt, BBC director-general, said: 'We can again apply normal and testing scrutiny to all sides in the debate.' Mr Tony Hall, managing director of BBC news and current affairs, said: 'Lifting the ban means that we are now able to do our job properly.'
