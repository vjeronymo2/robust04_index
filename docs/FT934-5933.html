931127 FT 27 NOV 93 / Iraq backs down in UN clash Iraq last night conceded UN Security Council demands for long-term monitoring to guard against any revival of its capability to produce weapons of mass destruction. The decision means that Baghdad has dropped its insistence that verification would constitute an unwarranted intrusion and an offence against the nation's sovereignty. In a letter to the council, Mr Mohamed Said al-Sahaf, the foreign minister, argued that 'this positive step' and other 'positive developments' justified the lifting of sanctions, including the embargo on oil sales. Mr Rolf Ekeus, head of the UN commission overseeing the elimination of Iraqi weapons, said the Iraqi move was a major breakthrough.
