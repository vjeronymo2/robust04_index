BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic developments monitored through 10 May. The Comptroller General's Office announced in a communique that on 5 May policemen will receive $1.477 in wage increases, which will be retroactive to 1 January. (Panama City EL SIGLO in Spanish 5 May 94 p 60) In 1993, the Colon Free Zone's overall commercial activities totaled $8.837.3 billion and generated approximately 9 percent of the GNP. Imports during the first quarter of 1994 have totaled $1.070.8 billion, compared to $790.2 million during the same period in 1993, an increase of $280.6 million. Meanwhile, first-quarter reexport figures totaled $1.161.3 billion in 1993 and have increased to $1.283 billion this year. Reexports totaled $5.110 billion in 1993, an increase of 6 percent over the 1992 figures. Total imports in 1993 were $4.493 billion. (Panama City EL PANAMA AMERICA in Spanish 6 May 94 p B-1) The Panamanian Government will exchange bonds for $452 million with Japanese and European financial markets. This transaction will be carried out soon in London. Planning Minister Delia Cardenas announced that the Panamanian Government is working on the final adjustments to renegotiate approximately $452 million owed to Japanese and European markets. With regard to the commercial debt, which totals $3.2 billion, Minister Cardenas said renegotiations are under way, counterproposals have been received, and the various alternatives are being studied. (Panama City Circuito RPC Television in Spanish 2300 GMT 5 May 94)
