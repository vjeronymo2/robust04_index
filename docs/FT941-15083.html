940117 FT 17 JAN 94 / Emerging Markets: News round-up Latin America Latin American borrowers issued a record Dollars 25bn (Pounds 16.8bn) in new debt last year in international markets, as new issue volume in the fourth quarter reached Dollars 10.4bn, Salomon Brothers has said. This compared with Dollars 11.5bn in 1992. Average maturity lengthened to 4.8 years from 3.7 years in 1992, with Mexican issues carrying an average 5.91 years. The private sector accounted for 64 per cent of new issues. Mexico accounted for 36 per cent, and Brazil and Argentina for 25 per cent each. Israel The country has agreed in principle to a second stock market, according to reports last week. The finance ministry is to prepare a list of regulations to govern an additional exchange by mid-April. Nepal The country's first stock exchange swung into action last Thursday with transactions totalling some Dollars 1,550. The bourse operates on the outcry system and currently trades for three hours from Sunday to Thursday and one hour on Friday. There are 62 companies listed on the stock exchange and the authorities aim to establish two indices in the near future. Warsaw The equity market was very active last week and the trend is likely to continue, traders say. The WIG index gained some 7 per cent during the week, as turnover reached a record Dollars 125m. Mexico Foreign investment in government instruments totalled 67.95bn new pesos in 1993, an increase of 53.1 per cent from a year-earlier, according to central bank data. The majority of the holdings were concentrated in Treasury bills, accounting for some 47.7bn new pesos. Further coverage of emerging markets appears daily on the World Stock Markets page.
