Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] In order to guarantee complete openness the presence of observers was ostensible at polling stations; they were observers from human rights organizations, as well as Tunisian and foreign journalists. They were all able to follow the voting process. The head of state had given his instructions to facilitate their movements in order to follow the whole voting process. Among those who were present was the secretary general of the European Socialist Party [as heard], who made the following statement to the press before leaving Tunis. [Begin unidentified voice recording, in French fading into Arab translation] I went to Sfax and Kairouan very early this morning to talk to voters and to those who were in charge of polling stations; I also talked to governors and to the Justice Minister. Everything went smoothly and I was very impressed with the high turnout and the calm that prevailed over the elections. In Kairouan, I talked to the local opposition leaders, who were fighting the elections there and, as far as I am concerned, the opposition was satisfied with the progress of the elections. [end recording]
