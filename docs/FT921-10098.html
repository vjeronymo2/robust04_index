920207 FT 07 FEB 92 / International Company News: Bombardier and Fokker in talks BOMBARDIER'S Canadair aerospace division confirmed it was holding talks with Fokker, of the Netherlands, on possible co-operation in Fokker's 70-passenger airliner project, writes Robert Gibbens. Bombardier's Short Brothers subsidiary in Belfast makes the wings for the Fokker F100, from which a range of aircraft would be developed to carry up to 130 passengers. Bombardier has preliminary plans to stretch its Canadair 50-passenger Regional Jet into a 70-passenger version and has had talks with potential customers and engine suppliers.
