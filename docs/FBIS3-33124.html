Article Type:CSO [Editorial Report] Managua LA PRENSA in Spanish of 21 January 1994 on page 8 reports that Mexican investors have expressed interest in purchasing the Chinandega peanut crop due to a drop in production in Mexico. The Chinandega peanut crop is expected to yield 150,000 quintals. The Mexicans will pay $20 per quintal after it has been processed. The article explains that Anagsa, an agroindustrial enterprise, is purchasing harvesting equipment with Mexican financing. According to local producers, Corinto port activity, local employment, and industry are expected to grow as a result of the investment.
