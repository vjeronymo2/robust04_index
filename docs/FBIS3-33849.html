Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tehran, Feb. 1, IRNA -- A man who shot five bullets in the air in a ceremony at the shrine of the late Imam Khomeyni this morning said at the initial interrogation that he did not mean to make an attempt against the life of any specific person. The 26-year-old Kourosh said "his real aim was to overshadow the joy of people at the start of the `ten-day-dawn' ceremonies," which marks the 15th anniversay of the victory of the Islamic revolution. He fired five bullets into the air while President 'Ali Akbar Hashemi-Rafsanjani was addressing a large gathering of people, among them foreign guests. He was immdiately disarmed by the crowd and handed over to the law enforcement forces. Major Panahi, a law enforcement commander for southern Tehran, told IRNA today that the person under arrest carried a revolver, from which the bullets were fired. The handgun is a U.S.-made low-caliber revolver, he added. Major Panahi quoted the culprit as saying "he did not plan to assassinate any specific person. Rather, he meant to terrorize people and spoil the celebrations."
