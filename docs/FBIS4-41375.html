BFN [From BELINFORM by a TASS correspondent] [Text] Minsk, 24 June -- It has not been possible to reveal who will be the first president of the Republic of Belarus: Such are the preliminary results of the first round of the elections which took place on Thursday [23 June]. And even though the final data will be received later it can already be said now that Aleksandr Lukashenko who collected 44-45 percent of the vote is more than twice ahead of his main competitor Vyacheslav Kebich (18-19 per cent). The other candidates rank in the following order: Zenon Poznyak, Stanislav Shushkevich, Aleksandr Dubo, and Vasiliy Novikov, with the leader of the Belarusian People's Front Zenon Poznyak having approximately five percent less votes than V.Kebich. Chairman of the Central Electoral Commission Aleksandr Abramovich said that for the time being three dates for the second round are being discussed: 7, 10, or 17 July 1994. In accordance with the legislation, Aleksandr Lukashenko and Vyacheslav Kebich will be permitted to take part in this round.
