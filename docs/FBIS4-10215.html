BFN [Text] Cuban Foreign Minister Roberto Robaina is in the Argentine capital on a two-hour [as heard] technical stopover en route to Peru. During this required technical stopover, Robaina will spend most of his time holding news conferences with Buenos Aires' most important newspapers and television and radio news networks. Exclusive interviews with Channel 13, the newspaper EL CLARIN, PAGINA DOCE, Radio America Dos, and the news agencies ANSA and EFE have already been confirmed. Today, Roberto Robaina will meet with Argentine Foreign Minister Guido di Tella, who for work reasons will not be in Buenos Aires during the Cuban delegation's official visit. In the same way, several entrepreneurs in the pharmaceutical sphere have expressed great interest in speaking to Minister Robaina on Friday [8 April] to learn of Cuba's achievements in the area of biopharmaceuticals. With this 48-hour technical stopover in Buenos Aires en route to Peru, the subject of Cuba; its current reality; and its economic, political, and social prospects are beginning to be heard in Argentine public opinion before the delegation's official visit.
