Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, Dec. 24 (YONHAP) -- North Korea has developed mobile launchers for its Nodong-1 missiles, making it extremely difficult to intercept the missiles or to locate launch facilities and knock them out of commission, the YOMIURI SHIMBUN newspaper reported Friday. ``North Korea will put the Nodong-1 missile into operational deployment next year,'' the YOMIURI quoted international military sources as saying. ``When North Korea succeeded in test firing the Nodong-1 in late March, it was launched from a fixed platform, so we thought that changing the location was very difficult,'' a Japanese Defense Agency spokesman was quoted as saying. ``But later the United States and Japan learned that a mobile launch was possible by analyzing intelligence.'' ``The Nodong-1 is so large that launching it from a ship proved impossible, however,'' the senior official reportedly said. ``Since it does not use a fixed launch platform, it is hard to discover the launch location. Moreover North Korea has transferred most of its military facilities underground, so an attack by U.S. forces on the missile facilities is difficult.'' The Nodong-1, a modified Scud-C ballistic missile having a 1,000-kilometer range, is capable of reaching most areas of Japan except parts of Hokkaido.
