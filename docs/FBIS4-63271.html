BFN [Text] Issues concerning supplies of modern weapons to the Ukrainian Armed Forces and use of the country's unique industrial and scientific-technical potential became the center of attention at a briefing held yesterday at the Ukrainian Defense Ministry. Lieutenant General Lev Hnatenko, first deputy chief of the Ukrainian Armed Forces Armaments and chief of the Armaments General Headquarters, spoke with journalists in detail about Ukraine's implementation of international agreements on nuclear disarmament and reduction of conventional weapons. He emphasized that, despite economic hardships, Ukraine is able, and should, produce military equipment which would be comparable to the world's best standards and become competitive on world markets. Lt. Gen. Hnatenko also reported on certain problems of material provisions in the process of servicing already existing weapons. However, the main conclusion is that the Ukrainian Armed Forces is equipped with all necessary means to perform its tasks, which are aimed at protecting Ukraine's sovereignty and territorial integrity.
