920625 FT 25 JUN 92 / Observer: Disconnection Not everyone is impressed with Malaysia's efforts to promote itself as the champion of the poorer Third World countries, judging by the barrage of criticism coming out of the annual conference of The International Labour Organisation, just ended. Malaysia's longstanding ban on unions in its electronics industry, in defiance of ILO rules governing freedom of association, was roundly condemned by both rich and poor countries. To add to the embarrassment, Ahmad Mohammed Idrus, Malaysia's government representative, was halted in mid-speech for over-running his time defending his country's anti-union policies.
