Article Type:CSO [Editorial Report] Managua BARRICADA in Spanish of 25 January 1994 on page 4 reports that the Ministry of Economy and Development (Mede) has opened a deregulation office to reduce red tape based on input from businessmen to facilitate the distribution of goods. For example, representatives of the Cristal biscuit factory complained that the Ministry of Agriculture and Livestock required an export certificate which was not required by the country to which their products are exported, while fishing industry representatives are seeking to abolish permits required for exports to the United States, which does not require a similar document, according to the article. Mede Vice Minister Enrique Brenes explains that, in addition to eliminating trade barriers, the office is sponsoring training seminars for entrepreneurs.
