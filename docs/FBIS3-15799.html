Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [INTERFAX report under the "In Brief" rubric: "Moscow"] [Text] Russia has a special place in the world, and other countries should pay heed to its stance. This is what Grigoriy Yavlinskiy, leader of the State Duma's "Yabloko" faction, said during his meeting with Richard Nixon, former U.S. president, in Moscow. Yavlinskiy attributed that not only to Russia's nuclear potential, but also to its geopolitical situation. The "Yabloko" leader also spelled out to Richard Nixon his vision of the problems facing economic and political reform in Russia, Western assistance, and the Russian Federation's place in world politics.
