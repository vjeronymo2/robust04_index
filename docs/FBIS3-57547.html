Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] In Georgia the visit of Eduard Shevardnadze to France is estimated to be positive in the whole. Commenting the results of the Paris negotiations, Giso Gilieshvli, the chairman of the Committee for External Economic Relations, told the agreement on the investments protection is ready for signing. French capital will be invested in the extension of the Poti port. Besides, French specialists will take part in the Tbilisi-Baku railway reconstruction to create a safe Europe-Caucasus-Asia way. It is also supposed to create a joint French-Georgian Bank. Irakli Shenghelia, the leader of the Georgian Christian-Democratic Union, said France is the initiator of the European integration idea, and Georgia has to join this process. Besides, there was a necessity to examine the reaction on the Georgia's entrance in CIS. In all probability, E. Shevardnadze tried to clear up in Paris the attitude of France to the coming discussion of the Georgian-Abkhazian conflict in the UN Security Council. On his return to Tbilisi the Georgian leader declared on a press-conference there was understanding of the necessity of the Georgia's entrance to CIS. "It was a right decision which should be acceptable for Georgia as well as for Europe and CIS", - said Shevardnadze. The agreement for Friendship, Cooperation and Mutual Aid was signed during the visit.
