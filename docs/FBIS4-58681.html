BFN [Text] Brother Lieutenant General 'Ali 'Abdallah Salih, chairman of the Presidential Council, received at his office today, Robert Pelletreau, U.S. assistant secretary for Near Eastern Affairs, who conveyed to the brother president a message from the U.S. Administration dealing with bilateral relations, fields of cooperation, and issues on interest to both friendly countries, in addition to the current developments in our country. During the meeting, the two sides reviewed the means of promoting bilateral relations and expanding fields of cooperation. They also discussed the situation in our country in light of the explosve military situation which was caused by the secessionist forces rebelling against the constitutional legitimacy, particularly some members the Yemeni Socialist Party [YSP] leadership, who seek to fragment the homeland and involve the sons of our Yemeni people, their Armed Forces, and security forces, in the furnace of fighting. The U.S. assistant secretary expressed the United States' support for unity and democracy in our country, and its concern about all the challenges facing it as a result of the crisis. The brother Presidential Council chairman expressed his regret and deep sorrow over the regrettable events in our country as a result of the aggressive and adventurous actions by the secessionist elements of the YSP leadership which has obstinately rejected all good efforts and endeavors to contain the crisis and have caused a comprehensive military explosion. The brother president praised the United States' stand and support for the Yemeni unity, democracy, and free economy approach. He noted that our people greatly appreciate this stand. He stressed our country's concern about promoting its relations with the United States in the interest of both friendly countries. The brother Presidential Council chairman said that the main concern for the sons of our Yemeni people and their leadership is to build a new Yemen, the Yemen of 22 May 1990, which is based on democracy, unity, political and economic freedom, social justice, comprehensive development, respect for human rights, the planning of institutions, and the building of the state of law and order. Attending the meeting were Foreign Minister Muhammad Salim Basindwah and U.S. Ambassador in Sanaa Arthur Hughes.
