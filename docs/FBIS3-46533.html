Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Ulaanbaatar, December 25 (OANA-MONTSAME) -- The Monglian telegraph agency MONTSAME considers the following events as ten biggest events of 1993 in Mongolia and in the world: In Mongolia: 1) the first general elections of the president; 2) official support on the part of the Russian Federation, the U.S.A., the PRC and Great Britain for declaration of Mongolia a nuclear weapon-free zone; 3) the visit of the Mongolian President P. Ochirbat to the Russian Federation and signing of the treaty on friendly relations and cooperation between the two countries; 4) the official visit of the Prime Minister P. Jasray to Japan, his visit to the U.S.A.; 5) putting into operation of the metallurgical enterprise in the city of Darhan; 6) the Tokyo meeting of the representatives of donor countries; 7) the natural calamities in the three western Aymags, the crash of the plane of the Mongolian Civil Aviation Company, the auto-accident in Arhangay Aymag; 8) bringing from India to Ulaanbaatar the ashes of Buddha; 9) ensuring of convertibility of the national currency, slackening the inflation rate, a tendency for stabilization of prices; and 10) the world forum of the Mongols. In the world: 1) the October events in Moscow; 2) the parliamentary elections in Russia; 3) the inauguration of the President of the U.S.A. Bill Clinton; 4) successful completion of peace mission of the UN in Cambodia and conducting of the first free elections in this country; 5) the signing of the Israel-Palestine agreement; 6) arguments around nuclear problems on the Korean peninsula and conflicts in Bosnia-Herzegovina; 8) the series of Uruguay talks on GATT; 9) the nuclear weapon test in the PRC; 10) reaching an agreement on the draft new constitution of the Republic of South Africa.
