BFN [Text] The next round of talks between the Tajik leadership and the opposition is planned for 4 June in Tehran, one of the leaders of the Tajik opposition, former Muslim leader of the Republic and now Deputy Chairman of the Tajik Islamic Revival Movement, Akbar Turandzhonzoda, told INTERFAX on Monday [16 May]. He said that the terms of the talks were agreed with the UN General Secretary's special envoy for Tajikistan Ramiro Prisi Bolion and Deputy UN General Secretary for political issues Marek Goulding. Turadzhonzoda confirmed the opposition's readiness to sign an agreement on a temporary cease-fire. "I am surprised Dushanbe did not agree to sign this agreement during the first round of talks in Moscow on 5-19 April," he pointed out. In the meantime, Tajik Foreign Minister Rashid Alimov did not confirm to INTERFAX that the talks with the opposition would continue in Tehran on 4 June. "In this issue we are oriented by UN representatives, but not Taradzhonzoda," he noted. According to Alimov, a visit by UN representatives Bolion and Goulding to Dushanbe is planned for 25-27 May.
