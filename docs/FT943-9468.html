940811 FT 11 AUG 94 / Portillo plan 'could damage Tory vote' Labour yesterday claimed that Mr Michael Portillo, the employment secretary, could severely damage the Conservatives' chances in the next general election if his call for a cut in state support for industry is implemented. Mr Frank Dobson, Labour's acting campaign manager, strongly attacked Mr Portillo for showing with his recent call for a reduction in the Department of Trade and Industry's budget that 'he was out of touch with the real world'. The attack coincided with claims by Mr Derek Fatchett, shadow industry minister, that 182 Tory MPs could lose their seats if the DTI is forced to scale back its programme of regional assistance in the way Mr Portillo recently demanded in a letter to Mr Michael Heseltine, the trade and industry secretary. 'Mr Portillo's proposals are a direct attack on his own party's MPs,' said Mr Fatchett. 'But more importantly, they will hurt British business. His colleagues must now decide whether to back their local industries or sack Mr Portillo.'
