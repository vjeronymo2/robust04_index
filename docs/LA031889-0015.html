March 18, 1989, Saturday, Home Edition

FUROR OVER BIBLE SCHOLARS ON SECOND COMING OF CHRIST

The Jesus Seminar scholars are fulfilling Bible prophecy they decry when they proclaim that Jesus did not say He would return. The Bible says, " . . . In the last days mockers will come with their mocking . . . saying, 'Where is the promise of His coming?' For ever since the fathers fell asleep, all continues just as it was from the beginning of creation" (II Peter 3:3,4).

The scoffers of the academe must establish their biblical credibility by explaining who Jesus was. In John 14:28, Jesus said to his disciples and to the multitudes who would believe in him throughout the ages, "I said unto you, I go away, and come again unto you." If he does not return, then he is a liar and the entire Christian faith is based upon a falsehood. The truth of the second coming dominates the New Testament; it is mentioned 318 times, more often than any other fundamental doctrine.

These academicians may be wise in other subjects, but they should not be called biblical scholars, since they would have to cut out a major portion of their Bibles. They would not be left with a shred of evidence for their premise. We are grateful to Beutner for warning us that most major universities and seminaries agree with the findings. It should urge open-minded people to search the Scriptures for themselves -- before it is too late.

CAROLE C. CARLSON

Solvang
