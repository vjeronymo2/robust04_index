920715 FT 15 JUL 92 / Corps may build Cambodia roads JAPAN, preparing the way for its first overseas troop deployment since the second world war, may send an engineering corps to Cambodia to build and repair roads and bridges, Mr Sohei Miyashita, the defence minister, said yesterday, Reuter reports from Tokyo. The Japanese mission to serve in the United Nations peace-keeping operation in Cambodia was expected to comprise an engineering corps and a corps to assist helicopter transport of ceasefire monitoring missions, Mr Miyashita said. Japan's parliament passed a law on June 15 allowing it to send up to 2,000 troops overseas to serve in UN peace-keeping missions.
