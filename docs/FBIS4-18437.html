BFN [Announcer-read report over video] [Text] Murat Karayalcin, leader of the Social Democratic Populist Party [SDPP], state minister, and deputy prime minister, has said that the economic stability package envisages an equal share of the burden by all the sectors of the community, and that there will be no freeze on salaries. Replying to a journalist's question during a meeting of the SDPP Central Executive Council, Karayalcin said that the economic stability package will not place a burden on the working people. You will see, he said, that all the sectors of the economy will share an equal burden. Addressing the meeting, Karayalcin said that organizational readjustments will be made within the SDPP soon. We are also on the threshold of a new period in government activities, he said. He denied that the SDPP wants the government protocol to be changed. [Begin Karayalcin recording] However, we consider it necessary that a new priority list be drawn up about the issues the SDPP is sensitive about. This list will indicate when these issues will be implemented, or when they will be submitted to the Turkish Grand National Assembly. [end recording] Karayalcin announced that he will work on whether or not to start a negotiation process toward the unification of the leftist parties. He pointed out that he came together with Republican People's Party leader Deniz Baykal on Turkish Radio and television's "Firing Line" program today. The SDPP leader pointed out that he will meet with Baykal as soon as possible to assess the situation. Karayalcin said that the SDPP does not posit any preconditions or expectations for the unification of the leftist parties. He said: I believe that no political project is as important as the unity of the left. Asked if he would give up the SDPP leadership for the unity of the leftist parties, Karayalcin replied: Both as an individual and as a party we are ready to make every kind of sacrifice. Replying to another question, Karayalcin said that the SDPP will work on the issue of possible changes in the SDPP wing of the cabinet. He added that the decision to be reached will not be his own personal decision.
