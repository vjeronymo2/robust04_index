Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Shanghai, February 25 (XINHUA) -- The Shanghai office of the Morgan Stanley group of the United States opened today, John Mack, president of the global financial institution, announced here. The reason the group has chosen Shanghai for its first office in China is that the city is becoming China's financial center, Mack said at a ceremony to mark the opening of the office. He said, "I am sure that China will play an increasingly important role in the global financial market." Therefore, the Chinese market has become the focus of the group's operations, he said. The Shanghai office will offer consultancy and advice on how to turn Chinese enterprises into shareholding companies, he added. According to Mack, the office will also help Chinese companies issue shares in the United States and Europe; help Chinese securities institutions enter the international market; become involved in issuing shares in China's securities markets; offer advice on how to raise money for large industrial and infrastructural projects in China; and seek opportunities for cooperation with Chinese financial institutions. The Morgan Stanley group intends to undertake a large real estate project and plans to participate in foreign exchange and futures trading in Shanghai, he said. The group is applying to open another office in Beijing, he said.
