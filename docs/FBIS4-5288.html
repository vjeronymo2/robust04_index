BFN [Text] The "Foreign Policy Forum" headed by House of Representatives member Hajime Ishii, a ruling coalition policy study panel, held its second round of meetings in the Diet on 6 April. Issei Inoue, vice chairman of the ruling Social Democratic Party of Japan [SDPJ], briefed the forum on the results of his recent visit to China and the situation in North Korea. During the meeting, Mr. Inoue said: "In an effort to resolve the issue of North Korea's suspected nuclear development, I propose sending a delegation composed of legislators from the ruling coalition parties to the United States, China, North and South Korea. I also want to work for the stability of Northeast Asia by arranging a meeting between President Clinton and North Korean President Kim Il-song." It was decided at the meeting that senior legislators of the ruling coalition parties would discuss the proposal.
