Language: Cambodian Article Type:BFN ["Cambodian-Thai Joint Communique"; issued in Phnom Penh on 13 January -- read by announcer] [Text] 1. At the invitation of Prince Krompreah Norodom Ranariddh, first prime minister, and His Excellency [H.E.] Hun Sen, second prime minister of the Royal Government of Cambodia [RGC], H.E. Chuan Likphai of the Kingdom of Thailand [KOT], paid an official visit, the first by the Thai Prime Minister, to the Kingdom of Cambodia [KOC] from 12 to 14 January. 2. During the stay in Cambodia, H.E. Chuan Likphai met with Samdech Chea Sim, chairman of the National Assembly and acting head of state. Samdech Chea Sim and H.E. Chuan Likphai exchanged views on the current situation and the relations between the KOC and the KOT and expressed satisfaction at the continuation of close ties of friendship and cooperation between the two countries. H.E. Chuan Likphai conveyed the best wishes of King Phumiphon Adunyadet and the Thai people, through Samdech Chea Sim, to His Majesty [H.M.] King Norodom Sihanouk, good health, longevity, and happiness. 3. During the visit H.E. Chuan Likphai and delegation laid wreaths at the independence monument and visited many important cultural and historic centers in Phnom Penh City and in Siem Reap Province. H.E. Chuan Likphai and H.E. Hun Sen presided over the opening of National Route 5 between Poipet and Sisophon which was repaired and restored by the Royal Thai Government. H.E. Chuan Likphai and H.E. Hun Sen also took part in inaugurating the friendship bridge at Khlong Loek-Poipet, which was funded by the British Government and built by the Royal Thai Government. 4. The official talks between the delegation of the Royal Thai Government led by H.E. Chuan Likphai and that of the RGC led by Prince Krompreah Norodom Ranariddh and H.E. Hun Sen were held on 12 January at the prime ministers' office. The talks went on in an atmosphere of friendship, mutual understanding, and cooperation. 5. The Cambodian side welcomed the first official visit to Cambodia by the Thai prime minister and noted the importance of this visit, which opens a new page in the two countries' relations. The Thai side expressed its admiration for the warm welcome and the cordiality offered by the Cambodian side. 6. The Thai side congratulated H.M. King Norodom Sihanouk on the occasion of his accession to the throne and highly valued his leading role in contributing to the development of peace and national reconciliation in Cambodia. 7. The two sides noted that the end of the cold war has brought an atmosphere that increases mutual understanding and cooperation among nations in Southeast Asia. 8. The Thai side welcomed and voiced strong support for the establishment of a new democratic Cambodian Government. The Thai side praised the RGC's continued efforts aimed at bringing about genuine national reconciliation in accordance with the lofty initiative of H.M. King Norodom Sihanouk. 9. The Cambodian side expressed gratitude to the Thai people for their firm and constant support for the peace process in Cambodia and the constructive role in the successful implementation of the Paris peace agreement. 10. The Thai side informed the Cambodian side that for the 1993-94 fiscal year, the Royal Thai Government has provided 30 million baht to assist Cambodia, particularly to develop human resources and technical assistance in various fields such as public works, public health, agriculture, vocational training, education, banking, civil aviation, science, and technology. The Cambodian side thanked the Thai side for providing this assistance. 11. The two sides expressed satisfaction at the current situation of relations betwen the two countries and reaffirmed their determination to constantly and concretely heighten bilateral relations on the basis of mutual interest and respect for each other's independence, sovereignty, and territorial integrity, and noninterference in each country's internal affairs. 12. With the aim of promoting trade and investment, the Cambodian side reaffirmed its policy to respect all legitimate contracts signed between Thai businessmen and the Cambodian side and by Cambodian businessmen with the Thai side. In cases of unclear trade contracts, the Cambodian side informed that it will properly reexamine those contracts without bias. While waiting for changes to be made to them, discussions will be held with relevant Thai businessmen aimed at ensuring legitimacy and mutual benefit for all sides. Responding the Cambodian side's request, the Thai side agreed that the Cambodian side will export 10,000 tonnes of soybeans until July 1994. 13. The two sides exchanged views on expanding bilateral relations and agreed that potential for broad cooperation rests on aquatic products, joint development of the coast, and tourism. Criminal and illegal activities by individuals or armed groups along the Cambodian-Thai border, which affect the two countries' security, will be seriously dealt with. In this sense, an agreement on the establishment of a joint commission for Cambodian-Thai bilateral cooperation was signed by the two countries' foreign ministers. The two side also agreed to set up a joint Cambodian-Thai committee on boundary at an appropriate time. 14. The two sides agreed in principle that the royal visit to be made by H.M. King Norodom Sihanouk to the KOT will expand the existing close and happy relations between Cambodia and Thailand. The date and the agreement on this visit will be communicated through diplomatic channels. 15. The two sides welcomed the results of the visit to the KOC by the Thai Prime Minister, which is an important basis in the history of good relations between the two countries. The two countries also welcomed regular exchanges of visits by the two countries' leaders, which will promote and heighten the bilateral relations. The two sides also agreed to increase such visits in the future. [Dated] Phnom Penh, 13 January 1994 [Signed] For the RGC, Prince Krompreah Norodom Ranariddh, RGC first prime minister, H.E. Hun Sen, RGC second prime minister For the Royal Government of the KOT, H.E. Chuan Likphai, prime minister of the KOT
