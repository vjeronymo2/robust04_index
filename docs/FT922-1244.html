920624 FT 24 JUN 92 / Survey of Indonesia (12): Decree will reform curbs on bank ownership A NEW decree allowing greater foreign ownership of banks is imminent. At present foreigners are: Allowed to take up to an 85 per cent stake in joint-venture banks but these cannot be listed on the stock market. Barred from buying shares in domestic private banks, including those listed on the stock market The new decree is set to: Permit joint-ventures to float shares, of which foreign investors may purchase up to 49 per cent. Allow foreigners to purchase up to 49 per cent of quoted domestic private banks. Allow state-owned banks to list up to 49 per cent of their shares. Of the listed shares, foreigners will be allowed to buy up to 49 per cent.
