Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Bratislava, Jan 13 (CTK) -- Within the Partnership for Peace, Slovakia has offered NATO the capacities of its arms industry and cooperation in developing military equipment, Slovak Premier Vladimir Meciar told journalists today. Speaking at a press conference after top Visegrad Four (the Czech Republic, Hungary, Poland, and Slovakia) representatives met with U.S. President Bill Clinton on Wednesday in Prague, Meciar said that Slovak arms production could not compete with that of NATO's, given, among other things, the existence of two military systems in the past. Slovak heavy weaponry is of good mechanical quality, while its electronic systems are improving, Meciar stated. Slovakia's arms production dropped in 1993 to 8 to 9 percent of its 1989 level. This is a huge decline, which cannot be called conversion to peaceful purposes. The conversion should represent a change of 5 percent a year, Meciar stressed. The present situation in the Slovak arms industry is due to bad decisions by the former Czechoslovak Federal Government, which halted the final production of arms without regard to continuing supplies, which mostly came from the Czech Republic. This resulted in billion losses, Meciar said. He stressed that the Czech Republic was now striving to revive the production of military systems, which had been halted in Slovakia. A joint-stock company with state participation, aimed at exporting Slovak arms, has been founded in Slovakia. A commission has also been set up to decide on each deal, Meciar concluded.
