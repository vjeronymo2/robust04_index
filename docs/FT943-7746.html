940822 FT 22 AUG 94 / World News in Brief: European Monetary System Against a backdrop of generalised D-Mark strength, bolstered by upgraded growth forecasts and firm interest rates, the EMS grid was little changed. The spread between strongest and weakest currency was maintained, with only the Irish punt and French franc swopping places. Currencies, Page 27 The chart shows the member currencies of the exchange rate mechanism measured against the weakest currency in the system. Most of the currencies are permitted to fluctuate within 15 per cent of agreed central rates against the other members of the mechanism. The exceptions are the D-Mark and the guilder which move in a 2.25 per cent band.
