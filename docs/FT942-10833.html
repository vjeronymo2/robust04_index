940509 FT 09 MAY 94 / Scheme for school leavers supported Almost two-thirds of young people believe the government should introduce a community service scheme for all school leavers, a survey shows today. The study, conducted by Gallup for the charity CSV which places young people in voluntary posts, shows that 64 per cent of 16 to 24-year-olds believe the government should establish a voluntary programme available to all young people. Although community service has supporters among individual politicians, the parties have tended to shy away from the idea - particularly in any form that might look like the introduction of a compulsory alternative to national service. The poll showed that 31 per cent believe that community service for school leavers should be compulsory. 'Over 6m carers nationwide need respite care and support,' said Ms Elisabeth Hoodless, executive director of CSV. 'Schools in our inner cities would be transformed if each pupil had a volunteer mentor or tutor to help with their reading. Crime would be cut by giving young people the chance to put their energy and enthusiasm to good use.'
