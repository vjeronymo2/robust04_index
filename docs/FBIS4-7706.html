BFN [Excerpts] Belgrade, April 5 (AFP) -- Bosnian Serb forces confirmed Tuesday [5 April] they had broken through government defence lines in the eastern enclave of Gorazde in response to a government "offensive" and landed on the western bank of the Drina River. In Geneva, the UN High Commissioner for Refugees said 50 people had been killed and 250 injured since last week in the fighting in Goradze. Chief of staff Manojlo Milovanovic told the Bosnian Serb agency SRNA he had ordered his troops to launch a "counter-attack" towards Cajnica, southeast of Gorazde, close to the Serbian border, late Monday since "Moslem formations were trying to join up with forces from Sarajevo and the other enclaves." SRNA said that Moslem units had fallen back to the villages of Vitkovici and Mrvavici. [passage omitted] Milovanovic said the Gorazde defenders were attempting "to join up with Sandzak and beyond that with Kosovo." [passage omitted]
