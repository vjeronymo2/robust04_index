BFN [Text] Reports from Lesotho say gunfire echoed overnight around the capital Maseru as a crisis sparked by a police strike entered its 12th day today. Sporadic shooting was heard late yesterday and early today. The crisis deepened yesterday when talks between the government, the striking police, prison officers, and the official opposition broke down. Prime minister, Mr. Ntsu Mokhehle has rejected police demands for a 60-percent pay rise, and said the country cannot afford it. The opposition Basotho National Party walked out of a meeting with the government yesterday after Mr. Mokhehle accused the opposition of being involved in the police strike. South Africa's Archbishop Desmond Tutu is in Maseru at the invitation of church leaders in a bid to mediate in the dispute.
