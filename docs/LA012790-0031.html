January 27, 1990, Saturday, Home Edition

PBS BROADCAST OF AN ISRAELI VIEW OF INTIFADA

In his Jan. 16 review of the documentary "A Search for Solid Ground: The Intifada Through Israeli Eyes," Howard Rosenberg announces that his suspicions have been confirmed: "PBS has a pro-Jewish double standard" concerning the intifada.

It is not that he finds the film unbalanced; indeed he finds it "more balanced" than the controversial "Days of Rage."

In fact, Rosenberg praises "A Search," acknowledging the program's quality is not at issue -- a point that doesn't stop him from drawing gratuitous parallels between Israel and South Africa or the Soviet Union.

According to Rosenberg, the proof of PBS' "pro-Jewish double standard" is that "A Search" is accompanied by a 30-minute panel discussion, unlike the 60-minute wrap-around of "Days of Rage."

JERRY SHAPIRO, Anti-Defamation League, Associate Director, Los Angeles
