921103 FT 03 NOV 92 / UK Company News: BAe sells communications subsidiaries BRITISH AEROSPACE has dismantled its communications division and sold most of the parts with the loss of 35 jobs. The company said the division was 'not part of its core businesses'. Turnover of the division in 1991 was about Pounds 10m. Only the ground equipment for the company's Orion communications satellite project will remain associated with BAe through a joint venture with Kingston Communications, the telecommunications infrastructure operator for the city of Hull. Business Television and Services to Broadcasters, which broadcasts to specified groups of people, has gone to Maxat. The Medical Television Network, which provides programmes for doctors to medical schools was sold to Satellite Information Services, which provides television pictures to betting shops. Bishopsgate Systems' data broadcasting goes to Alphameric.
