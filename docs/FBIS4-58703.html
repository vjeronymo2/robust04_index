BFN [Unattributed report: "Palestinian Chief of Security and Police Tells AL-SHARQ AL-AWSAT: Our Forces Will Enter Gaza and Jericho Not Later Than 20 May"] [Text] Tunis, AL-SHARQ AL-AWSAT -- The Palestinian chief of security and police has confirmed that the Palestinian police forces will enter the Gaza Strip and Jericho by 20 May at the latest. He said that the entry will be completed within two weeks after the signing of the final agreement on the implementation of autonomy. In a statement to AL-SHARQ AL-AWSAT, Major General Mudar Yusuf (Abu-Yusuf) revealed that the forces will be transported to al-'Arish airport in Sinai by military transport planes. He denied recent rumors that Israel will hand over to the PLO five important police posts in the occupied Gaza Strip next Tuesday. Maj. Gen. Yusuf said: "These reports are unfounded." He added that in the first stage, the Palestinian police will enter Gaza and Jericho at the same time but the main effort will be concentrated on Gaza, which is densely populated, to provide security and stability there. He stressed that preparations have been made in all areas where Palestinian police and security forces are present and that they are awaiting orders to move. Abu-Yusuf said that, in the first phase, some 6,000 policemen will arrive in Gaza-Jericho, 4,500 of them going to Gaza and 1,500 going to Jericho, and these forces will enter as units in their present formations. Some of these formations may later be changed depending on circumstances on the ground and need. He added that the PLO recently received special military uniforms for the Palestinian police from Norway, and the first consignment of U.S. aid, consisting of police vehicles, has already arrived. In reply to a question about the structure of the police forces and whether they mainly consist of Fatah elements, Maj. Gen. Yusuf emphasized that the forces which will be responsible for security and stability in the Gaza Strip and Jericho "are not factional forces. They are national forces belonging to all the Palestinian people." He confirmed that "the Palestine Liberation Army [PLA], which includes elements from all the factions as well as elements not affiliated with any factions, has now become a security and police force." Maj. Gen. Yusuf revealed that "a large number [of people] from the factions opposed to the Oslo agreement have sent us applications to join these forces." He added that in the coming phase, the Palestinian police forces will not operate on a factional basis but on the basis of the Palestinian authority which will consist of various sectors of the Palestinian people regardless of their political affiliations. Maj. Gen. Yusuf ruled out the possibility of any major problems when the Palestinian police forces arrive. He also ruled out the possibility of friction between them and Hamas "because the brothers in Hamas have repeatedly stated their opposition to Palestinian infighting, and we will make every effort to promote understanding and agreement with them." Maj. Gen. Yusuf urged all sectors and factions of the Palestinian people "to extend a helping hand to the Palestinian police and security forces for the sake of building our future authority, easing our people's suffering, and providing security and stability for them." On the spread of arms possession in the city of Gaza and whether that will be a security problem for the Palestinian police forces, he said: "Arms do not scare us."
