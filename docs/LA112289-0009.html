November 22, 1989, Wednesday, Home Edition

INTERPRETING ELECTION RESULTS

I wondered what explanations the fundamental liberal media elitists would conjure up to save face after yet another attempt to brainwash the public into thinking that a handful of "religious extremists" was all that was backing passage of the repeal of special rights ordinances in Irvine, Concord and San Francisco.

I thought the excuse for San Francisco was original: the earthquake. And I also heard someone on a San Francisco talk show say that in off-year elections, there's a more conservative turnout. I guess you could use that alibi for the Irvine and Concord results as well. I have an explanation of my own. How about this: The majority of voters in these cities (and they can't all be "religious extremists") did not want the unequal special rights ordinances forced on them by a few politicians.

BRENDA HESTER

Garden Grove
