Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Moscow, January 13 (XINHUA) -- While U.S. and Russian presidents were holding their first round of talks this morning, their foreign minister and secretary of state prepared a package of draft agreements due to be signed by the two leaders. Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev and U.S. secretary of state Warren Christopher held close-door meeting in the Yellow Hall of the Kremlin to complete the documents today Among the major documents to be signed are the Moscow Declaration on the Outcome of the Clinton Visit, a memorandum on cooperation in export controls, and a joint statement on the Middle-East peace process, according to official sources here. The two foreign ministers also discussed the result of the NATO summit held earlier this week in Brussels. Kozyrev said the U.S. Partnership for Peace Proposal, adopted by the NATO summit, "suits Russia well," and that Russia considers it capable of leading to large-scale cooperation among all countries "interested in it."
