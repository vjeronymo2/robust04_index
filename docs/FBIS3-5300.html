Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, March 3 KYODO -- Japan's steel exports in January rose 20.6 percent from a year earlier to 1,495,000 tons, an industry association reported Thursday [3 March]. The exports, however, fell 33.1 percent from the previous month, the Japan Iron and Steel Federation said. January exports were valued at 961.66 million dollars, up 7.1 percent from a year earlier, or 107.6 billion yen, down 4.0 percent. The yen value posted the sixth consecutive year-on-year fall. Exports to South Korea surged 186.8 percent to 258,000 tons, due to increased pig iron exports, surpassing those to China. Exports to China totaled 232,000 tons, up 4.2 percent, Taiwan 192,000 tons, up 17.2 percent, and the United States 164,000 tons, up 43.9 percent. The federation said imports of ordinary steel in January fell 5.1 percent from a year earlier to 470,000 tons for the fifth consecutive year-on-year decline.
