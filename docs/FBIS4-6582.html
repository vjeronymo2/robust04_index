BFN [Text] Pyongyang, April 22 (KCNA) -- The fact-finding group on the forcible drafting of Koreans on April 18 discovered among documents kept at the U.S. National Archives lists of about 10,000 Korean workers and women who were taken away by the Japanese imperialists for forced labour and as "comfort women for the army" during the Second World War, according to a KYODO report. It was learned that the names of Korean workers and women drafted for forced labour and as "comfort women for the army" were in the lists of 180,000 soldiers of the former "Japanese Imperial Army" and civilian personnel who were taken prisoners of war by the allied forces in the Philippines. The group plans to make public the lists soon.
