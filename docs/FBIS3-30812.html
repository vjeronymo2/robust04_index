Language: Polish Article Type:BFN [From the "Panorama" program] [Text] There is little chance of an accord, Aleksander Kwasniewski, leader of the Democratic Left Alliance [SLD] said after meeting with Prime Minister Waldemar Pawlak and before talks between the SLD and the Polish Peasant Party (PSL). The coalition politicians met at the government hotel at Parkowa Street in Warsaw. Wojciech Nomejko reports: [Nomejko] The coalition meeting was preceded by a private talk between Prime Minister Waldemar Pawlak and SLD leader Aleksander Kwasniewski. It took place at the office of the Council of Ministers and lasted for over an hour. After 1900 [1800 GMT], coalition politicians began arriving at the URM hotel on Parkowa Street. Just before 2000, those gathered there were joined by Prime Minister Waldemar Pawlak and Aleksander Kwasniewski. The leader of the Left said that the coalition still exists and that the talks will be long and difficult.
