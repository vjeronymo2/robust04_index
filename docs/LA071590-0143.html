July 15, 1990, Sunday, Orange County Edition

'VOTERS GIVE CONGRESSMEN GOOD REVIEWS'

The impression one gets from the results of the Times Orange County Poll on voters' attitudes toward Orange County congressmen ("O.C. Voters Give Congressmen Good Reviews," July 9) is not that these lawmakers are popular, but that their performance is predominantly uninspiring.

While it is true, for example, that Dannemeyer received the highest favorable rating (33% of those polled), 67% of voters either had no opinion of him or found him unfavorable. Opinions regarding the other congressmen showed even greater indifference. What this all seems to say is that what is truly conservative about Orange County is not necessarily its politics, but its enthusiasm for its politicians.

JOHN McGOWAN

Fullerton
