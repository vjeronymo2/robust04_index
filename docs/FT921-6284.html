920228 FT 28 FEB 92 / UK Company News: Cabra Estates' loss widens to Pounds 11.3m CABRA ESTATES, the property company which may evict Chelsea Football Club from its west London ground if it does not pay Pounds 22.85m for the site, yesterday reported a pre-tax loss of Pounds 11.3m for the six months to September 30, compared with Pounds 5.23m. On turnover reduced to Pounds 11.4m (Pounds 25.5m), Cabra made a small profit, before interest payable of Pounds 3.48m and exceptional charges of Pounds 8.41m for property write downs. In addition, a Pounds 10m cut in the value of Stamford Bridge, where Chelsea play, went straight to the balance sheet. Cabra said this left a net asset value per share of 17.13p making total assets of Pounds 16.5m. Net debt, which is on demand, remained at about Pounds 52m, according to Mr John Duggan, chairman. He said Cabra had the support of its bankers. Losses per share, after a Pounds 474,000 tax credit, were 11.34p (5.81p) and the interim dividend is passed (0.1p).
