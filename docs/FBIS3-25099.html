Language: Portuguese Article Type:BFN [Text] About 600,000 Mozambican refugees have returned home since the signing of the Rome accord in October 1992. This was disclosed to our correspondent today by (Nana Quy), regional coordinator of the Norwegian Refugee Council. She said the refugees returned from Malawi, Zimbabwe, Zambia, South Africa, and Swaziland. The returnees received agricultural equipment and seeds, and were helped to resettle in their home areas. She added that in 1994 returnees will continue to be registered at civilian institutions [words indistinct] and educational programs. A total of $3.5 million could be allocated for the execution of those programs.
