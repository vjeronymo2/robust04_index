BFN [Text] Foreign Affairs Secretary Roberto Romulo yesterday called on the People's Republic of China to "exercise restraint in discharging their responsibility over fearsome technology" and to forego any further nuclear testing after the Maoist state conducted another nuclear test at Lop Nor last 10 June. Mr. Romulo made the call on the eve of bilateral talks between the Philippines and China to be held today at the Department of Foreign Affairs office. Both governments will discuss a whole range of bilateral concerns, including Beijing's worry on the Philippines' growing relationship with Taiwan. "The Philippines finds this (nuclear test) extremely unfortunate and regrettable. Last October, during the United Nations General Assembly, the Philippines asked for a moratorium on nuclear testing. And because China will be participating in the ASEAN (Association of Southeast Asian Nations) Regional Forum in Bangkok this July, we will hope that they will seize and desist from further nuclear testing in this part of the world," Mr. Romulo said. As a signatory to the United Nations Treaty on non-proliferation of nuclear weapons, the Philippines has affirmed to all of the accord's objectives, he said. Mr. Romulo said under the Comprehensive Test Ban Treaty, the testing of nuclear devices should "cease forever" because it "gravely threatens the very existence of the human family." The 10 June incident was China's second nuclear test after it conducted a previous one in October last year. Mr. Romulo said the incident will seriously undermine the negotiations currently taking place in Geneva on a Comprehensive Test Ban Treaty. "It also breaks the current moratorium on nuclear testing being observed by other nuclear states," he added. The annual bilateral talks between Manila and Beijing will be chaired by Foreign Affairs Undersecretary Rodolfo Severino and his counterpart from the Chinese foreign ministry. The two panels are expected to discuss political and economic issues affecting both countries, as well as regional ones like the forthcoming ASEAN Regional Forum where China is expected to participate. Mr. Romulo, declined to comment on whether Manila's concern on China's nuclear testing will be a hotly debated topic during the bilateral meet. The two groups are likewise expected to tackle other issues like the Philippines' one China policy.
