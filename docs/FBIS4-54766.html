BFN [Excerpts] Sofia, May 4 (BTA) -- Over 800,000 members of 5,691 trade union locals nationwide took part in the warning strike called by the Confederation of Independent Trade Unions in Bulgaria (KNSB) today, KNSB Vice President Zhelyazko Khristov told a news briefing. He said that 641,392 people were on effective strike, suspending work for an hour. On April 14 KNSB staged another nationwide protest, a rally in Sofia. Members of the other major trade union, the Podkrepa Labour Confederation, as well as of the Association of Independent Trade Unions and the National Trade Union also joined in the protest, KNSB leader Krustyu Petkov said at a news briefing this morning. The leadership of the Podkrepa Labour Confederation supports KNSB's demands but advised its members not to take part in the strike. None of the political forces generally sympathizes with the KNSB, but they have not spoken openly against the confederation's demands and the means of protest it has chosen, either, Prof. Petkov said in an interview with the PARI daily which was published yesterday. Of the parliamentary political forces, the Union of Democratic Forces (SDS) sticks to a position of neutrality, while the Bulgarian Socialist Party (BSP) supports the strike provided that it is not politicized. Several political parties and public organizations voiced support for the KNSB. According to Prof. Petkov, the strike has been well organized. Teachers, civil servants, employees in transport, trade, services and some branches of industry are the most widely represented. A one-hour warning strike took place in over 4,000 schools, nursery schools and kindergartens with staff running up to 190,000, said a representative of the Teachers Trade Union with the KNSB. The bridge over the River Danube on the Bulgarian-Romanian border was blocked for an hour this morning, said Prof. Petkov. Two checkpoints at the border with Turkey, Kapitan Andreevo and Svilengrad-Ormenion, also suspended operation for an hour. Protest rallies and processions are being held. Strikers present their demands in declarations to parliament and the government. A rally of doctors and teachers in Plovdiv (southern Bulgaria) failed to take place because it was banned by city authorities. Today's warning strike is part of the preparations for the general strike scheduled to take place on May 17. The KNSB insists for incomes compensation. According to Prof. Petkov, 80 percent of the population is now below the subsistence level owing to the steep rise of the U.S. dollar against the lev in the recent months, the introduction of VAT [value-added tax] as of April 1 and the inertness of the government. The KNSB also demands that a government programme for the stabilization of the finance market be developed, and that the cabinet take a clear stand on the means to defend Bulgarian producers. Another demand is transparency as to Bulgaria's commitments to international institutions. At a rally in Pernik (western Bulgaria) today Prof. Petkov said that the KNSB demands that an industry-wide collective agreement be signed for 1994. The agreement is ready and only the part concerning wages is yet to be agreed, he explained. [passage omitted] Yesterday Prime Minister Lyuben Berov notified Prof. Petkov that he was ready to start negotiations on May 11 and would personally head the government's delegation (as demanded by the KNSB). The start of negotiations, however, does not yet mean giving up the strike, said Prof. Petkov. As he told PARI, the prime minister would go to considerable compromise to get an agreement. However, the stands of the prime minister and the KNSB as to the protection of the population's savings and of local producers are completely opposite, he said. If the demands of the KNSB are to be met, that would require actions by parliament, too, Prof. Petkov pointed out repeatedly. During the period between May 4 and May 17, there will be regional protests as well as protests in different branches and enterprises, indefinite strikes included. The KNSB press centre reported that the police in Burgas (southern Bulgaria, on the Black Sea) and Dupnitsa (western Bulgaria, 60 km from Sofia) arrested seven trade union activists, including the Confederation's Executive Secretary Doncho Donchev. [passage omitted] KNSB leader Krustyu Petkov met with Interior Minister Viktor Mikhaylov and the Chief Secretary of the Interior Ministry Kosta Bogatsevski. According to Mr. Mikhaylov and Mr. Bogatsevski, the trade union leaders had not been arrested. They were only taken to the police station for questioning and statements were drawn up. Tomorrow morning and the day after tomorrow Prof. Petkov and General Kosta Bogatsevski will go to Dupnitsa and Nesebur to sort things out on the spot.
