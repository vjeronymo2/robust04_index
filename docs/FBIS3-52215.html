Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The first round of talks between the foreign secretaries of Pakistan and India on Kashmir and other related issues was held at the Foreign Office in Islamabad this morning. After the first round, the Indian foreign secretary, Mr. J.N. Dixit, and his Pakistani counter, Mr. Shaharyar Khan, left for Karachi where the Indian foreign secretary will call on the prime minister, Ms. Benazir Bhutto, this afternoon. Another round of talks between the two foreign secretaries will be held tonight. Earlier, the Indian foreign secretary called on the president, Sardar Farooq Ahmad Khan Leghari. A Foreign Office spokesman told our representative that the president reiterated Pakistan's desire for a peaceful solution of the Kashmir dispute which is the core problem between Pakistan and India. The president urged India to end its repression in occupied Kashmir.
