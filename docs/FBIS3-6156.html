Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Industry Minister Sanan Khachonprasat will visit Rangoon next month to witness the signing of a Memorandum of Understanding [MoU] if a deal to purchase gas from Burma is completed. Negotiations between the Petroleum Authority of Thailand and Burmese government to buy natural gas from the Martabang site are expected to be settled this month, said Maj-Gen Sanan vesterday. The gas project covers two exploratory plots, M5 and M6, over an area of 26,140 square metres. France's Total Co Ltd has been developing the site since 1992 with Unocal Myanmar Co holding shares in the company. "The Burmese side would like to have the minister witness the MoU signing after the deal is settled, as far as I know," said Thai Ambassador to Rangoon Wirasak Futrakun by telephone. Maj-Gen Sanan said he would also meet privately with Burmese Forestry Minister Lt-Gen Chit Swe to discuss an extension to the timeframe during which Thai companies can remove felled logs still in Burmese territory.
