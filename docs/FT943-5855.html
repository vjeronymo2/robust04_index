940901 FT 01 SEP 94 / World Stock Markets: Emerging Markets Politics continued to dominate the Brazilian market last week as the latest opinion polls suggested that Mr Fernando Henrique Cardoso could win in the first round of the general election on October 3. At the same time, steady inflows of foreign cash continued to bolster the local currency, which has appreciated against the US dollar by 11 per cent since the reais was introduced on July 1. Mr Anthony Parker at Baring Securities commented that experience showed that successful investors traditionally sold when the market looked expensive and bought when it looked cheap, but the country could now be at a watershed and have a reforming president for the next four years, or even longer. 'This could mean that investors will continue to buy,' he said. 'We think the market could comfortably go up by another 15 per cent before investors become worried about the multiple.
