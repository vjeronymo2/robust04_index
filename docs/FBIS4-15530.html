BFN [Text] At the initiative of the United Nations High Commission for Refugees [UNHCR], quadrilateral talks were held in Termez, Uzbekistan, attended by Tajikistan, Russia, Uzbekistan, and the Islamic State of Afghanistan. The speedy repatriation of Tajik refugees from Afghanistan were discussed at the talks. Summing up the talks, Himmat Davlatov, deputy minister and head of the refugees affairs department of the Tajik Ministry of Labor and Employment, told the Khovar news agency that he and Deputy Foreign Minister Mirzoyev represented Tajikistan at the talks. Also present were Zainulobiddinov, consul of the Russian Federation in Mazar-e Sharif, Afghanistan; (?Irgachev), ambassador on special affairs at the Foreign Affairs Ministry of Uzbekistan; and General Sakhi Hashem, deputy to General Dostum and representative of northern regions of Afghanistan. Participants in the talks unanimously decided that the social and political situation in Tajikistan is returning to normal and that refugees can return to their permanent places of residence without any problems. The UNHCR representative at the talks said that Tajik refugees returning home from the Sakhi camp are being given three months of food rations. The same humanitarian assistance applies to refugees who returning home from Konduz Province in Afghanistan.
