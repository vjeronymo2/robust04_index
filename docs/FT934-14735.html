931015 FT 15 OCT 93 / Uruguay Round draws fire from Perot camp MR ROSS PEROT, the Texas billionaire, is refusing comment on a report that he will pitch his political organisation, United We Stand, and his extensive resources behind opposition to a Uruguay Round agreement, Nancy Dunne writes from Washington. A spokesman for United We Stand said he has not yet taken a position on the Round, in order to focus his energies on defeating the North American Free Trade Agreement. Mr Perot co-wrote a book detailing criticism of Nafta after claiming, during the presidential debates, that 'a giant sucking sound' would draw American jobs over the US-Mexican border. His co-author, Mr Pat Choate, yesterday said he opposed Uruguay Round provisions weakening US dumping laws and establish-ing a multilateral trading organisation to oversee the world trading system. Ms Lori Wallach, a prominent anti-Nafta organiser with Public Citizen, which works closely with the Perot forces, said many of the same groups which oppose the Nafta will also fight a Uruguay Round pact. 'Ross Perot feels about Nafta the way a vampire feels about garlic,' she said. 'Ross Perot feels about Gatt the way a vampire feels about daylight. One he regards with revulsion; the other means death.'
