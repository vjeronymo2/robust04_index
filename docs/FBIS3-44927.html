Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Jakarta, January 7 (XINHUA) -- Ren Jianxin, visiting president of the Chinese Supreme People's Court, held talks here today with Purwoto S. Gandasubrata, chief justice of Indonesia. During the talks, the two sides exchanged views on cooperation in the judiciary field. For his part, President Ren Jianxin raised five proposals which received positive response from the Indonesian side. Ren Jianxin suggested to strengthen exchanges of visits between top judiciary leaders of the two countries. He invited Indonesian Chief Justice Purwoto to visit China at his convenience, and Purwoto accepted the invitation. Ren Jianxin also proposed that the exchanges of visits will be held among local courts and ordinary justices between the two countries, adding that the two countries should provide each other with judiciary data and documents. He wished that the two countries will continue to implement the memorandum of understanding on legal affairs cooperation signed by justice ministers of China and Indonesia in 1992. He suggested that the two countries consider the signing of an agreement on judiciary assistance. Chief Justice Purwoto briefed the Chinese guests on Indonesia's legal system and the working state of affairs of the court. Earlier, President Ren Jianxin paid a courtesy call on H. Wahono [name as received], chairman of the House of Representatives and chairman of the People's Consultative Congress. President Ren Jianxin and his entourage left here today for Yogyakarta and Bali. They will leave Indonesia on January 11 for Singapore.
