Language: Portuguese Article Type:BFN [Text] Lieutenant Colonel [name indistinct], British military attache in Mozambique, told Radio Mozambique today that the training of the new army's first six infantry battalions is expected to begin on 21 February. The training to be provided by Mozambican instructors trained in Nyanga, Zimbabwe and will take place in Boane, Manica, and Dondo training centers. Each battalion will be made up of 700 men to be recruited from the government and Renamo armies. The training will be supervised by British troops which will begin to arrive in Mozambique next week, coming from Zimbabwe and Great Britain [words indistinct] three weeks.
