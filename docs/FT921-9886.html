920210 FT 10 FEB 92 / Quayle favours UK nuclear deterrent US VICE-PRESIDENT Dan Quayle, who arrives in London today for talks with Conservative and Labour opposition leaders, strongly favours Britain retaining its nuclear deterrent, writes Lionel Barber in Washington In an interview with the Financial Times, Mr Quayle said the deterrent remained a force for stability in Europe. 'If Great Britain wants to relinquish its nuclear capability, who do you want to abdicate this to? Pakistan?' he asked. Page 28; Editorial comment, Page 12
