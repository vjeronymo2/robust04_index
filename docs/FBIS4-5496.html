BFN [Text] Tokyo, April 12 KYODO -- Prime Minister and Japan New Party (JNP) leader Morihiro Hosokawa indicated Tuesday there will be a recombination of parties in the eight-month-old ruling coalition, party officials said. Hosokawa, in a meeting with several JNP legislators, was quoted as saying, "the coalition needs to accept a new group." The seven party coalition has shown signs of a split over the choice of a successor to Hosokawa who announced the resignation last Friday. Three of the coalition parties -- Shinseito, Komeito and the JNP -- have strengthened solidarity, while the Social Democratic Party, new party Sakigake and the Democratic Socialist Party are forming a bloc against them. There have [been] emerging signs of a split also in the main opposition party, the Liberal Democratic Party (LDP). Former Foreign Minister Michio Watanabe of the LDP has indicated willingness to join hands with the Shinseito-led group to run for the premiership.
