Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Ulaanbaatar, February 23 (OANA-MONTSAME) -- The central power system of the Mongolian capital beginning since March 1 is likely to have to restrict the electricity consumption in pursuant to a specific schedule to be arranged. That is connected with the running out stock of coal at the electric power stations as well as with the financial difficulties. Due to the fact that corresponding payments are not being paid timely by its consumers the central power system has had arrears of more than four million US dollars ought to have been paid to the Russian Federation for the electricity supplied in the previous year. Also it failed to pay off its tariffs to be paid in advance for electricity for the current year which runs to 2.5 million dollars.
