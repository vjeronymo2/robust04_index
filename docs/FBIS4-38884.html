BFN [Text] Belarusian Prime Minister Vyacheslav Kebich's political popularity has doubled, officials at the Belarusian Political Research Center Eridan announced today. Kebich leads the list of the officially registered candidates to the post of president with 372,000 signatures in support of his candidacy. Ex-speaker of the Belarusian parliament Stanislav Shushkevich and leader of the Agrarian Party Aleksandr Dubko secured the smallest number of signatures (123,000 and 117,000, respectively). The other candidates are former head of the temporary parliamentary commission for combating corruption Aleksandr Lukashenko, Secretary of the Communist Party's Central Committee Vassiliy Novikov and leader of the Popular Front Zenon Poznyak. An opinion poll held by the Eridan Center revealed that the popularity of Lukashenko, Novikov and Dubko has increased from 2 to 4 percent since April, while that of Shushkevich has gone down from 9.8 to 7.1 percent and Poznyak -- from 5.4 to 4 percent. The same poll showed that 57.8 percent of the respondents welcome the unification of the Russian and Belarusian monetary systems, and 77.7 percent favored the creation of a Belarusian-Russian financial and economic union.
