BFN [Text] Bucharest ROMPRES, 19/4/1994 -- The minister of foreign affairs of the Republic of Armenia, Vahan Papazyan, was received on Tuesday, April 19, at the Cotroceni Palace, by Romanian President Ion Iliescu. In a statement to the press made at the end of the interview, the Armenian chief diplomat underscored the necessity of developing the bilateral relations as the main subject of his dialogue with the Romanian head of state along with an analysis of several issues of the international life, especially the situation in Nagorno- Karabakh and in Bosnia-Herzegovina as Romania and Armenia were equally interested in their settlement. The Armenian minister of foreign affairs delivered to the Romanian president a message from the part of the Armenian president together with the invitation to visit Armenia. The interview was attended also by Romanian Foreign Minister Teodor Melescanu, Presidential Adviser Traian Chebeleu and Varujan Vosganian, deputy in the Romanian Parliament representing the Armenian minority of Romania. Armenian ambassador Guergui Kazinyan [name as received] presented on Monday, April 18, his credentials while the Armenian Embassy in Bucharest is going to be officially opened within the following days.
