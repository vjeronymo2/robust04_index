(34) CFC&hyph;12 Motor Vehicle Air Conditioners, Retrofit. (a) HFC&hyph;134a. HFC&hyph;134a is acceptable as a substitute for CFC&hyph;12 in retrofitted motor vehicle air conditioners. HFC&hyph;134a does not contribute to ozone depletion. HFC&hyph;134a's GWP and atmospheric lifetime are close to those of other alternatives which have been determined to be acceptable for this end-use. However, HFC&hyph;134a's contribution to global warming could be significant in leaky end-uses such as MVACS. EPA has determined that the use of HFC&hyph;134a in these applications is acceptable because industry continues to develop technology to limit emissions. In addition, the number of available substitutes for use in MVACS is currently limited. HFC&hyph;134a is not flammable and its toxicity is low. While HFC&hyph;134a is compatible with most existing refrigeration and air conditioning equipment parts, it is not compatible with the mineral oils currently used in such systems. An ester-based lubricant should be used rather than mineral oils.
