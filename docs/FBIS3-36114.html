Language: Estonian Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Foreign Minister Juri Luik signed the Partnership for Peace agreement at the NATO headquarters, at a NATO secretariat session. NATO had already signed the agreement previously. The secretariat session was chaired by Deputy Secretary-General Amedeo De Fanchis. After the signing Fanchis and Luik intend to have a tete-a-tete conversation. [passage omitted] Estonia is the fourth country to join the program, after Poland, Romania, and Lithuania.
