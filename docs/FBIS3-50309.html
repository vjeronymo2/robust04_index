Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Prague, Jan 25 (CTK) -- The number of job seekers registered in the Czech Republic at the end of 1993 reached 185,000, which represented 3.5 percent unemployment rate, according to a Czech Statistical Office (CSU) report made on the basis of data from the Czech Ministry of Labor and Social Affairs. The number of unemployed reached its peak in December. It was falling at the beginning of the year (from 158,000 in January to 136,000 in May) and then increasing again. There were 53,938 available jobs in December which was the lowest number for the entire last year. It means there were 3.4 applicants for each vacancy. The highest unemployment rate was in north Moravia (6.2 percent) and north Bohemia (4.2 percent). As for districts, the north Moravian Bruntal was the most affected (8.7 percent), followed by Znojmo, south Moravia, with 8.5 percent. The lowest unemployment rate was in Prague (0.3 percent) and west Bohemia (2.6 percent).
