Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, March 14 (XINHUA) -- Chinese President Jiang Zemin, general secretary of the Central Committee of the Chinese Communist Party (CPC), met here this afternoon with Japanese Diet member Susumu Nikaido, adviser to Japan's Liberal Democratic Party. Jiang welcomed Nikaido and his party, saying that Nikaido, an old friend of the Chinese people, had made outstanding contributions in helping the former Japanese Prime Minister Kakuei Tanaka in 1972 realize the normalization of bilateral relations and the development of Sino-Japanese friendly relations. Jiang said that since the normalization of bilateral relations, friendly cooperation between the two countries in all fields had made much progress and bilateral relations had entered a new stage. He pointed out that China has long paid much attention to the development of a good-neighborly relationship with Japan, hoping that the two countries will continue to enhance understanding and strengthen cooperation so as to bring Sino-Japanese relations to a new height. Jiang said: "I met with Japanese Prime Minister Morihiro Hosokawa last year in seattle and we are expecting Hosokawa to visit China." Nikaido said that since relations between the two countries returned to normal in 1972, bilateral friendly relations and cooperation have been developing. He said that although he was now aged 84, he would try to contribute to the further development of bilateral friendly relations. President of the China-Japan Friendship Association Sun Pinghua and Vice-Foreign Minister Tang Jiaxuan took part in the meeting. Nikaido and his party came here at the invitation of the China-Japan Friendship Association. Before coming to Beijing, Nikaido attended a ceremony on March 12 to mark the completion of the Shanghai Baori High-Molecular Material Co., which is a joint venture between the Shanghai Metallurgical Construction Corporation and the Sanken Industry Co. of Japan. The joint venture is expected to produce heat-insulating and fire-proof materials.
