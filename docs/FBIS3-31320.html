Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Text] A meeting between Army General Rasim Delic, commander of the General Staff of the Supreme Command of Bosnia-Herzegovina Army, and General Jean Cot, UNPROFOR commander for the former Yugoslavia, has been held at one of the UNPROFOR bases in central Bosnia today. The talks were attended by Brigadier General Vahid Karavelic, commander of the 1st Corps of the Army of the Republic of Bosnia-Herzegovina. The subject of the talks was the UNPROFOR activity to implement the 10 February NATO declaration on removing Serbian aggressor's heavy weapons to 20 km from Sarajevo and putting it under UNPROFOR control. On this occasion, Gen. Jean Cot informed generals Delic and Karavelic about UNPROFOR activities undertaken so far on implementing NATO's decisions, expressing their total determination to bring to a successful conclusion the job that has been begun. During today's talks, Gen. Delic pointed out that the Army of Bosnia-Herzegovina will completely respect all the obligations that our country's state bodies have undertaken in realizing this significant task. Apart from the talks on withdrawing the Serbian aggressor's artillery from around Sarajevo, also touched upon were measures that the international community and UNPROFOR should undertake to stop the offensive on Bihac, a region that the Security Council has proclaimed a protected zone.
