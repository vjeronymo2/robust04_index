BFN [Text] Iraq is closing its embassy in Australia as part of a second round of closures of missions in middle-ranking countries. Financial reasons are being cited, according to Australian officials. The first round of closures occurred six months ago. Iraq's missions in Australia, South Korea, Argentina, and Hungary are among the closures in this second round. Australian officials say the Iraqi Government plans to maintain some properties in Australia, but it is yet to decide how it will be represented in Australia. Australia does not maintain a mission in Iraq, but it is represented via Amman in Jordan.
