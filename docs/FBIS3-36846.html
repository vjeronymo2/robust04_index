Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Mikhail Shevtsov] [Text] Moscow February 9 TASS -- Russian Defence Minister Pavel Grachev ended a three-day visit to the Volga Military District and returned to Moscow on Wednesday evening. In an interview with ITAR-TASS before departure to Moscow Grachev said that he was satisfied with good combat readiness of Russian troops which was confirmed by the results of an inspection of five military units belonging to the military district. The defence minister positively assessed the construction of houses for Russian servicemen withdrawn from abroad, adding that the construction was going ahead at a fast pace. Grachev visited three regions and one republic and pointed out that he was satisfied with the attitude of local authorities to servicemen. On Wednesday, Grachev visited troops stationed in the Ulyanovsk region, defence-related enterprises and met with regional heads and the city administration. While at the "Aviastar" joint stock society, the biggest aviation enterprise in Europe, Grachev said that in the near future priority in defence aircraft will be given to military-transport planes capable of transporting forces of the Defence Ministry to any region. This task is envisaged by the Russian military doctrine which lays the emphasis on mobile rapid deployment force.
