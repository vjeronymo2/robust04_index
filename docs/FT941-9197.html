940216 FT 16 FEB 94 / Threat to miners' gala mounts The survival of the Durham Miners Gala, Britain's biggest and most evocative annual working-class rally, looked further in doubt yesterday after the Durham Miners Association executive concluded that it could not afford to finance the event this year. Faced with the demise of the Durham coalfield and the consequent loss of income, it is to appeal to the trade union and Labour movement to try to stage this year's 110th gala. The northern region executive of the Trades Union Congress will discuss the appeal today. Mr Dave Hopper, general secretary of the association and the National Union of Mineworkers' north-east area, said after yesterday's meeting that if this year's gala were held, it would constitute a 'dignified end'. At issue is not only the Pounds 20,000 needed to mount the Durham city event but its identity as a socialist rally to the left of the present Labour leadership. In earlier years it staunchly promoted Labour policy. But the party leader has not attended since 1987. In spite of the gala's emotional resonance, it looks improbable that other unions will provide unconditional funding. Mr Bob Howard, northern TUC secretary, said: 'If it was to be without control of the mainstream TUC and Labour movement, it's unlikely that would be acceptable.'
