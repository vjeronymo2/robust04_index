December 30, 1989, Saturday, Valley Edition

DIGEST / LOCAL NEWS IN BRIEF: 2 SHELTERS FOR THE HOMELESS TO STAY OPEN ON HOLIDAY WEEKEND

Two cold weather shelters for the homeless will be open throughout the holiday weekend in the San Fernando Valley, Los Angeles city officials said Friday.

Single adults and adult couples can find shelter in the Van Nuys National Guard Armory at 17330 Victory Blvd. Families with children will be housed at the Sylmar National Guard Armory at 12860 Arroyo St.

The shelters, which opened Friday night, will remain open at least through Tuesday morning, city officials said.

The city's emergency program for housing the homeless is activated when the temperature dips below 40 degrees or when the temperature drops below 50 and there is a 50% chance of rain. Temperatures in the San Fernando Valley were forecast to fall below 40 degrees Friday night.

The city will provide transportation to the shelters between 5:30 and 7 p.m. from four sites in the Valley: Van Nuys City Hall, 14410 Sylvan St.; Valley Community Clinic, 5648 Vineland Ave., North Hollywood; Valley Rescue Mission, 11741 Glenoaks Blvd., San Fernando; and El Centro de Amistad, 7024 Deering Ave., Canoga Park.

More information about emergency homeless services may be obtained by calling a toll-free number, 1-800-548-6047.
