BFN [Text] The Human Rights Committee of South Africa says thedeath toll from violence in South Africa has dropped by 60 percent from 487 in April to 195 last month. The committee says in a report that the decrease gives a clear signal that the peace, which prevailed during the election days, has been maintained. It says that despite ongoing tensions in KwaZulu-Natal, violence has continued to drop in this region, with the death toll last month being the lowest recorded by the committee since January 1993.
