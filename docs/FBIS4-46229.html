BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on Colombian guerrilla activities monitored through 1 June. A few hours after the Cano Limon-Covenas Pipeline had resumed operations following 20 days of semiparalysis by the fire that destroyed five plants, guerrillas dynamited the line yesterday, knocking it out of service. The rebel attack, which caused the spilling of more than 5,000 barrels of crude, occurred at km 437 in the Municipality of El Tarra, in northern Santander Department. (Santa Fe de Bogota EL TIEMPO in Spanish 28 May 94 p9a) The murder of Aguedo Calle, a member of the Communist Party's regional electoral commission, Chigorodo, in the Uraba banana region, disclosed on Thursday evening the existence of a new group that takes justice into its own hands. Although the new, self- styled, paramilitary group the "People's Children" (Hijos del Pueblo) has not claimed responsibility for the crime, authorities believe it was one of its actions. Alleged spokespersons for that group have sent a communique to the Chigorodo Municipal Prosecuting Office claiming responsibility for the murder of Gabriel Ortega, the Patriotic Union candidate for mayor, who was shot in Chigorodo last week. (Santa Fe de Bogota EL TIEMPO in Spanish 28 May 94 p 9a) A guerrilla terrorist plan to destroy political and government offices, as well as the Justice Palace in Cucuta, was discovered in northern Santander Department yesterday. In addition, an action to disrupt the elections in Cali was discovered by the 3d Army Brigade. Meanwhile, the killing of two soldiers and wounding of six were the results of a clash between troops of the 12th Army Brigade and FARC (Revolutionary Armed Forces of Colombia) rebels in Guacamayas, San Vicente del Caguan (Caqueta). (Santa Fe de Bogota EL TIEMPO in Spanish 29 May 94 p 24A) Three members of the Civil Registry Office in Cucuta who were kidnapped last Sunday by Army of National Liberation rebels were released last night. The three officials are safe and sound. (Santa Fe de Bogota Emisoras Caracol Network in Spanish 1200 GMT 31 May 94)
