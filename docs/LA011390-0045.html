January 13, 1990, Saturday, Home Edition

50 NORIEGA ASSOCIATES, WIFE FACE CHARGES

Atty. Gen. Rogelio Cruz has brought charges against 50 members of the regime of deposed strongman Manuel A. Noriega, the independent La Prensa newspaper reported Friday.

The targets of the charges, which range from illicit use of government funds to cover-up, include Noriega's wife Felicidad, now in hiding in the Cuban ambassador's residence, and former President Manuel Solis Palma, the newspaper said.

La Prensa said that Solis Palma had been called in twice for questioning by the attorney general's office on charges of "impeding the formation, functioning and renovation of state organs." If convicted, he could face 15 years in jail, it said.
