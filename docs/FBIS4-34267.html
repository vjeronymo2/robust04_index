BFN [Text] Buenos Aires, 16 Jun (EFE) -- Panamanian President Guillermo Endara today began a private visit to Buenos Aires through a special invitation from his Argentine colleague, Carlos Menem. The two chiefs of state travelled together to Buenos Aires from the Colombian city of Cartagena de Indias, where the Fourth Ibero-American Summit closed on 15 June. During the summit, the Central American countries obtained economic support from Colombia, Argentina, and Venezuela and political support for Honduras in its quest for a seat on the UN Security Council and the holding of the Ibero-American Summit in Panama in the year 2000. Endara's stay in Buenos Aires, where he will meet several times with Menem, will conclude on 19 June, according to the Honduran [as received] Embassy in Buenos Aires.
