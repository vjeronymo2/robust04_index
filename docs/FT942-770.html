940628 FT 28 JUN 94 / Bottomley attacks 'federalist dreams' Mrs Virginia Bottomley, the health secretary, yesterday underlined the growing appeal of anti-federalist thinking in the Conservative party by endorsing the prime minister's recent statements on Europe. Mrs Bottomley has traditionally been associated with the centrist and pro-European part of the Tory party. But in a surprisingly Euro-sceptic speech she attacked the 'pied pipers of federalism', saying they were 'losing their followers, even in countries where their ideas were once so applauded'. Delivering the Alison Tennant Memorial lecture in London, Mrs Bottomley said the institutions of Europe needed to forget their 'centralist and federalist dreams'. She applauded the prime minister's recent call for a multi-speed, multi-track Europe, saying that it was an idea that appealed far beyond the British Conservative party. 'Some have argued that the only way to consolidate Europe's achievements is to roll forward towards ever closer economic and political integration,' she said. 'That view is misguided. The voters have rumbled it. Increasingly the call is for nations to build Europe, not for Europe to supersede nations.'
