April 9, 1989, Sunday, Orange County Edition

LETTER'S EFFECT ON OLD ACQUAINTANCE

Re the letter by Dr. William Moss (April 2):

We came to Santa Ana 33 years ago, the same year as Moss. We knew him then as a fine general surgeon who practiced high quality life-saving medicine. He later chose to limit his practice to life-preventing medicine (performing tubal ligations and vasectomies), and now he is specializing in life-destroying procedures. It saddens us to know that he is the face and name behind the Tustin abortion clinic.

His letter of thanks to the Tustin police and the pro-choice advocates who were at his clinic to "protect" him and his patients has made us even more aware that those of us who are advocates of the pro-life cause -- which holds that all human life is sacred and has dignity and value -- must become more vocal and visible.

It seems abortionists have protectors, animals have protectors, even car owners are told by an American manufacturer that "protection is your right." Only human babies not yet born are unprotected.

Dr. JOHN McANDREW

THERESE McANDREW

Santa Ana
