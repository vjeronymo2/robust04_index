BFN [Text] Cairo, 27 Jun (MENA)--President Husni Mubarak returned to Cairo this afternoon following a two-day visit to the Libyan al-Jamahiriyah, during which he held intensive talks with Libyan revolution leader Brother Colonel Mu'ammar al-Qadhdhafi that dealt with the Libyan-Western crisis, "Lockerbie," and Egypt's efforts to find a solution to it. The two leaders' talks also focused on developments in the Yemeni crisis, the Middle East peace talks developments, and the situation on all the tracks of the Arab-Israeli negotiations. The talks also dealt with developments in Africa, the outcome of the recent OAU summit in Tunis, and the consultations held by the African leaders on the summit sidelines. The Egyptian and Libyan delegations also discussed bilateral relations and assessed their views of the decisions already made by the higher Egyptian-Libyan committee. The two sides discussed within this context the implementation of the project to extend by 170 kms the railroad between Musa'id and Tubruq. The official delegation that accompanied the president during the visit returned with him.
