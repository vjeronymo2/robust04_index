BFN [Text] Beijing, May 15 (XINHUA) -- Ding Guangen, member of the Political Bureau and the secretariat of the Central Committee of the Communist Party of China (CPC), met with a delegation from the Social Democratic Party of Japan (SDPJ), headed by its Secretary- General Kubo Wataru, here this afternoon. The 27-member delegation including 11 Japanese congressmen arrived here today at the invitation of the CPC. Ding, on behalf of the CPC Central Committee, extended a warm welcome to the delegation, saying that the SDPJ had made long-term efforts for the normalization and sound development of Sino-Japanese ties before it set up relations with the CPC 10 years ago. After the establishment of relations between the CPC and SDPJ in 1984, he noted, the SDPJ has played a positive role in promoting Sino-Japanese ties. Ding stressed that in the current situation today, no matter what changes on the Japanese political scene, the CPC will positively develop its friendly exchanges and cooperation with the SDPJ on the basis of the principles of independence, total equality, mutual respect and non-interference in each other's internal affairs. He expressed the belief that the delegation's current China visit will make new contributions to the two parties' ties and the two peoples' friendly cooperation. Wataru said that his party attaches great importance to the ties with the CPC and China. The main purpose of their current China visit is to celebrate the tenth anniversary of the establishment of relations between the SPDJ and CPC, he added. He pointed out that a sound relationship between Japan and China is in the best interests of the peoples of the two countries and beneficial to world peace and stability. After the meeting Ding hosted a dinner in honor of the delegation.
