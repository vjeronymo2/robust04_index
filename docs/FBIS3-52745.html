Language: Persian Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] 'Aziz Morad, spokesman for the Afghan president, in an interview with the Central News Unit in Kabul spoke of current battles in Kabul as a holy war and a second revolution. He said: These battles will continue until there is total victory over the opposition. He also announced an agreement on a temporary cease-fire to transfer the wounded. The spokesman for Rabbani also spoke about heavy clashes in northern Afghanistan and around the city of Mazar-e-Sharif and denied the announcement of any cease-fire in that city. 'Aziz Morad said three areas in the Faryab Province, in northern Afghanistan, have come under the control of forces of General Esmai'l Khan, an important commander of Jami'at-e Eslami [Islamic Society of Afghanistan] in western Afghanistan. Hezb-e Eslami [Islamic Party of Afghanistan] and the Northern Meli-ye Eslami [Islamic Front] Movement say they have also achieved fresh victories in the battle fields in northern Afghanistan. They announced the main condition for cease-fire is the resignation of the president and the formation of a summit between the leaders of jehadi and political groups. [passage omitted]
