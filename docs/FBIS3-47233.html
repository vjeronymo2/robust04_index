Language: French Article Type:BFN [Text] Phnom Penh 11 Jan (AKP) -- The prevention of hostile activities by the Khmer Rouge is a duty of the Royal Government of Cambodia [RGC], but it is also a commitment of the international community. This was emphasized by Sar Kheng, deputy prime minister and co-interior minister, at a meeting on Sunday, 9 January, with a U.S. congressional delegation led by Mr. Wilson of the Foreign Relations Commission. Mr. Sar Kheng briefed his guests about the recent appointment of provincial governors, the new administrative structures organized in line with the principle of national reconciliation and unity, and the mutual understanding between major political parties elected to the National Assembly under the clear-sightedness of His Majesty Norodom Sihanouk. For his part, Mr. Wilson commended the close cooperation between the Cambodian People's Party and the FUNCINPEC [National United Front for an Independent, Neutral, Peaceful, and Cooperative Cambodia] Party. The United States, he added, is delighted to see respect for human rights and for the Kingdom of Cambodia's Constitution. The U.S. senator expressed his confidence that the RGC would be able to defend security and bring about peace for the Cambodian people. Mr. Charles Twining, U.S. ambassador to Cambodia was also present at the audience.
