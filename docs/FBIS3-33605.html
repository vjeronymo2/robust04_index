Language: Portuguese Article Type:BFN [From the FOLHA DE SAO PAULO Salvador bureau] [Text] After 36 years of activity the JORNAL DA BAHIA, the state's second oldest newspaper, yesterday fired about 200 workers and folded. A communique issued by the newspaper's board of directors reported that the economic crisis was responsible for the newspaper's folding. Chief Editor Isaias Raw, 62, said the newspaper had been operating for two years at a deficit. According to Raw, JORNAL DA BAHIA had an average edition of 18,000 copies (5,000 subscribers) and was mainly circulating in the capital of Bahia and in the metropolitan region of Salvador.
