May 6, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

THEY REALLY WANT TO GROW UP TO BE RICH

Dr. Joyce Brothers (April 24)) gives some fascinating explanations of why young people are motivated to enter various careers. She sees medicine as attractive to those interested in a "nurturing profession (where one) . . . can help others."

Law is attractive to young people "who feel they were unfairly treated by adults." On the other hand, Dr. Brothers takes the position that teaching, and religious ministry, are attractive to those who "like to give orders and be seen as authorities."

Get real, Joyce. Young people attracted to law and medicine are mostly drawn by the promise of big bucks; and the desire to help and nurture is stronger among those who go into teaching and the clergy.

I imagine that Dr. Brothers would assert that young people are drawn to the profession of psychology because they believe that they will eat of the fruit of the Tree of Knowledge and become like gods. After reading her column, I think she could use a few more mouthfuls.

LARRY SELK, Los Angeles
