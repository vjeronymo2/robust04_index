November 8, 1990, Thursday, Home Edition

WHO'S HOT

* Matthew Hicks, a 6-foot-5, 285-pound tackle for El Rancho. "He's the most dominant run blocker I've ever seen on the high school level," Dons Coach Dick Shelko said.

* Mitch Bledsoe of Valley Christian, who ran for 367 yards and five touchdowns as the Crusaders defeated Brethren, 33-21.

* Darnell Morgan of Norwalk, who gained 240 yards in a 35-25 victory over Artesia.

* La Mirada quarterback Carlos Jimenez, who completed 11 of 13 passes in a 36-7 victory over Bellflower.

* Downey quarterback Johnny Macon, who ran for 92 yards and kicked a field goal in a 24-6 victory over Dominguez.

Who's Not

* Pius X, whose 24-6 loss to Serra was its 28th defeat in a row. The Warriors have the longest current losing streak in the state.

* Centennial, which lost its 23rd consecutive game, 31-0, to South Torrance. The Apaches have been outscored this season, 354-34.

* Compton, which lost its seventh straight game, 55-12, to Jordan. The 42 points Jordan scored in the first half set a Moore League record.

* Bellflower, which lost its sixth game in a row, 36-7, to La Mirada. The Bucs are 2-7 overall and 0-4 in the Suburban League.

* Dominguez, which lost to Downey, 24-6, to slip to 5-4 overall and 1-4 in the San Gabriel Valley League.
