February 22, 1989, Wednesday, Home Edition

SOUTHLAND CONGESTION

Fielding's column was interesting and had some excellent suggestions but it certainly did not focus on the cause of downtown congestion -- too many people driving into the downtown area. The reason more and more people are coming downtown is plain enough to see if you come downtown -- high-rise office buildings of 40-plus stories. It is these hives that attract the bees.

It is obvious that the more hives you build the more bees you will attract and the more congestion you will have. Does this mean that our mayor and the developers want more congestion or don't see the connection between beehives and bees? Of course not. They know. But they have a plan to relieve future gridlock -- the subway at $259 million a mile.

Will the subway relieve congestion? It hasn't in San Francisco, Miami or any other city where it has been tried. They are all multibillion-dollar white elephants that have to be subsidized by the taxpayer. But the Los Angeles Metro Rail will be different say its sponsors. It will be as successful as New York City's subway. At the rate we are building high-rises downtown that may well prove true. I can hardly wait.

JOSEPH S. DUARTE

Whittier
