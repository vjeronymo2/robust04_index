931025 FT 25 OCT 93 / Building chiefs warn of job losses AT LEAST 100,000 further jobs could be lost in the construction industry if next month's Budget fails to maintain capital spending, industry leaders have warned. Mr Ian Dixon, chairman of the Construction Industry Council, and Mr Tony Jackson, chairman of the Construction Industry Employers Council, said the 'unique spectre' of a second Budget in one year had restricted the industry's recovery. They said any improvement in this recovery was so fragile that it could easily be shattered if there were signs of reductions in capital spending. 'More time is needed for the private sector to recover,' they said. Employment in the construction industry has fallen by more than 500,000 since the middle of 1989. They also warned that the prospect of private-sector finance going into public works was still distant, although the idea was strongly supported by the industry. There were fears in the industry that privately funded schemes might be seen as an opportunity to replace publicly financed infrastructure projects, when they should be used to accelerate infrastructure improvement. Meanwhile, the Federation of Master Builders reported a small improvement in workloads in its third-quarter state of trade survey. It said 18 per cent of companies reported an increase in work, 55 per cent stayed at the same level, and 27 per cent recorded a decrease. The second-quarter figures were 15, 55 and 30 per cent respectively. Mr Ted Evans, national president, said the improvement came mainly among companies doing repair and maintenance work. He added: 'However, the reports of improvement are so lean and patchy that little or no encouragement can be drawn from the figures.'
