BFN [Text] Brussels, April 1, (AFP) -- NATO officials Friday [1 April] welcomed Russia's assurances that it would join the Western security body's Partnership for Peace [PfP] program later this month as planned, after reports that there could be a delay. "Of course we welcome Russia's signing of Partnership for Peace, as we were expecting it to do by the end of April," said an official from the North Atlantic Treaty Organisation.
