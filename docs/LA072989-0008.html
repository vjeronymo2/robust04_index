July 29, 1989, Saturday, Home Edition

REBECCA SCHAEFFER'S MURDER: THINGS THAT WENT WRONG

A lonely man worships and adores a beautiful, distant woman. She's the only brightness in his life. Then, one day he shoots her. We react in disbelief. Who can fathom such behavior? Who can explain it? Finally, we content ourselves with a resigned "there is no explaining it."

But of course that's nonsense. The behavior is entirely fathomable, it's easy to explain. It's the behavior of a jealous child who takes his friend's new toy and breaks it -- it's "If I can't have it, no one else will." It's selfishness, only selfishness taken to the most awful level.

What is unfathomable is our part in this. We let it get to that awful level. We provide the gun.

Year after year America puts guns in the hands of sick men who are no better equipped to manage them than those jealous children would be. Then we react in disbelief when tragedy results. We make it easy to get guns and we make it easy to kill.

Gun control makes it harder. America still can't understand that. That's the only thing there truly is no explaining.

CHRISTOPHER LLOYD

Los Angeles
