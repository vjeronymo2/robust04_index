BFN [Text] Mexico City, 3 Jun (PL) -- The 20th Council of the Latin American Economic System (SELA) today approved a resolution rejecting the U.S.-imposed economic, trade, and financial blockade instituted against Cuba more than three decades ago. The document, which was studied by ministerial-level delegations, recognizes the importance of reaffirming the sovereignty of the region's countries and says in its text: "The need to put an end to the U.S.-imposed economic, trade, and financial blockade against Cuba. [sentence as received] "In view of paragraphs A and B of articles 3 and 4 and paragraphs 1 and 4 of article 5 of the Panama Agreement and considering resolution No. 112 regarding the `imposition of coercive economic measures' and resolutions 47/19 and 40/16 of the UN General Assembly, entitled `The Need To End The U.S.-Imposed Economic, Trade, and Financial Blockade Against Cuba'; "Whereas, there is the need to reaffirm, among other principles, the sovereign equality of states, nonintervention in their internal affairs, and the freedom of international trade and navigation recognized in numerous international legal instruments; "Whereas, free trade is an element that contributes to the people's economic development and well-being; and "Whereas, there is the need to boost trade relations in the American hemisphere, in adherence to the principle of free trade and navigation. "The Latin American Council resolves to reject the application of unilateral measures that affect free development of international commerce and contravene principles of international law. To this end, the Council urges the lifting of the economic, trade, and financial blockade against Cuba and expresses support for UN General Assembly resolution 48/16, which demands the elimination of unilateral actions that are incongruent with the international economic and political context."
