Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Datuk Sri Dr. Mahathir Mohamed says he will attend the Asia Pacific Economic Cooperation, APEC, informal summit in Jakarta in November, and again stressed the need for the forum not to be institutionalized. The prime minister, who skipped the first meeting in Seattle last year and caused dissatisfaction among some APEC members, said he would be attending the summit since it was decided by APEC. The inaugural summmit held in Seattle was initiated by United States President Bill Clinton. Speaking at the press conference after delivering a keynote address at the 10th International General Meeting of the Pacific Economic Cooperation Council in Kuala Lumpur, Datuk Sri Dr. Mahathir reiterated that APEC should conduct informal meetings. The prime minister said APEC should continue to exist as an informal meeting and should not institutionalized its meetings and have regular programs for a smooth [word indistinct]. But the forum should not bind its members and that these objectives should seek for members to know each other and understand their constructive point of views. To a question, Datuk Sri Dr. Mahathir said the Malaysian-initiated East Asia Economic Caucus, EAEC, is not institutionalized and that it is a forum to discuss common problems among countries in the region.
