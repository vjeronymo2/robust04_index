in Coahuila 94WB0038D Mexico City EXCELSIOR in Spanish 28 Apr 94 p 44-A 94WB0038D Mexico City EXCELSIOR Spanish CSO [Article by Rafael Medina Cruz] [Excerpt] Cocaine base and packed marijuana worth over 12 billion new pesos, and intercepted by Judicial Police agents in Coahuila State, were incinerated yesterday by the Attorney General's Office [PGR] in the municipalities of Saltillo, Torreon, Ciudad Acuna, Piedras Negras, and Monclova. Furthermore, during operations conducted in Baja California, Sonora, Baja California Sur, Jalisco, and Nuevo Leon, the police authorities arrested six presumed public poisoners, seizing more than 2.5 tons of cannabis indica intended to be taken to the United States for sale and consumption. The Attorney General's Office representative in Coahuila, Maria del Refugio Martinez Cruz, informed the assistant attorney for branch offices and inspections, Alfonso Cabrera Morales, that four tons and 182 kg of pure cocaine, and slightly over 3,000 kg of packed marijuana, were burned. The incineration took place in the town of Saltillo, in the presence of representatives from the National Defense Secretariat, the Mexican Navy, and the health sector, as well as from state and municipal governments. The official noted that, in the other aforementioned municipalities, various amounts of cannabis totaling seven tons and 825 kg were destroyed. They had been seized in operations conducted by Federal Judicial Police [PJF] personnel under orders from the force's deputy representative in the state, Juan Carlos Alvarado Aranda. It was emphasized at the drug burning ceremonies that the authorities had confirmed their steadfast intention of pooling efforts so as, together, to combat society's common enemy: the drug traffic. Moreover, the PGR representative in Sonora, Miguel Angel Cortes Ibarra, reported that federal agents under orders from the assistant representative, Julio Cesar Moraila Amador, had intercepted a trailer truck at the highway inspection point located at Km 120 of the Hermosillo-Nogales route. It was carrying 1,709 kg of pressed marijuana, presumably intended to be taken to the town of Nogales, Arizona, for marketing. According to the report submitted to the PJF's General Directorate, headed by Commander Adrian Carrera Fuentes, Macario Ramirez Arcega was placed at the disposal of the Federal Public Prosecutor's Office. He was driving a vehicle with Federal Public Service registration 325-UBB. In the body of this vehicle 203 packages and 64 bags containing the aforementioned amount of drugs were discovered.
