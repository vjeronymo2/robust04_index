BFN [Text] The country's gross national product [GNP] grew by 4.84 percent in the first quarter of the year. That's a big leap compared to last year's 0.27 percent to the same period. Economic Planning Secretary Cielito Habito calls it a remarkable (?sailing). The growth rate was the highest recorded in the past three years. Habito said it meant new jobs for Filipinos and open business opportunities for local industries. Habito said at least 200,000 jobs would have been available starting last month. Habito also said prospects for the rest of the year are even brighter despite fears of rising prices of petroleum products and basic goods as a result of the implementation of expanded value-added tax.
