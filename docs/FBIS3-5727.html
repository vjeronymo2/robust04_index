Language: Thai Article Type:BFN [Text] The Foreign Ministry's Information Department has reported on assistance under the triangle program for the reconstruction of Cambodia. According to it, Thailand has proposed to major donors cooperation under the triangle program of assistance for Indochina -- with Thailand serving as the center, or providing facilities and personnel for projects being carried out. Starting in 1994, Thailand, Japan, Malaysia, the Philippines, and Indonesia begin giving assistance to Cambodia under the triangle program by assisting Cambodian refugees who have returned home for resettlement in Kompong Speu and Takeo Provinces. They will be given guidance in rice cultivation, basic farming, fishing, fresh water fish farming, vocational training, training in sewing and handicraft, and primary health care. Japan will provide financial support while Thailand and other ASEAN countries will provide technical assistance. Under the program, which began on 1 March, 10 experts from Thailand's Education, Agriculture and Cooperatives and Public Health Ministries will spend a year working in Cambodia. One group of four officials will be dispatched first, the second will arrive in Cambodia on 4 April. Their tenure may be extended later. There will also be an adjustment in the format and method of assistance in keeping with the level of development which Cambodia achieves.
