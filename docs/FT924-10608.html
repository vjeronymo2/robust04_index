921030 FT 30 OCT 92 / Parliament and Politics: Lawson defends his record LORD LAWSON, the former chancellor, has accused the Conservatives of making him a scapegoat for Britain's current economic difficulties. In a BBC television programme to be shown tomorrow, Lord Lawson says 'it is very difficult to attribute everything that's happening now to me'. However, he admits that he regrets his reference to a 1980s 'economic miracle'. 'I think I once mentioned an economic miracle when I was carried away, a wind-up debate in the House of Commons . . a late night after dinner.' he says.
