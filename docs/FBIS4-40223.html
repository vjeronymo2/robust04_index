BFN [Text] Today, at the house of parliament, the NATO delegation led by General Peter Heinrich Carstens, chief of staff of Supreme Headquarters Allied Powers Europe [SHAPE], was received. In the conversation that took place, deputy speaker Kuanysh Sultanov told the guest that Kazakhstan's new foreign policy is based on that of a non-nuclear country. The parliament, he stressed, endorsed the fundamentals of the defense doctrine. Much attention is paid to building and developing the armed forces. He expressed hope for the possibility of cooperation with all NATO member states for the sake of preserving peace and good relations. The general spoke about the concept of the Partnership for Peace program and its future development. He expressed satisfaction that three Central Asian states, including Kazakhstan, signed the document on cooperation in the framework of their program. At its early stage, establishment of political contacts is envisaged. In September, the first joint military training exercise will take place, and observers from Kazakhstan are invited to participate.
