Language: English Article Type:BFN [Unattributed article: "Russia Keen To Join EU -- Chernomyrdin"; as released by Brussels TEXTLINE database] [Text] Russia will not turn back plans to integrate its economy in the world economy, but it needs a "therapy" to diminish the effects of the "shock therapy" that it has undergone following current economic reforms. This statement was made by Russian Prime Minister Viktor Chernomyrdin at a press conference in Davos, Switzerland, where he was participating in the World Economic Forum. "We want," he added, "to become members of the European Union and we want to become members of the G-7, turn it into the G-8." The Russian Prime Minister, who met in Davos on Sunday [30 January] with OPEC Secretary General Dr. Subroto, also said that "we will not object if they want to examine the possibility of Russia's participation" in the organization of oil exporting countries.
