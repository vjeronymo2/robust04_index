BFN [Excerpt] Kyoto, April 14 KYODO -- The world's energy policy experts jointly called for stronger global cooperation for stable economic growth and energy policies at a seminar under sponsorship of the International Energy Agency (IEA) in Kyoto on Thursday [14 April]. The seminar, hosted by Japan to commemorate the 20th anniversary of the agency's establishment, gathered some 300 participants from 26 countries around the world, including four nonmember nations of the agency such as Russia. The guests, including Bob Hawke, former Australian prime minister, and Brent Scowcroft, former U.S. presidential assistant, discussed current problems in global security policies in relation to energy in the post-Cold War era. They stressed it is important for all countries in the world to strengthen cooperation through international organizations like the IEA to maintain stable economic growth and energy policies. Otto Lambsdorff, former German economic minister, discussed the global warming problem stemming from increasing energy use. He suggested creation of a tax on carbon dioxide emissions and said countries capable of introducing such a tax should do so as soon as possible. [passage omitted]
