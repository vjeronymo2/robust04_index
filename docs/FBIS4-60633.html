BFN [Text] Jeddah, May 25, SPA -- The minister of information, 'Ali al-Sha'ir held a reception party on Tuesday [24 May] night to honour heads and members of the information delegations who came from various parts of the Arab and Islamic world to cover events of the just concluded haj season. The occasion was attended by Dr Fu'ad al-Farisi, undersecretary of the Ministry for Information Affairs, Ahmad al-Katani, under-secretary and general supervisors of the minister's office, Dr Shihab Jamjum, undersecretary for foreign information and senior officials of the ministry. Addressing the gathering, minister al-Sha'ir welcomed the newsmen and conveyed to them the greetings of custodian of the two holy mosques, King Fahd Bin-'Abd-al-'Aziz and Crown Prince 'Abdallah Bin-'Abd-al-'Aziz and congratulated them on completion of the haj rituals. Referring to the current Arab and Islamic causes and the best means to promote them through various media means, minister al-Sha'ir cited the noble meanings included in the royal speech delivered by King Fahd and Crown Prince 'Abdallah to the Muslim nation during the annual reception ceremony held in Mina last Sunday on the best ways to face up seditions, conflicts and challenges engulfing the Arab and Muslim worlds and he reminded the newsmen on their national and Islamic duty to repulse such hostile and unfair allegations. He recalled a speech he delivered during the last year's reception party in which he tackled the hostile and fierce media campaigns waged by some malicious and spiteful parties against the Islamic faith, some Islamic states and people in a desperate attempt to insult them, proposals for working out an informational method on basis of the information charter of honour with the aim for facing up the biased and false allegations against the nation and some Islamic states. Al-Sha'ir expressed delight over the successful efforts made during the year to realise the noble purpose and thanked all fair writers and journalists who tried to confront the hostile campaigns. Al-Sha'ir urged the members of the delegations to have a close look on the giant and brilliant achievements accomplished by the government of custodian of the two holy mosques, King Fahd Bin-'Abd-al-'Aziz and Crown Prince 'Abdallah Bin-'Abd-al-'Aziz at the grand mosque and nearby holy places in the holy city of Mecca and the blessed mosque of the prophet in Madina which were completed in a record span to convey what they have seen to their peoples. He expressed thanks and appreciation to the various Saudi information bodies and personnel who took part in the coverage of the events of the haj and thanked Arab and Islamic brothers who participated in transmission of the haj rituals through radio and television networks for the outside world. The gathering was also addressed by Amin Basyuni, chairman of trustees council of the Egyptian Radio and Television Federation, Qurban Alesen, the deputy information minister of turkmenstan who was speaking on behalf of the Asian Islamic states, Khero Zain, the head of the Indonesian information delegation, Mohammed al-Hadi al-Teraiki, director general of the Tunisian news agency who all highlighted the distinguished preparations made by the kingdom, under the leadership of King Fahd, for the haj season and the great services and facilities made for the comfort and security of pilgrims. They also cited with appreciation the great efforts being made by custodian of the two holy mosques, King Fahd Bin-'Abd-al-'Aziz to realise Islamic solidarity and constructive cooperation under the umbrella of the Islamic teachings and his constant efforts to serve the Muslims, their sancturies and just causes on all regional and international forums. On the other hand, the information delegations which participated in this haj season, expressed the heartfelt congratulations to King Fahd, Crown Prince 'Abdallah, the Saudi people on the occasion of the successful end of this haj season and Eid al-Adha. They cited with gratification to the marvelous expansion and development projects implemented at the holy places in Mecca and Madina as a civilized landmark of the era of custodian of the two holy mosques, King Fahd Bin-'Abd-al-'Aziz. They also thanked the Saudi concerned authorities for the comfortable means and integrated services rendered for all pilgrims.
