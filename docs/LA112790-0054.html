November 27, 1990, Tuesday, Home Edition

MORNING REPORT: TV & VIDEO

NBC Makes Changes: "Amen" will rejoin NBC's prime-time lineup Saturday and two shows from the news division -- "Real Life With Jane Pauley" and "Expose" -- will replace the low-rated medical anthology "Lifestories" on Sunday night next month, the network announced Monday. "Amen" will air at 8 p.m. Saturday, replacing "Parenthood."

A network spokeswoman said that "Parenthood" has not been canceled, but a decision on its future is expected by Dec. 15. NBC also disclosed that two of its new sitcoms -- "Working It Out" and "The Fanelli Boys" -- will switch time slots beginning Wednesday. "Working It Out," will move to 9:30 p.m. Wednesday, and "The Fanelli Boys" to 8:30 p.m. Saturday. The low-rated medical anthology "Lifestories" will be seen on a monthly basis for the remainder of the season, NBC said. CLAUDIA PUIG
