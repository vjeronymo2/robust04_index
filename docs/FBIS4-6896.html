BFN [Text] Defense Minister Datuk Sri Najib Tun Razak said the Malaysian Armed Forces is drafting a contingency plan for its peacekeepers in Bosnia-Herzegovina to meet any eventuality due to worsening situation there. He said the plan included steps to be taken to ensure the safety of the Malaysian troops serving with the United Nations Protection Force -- UNPROFOR -- in the war-torn country. He said the Defense Ministry will ensure that the Malaysian Battalion -- Malbatt -- members in Bosnia to be prepared to meet any eventuality, including attacks from the Serbs. Datuk Sri Najib was speaking to reporters after opening an UMNO [United Malays National Organization] seminar in Kerteh in the state of Negeri Sembilan. He said but to do this Malaysia needs the approval of the United Nations. Datuk Sri Najib said so long as Malbatt was [words indistinct], the Malaysian soldiers in Bosnia- Herzegovina were bound by a UN guideline.
