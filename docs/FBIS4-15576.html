BFN [Text] Baku (April 8) TURAN--"Democratic Congress is deeply concerned about return of Russian troops in Azerbaijan", says the statement distributed yesterday in Baku. The statement says that the return of Russian troops will not help to settle the Armenian-Azerbaijani conflict. Democratic Congress considers that the priority in the settlement of the conflict should be given to the UNO and the CSCE organizations, but not to the efforts of one state. Democratic Congress mentions that Azerbaijan's conservative forces can make use of the return of Russian troops and it can lead to tragic consequences. Democratic Congress says that it will use all democratic means to prevent the return of Russian troops in Azerbaijan. The statement was distributed due to resolution of Russian President concerning the creation of military bases of the Russian army on the territory of the CIS. Earlier Russian Defense Minister, Pavel Grachev, said that Russia intends to create one military base in Azerbaijan as well. Democratic Congress was founded on March 23 by the opposition parties of the country. It includes Democratic Party, the Popular [People's] Front, "Ana Torpag" party and the party of Nation's Freedom.
