BFN [By ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondents Andrey Kirillov and Vladimir Solntsev] [Text] Moscow, 28 Jun -- Talks between Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev and his Chinese counterpart Qian Qichen culminated today in the initialing of a bilateral agreement on the western sector of the Russian-Chinese border. The document is expected to be signed in early September during the second Russian-Chinese summit meeting. The USSR and China concluded an agreement on the eastern sector of the border in 1991. ITAR-TASS was told at the Russian Foreign Ministry that agreement has now been reached on a 55-km stretch of the border with Altay Kray, on which Russia has never had a treaty with contemporary China. "There are no problems or disagreement between us, so the talks were a success," a Foreign Ministry official said. He added that there have been no territorial concessions by either side. After the agreement is signed it will have to be ratified by the parliaments of both countries.
