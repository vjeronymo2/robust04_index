July 21, 1990, Saturday, Home Edition

ANSWERS FROM BABY DOOMERS ON WHETHER THEY'RE APATHETIC

Why should it come as a surprise to anyone that America's youth are unlikely to comprehend much less take interest in public affairs ("Media Study Finds 'Age of Indifference,' " Part A, June 28).

As a child of the eighties, I found the report less disheartening than comic.

The very notion that today's youth -- unknowingly saddled with an economy in disarray, an ailing social welfare system, and an impending ecological disaster -- could fail "to find public events compelling" makes for a passable joke.

What is really funny, though, is that older Americans -- having fed their children a steady diet of meaningless Hollywood fantasy, denied youth a proper education in favor of our "vital" defense budget, and left my generation with the likes of Arnold Schwarzenegger and Andrew Dice Clay for role models -- could ever express shock at the situation. Ha ha ha ha.

PETER A. ZELLNER

La Jolla
