June 2, 1990, Saturday, Home Edition

REFRESHING MEMORIES ABOUT CLAY-LISTON II

Earl Gustkey's recollection of Clay-Liston II is a tad time-warped. When the facts are more interesting than the legends, print the facts.

Liston was in the best shape of his career for the rematch, then the fight was postponed six months when Ali went under the knife for hernia surgery. By fight night, Liston had lost his physical and mental edge and had gained six pounds.

Liston lost by KO on his feet, and I lost $28.

MARSHALL HUTCHASON, Los Angeles
