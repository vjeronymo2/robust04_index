Language: Persian Article Type:BFN [Report by ETTELA'AT Ardabil correspondent] [Text] Hojjat ol-Eslam val-Moslemin Mohammadi 'Eraqi, the head of the Islamic Propagation Organization [IPO] returned from Baku, in the center of the Republic of Azerbaijan, and explained the cultural and religious needs of the Azerbaijani refugees who are housed in the camps set up by the Islamic Republic of Iran. According to the IPO public relations department, Hojjat ol-Eslam val-Moslemin Mohammadi 'Eraqi was addressing the hail-and-farewell ceremonies that are held at the Azerbaijani Governorate upon the departure of former officials and the introduction of replacements in the IPO branch at Ardabil. He spoke on the needs of the refugees of Azerbaijan and stated: At present there are 25,000 Azerbaijani men, women, and children who are refugees and require humanitarian aid from Iran. Referring to the establishment of the Headquarters for Cultural and Propagandistic Support in the camps for Azerbaijani refugees, he mentioned their enthusiasm in learning Farsi. The IPO head then recounted their needs for a Persian language teacher, a Koran teacher, tents, Islamic apparel, religious texts, and veils. He requested the Hezbollah nation, organizations and institutions to donate unstintingly to the Headquarters for Cultural and Propagandistic Support at the refugee camps and to assist in catering to their needs. During these ceremonies, Hojjat ol-Eslam val-Moslemin Ahadi was introduced as the new IPO official in Ardabil. He expressed his appreciation for the services of his predecessor Hojjat ol-Eslam Ne'mati. The IPO chief Hojjat ol-Eslam val-Moslemin Mohammadi 'Eraqi announced at the ceremonies to introduce the IPO official in charge of the Ardabil branch that the IPO is to set up a vast Islamic cultural center in Baku, the capital of Azerbaijan. He added: Some educational institutes and a dormitory to house the theological seminary students of Azerbaijan will be set up by the Islamic Republic of Iran in Baku. Mohammadi 'Eraqi then referred to the Azerbaijani people's enthusiasm for learning Farsi and Islamic injunctions and called on the cultural and propagandistic organizations of the country to assist the Azerbaijanis in this regard.
