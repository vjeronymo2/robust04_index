BFN [Text] Tehran, April 25, IRNA -- The governor of the Central Bank of Iran Mohammad Hoseyn 'Adeli Sunday announced that Iran and Denmark have reached an agreement for rescheduling the payment of Iran's delayed debts. 'Adeli, who is currently in Washington D.C. to attend the joint session of the World Bank and International Monetary Fund, told IRNA Sunday that Denmark has agreed to grant a credit of dlrs. 210 million to Iran for the payment of its debts including those scheduled for 1994-5. Therefore, he added, the total debts of Iran to Denmark are rescheduled to be paid off within a period of six years, he said. He also added that some of the German corporations have also agreed to reschedule the payment of German marks 520 million of Iran's debts.
