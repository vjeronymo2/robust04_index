930818 FT 18 AUG 93 / Rural car crime grows fastest CAR crime is growing faster in rural areas in England than in the big cities, says a national survey. Lincolnshire saw the greatest rise in car crime - up 29.8 per cent from 1991 to 1992 - followed by Cambridgeshire, Gloucestershire and Derbyshire. Greater Manchester tops the table of risk areas, according to figures compiled with the help of police statistics by the direct selling company Insurance Service.
