Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Aleksandr Yakovlev] [Text] Prague March 18 TASS -- The economic forum of the Conference on Security and Cooperation in Europe (CSCE) considers European economic problems, paying special attention to the difficulties which are faced by the countries implementing economic reforms in the transitional period, Pavel Smirnov, head of the Department of Economic Cooperation of the Russian Foreign Ministry, told ITAR-TASS on Friday, after the second session of the economic forum concluded here. According to Smirnov, who led the Russian delegation, participants in the session focussed mainly on economic situation in the countries undergoing economic reforms. The Russian delegates drew attention to regional integrating processes with a view to preventing discrimination against the Russian Federation. They also raised the question of developing production cooperation within the framework of the "Partnership for Peace" initiative. We should immediately begin working on the economic aspects of the initiative, first of all, in the interests of our industry and state military-industrial complex, Smirnov said. The third session of the CSCE economic forum will be held in June 1995.
