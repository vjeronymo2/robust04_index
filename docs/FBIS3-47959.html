Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, Jan. 20 KYODO -- The Commerce Chamber head on Thursday [20 January] urged the government to carry out an income tax reduction of 7 trillion yen in order to revive the ailing Japanese economy. A substantially large-scale income tax cut is necessary in light of the delay in implementing a tax reduction, Kosaku Inaba, chairman of the Japan Chamber of Commerce and Industry, told a press conference. Inaba also said the government should begin studying a consumption tax hike as a means of covering the income tax cut, after confirming the economy has turn around. Inaba said the ruling coalition parties and the opposition camp should do their utmost to avoid creating a political vacuum over the issue of political reforms. The most urgent task facing the government is the formation of a third supplementary budget for fiscal 1993 and the main budget for fiscal 1994, he said.
