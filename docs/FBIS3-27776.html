Language: Indonesian Article Type:BFN [Text] Indonesia was of the opinion that the second round of talks with Malaysia on the disputed Sipadan and Ligitan Islands have achieved maximum progress even though a settlement has yet to be realized. Izhar Ibrahim, the Indonesian delegation leader to the talks in Kuala Lumpur, expressed hope that the progress would pave the way toward the achievement of further progress in ensuing talks. No time frame has been set for the settlement of the dispute although the two parties agreed to hold further talks in Jakarta in four months' time. Apart from this, the two nations also agreed that the issue of Sipadan and Ligitan Islands settlement be based on the principles of international law.
