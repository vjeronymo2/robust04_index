BFN [Excerpt] Top American companies have evinced interest in investing in the Indian infrastructural sector. At the roundtable meeting with the prime minister, Mr. P.V. Narasimha Rao, in Houston the chief executives of several companies expressed an upbeat mood to invest in India. Over 100 chief executive officers of major companies attended the meeting. The prime minister outlined the salient features of the economic reforms and invited American businessmen to become partners for further mutual benefit. Our correspondent Rama Rao accompanying the prime minister reports that for the first time since the launching of economic reforms, the foreign investors focussed attention on specific pattern of their projects. In another significant change, the investors themselves have analyzed the relative advantages of various markets in South Asia and have ranked India well above others. Our correspondent further states that the new projects outlined at the meeting include the setting up of a commercial bank, mining and exploitation of bauxite in Andhra Pradesh and Orissa, and a computer company. A heavy schedule awaits the prime minister when he arrives in Washington DC tonight on the last leg of his week-long visit to the United States. [passage omitted]
