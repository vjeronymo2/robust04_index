BFN [Report by Rayma Rondon from "The News" newscast] [Text] Less than 24 hours after congressional approval, President Rafael Caldera signed the Special Powers Law. The new law, which grants special powers to the president, allowing him to legislate economic and financial issues, will become effective on 20 April, when it will be published in the official gazette. President Caldera is to discuss in full that same day the Special Powers Law during the Council of Ministers meeting. An official source has disclosed the Projects Concessions Law [Ley de Obras por el Sistema de Concesiones] will be one of the first laws up for approval. The executive branch believes this is an important law which is expected to stimulate the economy. [Begin recording] [Allan Viergutz, Venezuelan Oil Chamber president] We believe the Concessions Law is very convenient. We believe there is a need for creative measures that would lead to new options that will benefit the country. These measures will allow the government to deal more efficiently with the fiscal deficit, high interest rates, and the problem of unemployment. This law will stimulate the economy. [end recording] It was learned the executive branch, during the 30-day period the Special Powers Law allows, is prepared to promulgate all decrees established in that law.
