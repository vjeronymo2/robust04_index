BFN [Text] Yutaka Kume, chairman of the Japan Automobile Manufacturers' Association, who will step down from his post in May, looked back at his term of office during which he was quite busy in dealing with economic frictions between Japan and the United States. On the Big Three, he said: "We can welcome the fact that they are beginning to respond to our demand that `they should make cars which suit the Japanese market.'" In this way, he positively assessed the changes in the stand of the Big Three, who have begun to produce cars with a steering wheel on the right-hand side.
