BFN [Text] Mexico City, 28 Apr (AFP) -- The Energy, Mines, and Parastate Industry minister has reported that the Mexican Government today opened a pilot plant to manufacture nuclear fuel to be used by the Laguna Verde Nuclear Plant, which is located on the coast of the Gulf of Mexico. The pilot plant, situated in the State of Mexico near Mexico City, was built at a cost of about $10 million, and, according to the minister, it was constructed "in line with the strictest security and quality standards." The minister added: Experts of the International Atomic Energy Agency will train the technicians who will operate the plant.
