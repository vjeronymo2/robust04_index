Language: Romanian Article Type:BFN [Text] The Romanian Ministry of Foreign Affairs welcomes the signing in Geneva of an understanding between the Federal Republic of Yugoslavia and the Republic of Croatia on relations between the two countries. The Romanian Foreign Ministry issued a communique in which it expresses the belief that the positive development registered in the relations between the two countries represents an important and encouraging moment which will make a substantial contribution and provide an impetus to the peace process in Bosnia-Herzegovina, reduce tension, and create conditions for a new consensus on coexistence in the area of the former Yugoslavia. As a matter of fact, this moment is in line with the constant position promoted by Romania, which is to stimulate the process of normalizing relations between all countries that have emerged in the area of the former Yugoslavia and to hold talks between them. The Romanian Foreign Ministry, reads the communique, expresses the hope that this process will continue and, thus, contribute to the promotion of political stability in this area and in the entire region.
