BFN [By unidentified ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondent] [Text] Moscow, 20 Apr -- Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev and Lord Owen and Thorvald Stoltenberg, the co-chairmen of the coordination committee of the international conference on Yugoslavia have spoken in favor of holding urgent consultations between special representatives of Russia, the United States, the United Nations, and the European Union in order to work out a common stance on the Bosnian settlement. They held talks in Moscow today. Simultaneously, Kozyrev had a telephone conversation with Warren Christopher, suggesting to him that an American representative be sent to these quadrilateral consultations. The U.S. secretary of state has so far neither accepted nor rejected the proposal. The result of the consultations should be a concrete plan for a postwar structure of Bosnia-Herzegovina. It will then be discussed at a quadrilateral summit and presented to the conflicting sides.
