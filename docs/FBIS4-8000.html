BFN [Text] In connection with UNPROFOR [UN Protection Forces] reports concerning the possibility of a meeting between representatives of the Bosnia-Herzegovina Army and the Serb aggressor side under the chairmanship of General Michael Rose, the press center of the Bosnia-Herzegovina Army has been authorized to issue the following statement: 1. In order that talks on a general cease-fire between the Bosnia-Herzegovina Army and the Serb aggressor side can start it is vital that the Serb aggressor side immediately stop all offensive actions against the towns of Gorazde and Bihac, which have been proclaimed protected areas by the UN Security Council. 2. The Serb aggressor formations should return to their starting positions, which they held on 3 March this year when they launched an offensive against Gorazde. 3. In order that talks with representatives of the Serb aggressor side can begin it is necessary to have clearly defined political prerequisites which guarantee the continued political process of resolving the Bosnia-Herzegovina problem as a whole. 4. Representatives of the Bosnia-Herzegovina Army, led by Army General Rasim Delic, are ready at any time to have talks with representatives of the UNPROFOR and with other international organizations concerning the situation in Gorazde or in any other part of Bosnia-Herzegovina. Gen. Rose was told this during his talks today with Army Gen. Delic, the statement issued by the press center of the Bosnia-Herzegovina Army reads.
