940623 FT 23 JUN 94 / Minister defends Pounds 2.2bn aid budget Britain's Pounds 2.2bn overseas aid programme was the sixth largest in the world and was targeted closely on the needs of developing countries, Mr Alastair Goodlad, Foreign Office minister, told the Commons last night, Ivor Owen writes. He said statistics to be published by the Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development would show that UK aid last year was above the average at 0.31 per cent of gross national product. Mr Tom Clarke, Labour's shadow overseas aid minister, condemned the link between British aid and the sale of arms. He cited the Pergau dam project in Malaysia as an example of the government's failure to target aid on countries in greatest need. He said that if appraisal documents had been made public the aid for the dam would never have been approved. Sir David Steel, foreign affairs spokesman for the Liberal Democrats, called for the register of arms sales instituted by the United Nations to be upgraded into an instrument to control the trade. He said: 'There is no more appalling hypocrisy in international politics than seeing the countries in the developed world compete in the sale of arms to unstable regimes and then stand around wringing our collective hands the moment they are used.'
