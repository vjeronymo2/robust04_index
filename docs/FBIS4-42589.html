BFN [Text] Ankara, June 3 (AA) -- Israel's Minister of Economics and Planning, Shim'on Shetrit, currently on an official visit in Turkey, on Friday [3 June] met with State Minister Aykon Dogan. During meetings between delegations of both countries, both sides reiterated mutual wishes that the free trade agreement procedure between Turkey and Israel should be finalized in the month of July. A meeting on the issue will be held during the third week of July. Sources said that discussions still continue concerning this agreement which was on the agenda during a recent visit to Turkey by Israeli Foreign Minister Shim'on Peres on April 10-12, between the Israeli Foreign Ministry and Ministries of Industry and Trade. Dogan told reporters that Turkey's and Israel's economy are of a nature that complement one another adding that Israel considers Turkey a country with great potential in the Middle East in economic, political and social fields. Dogan also said that a great number of Israeli tourists were expected in Turkey in 1994, and Turkey also aims to increase its exports to Israel. He added that Israel is also interested in investments in industry, tourism, transport and in the irrigation projects in Southeastern Anatolian Project (GAP) region. Dogan said that Turkey has a unifying characteristic in the region adding, Israeli businessmen have also mentioned such projects as entering into joint partnership projects with Turkey's experience aiming at the CIS countries.
