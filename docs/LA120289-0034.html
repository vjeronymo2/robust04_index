December 2, 1989, Saturday, Home Edition

HIGH SCHOOLS DON'T EXIST JUST TO TRAIN ATHLETES

Pity the poor Los Angeles City high school football teams that will lose players just before playoff time because of academic ineligibility. Pity the coaches who demand that the players spend so much of their non-school time practicing.

Judging from Lonnie White's column on Nov. 21, you would think that the high schools existed for the purpose of training athletes and not minds. Let's get our prioities straight. Students attend school to get an education first. Extra-curricular activites should be considered only after students have proven themselves capable of meeting their academic responsibilites.

JIM HAYES, Torrance
