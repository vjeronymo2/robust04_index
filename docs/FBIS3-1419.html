Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Cairo, 28 Mar (INA)--Iraq has informed the Arab League that the rulers of Saudi Arabia and Kuwait continued to offer facilities to U.S., British, and French aircraft to violate Iraqi airspace, noting that this seriously jeopardizes Iraq's security, sovereignty, and national unity. In a message addressed to the league's secretary general, the Iraqi permanent representative to the Arab League said these aircraft carried out 321 hostile air sorties, flying from bases in Saudi Arabia and Kuwait on 17-24 March. A list attached to the letter detailed the airspace violations.
