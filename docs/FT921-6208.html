920228 FT 28 FEB 92 / Gulf war 'damaged environment' The Gulf War caused irreparable damage to the environment and its legacy poses a serious health hazard, according to a Greenpeace survey released today. . The war 'has resulted in an unprecedented disaster for the region, which has been left with a serious impact on the sea, land and atmosphere that will take years to heal,' the group concluded. The Meteorological Office in London has said its studies of the region indicate the damage is not as bad as first predicted, although long-term effects could take years to emerge.
