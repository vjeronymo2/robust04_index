July 8, 1990, Sunday, San Diego County Edition

DORM DILEMMA

John Berol ("A Solution to Mini-Dorm Dilemma," June 24) makes an eloquent appeal for protecting our neighborhoods from the insidious erosion inflicted by unregulated housing.

The economic appeal provided by renting single-family houses to students is hard to resist.

Pacific Beach, like La Jolla and the college area, is particularly hard-hit by the additional traffic, noise and all-around density fostered by this practice.

The result: as houses fill to capacity and streets become long parking lots, any semblance of a single-family neighborhood is lost to the expediency of housing more and more people. As long as the colleges and universities ignore their responsibility to house the students they attract to our city, the city at large will suffer, students and residents alike.

ALFRED C. STROHLEIN

San Diego
