BFN [Unattributed report under the "In Brief" rubric: "Orenburg"] [Text] "The state does need the press and television, but not in the amounts in which they currently exist," Russian Federation Prime Minister Viktor Chernomyrdin told journalists in Orenburg, where he arrived to attend festivities on the occasion of the 250th anniversary of Orenburg Guberniya's formation. The premier pointed out that the state would maintain a number of newspapers and television channels, but this would not apply to all existing mass media. According to the head of government, the system of state support for newspapers and television will be preserved only during a transition period.
