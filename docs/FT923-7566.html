920817 FT 17 AUG 92 / Smaller businesses warn of long slump THE recession has turned into a slump and is set to last for at least another two years, according to the Federation of Small Businesses. The prediction follows a poll the federation conducted last week among 350 small companies and is accompanied by a warning of impending 'catastrophe' in the sector. The survey found that nearly half of the companies contacted saw no improvement in projected turnover levels before the end of 1994 at the earliest. Companies said a cut in interest rates was the measure most needed to help combat the recession. The federation forecasts that, if its members' fears are confirmed, unemployment could double in the next two years. Mr Ian Handford, the federation's policy unit chairman, said: 'Statements from the Treasury urging a sit-tight-and-wait policy do nothing to alleviate the fears of small businesses and destroy any sign of hope or confidence in the foreseeable future.'
