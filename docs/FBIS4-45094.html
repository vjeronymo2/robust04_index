CSO [Text] In the 12 months of the year 1372 [21 March 1993-20 March 1994], 17.61 million tons of nonoil goods was imported into the country, 91 percent of which came in through ports and the other 9 percent of which came into the country across land borders. Of the imported goods, 7.1 million tons was basic goods, and the remaining 10.5 million tons was nonbasic goods. The shares of the ports and the land borders in unloading basic goods were 98 and 2 percent, respectively. The Public Relations Office of the Ministry of Roads and Transportation reports that during this period 15.2 million tons of imported goods was shipped into the country (6.4 million tons of basic goods and 8.8 million tons of nonbasic goods), of which 12.3 million tons was shipped by truck (81 percent of the shipping tonnage) and the remaining 2.9 million tons was shipped by rail (19 percent of the shipping tonnage). Likewise, during this period 6,923,000 tons of oil products was unloaded at the ports, and this figure was not included in the total imports figure. At the end of the month of Esfand [19 March 1994] 758,000 tons of goods was left in storage at the border entry points.
