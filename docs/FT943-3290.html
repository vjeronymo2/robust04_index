940915 FT 15 SEP 94 / Economic Watch: EU unemployment falls Seasonally adjusted unemployment in July in the European Union fell 0.1 percentage points from June to 10.8 per cent. The fall was particularly apparent among women of all ages, and men below 25. Total female unemployment fell by 0.4 of a percentage point to 12.4 per cent, compared to the male rate of 9.6 per cent. Male youth unemployment fell to 18.6 per cent, down 0.4 of a percentage point, while total youth unemployment fell from 19.9 per cent to 19.6 per cent. The only countries which recorded a rise in unemployment were Denmark (from 10.4 to 10.6 per cent) and the Netherlands (9.7 per cent to 9.8) The most marked falls in youth unemployment were recorded in Belgium and Spain. West German wholesale prices rose 0.2 per cent in August from July, up 2.2 per cent from a year earlier, the Federal Statistics Office reported. Spain's central bank announced that monetary growth was on target during the first eight months of 1994. M4 grew by 5.8 per cent during the year until September.
