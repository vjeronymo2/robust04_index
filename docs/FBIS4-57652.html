BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities monitored through 19 May. The State Petroleum Agency has hit a production record with the exploitation of well 148-D in the Vivian oilfield in the northern jungle. Its initial production totals 6,728 barrels per day. The exploitation of wells 148-D and 147-D produces 35,118 barrels per day, the highest in the past 10 years. (Lima EXPRESO in Spanish 7 May 94 p A22) The National Mining Association, SNM, reported that mining exports dropped 13 percent in 1993. Lead production, however, rose 12 percent; zinc 10.26 percent; silver 2.7 percent; and iron 84 percent, when compared with 1992. Gold production increased 36 percent with the exploitation of the Yanacocha mine. (Lima EL COMERCIO in Spanish 9 May 94 Economic Section p B11) The SNM reported that the mining industry accounted for 36 percent of foreign investments in 1993. The National Commission for Foreign Investment and Technology reported that foreign capital totalled $1.608 billion through February 1994. (Lima EL COMERCIO in Spanish 9 May 94 Economic Section p B11) On 10 May, Ricardo Marcenaro Frers, Democratic Constituent Congress Labor Committee coordinator, reported that 77.4 percent of the economically active population was underemployed in 1993; and unemployment and employment rates were 9.9 percent and 12.7 percent respectively. (Lima EL COMERCIO in Spanish 11 May 94 p A4) On 11 March, the international financing community decided to grant a $1.1 billion loan to Peru during the 1994-95 fiscal year running from July 1994 to June 1995. In face of the good results obtained by the Peruvian Government's economic program, approximately 20 countries and financial institutions agreed to help Peru during the meeting of the Peruvian Consultative Group sponsored by the World Bank in Paris. (Lima LA REPUBLICA in Spanish 12 May 94 Political Section p 2)
