BFN [Text] The Union of Russian Federation Citizens of Tallinn [Soyuz Rossiyskikh Grazhdan Tallinna] was formalized on 9 April at an inaugural assembly in the Estonian capital. Yuriy Kotenkov, one of the founding members, told an RIA correspondent that the union intends to defend the rights, freedom, and sovereignty of the 400,000 Russians in the republic. As a last resort to accomplish their goal, Kotenkov said, the organization reserves the right to rise up in revolt against tyranny and oppression.
