Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] India and Germany have expressed concern at the dangers posed by religious extremism. The prime minister, Mr. Narasimha Rao, and Chancellor Helmut Kohl called for immediate steps to check the spread of this threat. Briefing newsmen on the one-to-one talks between Mr. Rao and Dr. Kohl, an official spokesman said in Bonn that the two leaders discussed Indo-Pakistan relations. The prime minister reiterated that India is in favor of peaceful relations with Pakistan. Mr. Rao, however, regretted that despite New Delhi's efforts, the Pakistan prime minister, Ms. Benazir Bhutto, has taken a strident view. Referring to the package of proposals offered by India following the recent foreign secretary-level talks, Mr. Rao expressed the hope that continuance of worthwhile dialogue will yield positive results. During the talks, Bonn promised to take steps to speed up access of German technology to India. The German chancellor, Dr. Helmut Kohl, said bottlenecks, if any, will be removed. He promised to support India in its dealings with the European Union. Germany has a prominent place in the Union. Our correspondent, S. Sudarshanam, understands that Mr. Rao spoke about human rights environment in India and the steps taken to protect and promote the human rights aspirations of the people. Earlier, the German foreign affairs minister, Dr. Klaus Kinkel, called on the prime minister. The prime minister is visiting Berlin later today. Our special correspondent, Karuppaswamy, reports: [Begin Karuppaswamy recording] Berlin is one of the very big cities in the world outside India with a full-fledged museum dedicated exclusively to Indian art. There are several streets named after Ravindranath Tagore, Jawaharlal Nehru, and Indira Gandhi. And Netaji Subhash Chandra Bose visited the city for the possible assistance during freedom struggle. Mr. Narasimha Rao will be received by the federal president, Dr. Richard Von Weizsacker. His engagements during his two days of visit to Berlin include a meeting with the governing mayor of the city, the German indologists, and representatives of the industry and commerce. [end recording]
