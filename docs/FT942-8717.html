940519 FT 19 MAY 94 / People: CBI Sir Bryan Nicholson, 61, (above), a former chairman of the Post Office, took over as president of the Confederation of British Industry yesterday. He succeeds Sir Michael Angus, 64, chairman of Whitbread, who has served for two years. Sir Bryan, like his predecessor, started his career as a Unilever marketing man, joined Rank Xerox in 1972 and became chairman in 1979. Five years later he moved into the public sector as chairman of the Manpower Services Commission and served as chairman and chief executive of the Post Office from 1987 to 1992. He led the CBI's taskforce on educational and vocational training and has been heavily involved in the CBI's contribution towards bringing about a skills revolution in Britain. Meanwhile, the CBI appointed Peter Agar, 44, a management consultant, as a deputy director general. He joins Maurice Hunt, 57, and succeeds Richard Price, who died last December. Agar, who has an economics degree from Cambridge and an MBA from London Business School, is a former civil servant who joined Outram Cullinan & Co, strategic consultants, in 1987.
