BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities in Bolivia monitored through 16 June. The Central Bank reported that net international reserves were $498 million through May, the highest in the past few years. The reserves have risen $37.2 million so far this year, an increase of 10 percent compared with December 1993, and $22.2 million in May, an increase of 5.75 percent. The level of reserves has been positive in all months except for January. (Santa Cruz EL MUNDO in Spanish 25 May 94 National Section p 1) Finance Secretary Gaby Candia reported that public investments totalled $60 million in the year's first quarter, 10 percent of the amount projected for this year. She explained that this conservative disbursement was caused by the late approval of the general budget. A total of $566 million was approved for the current public investment program, an increase of 18 percent compared with 1993. According to official reports, 52 percent will be invested in economic development, 44 percent in human development, and 3.2 percent in sustainable development. This program seeks to guarantee an economic growth of 4 percent with projects to improve the economic infrastructure. La Paz accounts for 23.2 percent of the investments; Santa Cruz 22.7 percent, and Cochabamba 22.7 percent. (Santa Cruz EL MUNDO in Spanish 25 May 94 National Section p 1) A record monthly stock exchange operation of $100.2 million was registered in the Bolivian Stock Exchange Market late in May. It rose $36.1 million compared with April, an increase of 58 percent. (La Paz LA RAZON in Spanish 4 Jun 94 p B4)
