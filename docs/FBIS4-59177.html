BFN [Text] Kathmandu, May 7 (AFP) -- Prime Minister Girija Prasad Koirala of Nepal returned home in high spirits from the United States Saturday after a medical checkup there which gave him the all clear. Talking to newsmen at the airport soon after his arrival Koirala said "I am not suffering from any diseases and my health condition is now normal. I will be joining official duties from Sunday." Koirala's personal physician, Madhu Ghimire, said the prime minister had undergone a detailed checkup at the Walter Reed Hospital in Washington and had been given a clean bill of health. Koirala was advised to go to the United States for the checks after he suffered a series of fainting spells earlier this year. He left on April 22. Before heading home Koirala stopped in Germany for two days to rest, a member of his family said.
