Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on Colombian economic developments monitored through 3 January. Source is cited in parentheses following each item. Rodolfo Uribe, director of the National Administrative Department of Statistic (DANE) has disclosed that inflation in Colombia during 1993 had reached 22.6 percent, 2.5 points less that in 1992. He indicated that despite the government's inability to reach its 22 percent inflation goal, it came pretty close, evidence that "it is winning the battle against inflation." Uribe recalled that in 1993 accumulated inflation in Colombia reached 25.13 percent. In December 1993, cost of living increased most in Cali at 1.67 percent, followed closely by Medellin with 1.46 percent. Santa Fe de Bogota recorded inflation of 1.03 percent in December. Colombian cities with the lowest increase in cost of living were Cucuta with 0.85 percent and Pasto with 0.15 percent. President Cesar Gaviria's administration has set 19 percent as the 1994 inflation goal. (Hamburg DPA in Spanish 1752 GMT 2 Jan 94) Figures made public today by DANE reveal that unemployment fell during 1993 from 9.6 percent to 7.9 percent. According to DANE figures, unemployment levels in the four leading metropolitan areas of Santa Fe de Bogota, Cali, Medellin, and Barranquilla were less than 7.6 percent. The report also reveals that the private sector created at least 75,000 jobs in the past year. Unemployment in the four leading metropolitan areas during December totalled approximately 354,000, of an economically active labor market of 4.7 million Colombians. (Madrid EFE in Spanish 2041 GMT 28 Dec 93) Gasoline prices were adjusted by 19 percent throughout Colombia on 27 December. The price of regular gasoline increased from 578 to 688 pesos, and premium gasoline prices increased from 718 to 854 pesos. Cocinol, fuel distributed mainly in Santa Fe de Bogota's lower income communities, rose from 85 to 100 pesos, or 17.65 percent. It is the first time in six years that the government has increased gasoline prices based on the 1994 inflation goal of 19 percent. Previously, price adjustments were implemented with the intention of covering fluctuations in the cost of living index during the current year. (Santa Fe de Bogota EL TIEMPO in Spanish 20 Dec 93 pp 1a, 8a)
