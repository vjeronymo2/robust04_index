941119 FT 19 NOV 94 / Weekend Money (Financial Planning): Reducing a CGT bill - Briefcase, Q&A Some time soon, I intend to buy a house for my mother and stepfather. Because it will be solely for their use, does this exempt me from CGT whilst they are resident there, or will I be faced with it when eventually I come to sell? CGT relief for houses occupied by dependent relatives was abolished (except for some existing cases) by the Finance Act 1988. But you could get a substantial reduction in the prospective CGT bill by: 1. Granting your parents a formal tenancy (at modest rent, perhaps, but not a tenancy for life, preferably). 2. Living in the house for a short time (perhaps before they move in). 3. Giving judiciously-timed main-residence notices under section 222(5)(a) of the Taxation of Chargeable Gains Act 1992 (jointly with your wife, if appropriate under subsection 6(a) of that section). The guidance of your solicitors - or other experts in the tax rules and practice relating to domestic property transactions - is essential: the CGT rules are quite arbitrary and generally are enforced rigidly with scant regard for equity or logic.
