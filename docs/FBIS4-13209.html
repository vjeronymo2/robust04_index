BFN [Text] Marrakech, Apr. 16 (MAP) -- Over one hundred nations have laid the foundations of a new world economic order that they wished to be more prosperous and just when they adopted here Friday the Uruguay round final act on the largest liberalization of world trade in history. Morocco, the host country was the first to initial the documents and Zimbabwe was the last to append its signature on the final act concluding thus a signing ceremony that lasted for over four hours and during which the participating countries succeeded one another at the signing desk in English alphabetical order. Besides the final act, the participating countries also signed the accord setting up the World Trade Organization as well as three accords on public projects, dairy products and bovine meat. The participating countries to the negotiations which have not signed the documents Friday in Marrakech can do so in Geneva where the GATT is based when they wish.
