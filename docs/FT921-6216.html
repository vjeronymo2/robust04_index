920228 FT 28 FEB 92 / Britain to help Russians dispose of nuclear arms Britain yesterday announced a package, worth about Pounds 10m in the first year, to help Russia dispose of thousands of nuclear weapons, Reuter reports from London. Defence secretary Tom King said Britain would provide 250 special containers for the secure transport of nuclear weapons and up to 20 special armoured transport vehicles to carry them. Russian technicians will dismantle the weapons. The former Soviet Union had 30,000 nuclear warheads and the Russians plan to dispose of 20,000 of them over the next 10 years.
