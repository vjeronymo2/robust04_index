Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Political sources in Jerusalem say the conditions are not right yet for renewing negotiations with the PLO. At the same time, the sources told Qol Yisra'el's political affairs correspondent that Yasir 'Arafat's remarks in Cairo to senior Israeli officials leaves grounds for optimism. The contacts would enable further exchanges between the parties. Attending the meeting with 'Arafat in Cairo were Prime Minister Rabin's political adviser Jacques Neriya, his deputy military secretary, Lieutenant Colonel 'Amos Gil'ad, and Israel's Ambassador David Sultan. Premier Rabin was briefed last night on the contacts and will today hold consultations on what transpired in Cairo. 'Arafat has meanwhile returned to Tunis.
