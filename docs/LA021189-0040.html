February 11, 1989, Saturday, Home Edition

CONTROVERSY OVER GUN CONTROL LAWS

Of course, I don't enjoy the perspective of Davis on the gun control issue, but it does appear to me those favoring control have offered compromise over and over again only to meet with adamant opposition from the gun lobby and its friends in national and local legislatures.

The truth is the National Rifle Assn. has mounted effective campaigns at every level to block attempts to regulate plastic guns, Teflon-coated ammunition, Saturday night specials, etc.

Accusing anti-gun forces of "sitting like pigs in the middle of the road" is a cop out. The pun is intentional.

LOUIS M. ST. MARTIN

Pomona
