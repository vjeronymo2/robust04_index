BFN [Excerpt] As you heard in the news bulletin, Majles Speaker Nateq-Nuri met and conferred with Sri Lankan officials today. Our correspondent reports. [Correspondent] The need for Iran and Sri Lanka to expand mutual ties, take common stances, and also have more cooperation in economic fields was stressed at today's meeting between the speakers of the Iranian and Sri Lankan parliaments. At the meeting this morning with Mr. Mohamed, speaker of the Sri Lankan parliament, Majles Speaker Nateq-Nuri, who arrived last night in Colombo, the Sri Lankan capital, stressed the importance of more unity between Islamic and regional countries. He thanked Sri Lanka for her stances at international forums on Iran's policies. The Sri Lankan parliament speaker described as very constructive and positive the understanding and good relations and proximity of the two countries' stances. He called for the expansion of those ties, especially in the economic and agricultural fields. Mr. Mohamed thanked our country for her stance at international forums on the Bosnia-Herzegovina and Palestine crises. Mr. Nateq-Nuri also met with Mrs. Bandaranaike, the former prime minister of Sri Lanka, at the Sri Lankan parliament this morning and discussed regional and international issues. Cooperation and unity between members of the Nonaligned Movement and the need for more activity by that movement were emphasized at the meeting. Mrs. Bandaranaike is at present the leader of the opposition in the Sri Lankan parliament. [passage omitted]
