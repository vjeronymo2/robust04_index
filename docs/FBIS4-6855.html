BFN [Text] Tokyo, April 25 KYODO -- The business community welcomed the election Monday [25 April] of Foreign Minister Tsutomu Hata as Japan's new prime minister. Shoichiro Toyoda, chairman of Japan's top automaker Toyota Motor Corp., said he expects Hata, with deep perspectives and rich experience in administration, to demonstrate his leadership. Yasuo Shingu, chairman of Sumitomo Metal Industries Ltd., expressed hope that the Hata government will take quick action in dealing with pending problems. Reiichi Yumikura, chairman of the Japan Chemical Fibers Association, urged the government to carry the fiscal 1994 budget through the Diet as soon as possible in view of the slow recovery in the domestic economy. Masaru Hayami, head of the Japan Association of Corporate Executives (Keizai Doyukai), said the government should try to cut expenditures and abolish or amend the consumption tax to create new sources for public welfare funds. Yutaka Kume, chairman of the Japan Automobile Manufacturers Association, expressed hope that the government will pave the way for a smooth settlement of external trade problems ahead of a summit of the Group of Seven nations to be held in Naples, Italy this July. Katsuhiro Utada, vice chairman of the powerful Federation of Economic Organizations (Keidanren), described Hata as the right person to tackle the major domestic and international issues facing Japan. Utada cited the issues as an early enactment of the 1994 state budget pending before the Diet and resumption of negotiations for a new trade framework between Japan and the United States. Utada, honorary chairman of Ajinomoto Co., said the urgent task facing the ruling coalition is to stabilize the political situation and Hata is the most suitable person for the mission.
