Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Bucharest ROMPRES, 8/2/1994 -- "An Arbitrary Differentiation That Defies Historical Truth" is the title under which the editorial of the daily newspaper DIMINEATA comments the recent statements made by the president of the Republic of Moldova, Mircea Snegur, relative to the existence of a "Moldovan people," allegedly different from the Romanian people. As is known, the Republic of Moldova is inhabited by 64 percent ethnic Romanians, speakers of a genuine albeit slightly archaic Romanian, and live on more than three quarters of what used to be the historical Romanian province of Bessarabia, annexed in 1940 by the former Soviet Union on the strength of the Soviet-German pact signed by the then foreign ministers Molotov and Ribbentrop. After the fall at the end of the ninth decade of the totalitarian regimes in the East of Europe, more and more voices in the Republic of Moldova proclaimed the return of this Romanian territory to its mother land Romania. The editorial quoted above cautions that the reoccurrence in Snegur's declaration of older theses of the Soviet ideology periodically aired, first in the twenties and thirties, then in the forties and fifties and in the Brezhnev epoch, which advocate a clear distinction between the Romanian people and culture and the "Moldovan people and culture." TINERETUL LIBER finds Snegur's attitude as a strategic response to Moscow's offensive attempts to regain its commanding role over its former republics, but finds no excuse for his impudence of "denying his own roots, and doing this on behalf of those he is representing." The newspaper terms Bucharest's reaction as "still pale" and warns that Romanian should not neglect the national interest and overlook the political significance and the emotional impact Snegur's statement carries. Under the heading "Romanian Political Circles Vehemently Condemn Mircea Snegur's Switch" CURIERUL NATIONAL groups opinions by Romanian political forces on Mircea Snegur's statements. "Preposterous" is the label applied to them by Peasant Party [PNTCD] Vice President Ioan Diaconescu. The ruling Social Democracy Party of Romania (PDSR) finds them "inciting and offending," and Romanian National Unity Party (PUNR) Vice President Cornel Brahas qualifies them as "an overt attempt at our integrity, a tragical treason of the Romanian kin." COTIDIANUL defines the lines of action against such aberrations in the following terms: "The awareness of the physical, spiritual and moral geography, the unharnessing of the Romanians' power to create, to give vigour and substance to the national idea through the opening toward the European spirit have become the commandments of our ties against the dangers springing from the rebirth of the Zhirinovskiy-type [Vladimir Zhirinovskiy, leader of the Liberal Democratic Party of Russia] imperial nationalism and expansionism."
