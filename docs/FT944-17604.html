941007 FT 07 OCT 94 / The Labour Party in Blackpool: Bill of rights to be introduced A Labour government would introduce anti-discrimination measures backed by law, Mr Jack Shaw, shadow environment secretary, said yesterday. He told the conference that a bill of rights would provide people for the first time with enforceable rights, written into the statute book. Discrimination on grounds of race, sex, sexuality or disability would be outlawed. In a debate on modern democracy, Mr Straw accused the Tories of centralising power on a previously unimagined scale.
