BFN [Text] Huhhot, April 30 (XINHUA) -- Construction of the Inner Mongolian chemical fertilizer plant, a major national project for the Eighth State Five-Year Plan (1991-95), has been proceeding smoothly. So far, construction of the living quarters, auxiliary public facilities and production workshops has been completed. In addition, the main production installations, including the urea grain formation tower and the high-pressure boiler, are under construction. The construction of the plant began on December 20, 1991, and is expected to be completed at the end of 1995. With the total investment reaching 2.4 billion yuan (277 million U.S. dollars), the plant is designed to produce annually 300,000 tons of synthetic ammonia and 520,000 tons of urea. The plant is expected to start trial production in 1996.
