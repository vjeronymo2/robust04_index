940817 FT 17 AUG 94 / Arts: Today's Television Why do international sports authorities not present gold medals for cha cha or the military two step? After all you can win medals for dancing on ice and even for dancing horses. And now, we are assured, the great passion all over Britain is for 'synchronised swimming', where you put a clothes peg on your nose, stick your head under water, and waggle your posterior in time to the music. Short Stories watches Hayley, 10, and Victoria, 13, as they practise (8.30 C4). Hard to believe that Anna Reynolds' first television drama can be as poignant as the news story upon which it is based. A Belgian family of four locked their car in the garage and themselves in the cellar and stayed there for a fortnight to avoid the social stigma of not being able to afford a summer holiday. 'Paradise' in the BBC2 studio drama series Stages uses exactly that plot (9.25). After building a camera into the cricket stumps there was nowhere really important left to go but BBC1 makes an effort by sending a miniature camera up the vagina during the episode on sex in Desmond Morris's The Human Animal (9.30).
