940406 FT 06 APR 94 / Alzheimer's drug hope for sufferers Alzheimer's disease sufferers may experience significant benefit from daily treatment with the drug Tacrine, says a report in the Journal of the American Medical Association. A US study found that when Tacrine was given in higher doses and for longer than in previous research, 42 per cent of patients reported an improvement. Mr Gordon Wilcock, Bristol University's professor of care of the elderly, said the drug offered 'immediate hope of significant benefit to a proportion of sufferers'.
