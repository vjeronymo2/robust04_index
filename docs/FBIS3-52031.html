Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] Foreign Minister Luis Maria Ramirez Boettner stated on 26 January that our country will not establish diplomatic relations the PRC. He indicated that the two countries will seek to expand trade relations and that the possibility of opening trade offices in Asuncion and Beijing will be studied. The foreign minister outlined the government's official position following the first visit to Paraguay by a PRC business delegation, headed by the president of the Chamber of Commerce of Beijing. The PRC delegation met with local businessmen and with directors of the Chamber of Commerce seeking to increase bilateral trade. Ramirez Boettner explained during a news conference: "We maintain relations with the Republic of China, but this in no way prevents us from maintaining private trade relations with the PRC. A trade delegation has visited. I have met with them to see whether trade and economic exchanges can be increased, but no further steps will be made toward establishing diplomatic relations." Also on 26 January, the foreign minister received copies of the credentials of the new ambassador of the Republic of China (Nationalist China), Agustin Tingtsu Liu [name as published], who will present his credentials to President Juan Carlos Wasmosy when he returns from his visit to Ecuador and Peru. Ramirez Boettner and Agustin Tingtsu Liu established a line of action to increase bilateral relations in the spheres of training, technical cooperation, and investment. [passage omitted]
