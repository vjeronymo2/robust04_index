BFN [Text] Sixty-four projects with foreign investments of over $415 million were cleared during the five-day Investmart which concluded in New Delhi today. In addition, specific interests were shown in over 100 other projects. Giving details of the investments, the secretary, industrial development, Mr. Surender Singh, told newsmen that the largest investment of $152 million was being made in the food processing sector followed by $142 million in metallurgy and $85 million dollars in the chemicals sector. While Italy led with the highest investment of $91 million, France signed the maximum number of 25 projects with Indian companies. Thirty two countries were represented at the Investmart which was organized jointly by the Industry Ministry and UNIDO [United Nations Industrial Development Organization]. Mr. Singh said that the government will be following up all the proposals for which letters of intent have been signed.
