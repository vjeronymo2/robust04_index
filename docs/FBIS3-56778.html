Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Text] According to a NeGa agency report, the damage from the explosion yesterday on the Georgian section of the pipeline providing gas to Armenia is greater than that caused by earlier acts of sabotage. Some 800 meters of the main trunk line were put out of action, the bridge over the Kura River was destroyed, and the back-up pipeline was badly damaged. As is known, as a result of the earlier acts of sabotage, no gas has been pumped to Armenia since 18 December. Experts believe that it will be a month before the gas pipeline is back in service, since the approaches to the damaged area have been mined. The Armenian side is blaming a subversive group from Azerbaijan for the sabotage.
