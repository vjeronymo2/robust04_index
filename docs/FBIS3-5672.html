Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Kanchanaburi -- The New Mon State Party [NMSP] headquarters, on the Thai-Burmese border opposite Sangkhla Buri District of this western province, could be attacked any day now by Burmese army troops, an NMSP source said yesterday. The source told the BANGKOK POST that two Burmese army battalions of about 800 soldiers had set up positions near the NMSP headquarters at Baho and appeared poised for an attack. The 104th and 433rd Battalions from Tavoy township had been located near Ale Sakan village, only about 30 kilometres south of the NMSP headquarters. The two infantry battalions are believed to be poised for an attack, the source said. The NMSP headquarters is located about 50 kilometres south of the Three Pagoda Pass. The evacuation of Mon ethnic refugees from Lo Loe Camp in Sangkhla Buri District to a new camp at Hlakhani in Burma has continued. About 300 Mon refugees were moved to the new camp yesterday. About 8,000 Mon refugees had been housed at Loh Loe camp, and about 5,000 of them have been moved to the new camp since last month. The Mon believe that Thailand wants to please Burma so that in cooperation with the Petroleum Authority of Thailand they can lay a gas pipeline from the coastal town of Amherst in Burma to Kanchanaburi Province.
