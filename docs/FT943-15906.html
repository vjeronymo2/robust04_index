940707 FT 07 JUL 94 / Pensions appeal sent to Lords A man yesterday won permission to challenge in the House of Lords a court decision giving his divorced wife a right to a share in his pension as a 'post-nuptial settlement'. In a two-to-one majority ruling in May the Court of Appeal ruled that the wife was entitled to a share of the self-administered pension scheme set up by the husband, who had a controlling interest in a company where his wife worked as secretary. Because of the importance of the case the three appeal judges took the rare step of granting leave for it to go straight to the House of Lords.
