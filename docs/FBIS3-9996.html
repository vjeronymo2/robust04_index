Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Report by Herold Jean-Francois] [Text] Port-au-Prince, 28 Feb (EFE) -- The Justice and Peace Commission of Haiti, assigned to the Catholic Church in Gonaives Municipality, accused the Dominican Republic of violating the oil embargo decreed by the UN Security Council. In a report published in the Haitian capital today, the commission assigned to the diocese of Gonaives said Dominican and Haitian soldiers cooperate in transporting gasoline across the border that divides both countries. On 24 February, an investigative committee of the Justice and Peace Commission saw movement of fuel contraband at the northeast border, at the Haitian city of Quanaminthe, near the Dominican city of Dajabon. A large number of uniformed Dominican soldiers were seen participating in the operation, the religious organization reported. The commission said the soldiers, in front of the civilians, direct the transfer of an impressive motorcade of freight and personnel, who transport the fuel down the local river. According to the organization, nearly 250 carts, each one carrying three barrels of gasoline, are transported each day in 15 trips across the border. The organization added that the Haitian military takes charge of the fuel once it enters Haitian territory. The Justice and Peace Commission said an area has been set up near the border to store the fuel. The commission regrets that the UN Security Council has not denounced this situation. "The United Nations has not adopted any measures to supervise and enforce the oil embargo at the Dominican-Haitian border, which, as a consequence, is prolonging the crisis and at the same time strengthening the coupists' economic and political power," the commission said. The United Nations, after the 1993 efforts to restore Jean-Bertrand Aristide to power as the constitutional president of Haiti failed, decreed an oil embargo against Haiti. Meanwhile, today, the U.S. Coast Guard repatriated 141 Haitian refugees, who tried to abandon the country in a boat, which was located near the Windward Passage between Cuba and Haiti. Since 1 January, U.S. Coast Guard ships have repatriated 302 Haitian refugees.
