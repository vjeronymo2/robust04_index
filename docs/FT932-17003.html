930402 FT 02 APR 93 / UK Company News: Rentals cushion Gleeson decline HIGHER RENTAL income helped MJ Gleeson, the Surrey-based construction, housebuilding and property group, offset a 50 per cent drop in trading profits and so restrict the decline in pre-tax profits to 19 per cent. Profits decreased from Pounds 5.03m to Pounds 4.06m in the six months to December 31, on turnover down from Pounds 87.6m to Pounds 80.2m. Earnings per share declined 17 per cent to 27.09p (32.64p). However, the interim dividend is maintained at 3.35p, reflecting the group's 'underlying financial strength.' The group said the Pounds 7.43m decline in turnover was 'further evidence of the already well-documented problems confronting the construction and housebuilding sectors.' Trading profits were halved from Pounds 2.29m to Pounds 1.54m, but that was cushioned by higher investment property income of Pounds 2.39m (Pounds 1.98m). Net interest receipts fell to Pounds 516,000 (Pounds 763,000). Looking ahead the group, which was one of the sector's better performing organisations throughout the recession, said 'prospects for building and civil engineering contracting remain poor.' The family-run management said it was addressing the problem of surplus capacity in the UK construction market through 'rationalisation and a substantial reduction of overhead costs.' In the housebuilding division work in progress was being rigorously controlled. On a more positive note the group said: 'There are clear signs of increased interest among house buyers. 'If this trend is sustained, a satisfactory result will be achieved in the current financial year.'
