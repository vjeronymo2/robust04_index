BFN [Text] Shanghai, May 16 (XINHUA) -- Shanghai's banking setup is developing in a healthy direction this year, according to Mao Yingliang, president of the Shanghai branch of the People's Bank of China. The savings deposits in the various banks in this, China's largest economic center, increased by 22.63 billion yuan in the first four months of this year, an increase of 85 percent over the same period of last year. Mao noted that Shanghai has made rational use of credits and loans to ensure the development of the key state capital projects and its new pillar industries, including motor vehicles, telecommunications, chemicals, machinery and electrical appliances, and the basic industries of power, textiles and steel products, as well as people's daily necessities. The China foreign exchange center, which opened for service here April 4, has been linked up with 12 cities, and concluded transactions worth a total of three billion U.S. dollars and 2.66 billion Hong Kong dollars so far. The transactional volume of the Shanghai Stock Exchange in the first four months of this year amounted to 243 billion yuan-worth, up 58.8 percent over the same period of last year. The interbank loans in Shanghai climbed to 56.24 billion yuan in the January-April period. At present, the city has more than 90 foreign-funded banking institutions and representative offices. Their capital was 2.892 billion U.S. dollars by the end of March this year, an increase of 16 percent over the same period of last year.
