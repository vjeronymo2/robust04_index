BFN [Text] Havana, 30 May (PL) -- The president of the Federation of University Students [FEU], Otto Rivero, confirmed today that the approximately 8,000 students who will graduate this year from Cuba's universities have job guarantees despite Cuba's adverse economic situation. Rivero said the conclusion of the school year is less than 45 days away and already 7,000 graduating university seniors have been informed where they will have a job. There are ongoing talks with authorities to determine where the rest will work. Rivero said a great effort is being made to guarantee jobs for all graduating students. Cuba is enduring severe economic difficulties following the loss of its main foreign markets as a result of the disappearance of the socialist camp in Europe and of the Soviet Union and as a result also of the tightened economic blockade imposed on Cuba by the United States more than three decades ago.
