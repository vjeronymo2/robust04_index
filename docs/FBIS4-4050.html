BFN [Text] Beijing, April 27 (XINHUA) -- The second plenary session of the Fourth Council of the Chinese Journalists Association (CJA) opened today in Beijing. CJA Vice-Chairman Li Yan made a report at the meeting on the work of the association. More than 200 members of the council in mass media across the country took part in the meeting. Li hailed the great contributions the association has made over the past three years in developing China's journalism. Talking about the association's future task, Li said the role of "bridges" and ties of the association as a non-governmental organization should be brought into full play. He said the association must better serve all journalists, uniting and promoting the press circles to make greater contributions toward new success in China's reform, opening and modernization drive. The vice-chairman added that the CJA should make positive efforts to push forward the exchanges and cooperation between Chinese journalists and overseas journalists. He noted that as an authoritative non-governmental organization the CJA should adapt itself to meet the demands of the time and the journalists, broaden mass viewpoints and play a better role as a bridge and tie in the new situation. He said that China has more than 500,000 people who are engaged in journalism. Xu Weicheng, deputy director of the Propaganda Department of the Chinese Communist Party Central Committee, also spoke at the meeting. Xu said that it is necessary to adhere to the correct orientation of the ideological and media work, serving the economic construction, reform and opening, and social and political stability.
