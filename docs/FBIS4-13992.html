BFN [Text] The All India Urdu Editors and Intellectuals Conference has strongly condemned Pakistan's support to terrorism in Jammu and Kashmir. In a resolution in New Delhi today, the conference said while fighting militancy in the valley, it should be ensured that the lives of innocent people are protected. It suggested the government to send a delegation of Urdu editors to Kashmir to assess the situation there. The conference also stressed the need to review all TADA [Terrorism and Disruptive Activities Act] cases and demanded the release of innocent persons. Addressing the conference the railway minister, C. K. Jaffar Shariff, said that efforts should be made to enlist people's support in restoring normalcy in Kashmir. The tourism and civil aviation minister, Mr. Ghulam Nabi Azad, warned Pakistan against meddling in India's internal affairs. He said Pakistani misadventures are only creating more problems for minorities rather than serving any purpose.
