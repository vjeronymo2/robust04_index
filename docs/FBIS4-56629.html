BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities monitored through 5 May. Energy Minister Alexis Stepanenko on 29 April announced that Petrobras -- Brazilian Petroleum Corporation -- may no longer pass on the cost of oil importation taxes to consumers in the price of fuel. The taxes affect the importation of oil and by-products. Petrobras has so far paid a 38 percent tax on crude oil imports, which was passed on to consumers in the form of higher fuel prices. The corporation will now have to absorb the cost of this tax. (Rio de Janeiro O GLOBO in Portuguese 30 Apr 94 p 1) The Institute of Nonferrous Metals has reported that in the first quarter of 1994 the production of nickel stood at 3,491 tonnes, and that during the same period in 1993 nickel production stood at 3,915 tonnes. These figures show that during the first quarter of 1994 Brazilian nickel production dropped by 10.82 percent with respect to the same period in 1993. (Sao Paulo GAZETA MERCANTIL in Portuguese 3 May 94 p 14) The last stage of the privatization of the National Iron and Steel Company -- CSN -- was concluded during the week ending 23 April. The shares that still had to be sold, amounting to 9 percent of the enterprise's capital, were sold in two auctions conducted in March and last week. The operation yielded $1.494 billion. (Sao Paulo O ESTADO DE SAO PAULO in Portuguese 28 Apr 94 p B4) The average general price index stood at 40.91 percent in April. This figure stands almost 5 percentage points below the 45.7 percent rate that was registered in March. (Sao Paulo O ESTADO DE SAO PAULO in Portuguese 1 May 94 p A14) Professor Roberto Cavalcanti, of the National Institute for Advanced Studies, on 27 April submitted a research paper reporting that there are 39.2 million poor people in Brazil, of whom 21.7 million are in the northeastern region, and that there are 3.1 million poor people in the three southeastern metropolitan regions -- Sao Paulo, Rio de Janeiro, and Belo Horizonte. Cavalcanti considered poor the members of a family whose monthly per capita income stood below $35, in values that were in force in 1990. (Sao Paulo FOLHA DE SAO PAULO in Portuguese 28 Apr 94 p 10) The balance of trade registered a $1.03 billion surplus in March. This figure is 31.97 percent lower than the figure registered in March 1993. Ailton Barcellos, executive secretary of the Ministry of Industry and Commerce, on 27 April reported that exports in March 1994 -- which stood at $3.351 billion -- dropped by 4.67 percent with respect to March 1993. Imports, which stood at $2.321 billion in March 1994, grew by 15.99 percent. (Sao Paulo O ESTADO DE SAO PAULO in Portuguese 28 Apr 94 p B7) On 29 April Petrobras began operating the 1,027 meter deep Marlim-4 oil well in Campos Basin, off the coast of Rio de Janeiro. This is Petrobras' deepest underwater oil well. The well is part of the Marlim Sur Master Project, in which $34 million has been invested. Marlim-4 will produce 6,300 barrels per day at a cost of $3.45 per barrel. (Sao Paulo GAZETA MERCANTIL in Portuguese 2 May 94 p 13)
