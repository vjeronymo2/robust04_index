941111 FT 11 NOV 94 / Commodities and Agriculture: Sugar futures climb to new highs White sugar futures climbed to fresh four-year highs at the London Commodity Exchange yesterday as fears of supply tightness kept the recent rally going. Traders told the Reuters news agency that China, Yemen and Iran were each rumoured to have bought a cargo of whites originally destined for India. In late trading the March delivery position reached Dollars 370 a tonne, up Dollars 6.80 on the day and Dollars 39 since the start of October.
