940602 FT 02 JUN 94 / Arts: Today's Television According to today's edition of The Business, bullying is not limited to school playgrounds but is commonplace in industry and commerce. One 'stress expert' says that if enough employees are bullied it can put a company out of business. It seems that one senior manager at Ford was so demoralised by his new bosses that he left, brought a case of constructive dismissal, and won a large settlement (7.30 BBC2). This week's episode in Jack Rosenthal's drama about removal men, Moving Story, lasts 60 minutes which must surely mean an improvement on last week's 90-minute episode. As we have come to expect from Rosenthal, the quality is largely in the characters and the opening story was over-stretched (9.00 ITV). The outrage from some medical people at the portrait of hospital life in Cardiac Arrest must stem either from blindness or from the same rage that affected Caliban upon looking in the mirror. In the last episode of the opening series (9.35 BBC1) life and death decisions are being taken on the hoof, as ever, and some of the staff are behaving badly.
