Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Jurmala, Latvia, Feb 16, BNS -- Latvia and Russia have fully agreed on the text of an interstate agreement on frontier authority, Latvian Cabinet's spokespeople told BNS. The agreement was reached during the current round of interstate talks in Lielupe, Jurmala. The draft agreement provides for settlement of various conflicts and problems on the state boundaries without involving both countries' Foreign Ministries, Reinis Kazradzis, member of the Latvian group of experts, told BNS. The conflicts, which can be settled in compliance with the agreement, include exchange of frontier regime violators, use of common water reservoirs and return of straggling cattle. Kazradzis prognosticated that the agreement would be initialed before the talks conclude. Latvia has already initialed a similar pact with Belarus; it plans to soon initial pacts with Lithuania and Estonia, Kazradzis said. The groups of experts on social and Skrunda problems also continued their consultations today. This round of interstate talks will be wrapped up today or continued tomorrow in accordance with their procedure.
