930106 FT 06 JAN 93 / Bush hoping for trade pact American trade officials yesterday were said to be mulling over potential US concessions designed to achieve the breakthrough needed if the Uruguay Round of trade liberalisation talks is to conclude during the tenure of President George Bush, writes Nancy Dunne in Washington. Mr Bush, apparently determined to 'leave the desk clean' when he turns over the presidency to Governor Bill Clinton on January 20, has been pushing to meet the January 15 deadline he last month set with British Prime Minister John Major for final resolution of the most important issues. After signing the Start 2 nuclear arms accord in Russia at the weekend, the president stopped off in Paris to discuss the General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade with French President Francois Mitterrand. Reportedly, he made little headway. At the same time, Mrs Carla Hills, the US trade representative, met Sir Leon Brittan, newly appointed as EC Commissioner for External Relations and Trade to discuss 'a shopping list' of issues.
