BFN [From the "Presidential Bulletin" feature: No. 118 (608); compiled by Nikolay Zherebtsov and Andrey Petrovsky; edited by Vladimir Shishlin] [Text] President Nursultan Nazarbayev has said in a televised interview the latest changes he made to the structure of executive government and the personnel reshuffles in the cabinet were linked with what he described as a new anti-crisis policy developed by the government. President Nazarbayev said the anti-crisis program would be discussed in parliament in a few days and then approved by the President. President Nazarbayev said the government of Kazakhstan had proposed a "team prepared to act on it." He said he had agreed with the proposed reorganization of ministries, personnel changes to the cabinet and the leadership of some ministries and authorities in regions and in the capital President Nazarbayev believes that "new, younger people having a good idea of what business and economic reform are about have joined the Cabinet". In particular, he emphasized the appointment of 40-year-old Kozykorpesh Yesenberlin as Chairman of the State Property Committee and 32-year-old Galymzhan Zhakiyanov as head of the Semipalatinsk regional administration. Both have a record of work in "commercial structures." President Nazarbayev said that the government would be given carte blanche for 15 months to act on the program, and the presidential staff and the president himself would closely monitor progress in its implementation. The future of the government will be decided upon the end of the term. "If the program is not implemented, the Cabinet will resign," President Nazarbayev said.
