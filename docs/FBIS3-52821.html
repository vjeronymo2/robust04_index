Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Cairo, 9 Jan (MENA)--Dr. Yusuf Wali, deputy prime minister and secretary general of the National Democratic Party [NDP], has announced that the party's bureau staff had met with eight opposition party leaders as part of the exchange of views on the national dialogue called for by President Husni Mubarak in his address to the joint meeting of the People's Assembly and Consultative Council. In a speech to the NDP's political club last night, Dr. Wali said that the points of agreement with the opposition leaders were far greater than those of disagreement. He said the dialogue is a good initiative because it has enabled the opposition parties to comprehend the NDP's views. It has also enabled the party's bureau staff to understand the concepts of the opposition party leaders concerning the national dialogue issue. Dr. Wali said that he will meet with the leaders of the Greens Party and the Democratic Unionist Party by the end of this week. Meanwhile, the NDP's bureau staff will meet with the officials in charge of AL-AHRAM's Strategic and Political Studies Center, which is exploring the various opinions about the national dialogue issue.
