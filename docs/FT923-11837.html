920722 FT 22 JUL 92 / International Company News: PepsiCo makes strong advance PEPSICO, the US soft drinks, snacks and fast-food restaurant group, yesterday unveiled a 20 per cent improvement in second-quarter net profits, with strong gains in overseas markets. Net income for the three months to June 13 totalled Dollars 383m, or 48 cents a share, compared with Dollars 318.3m, or 39 cents, in the same period last year. Sales advanced 11 per cent to Dollars 5.21bn. Mr Wayne Calloway, chief executive, said earnings momentum had been based on 'strong and balanced operating results, with all three lines of business achieving double-digit growth in both their US and international operations'. PepsiCo saw a 9 per cent increase in soft drink sales to Dollars 3.42bn, with international sales for the division surging 18 per cent to Dollars 926.9m. Operating profits for the division rose 8 per cent to Dollars 452.2m. PepsiCo's snack foods operations had operating profits of Dollars 459.1m, up 20 per cent, on sales which rose 8 per cent to Dollars 2.74bn. The improvement was driven by strong gains overseas, where operating profits soared 39 per cent to Dollars 125.5m. For the first half, PepsiCo posted a 19 per cent gain in net income to Dollars 624.5m, or 78 cents a share, on sales which rose 11 per cent to Dollars 9.8bn.
