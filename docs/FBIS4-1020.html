BFN [Report by A. Thomson] [Text] Johannesburg April 18 SAPA -- The Inkatha Freedom Party [IFP] Youth Brigade on Monday [18 April] evening announced it had postponed plans for a stayaway in the Johannesburg area on Tuesday, after talks with the South African Council of Business [name as received]. Spokesman for the brigade's PWV [Pretoria, Witwatersrand, Vereeniging] branch, Mr Carter Ndlovu, told SAPA by telephone talks with SA Chamber of Business [SACOB] in Johannesburg on Monday had been "fruitful and constructive". SACOB delegates had expressed concern about the effect of the stayaway on business. Mr Ndlovu said the talks were continuing and a further statement would be made later.
