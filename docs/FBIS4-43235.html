BFN [Report by "Welt-Nachrichtendienst": "Major Gains for PDS in East"] [Text] Despite losses of up to 3 percent in the four municipal elections in eastern Germany, the Christian Democratic Union [CDU] has remained the strongest party. According to initial, the Social Democratic Party of Germany [SPD] and the Party of Democratic Socialism [PDS] achieved significant gains. Final results are not available from every city yet. In Mecklenburg Western Pomerania and Saxony Anhalt, the CDU scored about 33 percent, in Saxony and Thuringia 39 percent. The SPD remained in second place, increasing its share between 6 and 8 percent and achieving 22 (Saxony) and 30 percent. The PDS, the successor party to the Socialist Unity Party of Germany, achieved spectacular gains in the east. In Thuringia and Saxony it polled 15-16 percent, in Saxony Anhalt and Mecklenburg Western Pomerania 20 and 25 percent respectively, In several cities it became the strongest party. In Schwerin and Rostock, the PDS is now number one with 34 and 33 percent respectively. In most major cities in Saxony Anhalt, Thuringia, and Saxony, run-off elections will have to be held to elect mayors. In Hoyerswerda, Magdeburg, Suhl, and Dresden, the PDS took its candidates into the second round. In the Saarland, Rhineland-Palatinate, and Baden-Wuerttemberg, the governing or cogoverning SPD lost votes while the CDU increased its share. According to preliminary official final results from the Saarland, the SPD's share sank from 45.7 to 44.4 percent, while the CDU slightly improved its score to 37.4 percent. In Rhineland-Palatinate, where the SPD chancellor candidate heads the government in a coalition with the Free Democratic Party of Germany [FDP], the SPD suffered severe losses according to initial individual results. In the capital, Mainz, it dropped from 40.6 to 29.9 percent. In Baden-Wuerttemberg, where the final results are not expected before the middle of the week due to the complicated electoral system, everything is pointing toward an overall CDU victory. The FDP has obviously lost in all seven municipal elections, while Alliance 90/Greens increased their share in the vote. In the repetition of the election to the Munich City Council, the SPD dropped from 42 to 36.1 percent according to first trends. The CSU increased its share from 30.1 to 34.3 percent. Munich has been ruled by a SPD/Greens majority since 1990. However, the vote had to be repeated after the result had been challenged.
