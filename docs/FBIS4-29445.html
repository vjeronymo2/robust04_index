BFN [Text] Datuk Sri Dr. Mahathir says the enforcement of the unanimously approved amendments to the UMNO [United Malays National Organization] constitution to curb money politics will not be back dated. However, the prime minister said that UMNO members who resorted to money politics in the party election last year might lose their chances to be chosen as Barisan Nasional's candidates in the next general election. The amendments will be taken into account in deciding UMNO candidates to be fielded under the Barisan Nasional ticket at the general election. He said this at the news conference at the end of the special UMNO general assembly in Kuala Lumpur. Datuk Sri Dr. Mahathir, who is UMNO president said that the date of the enforcement will not be back dated to ensure fairness to the members. However, proof for the use of money in the UMNO election last year would be taken into account to ensure that the party's candidates who were clean would represent the Barisan Nasional in the general election. The prime minister also that Malaysia is not yet prepared to establish diplomatic relations with Israel because the government is not convinced that the peace process between Israel and the Palestinians is settled. Replying to questions from newsmen at the end of the special sitting of UMNO general assembly this evening, the prime minister disclosed that he has so far received three letters from Yitzhaq Rabin. In his letters to Datuk Sri Dr. Mahathir, the Israeli prime minister has indicated his desire to establish diplomatic ties with Malaysia. Datuk Sri Dr. Mahathir said he has replied to all three letters and has also informed the cabinet about the correspondence. When asked to comment on the report that a prominent Malaysian businessman had visited Jerusalem recently, the prime minister said he is not aware of the visit. He also said that there is at the moment no defined punishment for such wrong. The government has also taken action against the Malaysian reporter who covered the peace talks between Israel and the Palestinians recently.
