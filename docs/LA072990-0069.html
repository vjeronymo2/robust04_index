July 29, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

BELIZE NOTED

I read with interest the article on Belize by Bill Belleville in your June 24 Travel Section ("The Other Caribbean"). In March of this year, three couples, including ourselves, rented a home on North Ambergris Caye.

For those who are considering spending some time on the Caye, I would like to point out two important facts.

The Caye is divided by El Rio. South Ambergris, where the town of San Pedro is located, has the shops, more hotels, restaurants and dive shops. North Ambergris has fewer hotels, is more tranquil and water transportation to town is provided.

The diving, snorkeling and fishing are indeed excellent. However, the swimming is poor due to turtle grass and soft sand. Beautiful-looking beaches, but poor for swimming.

Condos and houses are available on both sides of El Rio. Reservations should be made as far ahead as possible for the best choices. Our three-bedroom, two-bath home, with private dock and daily maid service, plus transportation to San Pedro, was $200 U.S. per night. It was very well-equipped. Sundial Travel, 9430 Warner Ave., Fountain Valley, (714) 962-1176, handled our arrangements. For information on houses and condos on the Caye, contact Mary Hawthorne (call 011-501-2531). She and her partner, Carlos, manage several of the nicer rentals on the Caye, and were a great source of information and one of the prime reasons our trip was so rewarding.

SARAH U. McBRIDE

Bakersfield
