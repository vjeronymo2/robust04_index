921202 FT 02 DEC 92 / Pensions charter group launched THE union-backed Charter for Pension Fund Democracy was launched formally yesterday at a meeting of 300 pension fund trustees. The meeting was held to debate the charter's evidence to the Goode committee on pension fund reform, set up in the wake of the Robert Maxwell affair. The charter has called for a new Pensions Act that would require the majority of trustees of any occupational scheme to be elected by members of the scheme and would prevent contribution rates and benefits from being unilaterally altered by companies. Ms Anne Simpson, one of the charter leaders, said that many of the difficulties faced by the 11m members of pension schemes did not stem from fraudulent activity but from the current legal framework 'which puts all the cards in employers' hands'. Professor Ray Goode, chairman of the Goode committee which is due to report in June, attended the conference with several members of the committee. The deadline for submissions to the committee is December 15. Mr John Edmonds, leader of the GMB general union, said pension scheme members had alarmingly little control or protection over what was an important part of their pay. He added: 'But employers have a battery of resources at their command and a very strong vested interest in maintaining the status quo, as do the so-called 'independent experts', who are often neither independent or even competent, let alone expert.'
