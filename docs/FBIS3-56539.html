Language: English Article Type:BFN [By BELINFORM correspondent for TASS] [Text] Minsk January 15 TASS -- A memorandum on mutual understanding, which expresses the intention to create a Belarusian-U.S. commission on agricultural businesses and development of rural areas was signed here today during the Minsk stay of U.S. President Bill Clinton. The U.S. President voiced the availability of additional credits for Belarusian purchases of U.S. foodstuffs. Shushkevich and Clinton are satisfied with a bilateral agreement on science and technology which provides for quick expansion of scientific information exchange and joint R&D. An agreement to continue the elaboration of documents against double taxation and on cooperation in peaceful uses of nuclear energy has been reached as well. Bill Clinton told the Belarusian leader they will pass over to Belarus ten million dollars worth of medical equipment. The sides discussed prospects for democratic reforms in Belarus and parliamentary elections. Clinton expressed the hope the elections will be held in 1994 and promised technical assistance to their organization. The leaders voiced their support to international efforts for settlement of international conflicts, consolidation of independence, sovereignty and territorial integrity of all states, including new independent nations of the former Soviet Union. The Belarusian parliament speaker and the U.S. President noted recent progress in overcoming the division of the European continent and outlining ways towards more close cooperation between states. President Clinton dwelt on his initiative Partnership for Peace, and Shushkevich assured the President Belarus will thoroughly analyze a possibility of their participation in the initiative. Shushkevich and Clinton expressed common opinion the Belarusian visit of the U.S. President made a weighty contribution to consolidation of bilateral friendship, cooperation and mutual understanding. President Clinton expressed profound gratitude to the Belarusian Supreme Soviet, government and people for warm reception of his family, the U.S. delegation and himself.
