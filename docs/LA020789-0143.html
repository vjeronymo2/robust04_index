February 7, 1989, Tuesday, Orange County Edition

BRIEFCASE: SCIENCE/TECHNOLOGY

The president and chairman of Standard Logic Inc. has resigned after a dispute with company directors over management of the small Anaheim electronics manufacturer.

Dennis A. Wade resigned Jan. 27. His replacement is Wes Baumgardner, a company founder and president and chairman from 1967 to 1987.

The company also said it has canceled a letter of intent to merge with U.S. Metals & American Welding Supply, a metal manufacturer in Sandy, Ore. "The terms they submitted to us were not acceptable," Baumgardner said. He said Standard Logic will continue to seek other merger candidates.

Baumgardner said the differences between Wade and the board coincided with a worsening financial picture in November. The company expects to report a loss of more than $1 million on revenue of $2.4 million for its fiscal year ended Oct. 31.

Baumgardner said the firm is "reevaluating its assets" after a sharp decline in sales and new orders in the first quarter ended Jan. 31. Revenue declined 56% to $232,000, from $534,000 a year earlier.

He said the company has "redirected" its marketing program and hopes to return to profitability within 3 months.

Standard Logic, which employed 130 people and had sales of more than $8 million in the 1974-75 period, currently has only 13 employees.
