July 28, 1989, Friday, Home Edition

DEBATE OVER RAIL LINES

In reading your article regarding transit ("Rail Lines Offered for Sale Are Focus of Transit Debate," by Ronald Taylor, Metro, July 18), I couldn't help but notice the comments of Westwood resident Steve Kunin.

If he doesn't believe we should use existing rights of way for rail transportation, what does he propose we do about the congestion on our freeways, surface streets and neighborhoods? We got rid of the old rail system to, among other things, build more freeways and that "solution" has run its course.

In stating that rail transit isn't needed, he is ignoring the cheapest, most convenient alternative to a congestion problem the rest of us believe exists.

Steve, maybe the next time you are stuck in traffic along Santa Monica Boulevard Westwood you could look down between the medians that separate the street. It may be hard to believe but at one time those rails carried scores of passengers from the Eastside to the Westside and beyond.

If the Los Angeles County Transportation Commission is allowed to purchase these rights of way, maybe we can someday ride that line and others like it again.

NICK SANTANGELO

Long Beach
