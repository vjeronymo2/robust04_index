Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] Obrenovac, 19 Jan (TANJUG) -- Taking into consideration the intensive plenary and bilateral negotiations in Geneva, it could be said that we are a step closer to peace, particularly after the Serbian proposal to immediately establish peace and a modus vivendi, as well as solve other remaining political problems later, Serbian Vice President Nikola Koljevic told Radio Obrenovac today. "Therefore, it is very important the three sides accept the establishment of peace and resolve the remaining disputes peacefully," Koljevic pointed out. Koljevic said the joint declaration signed by the Federal Republic of Yugoslavia and the Republic of Croatia was "a significant step for overall Serb-Croat relations," stressing that progress was much slower in the negotiations with the Muslim side.
