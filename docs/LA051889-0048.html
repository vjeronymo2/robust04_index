May 18, 1989, Thursday, Home Edition

MORNING REPORT: RADIO

Geoff Edwards, the affable host of television's "Big Spin," was due back on radio Wednesday night to fill in for Tom Snyder on Snyder's ABC call-in show. In a telephone interview earlier in the day, Edwards said he has weathered the storm that two months ago led to his resigning as a KFI talk show host. Edwards objected to the station's promotion of fellow talk show host Tom Leykis' plan to destroy Cat Stevens' recordings as a protest against the singer's endorsement of the Ayatollah Ruhollah Khomeini's death threat against Salman Rushdie. Edwards has received about 500 letters in support of his position, and says, "If I had to do it all over again, I'd do exactly the same thing." ALEENE MacMINN
