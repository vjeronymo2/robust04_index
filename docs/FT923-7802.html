920815 FT 15 AUG 92 / International Company News: Three exchanges decide to stay in New York THREE OF New York's five commodities exchanges are to stay in the city after local government agreed to pay half the cost of a new Dollars 150m trading centre. The decision by The Coffee, Sugar and Cocoa Exchange, the New York Cotton Exchange and the Commodity Exchange to stay in the city will preserve 9,000 jobs in the downtown area of Manhattan. The exchanges, along with the New York Mercantile Exchange and The New York Futures Exchange, had threatened to move across the Hudson River to New Jersey.
