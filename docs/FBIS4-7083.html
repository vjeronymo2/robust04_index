BFN [Text] Tokyo, April 22 KYODO -- U.S. Defense Secretary William Perry warned Friday that North Korea will soon be able to produce "10 to 12" nuclear bombs a year. "Left unchecked (North Korean) nuclear capability would disrupt the security of all Asia," he said in a speech at the Foreign Correspondents' Club of Japan in Tokyo. Perry said in Seoul on Thursday that intelligence reports indicate North Korea will have the plutonium required for "four to five" nuclear weapons very soon, but his speech Friday is apparently the first time he has indicated a north korean nuclear arsenal could grow so quickly. Before his press club appearance, Perry met with Foreign Minister Tsutomu Hata, and Foreign Ministry officials said the two renewed their determination to get tough on the North Korean nuclear issue. Hata and perry also emphasized the need for a diplomatic solution to the nuclear standoff, the officials said, adding the two also confirmed that Tokyo and Washington will continue to maintain close contact with South Korea.
