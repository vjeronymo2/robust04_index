Aizpeolea report: "Fraga Asks Gonzalez for Overall Pact To Complete Implementation of Autonomy"] [Text] Madrid -- The autonomy battle is reaching fever pitch again. While Catalonia and the Basque Country are in favor of developing a bilateral relationship with the central government, other communities are aiming at an overall agreement in order to complete the implementation of autonomy. Galician government Premier Manuel Fraga (People's Party) proposed the latter option -- a national pact with the participation of all the communities -- to Felipe Gonzalez at the Moncloa Palace yesterday. Fraga's stance is similar to that of Socialist Manuel Chaves, the Andalusia premier, who a week ago criticized the boost to autonomy in some communities, referring to Catalonia and the Basque Country, and proposed to Felipe Gonzalez that the implementation of autonomy be completed in a balanced manner for all regions. Gonzalez has shown himself receptive to these proposals, according to what Fraga stated yesterday, following a meeting lasting over an hour with the prime minister. The similar proposals made by the Galician and Andalusian premiers assume major political importance by being put forward after the central government offered the Basque government a package of 20 transfers [of areas of responsibility] and on the eve of Generalitat [Catalan autonomous government] Premier Jordi Pujol's meeting with Felipe Gonzalez in order to secure a breakthrough in autonomy for Catalonia. Both the Catalan Generalitat and the Basque government want to maintain bilateral relations with the state, but the Galician premier, in harmony with the stance upheld by Chaves a week ago, showed his rejection of that course yesterday. "We do not believe in a bilateral negotiation between the central government and an autonomous government. My administration's proposal never casts doubt on the idea that the formulas are valid for all the autonomous communities. Now that there is an agreement for the `slow track' autonomous communities, as enshrined in the 1982 autonomy pacts, and agreement that another pact must be reached for the municipalities, this is the time to reach a major national agreement in order to complete the implementation of autonomy," Fraga said yesterday.

Single Administration
The Xunta [Galician autonomous government] premier also confirmed that he will send his single administration proposal, which will be contained in a white paper, to the prime minister and to the premiers of the 17 autonomous communities. Yesterday Fraga demanded from Gonzalez a package of transfers -- the Social Institute of the Navy, the material resources of the administration of justice, vocational training, the National Employment Institute, the ports of general interest, and the libraries. Gonzalez and Fraga examined the problems of the dairy sector in his community. The head of government proposed to Fraga that the 10 billion peseta fine for milk surpluses in Galicia during the past farming year be renegotiated with the Agriculture Ministry and the European Commission. The understanding between Gonzalez and Fraga on an issue that concerns the government as much as the configuration of the state was "tremendously important," according to the Xunta premier. Fraga gave Gonzalez a helping hand by stating that he finds no power vacuum, in clear contradiction with the assertions by the previous day by Rodrigo Rato, his party's parliamentary spokesman.
