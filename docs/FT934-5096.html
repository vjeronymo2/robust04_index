931201 FT 01 DEC 93 / Budget 93: Job help for single parents - Childcare A new childcare allowance, worth up to Pounds 28 a week, will be introduced this autumn in a measure which will enable more people, particularly single parents, to take jobs. Single parents have recently been central to the government's review of welfare spending. The allowance will be available to those on Family Credit, a supplement paid to low wage earners. The level of assistance depends on an individual's wages and on how many children there are in a family. People can be eligible who earn up to Pounds 180 a week. There are now about 420,000 beneficiaries. The chancellor said the new allowance 'should help tens of thousands of mothers to get back into work and off income support'. The Campaign for Tax Relief and Childcare welcomed the new measure for families on Family Credit but criticised the chancellor for failing to increase employer-funded childcare and to 'provide practical childcare solutions for thousands of working parents.'
