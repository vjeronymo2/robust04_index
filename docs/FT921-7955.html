920220 FT 20 FEB 92 / Parliament and Politics: Backbench attempt to break deadlock -Sunday Shopping A FRESH attempt to break the deadlock on reform of the Sunday trading laws was made yesterday in a backbench bill which would allow limited types of shop to open but sets stiffer penalties for those which break the law. General stores, petrol-station shops, souvenir shops in zoos, museums or parks, antique shops and shops at sportsgrounds would be allowed to open at any time on a Sunday, as could petrol stations and airport shops. Supermarkets would not be allowed to do so. Other shops, such as garden centres and chemists would be able to open for more limited periods. Stores trading illegally on a Sunday could face fines of Pounds 50,000. Although the new opening hours would apply only to England and Wales, the bill would extend to Scotland in its protection of employees against dismissal for refusing to work on a Sunday.
