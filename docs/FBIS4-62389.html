BFN [Text] A chain of explosions was set off today by a fire at the Pacific Fleet aviation bomb depot, situated near the settlement of Novonezhino, 100 kilometers from Vladivostok. Our correspondent Olga Zhurman has further details. [Zhurman] The fire alarm was sounded at 1520 hours. According to preliminary information, it was caused by the spring stubble burning. The shell explosions spread over a distance of approximately six or seven kilometers. According to the military, no nuclear or chemical weapons were stored at the depot. The fire is still going on. The governor of the kray, head of the internal affairs department, and head of the kray's federal counterintelligence service are at the site of the accident. According to them, there are no casualties. Several houses in Novoneshino had their roofs blown off. The explosion wave broke a large glass display window of a department store in the town of Artem. Explosions can be heard in one of the Vladivostok districts across the Ussuriysk Bay, which separates the city from the accident site. The inhabitants of Novonizhino and the nearby settlements are being evacuated. The site of the accident has been cordoned off.
