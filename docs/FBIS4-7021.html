BFN [Text] Pyongyang, April 23 (KCNA) -- An international organization affiliated with the U.N. Economic and Social Council and the Human Rights Information Centre under it in Moscow issued an information on the human rights situation in South Korea on April 7. The information said despite the adoption of the agreement on reconciliation, nonaggression, cooperation and exchange between the North and the South, activities for national reunification are not only an entity of an acute political struggle but also a sphere of suppression of dissidents in South Korea. Restrictive measures against national peace and reconciliation, free travel and contact between people with different ideologies must be revoked, said the information.
