BFN [Text] Russian Deputy Prime Minister Sergey Shakhray told Interfax that the country's regions and territories would inevitably acquire the economic and legal rights now enjoyed by its republics. He said that 18 out of 21 republics incorporated in Russia were much weaker economically than nearly every region. Shakhray thought that inter-regional association such as the Siberian Accord had become a way to achieve horizontal integration of territories and regions and a new Russian regional policy. Shakhray said that an agreement between the central government and eight inter-regional associations that had been drafted more than six weeks ago could be signed very shortly.
