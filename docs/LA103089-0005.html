October 30, 1989, Monday, Home Edition

NICARAGUA

How short is our memory regarding Nicaragua? Prevented by public opinion from launching an invasion of Nicaragua, the Reagan government bought and created mercenaries, the Contras.

When the law said, "don't arm the Contras," it was continued illegally. Caught in the act, our government manages to supply the Contras, who still kill civilians in Nicaragua.

The U.S. has strangled Nicaragua with a paralyzing embargo and CIA economic sabotage.

Unable to defeat the Nicaraguan people in other ways, it's trying to rig the upcoming elections. President Bush is asking $9 million over the next four months to buy the Nicaraguan election (Part A, Oct. 17).

A small, poor country, receiving more than $5 American dollars per every man, woman and child, could have a consequential impact.

Even so, the U.S. money will not be seen by thousands of poor, war-ravaged people. $1.3 million will go to rent cars at $20 a day for presidential candidate Violeta Barrios de Chamorro's National Opposition Union party.

A U.S. senator is quoted as asking, "Do we really want free elections and fair elections in Nicaragua, or do we want an election bought and paid for by the U.S. government?"

HASKELL WEXLER

Santa Monica
