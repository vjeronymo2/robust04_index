BFN [Excerpts] Mohamed Farah Aidid, chairman of the executive council of the Somali National Alliance, SNA, and chairman of the United Somali Congress, USC, attended the 'Id al-Adha prayers with many Muslims. [passage omitted] Addressing the congregation after the prayers, the chairman of the SNA, Mohamed Farah Aidid, prayed to Allah to accept the good deeds of the Muslims and unite the Muslims wherever they are. [passage omitted] While touring the various sections of the Ministry of Information's headquarters, Mr. Aidid saw the destruction caused by UN Operation in Somalia [UNOSOM] forces to Radio Mogadishu's infrastructure on 12 June 1993. [passage omitted] Aidid, addressing the people who attended the 'Id al-Adha celebrations, thanked all those who attended the various 'Id al-Adha parties and requested them to deliver his greetings to all their friends and supporters. Mr. Aidid narrated the long struggle of the Somali people since 1943 in which the Somali people strived for justice and self-determination. However, this has always been spoiled by foreigners and their surrogates who had their own interests. Talking about the struggle against foreigners and recent meetings held in some Horn of Africa countries where ways of finding a solution to the Somali crisis were discussed, he said that from now on, as agreed, all meetings on self-determination for the Somali people will be held in the country. This will allow the Somali people to participate and to know who is serving their interests and who is obstructing the achievements of unity and progress.
