Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, February 1 (XINHUA) -- Officials with the Beijing Municipal Government and the Beijing Youth Federation held a reception this afternoon together with more than 100 religious leaders and young clergymen. Religious groups in Beijing have made contributions to the capital's economic development and social stability, government officials said. Monsignor Michael Fu Tieshan, a member of the National People's Congress Standing Committee and Catholic bishop of the Beijing Diocese, on behalf of the religious circle, subscribed 100,000 yuan for the national "Hope Project", which promotes children's schooling in poor areas. The city now has more than 400 devotees to religious service, nearly half of whom are young people.
