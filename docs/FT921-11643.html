920130 FT 30 JAN 92 / Observer: Digger's double It is hard to believe there is such a thing as a Finnish double of News Corporation's Rupert Murdoch, but it looks as though the digger has just recruited one to his board. The 59-year-old Aatos Erkko, chief executive of The Sanoma Group for the past 20 years, is nowhere near as well known as Hamish Maxwell, the former chief executive of Philip Morris who has also joined. But our man in Helsinki reports that he is a very big wheel. He owns Finland's biggest selling daily, Helsingin Sanomat, and has extensive interests in magazines and cable TV. In the past his family has provided several politicians, but the publicity-shy Erkko does not interfere editorially in his papers. He also differs from Murdoch in that he does not need to borrow from banks and has no heirs. Part of the attraction, perhaps?
