BFN [Excerpt] The Senate this morning approved the Washington protocol which establishes sanctions against governments installed through illegal means. The protocol has been adopted by the foreign ministers of OAS country members on behalf of their governments. The document sent by the executive branch to the Senate on 13 May refers to a modification to the OAS charter. It adds rules to protect democratically elected administrations and sanctions against those that illegally oust their governors. The senators unanimously approved the protocol. Senator Carlos Alberto Gonzalez, head of the Foreign Affairs Committee, later emphasized its importance. One sanction contemplated in the document states that illegal administrations cannot participate in OAS meetings. The Senate also agreed to the executive branch appointment of Ruben Bareiro Saguier as plenipotentiary ambassador to France. [passage omitted]
