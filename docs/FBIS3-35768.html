Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tallinn, Jan 29, BNS -- Estonian Prime Minister Mart Laar met today in Davos with his Czech counterpart, Vaclav Klaus, the government's press service told BNS. Laar and Klaus discussed fostering economic cooperation between the two countries. They decided a memorandum on a free-trade agreement needs to be drawn up, and preparations for signing an agreement before the end of the year must be started. The two premiers noted that both Estonia and the Czech Republic have chosen a course of radical reforms which, however, seems to be the only resultful policy. Due to the similarity of the two countries' economic policies, their positions coincide almost fully even without prior coordination. The meeting also touched on security issues, particularly security guarantees for Central and East European countries. Laar and Klaus agreed that the Czech Government head will visit Estonia this spring. The visit will probably take place in May.
