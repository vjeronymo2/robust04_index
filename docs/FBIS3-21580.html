Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] President Boris Yeltsin signed a decree charging the Defense Ministry with outlining and carrying out by May 30 a package of measures to organize the daily activities of strategic nuclear forces equipped with non-targeted missiles. As INTERFAX learned at the presidential press service, the decree is aimed at implementing the Moscow Declaration signed by the Russian and U.S. presidents on January 14 and the joint statement by the Russian president and the British premier of February 15. These documents said that Russian nuclear missiles will no longer be targeted against the USA and Great Britain. By another decree Boris Yeltsin set up a commission on supreme military ranks and high military and specialized schools of the Presidential Council for Personnel Policy. The Russian presidential aide for national security Yury Baturin was appointed as the chairman of the commission, the presidential press service reported.
