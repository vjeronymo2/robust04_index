BFN [Text] Cairo, 4 Apr (MENA)--Dr. Mustafa 'Abd-al-'Aziz, Egypt's ambassador to Damascus, has made a statement at the conclusion of the first round of talks between President Husni Mubarak and Syrian President Hafiz al-Asad in Cairo today. He pointed out that the two Arab leaders are discussing the various aspects of the peace process and the obstacles facing it, since it is a major and pivotal issue. He added that the two leaders are also discussing a number of other issues of common interest as well as some pan-Arab issues, including the situation in Yemen and the ongoing efforts to achieve stability there. Dr. 'Abd-al-'Aziz added that the two presidents will meet again this evening to complete their discussion of issues of common interest. During the evening session, the two leaders are expected to review all matters related to bilateral relations between the two countries and assess what has been achieved during the meetings of the Egyptian-Syrian higher joint committee, he added. Dr. 'Abd-al-'Aziz described Egyptian-Syrian relations as excellent and said they are proceeding normally with no problems. Every now and then, he added, these ties need to be reviewed to give them momentum. President Mubarak will host a dinner banquet in honor of President al-Asad and his accompanying delegation this evening. The two leaders are expected to hold a news conference at the end of their talks, which may last until tomorrow morning.
