Language: Spanish Article Type:CSO [Article by Jose Mercedes Feliz: "Income Tax Collected Totals 3,777 Billion Pesos"] [Text] During the first 10 months of this year, the Income Tax Directorate collected revenue amounting to 3,777.9 billion pesos, an increase of 16.4 percent over the same period in 1992. According to a report on collection trends during the period from January to October of 1992, the institution took in 3,243.5 billion pesos. So far this year, the Income Tax Directorate has done well in terms of revenue from the different taxes collected. Institution officials had anticipated revenue totaling some 4,187.9 billion pesos for all of 1993, meaning that only 410 million remain to be obtained from the November and December collections. From January to October of 1993, the institution collected income tax revenue from individuals, plus surcharges and fines, totaling 54.9 million pesos, a drop of 38.1 percent compared with the same period of 1992, when the figure was 88.8 million. This was the only tax that recorded a drop during the first 10 months of the year. At the same time, from January to October of 1993, the institution took in 1,413.9 billion pesos from corporations, an increase of only .6 percent compared with the same period in 1992, when the amount collected was 1,404.5 billion. Additional payments also brought in 18.1 million pesos in revenue from January to October of this year, an increase of 112.9 percent compared with the same period in 1993, when the total was only 8.5 million. During the first 10 months of 1993, the amount of taxes withheld--including income tax--totaled 2,534.4 billion pesos.
