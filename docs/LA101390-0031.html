October 13, 1990, Saturday, Valley Edition

DIGEST: NATIONAL HARDCOURT EVENT SCHEDULED AT WESTLAKE CLUB

More than 150 players will compete in the U. S. Tennis Assn. National men's 50-55 hardcourt championships beginning Monday at Westlake Tennis and Swim Club in Westlake Village.

The tournament, which is open to the public at no charge, starts Monday at 1:30 p.m. and will continue through Oct. 21.

Top-seeded players in the age 50-and-older singles division include Len Saputo (Walnut Creek, Calif.), Bob Duesler (Newport Beach) and David Weber (Omaha). Top-seeded players in the age 55-and-older division are Gordon Davis (Encino), Paul Cranis (Boca Raton, Fla.) and Leonard Lindborg (South Laguna).

Pancho Gonzales and Alex Olmedo are featured in the 50-and-older doubles division and are seeded seventh.

Information: 818-889-6164.

The San Fernando Valley chapter of the National Football Foundation announced that high school football standouts Ron Adelman (Calabasas), Eric Arciniega (Burroughs), Tony Cesta (Bell-Jeff) and Jay Sumner (Burbank) have been selected players of the week by the coaches at their respective schools.

Each week, the foundation chooses a different group of schools from the 51 in the chapter's area, and coaches select their team's best player. Each school in the San Fernando Valley area will have a player honored by season's end.

The Valley chapter will hold an open-house meeting Oct. 25 at 6 p.m. at 16235 Barneston St., Granada Hills.

The meeting is open to anyone interested in high school football.

Information: 818-249-0131.
