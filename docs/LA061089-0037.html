June 10, 1989, Saturday, Home Edition

FRED ROGGIN'S HUMOR BELONGS IN LOCKER ROOM

I agree with Larry Stewart's comments regarding Fred Roggin's penchant for low humor.

Another factor that detracts from Roggin's work is his ongoing lack of respect for women. If a bikini-clad woman is in attendance at a sporting event, Roggin will comment:

-- There will be no tittering about this play.

-- The play was busted up.

-- It's a good day for a doubleheader.

-- He's just trying to keep us abreast of sports.

One expects humor like this in a junior high locker room, but it has no place in a news broadcast.

CHARLES GOULD

Los Angeles
