Language: Urdu Article Type:CSO [News Report: ``42 Kilogram Hashish Recovered After Firing Exchange''] [Text] Peshawar (News Reporter) -- In an exchange of gunfire between smugglers and the Pakistan Narcotics Control Board [PNCB], the latter recovered a huge amount of hashish and heroin, which was being smuggled to Punjab. The smugglers abandoned their car on the road and fled. The police have confiscated the car. According to details, Inspector Riza Khan and Wasih Allah led a special team from PNCB Peshawar to watch the GT Road. One car, tag number CD 122 AF, passed them, and they asked it to stop. The smugglers accelerated instead, however. The police gave chase, and there was an exchange of fire between the smugglers and narcotics personnel. The smugglers left their car in the dark near Hakimabad Naushara and fled. The Narcotics Control Board personnel searched the car and recovered 37 kilograms of hashish and five kilograms of high-grade heroin from hidden compartments. A case against the smugglers has been registered in the police station at Peshawar.
