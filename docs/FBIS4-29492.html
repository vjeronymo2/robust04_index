BFN [Text] The Senate meeting convened at Parliament this morning at 0935. The meeting considered the house-approved draft copyright bill. A number of senators debated in support of the bill saying that it will contribute to developing the national economy to be in line with the world economic situation. They said this will lead to greater development of the country's economy because it will also result in expansion of trade and industry. The draft bill will encourage Thai people to be enthusiastic in using new and modern inventions. Deputy Commerce Minister Churin Laksanawisit said that the draft copyright bill will provide protection for data work and actors' achievements. Regarding computer programs, over which many parties have expressed concerns, they will be protected by the existing law. After a considerable period of debate, the meeting decided to accept in principle the draft bill before being passed for further consideration by the commission. The meeting was adjourned at 1210. Earlier, it took the House of Representatives three weeks to deliberate and approve this draft copyright bill.
