940531 FT 31 MAY 94 / Airbus ministers get tough with US The four nations which make up the Airbus consortium yesterday urged the European Commission to 'act vigorously' in talks with the US on an international trade regime for civil aviation. European irritation has grown over alleged interference in the aviation market by the White House and the US Commerce Department, which are perceived to have leaned heavily on foreign governments to buy US aircraft. Trade and economy ministers from Britain, Germany, France and Spain were meeting in the margins of the Berlin Air Show to discuss the issue. Mr Gunter Rexrodt, the German economy minister, said he and his colleagues had decided to write to the Commission urging it to maintain a firm stance. Negotiators from the European Union and the US started work a month ago on renewing a trade pact sealed in July 1992 which covers civil aviation but is regarded by the Europeans as unsatisfactory. Another European complaint is that the US government is heavily subsidising US aviation through the back door by funding research which is nominally connected with defence but has beneficial side effects for the civil sector. Mr Rexrodt said: 'There are indications that the Americans are proceeding in a very unconventional way.'
