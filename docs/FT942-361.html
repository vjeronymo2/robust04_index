940629 FT 29 JUN 94 / UK Company News: Enlarged Debenham Tewson improves 44% Pre-tax profits of Debenham Tewson & Chinnocks, one of the few quoted estate agents and chartered surveyors specialising in commercial property, rose by 44 per cent, from Pounds 2.86m to Pounds 4.12m during the 12 months to April 30. The company also announced that Mr Anthony Turnbull intends to step down as chief executive at the beginning of next year. Mr Mark Struckett, currently managing director of the company's London operations, becomes group managing director. Turnover rose 40 per cent to Pounds 57.6m (Pounds 41m). Earnings per share rose to 6.17p (5.13p) and the final dividend is increased to 2p for a total of 3.5p (3p). The company, which last year merged with Bernard Thorpe, has expanded its regional network as well as increasing international operations. Mr Richard Lay, chairman, said that the results showed that the policies were starting to bear fruit. On the general state of the commercial property market, he said: 'We have seen a reversal in the trends of previous years and a significant recovery in the capital values of commercial investment property as yields have fallen to reflect the reduction in short term interest rates. 'However, we have not yet seen any return to growth in rental values across the country and until this does occur we shall not enjoy a sustainable recovery in our markets.'
