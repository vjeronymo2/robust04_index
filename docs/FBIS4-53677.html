BFN [Text] Tokyo, May 22 KYODO -- A Honduras-flagged freighter with some 60 non-Japanese Asians on board is approaching southern Japan, TBS TV reported Sunday [22 May]. Five patrol boats are keeping watch on the 500-ton vessel, identified as the Montana, which was spotted in waters some 35 kilometers off Tanegashima Island, Kagoshima Prefecture, Tokyo Broadcasting System said. The vessel is sailing slowly west-northwest, it said. Maritime Safety Agency officials suspect the passengers may be attempting to enter Japan illegally, TBS said. On Wednesday, a group of 138 Chinese were apprehended while hiding in a warehouse at Hakata port in Fukuoka. They were arrested for allegedly entering the country illegally.
