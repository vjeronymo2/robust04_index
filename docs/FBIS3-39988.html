Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] At a cordial and constructive meeting with Italian Foreign Minister Andreatta, Mate Granic was told that Italy is willing to help in the process of seeking solutions. Minister Andreatta -- who is very familiar with the situation -- expressed satisfaction with the fact that Italy is involved. He told Minister Granic that Italy has a great deal of understanding for Croatia's position. The talks resulted in the realization that relations between Croatia and Italy are being advanced to a great extent and that Italy can exercise a positive influence in the process of seeking solutions. The two ministers talked extensively over a working lunch. The Croatian side assesses the talks as cordial and very fruitful. Minister Granic and Ambassadors Rudolf and Livljanic talked early this evening with Monsignor Jean-Louis Tauran, the Vatican's state secretary. The sixth meeting between the two politicians took place in a very cordial atmosphere. The Holy See is following developments on the ground with great interest as well as the diplomatic process for resolving the crisis. It is paying particular attention to the agreement between Generals Roso and Delic. In the Vatican there are reports that this agreement has a good chance of success. The Holy See reiterated its opinion that a solution to the crisis should certainly be found, and also that agreement should be reached between the Croats and Muslims. The pope will be informed about the talks. Minister Granic has handed over President Tudjman's message to the pope. [passage omitted]
