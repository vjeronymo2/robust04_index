May 17, 1990, Thursday, Home Edition

BENNETT INVESTIGATION

Your story on Bennett's problems with his travel expense account is ironic when considering his recent action.

Not long ago the board angered a lot of Californians by billing them for sales tax on the trinkets they brought home from foreign trips. The public cry of anguish led the board not only to halt the practice, but to order all payments refunded. Not good enough for Bennett. He went to court and had the board's action stayed. He hopes the courts permanently prevent the refunds. "Ignorance of the law is no excuse," he says. I think he tripped himself up.

ROBERT E. RYAN

Rancho Palos Verdes
