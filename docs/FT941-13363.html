940126 FT 26 JAN 94 / VAT on adult education mooted The government yesterday admitted that it was considering whether value added tax should be imposed on adult further education classes. Ms Ann Taylor, shadow education secretary, said the proposal was 'appalling'. She said there should be 'no place in a civilised society for taxes on learning'. Sir John Cope, paymaster general, announced the review in a parliamentary written answer released as MPs began debating the finance bill implementing last November's Budget. Responding to a question from Mr Andrew Smith, a Labour Treasury spokesman, Sir John said Customs and Excise was considering the VAT position of evening classes as part of a review of education and training services.
