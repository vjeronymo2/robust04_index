Language: Chinese Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, 28 Mar (XINHUA) -- Korean [ROK] President Kim Yong-sam, who is currently visiting Beijing, said here today: Korea and China should build future-oriented, complementary relations of economic cooperation. Kim Yong-sam delivered a speech on Korean-Chinese economic relations and trade at a luncheon hosted by the China Council for the Promotion of International Trade, and the China Chamber of International Commerce. He said: Close cooperation between Korea and China can play its due role in promoting common prosperity in the two countries, as well as in stimulating the regional and world economies. He said: Economic cooperation between the two countries should go beyond the stage of merely trading in materials and goods. The two countries should build future-oriented, complementary relations of cooperation by further expanding joint development and financing for new technology. Kim Yong-sam expressed hope that Korean enterprises would actively participate in the Chinese market and seek common development with their Chinese counterparts. He said that Korean enterprises were capable of becoming good partners to their Chinese counterparts. In his speech, Zheng Hongye, chairman of the China Council for the Promotion of International Trade and the China Chamber of International Commerce, expressed his belief that the president's current visit would definitely have a positive effect on promoting trade, as well as economic and technological cooperation and exchanges, between the two countries. Before the luncheon, Li Lanqing, vice premier of the State Council, met Kim Yong-sam and his entourage at the Diaoyutai State Guesthouse. The two sides reviewed the development of economic relations and trade since their countries established diplomatic relations, unanimously maintaining that China and Korea should work harder to promote the further development of economic relations and trade.
