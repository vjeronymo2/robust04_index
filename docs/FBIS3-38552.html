Language: German Article Type:BFN [Text] Cologne -- Unlike Defense Minister Volker Ruehe (Christian Democratic Union), Foreign Minister Klaus Kinkel (Free Democratic Party of Germany) believes that a Bundeswehr strength of 370,000 men should be "preserved by all means." In an interview with the Cologne EXPRESS (Friday edition), the minister stated that he is aware of the defense minister's financial worries and concerns. Yet he added that, in the event of a further reduction of the Bundeswehr, the constitutional stipulation of equality in conscription would sooner or later become a problem. It must be determined what troop strength is needed for what purpose, and then the financial aspect must be discussed, and not the other way round, under the motto: "How much money do we have, and how many soldiers can we afford for that?" Ruehe stated in Bonn on Wednesday [2 February], that, in order to cut defense expenditure, the strength of the Bundeswehr should be reduced to 345,000 soldiers by the end of 1994 and not to 370,000, as originally planned.
