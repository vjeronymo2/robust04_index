BFN [Text] Chairman of the Council of Europe Parliamentary Assembly Miguel Martinez was sure that Russia would be admitted to the Council of Europe at its session in February, 1995. This was stated today at the meeting between Miguel Martinez and Russian State Duma Speaker Ivan Rybkin. Martinez recognized that some members of the Council of Europe Parliamentary Assembly had certain doubts on admission of Russia. He stressed that he was trying to talk three or four leading states into supporting the idea. Miguel Martinez emphasized that several stated would regard the presence of Russian troops in Estonia and Latvia as an obstacle to Russia's admission to the Council of Europe. Martinez himself did not see the problem as one of basic importance. Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev received Miguel Martinez on the same day. Kozyrev said that the Russian leadership was attracting much of attention to dynamic development of relations with the Council of Europe, the Russian Foreign Ministry told Interfax. Kozyrev suggested that adoption of the new constitution and the free democratic elections in Russia had solved all key-problems related to the admission of Russia to the Council of Europe. He hoped that Russia's application for admission would be considered timely.
