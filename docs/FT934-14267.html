931018 FT 18 OCT 93 / Construction Contracts: Education work starts Nearly Pounds 9m worth of new contracts, mostly in the education field, have been won by Mowlem South Wales of Cardiff and Llanelli, part of John Mowlem Construction. In the Rhondda valley, Mowlem has won a Pounds 3.14m contract from Mid Glamorgan County Council education committee, to replace fire-damaged classrooms at Ferndale Comprehensive School. At Trinity College, Carmarthen, Mowlem has been awarded a Pounds 1.5m contract to design and build two accommodation blocks for 144 students. The blocks will contain flats with eight bedrooms, each with en suite shower room, toilet and wash basin. Mowlem has also won work for a new Pounds 850,000 visitors' centre for Dyfed Wildlife Trust, Cardigan. The contract involves a three-storey timber and steel framed building with walls of oak and softwood boarding, cedar shingle roof covering and a fully glazed elevation to the Valley Meadow. Completion of work is expected by Christmas. Other contracts recently awarded include alterations to Lloyds Bank, Bridgend (Pounds 580,000); a new Job Centre in Port Talbot (Pounds 400,000); the design and construction of workshop units at Lamby Way (phase II) for Cardiff City Council (Pounds 380,000), the refurbishment of National Westminster Bank at Pontypridd (Pounds 180,000) and improvements to Wedal Road, Cardiff for Cardiff City Council (Pounds 170,000).
