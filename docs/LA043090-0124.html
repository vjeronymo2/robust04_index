April 30, 1990, Monday, P.M. Final

SHORT TAKES;

MOVIE ON GM AMUSES EAST BLOC

The movie "Roger and Me" is a hit in Eastern Europe because it shows audiences what 80 years of capitalism have done to the General Motors Corp. company town of Flint, director Michael Moore said.

But Moore said Warner Brothers, which distributed his documentary film about GM layoffs and his comically futile efforts to interview Chairman Roger Smith, is happy with "Roger and Me" for reasons different than his.

The film has grossed more than $7 million since its release in January and still plays in 35 U.S. cities, Moore said Saturday at a fund raiser for a legislative candidate.

"Warner Brothers is not promoting the film because they are at the forefront of political revolution," he said. "They're doing it because they think it will make money."
