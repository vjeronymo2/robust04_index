Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Sweden has turned down a United Nations request to send more troops to Bosnia on the grounds that it presently has no soldiers trained to carry out the assignment. Sixteen other countries, including Norway, have also been approached. And, 11 Swedes are receiving intensive training in Serbo-Croatian in order to serve as interpreters in the Nordic UN battalion in Bosnia. They are to replace Swedish interpreters of Yugoslavian origin who, it is feared, run the risk of being considered biased by Bosnia's ethnic groups on the grounds of their accents or family names.
