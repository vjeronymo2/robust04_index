BFN [Text] Beijing, June 7 (XINHUA) -- Chinese Vice-Premier and Foreign Minister Qian Qichen met here today with a Buddhist delegation headed by the Myanmar [Burma] minister of religious affairs, Lieutenant-General Myo Nyunt. The delegation arrived here on June 5 to convey Buddha's tooth relic back to China. The sacred tooth of Buddha of China was allowed to be taken to Myanmar (formerly Burma) from April 20 to June 5. Qian said that the sojourn of the tooth relic in Myanmar was an important event in Sino-Myanmar links and religious relations between the two countries. He expressed thanks for the careful arrangement the Myanmar Government had made for the sojourn. Nyunt said that to make the event, a milestone of their bilateral relations, the Myanmar Government decided to build two Buddha towers in Yangon [Rangoon], its capital, and Mandalay, its second largest city, using donations of the Myanmar people. Over 85 percent of the Myanmar population are Buddhists, and the country's rich cultural heritage, glittering temples and pagodas are well-known throughout the world.
