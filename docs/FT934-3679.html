931208 FT 08 DEC 93 / Homes 'not good investment' Thousands of homeowners are likely to remain trapped until the next century in properties worth less than the money borrowed to buy them, according to a study published today by Legal & General insurance group. Mr David Shaw, investment strategy director, said: 'No one should invest in housing in the 1990s if they want competitive high returns and capital growth. Equities, bonds, commercial property and, perhaps, even cash will remain far more attractive.'
