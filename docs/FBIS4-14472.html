BFN [From the "Diplomatic Panorama" feature: by correspondents Dmitriy Voskoboinikov, Igor Porshnev, and Vitaliy Trubetskoy] [Text] The proposal made by the Kazakh President Nursultan Nazarbayev on the setting up of a Euro-Asian Union of CIS member states would generate more questions than answer them, a highly placed Russian Foreign Ministry official in charge of CIS affairs told Interfax. He described Nazarbayev's idea as interesting "but every idea must be thoroughly prepared. In particular, there is no saying what consequences would follow for the CIS that has just won the status of an observer in the UN. If closer ties between two or more CIS member states frighten the others and the union becomes a purely formal body, this will hardly be a desirable outcome." In his words, Russia wanted a stronger Commonwealth. "Ways have to be found to implement the Kazakh president's proposals that would be conducive to this goal," the diplomat said.
