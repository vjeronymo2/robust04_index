BFN [Text] Suzhou, April 20 (XINHUA) -- The Suzhou State High-Tech Development Zone in east China's Jiangsu Province has achieved good success, attracting increasing numbers of investors from at home and abroad. It now has over 170 foreign-funded projects, with a total investment of 1.7 billion U.S. dollars. The firms are mainly engaged in electronics, biology, pharmaceuticals and other technology-intensive industries. China now has a total of 52 such state-level high-tech zones, which serve as major implementation bases of the "Torch Program", a national high- and new-technology development blueprint. According to the State Science and Technology Commission, fixed assets investment in these zones was 13 billion yuan (about 140 million U.S. dollars) in 1993. Their development areas total over 60 sq km and nearly six million sq m [square meters] of floor space of buildings have been completed and over 12 million sq m of buildings are under construction. The gross industrial and commercial income of the zones is now 57 billion yuan (about 630 million U.S. dollars), six times that of 1991. Their export volume was over 700 million U.S. dollars last year. By the end of last year more than 10,700 high-tech enterprises had been set up in the zones. Industries such as in optical-fiber communications and electronic information have become pillars of the zones. Some zones have expanded from sole industrial development to comprehensive communities, where a service system covering finance, information, insurance, accounting, law, education, transportation, commerce and trade has emerged.
