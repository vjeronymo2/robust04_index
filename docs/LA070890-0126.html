July 8, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

CURRENT PAPERBACKS: THE 1990 DOONESBURY STAMP ALBUM BY GARRY TRUDEAU (PENGUIN: $8.95)

Unlike most contemporary cartoonists, Garry Trudeau has stubbornly resisted attempts to merchandise his strip. The first "Doonesbury" license product was created to raise money for "Writers' Voices," a national program that provides accessible reading material to the 27 million American adults who are illiterate or have only limited reading skills.

This spoof of a conventional stamp album allows Trudeau an opportunity to show off his skill as a graphic artist. The cleverly designed pseudo-stamps include images of his familiar characters (Zonker darkens like a roasting Thanksgiving turkey as his tan deepens from "Gaucho Glow" to "Malibu Melt") and a series of mordant lampoons of George Bush. A map showing the homes of the regular "Doonesbury" cast includes special graphic symbols indicating Elvis sightings, oil spills, Geraldo-free zones and Towns Without Pity.
