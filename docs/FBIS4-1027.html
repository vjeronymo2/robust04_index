BFN [Report by N. Lewis] [Text] Pretoria April 19 SAPA -- The South African Police [SAP] on Tuesday [19 April] appealed to the public to disregard alarmist rumours about impending widespread violence, food shortages and similar speculation in the run-up to the elections. It said it was aware of the rumours and a growing number of facsimile messages urging the public to take special precautions. "It is in our best interest that we remain calm and rational. The SAP requests the public to disregard irresponsible, inaccurate rumours. There are assurances from many companies that the services they provide will continue to be provided during the election period."
