February 14, 1990, Wednesday, Home Edition

VITAMIN INTAKE

Question: Correct me if I'm wrong, but I believe that some vitamins are what is called water-soluble.

I also believe that if these vitamins are taken in large doses, they will simply be flushed out of the body. As I think you once said, this may result in expensive urine, but what harm has been done? Would there be any possible harm in taking vitamins as long as they're water soluble?

Answer: Taking large amounts of some water-soluble vitamins can be toxic. If you feel the need for a supplement, first consult your physician.
