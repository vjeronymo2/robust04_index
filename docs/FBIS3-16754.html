Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Moscow March 21 TASS -- Russian First Deputy Prime Minister Oleg Soskovets denied press reports about his involvement in the alleged "anti-Yeltsin coup" and asked the office of the prosecutor general to clear up the accusations. The "OBSHCHAYA GAZETA" said in its 11th edition that a "group of state officials" planned "to oust President Boris Yeltsin" and listed Soskovets among them. Acting Prosecutor General Alexey Ilyushenko ordered to check the claims, the prosecutor's office said on Monday adding that the Interior Ministry, [and] the Federal Counterintelligence Service are also engaged in the investigation. It added that all measures are being taken to check whether the press report corresponds to reality and warned that the newspaper may face criminal charges "of slander combined with accusation of a state crime" if the report was false.
