BFN [Text] Paris, May 26 (AA) -- Foreign Minister Hikmet Cetin said on Thursday [26 May] that the initiatives taken toward Europe's security and stability were very appropriate. Speaking during European Stability Conference, Cetin revealed Turkey's views. Cetin said the violence and ethnic cleaning in Bosnia has not been responded to adding that the bloodshed in Bosnia-Herzegovina has not ended yet. Cetin said Europe is passing through a hard transition period. `The problems created after the collapse of iron curtain need attention,' Cetin said. He said the incidents happening in Bosnia today create dangerous examples for future `extreme nationalism, religious and cultural intolerance and racism which could arise again in various places in Europe.' During his speech Cetin stressed good-neighborly relations and the importance of regional cooperation. He said Turkey is ready to contribute to regional cooperation programs within the framework of European Stability Agreement. Turkey is an associate member of European Union and aims at full membership.
