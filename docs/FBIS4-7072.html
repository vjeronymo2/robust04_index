BFN [Text] Seoul yesterday welcomed the inauguration of the new Japanese government led by Prime Minister Tsutomu Hata. Foreign Ministry spokesman Chang Ki-ho hoped, in a commentary, that South Korea and Japan, sharing common values and ideology, will further strengthen friendly and cooperative ties while the new Japanese government is in office. "We expect South Korea and Japan to be able to build a new dimension of cooperative relations in preparation for the coming 21st century," and Chang. He also praised Hata's predecessor, Morihiro Hosokawa, for his efforts to promote bilateral relations between South Korea and Japan.
