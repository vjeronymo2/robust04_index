920210 FT 10 FEB 92 / Observer: Poor example Meanwhile, I hear that Sir Frank Rogers, chairman of the Newspaper Publishers Association, is a mite embarrassed by the activities of some of his members. For more than 20 years now, Sir Frank, who is also deputy chairman of the Telegraph group, has been urging the newspaper industry to fight the competition from TV by arguing the case for newspaper advertising collectively. In November and December the industry spent Pounds 7m on more than 140 full page ads with provocative copy lines such as ' Some people are being conned by TV commercials. The people who pay for them.' Unfortunately, some of Sir Frank's fellow publishers, led by the Mirror Group, don't seem to have much faith in their own message. Last month was almost certainly a record for newspaper spending on TV advertising.
