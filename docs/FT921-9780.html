920210 FT 10 FEB 92 / Treasury asks for forecasting input from industrialists TOP INDUSTRIALISTS are to be invited to an advisory panel to feed insights on economic trends to the Treasury under a plan to improve the government's forecasting record and strengthen dialogue with business. The initiative comes as the government seeks to counter criticism ahead of the general election that it has misjudged the seriousness of the recession and become too detached from the views of industry. There would be regular meetings between senior Treasury officials, led by Sir Terry Burns, permanent secretary, and a team of industrialists. Treasury economists are revising down their forecasts for this year's growth after conceding that the recession is dragging on longer than they expected last year. The new forecasts will be announced by Mr Norman Lamont, the chancellor, in his Budget statement on March 10. In recent months, ministers have indicated concern that Labour, which is stressing a pro-industry stance, may capture a large share of the votes of business people. Mr John MacGregor, leader of the Commons, is co-ordinating a broad government effort to improve relations with industry. The Treasury said that the idea of holding 'systematic and regular' contacts with industry had come from business representatives. The timetable for establishing the meetings has yet to be decided but the department said that 'nothing is likely to be announced before the Budget'. Senior business representatives welcomed news of the initiative. Mr Miles Middleton, president of the Association of British Chambers of Commerce, said the Treasury had made a 'fundamental mistake' in recent years in not talking enough to people from industry. He added that the Treasury's forecasting record was 'lousy'. Mr David Wigglesworth, chairman of the Confederation of British Industry's economic situation committee, said: 'Anything that improves the links between the Treasury and industrialists would be to everyone's advantage.'
