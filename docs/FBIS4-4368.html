CSO [Text] Kota Kinabalu, 13 Feb--The ruling party, the Sabah United Party (PBS), has never been serious in its efforts to develop and advance the state. Deputy Prime Minister Datuk Sri Anwar Ibrahim said the matter is evident from the attitude of the PBS, which has never made any request of the Federal Government regarding development and progress for the benefit and comfort of the people of Sabah. "In the more than four years since the PBS left the National Front (BN), the PBS has never made any clear or serious request for the purpose of developing the State of Sabah," he told reporters today at Papar Mosque, about 50 kilometers from the state capital, when interviewed at a meeting with the public to break the fast. Also present at the meeting were Osu Sukam, BN candidate from the Kawang District, and Rahim Ismail, BN candidate from the Buang Sayang District, who are, respectively, president and deputy president of the UMNO [United Malays National Organization] Papar Division. Anwar, who is also minister of finance, said that from the time the PBS stabbed the BN in the back until now, the Central Government has never received any letter from the party for the purpose of obtaining allocations for development for the benefit of the people of Sabah. "All that the Central Government received from the PBS was a request for privatization projects, including the privatization of Sabah Forest Industries (SFI), which would benefit merely a few PBS leaders," he said. On the same subject, Anwar advised the people of Sabah to be cautious of PBS political propaganda as the election approaches. He said the people of Sabah must not let the PBS buy their dignity during the "election fever" season before the eighth Sabah State Legislature election, now only about four days away. According to Anwar, the PBS will soon use its money "distribution" tactic among the people of Sabah. "I will not be surprised if the PBS soon distributes two or three hundred ringgit to every Sabahan. But what is the value of that amount of money over five years? Where are our national values and what is the value of our dignity if our eyes are closed by two or three hundred ringgit distributed every five years?" he asked. Anwar called on the people of Sabah to eliminate the PBS in this election in order to move in a more meaningful direction in the future. He said the people of Sabah who are Muslims have no choice other than to bring down the PBS, which does not respect Sabahans who follow Islam.
