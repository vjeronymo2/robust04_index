Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The commerce minister, Mr. Pranab Mukherjee, says the world business community is keen on investing in India. He said with a fully developed industrial base and trained manpower of 3.8 million scientists and technically qualified people, the foreign investment in India is bound to grow. Mr. Mukherjee, who is in Davos to attend the World Economic Forum meeting met several world economic leaders. Our correspondent Sudarshanam understands that Mr. Mukherjee also had four sessions with various businessmen and industrialists. In these meetings he made it clear that the economic liberalization initiated by the government is irreversible. He pointed out that India has a huge market to offer to the investors which is unmatched in any part of the globe. Speaking on global urbanization in Davos, the Maharashtra chief minister, Mr. Sharad Pawar, called for more funds to help developing countries counter the adverse implications of urbanization in health and education. Mr. Pawar urged the developed countries to encourage research and training to reconcile city growth with healthy environment. He proposed the setting up of satellite towns to decentralize development and information, and said entertainment be brought by satellites to remote areas so that migration to cities can be checked. Mr. Pawar is participating in the World Economic Forum meeting at the invitation of the organizers.
