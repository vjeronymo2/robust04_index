BFN [Text] The Azerbaijani Army on Monday [18 April] morning resumed attacks on Nagorno-Karabakh positions in the Mardakert, Martuni and Fizuli sectors of the front, a Nagorno-Karabakh military source said. He told INTERFAX fighting was still in progress at noon. On Sunday, intense fighting was going on on a large section of the front in Mardakert and Fizuli Districts. The source said Nagorno-Karabakh forces had rebuffed all the strikes and had switched over to launching counterattacks in some areas, mainly in Mardakert District where it had destroyed some of the Azerbaijani firing positions. The source denied Azerbaijani reports that hundreds of Nagorno-Karabakh troops had been killed. He said Azerbaijan's losses had been much heavier than Nagorno-Karabakh's. Meanwhile, the Azerbaijani Defense Ministry said that, on Sunday afternoon, Armenian tank units positioned in northern Nagorno-Karabakh had made several attempts to advance towards the town of Terter, but were stopped by Azerbaijani forces, which destroyed six Armenian tanks and seized another two pieces of new armor. Early on Monday morning, the Armenians once again tried to break through to Terter, simultaneously attacking villages close to the town, but were thrown back around 0800 after heavy fighting, losing three tanks, one armored personnel carrier and about 90 troops, the ministry said.
