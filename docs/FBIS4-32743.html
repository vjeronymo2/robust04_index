BFN [Text] Pernik, June 26 (BTA) -- Considering the conditions and the way in which the census in Macedonia [The Former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia -- FYROM] is being conducted, the lack of normal conditions for the expression of ethnic identity, the curtailment of freedom of speech and the expression of personal views and critical statements, considering certain facts of police coercion, and the open anti-Bulgarian campaign waged by the government-controlled media, I cannot accept the results of the census as objective and accurate, Nikolay Slatinski MP, chairman of Parliament's National Security Committee, told BTA. He said he expressed his personal view. The official government position is delayed, obscure and inadequate, according to him. According to him, the policy pursued by the Macedonian authorities, of putting obstacles to normal good-neighbourly relations, could lead to a review of the Bulgarian position, and a firmer Bulgarian stand on Macedonia.
