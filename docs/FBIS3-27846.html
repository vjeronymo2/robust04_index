Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, Jan. 27 KYODO -- Japan on Thursday [27 January] agreed with Jamaica on rescheduling of Japanese loans to the country, government officials said. The agreement covers a total of 1.416 million yen worth of capital and interest payments due on September 30, 1995. The rescheduled debt is part of loans to Jamaica by the Export- Import Bank of Japan and Japan's Overseas Economic Cooperation Fund. Under the agreement, Jamaica is allowed to postpone to 2000 payments on loans from the bank, and to 2004 the repayment on loans from the fund, the officials said. It is the seventh debt payment rescheduling for Jamaica, they said.
