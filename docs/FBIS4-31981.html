BFN [Text] Sofia, June 15 (BTA) -- Stressing once more Bulgaria's position of noninterference in the Yugo-conflict in any form whatsoever, Georgi Dimitrov, leader of the Bulgarian delegation to the 27th regular meeting of the Committee of Senior Officials of the Conference on Security and Cooperation in Europe in Prague, stated that Bulgaria was the most badly hit by the embargo on the former Yugoslavia, BTA learned from the Foreign Ministry spokesman. Referring to the decisions of the CSCE Council of Foreign Ministers' meeting in Rome on "continuous control of the observance of CSCE standards and principles for human rights improvement and protection of minorities in Yugoslavia (Serbia and Montenegro)," Mr. Dimitrov praised the results achieved by the meeting of representatives of the Bulgarian minority in eastern Serbia and officials of the embassies of the CSCE participating countries held at the Italian Embassy in Belgrade in May. The leader of the Bulgarian delegation insisted on ensuring the conditions which would enable the Bulgarian minority in Serbia exercise their legitimate rights and freedoms in full in compliance with the CSCE standards.
