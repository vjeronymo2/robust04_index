June 21, 1990, Thursday, Home Edition

47 CERTIFIED MARKETS DOT REGION;

RETAILING: STATE APPROVAL GUARANTEES QUALITY STANDARDS AND THAT A FARMER -- NOT A MIDDLEMAN -- IS DOING THE SELLING.

There are 47 certified farmers' markets in Southern California -- stretching from Santa Maria south to Pacific Beach. Some are farmers' markets in the strictest sense, where the sole purpose is the direct sale of farm products. Others have expanded the concept to include educational demonstrations, entertainment and booths selling prepared foods or nonfood items.

Before a farmer can sell commodities at a certified farmers' market, the county agricultural commissioner must check that the farmer actually grows what is sold. This inspection guarantees buyers they are purchasing directly from the farmer, a family member or employee.

Certification also guarantees the produce meets the same quality and maturity standards required for supermarkets. It does, however, exempt the produce from normal standards for size and appearance, as well as packing and labeling requirements; therefore, some produce in the farmers' markets may vary from normal size and might be blemished.

Certified farmers' markets are part of a direct marketing program of the California Department of Food and Agriculture. The program offers technical assistance to nonprofit organizations, local government agencies and one or more certified producers who want to open and operate a certified market.
