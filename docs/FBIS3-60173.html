Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [From the "Newspaper of the Airwaves" newscast] [Text] President Alberto Fujimori and Alexander Watson, U.S. assistant secretary of state for Inter-American affairs, have discussed democracy, human rights, and drug trafficking. Justice Minister Fernando Vega Santagadea explained that the dialogue was smooth and that there were no points of disagreement: [Begin Vega Santagadea recording] Mr. Watson knows Peru's political and social situation very well. The dialogue went smoothly. There were no points of disagreement. Every topic was discussed, particularly those dealing with the administration of justice, human rights, the administration of jails, drug trafficking, the upcoming summit of heads of state proposed by the United States for the second half of 1994--a meeting that is to be held in the United States, but details of this will have to be furnished by Mr. Watson directly. I will meet with Watson's delegation tomorrow to discuss the justice problem. [end recording]
