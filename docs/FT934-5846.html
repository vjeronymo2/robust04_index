931127 FT 27 NOV 93 / Finance and the Family: Your CGT allowances The table shows capital gains tax indexation allowances for assets sold in October. Multiply the original cost of the asset by the figure shown for the month in which you bought it. Subtract the results from the proceeds of your sale and the balance will be your taxable gain or loss. Suppose that you bought shares for Pounds 6,000 in July 1986 and sold them in October 1993 for Pounds 14,000. Multiplying the original cost by the July 1986 figure of 1.454 gives a total of Pounds 8,724. Subtracting that from Pounds 14,000 gives a capital gain of Pounds 5,276, which is below the 1993-94 CGT allowance of Pounds 5,800. If you are selling shares bought before April 6 1982, you should use the March 1982 figure.
