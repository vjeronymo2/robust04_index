Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Prague, March 16 (CTK) -- In the new Slovak Government appointed today, Mikulas Dzurinda from the Christian Democratic Movement (KDH) holds the post of Minister of Transport, Communications and Public Works. Dzurinda was born on February 4, 1955. He graduated from the High School of Transport and Telecommunications in Zilina, central Slovakia. He worked in the Zilina-based Transport Research Institute, and later at the Bratislava-based Railways General Directorate. He helped introduce informatics and automated systems into the country's railways. In 1988 he ended his postgraduate studies with a thesis. He often published articles concerning his profession and lectured on the issue. When the Slovak Transport and Communications Ministry was established in 1991, Dzurinda became the economic secretary to the minister. He held the post until June 1992. In the June 1992 elections he was elected a deputy to the Slovak parliament. Since then he has been chairing the economic group within the KDH and has been the KDH's spokesman for economic questions.
