Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Ivan Ivanov] [Text] Moscow January 24 TASS -- Russian Prime Minister Viktor Chernomyrdin denied that he had contradicted with the president over the new government composition and said Boris Fedorov remains the finance minister for the time being. "There are no and will be no contradictions" with Boris Yeltsin. "I have never put my problems on the shoulders of the president and I am not going to do that", Chernomyrdin told reporters on Monday after a 30-minute meeting with the president, adding that the talks focused on the 1994 budget. Boris Fedorov remains the finance minister for the time being and it is the president who is to decide on his fate, according to the prime minister. "Fedorov continues to work in his position. I have not accepted his resignation", Chernomyrdin said adding that it is the president who is to decide on whether to accept Fedorov's resignation. "I do not know whether he will do it", Chernomyrdin said. The prime minister denied press reports that economist Leonid Abalkin was appointed his adviser. Abalkin was responsible for economic reform in the government of former Soviet Prime Minister Nikolay Ryzhkov. On Monday Chernomyrdin left for the city of Orel in central Russia where he is to meet 18 regional leaders to discuss the situation in agriculture.
