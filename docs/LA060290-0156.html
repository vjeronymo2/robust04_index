June 2, 1990, Saturday, Orange County Edition

BRIEFCASE: REAL ESTATE;

NEW JOURNAL OFFERS STATISTICS GALORE ON HOUSING MARKETS

In case your shelf of real estate periodicals isn't overflowing already, a trio of industry heavyweights has launched a new one.

The Meyers Group, a Chino-based real estate research and consulting firm, has teamed with Brea-based economist Alfred Gobar and Boud Real Estate Information Systems, a statistics specialist, to prepare and publish the Southland-oriented Real Estate Economic Journal.

Each of the 10 issues each year will concentrate on a single housing market.

Readers of the journal will get supply-and-demand summaries along with population, employment growth and building permit data; examinations of trends in residential construction and projections of what to expect from the local economy and from future home sales will be offered.

The first two issues of the journal already have hit the streets. The inaugural book dealt with Riverside County, while the second volume was devoted to the Antelope Valley. Next up, the publishers say, is San Diego County.

It won't be Orange County's turn until the ninth issue in November.

The books, priced from $950 to $1,500 an issue (the Orange County volume will run $1,250), are aimed at statistics junkies, builders, lenders, speculators and anyone else who can benefit from what amounts to an off-the-shelf market feasibility report.

Copies of the journal are available at the three Meyers Group offices.
