941214 FT 14 DEC 94 / International Company News: Restructure in Finland Two of Finland's leading industrial groups, Outokumpu and Tampella, yesterday announced moves to restructure the country's mining technology sector and enhance mutual co-operation, writes Christopher Brown-Humes in Stockholm. Outokumpu is selling two of its units to Tampella. In return it will gain 7m Tampella shares worth FM126m (Dollars 26m), giving it a 7 per cent stake in the company. The units being sold are Rammer, which manufactures hydraulic hammers and breakerboom systems, and Roxon, the world's biggest manufacturer of bulk handling conveyor components. Rammer's 1994 sales are estimated at FM230m and Roxon's at FM420m.
