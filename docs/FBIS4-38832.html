BFN [Text] Moscow, 2 June -- The visit of the head of the Republic of Korea is symbolic, "because it is taking place at a time when our countries are in the midst of socio-economic transformations and the break-up of out-dated systems and patterns of social development," State Duma Chairman Ivan Rybkin said after his meeting with ROK President Kim Yong-sam at the President Hotel today. The chairman of the State Duma hoped that multi-faceted ties will develop between the Republic of Korea and Russia and that contacts between their supreme legislative bodies--the Federal Assembly of Russia and the National Assembly of the Republic of Korea--play a proper part in this. In reply Kim Yong-sam said he was delighted to meet the leaders of a parliament that was elected in free elections for the first time in Russia. He expressed gratitude to the State Duma for its adoption of a legislative act rehabilitating repressed Koreans in Russia and touched on the problems of nuclear disarmament on the Korean peninsula and Russia's participation in the Asian-Pacific Economic Cooperation program. The Korean president expressed the hope that his "visit will serve as a stimulus for shifting our bilateral relations onto a qualitatively new and higher level of constructive and mutually complementary partnership."
