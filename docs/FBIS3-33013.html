Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on Ecuadoran economic developments monitored through on 15 February. The Agriculture Ministry announced on Wednesday, 9 February, that in January Ecuador surpassed its export record by selling 239,130 tons of agricultural products in the international market. A ministry spokesman indicated that Ecuador was able to sell 58,403 tons more than in January of last year, despite restrictions imposed by the European Union. (Paris AFP in Spanish 1633 GMT 9 Feb 94) Figures released on 9 February by the Ecuadoran State Petroleum Enterprise (Petroecuador) indicate a barrel of Ecuadoran oil sold for an average of $12.50 during in the early part of February. Last December, the average price per barrel dropped to $9. An attempt by the government to fill the gap in revenues caused by this drop led to a crisis in the country. (Madrid EFE in Spanish 1759 GMT 9 Feb 94) Mexican Petroleum (Pemex) has signed a one-year contract with Petroecuador to purchase 2.555 million gallons of fuel oil. Pemex indicated it will receive this oil in 13 shipments worth $2-3 million each. Petroecuador has already signed a similar agreement with the U.S. companies Clarendon and Vito. (Mexico City NOTIMEX 1712 GMT 14 Feb 94) In January, Ecuador began to withhold 20 percent of its coffee exports, joining producer countries that are seeking to stabilize coffee prices with this measure. During this period, Ecuador withheld over 11,500 sacks of coffee. (Quito Voz de los Andes in Spanish 2300 GMT 11 Feb 94)
