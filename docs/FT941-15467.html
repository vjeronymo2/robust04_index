940114 FT 14 JAN 94 / International Company News: Motorola net income doubled to top Dollars 1bn Motorola, the US electronics and semiconductor group, reported strong sales and earnings growth for the fourth quarter. Sales for the quarter increased almost 35 per cent to Dollars Dollars 4.9bn from Dollars 3.7bn in the same period last year. Net income jumped by 88 per cent to Dollars 340m from Dollars 181m. Earnings per share rose to Dollars 1.15, the high end of Wall Street expectations, from 64 cents. The news boosted Motorola's shares which surged in after-hours trading, from Dollars 95 1/2 at the official close to Dollars 98 1/4 . For the year, Motorola's revenues were Dollars 17bn, up from Dollars 13.3bn in 1992. Net income more than doubled to just over Dollars 1bn or Dollars 3.55 a share from Dollars 453m or Dollars 1.66. Motorola said semiconductor sales rose 28 per cent to Dollars 5.7bn. Operating profit in the semiconductor segment rose to Dollars 801m from Dollars 464m. General systems products sales were up 43 per cent at Dollars 5.2bn, with operating profits of Dollars 718m, up from Dollars 420m in 1992. Motorola's communications products segment had sales of Dollars 4.83bn in 1993, a 24 per cent increase, producing operating profits for that segment of Dollars 354m, up from Dollars 192m. The company's government and systems technology products segment lifted sales 32 per cent to Dollars 858m. But operating losses also rose to Dollars 17m in 1993, compared with an operating loss of Dollars 7m in 1992. Commenting on the outlook for the year ahead, Mr Gary Tooker, chief executive and vice chairman, said the company saw 'solid growth potential in developing markets such as China' and expected the broader Asia-Pacific region to continue to be the dominant area of growth for the company. 'We believe that steady, sustainable growth can continue in North America,' Mr Tooker said. 'Likewise, when economies begin to recover in Europe and Japan, we expect to see our customers investing in tools for greater productivity.'
