Language: Romanian Article Type:BFN [Text] Shortly after 1500 the Senate resumed its plenary session in Omnia Hall. After the agenda was agreed, they discussed a number of organizational issues. Following this, the chairman of the session, Senate Speaker Oliviu Gherman presented a communique released by the Senate Commission for Defense, Public Order, and National Security referring to the accusations of [Greater Romania Party] Senator Corneliu Vadim Tudor made during the plenary session of the Senate and by means of the mass media against the minister of national defense [Lieutenant General Nicolae Spiroiu] and other top officials in that ministry. The communique states that, quote, within the next two weeks the commission will examine the documents and the assertions underlying those accusations, as well as the extent to which the comply with reality. On the basis of the conclusions that will be drawn from those investigations the Commission for Defense, Public Order, and National Security will make suggestions to the Senate with respect to the way in which the case should be solved, unquote. Our correspondent, Victor Spirache, reports that the session then proceeded to hear a number of reports elaborated by the mediation commissions [mediating between the Senate and the Chamber of Deputies], after which the senators continued to discuss the articles of the draft bill on labor protection.
