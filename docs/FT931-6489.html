930301 FT 01 MAR 93 / Observer: Tory home rule One of the rules of thumb for up-and-coming Tory politicians is that reputations are more easily damaged than enhanced by a Home Office posting. Hence, some surprise at the extensive supporting cast for the Home Office's local government conference. Health secretary Virginia Bottomley banged on about the need for a firm stand on persistent juvenile delinquents as did John 'hellfire' Patten, the education secretary. Even Sir Norman Fowler, the Tory party chairman, was keen to get his message across, opining that law and order would be one of the two great national issues in the local elections in May. But despite all this tough talk about cracking down on offenders, the underlying impression was left that for many Tories the real villain of the piece is Tony Blair, the shadow home secretary, whose high-profile stance on crime over the past week or so has been threatening to rob the Tories of one of their most prized political assets - a popular belief that Labour is 'soft' on the issue.
