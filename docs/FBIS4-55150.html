BFN [Text] Budapest, 10 May (MTI) -- If voters allow, the national, Christian parties may make back some of their losses in the second round of voting on May 29, Laszlo Surjan, chairman of the Christian Democratic People's Party (KDNP), said after his party's board took two sessions on Monday and Tuesday to assess the situation. Surjan could imagine voters either becoming discouraged by the second round or realising they still have some political leverage. For this reason, the KDNP gives priority to mobilising voters and making them aware of their responsibility, he said. KDNP Deputy Chairman Laszlo Varga told MTI that the Christian Democrats have not yet coordinated second-round cooperation with any party. On Tuesday, they held an informal consultation with Hungarian Democratic Forum leaders. Varga added that the KDNP Executive Committee would decide tomorrow under which terms the party would be prepared to make an electoral deal for the second round.
