BFN [Text] Chisinau BASAPRESS, 16/6/1994 -- Petru Godiac, condemned by a Dniester court to two years of imprisonment following the Ilascu group's trial, was set free on 12 June, declared to BASAPRESS on behalf of Ivan Fuchiji, minister of interior of Tiraspol [Dniester Republic], Oleg Palamarchuk, chief of the Dniester Ministry of Interior's Secretariat. Palamarchuk refused to give any information on the conditions of detention of the remaining detainees. Valentin Dolganiuc, leader of the parliamentary block of the Alliance of Christian-Democratic Popular Front of Moldova [AFPCDM], confirmed in an interview to BASAPRESS that Godiac was set free and presently is with his parents in Stefan-Voda. Petru Godiac, born on 1 September 1967, in the village of Talmaz, Suvorov district, completed a secondary education, is married, and most recently worked as a driver at the Tiraspol spinning mill for the department of transportation. He was arrested on 12 June 1992 and condemned at the Ilascu group's trial, according with Article 227, the second point of the Criminal Code of the Moldovan Soviet Socialist Republic.
