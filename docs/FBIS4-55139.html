BFN [Text] Sofia, May 10 (BTA) -- The main parliamentary forces today met to decide their positions on the planned cabinet changes. Government spokesman Raycho Raykov does not believe that the changes will be discussed this week. The prime minister has yet to confer with the main parliamentary forces supporting his government. There will be no drastic changes in the cabinet's policy, said Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of Labour and Social Affairs Evgeni Matinchev, speaking at a working lunch today with the ambassadors of the 12 European Union [EU] member countries and the three applicants for EU membership: Austria, Finland and Sweden. The ex-Communist Bulgarian Socialist Party (BSP) reiterated the stance on the government it took in early April. "The BSP wants, as much as possible, to see the creation of conditions for the country's stabilization, for overcoming the unfavourable tendencies in the economy, the social sphere and international monetary and financial relations," BSP deputy floor leader Krasimir Premyanov told a news conference. "There are still no reasons to revise our policy line," said Vladimir Topencharov, member of the party leadership. According to Mr. Premyanov, the BSP has not yet received adequate guarantees that keeping Valentin Aleksandrov as minister of defence in the cabinet would ease tensions in the Bulgarian Army. Speaking to reporters today, Mr. Osman Oktay MP of the ethnic Turks' Movement for Rights and Freedoms (DPS) described as a "satisfactory option" the prime minister's supervisory function with respect to the "power" ministries. In a telephone interview on Bulgarian national radio on Sunday [8 May], Prime Minister Berov indicated that there will be no changes in the Ministry of Defence. According to officially unconfirmed reports, incumbent Finance Minister Stoyan Aleksandrov may be nominated for a deputy prime ministership. There are plans for three deputy prime ministers. One will be in charge of the financial and economic team of the government and of foreign debt matters, another deputy prime minister without portfolio will oversee the reform processes and especially the progress of land reform (Prime Minister Berov already nominated Associated Professor Rumen Gechev for this job), and a third one will deal with the social sector and no changes are expected on this count, government spokesman Raycho Raykov told reporters today, obviously referring to incumbent Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of Labour and Social Affairs Evgeni Matinchev. The government may well resign en bloc if parliament does not pass the moved amendments to the Privatization Act and a Bankruptcy Act, Mr. Matinchev told foreign diplomats today, according to the government spokesman. This is not a government's whim or wish to dictate terms but conditions set by the international financial institutions, Mr. Raykov emphasized. Approval of the cabinet changes will require an affirmative vote of more than half of the MP's present and voting. Interviewed on national radio this morning, the leader of the Alternative Social Liberal Party (affiliated to the New Union for Democracy, a parliamentary faction formed by expellees and defectors of the Union of Democratic Forces -- SDS) Nikolay Vasilev said that the union will not take part in the vote on the cabinet changes. The Rally for Democracy (another faction of SDS expellees and defectors) will most probably refuse to support the structural and personnel cabinet changes proposed by Prof. Berov, according to Rally deputy leader Ruslan Semerdzhiev. DPS spokesman Yunal Lyutfi said yesterday and today that when the changes are put to the vote, the government will get less support than in previous such votes. Personally, he believes that the government deserves one more chance. The DPS top leadership held a meeting today. Mr. Oktay told reporters that according to information which the movement has, the Chairman of the Committee of Tourism Prof. Emil Lozanov already has resigned. He also said that according to the DPS there are unclear questions concerning the future structure of the Committees of Tourism and Forestry. The government spokesman said that the stand on the Tourism and Forestry Committees has yet to be finalized. The structural changes matter more than the personnel ones, as far as the DPS is concerned, Mr. Oktay emphasized. "The situation surrounding the government is getting more disturbing by the day," said SDS leader Filip Dimitrov after a meeting of the Union's top leadership. In his words, there is already public awareness that the government is unfit to perform its functions and bear responsibility. "The obvious impossibility to achieve understanding on the cabinet changes, which delays their introduction before parliament, makes the picture in the country only gloomier," Mr. Dimitrov went on to say. He did not specify the actions which the Union is planning to take in the coming days when Prime Minister Berov is expected to table the cabinet changes. According to Mr. Dimitrov, on Wednesday [11 May] the majority will be forced to have a say on the union's draft resolution to censure the Government's economic and financial policy. The SDS will hold a national conference on Saturday and Sunday [14-15 May]. At it, the union may decide to boycott parliament.
