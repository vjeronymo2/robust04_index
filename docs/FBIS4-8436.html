BFN [Text] Sofia, April 13 (BTA) -- No official report was released by 8 PM local time today on Prime Minister Lyuben Berov's health, after the government said this morning that he was taken back to hospital on suspicion of having contracted hepatitis. On March 8 the prime minister had a heart attack and on March 11 he underwent a quadruple bypass operation. Berov's doctors have not confirmed the hepatitis diagnosis, national radio quoted Gen. Vladimirov, boss of the National Security Service, as saying upon his return from the hospital. According to him, a medical statement will made by 1 PM tomorrow following more tests. The premier's return to hospital set politicians discussing the fate of his cabinet. At a scheduled press conference today, the leader of the Civil Alliance for the Republic Aleksandur Tomov said the Prime Minister's probable infection spoke volumes not only for the level of Bulgarian health care but for overall attitudes toward that man. According to Tomov, all people involved in the prime minister's treatment, including the health minister, should offer to resign. According to Acting Prime Minister Evgeni Matinchev, Prof. Berov will be under quarantine for about a week. Berov's health did not prevent him from having two lengthy meetings with Matinchev, Bulgarian National Television said in its evening news broadcast. Berov's return proved more than what he was equal to, Matinchev told national television. He said he today advised the Prime Minister to reduce his contacts as much as possible, relinquishing his duties to his two deputies. "I am sure Prof. Berov has a will to live and, most importantly, to rule this country", the ethnic Turks' leader Ahmed Dogan told the press following a meeting with President Zhelev. According to the Presidential Press Office, the president and Dogan discussed the country's finances and economy. At this stage, a cabinet overhaul may be appropriate but it should be proposed by the prime minister and coordinated with all parliamentary factions, said Dogan. Parliament and politicians may be unprepared for any other option, said Dogan. According to him, the current developments may grow into a political crisis. The health of the state depends on the health of a single state leader; Bulgaria cannot do without a prime minister under the present Constitution, Stefan Savov, floor leader of the Union of Democratic Forces (SDS), today told national television. The coalition led by the Bulgarian Socialist Party (BSP) today discussed possible action in case the cabinet cannot stay in control in Berov's absence, BSP floor leader Nora Ananieva told the same broadcast. Berov's sickness necessitates immediate consultations, said Gospodin Atanasov MP of the New Union for Democracy (NSD). A resignation tendered by the premier would be in keeping with the Constitution; otherwise, the way out is a vote of no confidence, said another NSD MP, Ruslan Semerdzhiev, adding he would vote no confidence in such circumstances.
