920401 FT 01 APR 92 / Accountants protest at proposed standard THE PROPOSED accounting standard on the structure of financial statements is not detailed enough, the Institute of Chartered Accountants in England and Wales warned yesterday. The definition of 'extraordinary items' - the one-off profits or losses from activities outside a company's normal business - should be tightened or the concept banned, it argued in a submission to the Accounting Standards Board. Its comments on the first financial-reporting exposure draft (FRED 1) produced by the board also included a proposal that exceptional items should be separately listed, not brought together under a single heading. Standard-setters should also focus their approach more tightly and draw a distinc-tion between listed and unlisted companies, the institute said. Mr Paul Rutteman, chairman of the institute's financial reporting committee, said: 'While we agree that standards should contain principles rather than be a detailed rulebook, a standard should contain much more guidance than is in FRED 1.'
