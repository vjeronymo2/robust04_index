June 18, 1990, Monday, Home Edition

EDUCATION SECRETARY CAVAZOS' CRITICISM OF LATINO PARENTS

The criticism of Latino parents by Cavazos was inaccurate, simplistic, crude and irresponsible.

Students, teachers and parents are equally accountable for attaining educational goals.

To single out the parental role as a primary cause of the 40% dropout rate among Latino students unfairly cast a perception of negligence upon the people most deserving of respect.

The value of learning is a central theme to us. Assimilation requires a daily assessment of this value. The Hispanic-American family is not anti-education. We are striving to assert our presence within an educational system that is culturally biased toward Anglo-American values; individualism, rationalism, empiricism.

Adopting English as a second language causes students to make decisions regarding attachment and loyalty. English speakers are the public role models in the classroom. Spanish-speaking parents are a private resource outside the cultural mainstream. Latino students are asked to bridge the language and cultural gaps between home and school. The high number of dropouts indicates the schools are losing this battle.

Cavazos is alerting Latinos to a future of limited opportunity due to low educational advancement. To avert this plight parents are calling for courageous leadership and not petty backbiting from the nation's top education official.

EDDY POLANCO

Los Angeles
