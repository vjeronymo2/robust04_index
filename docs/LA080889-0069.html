August 8, 1989, Tuesday, Orange County Edition

THE NATION

About 70 members of a Mohawk Indian band opposed to gambling on their reservation staged a quiet demonstration outside a polling station as residents began two days of voting on whether to allow casinos on their land. The protesters picketed the tribal council building, demanding a halt to the referendum, which they said was illegal and in violation of tribal law. At a meeting Saturday of the tribal council, members voted to postpone the vote indefinitely until a mechanism could be found for traditional Mohawks and those on the Canadian side of the reservation to express their views on the gambling issue.
