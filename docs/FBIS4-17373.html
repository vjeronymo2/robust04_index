BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Semyon Ivanov] [Text] Moscow April 23 TASS -- The Cosmos-2278 satellite was launched from the Baykonur Cosmodrome in Kazakhstan at 11.02, Moscow time, on Saturday. The launching was carried out by the Space Armed Forces in the interests of the Russian Defence Ministry. The satellite was put into orbit by a Zenit-2 rocket-booster. This was the first launching of a rocket of this type and the 22nd since the first firing-off in April, 1985, a spokesman for the Space Armed Forces told ITAR-TASS. The Cosmos-2278 satellite is the 1075th spacecraft launched from the Baykonur Cosmodrome. The first one was put into orbit on October 4, 1957.
