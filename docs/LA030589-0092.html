March 5, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

FLIPPING OUT

Regularly each week, I read the entire Book Review section to an elderly friend who is blind. She can be a little testy. Three-fourths of the way through Richard Eder's "Richard Poor" Endpapers, she interrupted with, "Yes, yes. Let's stop before he says that reverses are proverbial."

I obeyed willingly. It is a poor piece, clumsily arch.

FRED SCIFERS

INGLEWOOD
