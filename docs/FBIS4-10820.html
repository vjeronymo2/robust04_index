BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities in Brazil monitored through 14 April: The Brazilian car manufacturing sector produced 140,000 units in March. This is the highest monthly production figure in 40 years. Executives of the sector expect production in 1994 will surpass that of 1993, which amounted to 1,391,000 units. (Rio de Janeiro JORNAL DO BRASIL in Portuguese 6 Apr 94 Business and Finance Section p 6) Brazilian Petroleum Corporation, Petrobras, has signed a contract with Central Puerto -- a private Argentine company which supplies 40 percent of the electrical energy consumed in Buenos Aires -- for the supply of 2.7 million barrels of fuel oil. The deal is worth $45 million. The fuel oil will be delivered between May and September. Alberto Guimaraes, Petrobras assistant superintendent for foreign trade, has reported that the profile of Brazilian crude oil imports has changed and that Argentina has become our second oil supplier. He added that between January and March, Brazil imported crude oil from the following countries: Saudi Arabia (150,000 barrels per day); Argentina (110,000 barrels per day); Kuwait (80,000 barrels per day); and Algiers (70,000 barrels per day). In 1993, Petrobras imported an average of 500,000 barrels per day of oil and 280,000 barrels per day of oil byproducts. (Rio de Janeiro JORNAL DO BRASIL in Portuguese 9 Apr 94 p 6)
