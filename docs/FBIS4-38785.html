BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondents Viktor Demidenko and Mikhail Melnik] [Text] Kiev June 1 TASS -- "The wrong regional policy of the former Soviet Union and the uncertainty of the fate of the Black Sea Fleet are the top two objective causes of the Crimean problem," Ukrainian parliament chairman Oleksandr Moroz said. Speaking at a session of the parliament on Wednesday, Moroz said that among the subjective causes are "intolerance in solving ethnic problems and the clash of several nationalist tendencies." "It is necessary now to neutralise these factors as soon as possible by restoring economic ties and providing conditions for normal rest and recreation in the Crimea," Moroz said. He stressed that Ukraine must use all possible diplomatic channels to ease tension and put the problem within the constitutional framework, without using force.
