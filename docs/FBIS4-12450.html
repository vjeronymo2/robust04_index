BFN [Text] Military Court investigators in Lebanon have interrogated Lebanese Army Sergeant Sharfan Bulus and two former members of the disbanded Lebanese Forces accused of plotting to kill President Ilyas al-Hirawi. Lebanese judiciary sources say that the Military Court will try Ghassan Tuma, head of the Lebanese Forces' security apparatus, in absentia. Tuma asked Sergeant Bulus, a bomb expert working at the Presidential Palace, about the possibility of planting a bomb, the target of which would be the president, in a flower vase, a car, or a telephone set, according to the same sources.
