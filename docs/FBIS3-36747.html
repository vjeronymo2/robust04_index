Language: English Article Type:BFN [by ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondents Boris Krivoshey and Dmitriy Chubarov] [Text] Moscow February 8 TASS - Russia can operate a radar station in Skrunda, Latvia, for another four years, under a package of documents prepared for signing by a recent round of the Russian-Latvian state negotiations on Russian troops withdrawal from Latvia, Russian delegation head Sergey Zotov told a briefing here today. The station will enjoy additional 18 months for dismantling. The sides discussed the main agreement on troops withdrawal and agreements on social protection of servicemen and military pensioners, as well as the legal status and conditions of the radar station functioning. "We have only technical issues left. Their immediate solution will give way to the finalization of the whole package of agreements to be submitted to the Russian and Lithuanian [as received] governments for signing," said Zotov. Zotov said the Russian Government may receive the agreements by the end of this month.
