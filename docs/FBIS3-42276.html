Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Cairo, Feb 4 (MENA)--Ahmad Yahya, owner of a car agency in Shibin al-Qanatir, lower Egypt, was killed tonight along with two others when unidentified persons opened fire at his workplace. Another person was wounded in the incident. Senior Interior Ministry officials rushed to the scene and started investigations. [Paris AFP in English at 0147 on 5 February, in a related Cairo-datelined item, adds the following: ["Gunmen late Friday shot dead four people here, including a prosecution witness in a trial of Moslem fundamentalists, police reported. ["They said unidentified assailants opened fire on Sayyid Ahmad Yahya, an automobile dealer, in his showroom. ["Three other people at the scene -- a security guard, an employee and a client -- were killed and a policeman was hurt, police said. ["Yahya was a government witness in the trial of Moslem militants accused of having tried to assassinate Egyptian Prime Minister 'Atif Sidqi. ["Those charged in the attack on Sidqi are alleged to have bought a car from Yahya that was then booby-trapped as part of the November 25 attempt on the prime minister's life. ["A second hearing in the trial of nine fundamentalists accused of plotting to kill Sidqi was scheduled to open here on Saturday. They are members of the Tala'i' al-Fath (The Leaders of the Conquest), considered by authorities to be the military wing of Al-Jihad fundamentalist movement, which claimed responsibility for the operation against the prime minister."]
