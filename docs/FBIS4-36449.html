BFN [Text] Cairo, 6 Jun (INA) -- Iraq has protested to the Arab League General Secretariat against the continued facilities provided by the rulers of Saudi Arabia and Kuwait to U.S., British, and French aircraft violating Iraq's airspace, noting that this poses a serious threat to Iraq's security and sovereignty. In a letter addressed to Arab League Secretary General Dr. 'Ismat 'Abd-al-Majid, Iraq's permanent representative said these aircraft continued their violation of Iraqi airspace during the period 24-31 May. They carried out 352 sorties flying from their bases in Saudi Arabia and Kuwait. Iraq's representative asked that the letter be circulated, along with a list detailing the violations, to the Arab member states to acquaint them with these regrettable facts.
