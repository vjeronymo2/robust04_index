BFN [Text] Hanoi VNA May 6 -- A grand meeting was held here today to mark the 40th anniversary of the Dien Bien Phu battle -- most resounding victory of the Vietnam Army over the French expeditionary force (May 7, 1954). Prominent among those present at the event were party General Secretary Do Muoi, President Le Duc Anh, Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet National Assembly Chairman Nong Duc Manh, and General Vo Nguyen Giap, former commander-in-chief of the VPA and commander of the Dien Bien Phu battle. In the key-note speech at the meeting, Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet said: 'The Dien Bien Phu victory entered into the history of the Vietnamese people as a bach dang, a chi lang, and a dong da battle in the 20th century, a golden marker in the thousand-year history of building and defending the nation, a real and legendary exploit in the Ho Chi Minh time. That is an ever lasting pride of the Vietnamese people, a source of encouragement to all nations in struggle for national independence, freedom and the human values'. The prime minister pointed out that the Dien Bien Phu victory was made from the braveness and growth of the whole nation, the correctness of the revolutionary line, the vitality new regime, new society and new state in face of war challenges, the strength of the Peoples Armed Forces and the international support which the Vietnamese people remember and for which they were grateful to forever. The victory, he said, is heritage of the national tradition, the development of the august revolution, and the crown of the first war of resistance. The prime minister said that the Vietnamese people were launching a new struggle for prosperity, happiness and social justice. Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet said he strongly believed that the Vietnamese people would push up the current process of renovation and development to steady success. After reaffirming Vietnam's persistent foreign policy of being friends with all countries in the world, Prime Minister Kiet noted: `After Dien Bien Phu especially in recent years, the relations between Vietnam and France has clearly entered into a new period. The multi-faceted cooperation between the two countries has proved that when the two parties have mutual understanding and goodwill, they could totally close their past pages to develop their normal fine relations. The prime minister called on the entire nation to concentrate on building the country as well as the tasks of defending national independence, sovereignty, territorial integrity and national security. `We should exert our efforts in strengthening the people's army, the people's security force and other people's armed forces to ensure that they would always play their key role together with the entire people, to firmly defend the airspace, the sea, and the territory, including the islands of our motherland', the prime minister stressed.
