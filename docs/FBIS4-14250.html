BFN [Text] Riyadh, 27 April (KUNA) -- U.S. Secretary of State Warren Christopher will this afternoon hold a meeting here with his counterparts from the Gulf Cooperation Council [GCC] states to discuss the latest developments in the Middle East peace process and the situation in the Gulf. The U.S. secretary of state arrived here early this morning preceded by the GCC foreign ministers who had arrived here late last night. Christopher is due to meet with King Fahd Bin-'Abd-al-'Aziz, custodian of the two holy mosques, today before leaving for Cairo to complete his Middle East tour. The GCC foreign ministers held a meeting here last night in preparation for the talks which they will hold with Christopher today. Sources from the meeting told KUNA that at the short meeting held at the airport the agenda for the meeting [with Christopher] prepared by the GCC general secretariat was reviewed. Official political sources said that during their short meeting the ministers discussed the agenda of topics to be discussed with the U.S. secretary of state during the meeting to be held at the GCC general secretariat at noon today which will be followed by a joint news conference by Christopher and his Saudi counterpart Prince Sa'ud al-Faysal. Saudi Foreign Minister Prince Sa'ud al-Faysal, who received the GCC foreign ministers and U.S. Secretary of State Warren Christopher, said that the meeting will focus on three issues [as received]: namely developments in the Middle East peace process, and the need for the co-sponsors of the peace conference -- particularly the United States -- to exert greater efforts to overcome the obstacles facing the process. Prince Sa'ud al-Faysal added that the meeting will also discuss the situation and developments in the Gulf region and the issue of Bosnia-Herzegovina. Sources expected that the meeting, which is taking place within Christopher's tour of the region, will focus on giving impetus to the peace process in the Middle East, the situation in the Gulf, and the Arab boycott of Israel.
