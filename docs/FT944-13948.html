941025 FT 25 OCT 94 / World Stock Markets: Equity markets The weakness of the dollar exerted its influence over the world's equity markets last week, with heavy falls seen among senior bourses. The two high spots were Australia and South Africa which, measured by the FT-Actuaries World index series, managed respective rises of nearly 2 per cent and 3 per cent in local currency terms. South Africa has been attracting investment interest following the election of the country's first ever multi-racial democratic government earlier this year. Lehman Brothers notes that the future direction of the country's financial markets jwill depend on whether the government can carry out necessary economic and social reforms. However, the broker concludes, 'barring any adverse political or external factors, the downside for the market remains limited, as valuations are supported by the stronger earnings momentum coming
