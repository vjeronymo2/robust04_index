Language: French Article Type:BFN [Text] Dakar, 25 Jan (AFP)--Senegal and the IMF have signed a "letter of intent" by which Senegal will receive a 27-billion-CFA franc financial package, official sources disclosed in Dakar this evening. The agreement also provides for a 40-billion-CFA franc annual grant to Senegal over a period of three years as part of the backup measures following the devaluation of the CFA franc. A similar letter of intent is expected to be signed between Senegal and the World Bank this week, according to World Bank sources. Jean-Louis Sarbib, head of the Sahel division of the World Bank, told the media that an "agreement has been reached" and that "the Senegalese authorities are expected to announce this and to give details of the decisions made."
