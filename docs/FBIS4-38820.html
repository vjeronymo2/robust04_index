BFN [By ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondent Aleksandr Kopnov] [Text] Moscow June 2 TASS -- In two years' time, Russia, by all appearances, will not prolong the treaty of friendship, cooperation and mutual help signed by the former USSR and People's Democratic Republic of Korea in 1961 for another five years, a high-ranking official quoted Russian President Boris Yeltsin as saying at a meeting with South Korean President Kim Yong-sam. In accordance with generally recognized norms, the treaty may be denounced at one year's notice, the source quoted Yeltsin as saying. The treaty does exist so far. However, Russia interprets its first article, which provides for offering North Korea military help automatically, if a war breaks out in the Korean peninsula, in a somewhat different way. This provision is actually ineffective, Yeltsin said.
