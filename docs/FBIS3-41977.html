Language: German Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] Bonn/Zagreb (DPA) -- Croatian President Franjo Tudjman today accused the Muslims of using chlorine gas shells in the fighting in central Bosnia. Following the departure of Bosnian President Alija Izetbegovic, with whom Tudjman had held talks at a two-day conference in Bonn, Tudjman also accused the Muslims of continued offensives. However, he described the Bosnia-Croatian conference at the Petersberg government guest house as useful. Even after Tudjman's remarks, it remained unclear whether there would be a cease-fire in central Bosnia. Izetbegovic said yesterday evening on ZDF [Second German Television Channel] that "no major results" had been achieved. Both presidents are now preparing for a new round of talks on 18 January in Geneva, this time involving all warring parties from the conference on former Yugoslavia. [passage omitted]
