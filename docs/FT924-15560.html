921003 FT 03 OCT 92 / Finance & The Family: The Week Ahead MORE bad news from the recession in construction, property and housing sectors is expected on Thursday with three victims reporting results. Higgs and Hill took exceptional charges of Pounds 20.9m and reported a pre-tax loss in the year to December 31, and is expected to post minimal interim pre-tax profits of Pounds 500,000-Pounds 800,000. The interim dividend will likely be slashed to 1.5p (6p). Ibstock Johnsen, the brick and building materials manufacturer, has also been having a tough time. Interim pre-tax profits of Pounds 3m-Pounds 4m (Pounds 7.2m) are forecast. Its interim dividend also looks under pressure and could be cut to 1p or even a halfpenny (2.25p). However hopes are brighter for Tilbury Douglas. Interim profits of Pounds 6.5m (Pounds 6.6m) are expected and the 10 1/2 p dividend looks safe. The company is expected to maintain its reputation as one of the few in the construction sector to be coming through the recession relatively unscathed. Grampian Holdings, the Scottish mini-conglomerate which set the ball rolling in the bidding for Macarthy last year, is expected to show on Wednesday an interim profit on its continuing activities of Pounds 2.5m, down from Pounds 3.4m. The picture is complicated by the sale of the Mitre business, maker of footballs and other sporting goods. The largest part of Grampian is the animal pharmaceuticals division. The performance of the tourist retailing side is traditionally biased towards the second half.
