Language: Latvian Article Type:BFN [Text] Polish President Lech Walesa arrived in Latvia today for a state visit. The Polish president is accompanied by an official state delegation of advisers and experts. Walesa was met at the airport by Latvian President Guntis Ulmanis, Prime Minister Valdis Birkavs, Foreign Minister Georgs Andrejevs, Dainis Turlais, the commander of the Defense Forces, and foreign ambassadors accredited in Latvia. The presidents of the two countries attended a parade by the Latvian defense forces. An hour ago at Riga Castle, Walesa and Ulmanis signed a joint political declaration on strengthening friendship and cooperation between Latvia and Poland, a declaration on the resumption of work of the agreements concluded between Latvia and Poland between 1922 and 1938--which provides for reconsideration and assessment of those documents--as well as an agreement on legal assistance between Latvia and Poland in civil, family, labor, and criminal cases. These documents were signed by Latvia's Foreign Minister Andrejevs and Iwo Byczewski, Polish deputy minister of foreign affairs.
