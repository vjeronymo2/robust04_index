BFN [Excerpts] Ninawa -- Trade Minister Muhammad Mahdi Salih and Agriculture Minister Bashir 'Alwan Hammadi have discussed with Ninawa Governor Staff Lt Gen 'Abd-al-Wahid Shannan Al Ribat the final preparations that have been made to begin the campaign for harvesting and marketing wheat and barley crops in the governorate. This was during an expanded meeting of the governorate's Central Harvest and Marketing Committee which was attended by comrade Najm 'Abdallah al-'Iraqi, secretary of the Ba'th Party Ninawa Branch; 'Abd-al-Hamid al-Sayigh, secretary of the Mosul branch; several directors from the two ministries, heads of the departments concerned with the campaign; and representatives of the Federation of Peasant Cooperative Societies in the governorate. The ministers praised the efforts and preparations that have been made by the Central Committee and the subcommittees in the Ninawa and Mosul party branches and other participant sides for the success of the harvest and marketing campaign in fulfillment of leader President Saddam Husayn's desire to ensure the campaign's requirements throughout our country, especially in Ninawa Governorate, in order to guarantee food for the people and to defeat the unfair blockade on our country. [passage omitted] It was decided during the meeting to increase the present storage capacity so as to absorb the entire quantity of grain to be bought. Some 300 trucks have been ensured for transporting grain. This is in addition to transport by rail. The agriculture minister affirmed that Ninawa will be the nail in the coffin of the food embargo imposed on our people. He said: This governorate, which leader President Saddam Husayn called "the bread basket of great Iraq," will play a major role in guaranteeing people's food. The ministers of trade and agriculture, accompanied by the governor, then made a tour of several harvest and marketing areas to inspect the preparations which have been made. The areas included: al-Hadr, al-Qayyarah, Shawrah, Mahallabiyah, Tall 'Afar, Sinjar, al-Bi'aj, Tall Kayf, and Ba'shiqah.
