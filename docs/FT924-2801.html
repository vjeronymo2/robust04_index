921210 FT 10 DEC 92 / Parliament and Politics: Hain opposes tax relief for Names A LABOUR MP last night tabled a parliamentary early day motion opposing any move to give Lloyd's Names additional tax relief against losses comparable to that granted to companies. The motion, tabled by Mr Peter Hain, MP for Neath, opposes such a change because Names are 'not equivalent to companies' since they enjoy 'the status and the advantages of 'sole traders.'' It follows remarks made this week by Mr David Coleridge, chairman of Lloyd's.
