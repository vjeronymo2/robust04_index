January 5, 1989, Thursday, Home Edition

COOK IT LIGHT: SALAD STARTS NEW YEAR OFF LIGHTLY

This is a beautiful as well as delicious salad. And, even better, all of the component parts have other uses.

ANTIPASTO SALAD

1 large (1 1/2-pound) head red or Boston lettuce

1/4 cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese

3/4 cup cooked garbanzo beans

3/4 cup Caponata Antipasto

1/2 cup (2 ounces) part-skim mozzarella cheese, diced

1/2 cup Italian Light Dressing

Wash and dry lettuce, then tear into bite-size pieces in large salad bowl. Toss lettuce thoroughly with Parmesan cheese.

Toss garbanzo beans, Caponata Antipasto and mozzarella cheese into salad, mixing thoroughly. Add Italian Light Dressing and mix well. Makes 8 cups, or 6 (1 1/3-cup) servings.

Each serving contains approximately:

Calories: 118

Cholesterol: 9 mg

Fat: 6 g

Sodium: 286 mg

Caponata Antipasto

1 tablespoon extra virgin olive oil

1 cup sliced celery

1/2 cup minced onion

1 small eggplant, unpeeled and cut in 1-inch cubes

1 cup tomato sauce

1 teaspoon anchovy paste

1 1/2 teaspoons sodium-reduced soy sauce

2 tablespoons capers

3 tablespoons red wine vinegar

1 cup water

1/2 teaspoon dried basil, crushed

1 tablespoon sugar

Place olive oil in large, heavy skillet or pot. Heat oil and add sliced celery. Cook, stirring frequently, 10 minutes.

Add minced onion and cook another 10 minutes. Remove celery and onion from pan and set aside.

Cook eggplant in same pan, over fairly high heat, stirring frequently, 10 minutes. Return celery and onion to pan with eggplant.

Add tomato sauce, anchovy paste, soy sauce, capers, vinegar, water, basil and sugar to pan. Simmer, uncovered, 30 minutes. Allow to cool to room temperature. Store, covered, in refrigerator. Serve cold as appetizer before any Italian dish. Makes 3 cups.

Each 1/4 cup contains approximately:

Calories: 35

Cholesterol: 1 mg

Fat: 1 g

Sodium: 158 mg

Italian Light Dressing

1/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper

1/2 teaspoon dry mustard

1/4 teaspoon dried oregano, crushed

1/2 teaspoon dried tarragon, crushed

1/2 teaspoon dried basil, crushed

1 teaspoon sugar

2 teaspoons sodium-reduced soy sauce

2 tablespoons red wine vinegar

2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil

1/2 cup water

Combine pepper, mustard, oregano, tarragon, basil, sugar, soy sauce and vinegar. Mix well. Add oil and water and again mix well. Makes 1 cup.

Each 2 tablespoons contain approximately:

Calories: 35

Cholesterol: 0 mg

Fat: 4 g

Sodium: 33 mg
