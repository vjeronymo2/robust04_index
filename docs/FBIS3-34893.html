Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [From the "With the Midday Events" feature] [Text] French Foreign Minister Alain Juppe has met with an East Jerusalem delegation led by Faysal al-Husayni. Talks dealt with the French aid to Palestinians during the forthcoming self-rule period. Our correspondent Ahmad 'Ubaydat reports on this meeting: [Begin recording] ['Ubaydat] The French Government is committed to supporting the peace process and helping the Palestinians in all domains in order to successfully establish their self-rule. This was stated by French Foreign Minister Alain Juppe during his meeting with some Palestinian figures at the Palestinian delegation's headquarters today. Juppe said: Today's meeting was cordial and positive. As you know, the French Government has for long been calling for and supporting the idea of direct dialogue between Israel and the PLO. This has actually been achieved following the signing of the principles agreement and the mutual recognition. The minister also discussed France's efforts in this regard, particularly its contribution to economic development in the territories. Faysal al-Husayni voiced optimism over this meeting. He said: [Al-Husayni] In fact, the meeting was very positive. They talked about their clear political position on the peace process as a whole and on the issue of Jerusalem. They also expressed their readiness to develop the aid extended to us, whether it is financial aid or training assistance. ['Ubaydat] The French foreign minister left for the Gaza Strip to acquaint himself with the situation there. He will then return to Jerusalem. [end recording]
