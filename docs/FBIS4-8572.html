BFN [Text] Budapest, 12 April (MTI) -- The Hungarian Foreign Ministry considers the immediate cessation of attacks against the civilian population of Gorazde, the prevention of conflict escalation, and the furtherance of the Bosnian peace process with the participation of all sides to be a prime necessity, Hungarian foreign affairs spokesman Janos Herman said at his customary press briefing in Budapest on Tuesday [12 April]. Herman was evaluating the situation that has come about in the wake of the Serbian attack on Gorazde. The Foreign Ministry is following the situation with particular attention. Its situational analysis starts out from the fact that the NATO action was carried out based on UN Security Council resolutions, and that the implementation of these is an obligation of the member-states. In answer to another question, Herman welcomed the Polish government's submission of its official European Union [EU] membership request to the current presidency of the European Union on April 8, parallel to the Hungarian move. Herman noted with satisfaction that these historical steps were prepared in close concert between the Hungarian and the Polish governments.
