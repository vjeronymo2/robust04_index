BFN [Text] Hanoi VNA June 22 -- Deputy Prime Minister [P.M.] Tran Duc Luong received here this afternoon a delegation of the Japanese Government led by Director of the Foreign Ministry Shigekazu Sato now on a working visit to Vietnam. Speaking to his guests, Deputy P.M. Tran Duc Luong said that the multi-faceted cooperation between Vietnam and Japan has recently seen fine steps of development. He stressed that the two countries would further strengthen their bilateral relations and make positive contributions to the cause of development in the Asian-Pacific region for peace, friendship and cooperation. Deputy Prime Minister Luong highly appreciated the Japanese Government's valuable assistance to Vietnam, especially the Official Development Assistance (ODA). He also expressed his wish for the further consolidation and development of the cooperation of mutual benefits between Vietnam and Japan. For his part, Mr. S. Sato noted that the aim of the Japanese delegation's visit is to exchange views with concerned officials of the Vietnamese Government to promote the economic cooperation between the two countries in the form of ODA loan. He highly valued the important achievements recorded by the Vietnamese people in their cause of socio-economic development.
