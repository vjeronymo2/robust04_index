NON-RESIDENTIAL BUILDING VALUATIONS

Along with home-building, non-residential construction is taking a beating in Orange County. The value of commercial (i.e., non-residential) building permits during the first nine months of 1990 is down 19% compared to last year.

Orange County has been hit harder than its neighbors, among whom San Diego is faring worst, with a 10% decline (and two, Riverside and San Bernardino counties, have increases over last year's comparable period). Statewide, valuations are down just 3%.

The only real bright spot in Orange County non-residential building is with stores, where there has been a 24% increase in permit values. All other categories have dropped, in a couple of cases by half.
