931208 FT 08 DEC 93 / UK Company News: City's busiest month breaks records A series of trading records were broken in the City last month amid exceptional conditions in equity and gilts markets. The Pounds 110.5bn turnover of UK and overseas stocks made it the busiest month ever for equity trading in London. Equity trading broke the previous record of Pounds 109.3bn set in August. A total of Pounds 52.6bn was traded in UK equities, the busiest month since July 1987, and it was the second busiest month for overseas equities, with turnover of Pounds 57.9bn. Figures from the Stock Exchange showed that November was also a record month for new issues, with 42 companies joining the market, raising a total of Pounds 1.8bn. This brings the year's total to 158 companies, raising Pounds 5.1bn. There were no new gilt issues during November, but turnover remained strong. The month's turnover was Pounds 143.6bn, bringing the year's total to a record Pounds 1,438bn. Twelve of the new companies were Lloyds investment trusts, which raised Pounds 758.9m, with an average market capitalisation of Pounds 82.9m. A total of 17 companies were capitalised at Pounds 50m or less, raising Pounds 368.5m between them. Seven companies were capitalised at over Pounds 100m. The largest was Elan Corporation, capitalised at Pounds 892.3m, which came via the Unlisted Securities Market.
