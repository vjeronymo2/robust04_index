920916 FT 16 SEP 92 / UK Company News: Acquisitions help Starmin to Pounds 1.35m Starmin, the Surrey-based quarrying and engineering group run by Raschid and Osman Abdullah and where Mr Cecil Parkinson, the former cabinet minister, is deputy chairman, yesterday reported sharply higher interim profits. On turnover ahead 26 per cent to Pounds 9.9m, pre-tax profits for the six months to June 30 jumped from Pounds 785,000 to Pounds 1.35m. The outcome included full contributions from businesses acquired last year. Earnings per share were unchanged at 0.3p on a greatly increased capital. The interim dividend is held at 0.1p.
