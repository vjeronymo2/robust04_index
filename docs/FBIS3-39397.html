Article Type:CSO [Editorial Report] In the final phase of the accession negotiations, EU and Swedish negotiators are trying to find a way to secure regional aid for the sparsely populated northern part of Sweden. Ingrid Hedstrom writes in the 17 January Stockholm Swedish daily DAGENS NYHETER on page 7 that at the end of December the EU had offered to classify the area as a first priority region, allowing it to receive EU support reserved for underdeveloped regions. To be classified as such, however, an area's GNP must be only 75 percent of the EU average; the GNP of the Swedish northern provinces is 113 percent of the average. Swedish negotiators are looking for new ways to calculate the GNP so that it will fall below the limit. They have excluded revenue from hydropower stations located on northern rivers, which raise GNP figures but do little to increase the welfare of the region. In this way, they succeeded in lowering the figure to below the 75-percent limit in certain areas. According to a 14 January Stockholm Swedish daily DAGENS NYHETER article on page 7 by Ingrid Hedstrom, EU officials are also trying to come up with new criteria for their regional policy. An EU source told the newspaper: "They [the new criteria] must be idiotproof and watertight, and applicable only to Sweden, Norway, and Finland.
