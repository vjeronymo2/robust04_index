Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] WELLINGTON, March 17 (AFP) -- New Zealand Thursday welcomed a United States decision to extend its moratorium on nuclear testing for another year and called on China to take similar action. On Wednesday President Bill Clinton advised congress that the moratorium would be extended a year until September 1995. Foreign Minister Don McKinnon welcomed the decision, saying it meant Clinton regarded worldwide restraint on testing as important in making progress in nuclear non-proliferation. "New Zealand agrees," he said in a statement. "I hope other nuclear weapon states which have suspended testing programmes will be reassured by the US decision. I think we can take it that existing moratoria will be maintained. "China still has to agree to a moratorium. "It would be fitting for China to show restraint at a time when the world has justified expectations of an end to the development of these terrible weapons," McKinnon said.
