Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] In a joint declaration Paraguayan President Juan Carlos Wasmosy and Bolivian President Gonzalo Sanchez de Lozada last night described the Pilcomayo River and the areas around it as a decisive factor in promoting integration among Argentina, Bolivia, and Paraguay, and decided to invite Argentine President Carlos Saul Menem to a meeting with a view to seeking formulas that would be instrumental in jointly undertaking mutually beneficial development projects. Paraguay and Bolivia also stated that they are committed to fighting the drug trade, and agreed to hold permanent consultations between senior officers of their two countries. The Paraguayan Government supports "the search for a negotiated agreement" to provide Bolivia with an outlet to the Pacific Ocean. Sanchez de Lozada has characterized this goal as "an unrenounceable right of the Bolivian people." In the joint declaration, which was signed at Lopez Palace, Wasmosy and Sanchez de Lozada attached special significance to the Pilcomayo River, but did not go into detail. They agreed, however, to invite President Menem to a joint meeting to undertake joint projects. They have not set any specific date for this meeting. They have also acknowledged that the abuse of and illegal trade in narcotics and dangerous substances are a serious threat to the health and well-being of all peoples, and noted that all nations must assume their share of responsibility in fighting this scourge at every level and in all of its forms. They reiterated their commitment to fighting the drug trade, and agreed to urgently implement bilateral actions. To strengthen friendly relations and cooperation between their two countries, they have decided -- based on a proposal from the Bolivian Government -- to hold consultations between senior officers of their respective Armed Forces, through periodic meetings to be held at least once a year, alternating in Bolivia and Paraguay. Regarding the gas pipeline, Wasmosy and Sanchez de Lozada emphasized the work of the energy technical commission that is studying the sale to Paraguay of Bolivian natural gas through the gas pipeline project, of liquefied gas, and of gasoline. They conveyed their desire to have the project implemented as soon as possible with the participation of the private sectors of both countries. They also expressed their satisfaction over the progress and consolidation of the democratic system both in the world and within the region. They regard this as a guarantee for the full exercise of human rights and a necessary condition for achieving socioeconomic development. They also mentioned the efforts made to find formulas that would enable Bolivia to gradually move closer to and join Mercosur [Common Market of the South]. They agreed on the need to strengthen the Agreement on Coordination and Cooperation among Bolivia, Paraguay, and Uruguay (Urupabol), which was reactivated on 16 October 1993, and emphasized the consultations taking place among the three countries with a view to holding, as soon as possible, a meeting of their three foreign ministers. Among other points, President Wasmosy informed his Bolivian counterpart that the Paraguayan Government is studying a project to build a dam in Bahia Negra to check the Paraguay River water flow. He noted that this project will generate a development process in vast areas in the region, will make the river navigable along its entire course, and will decrease ecological risks. They also stated that it would be useful to improve customs monitoring at their common border. A customs office will be set up in General Garay. Sanchez de Lozada stated that the goal of obtaining a sovereign outlet to the Pacific Ocean is a fundamental topic in Bolivian foreign policy, and "an unrenounceable right of the Bolivian people." Wasmosy voiced his government's unwavering support in the search for a negotiated agreement on this issue.
