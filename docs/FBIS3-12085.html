Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] Managua BARRICADA in Spanish on 23 March on pages 1 and 5 reports that Central Bank President Evenor Taboada has announced that the Government of Nicaragua has agreed to reduce public spending by more than 4 percentage points of the GDP, as demanded by the IMF as a condition for signing the Enhanced Structural Adjustment Facility (ESAF). Taboada added that figures show Nicaragua must transfer resources from the public sector to the private sector, while guaranteeing stability and the growth of the economy.
