BFN [Text] Bangkok, June 27 (XINHUA) -- Three delegations of both government and the private sectors will visit the Middle East, the United States and China from the end of June to July in an effort to extend trade and investment cooperation. The trips will provide the private sector an opportunity to exchange views and information with those countries, according to a statement of Foreign Ministry available here today. On June 26-July 3, Deputy Permanent Secretary Pradyatawee Tavedikul will lead a group of private businessmen to Kuwait, Bahrain and the United Arab Emirates to explore business opportunities in the fields of telecommunication, aviation, labor, and stock exchanges. Deputy Director-General of the Economics Department Chaiyong Sajjipanon, in cooperation with the Thai Chamber of Commerce, left for southern China on Sunday [26 June] with a private sector delegation to analyze the consumer goods, real estate and hotel industries. Economics Department Chief Sukhum Rasameethat will lead another delegation to Israel and the United States to explore the jewelery market on July 15-29.
