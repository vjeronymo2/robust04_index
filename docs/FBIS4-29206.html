BFN [Text] Tokyo, June 14 KYODO -- Following are highlights of proposals that members of the tax commission Tuesday indicated will be included in a report due out next week. -- Implementing multiyear income and other tax cuts, totaling 6.2 trillion yen a year. -- Hiking the consumption tax, without specific figures but hinting at a raise to at least 7 percent from the current 3 percent. -- The consumption tax hike will be implemented on April 1, 1997, following tax cuts in the previous three years. -- Reviewing the current income and consumption tax system, especially to revamp the ratio of direct and indirect taxes for more fairness. -- Securing net revenue surplus from the tax cut and hike and to spare funds for the aged society in the 21st century. -- Redeeming in 10 to 20 years the 13 trillion yen deficit-covering bonds to be issued to finance 5.5 trillion yen income tax cuts in the current year. -- Avoiding further issuance of deficit-covering bonds. -- Government leadership in fiscal and administrative reforms to win public support for the tax reform asking heavier burdens on taxpayers. -- Drafting tax reform bills by the end of June and enacting them into law by the year-end as promised globally in the March market-opening package. -- Opposing a regional consumption tax, and discussing further the issue of tax revenue sources by the local governments.
