BFN [Text] Damascus, 12 Jun -- His Royal Highness Prince Sa'ud al-Faysal, the minister of foreign affairs, said that he conveyed a message from Custodian of the Two Holy Mosques King Fahd Bin-'Abd-al-Aziz to President Hafiz al-Asad of the Syrian Arab Republic. His Highness said: "President al-Asad gave me a message in reply within the framework of the continuous coordination, consultation and assessment of the situation in the region." His highness the foreign minister said this in an exclusive statement to the Syrian news agency after his meeting with the Syrian president today. He described his talks with President Hafiz al-Asad as excellent. He stressed the need to end the fighting in Yemen, and his highness said that "war cannot be an instrument for cooperation between Arab societies." Prince Sa'ud al-Faysal expressed the hope that the mission of Lakhdar Brahimi, the UN special envoy to Yemen, would be successful, because it proceeds from a general international concern to halt the fighting and end the current bloody struggle between the two Yemens. His highness stressed that all the disputed issues could be settled peacefully on the basis of affection and brotherliness, not fighting. He said: "War cannot be an instrument in cooperation and cohesion between Arab societies. Proceeding from this, we are all concerned with the need to end the fighting as soon as possible. I have also noticed this concern from our brothers in Syria". His Royal Highness Prince Sa'ud al-Faysal left Damascus this evening after a short visit to Syria.
