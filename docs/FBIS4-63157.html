BFN [Report by ITAR-TASS correspondent Mikhail Shevtsov] [Text] Moscow 20 May TASS -- Azerbaijan's Defence Minister Mamadrafi Mammadov, who arrived in Moscow on Thursday, has not yet signed the protocol on ceasefire and disengagement of the warring troops, which was prepared for signing on 17 May. Azerbaijan's Ambassador to Russia Ramiz Rizayev told ITAR-TASS that the Moscow meetings between the Azeri minister and Russia's Deputy Defence Minister Georgiy Kondratyev have failed to identify "common approaches, and Mamadrafi Mammadov intends to meet Russian Defence Minister Pavel Grachev today. Azerbaijan insists on the withdrawal of the Armenian troops from the territories captured simultaneously with the ceasefire and disengagement of troops. It calls for recording this provision in the protocol. "We would like to combine the first and the second phases of the settlement of the conflict," Azerbaijan's ambassador told ITAR-TASS. According to Ramiz Rizayev, Azerbaijan's proposals have met with support in principle of the Russian minister of foreign affairs, Andrey Kozyrev. Experts of the Russian Defence Ministry do not think that Pavel Grachev will agree to the amendments to the protocol proposed by Azerbaijan. The protocol as is has already been signed by representatives of Armenia, Nagorno-Karabakh and Russia.
