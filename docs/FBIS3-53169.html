Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Prime Minister Dr. 'Abd-al-Salam al-Majali received in his office at the Prime Ministry today Japanese Deputy Foreign Minister Hiroshi Fukuda. Fukuda said they discussed the Middle East peace process and its prospects, in addition to bilateral relations and the need to consolidate bilateral cooperation in various fields. He expressed Japan's determination to cooperate with Jordan in all possible fields in the present and future, stemming from Jordan's political importance, known contributions, and efforts made in various areas to support the peace process and achieve regional stability. Fukuda pointed out that an integrated program is currently being prepared on the aid Japan can extend to Jordan, so this aid package can take its final shape. Regarding Japan's role in promoting the peace process, Fukuda said: We are determined to intervene politically in this process, and we seek an effective role in the multinational talks. He pointed out that the peace process managed to achieve progress, but that there are some obstacles to this process. However, the peace process in itself is important, sensitive, and historic, and useful from various aspects, he added.
