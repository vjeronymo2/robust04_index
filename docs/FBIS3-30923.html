Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Pale, Bosnia-Herzegovina, Feb 10 (AFP) -- Bosnia's Serbs Thursday [10 February] rejected NATO's ultimatum to end their siege of Sarajevo and said their heavy weapons would not be removed from around the city. "Despite this ultimatum, the Serb artillery will not move an inch," said their main spokesman, Miroslav Toholj. "We are ready to retaliate." He called the NATO threat to hit Serb positions with air strikes if the artillery is not pulled back within 10 days as "a terrorist threat" and said the Serbs would respond "by all means" to any attack.
