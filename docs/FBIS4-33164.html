BFN [Text] Havana, 1 Jun (PL) -- Honduran Foreign Minister Ernesto Paz Aguilar will visit Cuba at the invitation of the Foreign Ministry. An authorized Cuban Foreign Ministry source confirmed Paz Aguilar's trip to PRENSA LATINA today. The date for the trip will be coordinated by the two countries soon. The Honduran foreign minister met with Roberto Robaina, his Cuban counterpart, in Cairo, taking advantage of their participation in the meeting of foreign ministers of the Nonaligned Movement which is being held in the Egyptian capital. On this occasion, he asked Robaina to convey greetings from Honduran President Carlos Roberto Reina to Cuban President Fidel Castro and referred to the possibility of a meeting between the two chiefs of state during the upcoming Fourth Ibero-American Summit to be held in Cartagena de Indias. Cuba and Honduras do not have diplomatic relations. Diplomatic ties were suspended by the Honduran Government in April 1961 following an OAS agreement.
