940801 FT 01 AUG 94 / Call for Abiola's release likely Delegates to Nigeria's constitutional conference, assembled by the military regime to draw up the programme for a return to civilian rule, are today expected to call for the release of detained opposition leader Mr Moshood Abiola in an effort to defuse the country's deepening crisis. A leading delegate at the conference in central Nigeria is due to table a motion asking the head of state, Gen Sani Abacha, to release Mr Abiola. The opposition leader's trial for treason, under way in Abuja, was adjourned last week until tomorrow amid violent clashes between armed police and demonstrators. A four-week strike which has already forced a 300,000 barrels a day cut in oil production - about 20 per cent of normal output - is set to intensify this week. The Nigeria Labour Congress, which represents over 40 main trade unions, has called a national strike from Wednesday. The Rev Jesse Jackson, sent by the White House last week to mediate in the crisis, feared 'Nigeria only has a few more days to go, for the escalating tensions might very well explode.'
