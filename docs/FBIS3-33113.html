Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on developments related to the Mexican elections scheduled for 21 August 1994. Luis Donaldo Colosio, presidential candidate for the Institutional Revolutionary Party, PRI, led a meeting in Mexico City to discuss his campaign funding. PRI President Fernando Ortis Arana swore in the members of the party's national and state funding commissions. Colosio told the commission members that "the people are right to reject waste, mismanagement, and the splurging of money, as they offend the people, given the current social situation." The party plans to spend less than the government- imposed ceiling of 134 million new pesos and will not use public funds or property. (Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 0435 GMT 9 Feb 94) Colosio has proposed a five-point program to rescue Indians throughout the country from the neglect and discrimination they have always suffered. The program, among other things, reforms the judicial system, protects Indian property, and provides education and training for Indians. (Mexico City Canal 13 Television Azteca Network in Spanish 1300 GMT 16 Feb 94) The Party of the Democratic Revolution, PRD, has announced a series of demonstrations at the Zocalo in Mexico City to promote an extraordinary session of the Chamber of Deputies to discuss electoral reforms. Although no dates were given, the first event will be a 100-hour march, followed by a 500 [number as heard] km run around the legislature buildings. (Mexico City Canal 13 Television Azteca Network in Spanish 1300 GMT 9 Feb 94) Cuauhtemoc Cardenas, the presidential candidate for the PRD, went to Constitution Square to participate in the so-called "100 Hours for Democracy" march, which his party organized to demand electoral code reforms. He told reporters that political will alone is not enough to ensure a better election process. He said that talks between the Government Secretariat and political parties on electoral changes continue. (Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 0435 GMT 12 Feb 94) The so-called 100 Hours for Democracy march ended on 13 February. Cuauhtemoc Cardenas, PRD presidential candidate, walked the final laps with party members. He said the solution to the Chiapas conflict lies in "the land distribution system. To ensure a long lasting solution, the country's peasants must be sure they have the right to claim and posses land of their own." He added that certain principles must be included in the Constitution "to recognize the individual political, social, and economic rights of the peasant communities and their inhabitants. He gave the government 10 days to respond to the party's call for extraordinary congressional sessions to discuss this issue and electoral code reforms. (Mexico City Canal 13 Television Azteca Network in Spanish 1300 GMT 14 Feb 94) Diego Fernandez de Cevallos, the presidential candidate for the National Action Party, PAN, met with a group of demonstrators on their way to the Los Pinos Presidential Residence to demand the removal of several municipal officials. He took the opportunity to criticize media coverage of his campaign and asked for the Mexican voters to vote decidedly. (Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 0435 GMT 9 Feb 94) PAN Presidential Candidate Fernandez de Cevallos began a two-day tour to Jalisco State on 11 February. He visited the San Juan de Dios marketplace in Guadalajara, where he met with rival vendors and spoke to them about pluralism in a democracy. Fernandez de Cevallos plans to meet with cattle breeders, farmers, and students. (Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 0435 GMT 12 Feb 94) Diego Fernandez de Cevallos said on 13 February that all those party members who opposed his presidential nomination now support him. "I know, and I always knew, how much it hurt certain party members when I won the nomination," he pointed out. "I also know that those same party members now support Diego Fernandez de Cevallos in spite of Diego Fernandez de Cevallos, because it is the PAN's democratic process at work." In a 30-minute speech in a low income neighborhood in Mexico City, he rejected the use of punishment votes and criticized those who vote out of fear. (Mexico City Canal 13 Television Azteca Network in Spanish 1300 GMT 14 Feb 94) Diego Fernandez de Cevallos met on Tuesday 15 February with Iberoamerican University students in Mexico City in a packed auditorium. Fernandez announced the demise of the current Mexican political system and denounced the corruption, lies, and vested interests that have led to the social and political neglect of millions of Mexicans. "The stability of the Mexican State," he said, "has not been based on a fair and democratic social order because corruption and lies have prevailed..." (Mexico City Radio ACIR Network in Spanish 0030 GMT 16 Feb 94) Cecilia Soto, the presidential candidate for the Labor Party, PL, toured Aguascalientes on 8 February. She pointed out that there are several agreements that must still be reached among the political parties to ensure a clean and honest election. She stressed that the election process is the time to fight abstention. (Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 0435 GMT 9 Feb 94) PL Candidate Soto told workers in Guanajuato that the party's platform is different and offers a new option for the working class. Before ending her tour she witnessed the signing of an alliance agreement between independent farmers and workers and the PL. (Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 0435 GMT 12 Feb 94) Cecilia Soto says that clean elections in August can only be guaranteed through the reformation of the Electoral Law. She agreed with PRD candidate Cardenas that if the government's electoral policies are not reformed the people will seek solutions through the use of force. Soto also denounced the alleged proselytizing of Santiago Onate, Mexico's consul in the United states, who met with Mexican residents in Los Angeles to explain the positions held by the ruling party and candidate Colosio on several national matters, including Chiapas. (Mexico City Radio ACIR Network in Spanish 0030 15 Feb 94) Jorge Gonzalez Torres, the presidential candidate for the Mexican Green Ecologist Party, PVEM, kicked off his campaign for the State of Guanajuato at a rally held at an Indian reservation. "We must reconsider the way we deal with the native population all over the country," he said, "and recognize them as our true origin, care for them, protect them, and offer them the same opportunities other groups have." (Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 0435 GMT 9 Feb 94) PVEM Presidential Candidate Torres stressed the need for extraordinary congressional sessions to discuss electoral reforms, including article 82, with which his party does not agree. He said that those candidates who spend more than the imposed spending ceiling should be punished strictly, and not with what seem more like traffic fines. (Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 0435 GMT 12 Feb 94) Marcela Lombardo, the presidential candidate for the Popular Socialist Party, PPS, toured Baja California, where she met with authorities of the Binational Human Rights Center, located at El Bordo, a well-known illegal crossing point into the United States. She talked to several people planning to cross and asked them to reconsider their decision. (Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 0435 GMT 12 Feb 94) PPS Presidential Candidate Marcela Lombardo continued her campaign on 13 February in Baja California. There she demanded that Catholic Bishop Samuel Ruiz stay out of the Chiapas negotiations. She said that it is evident that the leaders of the Chiapas rebels are not Indians and, therefore, do not respond to Indian interests. She also reaffirmed support for legalizing abortion to prevent the many deaths produced by clandestine abortion clinics. She said that the real enemy to beat in August are the reactionaries and the Yankees. (Mexico City Canal 13 Television Azteca Network in Spanish 1300 GMT 14 Feb 94) Presidential candidate for the Cardenist Front for National Reconstruction Party, Rafael Aguilar Talamantes, campaigned in Chihuahua. He held coordination meetings with party members and chatted with the press on 11 February. (Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 0435 GMT 12 Feb 94) Cardenist Front Candidate Talamantes said on 13 February that it would be naive to believe holding special congressional sessions and reforming the electoral code will do any good. He pointed at political balance in the congress and the little time left for such significant debates. He said the Cardenists are in favor of the changes, but now is not the time. (Mexico City Canal 13 Television Azteca Network in Spanish 1300 GMT 14 Feb 94) Alvaro Perez Trevino, the presidential candidate for the Authentic Party of the Mexican Revolution, PARM, took his campaign to Tlaxcala on 13 February. There he forecasted that his party will be the second strongest party in the country. He criticized the government's agricultural relief program saying it offers no long term solutions and benefits PRI farmers. A former PRI member, Trevino called NAFTA a way of bringing disloyal competition for Mexican products. He met with farmers and party members. (Mexico City Canal 13 Television Azteca Network in Spanish 1300 GMT 14 Feb 94) The Federal Electoral Institute has reminded all political parties to file a copy of their government platform. The Electoral Code establishes this as one of the requirements for registering presidential candidates. The institute called on candidates and parties in general to be respectful of the rights of third parties. (Mexico City Canal 13 Television Azteca Network in Spanish 10 Feb 94)
