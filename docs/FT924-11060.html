921028 FT 28 OCT 92 / Observer: New(s) stable After the hurly-burly of Wapping, Murdoch and The Times, Charles Wilson was no doubt overjoyed, when in 1990, he was asked to be editor-in-chief of Robert Maxwell's Sporting Life. Sunday For Monday, the horse in which he had a stake along with a bunch of Times journalists, hadn't exactly set the world alight. But Charlie Wilson must have been looking forward to nothing more exciting in prospect than a steady series of race meetings. But it didn't work out like that. First he became editor-in-chief of Mirror Group Newspapers, then he had to handle the world's press when Robert Maxwell went missing from his yacht. Then there was the trauma of the daily revelations of missing money. And the papers had to keep coming out through it all. Now he is really getting into his retirement by becoming managing director of MGN to chief executive David Montgomery, also a former News International editor.
