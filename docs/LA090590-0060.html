September 5, 1990, Wednesday, Home Edition

IRAQ POSTAL NUMBERS SET UP FOR DETAINEES

Iraq announced Tuesday three post office box numbers where relatives and friends can send letters to detained foreigners.

State-run Baghdad Radio said the "foreign guests" can receive mail via the "mail boxes of peace." The addresses are P.O. Box 39090, P.O. Box 50300 and P.O. Box 50285, all in Baghdad, Iraq.

However, the U.S. Postal Service last week suspended service to Iraq and Iraqi-occupied Kuwait, citing difficulties in transportation. A postal service spokesman in Washington could not immediately say if or how letters sent to the Iraqi post office boxes could be delivered.
