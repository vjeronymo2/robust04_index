Language: Japanese Article Type:BFN [Text] Prime Minister Morihiro Hosokawa invited on 30 January business leaders, including Nikkeiren [Japan Federation of Employers Associations] President Takeshi Nagano, Toyota Motor Corp. Chairman Shoichiro Toyoda, and Nomura Securities adviser Setsuya Tabuchi, to the Prime Minister's Official Residence. During this meeting which lasted about one hour, they exchanged views on ways to handle the Japan-U.S. summit talks scheduled on 11 February. Discussions focused mainly on the auto issues of the Japan-U.S. economic framework talks which are experiencing rough sailing. Business leaders brought up various requests and said: "Japan should maintain its opposition to the U.S. demand for setting numerical targets."
