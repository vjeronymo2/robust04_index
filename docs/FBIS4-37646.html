BFN [Text] In its session yesterday the Supreme Administrative Council [SAC], which was chaired by President Hashemi-Rafsanjani, approved the assignment to the private sector of part of the tasks of the government sector, particularly general and service tasks. Under the approval, all ministries and government-affiliated organizations, institutions and companies -- which are covered by the law provided they are mentioned by name -- are given five years to take steps to hand over general and service tasks to the private sector. Under the approval all sectors can hand over their service tasks to cooperatives which have been set up with the participation of government employees who have been made redundant. They are to afford them the necessary cooperation and give them priority when awarding contracts.
