Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Commander in Chief Fidel Castro attended the ceremony for the signing of a bilateral cooperation agreement between Cuba and Colombia. It was signed by the public health ministers of the two countries, Julio Teja and Juan Luis Londono. Fidel briefed the Colombian minister on the characteristics of the family doctor program, its origins, and the important role it plays in the community. Fidel further pointed out the program's contribution in reducing the infant mortality rate to 9.4 in 1993. He spoke of the training of specialists in general medicine, the elevated level of professionalism attained by the nurses, and the creation of medical science departments throughout the country. The agreement includes prioritized areas such as primary attention, epidemiologic surveillance, human resource training, essential and generic medicines, and vaccinations.
