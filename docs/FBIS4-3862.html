BFN [Text] Athens, April 23 (XINHUA) -- Greece today said it was satisfied with U.S. President Bill Clinton's statements on Macedonian issue made during a joint press conference Friday [23 April] with Greek Premier Andreas Papandreou following their talks at the White House. Government spokesman Evangelos Venizelos said that Greece was particularly satisfied with Clinton's statement on the issues of Macedonia, Greek-Albanian relations and the Cyprus problem. Venizelos, however, expressed reservations concerning Clinton's optimism on Cyprus. "In order to make progress in the confidence-building measures and the essence of the problem, we must have a substantial and clear change in the Turkish and Turkish-Cypriot stance. That is, Turkey and Turkish-Cypriot leader Rauf Denktash must tone down their intransigence," Venizelos said. On the Macedonian issue, the spokesman said that "It is important that President Clinton acknowledges Greek sensitivities, the soundness of Greece's position, and places such great emphasis on the mission of UN Special Mediator Cyrus Vance and Clinton's special envoy Matthew Nimetz." Referring to the meeting on Monday between Greek Foreign Minister Karolos Papoulias and Vance, the spokesman said that "We must be patient and restrained... [ellipses as received]."
