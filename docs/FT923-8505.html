920811 FT 11 AUG 92 / World Commodities Prices: Tea There was strong and active demand with 14,660 packages for the day reports the Tea Brokers' Association. Landed brightest East African teas and the better liquoring mediums showed further gains. Plainer mediums were a strong feature and often 5 to 10p dearer. Central African teas met fair competition. Ceylons attracted fair support at about last levels except the plainer teas which were often neglected. Offshore good demand with prices firm on last rates. The highest price realised this week was 205p for a Rwanda pf. 1. Quotations: quality 170, nom good medium 140p, medium 125p, low medium 80p.
