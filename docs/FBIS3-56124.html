Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Text] Representatives of the BPF [Belarusian People's Front] parliamentary opposition have had a meeting with journalists at the Supreme Soviet. BPF leader, Z. Paznyak, stated that he has proposed a meeting with President B. Clinton on 15 January during his visit to Kuropaty. "The initiative for the meeting came from the U.S. side," Z. Paznyak made clear. The leader of the parliamentary opposition sharply attacked the Supreme Soviet Speaker S. Shushkevich, for signing the agreement on collective security, accusing him of betraying the national interests of Belarus by this step. He expressed a negative attitude to the agreement signed in Moscow on merging the money systems of the Russian Federation and the Republic of Belarus, and also the likelihood of the Republic of Belarus joining NATO
