Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, March 15 KYODO -- The president of the Pacific island nation of Nauru expressed hopes for Japan's continued cooperation with the South Pacific region in a meeting with Prime Minister Morihiro Hosokawa on Tuesday [15 March], government officials said. Bernard Dowiyogo, who also heads the South Pacific Forum (SPF), an informal discussion body of 15 nations and regions including Australia and New Zealand, also thanked Hosokawa for Japan's assistance to the region. Dowiyogo said the South Pacific region hopes to "deepen dialogue" with Japan through an SPF meeting to be held in Australia in August. Since 1989 the annual SPF sessions have been followed by "dialogues" with representatives of important cooperation partners such as the United States, China and Japan. In response, Hosokawa pledged Japan's readiness to further develop ties with the region.
