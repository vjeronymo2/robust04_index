BFN [Text] Ankara, May 3, IRNA -- Iranian Deputy Foreign Minister Mohammad Hashemi here Tuesday night [3 May] in his visit with Turkish President Suleyman Demirel called for expansion of Tehran-Ankara cultural and security cooperation. Hashemi said that his first official visit to Turkey was made because he highly valued promotion of cooperation between the two neighbouring countries. "During my short stay in Turkey, useful discussions were made with the Turkish officials in the field of mutual, regional as well as international development which concluded with positive outcome," Hashemi said. He termed Iran-Turkey eco-political ties as "satisfactory" and called for further expansion of ties in the field of culture and security. Hashemi arrived in Ankara yesterday and has so far met with Turkish Foreign Minister Hikmet Cetin and Interior Minister Nahit Mentese.
