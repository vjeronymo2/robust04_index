931129 FT 29 NOV 93 / Survey of Software at Work - Winter 1993 (17): Supplier/product profile Product: Smallworld GIS; current release is Version2. First installed in September 1990 in Gelsenwasser, Germany. The product consists several of technologies claimed as unique to Smallworld: object-oriented programming environment, seamless continuous mapbase, mixed raster and vector data, version management. Suppliers: Smallworld Systems Ltd, founded in 1988 in the UK, is owned by its founding directors and staff. Southern Electric, a UK customer, has shareholding together with a small number of private investors. Turnover: Pounds 3.4m in 1992 (Dollars 11m worldwide), Pounds 1.1m in 1991. Employees: 80 plus. Number of users:120 plus sites worldwide. Price: Not disclosed. Hardware and operating systems: Open system operating on Unix-based workstations from Digital, IBM, Sun, Hewlett-Packard. Sales and support: Direct and from partners subsidiaries and distributors. Maintenance charged monthly at 1 per cent of cost (figures undisclosed). Other services available: Consultancy, training, implementation, development and training. User views: Pros: designed from scratch for GIS, excellent support, responsive UK-based supplier, excellent support. Cons: supplier new, comparatively small. No PC option. (Other software deficiencies mostly addressed in Version2). Difficult to implement distributed system to give corporate view. Coyness about cost from supplier and users alike suggests high price.
