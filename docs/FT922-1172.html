920624 FT 24 JUN 92 / International Company News: ABN Amro close to sale of travel arm ABN Amro, the Netherlands' biggest bank, is close to reaching an agreement to sell its domestic travel agency business to Holland International, the country's biggest travel group. Holland International is 80 per cent owned by Kaufhof, the German retailer, writes Ronald van de Krol. The bank's travel business generates annual turnover of more than Fl 100m (Dollars 56.5m) and comprises five travel agencies in Amsterdam, Rotterdam, Utrecht, Nijmegen and Groningen, as well as a business travel bureau. ABN Amro had said in March that it planned to sell the travel business but it did not identify the likely buyer. In addition, its German majority shareholder, Holland International is partly-owned by the Dutch railway, KLM Royal Dutch Airlines and the Nedlloyd transport group.
