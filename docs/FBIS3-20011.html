Language: Greek Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] Deputy Foreign Minister Yeoryios Papandreou is in Washington, where he had a meeting with high-ranking officials of U.S. President Bill Clinton's administration and members of Congress. [Begin recording] [Papandreou] It was a very important meeting with members of the National Security Council and State Department. We discussed a series of issues, such as Bosnia-Herzegovina and developments in the Bosnian crisis, and had the opportunity to discuss issues of interest to both countries, including Cyprus, Greek-Turkish relations, The Former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia [FYROM], and other general European issues. All our contacts are on our national issues, including FYROM. [Unidentified correspondent] Reportedly, Papandreou will inform the American officials of the Greek Government's answer and positive reply to Cyrus Vance's ideas on FYROM. According to Washington circles, the U.S. President's special coordinator is in continuous contact with UN mediator Cyrus Vance and is expected to visit Greece and FYROM soon.
