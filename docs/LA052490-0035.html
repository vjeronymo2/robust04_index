May 24, 1990, Thursday, Home Edition

CONTROVERSY OVER BART SIMPSON: 'UNDERACHIEVER AND PROUD OF IT'

As a teacher in an alternative high school diploma program for high-risk young people (dropouts and potential dropouts), I think Bart is one of the best things that has come along. The main reasons are this:

1.

There is less graffiti writing and more Bart doodling! One constant part of working in programs like this is keeping the classroom graffiti free.

2. All ethnic groups relate to Bart. It is important to have images that all students can relate to -- especially in terms of color, race and creed. Believe it or not, Bart being a cartoon character is more powerful than Bill Cosby in that whites, Latinos, Asians, Samoans -- all young males seem to relate to the character.

3. We, the adults in their lives, need to have clues on the "culture" of being young -- especially since so many of us have forgotten it.

High school students must dream, daydream, doodle, be understood, have something that connects them to each other over race lines and to us over age lines. Seeing students come to class in clean, brightly colored Bart T-shirts and doodling with Bart cartoons is wonderful!
