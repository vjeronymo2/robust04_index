Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Bratislava, Feb 23 (CTK) -- The talks on cooperation between the Russian and Slovak intelligence services are by no means directed against any third country, as they primarily concern international crime, Tatyana Samolis, the spokeswoman for the director of the Russian Counterintelligence Service Yevgeniy Primakov, told journalists today. Primakov, who arrived for a two-day visit to the Slovak Republic on Tuesday [22 February], met with deputy chairman of Slovak Parliament Augustin Marian Huska and Deputy Premier Jozef Prokes. He will also meet President Michal Kovac. Our guest did not raise any objections against Slovakia's effort to integrate with European systems, said Huska. "Shall we wait until various parts of the Russian or Chechen mafias establish themselves easily in Slovakia?," Huska replied to the question whether it was dangerous to establish contacts during the current unstable period in the two countries. Both Primakov and Samolis refused to comment on the "Ames spying affair."
