Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Text] Sarajevo, 17 Feb (TANJUG) -- Radovan Karadzic, president of the Srpska republic, and Fikret Abdic, president of the autonomous province of western Bosnia, have reached an agreement on the permanent cessation of hostilities and an orientation toward political negotiations. This is underlined in a statement issued today by the office of the president of the Srpska republic. The agreement on a permanent cease-fire will come into effect at 1200 [1100 GMT] on 18 February. "This is yet another important step toward peace in former Bosnia-Herzegovina," the statement says. Abdic promises to secure compliance with the cease-fire not only on the territory of the autonomous province of western Bosnia, but also on the left bank of the Una River, which is currently controlled by remnants of the 5th Corps loyal to the Muslim government in Sarajevo.
