BFN [Text] Tokyo, May 23 KYODO -- Japan and Vietnam on Monday [23 May] signed an aviation treaty aimed at opening regular flights between the two nations, the Foreign Ministry said. Japanese and Vietnamese Government officials signed the treaty in Hanoi, the ministry said. With the signing of the treaty, the two countries will boost bilateral relations and address mounting air transportation demands, it said. Vietnam has been urging Japan to open regular flights between the two nations since 1990 and the two sides began negotiations on the signing of the treaty last September, it said.
