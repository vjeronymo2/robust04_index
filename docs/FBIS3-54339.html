Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Cairo, 25 Jan (MENA)--Palestinian president Yasir 'Arafat has praised President Husni Mubarak's efforts to surmount the obstacles to the prompt, sure implementation of the Palestinian-Israeli Declaration of Principles. Following a two-hour meeting with Foreign Minister 'Amr Musa today, 'Arafat said that the latest Egyptian effort in this regard was Musa's visit to Israel, where he discussed the outcome of the meeting in Oslo between 'Arafat and Israeli Foreign Minister Shim'on Peres. 'Arafat said President Mubarak and his foreign minister deserve credit and gratitude for their efforts, which have boosted the Palestinian cause. We expect and hope to overcome the obstacles still facing the Declaration of Principles within the next few days, 'Arafat said. He added that important talks will be held tonight between Israeli officials who will arrive in Cairo tonight and the Palestinian delegation. The talks will continue tomorrow and may go into Thursday and Friday if needed. 'Arafat pointed out that Musa returned from Israel with important results. The Palestinian president said the Israeli delegation that will arrive tonight is the same delegation that accompanied Peres to the Oslo talks last week. 'Arafat said the Palestinian delegation is eager to see what the Israelis will bring to the Cairo talks. On his visit to Saudi Arabia, 'Arafat said he was pleased with it and with the meeting he held with Saudi King Fahd, custodian of the two holy mosques. He described his talks with King Fahd as good, constructive, and positive. He said he explained everything that has been achieved in the peace talks and thanked the king for his efforts to stimulate the peace process. The Palestinian president said he asked King Fahd to continue exerting efforts on the international, Arab, and regional levels on behalf of the Palestinian people, their cause, and the sacred cause of Jerusalem. Musa said 'Arafat talked with President Mubarak on the telephone. And Mubarak was in constant touch with us during the talks we held tonight, he remarked. The Egyptian foreign minister said the arrival of the Israeli delegation in Cairo tonight for talks with the Palestinian delegation to explore ways to overcome the remaining problems is a new and satisfactory development. He revealed that he will meet 'Arafat and Israeli Foreign Minister Peres in Davos, Switzerland. Musa added that efforts to settle the remaining problems will continue in the next few days.
