BFN [Text] Military sources say National Union for the Total Independence of Angola [UNITA] antiaircraft forces shot down a Popular Movement for the Liberation of Angola-Labor Party [MPLA-PT] airplane in Huila Province on 26 June 1994. The sources say a Russian-made MiG-23 was hit while attacking civilian targets in Ngola region, north of Lubango. It all happened at about 1240. After being hit the aircraft tried to return to the Lubango Air Force Base, but exploded near the runway, killing all its crew. The sources added that UNITA armed forces are able to thwart the MPLA-PT's air force arrogance. Meanwhile, it has been reported that fierce clashes took place in Huila Province where the MPLA-PT is involved in military offensives against UNITA. In defensive operations carried out by UNITA's Armed Forces for the Liberation of Angola, an MPLA motorized convoy was attacked on the road between Cacula and [name indistinct]. Our forces killed 14 MPLA-PT soldiers and wounded 32 others. UNITA also captured MPLA Major Manuel Bravante Ngongo, born in Pambangala, Cuanza Sul Province's Cassongue District. Maj. Ngongo was the commander of the 4th Motorized Infantry Battalion stationed in Cacula and the leading officer behind the offensive in the northern region of Huila Province.
