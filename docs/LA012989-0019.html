January 29, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

OUTTAKES: LYNCH MOB

An iconoclastic director like David Lynch -- of "Eraserhead," and "Blue Velvet" fame -- working in network TV? Come on!

But sources tell us that Lynch will start a pilot movie for a mystery-thriller series, "Northwest Passage," in Seattle, come March, for ABC. Kyle MacLachlin stars as an FBI agent investigating the murder of a high school homecoming queen. Also cast: Isabella Rossellini and Harry Dean Stanton.

The movie was written by Lynch and Mark Forest and is set in a small Peyton Place-type community populated by unusual characters -- such as a deputy sheriff who breaks into tears whenever he's faced with an emotional situation.

According to a source close to the production, "Melodrama is too tame a word to describe the emotional tone. Every aspect of story and style have been heightened."

David Latt produces with Propaganda Films. Leonard Klady
