Language: Latvian Article Type:BFN [Text] The Latvian National Independence Movement [LNNK], the alliance For the Fatherland and Freedom, the People's Front of Latvia, the Latvian Green Party, the Latvian Christian Democratic Union, the Environment Protection Club, the Latvian National Soldiers' Society, the Latvian National Union of Falcons of the Daugava, the Latvian Association of the Politically Repressed, the Livonian Association and the Latvian National Partisans' Union, all of which are participant groups in the union of Latvian National Forces, have signed a protest statement against the maintenance of the Skrunda radar station in Latvia for another five and a half years. This was announced today at a news conference by Baiba Petersone, deputy chairman of the LNNK board. The Union of National Forces calls on the Latvian government delegation, the Saeima, and the state president not to accept a treaty with Russia if it even partly legitimizes Russia's military presence in Latvia. Otherwise, the statement says, the Union of National Forces envisages using the forms of direct democracy and the experience of non-violent resistance until the full withdrawal of a foreign country's troops.
