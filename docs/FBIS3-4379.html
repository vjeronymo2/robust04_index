Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, March 29 (XINHUA) -- Chinese President Jiang Zemin had a cordial talk with Reginald K. Brack Jr., president of the Time-Warner publishing company of the United States, here today. During their meeting, Jiang briefed Brack and his group on China's changes since the country took on opening and reform programs in the late 1970s. Brack said that his last China tour was made a decade ago. This time he was impressed by China's great improvement in urban construction, cultural facilities and public health. The Chinese president also introduced to the guests China's latest efforts in establishing a socialist market system. Those present at the meeting included Chinese Vice-Foreign Minister Liu Huaqiu and J. Stapleton Roy, U.S. Ambassador to China. The U.S. visitors are here as guest of the Chinese People's Institute for Foreign Affairs.
