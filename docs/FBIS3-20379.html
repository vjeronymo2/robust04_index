Language: Turkish Article Type:BFN [Text] Atay Ahmet Rasid, minister of foreign affairs and defense, has said that the Greek Cypriot side must quickly abandon its inflexible approach and refrain from making statements and accusations against the Turkish Cypriot side that are aimed at misleading the public, so that a balanced and just result can be achieved from the talks that are being held on the confidence-building measures. In a written statement yesterday, Rasid recalled Greek Cypriot Foreign Minister Alexandros Mikhailidhis' effort to blame the Turkish Cypriot side for the deadlock in the talks on the confidence-building measures and said that Mikhailidhis' effort was aimed at distorting well-known facts. Rasid stressed that the introduction of new elements in the talks -- elements aimed at satisfying the Greek Cypriot side -- will create a deadlock in the negotiating process. He said: "As the experts who follow the talks on the confidence-building measures on Maras [Varosha] and the Nicosia International Airport have seen, the main reasons for the present deadlock are the Greek Cypriot side's pressure and the attempt to deviate from the original package of confidence-building measures and add new elements to it in order to satisfy Glavkos Kliridhis and the Greek Cypriot side."
