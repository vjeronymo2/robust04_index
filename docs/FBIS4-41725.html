BFN [From the 28 Jun EESTI SENUMID, p.3; "News Bulletin" No. 122/94] [Text] The CSCE permanent committee in Vienna will extend the mandate of the CSCE mission in Estonia by six months from July 1. Representatives of several countries pointed out that the mission should also pay more attention to the monitoring of the Russian troops withdrawal, the Foreign Ministry press service announced. The Estonian delegation to the CSCE permanent committee also started a discussion of the Estonian-Russian border issue in connection with the Russian presidential decree on the unilateral marking of the border line. The delegation stressed that border issues must be settled via bilateral negotiations, according to the CSCE principles and other international standards. No resolution was passed due to lack of consensus on the issue.
