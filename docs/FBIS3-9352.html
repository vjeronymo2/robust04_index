Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Bucharest ROMPRES, 22/3/1994 -- A main railway with higher speed and European gauges on the Iasi (Romania)-Chisinau (Republic of Moldova) route will be built with financial support given by Romania's Government, told daily VOCEA ROMANIEI Teodor Groza, secretary of state, head of the railway department. The funding, worth 95 million lei, including 70 million lei for the drawing up of technical studies on the integration of the Ungheni (customs point)-Chisinau railway in the European transportation network, and 25 million lei for the technical surveys on the rebuilding of an important railway section in the Republic of Moldova, were earmarked under a Romanian Government resolution out of the fund placed at the Romanian Government's disposal for the relations with the Republic of Moldova for 1993. The state secretary said that experts of the railway transportation ministries of the two states held several meetings to discuss "the integration of the railway transportation of the Republic of Moldova in the European transportation." Teodor Groza said in the interview that the Romanian party offered the railways of the Republic of Moldova the possibility of having the equipment repaired in workshops in Romania. As a member of the Inter-Ministerial Committee for Romania's Relations with the Republic of Moldova, Teodor Groza said that the Romanian party gave Moldova assistance in the working out of technical surveys on railways, tariff policy, and training of personnel. As for road transportation, he said, Romania's Government will give financial support for the repair of the bridge across the Prut River at Radauti-Lipcani, and is seeking technical solutions for the building of a road bridge at Ungheni.
