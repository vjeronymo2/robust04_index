921015 FT 15 OCT 92 / Unified strategy urged for Midlands BUSINESS leaders in the west Midlands anxious for more inward investment to stimulate the regional economy, are demanding closer co-ordination by the many organisations responsible for attracting companies to the region. A survey by Coopers & Lybrand, the accountancy firm, in Birmingham, found that about three quarters of 300 private and public-sector executives perceived conflicts of interests between the organisations involved. More than half the private-sector executives and nearly three quarters of the public-sector executives were dissatisfied with regional promotion. The disquiet reflects the downturn of inward investment in the region over the past two years. During the 1980s, the west Midlands successful in attracting overseas companies. In 1986-90, 21 per cent of British inward investment projects went to the west Midlands, a figure that dropped to 14 per cent last year.
