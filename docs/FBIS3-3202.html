Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Moscow, March 15 (XINHUA) -- Russia today initialed with Latvia an accord on the withdrawal of troops from the Baltic state by the end of August. Under the accord, Russia will keep its Skrunda radar station in Latvia for four more years and have another 18 months for its dismantlement, the ITAR-TASS NEWS AGENCY reported. Chief Russian negotiator Sergey Zotov described the accord as "a victory for common sense" and said it paved the way for the signing of a final agreement probably before mid-April when the presidents of the two countries meet. The bulk of the 20,000 ex-Soviet troops in Latvia and Estonia were stationed in Latvia. Russia completed the withdrawal of troops from the third Baltic state of Lithuania last year.
