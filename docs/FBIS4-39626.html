BFN [Text] Poachers tried to enter the Russian waters off the isle of Kunashir in the Russian Far East on 14 occasions from June 1 to 6. They approached from the Japanese island of Hokkaido without identification signs or navigation lights in poor visibility. The Russian border guards had to fire warning shots, Lieutenant General Vitaliy Sedykh, commander of the Pacific border guard district told the Japanese consul in Vladivostok Wednesday. The Russian border guard press office reported that the poachers did not respond to all demands to stop. As they escaped, they cut off the nets in which large amounts of fish died. Sedykh said that warning shots were fired in the early hours of June 4 and 6 when two poaching schooners and six schooners respectively were sighted. The border guards fired over 300 30 mm and around 10 76 mm shells.
