920914 FT 14 SEP 92 / Arts: Today's Television The further we move from the days of British (and French and Belgian and Italian) imperialism the more difficult it becomes to blame all the ills of African countries today on colonialism. Given that local tribal, religious and political antagonisms are blamed everywhere else in the world it seems reasonable to expect similar causes in Africa. Out Of Darkness (7.50 BBC2) looks at the position in Ethiopia, an example of a country less affected than most by European imperial activity which, nevertheless, is now in a position not so very different from that of Yugoslavia. Unfortunately BBC2 is showing the elongated cinema version of Steven Spielberg's Duel, a weird thriller about a car driver persecuted by a huge articulated tanker. The original television version is shorter and better (8.30). Panorama investigates the way that the pay of British company directors has risen faster than ever during the recession while others have been tightening their belts (9.30 BBC1). ITV shows The London Mozart Players At The Fairfield performing a Rossini overture and then Mozart's Piano Concerto No 23 with pianist Howard Shelley (10.40).
