Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] On 6 February, Qatari Foreign Minister Hamad Bin-Jasim Bin-Jabbur Al Thani concluded his visit to Cuba. According to his own statements prior to his departure from the Jose Marti International Airport, the 24 hours he spent in Cuba were very productive. The foreign minister met with Commander in Chief Fidel Castro, with whom he analyzed various issues of bilateral interest, and said that he was very impressed by the meeting and Fidel Castro's personality. In that respect, the visitor said that they arrived at an agreement on how to continue developing the relations between the two countries. It was agreed to take the relations between Cuba and Qatar to the ambassadorial level. Regarding cooperation, the Qatari minister said that the results had been very positive. He announced that the two countries would exchange interests in the health care sector, especially in areas of medical and paramedical personnel, medicine, and vaccines. He also stated that his country has a special interest in the area of sports advisers. Regarding the agreement signed by Foreign Ministers Roberto Robaina and Hamad Bin-Jasim Bin-Jabbur Al Thani, Cuba will open its embassy in Doha, the capital of Qatar, within the first six months of 1994; Qatar will do the same in Havana during the year. At the ceremony, Robaina described the visit of his Qatari counterpart as a point of reference to increase ties of friendship between the two countries, which need to advance on the path of cooperation and exchange. The visitor also met with Jose Luis Rodriguez, minister president of the State Committee for Finance, and expressed interest in the conditions that exist in Cuba for the investment of foreign capital and the signing of mutually beneficial agreements. The foreign minister of Qatar stated that this was the first step for possible future agreements.
