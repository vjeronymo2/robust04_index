BFN [By ITAR-TASS special correspondent Gennadiy Yezhov] [Text] St. Petersburg, 17 Apr -- The coordination of international assistance for Russian reforms, particularly the problem of creating a multilateral agency to guarantee foreign investment against non-commercial risks -- this and other issues were discussed at a closed meeting held today in the Yusupov Palace in St. Petersburg between Russian Prime Minister Viktor Chernomyrdin and delegations from the Group of Seven industrially developed Western countries and international financial organizations. Chernomyrdin noted at the meeting that the Russian leadership is firmly adhering to its policy of continuing the reforms and sees no other way forward. We have come a long way in implementing market transformations and one can already talk about certain successes, the Russian prime minister said. In the first place we have brought down inflation, and steadied it at eight or nine percent a month. Chernomyrdin thanked the Western partners for taking part in the meeting. Taking part in the meeting on the Russian side were Deputy Prime Minister Aleksandr Shokhin, Central Bank Chairman Viktor Gerashchenko, Acting Finance Minister Sergey Dubinin and other officials.
