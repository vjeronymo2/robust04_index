Language: Slovak Article Type:BFN [Text] The Hungarian Christian Democratic Movement [MKDH] republican assembly opened in Dunajska Streda this morning. It is attended by President Michal Kovac, who, in his greeting address, emphasized the importance of Hungarian political parties on our present political scene and said that difficulties arising in connection with minorities should be solved on the basis of civic principles. Equality of all citizens is the prime and basic cornerstone of democratic states and must not be questioned. The speech by MKDH Chairman Vojtech Bugar analyzed the current political scene and emphasized that the MKDH considered that one of the most important tasks is to implement [Slovakia's] administrative and territorial division and to speed up putting self-administration in the sphere of education and culture on a legal basis.
