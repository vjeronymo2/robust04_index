940507 FT 07 MAY 94 / International Company News: French crystal name taken over Lalique, one of the oldest and most famous names in French crystal making, is to lose its independence through a takeover by Pochet, another prestigious French glassmaker, writes Alice Rawsthorn in Paris. Pochet, which specialises in making bottles for sectors such as the perfume industry, already owns 9.4 per cent of Lalique, and has agreed terms with Ms Marie-Claude Lalique, the owner, to buy her 60.4 per cent stake. Lalique, founded in 1922, has sales of FFr370m (Dollars 65m).
