Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tehran, Jan. 19, IRNA -- Iran's Foreign Minister 'Ali Akbar Velayati Tuesday underlined the need for providing further relief supplies to Azeri regugees. Velayati made the remark in a meeting in Geneva with World Health Organization (WHO) Director General Hiroshi Nakajima and head of the U.N. High Commissioner for Refugees (UNHCR), Ms. Sadako Ogata. Velayati was in Geneva to take part in the meeting of foreign ministers of the contact group of the Organization of the Islamic Conference (OIC). Pointing to the measures Iran has so far made to provide over 50,000 Azeri refugees with health care and welfare facilities, the Iranian minister called on the U.N. officials to pay further attention to the health of the refugees in Azerbaijan, Afghanistan and Pakistan. Ms. Ogata termed Iran's relief supplies to Azeri and Afghan refugees as significant and appreciated the Islamic Republic's hospitable attaitude towards the refugees, Velayati concluded his Geneva talks and arrived here this morning.
