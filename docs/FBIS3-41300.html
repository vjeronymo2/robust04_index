Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] A drug trafficker's testimony is the cause of controversy in the political arena. The following is a report by Mario Espinoza, who will tell us about this and other issues such as the bid for the Cotapata-Santa Barbara road construction project and the government budget. [Mario Espinoza] The testimony of drug trafficker Meco Dominguez, which was reported by the Fides news agency and carried by newspapers in La Paz and nationwide, has caused a true political earthquake. The MIR [Movement of the Revolutionary Left] has come out to defend its party members and former President Paz Zamora by stating that [former Interior Minister] Guillermo Capobianco has called a news conference this morning in Santa Cruz and is willing to subject himself to any investigation. At any rate, we are facing a unique situation because the drug trafficker has caused a political earthquake by slandering respectable institutions such as the Bank of Santa Cruz. Moreover, MIR President Oscar Eid Franco has denied Dominguez' charges. [passage omitted] [Unidentified anchorwoman] As was reported by Mario Espinoza, drug trafficker Meco Dominguez has made charges against the private banking industry and the Armed Forces. A Fides news agency report reads, text, that for the first time a Bolivian bank has been charged in alleged involvement in money laundering activities. The report adds that the bank, which has connections with U.S. and Panamanian banks, obtained the funds from the Colombian and Bolivian drug cartels. Moreover, according to what the controversial Meco Dominguez told fiscal and police officials, the Monchego regiment has become a cocaine manufacturing center. Regarding the alleged money laundering activities conducted by the Bank of Santa Cruz, Meco Dominguez alleges he held a meeting with bank officials in 1990. He added that in 1989, through Juan Carlos Pereira Paz, bank transactions were conducted with this gentleman for the cashing of six checks for $50,000 each for the Cali cartel.
