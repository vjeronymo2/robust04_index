BFN [Report by ITAR-TASS correspondents Viktor Demidenko and Mikhail Melnik] [Text] Kiev 29 June TASS -- "The progress of elections forces me to make the conclusion that the Central Election Committee, the state-run broadcasting and other mass media, especially the parliamentary newspaper GOLOS UKRAINY, blatantly disregard the law on presidential elections, as well as parliament's resolution guaranteeing equal opportunities for electioneering and campaigning", presidential nominee Leonid Kuchma said on Wednesday [29 June] in an address to Ukraine's parliament. According to former Prime Minister Kuchma, "for the past month, Ukraine's national television has been virtually transformed into daily air time of one presidential nominee -- Leonid Kravchuk." The ex-premier is particularly concerned over the fact that "the Central Election Committee pretends not to notice these irregularities and ignores gross violations of the election procedure reported by some foreign observers." With this in view, Kuchma urged the parliament presidium "to consider the issue of disregarding the election law by the State TV and Radio Company and other central mass media, as well as the political stand taken by the Central Election Committee." The former prime minister also lodged a protest with the Central Election Committee, demanding that resolute measures be immediately taken to implement the law on presidential elections, which provides for equal opportunities for candidates to carry out election campaign.
