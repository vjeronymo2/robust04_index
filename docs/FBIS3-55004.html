Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Report from the PRAVDA, ITAR-TASS column: "Estonian President Unhappy"] [Text] Estonian President L. Meri has sharply criticized B. Yeltsin's New Year address, in which the Russian president advocated that the interests of the 25 million Russian citizens living in the republics of the former Soviet Union be better protected. In an interview with AP, Meri claimed in particular that: "The words and tone used by Yeltsin could be evidence of how weak democratic forces in Russia are. So Russia has chosen the path of utilizing the aggressive policy typical of the former USSR."
