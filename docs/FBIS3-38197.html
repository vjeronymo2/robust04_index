Language: Urdu Article Type:BFN [Text] In Davos, the prime minister has discussed multi-billion dollar investment projects in Pakistan with some industrial and investment teams from around the world. The prime minister's discussions centered around the construction of the (Lawari Tob) tunnel, to link Pakistan with Central Asia through Tajikistan; the establishment of rail and road links between Pakistan and Central Asia, to enhance global trade; setting up an industrial estate in Pakistan; and financing private thermal power units in Pakistan. The Swedish prime minister, who met with Ms. Benazir Bhutto in Davos yesterday, discussed the expansion of bilateral cooperation. He expressed interest in possible investment in the (Lawari Tob) tunnel project, because this could greatly expand global trade with the regions of the former Soviet Union. Ms. Benazir Bhutto invited Swedish investors to invest in power plants in Pakistan. Singapore Senior Minister Lee Kuan Yew, during his meeting with the prime minister, emphasized his country's strong interest in investing in rail and road projects between Pakistan with Central Asia. Ms. Benazir Bhutto offered to let Singapore establish an industrial estate in Pakistan to help its industrial growth. Both leaders agreed to identify an effective means for transferring technology from Singapore to Pakistan.
