Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Mexico City, 3 Jan (AFP) -- The Mexican Army has 12,000 soldiers who are "trying to repel the uprising" of Indians of the Zapata National Liberation Army (EZLN), initiated on 1 January in the southern State of Chiapas, the newspaper ULTIMAS NOTICIAS reported this Monday. The Armed Forces have reinforced their troops with 6,000 soldiers in the area of conflict, where there were already a number of soldiers prior to the EZLN-launched offensive, the newspaper stated, not citing its sources. This report has not yet been officially confirmed. According to Chiapas authorities, the guerrillas "are just a few," possibly some 400, but estimates made by journalists who have traveled to the region list the EZLN members at between 800 and 1,000. A guerrilla has told the press that his troops consist of approximately 5,000 men. The area of conflict is located approximately 700 km south of Mexico City.
