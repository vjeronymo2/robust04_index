BFN [Text] Tehran, June 14, IRNA -- The Islamic Republic of Iran has pardoned German spy Helmut Szimkus, who had been arrested here in 1989. Foreign Minister 'Ali Akbar Velayati in his meeting with German Chancellor Helmut Kohl in Bonn informed him of the pardon. Szimkus had been condemned to death by a court after his trial. German Chancellor Kohl and Foreign Minister Klaus Kinkel had previously appealed for the Szimkus' release. A diplomatic source said after the appeal, related authorities reviewed the case in light of the principled and realistic stances of the Bonn government vis-a-vis Iran, and Szimkus was eventually pardoned. His pardon is expected to open a new chapter in Tehran-Bonn relations despite the impediments made by the U.S. and Britain.
