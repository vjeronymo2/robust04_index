August 28, 1990, Tuesday, P.M. Final

PEOPLE'S PHARMACY: ANTIVIRAL DRUG CAN EASE HERPES PAIN, AVERT LESIONS

Question: When we got married 20 years ago, I had herpes. We were careful, but 10 years later my husband caught it.

My case has gradually improved to the point that I now have an outbreak only every other year. But my husband suffers terribly with an episode at least once a month.

Before he came down with genital herpes, we were extremely happy. Since then, he has not touched me -- no kiss, no hug, no tender word. He has abandoned me emotionally, spiritually, financially and physically. We have separate bedrooms and bank accounts.

Herpes has been an unbelievable evil in our lives. Is there any hope for a cure?

Answer: Although it is unfair, your husband seems to be blaming you for his misery. Unfortunately, there is still no cure for herpes, but it may be possible for him to control his outbreaks.

Zovirax (acyclovir) is an effective antiviral medication that can prevent lesions when it is taken on a daily basis. These prescription pills have revolutionized the treatment of genital herpes. They can also speed healing of cold sores or fever blisters caused by herpes 1.

Your husband should discuss his problem with a dermatologist or urologist. In addition, both of you would perhaps benefit from marital counseling to help overcome the years of bitterness caused by a viral infection.

Q. Both my daughter and I have had problems with malodorous perspiration. The odor is very difficult to launder out of clothing.

We found the solution in Prevention magazine, and want to pass it along. Zinc gluconate, 20 mg., prevents smelly perspiration for us. Perhaps others will benefit as well.

A. In Germany, zinc compounds are included in deodorants to prevent unpleasant smells from sweat. We have been unable to locate any scientific studies verifying that oral zinc supplements can have this effect. However, the dose of zinc that you are taking is within a safe range, and as far as we know, is not likely to cause any complications.

Foods that are rich in this mineral include whole grains, especially wheat germ, seafood, brewer's yeast and meat.
