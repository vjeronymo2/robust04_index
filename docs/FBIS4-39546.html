BFN [By ITAR-TASS special correspondents Tigran Liloyan and Mikhail Shevtsov] [Text] Yerevan, 8 Jun -- Russian Defense Minister Pavel Grachev, who arrived in Yerevan today, met Armenia's President Levon Ter-Petrosyan this evening. He received the consent of the head of this state to the establishment of Russian military bases on Armenia's territory. Pavel Grachev said that the final calculation of the number and armaments of Russian troops in Armenia will be agreed upon at by experts tomorrow. The Russian minister said that an agreement on Russian military bases in Armenia will in all probability be signed by 1 September of this year. He said that, as is intended, the motorized rifle division in Gyumri and the morotized rifle regiment in Yerevan will remain in Armenia. The general made it clear that it is intended to supply more equipment to the Russian troops with more to make them mobile in the air. Levon Ter-Petrosyan agreed that within the framework of ensuring CIS collective security, it is necessary to set up an air defense system on Armenia's territory. Touching on the agreement on a cease-fire and the disengagement of troops in the zone of the Armenian-Azerbaijani conflict, Pavel Grachev said that Armenia's president confirmed his readiness to keep to the accords reached. Tomorrow, the Russian military delegation will continue its work in Armenia, will visit Russian military facilities, and will fly to Tbilisi in the evening.
