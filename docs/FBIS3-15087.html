Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Armenian forces in Nagorno-Karabakh came under bombardment from Azeri troops both last night and Tuesday morning despite the recent ceasefire agreement Stepanakert and Baku had signed with mediation from Russian Defense Minister Pavel Grachev. The agreement is effective from March 1. Three Armenian soldiers were wounded as the Azeri army mounted artillery fire in the north of the Martuniskiy Region, the press office of the Karabakh Defense Forces told Interfax. The Azeri fire was not returned because of the ceasefire agreement, it said. However, Armenian forces reserve the right to retaliate if Azerbaijan continues to violate the agreement, Chief of Staff of Karabakh Forces Seiran Oganian said.
