BFN [Text] Managua, 22 Apr (ACAN-EFE) -- Today, Friday, the Nicaraguan Government Ministry confirmed it has ordered the arrest of U.S. citizen John Hull, a naturalized Costa Rican citizen. Hull has several criminal charges pending in Costa Rica and is believed to be in Nicaragua, although his whereabouts are unknown. Today, an official spokesman said the police began an investigation to locate the 74-year old Hull, who is charged with attacking anti-Sandinist guerrilla leader Eden Pastora in a place called La Penca on the Nicaraguan border 10 years ago. Hull, who is also accused of drug trafficking and supplying arms to the former Nicaraguan contras, fled Costa Rica to the United States several years ago, according to Costa Rican newspaper sources. Today, BARRICADA published a report that Hull arrived in Nicaragua on 17 February through El Espino customs checkpoint on the Honduran border and that he is currently living on a cattle ranch near Juigalpa in the central Department of Chontales. Even though police authorities have confirmed Hull's arrest warrant, a Nicaraguan Foreign Ministry spokesman told ACAN-EFE that the Costa Rican Government still has not requested extradition of the American, who is now a naturalized Costa Rican citizen. On Monday, Costa Rican President Rafael Angel Calderon Fournier reported that his government sent a note to the U.S. authorities requesting Hull's extradition.
