Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Suwon, March 2 (YONHAP) -- Amid a dry weather alert, three mountain fires broke out in Kyonggi Province on Monday [28 February], destroying an area covering 264 hectares. A blaze that started on a mountain in the demilitarized zone in Torasan-ri, Changso-myon, Paju-kun at 4:00 PM engulfed a 260-hectare area in 20 hours before dying out at 11:30 PM. During the fire, some 50 mines buried in the DMZ exploded, witnesses said. When the fire became stronger around noon, army posts to the south of the Military Demarcation Line started a counter blaze to keep the flames from spreading further. In Towon-ri, Chongun-myon, Yangpyong-kun, the province's second mountain fire of the day broke out at 2:25 PM, destroying an area of 3.5 hectares. The blaze was brought under control in three hours, thanks to a Forestry Administration helicopter and 100 villagers. The third fire started on a mountain in Chinjung-ri, Choan-myon, Nam Yangju-kun at 6:00 PM and destroyed 0.5 hectares in about an hour.
