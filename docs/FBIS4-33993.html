BFN [Excerpts] As of 14 June, President Juan Carlos Wasmosy will make his debut in Ibero-American summits. The chief executive traveled today to Cartagena, Colombia, where he will ask for the other nations' support for the nascent Paraguayan democracy. Even though Paraguay has no diplomatic ties with Cuba, the chief executive said he would meet with Fidel Castro. Paraguayan President Juan Carlos Wasmosy left this morning for the summit of presidents and heads of state of Ibero-American countries scheduled in Cartagena, Colombia. This will be the fourth Ibero-American summit. Former President Andres Rodriguez represented our country in the three previous Ibero-American summits which were held in Guadalajara, Mexico; Madrid, Spain; and Salvador de Bahia, Brazil. [passage omitted] Foreign Minister Luis Maria Ramirez Boettner, as well as congressmen from various parties, make up President Wasmosy's entourage. Wasmosy will stay at the Cartagena Hilton. The official delegation will participate in various forums through 15 June. Their return has been scheduled for noon on 16 June. [passage omitted]
