Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] Buenos Aires CLARIN in Spanish on 7 March publishes, on pages 18 and 19, a 1,200-word article about the "enthusiastic reaction" by the Argentine Economy Ministry to the Brazilian economic program and to the meeting between Brazilian Finance Minister Fernando Henrique Cardoso and Foreign Minister Celso Amorim with Argentine counterparts Guido Di Tella and Domingo Cavallo in Buenos Aires on 9 March. The article also comments on how Argentina will profit if the Brazilian economy becomes stable and the Brazilian level of consumption increases. It also comments on the political meaning of the success of the economic program. The article states that Minister Cardoso will become a potential presidential candidate for the October elections and run against leftist leader Luiz Inacio Lula da Silva. The article quotes the positive reactions of several current and former Argentine officials to the plan, stating that they believe "the effects of this stabilization will become visible in six months." It concludes by stating that conservative Brazilians are seeking an "anti-Lula candidate" and that if his plan proves successful, Fernando Henrique Cardoso will be an "excellent" presidential candidate.
