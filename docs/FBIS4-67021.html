BFN [Report by Grania Langdon-Down, Crime Correspondent, PA News] [Text] The largest seizure of cocaine in the UK, valued at 250 million pounds sterling, was found in drums of bitumen on its way from South America to Poland, it was disclosed today. The 1,300 kilos of particularly high purity cocaine were discovered in a consignment of 48 drums by officers who searched a ship when it called in at Birkenhead, Merseyside, on January 24. Customs officers removed the cocaine and substituted other drums on board the Polish-American ship the MV Jurata, which was en route from Venezuela to Poland, and tracked it to Gdynia hoping to identify the smugglers, chief investigations officer Douglas Tweddle said today. One man is being interviewed by Polish police after the drums were delivered to a warehouse on the outskirts of Warsaw on February 3, but customs officers were not able to identify the Western European figures behind the deal. Mr. Tweddle said: "This massive seizure confirms intelligence reports that the South American `narco barons' are targeting cocaine shipments on Western Europe and are seeking to use South American countries as a back door. "We are delighted to have been able to work with the Polish authorities to inflict this major blow on these drug traffickers." Customs believe some of the cocaine would have filtered on to the streets of the UK. Forty officers were involved in Operation Ayala, named after a racehorse which ran in the Grand National. Customs identified the shipping route from South America to former Soviet states as a likely path for cocaine shipments. Based on that intelligence they decided to carry out an intensive search of the MV Jurata's load. The crew of the ship were not involved and the substitute drums were sent on unnoticed. It is the second large seizure of cocaine being routed by the South American cartels to Poland. UK customs tipped off Polish authorities about another consignment which led to 520 kilos of cocaine being seized in Poland. Suspicion had fallen on 48 barrels of bitumen found in the drums. The first one was genuine but the other 47 had inner containers each packed with up to 30 kilos of cocaine. They had been insulated to protect the drugs from the heat of the bitumen. Mr. Tweddle said in early January they had been able to identify a separate consignment travelling direct from South America to Poland which enabled the authorities there to seize 520 kilos of cocaine. The two operations meant the authorities had prevented two tonnes of cocaine with a UK street value of 400 million pounds sterling flooding on to the western European market. "There is little doubt some of it would have ended up in Britain, probably via Germany and Holland. We hope this success will act as a catalyst for further ones in the future," Mr. Tweddle said at the Customs House in London. He said the Jurata was a "tramp steamer" which had stopped off at Birkenhead to unload some legitimate cargo. It had then stopped at Hamburg before completing its journey to Poland. The seizure was the largest, not only in the UK, but also in central and eastern Europe. There have been bigger hauls in Spain and Holland. "With the collapse of the former Soviet Union, the Customs services in central and eastern Europe are not particularly well developed and traffickers see places like Poland and Russia as an easy back door to western Europe," he said. The previous biggest UK haul -- 907 kilos -- was discovered two years. [sentence as received]
