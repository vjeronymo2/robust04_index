Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] San Cristobal de las Casas, 8 Jan (NOTIMEX) -- Catholic Bishop Samuel Ruiz Garcia accepted here today a proposal made by the so-called Zapatist National Liberation Army to mediate the conflict that has been going on in this southeastern region of Mexico since 1 January. "I accept the proposal to serve as a mediator," said the bishop of San Cristobal de las Casas, Chiapas State, in reference to a communique issued by the armed group published by the Mexican newspaper LA JORNADA on Saturday. Official reports indicate that nearly 100 people have been killed and an undetermined number injured since the armed group occupied several towns of the region known as Los Altos de Chiapas last Saturday. Bishop Ruiz Garcia talked about "the urgent need to take specific steps toward a true consolidation of peace" and said that in these times of "sorrow and hope" we must act "with responsibility." In the communique the armed group proposed a "public and written" dialogue with the government with the mediation of Rigoberta Menchu, the Guatemalan Nobel Peace Prize winner, Mexican journalist Julio Scherer, director of PROCESO magazine, and Ruiz Garcia.
