930430 FT 30 APR 93 / International Company News: AIG shares close to yearly peak SHARES in American International Group, the leading US insurance group, edged towards a 52-week high yesterday afternoon in a declining stock market as investors registered their satisfaction with the company's first-quarter performance. At mid-session, the stock was up Dollars 6 1/4 at Dollars 126 1/8 in active trading. For the first three months of 1993, AIG's net earnings rose 12.8 per cent to Dollars 495.9m, or Dollars 2.34 a share, compared with Dollars 439.6m, or Dollars 2.06, a year earlier. Excluding accounting changes and realised capital gains, AIG's income rose 15.8 per cent to Dollars 452.7m, or Dollars 2.13, from Dollars 390.9m, or Dollars 1.83, last year. Revenues advanced 6.4 per cent to Dollars 4.64bn from Dollars 4.36bn. Mr Maurice Greenberg, chairman, said operating income before realised capital gains and income taxes rose 16.9 per cent in the quarter. The company's US general insurance operations posted reasonable results in spite of several catastrophes. AIG's general insurance business recorded pre-tax income of Dollars 351.8m, up 14.1 per cent in the quarter, excluding realised capital gains.
