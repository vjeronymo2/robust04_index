Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, Feb. 24 KYODO -- Honda Motor Co. will try to boost exports from its U.S. Ohio transplant this year by 80 percent from last year to 75,000 vehicles, positioning it as the largest auto exporter in the United States, a company official said Thursday [24 February]. "We will be increasing exports to Japan of our Accord and Civic models from Honda of America Manufacturing Inc. Our decision was backed by strong demand in Japan, as well as by the appreciation of the yen against the dollar," the official said. The decision was also made in line with the commencement of sales in Japan on March 1 of Ohio-made Accord wagons and Accord coupes, he said. Honda of America's top export regions will be Japan, followed by Taiwan, Europe, Israel and Latin America. A few more countries in Latin America could be added to the U.S. unit's current list of 28 export countries, bringing the total as high as 32, the official said.
