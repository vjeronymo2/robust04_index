Language: Hebrew Article Type:BFN [Report by 'Aqiva Eldar, Aluf Ben, Lili Galili, and news agencies] [Excerpts] Damascus has issued exit visas to all the 1,000 Jews who remained in Syria, and they are now allowed to leave the country if they so wish, Yosef Jatti, the deputy chief rabbi of Syria, stated yesterday. Diplomats said this was yet another gesture of good will on the part of the Syrian Government in a bid to improve its relations with the United States and promote the peace process in the Middle East. [passage omitted] Foreign Minister Shim'on Peres stated yesterday that Israel still needs a gesture on the part of the Syrian president that will prove the seriousness of his intentions with regard to peace. "If we are talking about confidence-building measures, then these measures are too small to build the necessary confidence," Peres commented yesterday. "The release of Jews or the willingness to issue them passports are nothing new. We would like to see something more serious, along the lines of a public declaration such as: `I want to make peace with Israel,' as al-Sadat did; or conversely, a visit to Israel or public negotiations on all levels," Peres said.
