Language: English Article Type:BFN ["Abolition of `National Security Law' Is Demand of Times " -- KCNA headline] [Text] Pyongyang, March 18 (KCNA) -- NODONG SINMUN in a by-lined article today urges that the Kim Yong-sam traitorous clique should look squarely at the trend of the times and scrap the ill-famed "National Security Law [NSL]" without delay as demanded by the people at home and abroad. The "NSL" in South Korea is an anti-communist fascist law of the worst kind in history, the article says, and goes on: It is an anti-national, anti-reunification evil law that defines the same fellow countrymen as the enemy and totally denies national unity. It is a fascist law that most ruthlessly tramples and cracks down upon the people's desire for independence, democracy and reunification in South Korea. The "NSL" defines all the organizations and population of the northern half of the country, the General Association of Korean Residents in Japan (Chongnyon) and Koreans in Japan and all the progressive overseas Koreans living in different parts of the world as "anti-state organizations" and "members of anti-state organizations" and regards them as objects of "punishment". It is a crime and a disgrace of the nation to have such anti-national, anti-reunification evil law like the "NSL" that denies the same fellow countrymen. Terrorism, murders, unwarranted arrests and killing tortures against dissident political forces and guiltless people committed by fascist dictators in South Korea up until now were "legalized" as a whole under the notorious "National Security Law". Unless the ill-famed "NSL" is abolished in South Korea, the independence and democratization of society cannot be realized, nor can the reunification of the country be achieved, nor can human rights abuses be uprooted in South Korea. The Kim Yong-sam clique of traitors, however, are intending to maintain the notorious "NSL" in a bid to incite North-South confrontation and block the reunification of the country and to further strengthen their "civilian" fascist rule in South Korea. Kim Yong-sam is, indeed, a traitor to the nation who is pursuing the treacherous policy against the nation, reunification and democracy more maliciously than any preceding dictators of the successive military dictatorial "regimes" did in South Korea.
