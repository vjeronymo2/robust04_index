931211 FT 11 DEC 93 / Sacked printers lose test case Sacked workers at JW Arrowsmith, the Bristol printing company which has been picketed for nearly eight months, have had their claim for unfair dismissal rejected by an industrial tribunal. About 120 staff - the majority of the workforce - were dismissed in April in a dispute over pay and conditions. Three of the printers, supported by their union the GPMU, brought what was regarded as a test case to the tribunal. After a nine-day hearing the tribunal ruled it did not have jurisdiction because the three had been offered their jobs back. Arrowsmith had offered to re-employ the workers at their previous pay, but said it would no longer recognise the union. It had said it could not afford to meet the demand for a Pounds 6.50 a week pay rise and the workers were dismissed after taking industrial action. Mr John Price, GPMU branch officer, said yesterday that the picketing would continue while the situation was reassessed. 'Obviously we are disappointed we have lost the first round,' he said. 'We will be taking legal advice and the general secretary will decide how to progress the dispute. Even at this late stage, we want a negotiated settlement.'
