BFN [Text] Warsaw, 21 Apr--A 48-hour strike in all lignite mines, organized by the Solidarity trade unions, began at 0700 hours. Four hours after the protest started, Miroslaw Maksymiuk. of the national load-dispatching unit [krajowa dyspozycja mocy], told a PAP journalist: "Nothing is happening. We are meeting the entire demand for electricity. If it stops coming from lignite-fired power stations, we still have other sources of energy." The extraction and consumption of lignite dropped in recent years from 73.8 million tonnes in 1988 to 66.8 million in 1992. Power and heat and power-generating plants located near lignite mines are using approximately 98 percent of all extracted lignite.
