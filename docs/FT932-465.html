930629 FT 29 JUN 93 / Power chiefs take large pay rises THE CHIEF executives of National Power and PowerGen, the generating companies, received substantial pay awards last year, according to accounts released yesterday. Mr John Baker, who runs National Power, had his basic pay increased from Pounds 246,250 to Pounds 267,750. In addition he received a Pounds 64,260 performance-related bonus. Including other benefits and pensions his package totalled Pounds 393,803, an increase of 6.2 per cent on the previous year. Mr Baker was also granted a further 93,525 share options at 264p a share. The annual report says that Mr Baker's bonus followed a year in which the total return to shareholders including capital gains and dividends, was 84 per cent. National Power shares closed at 368p last night, up from 242p a year ago. Mr Ed Wallis of PowerGen received a 10.2 per cent rise in his basic pay to Pounds 312,802, and his total package including benefits and pensions rose to Pounds 362,476. The average rise for National Power employees from January 1 was 3.95 per cent for a 15-month period. PowerGen workers last year got a flat 5.5 per cent increase, while for this year they have accepted a 2.5 per cent rise plus one-off lump sums related to productivity.
