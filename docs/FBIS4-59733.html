BFN [Text] Tehran, May 14, IRNA -- The visiting Kazakh Prime Minister Sergey Tereshchenko and Vice-President Hassan Habibi here Saturday described Tehran-Almaty relations as friendly and growing since Kazakhstan's independence. The Kazakh official said that the objective of his three-day official visit to Tehran is to remove the obstacles in the way of promotion of bilateral ties. He expressed hope that mutual cooperation in industial and agricultural fields as well as in air and marine transport would further expand and letters of understanding be signed in the said areas. The Kazakh premier noted that establishment of the joint chambers of commerce and exchange of views on bilateral trade will be among other topics he will discuss with Iranian officials. In reply Habibi said that said that grounds had been provided for the expansion of Tehran-Almaty economic, social and cultural relations in exchange of visits between President Akbar Hashemi Rafsanjani and his Kazakh counterpart Nursultan Nazarbayev in Tehran. He said that a number of agreements had also been inked by the two sides, adding that during its stay here the Kazakh delegation can pave the way for implmentation of the said agreements.
