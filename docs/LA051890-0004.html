May 18, 1990, Friday, Home Edition

PROPOSITION 111 PROS AND CONS AND ENDING TRAFFIC GRIDLOCK

Your May 14 editorial ("Making a Case for Car-Pool Lanes") chastises Orange County for not enacting a "transit tax" as in neighboring Riverside County. Orange County voters have twice rejected a sales tax increase, and may reject Prop. 111 due to specific projects it would fund.

The last I heard, we live in a democracy and it is our prerogative to vote for or against a measure based on merit.

Car-pool lanes have proved controversial, if not unpopular. Caltrans and the Orange County Transportation Commission steadfastly refuse to permit an A-B comparison study of mixed-use and car-pool lanes in such key areas as traffic efficiency, cost-effectiveness and safety.

Give Orange County resident some credit. We see gridlocked freeways, comparatively empty car-pool lanes, and plenty of self-serving statistics.

A no vote on Prop. 111 will send a message to the transportation mavens that "enough is enough."

RICHARD DEIGHT

Buena Park
