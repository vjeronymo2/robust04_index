Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Interior Minister Hasan al-Alfi has stated that a study is under way to set up special funds to provide life insurance for police officers. He emphasized that the ministry provides medical and social care to all policemen and to the families of martyred policemen. He noted in this regard that two hospitals had been built in Asyut and Tanta to offer services to policemen in the Delta and Upper Egypt regions. Al-Alfi pointed out that the current security strategy seeks to modernize the police department by supplying it with modern equipment, weapons, and technology, step up security patrols, and lift the morale of policemen. The minister denied that the Palestinian organization Hamas [Islamic Resistance Movement] is involved in terrorism in Egypt. He noted that Palestinian defendant Samir Isma'il Salih joined the terrorists in his personal capacity and that he is not connected with Hamas. Al-Alfi stressed that efforts are being made to extradite the remaining terrorists abroad, adding that so far 23 terrorists have been repatriated following intensive contacts with the countries they were in.
