October 1, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

'DO YOU HAVE A TABLE FOR TWO AT 8?'

The restaurant business, people in the business will tell you, is terrible. They blame it on the stock market, on the baby boom, on the changes in the tax laws.

But our almost-annual survey tells a different story. Every year or so we sit down at 4 o'clock on Friday afternoon and call restaurants all over town asking for a table for two at 8 that night.

The first time we did it we discovered that tables were available in all the top of the line expensive restaurants while the hip new grills and cafes simply laughed at the notion that a table might be available on such short notice. A year and a half ago things were different: At 4 o'clock on a Friday afternoon not a single restaurant in town turned us down. While many did not have a table at 8, most were able to accommodate us between 7:30 and 8:30.

Things have changed. These days if you want to make last minute plans for the weekend, you'd best be prepared for dinner at 6 or 10, because that's when a lot of local restaurants are going to ask you to eat. If you've got your eye on Saturday, things get even worse: only a quarter of the restaurants we called on Friday were able to offer us a Saturday night table at a reasonable hour.

Other than reaffirming the fact that Los Angeles loves a new restaurant (and especially a new and reasonable restaurant), few patterns emerged. The top of the line expensive restaurants are doing well, old favorites are doing well, new favorites are thriving. Los Angeles restaurants, in fact, are cooking.
