920407 FT 07 APR 92 / World Stock Markets (America): Oils feature trading as Dow climbs Wall Street US STOCKS posted good gains yesterday afternoon in thin trading, with most of the strength coming from the oil sector, writes Karen Zagor in New York. The Dow Jones Industrial Average of 30 blue chips finished 26.38 higher at 3,275.49. New York SE volume, however, was too light, at 179.9m shares, to indicate an overwhelming improvement in market sentiment, and on the big board advancing issues led those declining by 1,036 to 678. On Friday the Dow closed at 3,249.11, up 14.99. A slightly stronger gain was posted by the more representative Standard & Poor's 500, which added 4.04 at 405.59, while the Nasdaq composite of over-the-counter stocks moved more sharply higher, by 6.28 to 596.29. Energy issues dominated big board trading. Exxon and Texaco were among the most active stocks, moving ahead Dollars 1 7/8 to Dollars 57 3/4 and Dollars 2 1/8 to Dollars 61 1/8 respectively. Oil shares started to rally on Friday on the back of firming crude oil prices and positive comments by Merrill Lynch, which estimated that crude could rise to Dollars 24 a barrel by the end of the year if uncertainties over supply from unstable regions such as the former Soviet Union and Venezuela continue. Chevron firmed Dollars 7/8 to Dollars 67 3/8 in spite of reports that its Port Arthur, Texas, oil refinery might be shut down for two weeks following an equipment failure at the 180,000 barrels-a-day operation. Among other newsworthy oil companies, Amoco added Dollars 1 1/8 at Dollars 44 3/4 and Union Carbide firmed Dollars 1/8 to Dollars 24 3/4 after fires broke out in a number of Texas City plants. Amoco has said its operations were unaffected by the fire. Pacific Gas and Electric slipped Dollars 1/4 to Dollars 29 1/4 , Unocal climbed Dollars 1 1/4 to Dollars 22 3/4 and oil-services company Schlumberger rose Dollars 3 7/8 to Dollars 58 3/8 . Wal-Mart, the extremely successful Arkansas-based retail chain, climbed Dollars 1 1/4 to Dollars 52 7/8 in very active trading on news of the death of its founder. Amerada Hess rose Dollars 2 7/8 to Dollars 42 on unusually heavy volume after several analysts upgraded their ratings on the stock. India Growth Fund fell Dollars 1 1/8 to Dollars 23 7/8 after the fund changed its exchange rate for translating rupees into dollars. As a result, the fund's net asset value was devalued by about 20 per cent. USF&G jumped Dollars 1 to Dollars 9 1/4 after Kidder Peabody increased its rating on the insurance stock to 'buy' from 'hold'. In over-the-counter trading, Intel forged ahead Dollars 3 1/8 to Dollars 57 7/8 , Apple Computer advanced Dollars 1 3/4 to Dollars 60 3/4 and Microsoft added Dollars 3 3/4 at Dollars 121 1/4 . Canada SHARP GAINS in oil issues countered losses in banks, leaving Toronto flat after a day of slow trade. Escalating tensions between Iran and Iraq and a jump in IPE May Brent crude above an important resistance level of USDollars 19 per barrel boosted the oils sector. The composite index closed just 0.4 up at at 3,385.9. Falls led rises by 287 to 227 after volume of 19.6m shares. Olympia & York's debt troubles fuelled selling in bank shares while lifting Home Oil on speculation that the oil and gas company will be sold at a premium to the market price. Home Oil, controlled by O & Y, has been up for sale for about two years and the latter has said for over a year that it has had off-and-on talks with potential buyers. The former climbed CDollars 1 1/2 to CDollars 16 1/2 , a 52-week high. SOUTH AFRICA was closed yesterday for a public holiday and will re-open today.
