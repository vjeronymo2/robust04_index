BFN [Text] Prague, June 15 (CTK) -- The situation in the Central and Eastern European countries and the future policy and meaning of the Conference on Security and Cooperation in Europe (CSCE) was discussed at the three-day 27th meeting of CSCE High Commissioners which ended today. The participants discussed the role of CSCE missions in the countries of the former Soviet Union, namely Nagorno-Karabakh, South Ossetia and Georgia and in ex-Yugoslavia, as well as the presence of Russian troops in the Baltic states, Piotr Switalski from the Secretariat of the acting chairman of the CSCE High Commission told journalists. The commissioners also discussed the Budapest meeting to be held this December and other CSCE activities such as cooperation with the United Nations and the European Union.
