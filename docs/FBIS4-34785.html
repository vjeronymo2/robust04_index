BFN [Report by Jaime Guerrero from the "Early Morning" newscast] [Text] President Carlos Salinas says the reformation of Article No. 27 puts an end to the great Zapatist revolution. He said the struggle for land is over and now the battle is for production and democracy. The president distributed common land [ejido] titles and confirmed the legality of the documents. Thus far this year, 600,000 peasants have received land titles, thanks to an extensive statistical effort to establish boundaries. In Aguascalientes, dozens of peasants gathered to hear President Salinas deliver a message of reconciliation. [Begin Salinas recording] The men and women of the countryside no longer want to exhaust themselves in conflicts. The struggle for land is over, and the victors are the Mexican peasants. [end recording] The president said an effort will be made to prevent conflicts from eroding agrarian reform. He told the peasants who received titles that their agrarian certificates puts an end to the Zapatist revolution. The EZLN's lands are legally secure, but that is not enough in one of the most backward sectors of the economy. [Begin Salinas recording] A struggle is now being waged for the peasants to make their own decisions regarding production, organization, democracy, and freedom. [end recording] The president's tour was devoted to land ownership. In Matamoros, Tamaulipas, the ceremony was very symbolic. Among the people who gathered were 11 peasants who descended from the group to whom Lucio Blanco in 1913 presented the first plot of land resulting from the Mexican Revolution. Eighty-one years later, they have titles for their plots. [Begin Salinas recording] We have fulfilled the pledge of the government of the Mexican Revolution. [end recording] In this border city the president confirmed his pledge to defend the country's sovereignty and independence.
