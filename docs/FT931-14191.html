930120 FT 20 JAN 93 / Alexander Shokhin says Moscow attempting free trade agreements with other CIS states by February 1 Mr Alexander Shokhin, the Russian deputy prime minister for foreign economic relations, said that Moscow was currently trying to conclude a series of free trade agreements with the other members of the CIS by February 1, after which date it would levy export taxes, writes Leyla Boulton from Moscow. He added that he expected agreement to be reached at the Minsk summit on a commonwealth bank which would then be able to act as a clearing house for inter-republic trade.
