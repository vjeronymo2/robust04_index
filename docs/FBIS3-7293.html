Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, March 27 KYODO -- Japan's major commercial banks will provide full-scale long-term loans for China in collaboration with international lending organizations to develop the country's infrastructures such as power stations and petrochemical facilities, the NIHON KEIZAI SHIMBUN said Sunday. Local affiliates in Hong Kong of six major banks -- Fuji Bank, Sanwa Bank, the Bank of Tokyo, Sumitomo Bank, Mitsui Trust and Banking Co., the Long-Term Credit Bank of Japan -- will take part in April in a 380 million dollar joint financing led by the Asian Development Bank to the People's Bank of China as key members, the business daily said. The six Japanese banks and other foreign commercial banks will provide a total of 78 million dollars in cofinancing with the 15- year loan. The loan will carry an interest rate of that of libor, or London interbank offered rate, plus 0.7 percent, the NIHON KEIZAI said. The Bank of Tokyo has also decided to participate in 15-year joint loans totaling 470 million dollars initiated by the World Bank to the Chinese Government as a key managing bank, according to the paper.
