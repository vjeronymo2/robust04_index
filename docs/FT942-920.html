940627 FT 27 JUN 94 / Observer: Talent spotted One more tale to add to the Queens Moat Houses' collection. When the bankers to the heavily-indebted hotel group were scrabbling around last year wondering what to do as deadlines approached, they decided in desperation to call in an accountancy firm specialising in corporate breakdowns. Imagine the bankers' horror on finding that most of the experts had departed for the Society of Practitioners of Insolvency annual junket - in Melbourne. Fortunately, Alan Griffiths, head of 'corporate recovery' accountants Grant Thornton, was still manning his phone. He got the job while his competitors got sunburn. Barclays, the lead bank, stresses that Griffiths always had been its first choice. 'We had our fingers crossed that he would be there.' Of course.
