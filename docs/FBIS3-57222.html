Language: Ukrainian Article Type:BFN ["Ukraine's Law on Intensifying Criminal Responsibility for Some Crimes Against the State" issued in Kiev on 24 Dec 93] [Text] In order to intensify the protection of the constitutional system and territorial integrity of Ukraine, as well as public security, Ukraine's Supreme Council resolves as follows: Articles 56-1, 62, and 71 will read as follows: "Article 56-1. Actions aimed at changing by force or overthrowing the constitutional system or seizing state power, as well as conspiracy to commit such actions -- "are punishable by imprisonment of between five and 15 years with confiscation of property. "Public calls for changing the state power by force or overthrowing state power, as well as dissemination of materials calling for such actions -- "are punishable by five years' imprisonment or a fine of up to 100 times the minimum wage. "Actions specified by Part 2 of this article that are committed by a representative of power, repeated, or committed by a group of people as a conspiracy -- "are punishable by imprisonment of between three and eight years"; "Article 62. Encroachment upon Ukraine's territorial integrity: "Deliberate actions committed for the purpose of changing the territory or state borders of Ukraine in violation of the procedure established by the Ukrainian Constitution, as well as public calls or dissemination of materials with calls to commit such actions -- "are publishable by imprisonment of up to five years. "The same actions, if committed by a representative of power or by a group of people as a conspiracy, or if they involve incitement of ethnic or regilious hostility -- "are punishable by imprisonment of between three and 10 years. "Actions specified by parts 1 and 2 of this article that have resulted in loss of life or other grave consequences -- "are punishable by imprisonment of between 10 and 15 years"; "Article 71. Mass disorders: "The organization of state disorders, which involve violence against the individual, pogroms, arson, destruction of property, resistance to representatives of power with the use of weapons or other objects used as weapons, or active participation in mass disorders -- "is punishable by imprisonment of between two and 12 years. "Such actions that result in loss of life or other grave consequences -- "are punishable by imprisonment of between 10 and 15 years." [Signed] Ukrainian President L. Kravchuk [as published, laws are usually issued by the Supreme Council and signed by its chairman Ivan Plyushch] [Dated]Kiev, 24 December 1993
