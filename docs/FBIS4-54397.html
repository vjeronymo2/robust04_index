BFN [Text] Sofia, April 26 (BTA) -- Mr. Valentin Karabashev, deputy prime minister and minister of trade, today received Mr. 'Ali Akbar Torkan, Iran's minister of roads and transport, the government Press Office told BTA. Mr. Torkan is visiting here at the head of an Iranian delegation for the 9th session of the Bulgarian-Iranian Commission for Economic, Scientific and Technological Cooperation which opened yesterday [25 April]. The two ministers focused on bilateral trade and payments. The guest emphasized that Iran has problems in repaying its debt to Bulgaria in hard currency. Mr. Torkan suggested repayment in oil, gas and industrial goods of a value equal to Iran's obligations to Bulgaria. Mr. Karabashev stressed that the finances of most of Iran's Bulgarian creditors are now in a rather bad state. They will count on getting their money back from Iran in hard currency. Later in the day, Mr. Torkan was also received by Deputy Prime Minister Evgeni Matinchev. Mr. Matinchev was briefed on the results of the 9th session of the Bulgarian-Iranian Commission for Economic, Scientific and Technological Cooperation. The guest reiterated Iran's desire to repay its debt to Bulgaria in industrial goods and oil.
