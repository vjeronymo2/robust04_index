BFN [Text] Beijing, May 29 (XINHUA) -- Both China and Russia have expressed their satisfaction over the "effective cooperation" in political, economic, scientific, cultural, military spheres and in maintaining public order over the past years and wished to expand and strengthen their cooperations in all these fields. This was stated in a press communique on Russian Prime Minister V.S. Chernomyrdin's China visit issued today. The prime minister left Dalian for home late today after concluding his four-day official visit to China, during which he also made his stay in Beijing and Shanghai. The prime minister met with Chinese President Jiang Zemin and held talks with Chinese Premier Li Peng, extensively exchanging views on bilateral relations and some international issues of mutual interest. The visit has turned out to be quite a success, the communique says. Both sides which have promised to stick to the principles laid down in the joint statement on December 18, 1992, are determined to set their eyes on the 21st century to move the Sino-Russian relations to a new high. The 1992 joint statement provides the base for Sino-Russian relations. It is a common belief that the maintaining of a long-term, stable and good neighborhood relations characteristic of mutual beneficial cooperations accords with the fundamental interest of the two countries and peoples and constitutes an important factor in promoting peace, stability and development in the Asia-Pacific region and the world as a whole, the communique says. Advancing the cooperations in economic, trade and scientific and technological fields is the main theme of the talks between the leaders. While acknowledging the positive achievements in the fields over the past years, both sides held that there are huge potentials in developing the Sino-Russian economic ties and trade, bases on the advantages of bordering each other and the supplementary economies of the two countries. In this aspect, the two sides agreed to participate in the construction of some bilateral and multi-lateral projects, including "continental bridge" linking Europe and Asia. During the visit, the two countries signed seven cooperation agreements, including those on border management, marine transport, natural resources protection and fishery resources protection in the water areas bordering the two countries. The two countries will also continue to strengthen their military technology-related cooperation under the conditions of sticking to and abiding by international obligations as agreed by the two countries, the communique says. The two sides gave positive appraisal to the ongoing negotiations on borders and on cutting military presence in the border areas and enhancing mutual trust in the military field and held an agreement on the latter will be of important significance to enhancing mutual understanding and mutual trust between the two countries and to maintaining peace and stability in the Asia-Pacific region. China will stick to the provisions provided in the agreement on the eastern section of the Sino-Russian border while being willing to continue the talks on other left-over border issues and speeding up the preparation for the agreement on western section of the borders. In exchanging views on reforming the economic structures, the two sides shared quite a few common points of view with regard to the process of the reform. Despite the different conditions of the two countries, introductions of each other's theories and practice in the economic reform will be of mutual benefit. On the current international situation and some major international issues, leaders of the two countries held identical or similar views in many aspects, agreeing to reinforce constructive cooperations in regional and international affairs and to trigger mutual promotion in participating the Asia-Pacific economic cooperation. Both sides agreed to continue and develop the positive dialogues at all levels including those at the highest level. In this aspect, the Russian prime minister invited Chinese Premier Li Peng to pay an official visit to Russia in 1995, which was accepted by Li Peng with pleasure.
