BFN [Text] The prime minister, Mr. P.V. Narasimha Rao, has left New Delhi this morning for the United States. He was seen off at the airport by his cabinet colleagues, chief ministers of Andhra Pradesh, Haryana, and Madhya Pradesh, the governor of Uttar Pradesh and senior officials. The seven-day visit will take Mr. Narasimha Rao to four cities, including the capital Washington and will enable him to meet a broad segment of the Clinton Administration, congressmen, and business circles. Our correspondent, M. Rama Rao, covering the visit, says the main thrust of the prime minister's talks will be on enhanced economic and investment cooperation between the two countries. India will also reiterate its position on sensitive issues like Nuclear Nonproliferation, arms control, human rights, and Kashmir to the U.S. leaders. The Clinton-Rao talks slated for Thursday are expected to be open and frank, giving the leaders an opportunity to appreciate each other's point of view on world issues as also matters of mutual concern. A high-level official delegation is accompanying the prime minister. It includes the finance minister, Dr. Manmohan Singh; the minister of state for external affairs, Mr. Salaman Khurshid; and the minister of state in the prime minister's office, Mr. Bhuvanesh Chaturvedi. A 21-member trade delegation drawn from the three apex bodies of Indian industry is also accompanying the prime minister to help forge enhanced Indo-U.S. economic relations. The highlights of the visit include an address by Mr. Narasimha Rao to a joint meeting of the U.S. Congress and the lecture at the prestigious Harvard University.
