Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [From "The Voz Andes" newscast] [Excerpt] President Sixto Duran-Ballen has reiterated that despite protests by government officials, the economic measures will not be changed. Duran-Ballen said he will not change his mind about the measures. He said that if the Court of Constitutional Guarantees determines the measures are unconstitutional, the government will appeal the decision to the Supreme Court of Justice, which will have the final word. The Court of Constitutional Guarantees will submit a final report on the demands presented by three political parties regarding the unconstitutionality of the economic measures adopted by the government. When questioned about the protests, Duran-Ballen stated the government will not allow tantrums, and he said emergency measures will be taken if deemed necessary. [passage omitted]
