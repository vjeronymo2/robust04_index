940329 FT 29 MAR 94 / Arts: Today's Television Today's semi-final in the Young Musician Of The Year competition is for string players (6.55 BBC2). You could switch to Channel 4 at 9.00 and stay for four and a half hours. Without Walls begins with a report on Britain's cultural heritage as seen by Europeans who live here 'but whose affection for England and the English doesn't stop them from taking a clear-eyed look at our strengths and weaknesses'. Given our supposed inadequacies in cuisine, dress sense, and sex, it will be interesting to see what strengths we are allowed. The second half (9.30) sustains the European theme: Isambard Kingdom Brunel returns from the dead to inspect the Channel Tunnel. That is followed by Betty Blue, a 1986 French movie about love and obsession, noted for its sexual explicitness. This version is the director's cut which means three hours instead of two. The longer version is said to be slower and less amusing (10.00). In Omnibus the admirable Patricia Routledge plays Sister Hildegard, a 12th century nun, scientist, painter, and composer (10.25 BBC1).
