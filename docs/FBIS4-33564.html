BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on Guatemalan economic developments monitored through 7 June. The Guatemalan Congress has refrained from approving the renegotiation of the country's debt with the Paris Club. This decision places the country in a precarious position and could even close the doors to future international loans. Guatemalan Christian Democracy and Solidarity Action Movement deputies blocked a proposal in favor of the renegotiation. (Guatemala City PRENSA LIBRE in Spanish 1 Jun 94 p 79) The Central Bank of Guatemala has reported that between January and May 1994 the country's foreign exchange income rose by 18.7 percent. The country's foreign trade contributed greatly to this increase. Sales of traditional products increased 26.5 percent, despite declines in cotton and banana sales. Meanwhile, sales of nontraditional products increased 14.4 percent. (Mexico City NOTIMEX in Spanish 0256 GMT 7 Jun 94) Finance Minister Ana Ordonez has reported that during the first trimester of the year, Guatemala's fiscal deficit increased by some $52.2 million. The minister said the fact that taxpayers failed to pay taxes on time contributed to the shortfall. (Panama City ACAN in Spanish 1841 GMT 3 Jun 94)
