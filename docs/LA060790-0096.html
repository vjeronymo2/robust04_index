June 7, 1990, Thursday, South Bay Edition

SPORTSCOPE;

HEAT PLAYS SALT LAKE CITY TO OPEN ROAD SWING

The Los Angeles Heat, in fourth place in the Southern Division of the American Professional Soccer League with a 4-2 record, play the Salt Lake City Sting Saturday at Salt Lake City.

The game is the first of four road games in eight days for the Heat, which is 2-0 on the road. The Heat play the Colorado Foxes Sunday, the Seattle Storm June 15 and the Portland Timbers June 16.

Three teams qualify for postseason action, but because the APSL scoring system rewards teams that score goals, the Heat is 15 points behind front-running Real Santa Barbara. L.A. has scored only 11 goals, lowest in the league.

Goalkeeper Mike Littman has kept the Heat in contention by giving up only five goals, the lowest total in the league.

Heat forward Jeff Hooker, a member of the 1984 U.S. National Team that tied defending World Cup champion Italy, 0-0, said the United States will do well against Italy in their match Thursday at the World Cup. "Italy is very fast, very skillful and very powerful," Hooker said. For the United States to win, Hooker said it must utilize a man-to-man, trapping defense that "doesn't give Italy time to play its game."
