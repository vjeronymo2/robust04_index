July 21, 1990, Saturday, Ventura County Edition

THOUSAND OAKS COUNCILMAN'S INCONSISTENCY

I am puzzled by the inconsistent views of Councilman Larry Horner regarding the need to maintain business character in Thousand Oaks versus the need to maintain residential character.

He recently stated, "I'm going to be hard-nosed. . . . I'm not going to change the character of the business community because of advertising."

Why, then, is he willing to change the character of residential neighborhoods because of rooming houses, which bring blight, traffic, drugs and crime?

Horner's proposal, as chairman of the Code Enforcement Committee, will reduce residential zoning standards in violation of the city's ordinances that apply to occupancy levels, street parking, fire codes and infrastructure. His proposal does not solve the existing problems with illegal multiple rentals. In the name of "solving the overcrowding and enforcement problems," his proposal will increase density, increase street parking and encourage rooming houses to spring up in family neighborhoods.

I hope homeowner associations and concerned citizens will not be deceived by slogans but will read the fine print.

MARIE KOERNER

Thousand Oaks
