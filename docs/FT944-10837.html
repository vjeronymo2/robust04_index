941109 FT 09 NOV 94 / Ashdown urges end to donations to Tories Mr Paddy Ashdown, leader of the Liberal Democrats, yesterday sought to exploit the disillusionment of businesspeople with the government by asking companies to stop making political donations to the Conservative party. Repeating a thought which has been expressed privately by many industrialists, Mr Ashdown said: 'I do not believe you can say any longer that this is a government which has been good for business.' Mr Ashdown said at the CBI conference: 'Just as the TUC is beginning to recognise that too close a link to one political party is not necessarily in their interest, I hope that Britain's business community will begin to reassess the extent to which unquestioning support, including financial support, for the Conservative party is in their interest.' Seeking to tap the sentiments in favour of strengthening the European Union, manifest in CBI polls published earlier this week, Mr Ashdown urged the business community to speak up for its own interests. He said: 'It will be disastrous for Britain and business if we are excluded from a single currency. And if that's what you believe too, it's time to be saying it loud and clear.'
