February 7, 1989, Tuesday, Orange County Edition

THE PREPS;

GIRLS' ATHLETE OF THE WEEK;

LUNCEFORD'S HELPING FOOT BOOSTS TRABUCO HILLS SOCCER

The leading scorer for Trabuco Hills High School girls' soccer team is a dead ringer for Linda Lunceford, who led Esperanza to the 3-A finals last year.

That's because it is Lunceford.

Her family moved from Anaheim to Rancho Santa Margarita because her mother, a Methodist minister, was assigned to open a church in the area.

It was as if Lunceford, a junior, had gone from the Space Age to the Stone Age. Esperanza is 8-0 in Empire League play and has clinched the league title. Trabuco Hills, which was dismal last year, is 3-3 in Pacific Coast League play and struggling to make the playoffs.

Though mediocre by Esperanza's standard, Trabuco Hills' record is a definite improvement over last season. It is due in part to first-year coach Tony Cornfoot and Lunceford.

Lunceford, The Times' athlete of the week, leads the Mustangs in scoring with 29 goals in 15 games. Last Thursday, she scored four goals in a 5-0 victory over Laguna Beach.

"She will always score goals because she has a natural instinct to be in the right place at the right time," Cornfoot said.

Cornfoot, who coached soccer professionally in England, said Lunceford is one of the best girls he has seen and is so powerful that she can strike a ball as well as any boy.

As a coach in England, he has seen some good ones. "The English ladies won the World Cup in Italy last year, and she could fit into the ladies' English soccer team with no problems," Cornfoot said.

Making the transition from Esperanza has been difficult for Lunceford, who was an All-Southern Section 3-A soccer team selection as a sophomore. All she has known is success. She is an outstanding shortstop for the Ballhawks, the third-ranked club softball team in the nation. She also plays for the Shooting Stars, the No. 1 club soccer team in California.

"Mainly, I just kept telling myself we'll get better, we'll improve. I just felt that I had to work with it," she said.

Things have gotten better. The first half of the season, the Mustangs lost to Orange, 6-3. Two weeks ago, Trabuco Hills met Orange again. Orange was 4-0 in league play. But Trabuco Hills defeated the Panthers, 2-0, on Lunceford's two goals.

TOP PERFORMERS

Keri Erkenbrack scored 52 points and collected 18 rebounds in two Century League victories for Canyon (17-4, 8-0) last week.

Tustin's Teresa Perkins scored 39 points in Sea View League overtime victories against Estancia and University last week. She scored the winning basket against University.

In a 5-0 victory over Katella, Michelle Rullo scored the hat trick, getting her 16th, 17th and 18th goals for Esperanza, the No. 2-ranked girls' soccer team in 4-A.
