BFN [ITAR-TASS correspondent Vladimir Taranov] [Text] Moscow, 20 Jun (ITAR-TASS) -- Vladimir Lukin, chairman of the Russian State Duma Committee for International Affairs, thinks that Russia should "more closely coordinate" its actions with Washington on the issue of a possible introduction of sanctions against the DPRK. This issue was discussed today at a closed session of the Duma Committee for International Affairs. The DPRK's ambassador extraordinary and plenipotentiary to Russia participated in the meeting. Vladimir Lukin told the ITAR-TASS correspondent that the discussion showed that "the North Korean position is still not quite clear." According to him, the DPRK "is either trying to `bargain' for better conditions, or gain the time necessary to conclude work on the creation of a nuclear weapon."
