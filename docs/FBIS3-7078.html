Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, March 24 KYODO -- Private railway unions on Thursday [24 March] threatened a 24-hour strike Friday affecting more than 22 million people if they fail to resolve their differences with management over demands for a wage increase. The companies are asking their unions to accept a benchmark monthly wage increase of 10,800 yen, but the unions are pushing for a 4 percent increase of 11,600 yen. Late Thursday evening it was still unclear whether last-minute talks would avert a strike. Bus services by nine companies and train services by 12 companies could affect some 22,100,000 people. In the Kanto region centered on Tokyo they include the Tobu, Tokyu, Keisei railway lines and lines operated by Teito Rapid Transit Authority, Keio Teito, and Sagami Railway, and in the Chubu region centered on Nagoya, the Nagoya Railroad Union. In the Kansai region centered on Osaka and Kyoto they include the Hankyu, Hanshin, Keihan, and Nankai railway lines, and in Kyushu the Nishi Nippon Railroad lines.
