June 15, 1990, Friday, Home Edition

STANLEY CROUCH'S EAST L.A. COLLEGE ROOTS

In The Times' recent piece on the black writer Stanley Crouch he is quoted as saying he is "a graduate of no college." I believe that to be true, and I also believe it to be true, as the quote implies, that Crouch is largely self-taught and has achieved his present eminence in literary circles as a result of his own abundant energy and talent.

However, in 1965 and 1966 he completed 29 1/2 units at East Los Angeles College, including courses in composition, literature, and creative writing.

Of course I don't now what value he places on those courses, but I do know that we in the English Department recognized and nurtured his talent.

In the 1965 edition of the college literary magazine, Milestone, we published 14 pages of Crouch's poetry, citing him for "consistently high quality in his portfolio of poems." This had never been done before and has not since.

In addition, Stanley won first and second prize for poetry in our then semi-annual writing contest and wrote the introduction to that edition of Milestone, a still-impressive and lovely affirmation of the artist's role in contemporary society.

SAFFORD CHAMBERLAIN

Associate Professor of English (retired)

South Pasadena
