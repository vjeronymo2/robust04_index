Language: Russian Article Type:CSO [Unattributed article: "Omsk Oblast Increases Export of Goods"] [Text] According to the data of the Omsk Oblast Administration of Statistics, the oblast exported $335.4 million worth of goods in 1993, a 75.8 percent increase as compared with 1992. Specifically, export deliveries of the petrochemical complex comprised 83.1 percent of the overall volume of export (1.8 million tonnes). This included 15,600 tonnes of phthalic anhydride (base for dyes) and 20,600 tonnes of synthetic rubber. Timber materials were exported in the amount of 6,400 cubic meters. While in 1992 barter deals accounted for 30 percent of the export, in 1993 they accounted for 20 percent. The relative share of export deliveries in the overall volume of goods produced comprised 9.9 percent, while in 1992 it was 6.8 percent. Around 80 percent of the export deliveries were made under contracts concluded by the enterprises of Omsk Oblast directly with the foreign partners. The developing countries accounted for 53 percent of the export deliveries, while the industrially developed countries, and primarily Germany, accounted for around 30 percent. Import comprised $115 million. Great Britain (20 percent) and Germany (14 percent) were among the primary suppliers. A large part of the import was comprised of sugar (19 percent of the overall volume of import) and equipment (17 percent).
