Language: English Article Type:BFN [by ITAR-TASS correspsondent Albert Kochetkov] [Text] Tbilisi January 11 TASS -- The command of the "Mkhedrioni" (Horsemen) Georgia's most powerful paramilitary organisation, nominally loyal to Eduard Shevardnadze, have decided to stockpile their firearms. Longi Chelidze, a "Mkhedrioni" leader, told reporters on Tuesday that any member of the organisation refusing to obey the order to give up his weapon will be reported to the law enforcement bodies of the republic. By January 15, all weapons should be collected and sealed in dumps. The operation has been already completed on Tuesday in the republic eastern regions of Kakheti and Rustavi. Not long ago the "Mkhedrioni" units, playing a big role in the Georgian political drama, got the status of a governmental structure called "Georgia's Rescuers". Local observers believe the "Mkhedrioni" leaders decided to get rid of weapons in a bid to evade any accusations of illegal activities against the members of the organisation. The "Mkhedrioni" were founded in 1989 by Djaba Ioseliani, once a supporter of the late ex-president, Zviad Gamsakhurdia, who later inspired the downfall of the first popular-elected president of Georgia. In November 1990, Ioseliani told ITAR-TASS That the "Mkhedrioni" had been created to prevent ethnic conflicts and preserve the republic's integrity. The "Horsemen" actively participated in the Georgian coup d'etat of December 1991-January 1992, combat operations in Abkhazia and western Georgia on the side of the government troops. Experts assess that "Mkhedrioni" consist of some three thousand well-equipped gunmen, possess armour and modern means of liaison.
