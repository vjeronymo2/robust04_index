BFN [Text] Tehran, May 4, IRNA -- Pakistan regards the assassination of former Iranian cultural attache to Pakistan, Sadeq Ghanji, a great crime and is trying to arrest the murderer as soon as possible, said the visiting Pakistani minister of interior, Nasrullah Khan Babar. Babar was referring to the recent escape of Ghanji's murderer from police custody in Pakistan. Talking to the reporters upon arrival here Wednesday, Babar said that his delegation would discuss with Iranian officials issues of mutual interest including campaign against drug trafficking along Iran-Pakistan joint borders. Iran's minister of interior, 'Ali Mohammed Besharati, who greeted his Pakistani counterpart at the airport, said that the two countries would explore avenues to intensify campaign against drug trafficking. He further said that Iran was in the frontline of anti-drug campaign, adding that further cooperation between Iran and Pakistan would be fruitful in this regard.
