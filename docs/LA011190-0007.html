January 11, 1990, Thursday, Home Edition

CISNEROS CASE

In response to a letter (Dec. 28) by Debra J. Reed, I would like to point out that Ms. Reed takes out of context a quote of mine from an article ("Deals Won Jail Informant Freedom to Attack Again," Dec. 11). In the first place, there were no "deals" made with Stephen Cisneros on the Bittaker case.

A letter accurately stating what Cisneros did for Sheriff's Homicide in dealing with the key witness in the case was written at the request of Cisneros' defense attorney. The letter was not written as the result of a "deal" but as the result of a legitimate request by Cisneros' defense attorney to present those facts to the judge who was to sentence Cisneros on an unrelated case.

My statement that Cisneros was a "small, teeny little offshoot" of the Bittaker case (not of any informant program) was said to put the incident into perspective. The Bittaker case was a very complex case which took a year for investigation and preparation. Cisneros played a small role in the total case and was never used as a witness. I certainly feel bad that Cisneros went on to become a rapist but, frankly, it is quite evident that Cisneros would have become a rapist whether or not the letter was written.

STEPHEN R. KAY

Assistant Director, Bureau of Branch and Area Operations

Office of the District Attorney

Los Angeles County
