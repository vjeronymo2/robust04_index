Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities in Brazil through 24 February: The Brazilian Petroleum Corporation, Petrobras, today announced a new national oil production record: 723,063 barrels per day. This is mainly due to the production of three new wells in Campos Basin, on the northern coast of Rio de Janeiro State. (Brasilia Voz do Brasil Network in Portuguese 2100 GMT 17 Feb 94) Brazil saved nearly $6.5 million as the result of a cut in electricity demand countrywide during the daylight savings program. (Brasilia Radio Nacional da Amazonia Network in Portuguese 0900 GMT 18 Feb 94) The Brazilian Institute of Geography and Statistics reports that the GDP increased by 5 percent in 1993, mainly due to industrial activity. The services sector closed the year with 3.5 percent growth, while farming activities dropped by 1.9 percent. The manufacturing industry grew the most, registering 10.1 percent. Activities in the services sector increased mainly due to the increase of trade, communications, and transport. (Brasilia Voz do Brasil in Portuguese 2100 GMT 19 Feb 94) Plane tickets increased 18.1 percent today. This is the third increase this year. Plane tickets increased by a total of 128.58 percent so far this year. (Brasilia Radio Nacional da Amazonia Network in Portuguese 1000 GMT 21 Feb 94) Commercial exchange with Common Market of the South, Mercosur, members has gone from $4.5 billion to $8 billion in barely three years. Brazilian exports within Mercosur increased 35 percent and imports 58 percent. Brazilian regional investments will total $150 million this year. (Sao Paulo O ESTADO DE SAO PAULO in Portuguese 21 Feb 94 p B2) A study conducted by the Interunion Department for Statistics and Socioeconomic Studies shows that the purchasing power of Brazilian workers dropped 55 percent in 1993 compared with 1 March 1990 when the Collor Plan suspended the monthly salary readjustment, causing the biggest drop ever in the country's recent history. (Sao Paulo GAZETA MERCANTIL in Portuguese 21 Feb 94 p 6) The National Confederation of Industries on 18 February reported that Brazilian industry sales grew 7 percent in 1993 compared with the previous year. The total salaries paid to employees increased 2.4 percent, whereas the average installation capacity went from 70.9 percent to 74.2 percent. (Sao Paulo GAZETA MERCANTIL in Portuguese 21 Feb 94 p 7) Federal Government debt in securities increased by $4.3 billion in January. The record exchange balance of $3.5 billion was the main cause. (Rio de Janeiro O GLOBO in Portuguese 22 Feb 94 p 30) National Privatization Program Chairman Andre Franco Montoro Filho on 22 February reported that the Brazilian Government auctioned 25 companies valued at $6,447 billion in the 30-month period since the privatization program was implemented. (Madrid EFE in Spanish 2217 GMT 22 Feb 94) The Brazilian Capital Market Institute has reported that net profits of foreign companies working in Brazil increased 488.6 percent in the first nine months of 1993 compared with the same period in 1992. (Sao Paulo O ESTADO DE SAO PAULO in Portuguese 23 Feb 94 p 35) Fuel prices were hiked by 18.5 percent on 24 February, thus registering an accrued increase of 88.17 percent so far this year. A liter of gasoline now costs 334 real cruzeiros. (Brasilia Radio Nacional da Amazonia Network in Portuguese 1000 GMT 24 Feb 94)
