Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Cairo, 27 Feb (MENA)--The Supreme Military Court today continued to hear the defense plea in the case of the attempt to assassinate Prime Minister Dr. 'Atif Sidqi, which involves 15 defendants, including six being tried in absentia. The lawyer for the sixth defendant, 'Isam Muhammad 'Abd-al-Rahman, called for the examination of all the defendants to verify the claim that they were assaulted. He said that he joins the other lawyers in demanding that the court disqualify all the testimonies attributed to the defendants. Husayn Jabir Muhammad, the defense lawyer for the 11th defendant, Tuhami Ahmad 'Abdallah, called on the court to find his client not guilty for various reasons, including the invalidity of the arrest and search warrant because it was issued two hours after the accused were arrested and the invalidity of the testimonies and admissions attributed to him because they were taken under coercion. [passage omitted] The court adjourned until tomorrow, when it will continue to hear the defense case.
