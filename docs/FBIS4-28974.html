BFN [Text] Pyongyang, June 14 (KCNA) -- Solidarity messages came to the Central Committee of the Workers' Party of Korea from Indrajit Gupta, general secretary of the National Council of the Communist Party of India, and from the National Standing Committee of the National Convention Party of Ghana supporting the Korean people's struggle to defend their national dignity and the sovereignty of their country. The general secretary of the National Council of the Communist Party of India in his message said the council highly estimates the DPRK's stand against U.S. pressure and for the resumption of talks with the United States to solve the "question of nuclear inspection" of the DPRK. The National Standing Committee of the National Convention Party of Ghana in its message actively welcomed and supported the DPRK's proposal to hold negotiations with the United States over the establishment of a new peace arrangement that can prevent arms buildup and creditably ensure peace on the Korean peninsula. The National Convention Party of Ghana expresses firm solidarity for the struggle of the Workers' Party and people of Korea to achieve national reunification through a confederacy, the message said, and urged the United States to accede to the DPRK's negotiation proposal to replace the Korean armistice agreement by a peace agreement and to withdraw its troops and nuclear weapons from South Korea.
