920302 FT 02 MAR 92 / UK Company News: Fleming Income raises Pounds 92m Fleming Income & Capital Investment Trust has raised Pounds 92m in its offer for subscription, indicating that investors recent enthusiasm for investment trust launches has yet to be sated, writes Philip Coggan. The trust, like many other recent offers, had a split capital structure, and was linked to a Personal Equity Plan (PEP). Income and capital gains made within a PEP are free of tax. Fleming said it received applications for 25.7m package units (comprised of one ordinary income share and one zero prerefence share) and 45.5m ordinary income shares. In addition 20.5m package units, 200,000 ordinary income shares and 45.8m zeroes have been placed with investors. PEP applications were received in respect of 18.1m package units and 38.6m ordinary income shares. Investors can place Pounds 6,000 into an investment trust new issue PEP in a tax year. This is double the allowance that can be put in an existing investment trust and the result has been a burst of new issue activity in the sector.
