BFN [Text] Moscow June 18 TASS -- President Yeltsin visited today a special crack police division of the Interior Ministry troops (the former Dzerzhinskiy Division). On the occasion of the 70th anniversary of the creation of the division, the commander-in-chief viewed the parade of the division's personnel and the performance of the special task force. The president was invited to have lunch at the soldiers' canteen and had a conversation with servicemen. He was accompanied by Interior Minister Viktor Yerin and Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev. The history of the division is inseparably connected with the history of Russia and Moscow, its capital. Several generations of officers and men, serving in the division, fulfilled their duty with dignity. Nineteen of them were awarded the titles of the hero of the Soviet Union and hero of the Russian Federation, thousands were awarded orders and medals. Yeltsin sent today a congratulatory message to soldiers, sergeants, non-commissioned officers, officers and veterans of the division. "By successfully carrying out your tasks, especially during the troublesome recent years, you contributed a great deal to the preservation of civil peace in the country. I am confident that you will continue to ensure public security, acting jointly with other branches of the Interior Ministry, in the interests of the consolidation of the Russian state," the message said.
