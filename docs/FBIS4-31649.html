BFN [Text] Podgorica, 13 Jun (TANJUG) -- Montenegrin Prime Minister Milo Djukanovic met Albanian Prime Minister Aleksandar Meksi in Durres today. According to a statement issued by the Montenegrin Ministry of Foreign Affairs, the two prime ministers discussed bilateral relations and pointed out the importance of continuing the dialogue and the need for improving goodneighborly relations and cooperation, which are vital for the stability of the region. The resolution of the Yugoslav crisis was also discussed, and the need was stressed for a peaceful and just solution to the conflict based on the consensus of all warring sides. Future bilateral economic, cultural, scientific, and other cooperation was also discussed. The Montenegrin side was also represented by Deputy Prime Minister Krunoslav Vukcevic and Foreign Minister Miodrag Lekic.
