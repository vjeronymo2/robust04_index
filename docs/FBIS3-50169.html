Language: Slovak Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Following his meeting with Foreign Minister Jozef Moravcik and the subsequent signing of the memorandum on cooperation between UNIDO [United Nations Industrial Development Organization] and Slovakia, UNIDO Director General Mauricius de Maria y Campos was received this morning by Slovak President Michal Kovac. Jozef Rebo has the details: [Rebo] [passage omitted] Slovakia is ready for bilaterally advantageous cooperation with UNIDO. Regarding specific matters, the president said that Slovakia would welcome concrete assistance from UNIDO, in particular in economic transformation. Privatization is also closely linked to economic transformation. We also expect assistance in putting into effect environmental projects and new technologies. On the other hand, Slovakia wants to contribute to UNIDO work and get involved in assistance to Third World countries. We are able to join mining and metallurgy projects, projects in the construction industry, engineering, timber-processing, chemical, and electrotechnical industries. In reply, Mr. Campos said that UNIDO respected Slovakia's efforts in the sphere of industrial development and that UNIDO wished to continue to support our efforts. [passage omitted] Mr. Campos then met representatives of Slovak business circles, and, in the afternoon, he will visit Slovak Power Engineering Works in Tlmace.
