Language: Persian Article Type:BFN [Text] The joint naval maneuvers of the Islamic Republic of Iran and Pakistan in the northern water of the Indian Ocean ended last night. The Central News Unit reports from Islamabad that in the last phase of these maneuvers, defense operations against air threats and attacks on amphibian and submarine units were carried out. The participating units displayed the most modern tactics in these exercises. The four-phase maneuvers included four warships, several naval reconnaissance planes, and antisubmarine helicopters of the Iranian Navy as well as five warships, one submarine, and several planes and helicopters from the Pakistani Air Force. The plans for the joint maneuvers were drawn up by the Iranian Navy and the Pakistani Navy undertook the executive management.
