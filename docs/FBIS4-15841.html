BFN [Text] Riga, Apr 11, BNS -- Latvia's Prime Minister Valdis Birkavs maintains that the U.N. secretary-general should receive comprehensive and correct information on the development of Latvian-Russian relations, Birkavs told reporters Monday [11 April] on return from Geneva. Birkavs met with Butrus Butrus-Ghali April 9 in Geneva and introduced him to the Latvian situation in view of the agreement on troop withdrawal with Russia and President Boris Yeltsin's decision to establish bases in the CIS countries and Latvia. The officials also discussed the alleged human rights violations in Latvia. In addition, Birkavs invited Butrus-Ghali to visit Latvia. The visit could take place in the second half of this year, VIA [expansion unknown] said.
