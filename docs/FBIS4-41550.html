BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondents Oleg Velichko and Gennadiy Yezhov] [Text] Moscow June 27 TASS -- The inflow of foreign investments in the Russian economy will reach 10-15 billion American dollars per annum by the year 2000, according to First Deputy Economics Minister of Russia Yakov Urinson. He spoke at a meeting of the Consultative Council on Foreign Investments under the Russian Government which opened in Moscow's President Hotel today. The meeting is being attended by the leaders of 14 major transnational corporations of the world. "So far the annual growth rate of foreign investments has been recorded at a low level, Yakov Urinson said. This year, for example, the total amount of investments reached a mere one billion dollars, less than in Estonia. Russia is behind such countries as Poland, Hungary and some others as regards foreign investments. As of June 1, 1994, only 14,000 enterprises with foreign capital has have been recorded on the Russian state register. Nonetheless, its attempts to lure cautious Western investors Russia scored tangible results in stabilising its economic conditions, Urinson said. Economic restructuring is being carried out along with demilitarising the Russian economy -- over the past two years and a half the military order was cut by 70 per cent, inflation does not exceed 10 per cent a month over the past five months. More than 40 per cent of the Russian labour force are employed in private enterprises, and the rouble's buying capacity against the dollar has increased by 12 per cent over the past five months of this year. Enterprises with foreign capital have created approximately 300,000 new jobs, productivity has increased by 1.5 to two times on average as compared to similar domestic enterprises, Urinson said.
