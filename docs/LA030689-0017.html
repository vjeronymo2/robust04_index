March 6, 1989, Monday, Home Edition

'BAD SCENE IN SOUTH TEXAS'

Helton's polemic against the INS typifies blatant bias parading as reason. He would substitute for the "low-level functionaries" of the INS (an agency guilty of having a "law enforcement mentality) some low-level functionaries of the Justice Department. Is it because he thinks the difference lies in the fact that the Justice Department does not have a "law enforcement mentality"? Or is it perhaps the fact that the low-level functionaries of the Justice Department might be more inclined toward his political biases than are those of the INS?

JACK ISKEN

Encino
