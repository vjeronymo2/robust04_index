921118 FT 18 NOV 92 / International Company News: India's ITC up 28% in first half ITC, the diversified Indian group with interests in cigarette manufacturing, hotels and agribusiness, has reported improved profits and foreign exchange earnings for the first half to September, maintaining its status as India's leading exporter. The Calcutta-based company, in which the BAT Industries of the UK has a 32 per cent stake, reported higher turnover of Rs18.03bn (USDollars 635.75m), compared with Rs13.61bn a year earlier. Export sales rose to Rs2.97bn from Rs2.24bn, and gross profits advanced to Rs1.25bn from Rs1.01bn. Taxable profits moved up by 22 per cent to Rs1.15bn from Rs951m and net profits were 28 per cent ahead at Rs660m, against Rs517m. The improvement came in spite of a five-month industrial dispute at its Tribeni Tissues paper div-ision. The company says it will continue to give the highest priority to exports. It has set itself a target of achieving Dollars 1bn in international sales by the year 2000.
