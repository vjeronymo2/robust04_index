Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The State Duma has approved temporary regulations defining the procedure for electing the speaker, deputy speakers and their powers. According to the regulations, the parliament elects one first deputy speaker and four deputy speakers. The Duma Council consists of the parliamentary speaker and the heads of the parliamentary factions and deputies' groups. The council outlines a program for the session's work, a one-month timetable for consideration of issues included in the agenda and sends draft laws and resolutions to parliamentary committees. The chairman of the chamber informs the deputies of all decisions made by the council. The State Duma may annul any decision made by the council. The Duma Council holds daily sessions. Any deputy of the State Duma is entitled to attend to the council's meetings.
