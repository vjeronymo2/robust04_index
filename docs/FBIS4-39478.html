BFN [Text] Ukrainian President Leonid Kravchuk today received DPRK Foreign Minister Kim Yong-nam. [Unidentified correspondent] The Ukrainian president warmly greeted the DPRK's delegation led by Foreign Minister Kim Yong-nam. The DPRK was one of the world's first countries to recognize Ukraine's independence and to establish diplomatic relations with it. During a conversation, Leonid Kravchuk informed the Korean guests of the present situation in Ukraine and stressed that it is stable. Out state's president noted that political battles are being fought in Ukraine, but added that there is no acute confrontation. On the issue of Crimea, Leonid Kravchuk reiterated it is being settled within the framework of constitutional methods. The Korean side expressed interest in developing cooperation with Ukraine. Issues of mutual interest to both states were discussed. Ukrainian Foreign Minister Anatoliy Zlenko took part in the conversation. The DPRK delegation, led by Foreign Minister Kim Yong-nam, was today also received by Oleksandr Tkachenko, first deputy chairman of the Ukrainian Supreme Council, who told the guests about the activity of Ukraine's new democratically elected parliament. The quests were acquainted with the specialist work being carried out by the Ukrainian Parliament, which is fatefully entrusted with resolving many problems in order to pull the economy out of a crisis, and improve the social security of Ukraine's population at a hard time of market reforms. I note that agreements on trade, economic, and scientific and technological cooperation were concluded between Ukraine and the DPRK last year. The Korean side today takes interest in signing such interstate documents as a treaty on friendship and cooperation, agreements on cultural relations, legal assistance, and other documents which would promote bilateral ties. Anatoliy Zlenko and Borys Oliynyk, chairman of the Ukrainian Supreme Council Foreign affairs and CIS Relations Commission, took part in the conversation.
