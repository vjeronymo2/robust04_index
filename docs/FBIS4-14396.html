BFN [Report by unidentified correspondent in Tehran on 29 April] [Text] On his arrival at the Mehrabad Airport in Tehran, the deputy prime minister of Bosnia-Herzegovina said: The Muslim nation of Bosnia-Herzegovina continues to appreciate the support and assistance by their brothers and sisters in Iran and will never forget this affection. We believe that Iran is our best friend and supporter in today's sorrowful crisis. Referring to the condition in his country, Edib Bukvic stressed: After 20 days of aggression and the massacre of thousands of our compatriots, the war in Gorazde has relatively subsided. He warned: We believe that the Serbs are preparing for renewed attacks in northern and southern Bosnia-Herzegovina. The world today is amazed and saddened at the silence and indifference of the West and of Europe, who continue to profess the defense of freedom and human rights. The Bosnian deputy prime minister asserted: Of course we rely on God, our people, and the support of friendly and fraternal countries such as Iran, but we insist upon the lifting of the arms embargo on Bosnia-Herzegovina, so we can resist this unjust aggression and war, and defend our homeland and beliefs.
