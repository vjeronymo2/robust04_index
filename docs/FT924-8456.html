921111 FT 11 NOV 92 / Renewal offer to MMI clients LOCAL authorities unable to obtain insurance cover in the wake of the problems of Municipal Mutual (MMI), the UK's ninth largest insurer, are to be offered new facilities by Zurich Insurance. The giant Swiss company has been in negotiations with MMI over the past month, Richard Lapper writes. Up to 300 local authorities, whose policies come up for renewal between October and the start of January, could be insured by Zurich. Some 31,000 individual policyholders are also being offered renewals by the Swiss company. MMI, which has incurred heavy losses on its liability business, withdrew from the insurance market in September, leaving a number of local authorities temporarily without insurance. MMI insured nine out of 10 local councils but other UK companies have been reluctant to offer cover. However, Mr Brian Wright, group chief executive, said talks with Zurich were 'going well'. MMI hopes to be able to transfer most of its business to the Swiss company, which would also employ a large number of the 2,000 staff. Zurich is understood to be reluctant to assume any liabilities arising on policies already underwritten by MMI. Mr Dennis White, chief executive of Zurich Insurance UK, said: 'There are some complex issues involved which will yet need to be resolved but we are making genuine progress.'
