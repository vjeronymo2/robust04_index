Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, Jan. 27 KYODO -- Japan has disbursed a total of 2 million dollars to the World Food Program (WFP) to pay for food for refugees in the African nations of Zaire and Mauritania, the Foreign Ministry said Thursday [27 January]. The government has extended 1 million dollars to each nation to help the refugees through Japan's WFP international emergency food reserve, the ministry said. In Mauritania, there are about 50,000 refugees from neighboring Mali's Tuareg tribe due to clashes among ethnic groups and between the Mali army and the Tuareg tribe, the ministry said. In Zaire, there are some 140,000 people who are taking refuge in the northern region of Kasai because of regional ethnic disputes, the ministry said.
