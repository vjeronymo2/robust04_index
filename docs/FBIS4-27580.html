BFN [Text] Seoul, May 31 (YONHAP) -- South Korea has decided to apply to join the Organization for Economic Cooperation and Development (OECD) this year. The Finance Ministry held the first meeting of a working-level committee tasked with preparing for OECD membership, headed by Second Assistant Minister Sin Myong-ho, on Tuesday morning and decided that the Ministry will study OECD regulations in the third quarter and apply to join in December. The 40-member committee will discuss Korea's bid to enter the OECD with related ministries, analyze its liberalization regulations and study the systems of present OECD member countries. Earlier, Finance Minister Hong Chae-hyong appointed four lawyers as consultants to do research and offer advice on legal aspects of the matter.
